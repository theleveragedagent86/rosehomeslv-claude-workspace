# Content Rules — Local News

These rules apply to ALL output: video transcripts, blog posts, Instagram captions, YouTube descriptions, Reddit posts, and spreadsheet summaries. Every sub-agent must follow these without exception.

---

## Writing Style

- **No em-dashes.** Never use the em-dash character. Use commas, periods, semicolons, or the word "and" instead. This is the most important formatting rule.
- **6th-grade reading level.** Short sentences. Simple words. Talk like you are explaining to a friend over coffee.
- **Professional but warm tone.** Knowledgeable neighbor, not newscaster. Not salesy, not stiff.
- **Active voice preferred.** "The city approved the project" not "The project was approved by the city."
- **Short paragraphs.** 3-4 sentences max per paragraph.

## Factual Standards

- **Factual only.** Never fabricate news stories, dates, names, quotes, statistics, or details.
- **Mark gaps.** If a detail cannot be verified, mark it as `[NOT VERIFIED]` so a human can fill it in.
- **Every story must have a verifiable source URL.** No source URL means do not include the story.
- **Recency.** Only include stories from the past 7-10 days unless the story has ongoing significance.
- **No rumors or unconfirmed reports.** If a story is speculation, skip it.

## Geographic Scope

- **Clark County, Nevada only.** Las Vegas, Henderson, North Las Vegas, Boulder City, Summerlin, Spring Valley, Centennial Hills, Skye Canyon, Green Valley, Mountains Edge, Southern Highlands, Aliante, Inspirada, Cadence, and unincorporated Clark County.
- Be specific about location within Clark County. "Henderson" is better than "Las Vegas area."

## Calls to Action

- **Soft CTAs.** Invite, do not push. "Want to know more?" beats "Call now!"
- **Video CTAs.** Follow for more, share with someone who needs this, thought-provoking comment question.

## Hockey-Specific Rules

- Use full team names on first reference: "Vegas Golden Knights" and "Henderson Silver Knights." After first reference, "VGK" and "Silver Knights" are fine.
- Include venue names (T-Mobile Arena, Lifeguard Arena, City National Arena) for discoverability.
- For local rink stories, name the specific rink and neighborhood. "The Fiesta Rancho ice rink in North Las Vegas" not "a local ice rink."
- Treat youth hockey, travel hockey, and house league events as community news. These stories resonate with parents and families.

## Video-Specific Rules

- Transcripts are spoken aloud. Write for the ear, not the eye.
- Avoid complex sentence structures or subordinate clauses.
- No filler phrases like "in today's video" or "hey guys."
- Open with a hook: question, surprising fact, or "did you hear about..."

## Blog-Specific Rules

- **No metadata blocks in the blog body.** Never include category labels (`<span class="category-label">`), datelines (`<p class="dateline">`), or byline/author-meta blocks (`<div class="article-meta">By Ryan Rose...Published...`). No `.category-label`, `.dateline`, or `.article-meta` CSS. The blog content starts immediately after the H1 title, with no elements between them.
- Opening paragraph must directly answer the topic in the first 1-2 sentences.
- Use `"@type": "NewsArticle"` schema (not generic "Article").
- Include source attribution at the bottom of every blog.
- Include contact page hyperlink and 702-747-5921 CTA in every blog.
- Images use direct web URLs, not placeholder images.
- Blog URL pattern: `https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/[slug]` (singular /blog/, not /blogs/).
