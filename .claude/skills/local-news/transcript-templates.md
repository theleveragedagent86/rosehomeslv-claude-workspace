# Transcript and Output Templates — Local News

Templates for video transcripts, Instagram captions, YouTube descriptions, blog structure, and Reddit posts. All agents reference these for consistent formatting.

---

## Video Transcript Templates

### 30-Second Script (75-90 words)

```
[HOOK: Provocative statement or surprising fact that stops the scroll, 1 sentence]
[CONTEXT: What happened, where, and who cares, 2-3 sentences]
[THE TURN: The one detail or consequence most people don't know, 1-2 sentences]
[COMMENT QUESTION: Opinion-driving question tied to the story, 1 sentence]
```

CTA: End with the comment question only. At 30 seconds there is not enough time for share or follow CTAs.

### 60-Second Script (150-180 words)

```
[HOOK: Bold, provocative statement that reframes the story, 1 sentence]
[CONTEXT: Set the scene. What happened, where, who is affected, 2-3 sentences]
[FOLLOW CTA: 1 short sentence woven naturally into the flow]
[THE TURN: The part most people don't know. The consequence, the trap, the hidden detail that delivers real value, 2-3 sentences]
[LOCAL IMPACT: Name specific Las Vegas neighborhoods. Make it personal to the viewer, 1-2 sentences]
[SHARE CTA + COMMENT QUESTION: Area-specific share prompt + opinion-driving question, 2 sentences]
```

### 90-Second Script (220-280 words)

```
[HOOK: Provocative opening statement that reframes the story in a way nobody expects, 1 sentence]
[SOCIAL PROOF: Reference the buzz. A Reddit thread, a packed meeting, a viral reaction, community outrage, 1-2 sentences]
[SETUP: The basics. Who, what, when, where. Specific names, dates, dollar amounts, 2-3 sentences]
[FOLLOW CTA: 1 short sentence woven naturally into the flow]
[THE INSIDER DETAIL: Build to the part most people miss. The legal loophole, the financial trap, the policy detail that changes everything. This is the real value of the video, 3-5 sentences]
[ESCALATION: Specific consequences with local neighborhood names. Make the stakes personal and immediate. Use second person, 2-3 sentences]
[URGENCY + ACTION: One punchy urgency line, then tell them exactly what to do right now, 2 sentences]
[SHARE CTA: Area-specific share prompt, 1 sentence]
[COMMENT QUESTION: Provocative, slightly divisive opinion question that drives debate in the comments, 1 sentence]
```

### Hook Examples (make it a STATEMENT, not a generic question)
- "Um, excuse me. That ebike is a motorcycle."
- "The restaurant you grew up going to just served its last meal."
- "Clark County just quietly approved something that could change your property taxes forever."
- "CCSD does not want you to see these numbers."
- "That new development everyone is celebrating? It is going to destroy your commute."
- "Your HOA just got the power to do something you are not going to believe."

### Follow CTA Examples (pick one, vary across stories)
- "Follow for more local news like this."
- "Hit follow so you don't miss what's happening in Vegas."
- "Follow me for the local stories that actually matter."
- "If you live in Vegas, you should be following for stuff like this."

### Share CTA Examples (pick one, vary across stories)
- "Send this to every parent you know in Henderson."
- "Share this with anyone who lives near [specific area]."
- "Send this to someone in [Summerlin/Green Valley/etc.] who needs to see this."
- "Share this with anyone thinking about buying in Las Vegas right now."

### Comment Question Examples (must be specific and slightly provocative)
- "Should parents be held criminally liable for the actions of their children? Let me know in the comments."
- "Is this development good for the neighborhood or is it going to destroy property values? Drop your take below."
- "Should taxpayers be paying for this when teachers are underpaid? Comment what you think."
- "Would you pay that much for a burger at this new spot? Tell me in the comments."
- "Is the city making the right call or are they selling out the neighborhood? Let me know."

## Instagram Caption Template

```
[HEADLINE — Title case, 8-12 words, attention-grabbing framing]

[OPENING HOOK — 1-2 sentences setting the scene with a surprising fact or statement]

[FACTS — 3-5 short paragraphs, 1-2 sentences each, specific numbers/names/dates/locations]

[PIVOT — 1-2 short sentences reframing the story's bigger meaning]

[CONTEXT — 3-5 short paragraphs on why this matters, broader implications, opinion woven in]

[CLOSING QUESTION — 1 thought-provoking question driving comments]

For more Las Vegas local news, follow @rosehomeslv or visit rosehomeslv.com

Ryan Rose | Real Broker, LLC | 702-747-5921 | rosehomeslv.com
```

Rules: Zero hashtags. Zero emojis. 250-400 words body. Very short paragraphs (1-2 sentences). Opinion-driven.

## YouTube Shorts Description Template

```
[TITLE LINE — matches or closely mirrors the video hook]

[2-3 sentence summary of the story and why it matters to Las Vegas residents.]

Follow for more Las Vegas news you need to know.

Ryan Rose | Real Broker, LLC | 702-747-5921
rosehomeslv.com

**Tags:** [comma-separated tags, max 475 characters total]
```

---

## YouTube Tags Rules

- **Max 475 characters total** (count commas and spaces — YouTube's hard limit)
- Comma-separated, no # symbols, no quotes
- Order: story-specific terms first, then location terms, then brand terms
- Include 3-4 story-specific high-intent terms
- Include 3-4 location terms from: Las Vegas, Henderson, Clark County, Nevada (use what's relevant)
- End with 2 consistent brand terms: `Ryan Rose, rosehomeslv`
- No duplicate concepts
- Count characters before finalizing

## Reddit Post Template

```
Title: [Local News] [Conversational headline]

Body:
[Lead with the most interesting detail, 1-2 sentences]

[Context and key facts, 1-2 paragraphs]

[Why it matters to locals, 1 paragraph]

I made a quick video on this one too, check my profile if you want the visual breakdown.

Full story with more details: https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/[slug]

[Engagement question to close]
```

Flair mapping:
- Government/Development stories: "Educational" or "Market Data"
- Restaurant/Business stories: "Neighborhood Guide"
- School Board stories: "Educational"
- Hockey stories: "Neighborhood Guide"
