## Official Community Info
- HOA Website: Not found (Centennial Hills is a large area with multiple sub-HOAs rather than one master HOA)
- HOA Management: Varies by subdivision
- HOA Fees: Approximately $75/month for some communities; varies by subdivision
- Guard Gate: Partial — some subdivisions are gated, many are not. Gated communities exist with as few as 4-12 homes up to larger sections.
- Age Restrictions: None community-wide. No dedicated 55+ sections within Centennial Hills, though senior living facilities (Legacy House of Centennial Hills) serve the area.
- Key Amenities: Centennial Hills Park (120 acres), Centennial Hills Community Center YMCA (indoor lap pool, seasonal outdoor water park, fitness center, preschool, youth programs, senior programming), Centennial Hills Library, Centennial Hills Hospital, Centennial Center shopping plaza (900,000 sq ft), multiple neighborhood parks with playgrounds and splash pads

## Lifestyle & Location
- Drive to Strip: 20-30 minutes via US-95 (approximately 18 miles)
- Drive to Airport: 25-35 minutes (approximately 23 miles from Harry Reid International Airport)
- Drive to Downtown: 20-25 minutes (approximately 15-16 miles)
- Drive to Downtown Summerlin: 15-20 minutes
- Drive to Henderson: 30-40 minutes
- Drive to North Las Vegas employers: 10-20 minutes
- Nearest Hospital: Centennial Hills Hospital Medical Center, 6900 N. Durango Drive, Las Vegas, NV 89149 — an 8-story, 336-bed facility within the community; part of The Valley Health System; services include cardiology, gastroenterology, neurology/stroke care, oncology, orthopedics, surgery, and Women's Center
- Safety: Overall crime grade B+; safer than 74% of US neighborhoods; violent crime rate 2.901 per 1,000 (B+ grade, safer than 76% of neighborhoods); property crime rate 14.15 per 1,000 (A- grade, safer than 79% of neighborhoods); north part of neighborhood generally considered safest
- Demographics: Upper-middle income neighborhood; higher income than 73.8% of US neighborhoods; population over 100,000; covers approximately 54-56 square miles; zip codes 89130, 89131, 89149
- Pet Friendly: Yes — Centennial Hills Park has a dedicated dog park with separate small/large breed sections and agility equipment; Knickerbocker Park has separate small/large breed dog parks; Huckleberry Park has a dog area; Floyd Lamb Park offers 2,000+ acres for outdoor activities; multiple walking/jogging trails throughout community; Petco located in Centennial Centre
- Retirement Friendly: No dedicated 55+ communities within Centennial Hills, but retirement-friendly features include: Centennial Hills Hospital within community, Legacy House of Centennial Hills (assisted living and memory care starting at ~$4,800/mo at 6310 N Durango Dr), YMCA senior programming, quiet suburban streets, low crime rates, walkable parks, proximity to medical offices and shopping; 35 senior housing options available for rent in the area
- Notable: Named in a 2001 public contest with 2,222 votes; borders the Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument (a national park with Ice Age fossils) and is a short drive from Red Rock Canyon; centered around the I-11/US-95 and Las Vegas Beltway (CC-215) interchange; 110-acre mixed-use town center; not a transient community — residents tend to stay long-term; mountain views of Spring Mountains (west) and Sheep Mountains (east); established as one of the newer master-planned areas in the Las Vegas Valley; nightlife primarily along N Durango Drive in bar/gambling format
