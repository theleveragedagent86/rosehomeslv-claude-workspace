## Program Overview
- **Official Name:** Nevada Worker Advantage Program
- **Created By:** Governor Joe Lombardo's Nevada Housing Access and Attainability Act (AB540), enacted during the 2025 Legislative Session. AB540 received unanimous approval in the state Assembly and a 15-6 vote in favor by the Nevada State Senate.
- **Administering Agency:** Nevada Housing Division (part of the Department of Business and Industry)
- **Official Website:** https://www.homeispossiblenv.org/worker-advantage
- **Total Funding:** $18 million
- **Per-Household Amount:** $20,000
- **Target Households:** Approximately 900
- **Current Utilization:** 81 of 900 families have used the program (as of January 2026), leaving 819 spots remaining
- **Program Status:** Open, first-come first-served. Will remain open until all allocated funds are reserved. Launched December 1, 2025.
- **Broader AB540 Context:** The Nevada Housing Access and Attainability Act dedicated $250 million in state resources to support development of over $1 billion of affordable workforce and attainable housing. It appropriated $133 million for new attainable homes, financial assistance for low/moderate-income properties, land acquisition, homebuyer assistance for essential workers, and matching grants for local governments.

## Eligibility Requirements
- **Credit Score:** Minimum 640
- **Residency:** Must have lived in Nevada for at least 6 months at time of application
- **Income Limit:** Up to 150% of Area Median Income (AMI), varies by county. Clark County: $142,350. Carson City: $139,050. Other counties vary up to $156,750 (White Pine County).
- **First-Time Buyer Required:** No. This is a key differentiator — the program does not restrict assistance to first-time homebuyers. However, previous recipients of the Home Is Possible program are not eligible.
- **Maximum Purchase Price:** Up to $832,750 (per official Home Is Possible website; some press releases cite $806,500 — the official website figure of $832,750 appears to be the most current)
- **Occupancy:** Must occupy the property as primary residence
- **Employment:** At least one borrower must be employed full-time in a designated "essential worker" category
- **Homebuyer Education:** Must complete a homebuyer education course before closing
- **Compliance:** Must comply with Ginnie Mae, Fannie Mae, and Freddie Mac guidelines
- **Debt-to-Income Ratio:** Not specified for Worker Advantage specifically; standard HIP program allows up to 50% DTI (varies by credit score and loan type)

## Financial Terms
- **Assistance Amount:** $20,000 per qualifying household
- **Interest Rate:** 0% (no-interest second mortgage)
- **Monthly Payment:** None (no-payment loan)
- **Loan Type:** Non-forgivable 30-year second mortgage (junior lien)
- **Term:** 30 years
- **Repayment Trigger:** Due when the homeowner sells the property, refinances the loan, or reaches the end of the 30-year term
- **Allowable Uses:** Down payment, closing costs, and/or interest rate buydown via discount points
- **Rate Buydown Options:**
  - **Option 1:** Apply the full $20,000 directly toward down payment and/or closing costs
  - **Option 2:** Use funds to pay discount points equal to 2% of the principal loan amount to permanently buy down the mortgage interest rate, with any remaining funds applied toward down payment and/or closing costs
  - **Option 3:** Use funds to pay discount points equal to 4% of the principal loan amount to permanently buy down the mortgage interest rate, with any remaining funds applied toward down payment and/or closing costs
- **Note on Rate Buydown:** Reducing the interest rate can make a major difference in monthly payments over the life of a 30-year loan. A mortgage loan officer quoted in Fox5 emphasized that now is a good time because when spring comes, the housing market picks up, and "once the money is gone, it's gone."

## Comparable Programs

### Home Is Possible (Standard Program)
- **Assistance:** Up to 4% of total loan amount for down payment and closing costs
- **Loan Structure:** 30-year non-forgivable loan at 0% interest, no monthly payments
- **Forgiveness:** Debt forgiven after 3 years if borrower stays in the home
- **First-Time Buyer:** Yes, required (no home ownership in past 3 years)
- **Credit Score:** Minimum 640
- **DTI:** Up to 50% (varies by credit score and loan type)
- **Education:** Homebuyer education course required
- **Website:** https://www.homeispossiblenv.org/home-possible-program

### Home First Program
- **Assistance:** Up to $15,000 in down payment assistance
- **Forgiveness:** Forgivable after 3 years if buyer stays in the home
- **Max Purchase Price:** Up to $570,000
- **Residency:** Minimum 6 months Nevada residency
- **Loan:** 30-year fixed-rate mortgage

### Home Is Possible for Heroes
- **Target:** Honorably discharged veterans, National Guard, active military, surviving spouses
- **Assistance:** Below-market fixed interest rate 30-year loan
- **DPA:** $7,500 in down payment and closing cost assistance
- **Income Limit:** Below $95,500
- **Max Home Price:** Below $400,000
- **Credit Score:** Minimum 640
- **First-Time Buyer:** Not required
- **Can Combine:** With Mortgage Tax Credit (MTC) with program fees waived
- **Established:** 2014

### Home Is Possible for Teachers
- **Target:** K-12 public school teachers
- **Assistance:** Below-market interest rates on 30-year fixed-rate loan
- **DPA:** $7,500 in down payment and closing cost assistance

### Nevada Rural Housing Programs
- **Launchpad Homeownership Program:** 2%, 4%, or 5% DPA with below-market rate
- **Rural Rocks $20K Program:** Up to $20,000 for essential workers with 6+ months Nevada residency
- **Home At Last:** Second mortgage, no interest, no payments for 30 years, forgiven at end of term

### Clark County Community Housing Fund
- **Welcome Home Community Land Trust:** Homeownership opportunities for low-to-moderate income first-time homebuyers in Clark County. Expected to roll out late 2025, open applications Q1 2026. Email: CCCLT@ClarkCountyNV.gov

### FHA Loans (for comparison)
- **Down Payment:** 3.5% minimum with 580+ credit score
- **Credit Score:** 500-579 requires 10% down; 580+ requires 3.5% down
- **No geographic restriction or essential worker requirement**
- **Can be combined with Worker Advantage Program**
