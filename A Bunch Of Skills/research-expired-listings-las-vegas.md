## Listing Agreement & Legal Info (Nevada)

### Standard Nevada Listing Agreement

- **Most common type**: Exclusive Right-to-Sell agreement -- gives the listing agent exclusive privilege to market and sell the property within a pre-agreed timeframe
- **Typical duration**: 3 to 6 months (negotiable based on seller preference and market conditions)
- **Key components**: Listing price, duration, broker's commission, seller's obligations, confidentiality terms
- **Commission**: Negotiable; typically discussed and agreed upon before signing

### What Happens When a Listing Agreement Expires

- The broker's right to market the property and earn commission ends
- The seller is freed to: relist with the same agent, choose a new agent, sell independently, or take property off market entirely
- The agreement does NOT automatically renew -- it simply expires
- Any active showings or pending activity at expiration should be addressed in the agreement terms

### Expired vs Withdrawn vs Canceled Definitions

| Status | Definition | Contractual Impact |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Expired** | The listing contract period concluded without the property selling. No longer an active brokerage agreement with the seller. | Agreement is over. Seller is free to do as they choose. |
| **Withdrawn** | Listing removed from active marketing on MLS but the listing contract between seller and broker STILL EXISTS. | Broker relationship continues. Property just isn't being actively marketed. |
| **Canceled** | Seller and broker mutually agreed to terminate the listing agreement BEFORE the contract term expired. | Agreement is terminated early. Seller is free (subject to any protection clauses). |

### Seller's Rights at Expiration

- Full freedom to choose any agent or sell independently
- No obligation to renew with current agent
- Must still honor any safety/holdover clause in the original agreement
- Can relist immediately with a new agent (subject to MLS DOM rules)

### Early Cancellation in Nevada

- **Mutual consent**: Agreement can be terminated if both broker and seller agree in writing
- **Valid grounds for early termination**: Agent not fulfilling contractual duties (inadequate marketing, no regular updates); agent engaging in deceptive practices or misrepresentation; persistent negligence or unprofessional conduct
- **Consequences**: May include early termination fee, reimbursement for marketing expenses (photography, MLS fees, advertising costs)
- **Escalation**: If direct negotiation fails, sellers can escalate to the Nevada Real Estate Division
- **Legal advice recommended**: Disentangling from a listing agreement is a nuanced process

### Holdover / Safety / Protection Clause Details

- **Purpose**: Protects the listing agent from losing commission if a buyer they introduced purchases the home after the listing expires
- **How it works**: If a buyer who was shown/introduced to the property during the listing period purchases the property after expiration, the original agent may claim commission
- **Typical duration**: 30 to 45 days after the listing agreement expires
- **Protection for agents**: Prevents sellers from waiting for the listing to expire and then selling directly to a buyer the agent found, thereby avoiding commission
- **Seller tip**: Request a list of all buyers shown the property so you know who falls under the protection clause

## Pricing Analysis

### How Overpricing Affects Days on Market

- Homes priced 10% below list price spent **5x as long** on market as correctly priced homes
- Homes overpriced by 10%+ eventually sell for **10% or more LESS** than true market value
- Homes that fail to secure a buyer within a **10-day window** see sale prices up to **10% lower** than those priced near recent comps
- After 2 months on market, homes tend to sell for **5% less** than listing price
- 60% of homes sell within one month when priced correctly (2024 Realtor survey)
- In some markets, most homes sell within **1-3% of asking price** when correctly priced
- The longer a home sits, the lower the odds of selling at list price -- showings drop significantly after the first few weeks

### Price Reduction Strategy

- **How much to reduce**: 2-5% is often effective; experts suggest at least 3%; 5-10% if significantly overpriced
- **When to reduce**: Hard reevaluation by the 30-day mark; test market reaction for 2 weeks first
- **Price bracket strategy**: Drop into the next lowest price bracket (e.g., $415K to $399.5K) to appear in more searches
- **Avoid multiple small cuts**: A single, well-timed, meaningful reduction is better than a series of small cuts over time
- **Bonus**: Use price reduction as opportunity to refresh photos, update description, explore new marketing channels

### CMA (Comparative Market Analysis) Explained for Homeowners

- A CMA is a report prepared by a real estate agent comparing your home to 3+ recently sold similar properties in your area
- Factors compared: size, location, age, condition, quality, features
- Uses sales from the past 3-6 months for accuracy
- Adjustments made for differences (larger lot, extra bathroom, pool, etc.)
- Considers current market trends (buyer's vs seller's market)
- Typically provided FREE by your real estate agent
- Purpose: Set a realistic, competitive listing price that attracts buyers without leaving money on the table
- NOT the same as an appraisal (which is performed by a licensed appraiser for the lender)

## Buyer Perspective

### How Buyers Find Previously Listed Homes

- Buyers and their agents can see listing history, including previous prices, days on market, and status changes, through MLS data and public listing sites
- Relisting resets DOM counter but savvy buyers can trace past listings through Zillow, Redfin, and other portals that retain historical data
- Cumulative Days on Market (CDOM) tracked by MLS even after DOM reset
- Multiple delistings and relistings viewed as red flags suggesting hidden problems or unrealistic seller expectations

### Negotiation Leverage on Relisted Homes

- Buyers and investors recognize relisted properties aren't truly "new" and may submit lowball offers assuming seller desperation
- A home sitting unsold past the local average carries stigma (fairly or unfairly)
- Buyers in Las Vegas currently have significant leverage: #2 nationally for biggest swing from seller's to buyer's market
- 1 in 5 deals falling through gives buyers confidence to walk away and find better options
- Buyers want: clean pricing, credits for rate buydowns, move-in ready condition, and impeccable showing experience
- Buyer concession requests increasing: repairs, price reductions, closing cost credits, flexible timelines

### Why Deals Fall Through (Buyer Side)

- Buyers make offers before seeing home in-person, then bail after touring
- Buyers make offers contingent on selling their own home, then can't sell
- Buyers cancel after digesting monthly payment amounts at current rates
- Buyers find a better deal among 13,000+ competing listings and cancel current escrow
- Inspection findings trigger renegotiation or cancellation
- Appraisal comes in below contract price
