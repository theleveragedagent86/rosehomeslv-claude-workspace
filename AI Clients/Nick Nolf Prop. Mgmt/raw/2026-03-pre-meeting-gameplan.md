## Meeting Structure (60 minutes)

### Phase 1: Discovery (20 minutes)
**Goal:** Understand his pain. Let HIM talk. You're diagnosing, not selling.

**Questions to ask (in this order):**

1. "Walk me through a typical day for you and your team — from when you open the inbox to end of day."
2. "What tasks do you wish you could hand off completely?"
3. "How many emails does your team handle per day? Rough guess is fine."
4. "How many units are you managing right now?"
5. "What software are you using? Property management platform, email, etc."
6. "When a maintenance request comes in, what's the process from email to resolution?"
7. "What's the most frustrating part of your operations right now?"
8. "Have you tried any automation or AI tools before?"

**While he talks, write down:**
- Hours spent on email (his estimate)
- Number of emails per day/week
- Tools he uses
- Team size
- His #1 pain point (this becomes your lead offer)

> **Hormozi principle:** The person who asks the questions controls the conversation. You're a doctor diagnosing — not a salesperson pitching.

---

### Phase 2: ROI Calculation — LIVE (10 minutes)
**Goal:** Make the value undeniable with his own numbers.

**Do this on screen share or whiteboard:**

> "Based on what you just told me, let me show you what this is costing you right now..."

```
His numbers (example):
- Team handles ~40 emails/day
- Average 5-8 minutes per email
- That's ~4-5 hours/day on email alone
- 5 days/week = 20-25 hours/week
- At $20-25/hr (coordinator rate) = $2,000-2,500/month
- Annual cost: $24,000-30,000/year on email management alone

What automation handles:
- 60-80% of those emails can be auto-drafted or auto-responded
- Saves 12-20 hours/week
- Saves $1,200-2,000/month
- Annual savings: $14,400-24,000
```

> "So right now you're spending roughly $[X] per year on tasks that an AI system can handle in seconds. My job is to get you that time back."

**Then pause. Let that number sink in.**

---

### Phase 3: Demo (10 minutes)
**Goal:** Show, don't tell. Make it real.

Follow the demo script in demo-email-automation.md. Key moments:

1. Show a maintenance email being classified + response drafted (his jaw drops)
2. Show an emergency email getting flagged immediately (emotional hook)
3. Show a lease question answered with zero human effort
4. Ask: "Want to paste a real email from your inbox and see what happens?" (power move)

> **Key phrase:** "Your team doesn't disappear — they just stop doing the boring stuff. They review, approve, and focus on the work that actually needs a human."

---

### Phase 4: Present Options (10 minutes)
**Goal:** Give him 3 choices. He picks one — not "yes or no."

Screen share the proposal. Walk through all 3 options:

1. **Starter** — "If you just want to test the waters with one automation"
2. **Growth** — "This is what I'd recommend. Covers the three biggest time sinks and pays for itself in [X] months" *(point to the ROI math)*
3. **Full Suite** — "This is the full transformation — every email workflow automated"

**Pricing psychology (Hormozi + Nate Herk):**
- Present Option 3 first (anchor high)
- Then Option 1 (feels too small now)
- Then Option 2 as "the sweet spot" (most people pick the middle)
- Never discount. Ever. If he pushes back on price, reduce scope — don't reduce price.
- Frame it as an investment, not a cost: "You're spending $24k/year on this labor. This pays for itself by month [X]."

**If he says "I need to think about it":**
> "Totally understand. What specifically would you want to think through? I want to make sure I've answered everything."

This usually surfaces the real objection (price, trust, timing) so you can address it directly.

---

### Phase 5: Close (10 minutes)
**Goal:** Get the deposit or set a hard next step. Never leave with "I'll get back to you."

**If he's ready:**
> "Great — I'll send over the service agreement today. Once you sign and the deposit is in, I'll get started immediately. Sound good?"

**If he needs time:**
> "Totally fair. Let's put 15 minutes on the calendar for [2-3 days out] so I can answer any last questions and we can get started. Does [specific day/time] work?"

**Always get a SPECIFIC next step with a DATE.**

## Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "That's more than I expected" | "I get it. Let's look at the ROI again — you're spending $X/year on this work. Which option gets you the fastest payback?" |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | "I can absolutely adjust the scope. We could start with just the maintenance email automation at $[Starter price] and add more later." (Never discount — reduce scope) |
| "I need to talk to my partner/team" | "Makes sense. What questions do you think they'll have? Let's address those now so you can present it confidently." |
| "Can I see more examples/case studies?" | "This is my first build in the property management space, and honestly that's why you're getting my full attention and best pricing. I'm looking for one great case study to build my portfolio around — and I want that to be your company." (Turn the weakness into a strength) |
| "What if it doesn't work?" | "The 50/50 payment structure protects you — you don't pay the second half until you see it working. Plus you get [30/60/90] days of support after launch." |
| "We tried automation before and it didn't work" | "What tool did you use and what went wrong? [Listen] — That's actually really common with [X tool]. What I build is custom to your exact workflow, not a one-size-fits-all template." |
| "I want to start small" | "Perfect — that's what Option 1 is for. Let's pick the one automation that saves you the most time and nail it. You can always add more later." |

## Pricing Quick Reference

### The Formula (Nate Herk):
```
Hours saved/week x Hourly rate x 4 = Monthly savings
Monthly savings x 12 = Annual savings
Charge 20-30% of Year 1 savings = Your project fee
```

### Rules:
- Floor: Never under $1,500 for any package
- If >50% of prospects say yes, price is too low
- Bigger packages = lower percentage (volume incentive)
- Retainer = recurring revenue (the long game)
- 50% deposit, 50% on delivery — non-negotiable
- Never discount price. Reduce scope instead.

### Nick Saraev's Model:
- Project fee: $1,500-$5,000 per automation
- Retainer: $300-$2,000/mo depending on scope
- Always transition project clients to retainer

### Hormozi's Rules:
- Make it so good he feels stupid saying no
- Price high but fair — high price = high perceived value = higher commitment
- 3 tiers, anchor with the highest
- Never compete on price — compete on value

## Mindset Reminders

1. **You're not selling — you're solving.** He has a problem. You have the solution. The meeting is just connecting the two.
2. **His time is worth more than the automation costs.** The math proves it. Trust the math.
3. **First client = case study.** Even if you price this slightly lower than ideal, the testimonial and proof of concept are worth their weight in gold.
4. **Confidence comes from preparation.** You have the demo, the proposal, the agreement, and the gameplan. You're more prepared than 95% of people doing this.
5. **Silence is your friend.** After you state the price, shut up. Let him process. The first person to talk loses.
