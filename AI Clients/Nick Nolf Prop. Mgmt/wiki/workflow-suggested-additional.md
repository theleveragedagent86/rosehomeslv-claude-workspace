# Suggested Additional Workflows

These were identified from the meeting transcript as likely needs Nick didn't explicitly request but would benefit from.

## A. New Tenant Onboarding Packet Automation
Nick described 5-6 steps at every move-in (photos, videos, MLS listing, AppFolio entry, inventory documentation) but never asked for an orchestrating workflow. A single trigger (signed lease or move-in date) could kick off the entire sequence.

## B. Lease Expiration Early Warning System
Nick talked about cross-referencing lease end dates but never mentioned proactive notifications. In Nevada, lease renewals require advance notice (30-60 days). A workflow scanning for leases expiring in 30/60/90 days would trigger renewal or non-renewal processes automatically.

## C. Owner Reporting / Investor Communication
With 500+ investors, a monthly or quarterly automated summary per owner (maintenance completed, costs incurred, lease status) would save significant time. Nick described owners asking for specific records — this would preempt those requests.

## D. HOA Violation Response Workflow
Nick has templates for HOA violations but never described the full lifecycle. HOA violations have deadlines, require tenant notification, HOA response, and sometimes follow-up proof of compliance. Multi-step, deadline-driven automation opportunity.

## E. Move-Out / Turnover Coordination
The full turnover process (notice received, move-out inspection, cleaning/repairs, re-listing, scheduling next move-in photos) is a multi-week sequential process that wasn't addressed.

## F. Duplicate/Outdated File Cleanup in Google Drive
Given Nick's volume (tens of thousands of documents) and acknowledgment of human errors, a one-time deduplication and audit pass would catch orphaned files, misnamed documents, or duplicates.

## G. Incoming Email Triage and Urgency Flagging
Nick never distinguished urgent from non-urgent beyond business hours timing. A gas leak email is categorically different from a pet request. A triage layer flagging genuinely urgent emails for immediate human attention (even outside business hours) would be a safety net.
