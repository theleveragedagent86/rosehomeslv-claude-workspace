Aliante has become one of the most popular neighborhoods for military families stationed at Nellis Air Force Base, thanks to its proximity, family friendly amenities, and strong community feel. This North Las Vegas master planned community offers the schools, parks, and suburban stability that military families look for when choosing a home near base. ## How Close Is Aliante to Nellis Air Force Base? Nellis Air Force Base sits on the eastern side of North Las Vegas, and Aliante is located in the northwest portion of the city. The drive between the two typically runs around 15 to 20 minutes depending on the route, making it one of the more convenient off base housing options in the area. Multiple routes are available, so commute times stay consistent even during busier hours. For service members who need to report early or work irregular schedules, that short commute is a real advantage. Many military families also appreciate that Aliante feels distinctly separated from the base environment, giving them a true change of scenery when they come home. ## Why Do Military Families Choose Aliante? The reasons come down to quality of life. Aliante is a 1,905 acre master planned community with roughly 6,500 homes and a median household income around $105,090. The neighborhood includes highly rated schools like Goynes Elementary with an 8 out of 10 rating, Somerset Academy which ranks as the number 16 K through 8 school in Nevada, and Shadow Ridge High School. Beyond academics, Aliante offers a 24 mile trail system, Nature Discovery Park with 23.4 acres of open space, Deer Springs Park, and Aviary Park. The Aliante Casino, Hotel, and Spa and the Aliante Golf Club provide entertainment and recreation without needing to drive across town. For families managing deployments and PCS moves, having everything close by makes daily life significantly easier. ## Is Aliante Affordable for Military Families? Aliante offers a range of housing options that work within many military budgets, especially when factoring in BAH rates for the Las Vegas area. Homes range from starter properties to larger family layouts, and the HOA fees cover community maintenance that keeps property values strong. The neighborhood also benefits from newer construction, which means lower maintenance costs compared to older areas of the valley. Healthcare access is another practical consideration. The ER at Aliante, run by MountainView Hospital, is a 24 hour facility right in the community. UMC Quick Care at 5860 Losee Rd provides additional medical support nearby. ## Ryan Rose on Helping Military Families Find Homes Ryan Rose has worked with many military families relocating to the Las Vegas area and consistently recommends Aliante as a top choice.
