Aliante and Centennial Hills are two of the most popular master-planned communities in the Las Vegas Valley, and choosing between them comes down to budget, location preference, and lifestyle priorities. Both neighborhoods sit on the northern edge of the metro area, but they belong to different cities and offer distinct advantages. ## How Do Home Prices Compare in Aliante vs Centennial Hills? Aliante in North Las Vegas offers a median home price in the $444K to $460K range, making it one of the more affordable master-planned options in the valley. The community's HOA master fee sits at just $51 per month, which keeps overall housing costs lower than many comparable neighborhoods. Entry-level homes start around $347,000, giving buyers a wider range of price points to consider. Centennial Hills, located within the City of Las Vegas to the northwest, generally trends higher in median pricing. Newer construction and larger lot sizes contribute to the higher cost of living in that area. Buyers who prioritize value per square foot will find Aliante's average of $203.86 per square foot quite competitive. ## Which Neighborhood Has Better Amenities and Schools? Both communities deliver a strong amenities package. Aliante features the Gary Panks designed Aliante Golf Club, the Aliante Nature Discovery Park, a 24 mile trail system, and the AAA Four Diamond rated Aliante Casino Hotel and Spa. Centennial Hills counters with its own parks, shopping centers, and proximity to newer retail developments. Schools in both areas perform well. Aliante is served by highly rated campuses like Goynes Elementary (rated 8 out of 10) and Somerset Academy (B+ rating). Centennial Hills also has access to strong public and charter schools within the Clark County School District. Families will find solid educational options in either neighborhood. ## Is Aliante or Centennial Hills Safer? Aliante earns an A+ crime score, placing it among the safest communities in the entire Las Vegas metro. The neighborhood's master-planned design, well lit streets, and active HOA contribute to that reputation. Centennial Hills also maintains low crime rates relative to the broader valley, though specific scores vary by pocket within the larger area. ## What Makes Aliante Unique Compared to Centennial Hills? Aliante holds the distinction of being the first master-planned community in North Las Vegas, spanning 1,905 acres and opening in May 2003. It includes Sun City Aliante, a Del Webb 55 plus community that gives it a multigenerational appeal Centennial Hills does not match. The proximity to Nellis Air Force Base also draws military families, contributing to a strong sense of community and stable housing demand. ## A Local Perspective on Both Neighborhoods As someone who helps buyers evaluate Las Vegas communities every day, I find that Aliante consistently surprises people with how much it delivers for the price. The combination of low HOA fees, top tier amenities, and an A+ safety rating makes it hard to beat for families and retirees alike. Centennial Hills is a great neighborhood too, but Aliante offers more value per dollar in 2026.
