What Is Caprock at Ascension by Pulte Homes?

Caprock is the two story collection by Pulte Homes inside Ascension, the guard gated luxury community in Summerlin. Offering floor plans from 3,981 to 4,530 square feet with prices starting at $1,699,990, Caprock delivers the most square footage per dollar of any active collection in the neighborhood.

How Large Are the Caprock Floor Plans?

Caprock floor plans range from 3,981 to 4,530 square feet, with every model featuring five bedrooms and four and a half to five and a half bathrooms. Garage options include three car and four car configurations, a standout feature for families with multiple vehicles or those wanting dedicated workshop space. These homes sit on Pulte's lots, which average around 8,000 square feet.

Starting at $1,699,990, Caprock enters the market at a lower price point than every other active collection in Ascension. Lot premiums with Pulte Homes range from $25,000 to $300,000 depending on location and orientation. Buyers should also factor in design center upgrades, which allow full customization of finishes, fixtures, and layout options.

What Features Define a Caprock Home?

Pulte Homes builds Caprock with modern architectural lines that match the contemporary desert aesthetic found throughout Ascension. Expect dramatic great room ceilings reaching 14 to 22 feet, expansive storefront windows, and 16 foot slider doors connecting interior spaces to covered loggias. Wolf appliances come standard in the kitchen, consistent with the luxury positioning of the community.

The two story layout makes Caprock ideal for larger families or buyers who want distinct zones for entertaining, daily living, and private retreats. Ryan Rose notes that the four car garage option in Caprock is rare at this price point in Summerlin, making it a practical choice for collectors or families with teenagers who drive.

How Does Caprock Compare to Other Ascension Collections?

Caprock stands out as the entry point into Ascension's active inventory. The sold out Incline collection by Pulte started around $1 million but offered smaller single story plans between 2,297 and 2,875 square feet. Caprock nearly doubles that square footage while remaining below the $1.7 million threshold. For buyers comparing across builders, Toll Brothers' Crestline collection offers similar two story layouts starting at $2,054,995, roughly $355,000 more than Caprock's base price. Ascension is 85% built out with only 561 total homesites, so remaining Caprock lots will not last indefinitely.
