Ascension Summerlin home prices in 2026 range from approximately $1,200,000 for entry level floor plans to over $4,000,000 for premium Crestline homes with elevated lots and mountain views. The current median sale price sits around $2,330,000, making Ascension one of the premier luxury communities in all of Summerlin. ## What Do Homes Cost in Each Ascension Subdivision? Ascension features six subdivisions built exclusively by Toll Brothers and Pulte Homes. Two collections have already sold out: Incline by Pulte (the most accessible price point starting near $1,200,000) and Ridgeline by Toll Brothers. Four active subdivisions remain, with Crestline by Toll Brothers representing the top of the market at $4,000,000 and above for homes on premium lots. Pulte's lots average around 8,000 square feet, while Toll Brothers lots average 10,000 square feet, with select homesites reaching 15,000 to 20,000 square feet. ## What Features Justify These Price Points? Every home in Ascension delivers luxury specifications that match the price tag. Expect soaring great room ceilings between 14 and 22 feet, expansive storefront windows, 16 foot slider doors that blur the line between indoor and outdoor living, and gourmet kitchens outfitted with Wolf appliances. Primary suite retreats, flexible living spaces, and covered loggias come standard across collections. These are single family homes only; there are no townhomes or condos in the community. ## Are There Additional Costs Beyond the Purchase Price? Buyers should plan for monthly HOA fees totaling $322: $255 per month for the Ascension sub association and $67 per month for the Summerlin Master Association. There is also a one time amenity fee of $5,000. The good news is that Ascension carries no SID or LID fees, which is a meaningful savings compared to some other new construction communities in the valley.
