Ascension Summerlin Reviews: Pros and Cons

Ascension in Summerlin earns strong reviews from buyers for its guard gated security, exclusive amenities, and prestigious Peaks village location, though higher price points and ongoing construction are worth considering. Here is an honest breakdown of what residents and buyers report about living in this community.

What Are the Pros of Living in Ascension Summerlin?

Guard gated security with 24/7 staffed entry tops the list for most buyers. The exclusive 7,500 square foot clubhouse features two pools, four pickleball courts, a golf simulator, and a fitness center reserved only for Ascension's 561 households. Homes built by Toll Brothers and Pulte deliver modern floorplans with high end finishes, and the community carries no SID or LID fees, which saves buyers thousands compared to other new construction neighborhoods.

Location within The Peaks village places residents alongside The Ridges, Mesa Ridge, and the Summit Club. Red Rock Canyon views, access to 200 plus miles of Summerlin trails, and a 20 minute drive to the Strip and airport add daily convenience. Strong equity gains recorded through 2025 reinforce Ascension's value as both a lifestyle choice and a long term investment.

What Are the Cons of Living in Ascension Summerlin?

The entry price starts near $1.2 million with a median around $2.33 million, placing Ascension firmly in the luxury tier. Monthly HOA fees total $322 (combining the Ascension sub association and Summerlin master), plus a one time $5,000 amenity fee at closing. Construction is approximately 85% complete, meaning some residents still experience building activity in neighboring subdivisions. School ratings present another consideration: Durango High School currently holds a 2 out of 5 rating, which may concern families prioritizing public education. Desert climate summers with extreme heat are a reality for all Las Vegas communities.
