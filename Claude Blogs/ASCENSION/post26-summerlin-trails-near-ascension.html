What Trails Are Near Ascension in Summerlin?

Ascension residents in Summerlin have direct access to over 200 miles of interconnected trails, making it one of the most walkable and bikeable communities in all of Las Vegas. Whether you prefer a morning jog, a sunset bike ride, or a leisurely stroll, the trail network surrounding Ascension delivers year round outdoor connectivity.

How Big Is the Summerlin Trail System?

The Summerlin trail system spans more than 200 miles throughout the master planned community, connecting neighborhoods, parks, shopping centers, and schools. The 215 Beltway Trail alone covers over 10 completed miles of paved pathway designated for walking, biking, and running. This regional trail runs along the 215 Beltway and will eventually connect directly to Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, giving Ascension residents a seamless route from their front door to world class hiking.

Does Ascension Have Its Own Walking Trails?

Yes, Ascension features internal trails within the gated community designed for residents who want to stay active without leaving the neighborhood. These paths are perfect for quick walks, evening jogs, or cycling with the family. The internal trails connect to Summerlin's broader network, so residents can easily extend any outing into a longer adventure through nearby parks like Mesa Park and The Gardens Park.

Can You Bike or Run to Red Rock Canyon from Ascension?

Red Rock Canyon sits just 10 to 15 minutes west of Ascension, and serious cyclists regularly use the Summerlin trail network to reach the canyon's iconic 13 mile scenic loop. Runners and hikers can also access trailheads with a short drive. Ryan Rose often highlights this proximity as one of Ascension's strongest lifestyle advantages: few communities in Las Vegas put you this close to both luxury amenities and protected desert wilderness.
