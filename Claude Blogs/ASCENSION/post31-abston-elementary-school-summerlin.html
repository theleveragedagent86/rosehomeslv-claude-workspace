Abston Elementary School ranks in the top 30% of all Nevada elementary schools for overall test scores, making it one of the strongest public elementary options for families in the Ascension community. This K through 5 school serves as the zoned elementary for Ascension in Summerlin South, giving residents direct access to above average academics without a lengthy commute.

How Does Abston Elementary Perform Academically?

Abston Elementary consistently outperforms both Clark County and Nevada state averages in core subjects. In the 2023 to 2024 school year, 43.7% of students scored proficient or better in mathematics, compared to just 30.1% across Clark County. Reading results were equally strong, with 50.6% of students reaching proficiency versus the county average of 39.3%. These numbers place the school well above its peers in the district.

What Programs and Resources Does Abston Offer?

The school maintains a student to teacher ratio of 17 to 1, allowing for more individualized instruction than many CCSD schools. Abston has earned recognition for its STEAM education focus, integrating science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics into its curriculum. The school serves approximately 727 students and has built a reputation for academic consistency within the Summerlin area.

Why Do Ascension Families Choose Abston Elementary?

Location plays a significant role, as Abston is the zoned elementary school for the Ascension community. Families moving into Ascension's guard gated neighborhoods gain automatic enrollment access. The school also feeds into Victoria Fertitta Middle School, creating a clear academic pathway through the local public school system.
