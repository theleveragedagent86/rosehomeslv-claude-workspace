What Do Families Need to Know About Durango High School in Las Vegas?

Durango High School is the zoned public high school for Ascension in Summerlin South, serving approximately 2,600 students in grades 9 through 12 within the Clark County School District. The school holds a Niche grade of B minus and offers a range of academic programs, including a recognized STEM track that stands out within the district.

How Does Durango High School Perform Academically?

Durango's standardized test scores fall below state averages, with 13% math proficiency and 29% reading proficiency compared to Nevada's 30% and 44% respectively. However, the school's overall academic picture includes an average GPA of 3.37, a graduation rate of 84%, and average standardized test scores of 1120 on the SAT and 21 on the ACT. These metrics show that many students perform well despite the broader proficiency numbers.

What Programs and Activities Does Durango Offer?

Durango's STEM program has earned recognition as one of the strongest in the district, qualifying the school for STEM school designation. Beyond academics, students have access to a wide variety of extracurricular activities and athletic programs. The school provides multiple pathways for students to explore their interests, from career and technical education tracks to competitive sports teams.

Is Durango the Only High School Option for Ascension Families?

While Durango is the zoned high school, Ascension families also have access to alternatives within driving distance. Bishop Gorman High School, a nationally ranked private school, sits approximately five miles from the community. Several charter high schools in the Summerlin area also accept students through lottery enrollment.
