Why Is Bishop Gorman High School One of Nevada's Top Rated Schools?

Bishop Gorman High School is the oldest Catholic high school in the Archdiocese of Las Vegas and one of the highest ranked private schools in Nevada, with a 98% college bound rate and over 105 state championships in athletics. Located approximately five miles from the Ascension community in Summerlin, Bishop Gorman offers families a premier private education option without a long commute.

How Strong Are Bishop Gorman's Academics?

Bishop Gorman operates as a college preparatory school with three academic tracks: College Prep, Honors, and Scholars. The Class of 2024 produced 52 Scholars graduates, 140 Honors graduates, fifteen National Merit Scholarship Commended Students, eleven Finalists, and three National Merit Scholarship Recipients. That senior class collectively received $35 million in scholarship offers, reflecting the school's emphasis on preparing students for competitive universities.

What About Athletics and Extracurriculars at Bishop Gorman?

Bishop Gorman's athletic program is among the most decorated in the country, with over 105 state championships accumulated over its 70 year history. The school won back to back national football championships in 2014 and 2015. The Class of 2025 saw 40 seniors earn athletic scholarships to compete at the college level. The 36 acre campus includes a state of the art athletic training center, dedicated fields for baseball, football, lacrosse, soccer, and softball, along with two gyms, tennis courts, and a track.

What Is the Campus and Student Experience Like?

Bishop Gorman moved to its current Summerlin location in 2007, constructing a 187,000 square foot facility on 36 acres at a cost of $96 million. The school enrolls approximately 1,535 students in grades 9 through 12, with a student to teacher ratio of 21 to 1. Ryan Rose notes that Bishop Gorman's proximity to Ascension makes it especially appealing for families seeking a private school alternative to the zoned public high school.
