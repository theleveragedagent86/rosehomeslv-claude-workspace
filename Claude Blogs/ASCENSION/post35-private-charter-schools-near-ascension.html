What Private and Charter Schools Are Available Near Ascension Summerlin?

Families in Ascension have access to more than two dozen public, private, and charter schools within the greater Summerlin area, including nationally recognized private institutions and top performing charter networks. Summerlin boasts more schools than any other Las Vegas Valley community, giving Ascension residents a wide range of educational choices beyond the zoned public schools.

Which Private Schools Are Closest to Ascension?

Bishop Gorman High School sits approximately five miles from Ascension and ranks as one of the top private schools in Nevada, with a 98% college bound rate and over $35 million in scholarships earned by recent graduates. The Meadows School offers a K through 12 college prep curriculum with smaller class sizes. Faith Lutheran High School provides another private secondary option with strong academics. The Adelson Educational Campus, Nevada's only Jewish school, serves pre kindergarten through 12th grade in The Hills Village of Summerlin.

What Charter School Options Serve the Summerlin Area?

Doral Academy operates multiple campuses near Summerlin and holds a Niche grade of A minus, with a 100% graduation rate and an average SAT score of 1290. The Doral Academy network has won Best Charter School recognition in the Best of Las Vegas Awards. Coral Academy of Science Las Vegas focuses on STEM education and has been ranked the number one charter school in Nevada by U.S. News. Somerset Academy and Pinecrest Academy also maintain campuses accessible from the Ascension area, each offering lottery based enrollment.

How Do Charter Schools Differ From Traditional Public Schools?

Charter schools in Nevada are tuition free public schools that operate independently from the Clark County School District. Enrollment is typically determined by lottery rather than by residential zoning. This means Ascension families can apply to any charter school regardless of address, though acceptance is not guaranteed. Ryan Rose recommends that families research lottery timelines early, as application windows often open months before the school year begins.
