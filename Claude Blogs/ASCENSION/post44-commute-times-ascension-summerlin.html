Commute Times From Ascension Summerlin

Commute times from Ascension Summerlin to major employment centers in the Las Vegas valley range from 15 to 30 minutes, making this Summerlin South community a practical home base for working professionals. Direct access to Summerlin Parkway, US 95, and the 215 Beltway keeps most destinations within reach.

How Long Is the Commute to the Las Vegas Strip?

The drive from Ascension to the Las Vegas Strip takes approximately 20 minutes via Summerlin Parkway and I 15. During peak rush hour between 4:00 PM and 6:30 PM, the commute may stretch to 25 or 30 minutes. Residents who work in the resort and hospitality industry benefit from a straightforward, mostly highway route that avoids surface street congestion.

What About Commutes to Downtown and the Medical District?

Downtown Las Vegas and the Fremont Street area are roughly 25 minutes from Ascension via Summerlin Parkway and US 95. The Medical District, home to several major hospitals and healthcare employers, sits approximately 20 minutes east. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is the closest major hospital, just 10 to 15 minutes from the community, which matters for healthcare workers who want a short commute.

How Accessible Is Harry Reid International Airport?

Ascension residents can reach Harry Reid International Airport in about 20 minutes by taking I 15 south. This makes Ascension a strong fit for frequent travelers or professionals with business travel requirements. The 215 Beltway also provides alternate routes to the southern and western parts of the valley, connecting residents to employers in Henderson, the Southwest, and along the I 15 corridor.
