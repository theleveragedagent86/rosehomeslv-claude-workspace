Is Summerlin South Safe? Crime Rates 2026

Yes, Summerlin South is one of the safest areas in the Las Vegas valley, with a total crime rate that is 47% lower than the national average. Communities like Ascension benefit from this low crime environment along with additional guard gated security.

What Are the Crime Rates in Summerlin South?

Summerlin South's overall crime rate is 1,224 per 100,000 residents, well below the national average. The violent crime rate sits at 15.9 compared to the national average of 22.7, and the property crime rate is 30 versus the national average of 35.4. The chance of being a victim of violent crime in Summerlin South is just 1 in 556, making it significantly safer than most metropolitan areas of comparable size.

How Does Guard Gated Access Improve Safety?

Ascension is a guard gated community within Summerlin South, which adds a layer of security beyond the area's already low crime statistics. Controlled entry points, security personnel, and limited access reduce the risk of opportunistic property crime. Many of the surrounding communities in The Peaks village also feature gated access, creating a broader zone of residential security throughout the area.

What Makes Summerlin South Safer Than Other Las Vegas Neighborhoods?

Several factors contribute to the safety record. The master planned design of Summerlin incorporates well lit streets, maintained common areas, and active homeowner associations that enforce community standards. Higher home values and strong neighborhood engagement also correlate with lower crime. The area's distance from high traffic tourist corridors means fewer transient visitors, which reduces the types of crime that are more common closer to the Strip.
