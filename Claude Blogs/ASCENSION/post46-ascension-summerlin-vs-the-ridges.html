How Does Ascension Summerlin Compare to The Ridges?

Ascension and The Ridges are both guard gated luxury communities within The Peaks village of Summerlin South, but they serve different buyers at different price points. Ascension offers newer construction starting around $1.2M, while The Ridges caters to the ultra luxury market with custom homes ranging from $2M to over $10M.

What Are the Key Differences Between Ascension and The Ridges?

Ascension is the newer community of the two, with construction beginning in 2023 by Toll Brothers and Pulte Homes. The neighborhood includes 561 planned homes featuring modern architecture and pricing from $1.2M to over $4M. The Ridges, by contrast, is an established enclave known for its custom built estates and ultra luxury positioning. Homes in The Ridges regularly list between $2M and $10M or more, attracting buyers seeking one of a kind properties with maximum privacy.

Which Community Has Better Amenities?

Ascension features an exclusive residents only clubhouse with resort style pools, fitness facilities, pickleball and basketball courts, and an activity lawn. The Ridges does not have its own private clubhouse comparable to Ascension's, though residents enjoy full access to the broader Summerlin trail and park system. Both communities benefit from proximity to Summit Club and sweeping Red Rock Canyon views that define luxury living in Summerlin South.

Which Community Is the Better Value in 2026?

For buyers seeking brand new construction with modern floor plans and dedicated amenities, Ascension delivers strong value in the $1M to $4M range. The Ridges is better suited for buyers who want fully custom homes and are comfortable with the ultra luxury price tier. Ryan Rose often advises clients that the choice comes down to whether they prioritize new construction with built in community features or the prestige and customization of an established luxury enclave.

Ryan Rose's Local Insight

Both Ascension and The Ridges sit within minutes of each other in The Peaks village, so location is essentially identical. The real differentiator is lifestyle preference: Ascension's clubhouse culture and newer builds attract a social, amenity driven buyer, while The Ridges appeals to those who want architectural individuality and maximum lot size. Both communities hold their value well due to guard gated access and Summerlin's continued demand.
