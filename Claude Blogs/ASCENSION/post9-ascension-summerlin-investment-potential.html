Is Ascension Summerlin a Good Investment?

Yes, Ascension in Summerlin has proven to be a strong real estate investment, with homeowners reporting significant equity gains throughout 2025. The combination of limited inventory, guard gated exclusivity, and Summerlin's premier location in The Peaks village positions Ascension as one of the most promising luxury communities in Las Vegas for long term appreciation.

What Equity Gains Have Ascension Homeowners Seen?

Homeowners who purchased early at Ascension have experienced strong equity gains since sales began in late 2023. The sold out Incline collection by Pulte, originally priced from approximately $1,000,000, and the sold out Ridgeline collection by Toll Brothers, originally from $1,768,000, now command significantly higher resale values. With the community 85% complete and remaining inventory shrinking, scarcity continues to drive appreciation across all collections.

Why Does Ascension Hold Its Value So Well?

Several factors support Ascension's investment potential. The community is fully guard gated with 24/7 security, limited to just 561 homes across 261 acres. It sits within The Peaks village alongside established luxury communities like Mesa Ridge, The Ridges, and Summit Club. There are no SID or LID fees, which protects homeowners from unexpected assessments. The $322 monthly HOA covers resort caliber amenities including a 7,500 square foot clubhouse, pools, fitness center, pickleball courts, and a golf simulator.

Who Is Buying at Ascension and What Does That Mean for Values?

Out of state relocators represent a significant buyer demographic at Ascension, many coming from higher cost markets in California, Washington, and the Northeast. These buyers often pay cash or bring substantial equity, which supports pricing stability. With homes ranging from $1,200,000 to over $4,000,000 and a median price around $2,330,000, Ascension occupies the upper tier of Summerlin's luxury market where demand consistently outpaces supply.
