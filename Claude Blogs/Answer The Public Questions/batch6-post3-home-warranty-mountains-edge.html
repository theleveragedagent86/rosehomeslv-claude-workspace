Home Warranty Benefits for Mountain's Edge Sellers

Should Mountain's Edge Sellers Offer a Home Warranty?

Mountain's Edge sellers should consider offering a home warranty when selling homes with older systems or appliances, or when competing in a buyer's market where additional incentives help listings stand out. A seller's home warranty costs $450 to $600 on average and provides protection both during the listing period and transfers to the buyer at closing.

How Seller Home Warranties Work

A seller's home warranty covers repair or replacement costs for major systems and appliances that break down during the listing period, protecting sellers from unexpected expenses before closing. The coverage typically transfers to buyers for one year after the sale, giving them peace of mind against immediate post purchase repairs. Many warranty companies offer free or discounted coverage while your home is listed if you agree to purchase a full year warranty for the buyer.

Home warranties are service contracts, not insurance. They cover items like HVAC systems, plumbing, electrical, water heaters, and kitchen appliances when breakdowns occur from normal wear and tear. They do not cover structural issues, pre-existing conditions, or items damaged through neglect.

When Home Warranties Make Sense

Offering a warranty becomes most valuable when your home has older systems that might concern buyers, when competing against similar listings that include warranty incentives, or when you want to reduce the risk of post sale disputes. Real estate professionals report that warranties can save sales when unexpected repairs arise during escrow. If your HVAC fails before closing, the warranty covers the repair rather than requiring you to pay thousands out of pocket or renegotiate with buyers.

In hot seller's markets with strong demand, warranties may be unnecessary since buyers are already competing for limited inventory. In balanced or buyer's markets, the $500 investment can differentiate your listing and attract budget conscious buyers.
