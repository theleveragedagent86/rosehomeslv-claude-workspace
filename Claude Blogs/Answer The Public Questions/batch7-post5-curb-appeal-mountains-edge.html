Curb Appeal Tips for Selling Your Mountain's Edge Home

Curb appeal can increase a home's perceived value by 3 to 7 percent, with some estimates suggesting well-landscaped properties command 15 to 20 percent premiums. According to a 2023 Thumbtack and Nextdoor survey, over half of homeowners believe attractive landscaping and exteriors can boost resale value by at least $20,000. More than 70 percent of real estate agents report clients passing on homes due to poor curb appeal. In Mountain's Edge, first impressions begin at the curb whether buyers drive by or scroll through online listings.

Quick Wins for Mountain's Edge Exteriors

Start with cleanup basics that cost little but make immediate impact. Power wash driveways, walkways, and sidewalks to remove desert dust and stains. Clean exterior windows inside and out for sparkling glass that suggests careful maintenance. Remove cobwebs from eaves, light fixtures, and entry areas. Replace worn house numbers and outdated mailboxes. Touch up paint on shutters, trim, fascia, and the front door. These tasks require more effort than money but transform buyer perception within a weekend.

Desert-Friendly Landscaping That Sells

Mountain's Edge landscaping should look attractive while appearing low maintenance. Fresh mulch or decorative rock in planting beds creates a clean, finished appearance. Trim overgrown shrubs and remove dead or dying plants that suggest neglect. Add drought-resistant plants native to Southern Nevada that thrive with minimal water. Avoid elaborate gardens that signal high upkeep requirements to potential buyers. In 2025, eco-friendly landscaping with native species appeals to environmentally conscious buyers while promising lower water bills.

Lighting and Architectural Highlights

Curb appeal extends beyond daylight hours since many buyers drive by homes in the evening. Install LED pathway lights for elegance and safety. Add warm-toned porch lighting that creates welcoming ambiance. Consider solar spotlights to highlight architectural features, mature trees, or textured facades. Black or antique bronze fixtures trend well in 2025. Proper exterior lighting demonstrates attention to detail and helps your listing stand out in evening photography.
