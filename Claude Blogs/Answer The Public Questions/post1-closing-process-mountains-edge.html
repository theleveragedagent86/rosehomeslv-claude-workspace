## Mountain's Edge Sellers # What Happens During Closing When I Sell My Mountain's Edge Home? After accepting an offer on your Mountain's Edge home, the closing process typically takes 30 to 45 days during which the buyer completes inspections, secures financing, and the title company handles paperwork, culminating in a final settlement where you sign documents, transfer ownership, and receive your proceeds. ## The Escrow Timeline Nevada is an escrow state, meaning a neutral third party (the escrow company or title company) holds all funds and documents until both parties fulfill their contractual obligations. Once you accept an offer, escrow opens and the clock starts. During the first week, the buyer deposits earnest money into escrow. You must order the HOA resale package immediately; Nevada law requires sellers to provide this, and the HOA has up to 10 business days to deliver it. The buyer can cancel up to 5 days after receiving this package, so delays here risk extending your timeline. The buyer typically schedules a home inspection within the first 10 days. Following inspection, they may request repairs or credits. Negotiating these items requires prompt responses to keep the transaction moving. ## Appraisal and Financing If the buyer is financing their purchase, their lender orders an appraisal to confirm the home's value supports the loan amount. This typically occurs during weeks two or three. If the appraisal comes in lower than the purchase price, renegotiation may be necessary. The buyer's loan goes through underwriting, where the lender verifies all financial documentation. This process can encounter delays if the buyer has incomplete paperwork or changing financial circumstances. Cash buyers skip this step entirely, often closing in two weeks or less. ## Title Work and Final Steps The title company conducts a title search to verify clear ownership and identify any liens. Title insurance protects both parties from undiscovered title issues. In Nevada, escrow fees are typically split between buyer and seller. A few days before closing, the buyer conducts a final walkthrough to verify the home's condition matches the contract terms and that any agreed repairs were completed. This is not a new inspection but a confirmation visit. ## Closing Day At closing, you sign the deed transferring ownership, settlement statements showing all financial details, and various disclosures. The buyer signs their loan documents. Once everything is recorded with Clark County, funds are released: your mortgage is paid off, closing costs are deducted, and remaining proceeds are wired to your account or provided via check. ## Local Expert Insight Ryan Rose coordinates closely with title companies, lenders, and cooperating agents to keep Mountain's Edge closings on schedule.
