Should You Sell Your Mountain's Edge Home By Owner or Use an Agent?

Most Mountain's Edge sellers net more money working with an agent than selling FSBO (For Sale By Owner). FSBO homes nationwide sell for approximately $55,000 to $65,000 less than agent assisted sales, which typically erases any commission savings and then some.

The Statistics Behind FSBO Sales

According to the National Association of Realtors, FSBO sales dropped to just 5% of all home sales in 2025, an all time low. Only 11% of FSBO sellers successfully complete the sale without eventually hiring an agent, while 90% of agent listed homes sell successfully. In Mountain's Edge, where the median home price hovers around $475,000, the potential loss from a FSBO sale could exceed $50,000, far more than the commission you would save.

Why FSBO Homes Sell for Less

FSBO sellers face significant disadvantages: limited MLS exposure (only 10% use the MLS), no access to the buyer pool working with agents, difficulty pricing accurately without comparative market analysis tools, and challenges navigating Nevada's disclosure requirements and contract negotiations. Many FSBO sellers also skip buyer incentives that help homes sell faster and for more money. In Mountain's Edge specifically, understanding the nuances of different sections, multiple HOA structures, and buyer demographics requires local market expertise.

When FSBO Might Work

FSBO can succeed in limited situations: when you already have a buyer lined up (38% of successful FSBO sellers knew their buyer beforehand), when speed matters more than price, or if you have significant real estate experience. Even then, you will likely pay the buyer's agent commission of 2.5% to 3% since 75% of FSBO sellers still pay this fee to access represented buyers.
