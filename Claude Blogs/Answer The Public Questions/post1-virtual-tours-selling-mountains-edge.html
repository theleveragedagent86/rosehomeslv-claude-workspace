Why Virtual Tours Matter for Mountain's Edge Sellers

Mountain's Edge draws strong interest from out of state buyers, particularly families relocating from California and other western states. According to a Redfin survey, 63% of buyers submitted an offer on a home they had not physically visited, relying instead on immersive digital walkthroughs. A University of Texas at Dallas study found that homes with virtual reality tours reduced their days on market from 34 to 19 days on average.

The technology works especially well for Mountain's Edge properties because the community features newer construction with open floor plans, upgraded finishes, and well designed outdoor spaces that showcase beautifully in 3D format. Buyers scrolling through listings spend five to ten times longer engaging with virtual tours compared to static photo galleries, giving your home more opportunity to make a lasting impression.

What Sellers Should Know About Implementation

Professional 3D tours typically cost between $200 and $500 depending on home size and are created using specialized cameras such as those from Matterport. The investment is minimal compared to the potential return: some studies indicate homes with 3D tours sold for up to 9% more than comparable listings without them. Virtual tours also reduce unnecessary showings by allowing buyers to pre screen properties, meaning the visitors who do schedule walkthroughs are genuinely interested and more likely to make offers.

Gen Z buyers (a growing segment of the market) are 130% more likely to schedule a showing when a listing includes a virtual tour. With 54% of all buyers indicating they would not even consider a home without one, skipping this step puts Mountain's Edge sellers at a competitive disadvantage in today's market.
