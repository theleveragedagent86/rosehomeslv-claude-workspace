## Mountain's Edge Sellers # Will I Pay Capital Gains Tax When Selling My Mountain's Edge Home? Most Mountain's Edge homeowners will not owe capital gains tax when selling their primary residence because Nevada has no state capital gains tax, and federal law allows you to exclude up to $250,000 in profit ($500,000 for married couples filing jointly) if you owned and lived in the home for at least two of the past five years. ## Nevada's Tax Advantage Nevada is one of only eight states with no state income tax, which means no state capital gains tax on home sales. This provides significant savings compared to selling in states like California, where capital gains face state taxation on top of federal requirements. Only federal capital gains rules apply to Nevada home sales. For most homeowners selling their primary residence, the federal exclusion eliminates any tax liability entirely. ## The Primary Residence Exclusion To qualify for the federal exclusion, you must meet three requirements: ownership (owned the home for at least 24 months of the past five years), residence (lived in the home as your primary residence for at least 24 months of the past five years), and look back (have not claimed this exclusion on another home sale within the past two years). The 24 months do not need to be consecutive. For married couples filing jointly, only one spouse needs to meet the ownership requirement, but both must meet the residence requirement. Single filers can exclude up to $250,000 in profit. Married couples filing jointly can exclude up to $500,000. Given that the typical Mountain's Edge home sells in the $475,000 range and many owners purchased during the past decade, most sellers fall well within these exclusion limits. ## Calculating Your Gain Your taxable gain is not simply sale price minus purchase price. Calculate it properly: start with the sale price, subtract the adjusted basis (original purchase price plus cost of improvements like kitchen remodels, roof replacements, or pool additions), and subtract selling costs (commissions, closing costs, and transfer taxes). For example, if you purchased your Mountain's Edge home for $350,000, made $30,000 in improvements, and sell for $500,000 with $40,000 in selling costs, your gain is $80,000 ($500,000 minus $350,000 minus $30,000 minus $40,000). This falls well under the exclusion threshold. ## When You Might Owe Tax Capital gains tax may apply if: you owned the home less than two years, you did not live in it as your primary residence, you claimed the exclusion on another home within two years, or your profit exceeds the exclusion amount. In these cases, federal long term capital gains rates of 0%, 15%, or 20% apply depending on your income level. ## Local Expert Insight Ryan Rose recommends Mountain's Edge sellers consult with a tax professional before listing, particularly if ownership has been less than two years, the property was ever used as a rental, or the expected profit is substantial.
