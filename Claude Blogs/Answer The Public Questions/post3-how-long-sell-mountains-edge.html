## Mountain's Edge Sellers # How Long Does It Take to Sell a House in Mountain's Edge? Homes in Mountain's Edge currently take an average of 38 to 60 days to sell, with approximately 50% of properly priced properties going under contract within 30 days, though this represents a slowdown from 2024 when the community was a stronger seller's market with faster absorption rates. ## Understanding Current Selling Timelines The Mountain's Edge market has shifted significantly from 2024. Last year, homes averaged just 12 days on market before receiving offers. In 2025, that timeline has extended to 38 to 60 days as inventory increased and buyer competition decreased. This does not indicate a declining market but rather a normalization after several years of unusually fast sales. Of homes that sold in recent months, half closed within 30 days of listing. Another 41% sold between 30 and 90 days, while only 9% required more than 90 days. Properties exceeding 90 days typically share common issues: overpricing, deferred maintenance, or unfavorable locations within the community. ## Factors That Speed Up Your Sale Price accuracy is the primary driver of quick sales in Mountain's Edge. Homes priced at or slightly below market value based on comparable sales within the past 60 days attract immediate buyer interest. Those priced 5% or more above recent comparables often sit without showings, eventually requiring price reductions that extend the overall timeline. Condition matters significantly. Mountain's Edge buyers expect move in ready homes given the community's newer housing stock. Fresh paint, clean carpets or updated flooring, functional systems, and appealing curb appeal all contribute to faster offers. Homes requiring obvious work or showing deferred maintenance lose to competing listings. Timing also affects days on market. Listing in March through June captures peak buyer activity when families prepare for summer moves. Listing in November or December means competing against holiday distractions and reduced buyer pools, though less competition can offset slower activity for well positioned properties. ## What Extended Days on Market Signals When Mountain's Edge homes exceed 60 days without offers, buyers perceive something wrong. They wonder about hidden issues, question the seller's motivation, or assume the property failed inspections on previous contracts. This perception creates a cycle where longer market time leads to lower offers, which leads to rejections, which extends market time further. ## Local Expert Insight Ryan Rose advises Mountain's Edge sellers that the first two weeks on market are critical.
