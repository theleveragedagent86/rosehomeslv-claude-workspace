## Mountain's Edge Sellers # What Repairs Should I Make Before Selling My Mountain's Edge Home? Focus repairs on items that will appear in buyer inspections and create red flags: HVAC maintenance, water heater functionality, roof condition, plumbing leaks, and electrical issues, while cosmetic updates like fresh paint, clean carpets, and landscaping touch ups create strong first impressions without expensive contractor bills. ## High Priority Repairs for Mountain's Edge Homes HVAC systems face extreme demands in the Las Vegas desert. Before listing, have your system professionally serviced and documented. Buyers and inspectors will flag units showing deferred maintenance. If your system is 15 years or older, consider whether replacement makes more financial sense than selling a home with aging equipment that will concern every buyer. Water heaters in Mountain's Edge homes typically last 8 to 12 years. Check the manufacture date and condition. A water heater approaching end of life will appear in inspection reports and become a negotiation point. Proactive replacement costs less than the credits buyers typically request when inspections reveal issues. Roof inspections reveal problems invisible from ground level. Missing or damaged tiles, deteriorating flat roof sections, and compromised flashing all concern buyers. Address known issues before listing rather than defending them during negotiations. A clean roof inspection removes a common contingency concern. Plumbing and electrical systems require attention to obvious problems. Dripping faucets, running toilets, slow drains, and flickering lights create impressions of neglect. These affordable fixes prevent buyers from wondering what larger problems lurk behind walls. ## Cosmetic Improvements That Pay Off Fresh paint provides exceptional return on investment. Neutral, modern colors appeal to the broadest buyer pool. Focus on high traffic areas, scuffed walls, and any bold colors that might not suit buyer tastes. Paint transforms perception of a home's age and maintenance level. Flooring condition matters significantly. Worn, stained, or outdated carpet should be replaced or professionally cleaned at minimum. Many Mountain's Edge buyers prefer hard surface flooring, so consider whether carpet replacement with vinyl plank or tile makes sense for your price point. Landscaping creates first impressions before buyers enter your home. Desert landscaping should appear intentional and maintained rather than neglected. Trim overgrown plants, replace dead specimens, refresh rock and gravel areas, and ensure irrigation systems function properly. ## Repairs to Skip Avoid major renovations expecting dollar for dollar return. Complete kitchen or bathroom remodels rarely recoup full costs during resale. Similarly, adding pools, extensive hardscaping, or luxury upgrades appeals to your taste but may not match buyer preferences. Do not hide problems with cosmetic patches. Painting over water stains without addressing the source, covering foundation cracks, or masking odors creates liability and contract issues when inspectors discover underlying problems. Honest disclosure serves sellers better than concealment attempts. ## Local Expert Insight Ryan Rose recommends Mountain's Edge sellers obtain pre listing inspections to identify issues before buyers discover them.
