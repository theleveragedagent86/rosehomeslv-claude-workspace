## Mountain's Edge Sellers # When is the Best Time to Sell a House in Mountain's Edge? The best time to sell a house in Mountain's Edge is March through June when buyer activity peaks, homes sell 7 days faster than average, and sale prices run approximately 2% higher than annual averages due to families relocating before the new school year and pleasant spring weather ideal for showings. ## Spring Selling Season Advantages March through June captures the strongest buyer demand in the Las Vegas market. Families with school age children prioritize moving during summer break, creating urgency to find and close on homes during spring. Tax refund season puts extra cash in buyer pockets for down payments. The weather in Mountain's Edge is ideal for showings with temperatures in the comfortable 70s and 80s before summer heat arrives. Listing in late February to early March positions your home to appear when buyer activity begins ramping up. You benefit from the initial wave of serious buyers who have been waiting through the slower winter months, often resulting in multiple showings during your first weekend on market. ## Summer and Fall Considerations June through August remains active but with caveats. Summer heat in Las Vegas (regularly exceeding 110 degrees) makes home tours challenging. Buyers scheduling afternoon showings may rush through properties to escape the heat, missing features that would otherwise impress them. Families who did not secure homes in spring become more motivated but pickier. September and October offer a secondary opportunity. The heat subsides, snowbirds begin arriving, and serious buyers who missed spring inventory return to the market. Inventory typically decreases during fall, meaning well priced homes face less competition. Prices remain stable though slightly below spring peaks. ## Winter Realities November through February represents the slowest period for Mountain's Edge sales. Holiday distractions, travel, and cooler (though mild by national standards) weather reduce buyer activity. However, winter listing is not without merit. Buyers shopping during holidays tend to have urgent needs: job relocations, family changes, or specific timelines that motivate quick decisions. Inventory also drops significantly during winter. December 2024 data showed 15% to 35% fewer listings than spring peaks. Sellers willing to list during holidays face dramatically reduced competition, potentially offsetting slower buyer traffic with increased visibility for their property. ## Local Expert Insight Ryan Rose notes that Mountain's Edge sellers often overthink timing at the expense of pricing. A well priced home in January will outperform an overpriced home in May. That said, if you have flexibility, listing in late February to catch the spring market typically yields the best combination of price and speed. Prepare your home during January, complete repairs and staging, and hit the market as buyer activity begins accelerating. ## Planning Your Mountain's Edge Home Sale?
