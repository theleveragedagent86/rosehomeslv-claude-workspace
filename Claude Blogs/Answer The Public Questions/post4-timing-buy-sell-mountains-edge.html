How Do You Time Selling and Buying a Home at the Same Time in Mountain's Edge?

You have several options: sell first and find temporary housing, buy first if you can qualify carrying both mortgages, use a bridge loan to access your current equity for the down payment, or align closing dates through careful negotiation. Each approach has trade offs between convenience, cost, and risk.

Sell First Strategy

Selling first eliminates financial risk and strengthens your position as a buyer (no sale contingency needed). The downside is potentially moving twice: once to temporary housing and again to your new home. Moving costs in Nevada typically range from $880 to $2,600 for local moves. This approach makes sense when you need maximum negotiating power on your purchase or cannot qualify for two mortgages.

Buy First Strategy

Buying first requires financial strength to carry two mortgages temporarily. You avoid the hassle of temporary housing and can take your time finding the right home. However, if your Mountain's Edge home takes longer to sell than expected, the financial strain intensifies. This approach works best for sellers with significant cash reserves or high income relative to both mortgage payments.

Bridge Loan Option

Bridge loans let you access equity in your current home to make a down payment on your new home before selling. Interest rates typically run 1% to 3% higher than standard mortgages, with terms usually under 12 months. You must qualify based on income supporting both your current mortgage and the new purchase. When your Mountain's Edge home sells, proceeds pay off the bridge loan. Several Las Vegas lenders offer bridge financing, and "Buy Before You Sell" programs from companies like HomeLight combine bridge loans with coordinated transactions.

Aligned Closing Strategy

The most cost effective approach involves negotiating closing dates that align: you close on your Mountain's Edge sale in the morning and your new purchase in the afternoon, or within a few days. This requires coordination, flexibility, and sometimes rent back agreements where you stay briefly in your sold home while completing your purchase. An experienced agent managing both transactions can orchestrate this timing.
