First Steps to Selling Your Las Vegas Home

Preparing to sell your Las Vegas home starts three to six months before you want to close, giving you time to maximize your property's appeal and position it competitively in the market. These first steps set the foundation for a successful sale.

Get a Home Evaluation

Your first step is scheduling a home evaluation with a local real estate agent. During this one on one consultation, the agent tours your property and compares it to recently sold homes in your area. This Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) establishes your home's approximate market value and helps you understand what buyers in your neighborhood are paying. In early 2025, the median home price in Las Vegas reached approximately $455,000, though values vary significantly by location and condition.

Decide How You Want to Sell

Consider your options carefully. Working with a full service real estate agent provides professional marketing, negotiation expertise, and guidance through paperwork, but comes with commission costs averaging 5.61% in Nevada. Discount brokers offer similar services at reduced rates. For Sale By Owner (FSBO) saves commission but requires handling all aspects yourself. Cash home buyers offer speed and convenience but typically pay 70% of market value.

Address Repairs and Improvements

Before listing, walk through your home with fresh eyes. Focus on repairs that earn back the highest percentage of costs, like fresh paint, minor fixture updates, and landscaping improvements. In Las Vegas, prioritize HVAC maintenance given the desert climate and ensure pool equipment functions properly if applicable. Avoid expensive luxury remodels unless necessary, as you may not recoup those costs.

Prepare Your Home for Market

Declutter aggressively and depersonalize by removing family photos and personal items. Deep clean every surface, including windows, baseboards, and outdoor areas. Stage key rooms to help buyers envision themselves living there. With over 300 days of sunshine, Las Vegas buyers pay particular attention to outdoor spaces, so ensure patios and landscaping look inviting.

Gather Important Documents

Collect documents you will need during the selling process including your original purchase agreement, property survey, HOA documents and bylaws, records of major repairs or improvements, and utility bills. Having these ready prevents delays once you receive an offer.

Homes that are thoroughly prepared before listing attract more interest, receive better offers, and spend less time on market. Rushing to list before your home is ready often results in price reductions and extended selling timelines.
