Black Mountain in Henderson is one of the best neighborhoods in the Las Vegas valley for first time buyers who want safety, access to outdoor recreation, and home prices that remain within reach. The most accessible path into Black Mountain is through attached housing. Condos at communities like Bighorn at Black Mountain price around $300,000 and include clubhouse access with a $215 monthly HOA that covers exterior maintenance. For buyers with slightly more budget, Lennar's Highpointe collection offers brand new single family homes from $390,990 to $433,990 with 1,600 to 3,300 square feet. The overall Black Mountain median sits between $424,709 and $440,000. First time buyers using FHA or conventional loans with 3 to 5 percent down should target the condo and townhome segment or explore Lennar's entry level floor plans for the best fit. ## What First Time Buyer Programs Work in Henderson? Nevada Housing Division offers down payment assistance programs that can be paired with FHA, VA, or conventional financing. Henderson does not impose additional local restrictions on these programs, so Black Mountain properties generally qualify as long as they meet appraisal and loan limit requirements. Buyers should also investigate whether their employer offers housing assistance or whether they qualify for the Home Is Possible program, which provides up to 5 percent of the loan amount toward closing costs and down payment. ## What Should First Time Buyers Know About HOAs Here? HOA fees across Black Mountain range from $35 per month in parts of Black Mountain Ranch to $215 per month at Bighorn. First time buyers often overlook HOA dues when calculating monthly housing costs. A $215 HOA adds roughly $2,580 per year to your carrying costs, so factor that into your budget alongside your mortgage, taxes, and insurance. Review each community's CC&Rs carefully.
