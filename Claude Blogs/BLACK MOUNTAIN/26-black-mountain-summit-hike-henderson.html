The Black Mountain Summit hike in Henderson, NV is a 6.8 mile out and back trail that rewards hikers with panoramic views of the Las Vegas Strip, Red Rock Canyon, the Spring Mountains, and the Eldorado Range. The route follows BLM Trail 404 through volcanic terrain, and the final push to the summit involves class two rock scrambling over black lava cliffs. This is one of the most dramatic hikes in the Henderson area, and it starts just minutes from the Black Mountain neighborhood. ## How Hard Is the Black Mountain Summit Hike? This trail is rated strenuous. The first half follows a well defined desert path through the McCullough Range, gaining elevation steadily with exposed terrain and loose rock. The second half gets significantly more challenging, with steeper grades and uneven footing. The last half mile requires class two rock scrambling. Hikers need to use their hands to navigate large volcanic boulders near the summit. This section is not suitable for young children or inexperienced hikers. Sturdy hiking boots with good ankle support are essential, and trekking poles help on the descent. ## Where Is the Trailhead? The Black Mountain Trail trailhead is located at the intersection of Shadow Canyon Drive and Lewiston Place in Henderson. There is limited street parking near the trailhead, so arriving early is recommended, especially on weekends during peak season. No restrooms or water stations are available at the trailhead, so hikers should plan accordingly. ## When Is the Best Time to Hike Black Mountain? The ideal hiking season runs from September through May. Summer temperatures in Henderson regularly exceed 110 degrees, and the trail offers almost no shade. Even during the recommended months, hikers should start early in the morning to avoid midday heat. Carry at least two liters of water per person and wear sun protection. ## What Can You See from the Summit? The summit delivers a full 360 degree panorama. To the west, the Las Vegas Strip skyline rises from the valley floor. The Spring Mountains and Red Rock Canyon stretch along the horizon to the northwest. Looking south, hikers can see Jean Dry Lakes and the Eldorado Range. On clear days, visibility extends well beyond 50 miles.
