Black Mountain in Henderson, NV sits at the center of one of the best trail networks in the Las Vegas metro area. Residents have access to everything from strenuous volcanic summit hikes to smooth paved paths connecting Henderson to Lake Mead and Hoover Dam. Whether you prefer a quick morning walk or a full day backcountry adventure, the trails near Black Mountain cover every level of difficulty and distance. Here is a complete overview of the options. ## What Are the Best Hiking Trails Near Black Mountain? The Black Mountain Summit Trail (BLM 404) is the signature hike. This 6.8 mile out and back route climbs through volcanic terrain to a summit with 360 degree views of the Las Vegas Strip, Red Rock Canyon, and the McCullough Range. The final half mile requires class two rock scrambling, making it best suited for experienced hikers. The trailhead is at Shadow Canyon Drive and Lewiston Place. McCullough Hills Trail offers 16.1 miles of well graded, sandy terrain open to hikers, mountain bikers, and equestrians. The trailhead at 295 Mission Drive includes restrooms. Shorter loop options make this trail accessible for moderate fitness levels. The Amargosa Trail runs 4.6 miles out and back from Hidden Falls Park to the edge of Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area. This moderate route connects to ancient petroglyphs and offers a quick desert hiking experience close to home. ## Are There Paved Trails Near Black Mountain? Yes. The River Mountains Loop Trail is a 34 mile paved loop connecting Henderson, Boulder City, Lake Mead, and Hoover Dam. It is open to cyclists, walkers, runners, and leashed dogs. The smooth asphalt surface makes it accessible for strollers and wheelchairs as well. Multiple access points in Henderson put portions of this trail within easy reach of Black Mountain homes. The Pittman Wash Trail is another paved option that runs along Pittman Wash through Henderson. It provides a flat, easy route suitable for casual walks and family bike rides. ## Can You Hike Year Round Near Black Mountain? The best hiking season runs from September through May. Summer temperatures in Henderson regularly exceed 110 degrees, and most trails near Black Mountain offer minimal shade. During the cooler months, conditions are ideal for desert hiking with mild temperatures and clear skies. Even in peak season, starting before 8:00 a.m. is recommended to avoid midday heat. Always carry at least two liters of water per person on any unpaved trail. ## Trail Access That Adds Real Value to Black Mountain Ryan Rose consistently sees trail proximity listed among the top reasons buyers choose the Black Mountain area.
