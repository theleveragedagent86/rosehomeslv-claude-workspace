Ohjah Noodle House at 35 E Horizon Ridge Pkwy in Henderson, NV is a Japanese noodle restaurant specializing in authentic ramen, rice dishes, and Japanese appetizers. Located in Horizon Village Square just east of the Black Mountain neighborhood, the restaurant has earned a loyal following among Henderson residents for its rich broth, generous portions, and fast service. Most dishes arrive at the table in under five minutes. ## What Should You Order at Ohjah Noodle House? The tonkotsu ramen is the signature dish, built on a creamy pork bone broth that takes hours to prepare. The oxtail ramen is another standout, featuring tender braised oxtail pieces in a smoky broth with wood ear mushroom. Chicken katsu curry serves as the go to option for diners who want something beyond noodles, with a crispy breaded cutlet over rice with Japanese curry sauce. Appetizers include seaweed salad, shrimp skewers, and fried oysters. The menu also offers vegetarian ramen options for those who prefer a plant based meal. Portions are large enough that most diners leave satisfied without needing to order multiple dishes. ## What Are the Hours and Location? Ohjah Noodle House is open daily from 11am to 9pm Sunday through Thursday, with extended hours until 10pm on Fridays and Saturdays. The restaurant is located in Horizon Village Square at Suite 160, sharing the plaza with CVS and other neighborhood shops. Parking is available in the shared lot, and the location sits right along Horizon Ridge Parkway for easy access from any direction. ## Why Do Locals Keep Coming Back? Reviewers consistently highlight three things: speed, portion size, and value. The kitchen operates efficiently, and even during busy lunch hours, food comes out quickly. The quiet atmosphere makes Ohjah a comfortable spot for a solo lunch, a quick family dinner, or a casual meal with friends. Staff members are known for being friendly and attentive without hovering. ## Ramen Night, Five Minutes from Home Ryan Rose works with buyers who want a neighborhood where a great bowl of ramen is five minutes away rather than a 30 minute drive across the valley.
