Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area is a federally protected desert landscape located south of the Black Mountain neighborhood in Henderson, NV. The conservation area is known for its ancient petroglyphs, rugged desert terrain, and scenic canyon views. Residents of Black Mountain can access Sloan Canyon via the Amargosa Trail, making it one of the closest outdoor attractions to the neighborhood. ## What Are the Petroglyphs at Sloan Canyon? Sloan Canyon contains one of the most significant collections of Native American rock art in southern Nevada. The petroglyph site features hundreds of carved symbols and figures etched into the dark desert varnish on canyon walls. These carvings are estimated to be thousands of years old and represent the cultural heritage of indigenous peoples who inhabited the region long before modern development. The petroglyph area is accessible via a moderate desert hike. Visitors should bring plenty of water, wear sturdy footwear, and plan their trip during cooler morning hours, especially in summer months when temperatures regularly exceed 100 degrees. ## How Do You Get to Sloan Canyon from Black Mountain? The most direct access point is the Amargosa Trail, which connects to the conservation area from the southern edge of the Black Mountain neighborhood. This trail system allows residents to hike directly from the community into the protected desert landscape without needing to drive to a remote trailhead. The Amargosa Trail itself is a popular route for walking, running, and mountain biking before it transitions into the more rugged terrain of the conservation area. ## What Should You Know Before Visiting? Sloan Canyon is managed by the Bureau of Land Management and has specific rules to protect the petroglyph sites. Visitors should stay on marked trails, avoid touching the rock carvings, and pack out all trash. There are no restrooms or water sources within the conservation area. Cell service can be limited in the deeper canyon sections, so letting someone know your plans before heading out is a smart precaution. ## Desert Adventure Steps from Home Ryan Rose considers Sloan Canyon access one of the most unique features of living in Black Mountain.
