Black Mountain and Seven Hills are two of Henderson's most popular residential neighborhoods, and buyers frequently compare them before making a decision. Both offer proximity to outdoor recreation, strong schools, and easy access to the Las Vegas Strip. The differences come down to price, community feel, lot sizes, and HOA structure. Here is what you need to know to choose the right fit. ## How Do Home Prices Compare Between Black Mountain and Seven Hills? Black Mountain homes typically range from $300K to just over $1M, with a median price around $424,000 to $440,000. Seven Hills skews higher, with prices ranging from roughly $400K to $2M and beyond. Seven Hills features more luxury custom homes in its upper villages, which pulls the median up significantly. Buyers looking for a more affordable entry point into Henderson often find better value in Black Mountain. Both neighborhoods include a mix of single family homes, but Black Mountain's inventory tends to feature larger lot sizes, often one third of an acre or more. Seven Hills lots can be smaller in its townhome and attached home sections, though its estate lots in the guard gated areas rival anything in the valley. ## Is Seven Hills or Black Mountain More Gated? Seven Hills is a guard gated master planned community with controlled entry points and a more structured HOA framework. HOA fees vary by village, typically ranging from $50 to $300 per month. Black Mountain is only partially gated. Select sub communities like Heritage 55+, Juniper Trails, and the Golf and Country Club have gated access, but much of the neighborhood is open. This distinction matters for buyers who want a fully secured community versus those who prefer fewer restrictions and more independence. Black Mountain's partial gating means lower average HOA costs while still offering gated options for those who want them. ## Which Neighborhood Has Better Schools? Both neighborhoods are served by strong Clark County schools. Seven Hills feeds into Vanderburg Elementary, Del E Webb Middle, and Coronado High School. Black Mountain students attend Ulis Newton Elementary, Burkholder Middle School, and Foothill High School. All of these schools receive solid ratings from parents and community review sites. The school zones overlap in some areas, so specific address verification is always recommended before purchasing. ## What About Amenities and Recreation? Seven Hills offers seven community parks, pool complexes, and a trail network within its master plan. Black Mountain counters with direct access to the McCullough Range trailheads, the River Mountains Loop Trail, Hidden Falls Park, and the Black Mountain Recreation Center. Seven Hills leans more toward structured community amenities, while Black Mountain emphasizes open desert access and outdoor adventure. ## Ryan Rose on Choosing Between Black Mountain and Seven Hills Ryan Rose helps Henderson buyers navigate this comparison regularly. He notes that Black Mountain attracts buyers who want larger lots, a less HOA driven environment, and direct trail access at a more approachable price point. Seven Hills appeals to buyers who prioritize a fully gated community with resort style amenities and a more polished master plan feel. Ready to tour both neighborhoods and compare in person?
