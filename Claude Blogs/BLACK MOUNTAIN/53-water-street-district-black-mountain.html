Water Street is Henderson's original downtown corridor. It dates back to the city's founding and has served as the civic and commercial center for decades. In the last several years, the city invested heavily in revitalizing the area with new streetscaping, mixed use development, and incentives for restaurants and entertainment venues. The result is a district that blends Henderson's small town roots with a modern dining and nightlife scene. The district stretches along Water Street and the surrounding blocks, anchored by Henderson City Hall, the Henderson Convention Center, and a growing roster of locally owned businesses. ## What Restaurants and Bars Are on Water Street? Water Street now features a diverse mix of dining options. You will find craft breweries, taco shops, Italian restaurants, sushi spots, and upscale dining all within walking distance of each other. The nightlife scene includes cocktail bars and live music venues that draw crowds from across the valley, not just Henderson residents. Weekend mornings bring a different energy, with brunch spots and coffee shops filling up as families and couples stroll the district. The district hosts seasonal events including outdoor movie nights, holiday celebrations, farmers markets, and live music festivals. The Henderson Convention Center also brings events, conferences, and expos to the district throughout the year, adding foot traffic and energy to the surrounding businesses. ## How Far Is Water Street from Black Mountain?
