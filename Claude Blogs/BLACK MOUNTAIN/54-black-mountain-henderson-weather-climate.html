Summer highs in the Henderson area reach an average of 106 degrees Fahrenheit in July, with lows around 79 degrees. Winters are mild by national standards, with January highs averaging 67 degrees and lows dropping to about 37 degrees. Spring and fall offer the most comfortable conditions, with temperatures ranging from the mid 60s to low 90s depending on the month. The key takeaway for buyers is that Black Mountain's elevation, sitting at the base of the McCullough Range, occasionally delivers temperatures a few degrees cooler than the Las Vegas Strip. The difference is modest, but residents notice it during peak summer months. ## How Much Rain Does Black Mountain Henderson Get? Very little. Annual rainfall in the Henderson area averages roughly 4.2 inches, spread across occasional storms mostly concentrated in the late summer monsoon season and winter months. Snow is extremely rare at this elevation, though the surrounding mountains may see dustings a few times per year. The low precipitation means landscaping choices matter. Most Black Mountain homes feature desert landscaping with rock, native plants, and drip irrigation systems that keep water bills manageable. The dry climate also means less home maintenance related to moisture, mold, or water damage compared to wetter regions. ## When Is the Best Time to Be Outdoors in Black Mountain? October through April is the prime outdoor season. During these months, morning and evening temperatures are ideal for hiking the McCullough Range trails, walking the River Mountains Loop, or spending time at Hidden Falls Park. Many Black Mountain residents shift their outdoor routines seasonally, exercising early in the morning or after sunset during summer and enjoying midday activities during the cooler months. The 294 sunny days per year mean overcast days are uncommon. Pool season typically runs from late April through October, and many homes in Black Mountain include private pools that see heavy use during the long warm season. ## How Does the Climate Affect Energy Costs?
