Canyons Summerlin Home Prices 2026

Home prices in The Canyons village of Summerlin range from approximately $600,000 for condos to over $5,000,000 for custom estates, with most single family homes listing above the $1,000,000 mark in 2026. This established community remains one of the most desirable addresses in the Las Vegas valley.

What Do Condos and Townhomes Cost in The Canyons?

Condo and townhome buyers can expect entry points near $600,000 in subdivisions like Mira Villa and Dakota. These properties offer lock and leave convenience with resort style amenities, making them popular among retirees and professionals who travel frequently. Guard gated access in select condo communities adds a layer of exclusivity that justifies the premium over comparable Summerlin options.

What Is the Price Range for Single Family Homes?

Single family detached homes in The Canyons generally start above $1,000,000 and climb depending on lot size, subdivision, and custom upgrades. Communities like Canyon Crest and Palisades offer semi custom homes in the $1,000,000 to $2,000,000 range. For buyers seeking larger lots and fully custom builds, Bellacere and Eagle Rock feature estates priced from $2,500,000 to well over $5,000,000.

Why Are Prices Strong in The Canyons?

The Canyons is largely built out, meaning no new construction competes with existing inventory. Builders like Christopher Homes, Warmington Homes, and Beazer Homes developed these properties between 1995 and the early 2000s. The combination of mature landscaping, guard gated security, top rated schools, and proximity to TPC Las Vegas keeps demand consistently high and inventory tight.

Ryan Rose works with buyers and sellers throughout The Canyons and notes that homes in the guard gated subdivisions tend to hold their value exceptionally well. Limited turnover in communities like Aventura and Eagle Rock means that when a listing does appear, it moves quickly. Understanding the micro markets within The Canyons is essential for anyone looking to make a smart investment here.
