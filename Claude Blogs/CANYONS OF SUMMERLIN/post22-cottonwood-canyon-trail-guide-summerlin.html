Cottonwood Canyon Park Trail Guide: Routes, Distance, and What to Expect in Summerlin

The Cottonwood Canyon Park Trail in Summerlin covers 3.5 miles out and back with an easy 1.25 mile loop option, making it one of the most accessible and popular trails near The Canyons neighborhood. Here is everything you need to know before you lace up.

How Long Is the Cottonwood Canyon Park Trail?

The full Cottonwood Canyon Park Trail runs 3.5 miles as an out and back route, stretching from behind Costco west of Pavilion Center Drive all the way to Hualapai Way. The average completion time is about 1 hour and 13 minutes at a walking pace. For a quicker outing, the 1.25 mile loop option keeps you within the heart of the park and takes roughly 25 minutes to finish.

The trail surface is concrete throughout, making it suitable for road biking, running, walking, and strollers. Two overpass tunnels at Town Center and Pavilion allow you to cross major roads without stopping for traffic, keeping the experience uninterrupted and safe for families with children.

What Is the Difficulty Level?

The trail is rated easy with minimal elevation change across its entire length. Flat concrete pathways make it accessible for all fitness levels, from casual walkers to serious runners logging daily miles. The route passes through alternating sections of grassy open space and desert landscaping, offering visual variety without demanding terrain. Ryan Rose frequently recommends this trail to clients relocating to The Canyons who want an active outdoor lifestyle without driving to trailheads outside the community.

When Is the Best Time to Walk the Trail?

Early mornings and evenings are the most popular times, especially during summer months when Las Vegas temperatures climb. Well lit paths with benches spaced along the route make evening walks comfortable and safe. Spring and fall offer ideal conditions for midday outings. The trail connects to Summerlin's broader 200 plus mile trail network, so experienced hikers and bikers can extend their route well beyond the park boundaries.
