Hualapai Canyon Trail: Summerlin's Hidden Gem Near The Canyons

Hualapai Canyon Trail is a short, scenic walking path tucked into the Summerlin community near The Canyons, stretching less than one mile and best enjoyed during evening strolls. If you are looking for a quiet escape without leaving the neighborhood, this trail delivers.

Where Is the Hualapai Canyon Trail?

The Hualapai Canyon Trail sits within the Summerlin trail network near Hualapai Way, just minutes from The Canyons neighborhood. Often described as a hidden gem, the trailhead is not as prominently marked as larger Summerlin trails, which keeps foot traffic low and the experience peaceful. Residents of The Canyons can access it on foot through connecting pathways that run along the community's arroyo corridors.

How Long Is the Hualapai Canyon Trail?

At less than one mile in length, Hualapai Canyon Trail is designed for short outings rather than distance training. The trail accommodates walking, jogging, and light biking on a maintained surface. It takes most visitors about 15 to 20 minutes to complete at a relaxed pace. The compact distance makes it especially popular with families who want a quick outdoor break after dinner or on weekend mornings.

What Makes This Trail Different from Other Summerlin Trails?

While Cottonwood Canyon Park Trail draws crowds with its 3.5 mile route, Hualapai Canyon Trail offers solitude. The lower foot traffic and intimate desert setting create a noticeably calmer atmosphere. Evening light in this section of Summerlin casts long shadows across the natural landscape, making it a favorite for photographers and residents who prefer a slower pace.
