Bonner Elementary School Near The Canyons Summerlin

John W. Bonner Elementary is a public school serving grades K through 5 that draws students from portions of The Canyons village in Summerlin. Families on the eastern edge of the community are zoned for Bonner, giving them access to a solid neighborhood school within the Clark County School District.

What Grades and Programs Does Bonner Elementary Offer?

Bonner Elementary serves kindergarten through fifth grade and operates as a traditional public elementary within CCSD. The school provides core academics along with enrichment programs common to Summerlin area schools. Its location near The Canyons makes it a convenient option for families who live in the subcommunities along the eastern boundary of the village.

The campus serves a mix of students from surrounding Summerlin neighborhoods, which contributes to a diverse school community. Bonner follows the same CCSD academic calendar and curriculum standards as other district elementary schools in the area.

How Does Bonner Compare to Vassiliadis Elementary?

Vassiliadis Elementary, located within The Canyons, ranks as the number one elementary in Nevada with an A minus from Niche. Bonner serves as the alternate feeder school for parts of the village and provides a reliable public school option for families who fall outside the Vassiliadis attendance zone. Both schools feed into Sig Rogich Middle School, so students from either campus converge in sixth grade.

Families considering homes in The Canyons should verify zoning for their specific address, since attendance boundaries determine whether children attend Vassiliadis or Bonner. Zoning maps are available through the Clark County School District website and can shift during redistricting cycles.
