Canyons Summerlin HOA Fees

HOA fees in The Canyons village of Summerlin range from approximately $30 per month to over $1,000 per month, depending on the subdivision. Most residents also pay an additional yearly Special Improvement District (SID) fee. Understanding the fee structure before you buy is essential, because the variation across The Canyons' 14 plus subdivisions is significant.

Why Do HOA Fees Vary So Much in The Canyons?

The Canyons operates under a tiered HOA system. The master HOA, managed through Summerlin North, covers shared infrastructure like common area landscaping, trails, and community maintenance. Most subdivisions then have their own sub HOA that covers neighborhood specific amenities. Guard gated communities like Aventura, Bellacere, Eagle Rock, Mira Villa, and Palisades typically carry higher monthly fees because they fund 24 hour gate attendants, private landscaping, and enhanced security.

What Do HOA Fees Cover in The Canyons?

Master HOA dues generally cover access to Summerlin's trail system, common area upkeep, and community governance. Sub HOA fees in gated subdivisions typically cover guard gate staffing, private street maintenance, community pools, and exterior building maintenance for condo and townhome communities. Condo communities like Mira Villa and Dakota tend to have the highest monthly dues because their HOAs also cover building insurance, roofing, and exterior repairs.

What Is the SID Fee?

In addition to monthly HOA fees, most Canyons homeowners pay a yearly Special Improvement District assessment. This fee was established when Summerlin's infrastructure was originally built and funds bond repayment for roads, utilities, and public improvements. The SID fee appears on your annual property tax bill and varies by parcel. Buyers should request a SID payoff estimate during escrow to understand the full cost of ownership.

Ryan Rose advises every Canyons buyer to request a full HOA resale package before closing, which details current dues, reserve fund health, and any upcoming special assessments. Some subdivisions have very healthy reserves while others may face increases. Knowing the difference can save you thousands over the life of your ownership.
