Red Rock Canyon Near The Canyons Summerlin

Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is a 195,819 acre outdoor destination located just 10 to 15 minutes from The Canyons in Summerlin. Residents of this neighborhood have faster access to world class hiking, rock climbing, and scenic drives than nearly any other community in Las Vegas.

What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon?

Red Rock Canyon offers a 13 mile scenic drive that loops through towering sandstone formations, with pulloffs for photography and short trail access along the way. Beyond the drive, the conservation area features hiking routes ranging from quick 20 minute walks to full day treks into the backcountry. Rock climbing draws athletes from around the world, and mountain biking, road biking, and horseback riding are all available within the park boundaries.

The Visitor Center is open daily from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM and includes exhibits on the geology, wildlife, and history of the Mojave Desert. Timed entry reservations are required for the scenic drive between October 1 and May 31, and can be booked through Recreation.gov. More than three million visitors explore Red Rock Canyon each year, making it one of the most popular outdoor destinations in the Southwest.

How Close Is Red Rock Canyon to The Canyons?

The Canyons sits on the western edge of Summerlin, which places the entrance to Red Rock Canyon roughly 10 to 15 minutes away by car. That proximity is a defining feature of the neighborhood. While most Las Vegas residents need 30 minutes or more to reach the conservation area, Canyons homeowners can fit a morning hike into a normal weekday and still be home before lunch. The 215 Beltway and Charleston Boulevard provide direct routes from the neighborhood to the park entrance.
