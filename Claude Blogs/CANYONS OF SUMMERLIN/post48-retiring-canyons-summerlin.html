Retiring in The Canyons Summerlin: Why Retirees Love This Neighborhood

The Canyons in Summerlin offers retirees a rare combination of quiet guard gated living, walkable outdoor recreation, top tier healthcare access, and resort style amenities, all without Nevada state income tax.

What Makes The Canyons Ideal for Retirement?

The neighborhood's flat terrain and connected trail system make daily walks safe and enjoyable for active adults. Over 200 miles of Summerlin trails wind through the community, and Cottonwood Canyon Park provides 69 acres of open space just steps from many Canyons homes. TPC Las Vegas, the championship golf course at the center of the community, gives golf enthusiasts a world class course in their own backyard.

Guard gated subdivisions offer the peace of mind that retirees prioritize. Six of the community's subdivisions feature staffed gates with 24 hour access control, creating a secure and quiet environment. The low traffic, residential only streets are easy to navigate and feel distinctly removed from the busier parts of the Las Vegas Valley.

How Close Is Healthcare to The Canyons?

Summerlin Hospital Medical Center sits just one to three minutes from The Canyons at 657 N Town Center Drive. This 454 bed acute care facility on a 40 acre campus provides emergency services, surgical care, and specialty medicine without requiring a long drive. For retirees who need proximity to quality healthcare, this is one of the strongest advantages The Canyons offers over competing Las Vegas neighborhoods.

MorningStar Senior Living at The Canyons, located at 490 S Hualapai Way, provides 95 independent living suites, 49 assisted living suites, and 24 memory care suites for residents who want a full service senior living option within the community. Amenities include a pool, hot tub, outdoor bar, and club room.
