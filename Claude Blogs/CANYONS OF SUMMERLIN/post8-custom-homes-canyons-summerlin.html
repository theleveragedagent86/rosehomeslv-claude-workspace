Custom Homes in The Canyons Summerlin

The Canyons in Summerlin is one of the few Las Vegas communities where true custom and semi custom estates line a championship golf course, with homes in Bellacere and Canyon Fairways reaching well over $5 million. These residences were designed and built to the highest standards, reflecting the unique vision of each homeowner.

Where Are the Custom Home Communities in The Canyons?

Two guard gated subdivisions anchor the custom home market in The Canyons: Bellacere and Canyon Fairways. Bellacere contains approximately 57 custom and semi custom estates ranging from 3,000 to over 16,000 square feet, built primarily by Christopher Homes between 2000 and 2008. Canyon Fairways is a larger community of roughly 181 custom built residences, with homes spanning 4,000 to over 10,000 square feet. Both communities sit directly along the TPC Las Vegas fairways and offer sweeping views of the surrounding mountains.

What Architectural Styles Are Found in These Custom Homes?

The custom estates in The Canyons showcase a range of architectural influences. Mediterranean inspired designs with stucco exteriors, clay tile roofs, and arched entryways are among the most common. Contemporary residences with clean lines, floor to ceiling glass, and modern finishes are also well represented. Canyon Fairways includes Santa Fe style homes alongside traditional and transitional designs. Architectural guidelines in both communities ensure that each home complements the natural desert landscape.

What Features Define Custom Living in The Canyons?

These homes typically include resort style pools, outdoor kitchens, multi car garages, private courtyards, and expansive entertaining spaces. Many feature golf course frontage with unobstructed views of TPC Las Vegas. Interior finishes often include imported stone, custom millwork, chef grade kitchens, and smart home technology. Because The Canyons is largely built out with no new construction available, the existing custom inventory is finite and highly sought after.
