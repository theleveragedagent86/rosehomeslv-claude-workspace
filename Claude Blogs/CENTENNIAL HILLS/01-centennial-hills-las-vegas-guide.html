Centennial Hills is a master planned community in northwest Las Vegas known for spacious suburban living, top rated schools, and easy freeway access via the US 95 and CC 215 interchange. With over 100,000 residents and homes built primarily between 2000 and 2020, this neighborhood has become one of the most sought after areas in the valley. ## Where Is Centennial Hills and What Makes It Special? Centennial Hills sits in the northwest corner of Las Vegas, spanning zip codes 89130, 89131, and 89149. The community was officially named in 2001 through a public contest and borders the Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument. Residents enjoy mountain views, desert landscapes, and a location that feels removed from the hustle of the Strip while remaining just 20 to 30 minutes away. The neighborhood centers around Centennial Hills Park, a 120 acre recreational hub that serves as the community's gathering place. Centennial Center, a 900,000 square foot shopping plaza, provides convenient retail, dining, and services without leaving the area. The Centennial Hills Hospital Medical Center, a 336 bed facility, ensures quality healthcare is minutes from home. ## What Are Home Prices Like in Centennial Hills? Home prices in Centennial Hills range from $375,000 to over $800,000, with a median around $475,000 to $550,000 as of early 2026. Entry level homes typically offer 1,500 to 2,200 square feet with three to four bedrooms, while premium properties exceed 4,000 square feet. Ryan Rose often points out that comparable homes here run 10 to 20 percent less than similar properties in Summerlin, making it an excellent value for families and investors alike. Builders like Lennar, KB Home, D.R. Horton, Toll Brothers, and Pulte Homes have developed subdivisions throughout the area. New construction remains active, with over 120 new homes available at a median listing price of $520,000. Home styles include single story and two story single family homes, townhomes, condos, and even horse properties on larger lots. ## What Schools and Amenities Does Centennial Hills Offer? The community is served by the Clark County School District, the fifth largest in the country, with 13 public schools enrolling over 13,000 students. Standout options include Coral Academy of Science (rated 10/10), Somerset Academy Sky Pointe (rated 10/10), and Northwest Career and Technical Academy (rated 9/10). Math and reading proficiency scores here exceed Nevada state averages. Safety is another strong point, with Centennial Hills earning a B+ crime grade and ranking safer than 74 percent of U.S. neighborhoods. The community is pet friendly with multiple dog parks, and the Centennial Hills YMCA Community Center provides fitness and family programming year round. ## Ryan Rose's Take on Centennial Hills Centennial Hills consistently ranks among the best neighborhoods in Las Vegas for families, professionals, and retirees. The combination of newer construction, strong schools, and accessible pricing creates a rare balance that is hard to find elsewhere in the valley.
