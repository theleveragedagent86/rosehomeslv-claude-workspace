Centennial Hills is not a single neighborhood but a collection of distinct subdivisions, each with its own character, price range, and lifestyle appeal. From gated luxury enclaves to family friendly starter home communities, the best neighborhood for you depends on your budget, commute needs, and daily priorities. ## What Are the Best Neighborhoods in Centennial Hills? The top neighborhoods in Centennial Hills span all three of the community's zip codes: 89130, 89131, and 89149. Gated subdivisions built by Toll Brothers and Richmond American rank among the most desirable, offering premium homes priced from $600,000 to over $800,000 with upgraded finishes, private parks, and controlled access. These communities appeal to buyers who prioritize security, exclusivity, and resale value. For mid range buyers in the $475,000 to $600,000 bracket, neighborhoods developed by Lennar, KB Home, and D.R. Horton provide solid value with homes ranging from 2,000 to 2,800 square feet. Many of these subdivisions include community pools, walking paths, and easy access to top rated Centennial Hills schools, making them a strong fit for growing families. ## Which Centennial Hills Neighborhoods Are Best for Families? Family focused buyers tend to gravitate toward the subdivisions in the 89149 zip code, where newer construction, proximity to parks, and access to well reviewed schools create a strong combination. Homes in this area were largely built between 2005 and 2020, so the housing stock is modern and energy efficient. Builders like Woodside Homes, Pardee, and Harmony Homes have delivered much of the inventory here. Neighborhoods closer to Centennial Hills Park and the recreational facilities along Buffalo Drive also draw families who want outdoor space without driving across town. Ryan Rose notes that these areas tend to hold their value well because the combination of parks, schools, and newer construction creates consistent buyer demand. ## Where Should First Time Buyers Look in Centennial Hills? Entry level neighborhoods in the 89130 zip code offer some of the most accessible pricing in Centennial Hills, with homes starting around $375,000 for properties between 1,500 and 2,200 square feet. These subdivisions are slightly older but benefit from established landscaping, lower HOA fees in many cases, and direct freeway access via US 95. Compared to Summerlin, buyers here can expect prices that are 10 to 20 percent lower for comparable square footage and builder quality.
