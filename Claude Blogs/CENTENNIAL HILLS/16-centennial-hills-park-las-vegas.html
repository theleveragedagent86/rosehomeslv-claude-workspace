Centennial Hills Park is a 120 acre regional park located at 7101 N. Buffalo Drive in the heart of Centennial Hills, Las Vegas. With playgrounds, sports fields, a splash pad, dog park, and a 3,000 seat amphitheater, it is one of the most complete community parks in all of northwest Las Vegas. ## What Are the Main Features of Centennial Hills Park? Centennial Hills Park delivers something for every age group across its expansive 120 acres. Two age specific playgrounds serve toddlers (ages 2 to 5) and older children (ages 5 to 12), with accessibility ramps and climbing structures designed for all abilities. The splash pad offers a refreshing way to cool off during the warmer months, featuring geysers and water play elements that kids love. For sports enthusiasts, the park includes lighted baseball fields, soccer fields, basketball courts, tennis courts, and sand volleyball courts. A jogging and walking trail loops through the property with exercise stations spaced along the route. Picnic areas equipped with BBQ grills and reservable shelters make it easy to plan a family gathering or weekend cookout. ## Does Centennial Hills Park Have a Dog Park and Amphitheater? The dedicated dog park at Centennial Hills Park features separate sections for small and large breeds, along with agility equipment that gives your pup a real workout. Water stations throughout the dog area help keep pets hydrated, especially during Las Vegas summers. The space is fully fenced with grass covering, benches, and waste stations for convenience. The amphitheater is one of the park's standout features, with grass seating that accommodates more than 3,000 guests. It hosts free concerts, outdoor movie nights, and community festivals throughout the year, including the popular Las Vegas Bluegrass Festival each spring. Interpretive signage along the walking paths also highlights the geological and historical significance of the area, adding an educational layer to every visit. ## A Local Perspective on Centennial Hills Park Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas Realtor specializing in Centennial Hills, sees this park as one of the top lifestyle perks for residents of the neighborhood.
