Huckleberry Park is a 16 acre community park located at 10325 Farm Road in the Providence master planned community within Centennial Hills, Las Vegas. It offers playgrounds, a splash pad, sports courts, a dog park, and one of the only three hole putting greens you will find at a public park in the valley. ## What Amenities Does Huckleberry Park Offer? Huckleberry Park packs a surprising number of amenities into its 16 acres. Two age specific playgrounds keep younger children (ages 2 to 5) and older kids (ages 5 to 12) entertained with towering play structures, climbing obstacles, and slides. Right next to the playground, a splash pad features geysers, water tunnels, and dumping buckets, with shaded seating nearby so parents can stay cool while keeping an eye on the action. Sports courts include full court basketball, tennis, and pickleball, making it a go to destination for active residents. The park also features bocce courts, horseshoe pits, and a three hole putting green, which adds a unique recreational option that most Las Vegas parks do not have. Expansive grassy fields provide open space for everything from pickup soccer games to weekend picnics. ## Is Huckleberry Park Good for Dogs and Families? Absolutely. Huckleberry Park includes separate large and small dog areas where pets can run off leash in a safe, fenced environment. Dog owners appreciate the designated spaces that allow puppies and smaller breeds to play without being overwhelmed by larger dogs. The park is also home to a multi use events field and an outdoor stage area that hosts community gatherings. For families, the reservable picnic shelters with BBQ grills make party planning simple. Restrooms are on site, and the jogging and walking paths with fitness course stations offer a solid workout option for parents while kids play nearby. The park is open daily from 8 AM to 10 PM, giving plenty of time for morning walks and evening recreation. ## Why Residents Love Huckleberry Park Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas Realtor who specializes in Centennial Hills real estate, points to Huckleberry Park as a hidden gem within the Providence community.
