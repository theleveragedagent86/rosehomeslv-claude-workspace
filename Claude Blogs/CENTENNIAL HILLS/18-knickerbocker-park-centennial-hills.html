Knickerbocker Park is a 15 acre community park located at 10695 Dorrell Lane in the Providence neighborhood of Centennial Hills, Las Vegas. Known for its elevated location and stunning Las Vegas skyline views, this park features playgrounds, a splash pad, dog parks, and walking trails with exercise stations. ## What Makes Knickerbocker Park Special in Centennial Hills? Knickerbocker Park sits at one of the highest elevations in Providence, which gives it one of the best vantage points for watching the Las Vegas Strip light up at sunset. The $2.2 million park was designed as a multigenerational space, meaning it serves toddlers, school age kids, active adults, and seniors alike. Two age specific playgrounds cater to children ages 2 to 5 and 5 to 12, with a swing set and climbing features that keep families coming back. The splash pad is a major draw during the warmer months, featuring fountains and water tunnels that provide relief from the desert heat. A youth baseball field and multi use events lawn round out the athletic offerings, while the jogging path with exercise stations wraps through the southern portion of the park and offers multiple fitness stops along the way. ## Does Knickerbocker Park Have a Dog Park? Yes, Knickerbocker Park includes separate dog park areas for small and large breeds. Both sections are fully fenced, giving pet owners peace of mind while their dogs run and socialize off leash. The divided setup ensures that smaller dogs have their own safe space to play without being overwhelmed. Water access and waste stations are available throughout the dog park area for convenience. The dog parks at Knickerbocker are a popular morning and evening gathering spot for pet owners in the Providence and broader Centennial Hills community.
