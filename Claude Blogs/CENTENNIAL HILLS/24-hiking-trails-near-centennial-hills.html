Centennial Hills in northwest Las Vegas is surrounded by some of the best hiking trails in Southern Nevada, from gentle park loops and fossil bed paths to challenging desert canyon routes at Red Rock Canyon, all within a short drive of home. ## What Are the Best Hiking Trails Near Centennial Hills? Residents of Centennial Hills have access to a remarkable variety of trails without traveling far from the neighborhood. The Centennial Hills Park Walking and Jogging Trail offers a paved loop around the 120 acre park, complete with exercise stations and historical signage that makes it ideal for casual walkers and morning joggers. For something more adventurous, Floyd Lamb Park at Tule Springs provides multiple trails across more than 2,000 acres of scenic terrain, with routes winding around ponds and through a historic ranch setting that welcomes hikers, bikers, and equestrians. Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument, located directly north of the neighborhood, adds even more options with trails that cross 22,650 acres of protected desert. These paths pass through fossil bearing formations where Ice Age mammoths and ancient camels once roamed. Access is easy from Moccasin Road and Durango Drive at the northern edge of Centennial Hills. ## How Close Is Red Rock Canyon for Hiking from Centennial Hills? Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area borders Centennial Hills to the west and offers dozens of trailheads along its 13 mile scenic drive. Trails range from beginner friendly walks like the Moenkopi Loop to strenuous scrambles that reward hikers with sweeping valley views. Most Centennial Hills residents can reach the first trailheads in under 20 minutes, making it practical to squeeze in a hike before lunch on a weekend morning. The ability to hike world class trails before breakfast and still make it to a neighborhood coffee shop by mid morning is a lifestyle perk that few Las Vegas communities can match. ## Trail Tips for Centennial Hills Residents The best months for hiking in the Las Vegas area are October through April, when temperatures are comfortable and the desert landscape is at its most inviting. Summer hikers should start before dawn and carry extra water, especially on exposed trails at Red Rock Canyon and Tule Springs. Centennial Hills Park trails are a solid year round option thanks to their paved surfaces and proximity to parking and shade structures.
