The Centennial Hills Park Amphitheater is a popular outdoor entertainment venue located within the 120 acre Centennial Hills Park at 7101 N. Buffalo Drive, hosting free concerts, outdoor movie screenings, and community events throughout the year. ## What Events Are Held at the Centennial Hills Park Amphitheater? The amphitheater at Centennial Hills Park serves as the neighborhood's primary outdoor entertainment venue, offering a regular lineup of free concerts, movie nights under the stars, and seasonal community gatherings. The venue features a mix of fixed seating and open grass areas, giving attendees the choice of grabbing a seat up front or spreading out a blanket on the lawn for a more relaxed experience. Events typically run from spring through fall, taking advantage of the mild desert evenings. Free concert series are among the most popular offerings, drawing families and neighbors together for live music in a casual, family friendly setting. Outdoor movie screenings are another highlight, with residents bringing lawn chairs and picnic baskets to enjoy films projected on a large screen as the sun sets over the park. The amphitheater also hosts private events and community celebrations, making it a versatile gathering space for the neighborhood. ## Is the Centennial Hills Amphitheater Free to Attend? Most public events held at the amphitheater are free and open to all Las Vegas residents, making it an accessible source of entertainment for families on any budget. The City of Las Vegas Parks and Recreation department coordinates the public event schedule, and information on upcoming shows is typically posted on the city's website and local community boards. Parking is available within Centennial Hills Park, and the amphitheater's central location within the park means attendees can combine an event with a walk along the jogging trails or a visit to the playground.
