Northwest Career and Technical Academy is a public magnet high school located at 8200 West Tropical Parkway in Las Vegas, serving approximately 1,883 students in grades 9 through 12 within the Clark County School District. With a 9 out of 10 rating from Public School Review and a five star district rating, NWCTA offers Centennial Hills families a unique high school experience built around hands on career and technical education pathways. ## Career and Technical Programs What sets Northwest Career and Technical Academy apart from traditional high schools is its focus on specialized career pathways. Students choose a program track and spend their high school years developing real world skills alongside standard academic coursework. Current program areas include Biomedical Science, Engineering and Design, Culinary Arts, Hospitality and Marketing, Media Communications, Construction Management, Mechanical Technology, Early Childhood and Teacher Education, and Veterinary Sciences. Each program area occupies a dedicated section of the campus, complete with glass walled project rooms designed for collaborative, hands on learning. Students gain access to industry certifications, internship opportunities, and professional mentorship that prepare them for both the workforce and higher education. The school's mission centers on developing advanced skills through unique hands on experiences in professional settings, utilizing community partnerships, innovative ideas, and contemporary technologies. ## Academics and Test Scores Beyond career and technical education, NWCTA maintains a rigorous academic environment with Advanced Placement courses and Project Lead the Way curriculum. Reading proficiency reaches 84%, which is exceptionally strong and well above both district and state averages. Math proficiency stands at 39%, reflecting a student body that skews toward applied and technical learning while still maintaining solid fundamentals. The student to teacher ratio of 23 to 1 allows for a manageable learning environment, and the magnet school structure means students have actively chosen to be there, which tends to create a motivated campus culture. The school holds a five star rating within CCSD, placing it among the highest performing schools in the district. ## Admissions and How It Works As a magnet school, NWCTA accepts students from across the Clark County School District through an application process rather than neighborhood zoning. Prospective students attend campus tours, typically held between November and February, where they can explore the CTE programs, meet teachers, and learn about the application requirements. Admission is competitive, and families interested in securing a spot should plan to attend an information session early in the cycle. NWCTA operates on a nine month schedule, which differs from the traditional CCSD calendar. This is worth noting for families who need to coordinate schedules across multiple children attending different schools. ## Why It Matters for Centennial Hills Homebuyers Having a top rated magnet school within the Centennial Hills area gives families an additional educational option that goes beyond the traditional high school model.
