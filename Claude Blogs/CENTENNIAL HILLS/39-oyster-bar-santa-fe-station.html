The Oyster Bar at Santa Fe Station brings Cajun and Creole inspired seafood to northwest Las Vegas, offering Centennial Hills residents a taste of Louisiana without leaving the neighborhood. Located inside Santa Fe Station Casino at 4949 North Rancho Drive, the restaurant has built a strong local following for its fresh oysters, hearty pan roasts, and flavorful gumbo. ## What Is on the Menu at The Oyster Bar? The Oyster Bar specializes in seafood prepared with a Southern Louisiana influence, and the menu reads like a tour of New Orleans classics adapted for the desert. Fresh oysters headline the offerings, available chilled or prepared in a variety of styles. The pan roast is a signature dish, a rich and hearty preparation that keeps regulars coming back season after season. Gumbo and cioppino are both popular choices for guests who want something warm and satisfying. The cioppino features a mix of fresh fish and shellfish in a rich tomato broth, while the seafood jambalaya delivers a vibrant rice dish cooked with a medley of seafood and bold Cajun spices. For those looking for larger entrees, the menu includes Mediterranean bass, Alaskan king crab legs, lobster tails, jumbo battered shrimp, and a fisherman's stew loaded with shrimp, crab, cod, mussels, and clams. The beverage program matches the food's Louisiana roots, with classic New Orleans cocktails sitting alongside an extensive beer selection that includes Louisiana, Belgian, and California brews. Vintage wines and craft beers round out a drinks list that pairs naturally with the seafood menu. ## Hours and Location The Oyster Bar is open Monday through Thursday and Sunday from 11:00 AM to 11:00 PM, with extended hours on Friday and Saturday until 1:00 AM. The midday opening makes it a viable option for lunch as well as dinner, giving it more flexibility than many upscale restaurants in the area. The restaurant is located inside Santa Fe Station, which provides free parking and easy access from the main Centennial Hills residential areas.
