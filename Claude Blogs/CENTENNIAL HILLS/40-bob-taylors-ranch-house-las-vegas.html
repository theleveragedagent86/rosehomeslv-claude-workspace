Bob Taylor's Ranch House is the oldest steakhouse in Las Vegas, serving mesquite grilled meats and slow smoked prime rib at 6250 Rio Vista Street since 1955. Located near Centennial Hills, this legendary restaurant offers a dining experience rooted in the authentic Western heritage of the Las Vegas Valley. ## What Makes Bob Taylor's Ranch House a Las Vegas Legend? Bob Taylor's has been a Las Vegas institution for over seven decades, and the restaurant wears that history proudly. The interior is filled with Western memorabilia, including John Wayne collectibles and cowboy decor that transports guests to a different era. Walking through the front door feels like stepping into a piece of Las Vegas history that existed long before the Strip became the city's defining feature. The food is built around mesquite grilled steaks cooked over an open indoor pit, a preparation method that gives the meat a distinctive smoky flavor you will not find at modern steakhouses. The prime rib is the signature dish, slow smoked outside in dedicated smokers throughout the day and served with the kind of tenderness that only comes from patience and expertise. Every entree arrives with soup or dinner salad, garlic cheese bread, and a choice of baked potato, twice baked potato, ranch house potatoes, rice pilaf, or mixed vegetables. Beyond the steaks and prime rib, the menu features ribs, chicken, and seafood, along with starters like Cajun shrimp scampi and crab stuffed mushrooms dressed in cream sauce with a spritz of lemon. The kitchen stays true to recipes and techniques that have worked for decades, and that consistency is a big part of what keeps generations of Las Vegas families coming back. ## Hours and Getting There Bob Taylor's Ranch House is open Monday through Thursday and Sunday from 11:00 AM to 10:00 PM, with extended hours on Friday and Saturday until 11:00 PM. The restaurant is located on Rio Vista Street, a short drive from the main Centennial Hills residential areas via US 95. Parking is available on site, and the rustic ranch house setting provides a sense of arrival that sets the tone before you even walk inside. ## A Piece of Las Vegas That Belongs to the Neighborhood While tourists flock to the glitzy steakhouses on the Strip, Las Vegas locals know that some of the best dining in the city happens in places like Bob Taylor's. The restaurant represents a connection to the valley's ranching roots and a tradition of straightforward, quality cooking that has outlasted every trend in the food world.
