Centennial Hills is home to a growing collection of Mexican restaurants, each offering a distinct take on the cuisine. Whether you are craving traditional regional dishes, gourmet presentations, or casual fish tacos, the northwest valley has options within a short drive of nearly every neighborhood. ## Leticia's Cocina and Cantina Located inside Santa Fe Station at 4949 N. Rancho Drive, Leticia's is one of the most recognized Mexican restaurants in the Centennial Hills area. The menu pulls from culinary traditions across Mexico City, Puebla, Guadalajara, and the Mayan Peninsula. Standout dishes include the carne asada street tacos, cochinita pibil, and chile relleno. The kitchen keeps the menu roughly 97 percent gluten free, and the complimentary chips with smoky salsa and bean dip are a beloved starting ritual for regulars. With a 4.5 star rating from hundreds of OpenTable diners, it remains a consistent pick for date nights and family dinners alike. Read our full review in the [Leticia's Cocina and Cantina guide](/blogs/leticias-cocina-cantina-centennial-hills). ## Michoacan Gourmet Mexican Restaurant Michoacan Gourmet sits along Tropical Parkway at 7870 W. Tropical Pkwy, near the Centennial Centre shopping area. The restaurant opened in 2009 and has built a reputation for refined Mexican dishes prepared with fresh ingredients. Highlights include the tableside guacamole, camarones al cognac, carnitas a la coca cola, and pollo sarandeado. The tequila selection is impressive, featuring over 200 options. Live entertainment adds energy to weekend evenings, with a keyboard vocalist performing on select nights and a mariachi band on Saturdays. Hours run Sunday through Thursday from 11am to 9pm, and Friday and Saturday from 11am to 10pm. ## Wahoo's Fish Tacos For a faster, more casual experience, Wahoo's Fish Tacos at 7930 W. Tropical Pkwy brings a fusion twist to Mexican staples. The menu blends flavors from Baja, Brazil, China, and Greece into tacos, burritos, and bowls. Protein choices range from carne asada and carnitas to wahoo fish, salmon, and shrimp, with tofu and banzai veggie options for plant based diners. The restaurant accommodates gluten free, high protein, and low carb preferences, making it a flexible lunch stop for nearly any dietary need. ## What the Mexican Dining Scene Says About Centennial Hills The variety of Mexican restaurants in the area reflects the broader growth and diversity of Centennial Hills as a neighborhood. Residents do not need to drive to the Las Vegas Strip or downtown to find high quality, authentic food. Everything from a relaxed weeknight taco run to a special occasion dinner with live music is available within minutes of home.
