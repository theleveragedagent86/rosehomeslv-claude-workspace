Centennial Hills has become a destination for Japanese food lovers in the northwest valley, with a handful of standout restaurants offering everything from all you can eat sushi to handcrafted ramen bowls. Here is a closer look at the top Japanese and sushi spots in the neighborhood. ## Umami Sushi Umami is the premier sushi destination in Centennial Hills, located at 6640 N. Durango Drive, Suite 180. This locally owned restaurant offers an all you can eat sushi format with a focus on quality that sets it apart from typical buffet style operations. The menu features fresh rolls, nigiri, sashimi, tempura, gyoza, and yakitori, all prepared with care and presented in a modern, casual setting. Umami is open daily from 11am to midnight, with last call at 11:30pm. The restaurant does not accept phone reservations, so guests need to visit in person to secure a table. Despite the walk in only policy, the popularity of Umami speaks for itself, with over 3,000 reviews and a strong following among locals. For a deeper look, visit our full [Umami Japanese Centennial Hills guide](/blogs/umami-japanese-centennial-hills). ## Ichi Ramen House For ramen enthusiasts, Ichi Ramen House at 7920 W. Tropical Pkwy, Suite 170 is the neighborhood go to. The restaurant specializes in rich, flavorful ramen made with fresh noodles and broth that is simmered for three days. The Black Garlic Ramen is a standout, drawing consistent praise from regulars for its depth and complexity. Bowls are priced between roughly $8 and $12, making it one of the best values in the area for a filling, high quality meal. The space is cozy, with a handful of tables, booths, and counter seating that give it an intimate, neighborhood feel. Ichi Ramen House is open Monday through Saturday from 11:30am to 10pm and Sunday from 11:30am to 9pm. Takeout is available for those who prefer to enjoy their ramen at home. Vegetarian ramen options are on the menu as well, and the atmosphere is welcoming for families with children. ## Sushi Loca Sushi Loca at 6181 Centennial Center Blvd has been a familiar name in the Centennial Centre shopping area for its creative specialty rolls and traditional sushi offerings. The menu features signature rolls with playful names alongside teriyaki, udon noodles, and hibachi style entrees. Vegan and vegetarian options round out the selection. ## What Japanese Dining Adds to the Centennial Hills Lifestyle The presence of quality Japanese restaurants close to home is one of the everyday perks that residents of Centennial Hills appreciate.
