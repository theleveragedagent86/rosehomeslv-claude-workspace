Santa Fe Station Hotel and Casino is a locals favorite entertainment destination located at 4949 N Rancho Drive, offering dining, gaming, bowling, a movie theater, and live entertainment just minutes from Centennial Hills. ## What Does Santa Fe Station Offer Centennial Hills Residents? Santa Fe Station is one of the most popular off Strip casino destinations in northwest Las Vegas, and its proximity to Centennial Hills makes it a go to spot for dining, entertainment, and recreation. Operated by Station Casinos, the property delivers a full resort experience without the crowds and traffic of the Las Vegas Strip. For Centennial Hills residents, it serves as an everyday entertainment hub that is just a quick drive south on US 95. The gaming floor offers a full spread of table games and slot machines, but Santa Fe Station is known just as much for what surrounds the casino. A 60 lane bowling center gives families and leagues alike a reliable venue for recreation. The on site movie theater screens the latest releases, making date nights and family outings simple and affordable. For families with young children, Santa Fe Station also operates one of the few in casino supervised childcare centers in northwest Las Vegas. ## Dining Worth the Short Drive Santa Fe Station is home to a lineup of restaurants that rival many Strip properties. The Charcoal Room is an award winning steakhouse and seafood restaurant, delivering a fine dining experience in a relaxed atmosphere. The Oyster Bar has earned a loyal following for its fresh seafood and intimate counter seating. Brass Fork rounds out the options with classic American comfort food. For those looking for more casual fare, the property features Cabo with over 130 Mexican dishes, Villa Pizza, and the popular Feast Buffet. Several bars and lounges are scattered throughout the property, including regular viewing parties for Vegas Golden Knights games.
