Centennial Hills is one of the most pet friendly neighborhoods in Las Vegas, with multiple off leash dog parks, over 2,000 acres of open space at Floyd Lamb Park, and convenient access to pet supply stores and veterinary clinics. ## Is Centennial Hills a Good Neighborhood for Pets? Pet owners moving to Las Vegas will find Centennial Hills to be one of the most welcoming communities in the valley. The neighborhood offers dedicated dog parks, miles of walking paths, nearby open desert terrain, and the kind of quiet residential streets that make daily walks enjoyable for both dogs and their owners. The centerpiece for pet owners is the [Centennial Hills Park dog park](https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blogs/dog-parks-centennial-hills), located within the 120 acre Centennial Hills Park at 6851 N Buffalo Drive. This fully fenced facility features separate areas for large and small breeds, water faucets for thirsty dogs, shaded seating for owners, and waste stations throughout. The park also includes an agility course that keeps active dogs engaged and exercised. Morning and late afternoon hours tend to draw the biggest crowds, creating a social atmosphere where regular visitors get to know each other and their pets. ## More Dog Parks and Open Spaces Centennial Hills Park is not the only option. Knickerbocker Park at 6501 N Knickerbocker Avenue offers its own fenced dog area in a quieter setting, and Huckleberry Park provides additional off leash space for dogs who prefer a less crowded environment. Having multiple dog parks spread across the neighborhood means most residents are just a short drive or walk from an off leash option. For pet owners who want larger terrain, Floyd Lamb Park at Tule Springs sits at the northern edge of the community and covers more than 2,000 acres of open space. While dogs must remain on leash in this park, the shaded paths around the ponds and through the historic ranch grounds offer some of the most scenic walking in the northwest valley. It is a favorite destination for weekend outings with the whole family, pets included. ## Pet Services and Supplies Centennial Hills has a strong lineup of pet related businesses. Petco anchors the Centennial Centre shopping plaza, providing food, supplies, grooming, and veterinary services all in one stop. Hounds Town USA, located in the neighborhood, offers dog daycare and boarding for owners who travel or work long hours. Multiple veterinary clinics serve the area, so routine checkups and emergencies are handled locally without driving across the valley. ## Walking and Trail Access Beyond the parks, Centennial Hills benefits from wide sidewalks, low traffic residential streets, and proximity to desert trails. The neighborhood connects to hiking and walking paths that lead toward [Red Rock Canyon](https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blogs/red-rock-canyon-centennial-hills-access) and the upper northwest valley. These trails give active pet owners a way to explore beyond the neighborhood boundaries while staying close to home. ## Pet Friendly Housing Considerations Buyers with pets should pay attention to HOA rules, which vary by subdivision. Some communities restrict the number or size of pets, while others are more flexible.
