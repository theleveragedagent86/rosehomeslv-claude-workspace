Centennial Hills is one of the top family neighborhoods in Las Vegas, with 13 public schools, top rated charter academies, 120 acres of parkland, and crime rates lower than 74% of U.S. communities. ## Why Families Choose Centennial Hills Centennial Hills has earned a reputation as one of the most family friendly neighborhoods in the Las Vegas valley. Developed as a master planned community in the early 2000s, the neighborhood was designed from the start with families in mind. Wide residential streets, interconnected parks, quality schools, and convenient shopping create a daily environment that supports the routines of busy households with children of all ages. The community spans three zip codes (89130, 89131, and 89149) and is home to more than 100,000 residents. The upper middle income demographics and strong homeowner association presence help maintain property values and neighborhood standards that families appreciate. ## Schools That Outperform State Averages Education is often the deciding factor for families choosing a neighborhood, and Centennial Hills delivers. The area is served by 13 public schools enrolling over 13,000 students. Math proficiency among Centennial Hills students reaches 33%, compared to 28% statewide, and reading proficiency hits 55% versus the state average of 44%. The [Centennial Hills schools guide](https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blogs/centennial-hills-schools-guide) covers the full range of options, but two schools stand out. Coral Academy of Science earns a perfect 10 out of 10 rating and offers a rigorous STEM focused curriculum. Somerset Academy Sky Pointe also earns a 10 out of 10 rating and provides a college preparatory program starting in elementary school. Both are tuition free public charter schools, giving families access to exceptional education without private school costs. Beyond charter options, families have access to strong traditional schools including Bozarth Elementary, Cadwallader Middle School, and Centennial High School. Enrichment programs like iCode STEM classes and Champions before and after school care fill gaps for working parents. ## Parks, Recreation, and Things to Do Centennial Hills Park covers 120 acres and serves as the neighborhood's gathering place. Playgrounds, sports fields, walking paths, a skate park, and an amphitheater that hosts community events give families a reason to spend time outdoors year round. The adjacent [Centennial Hills Library](https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blogs/centennial-hills-library-guide) offers free children's programming, storytimes, and teen activities. The [Centennial Hills YMCA](https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blogs/centennial-hills-ymca-community-center) provides an indoor pool, outdoor water park, youth sports leagues, preschool, and summer camp programs. For families with young children, the YMCA is often the first membership they sign up for after moving in. Outdoor adventures extend beyond the neighborhood. Floyd Lamb Park at Tule Springs offers 2,000 acres of open space, and Red Rock Canyon is a short drive west for hiking and nature exploration. ## Safety and Community Feel The neighborhood earns a [B+ safety grade](https://www.rosehomeslv.com/blogs/centennial-hills-safety-crime-rate) with violent crime rates lower than 76% of U.S. communities and property crime rates lower than 79%. The master planned layout limits cut through traffic on residential streets, and active HOAs maintain neighborhood standards. ## Shopping and Daily Convenience The Centennial Centre shopping plaza covers 900,000 square feet and includes grocery stores, restaurants, a movie theater, and family oriented retailers. Daily errands rarely require leaving the neighborhood, which saves time for families juggling school schedules, sports practices, and weekend activities.
