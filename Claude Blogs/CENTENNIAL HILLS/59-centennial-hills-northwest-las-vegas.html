Centennial Hills sits in the northwest quadrant of the Las Vegas valley, centered around the interchange of I 11/US 95 and the Las Vegas Beltway (CC 215). The community spans three zip codes: 89130, 89131, and 89149. It borders the city of North Las Vegas to the east and north, while the western and southern edges connect to other unincorporated Clark County neighborhoods. The name "Centennial Hills" was chosen through a public naming contest in 2001, when 2,222 votes selected it from a list of candidates. Since then, the community has grown into one of the defining neighborhoods of northwest Las Vegas, with its own hospital, parks, schools, shopping centers, and a distinct residential identity. ## What Makes It Part of Northwest Las Vegas Northwest Las Vegas is generally defined as the area north of Summerlin and west of North Las Vegas, stretching from the US 95 corridor toward the Spring Mountains. Centennial Hills fits squarely within this geography. The community is not part of the City of Las Vegas proper or the City of North Las Vegas. It falls within unincorporated Clark County, which is common for many of the larger master planned areas in the northwest. The northwest designation matters for practical reasons. Schools in Centennial Hills are part of the Clark County School District's northwest region. Police and fire services are provided by the Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department and Clark County Fire Department, respectively. Shopping, dining, and medical services are all oriented toward the northwest corridor, with Centennial Centre, Santa Fe Station, and Downtown Summerlin serving as the primary commercial hubs. ## How Centennial Hills Compares to Other Northwest Neighborhoods Northwest Las Vegas includes several well known communities, with Summerlin being the most recognized.
