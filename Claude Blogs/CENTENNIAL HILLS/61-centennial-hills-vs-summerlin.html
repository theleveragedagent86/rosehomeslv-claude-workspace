Centennial Hills and Summerlin are two of the most popular master planned communities in Las Vegas, but they offer different lifestyles and price points that matter to homebuyers exploring the valley. ## How Do Centennial Hills and Summerlin Compare? Both communities deliver tree lined streets, quality schools, parks, and easy freeway access. The differences between them can shape your buying decision depending on priorities and budget. Summerlin is the largest master planned community in Las Vegas, stretching across the western valley with over 100,000 residents. It offers extensive retail and dining, multiple village centers, and a reputation built since the late 1980s. Centennial Hills, in the northwest, has grown rapidly since 2001 and now exceeds 100,000 residents as well. ## Price Differences Between the Two Communities This is where Centennial Hills stands out for value conscious buyers. Homes in Centennial Hills typically sell for 10 to 20 percent less than comparable properties in Summerlin. The median home price in Centennial Hills falls in the $475,000 to $550,000 range in 2026, while Summerlin's median sits between $550,000 and $700,000 or higher. You can often get a larger home with a bigger lot in Centennial Hills for the same price you would pay in Summerlin. That price gap is one of the biggest reasons buyers are choosing the northwest corridor. ## Lifestyle and Amenities Summerlin edges ahead in retail variety and upscale dining, particularly around Downtown Summerlin and the Red Rock Casino resort area. The community also sits closer to Red Rock Canyon, which appeals to hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. Centennial Hills counters with its own impressive amenities. The 120 acre Centennial Hills Park features sports fields, trails, a dog park, and a community center. The neighborhood is home to a YMCA, a modern library, and Centennial Centre, which offers over 900,000 square feet of shopping. Proximity to Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument gives residents a unique natural landmark that Summerlin simply does not have. ## Schools and Commute Both communities are served by the Clark County School District and offer strong public and charter school options. Centennial Hills has a slight edge in commute times to the northern end of the valley and areas along the I-15 corridor, while Summerlin provides quicker access to the western suburbs and the 215 beltway loop. ## Which Community Is Right for You? Buyers who prioritize brand recognition, walkable retail, and proximity to Red Rock Canyon may lean toward Summerlin. Buyers who want more home for the money, a strong community feel, and easy access to the US 95 corridor will find Centennial Hills to be the smarter play.
