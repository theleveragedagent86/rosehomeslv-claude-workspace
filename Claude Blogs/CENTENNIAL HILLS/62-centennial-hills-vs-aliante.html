Centennial Hills and Aliante are neighboring northwest Las Vegas communities that attract many of the same buyers, but key differences in pricing, location, and amenities set them apart. ## Centennial Hills vs Aliante: What Buyers Need to Know Centennial Hills and Aliante share a suburban feel, family friendly atmosphere, and access to the US 95 corridor, but meaningful differences can shape your decision. Centennial Hills spans roughly 54 to 56 square miles within the City of Las Vegas across zip codes 89130, 89131, and 89149. It was named through a public contest in 2001 and has grown into one of the most established northwest neighborhoods. Aliante is a master planned community within North Las Vegas that launched in 2003, covering a smaller footprint with a tighter design centered around its nature discovery park and golf course. ## How Home Prices Compare Centennial Hills offers a wider price range, with homes typically falling between $375,000 and $800,000 or more. The median sale price sits around $475,000 to $550,000 in 2026. Aliante skews slightly more affordable, with a median home price in the $380,000 to $450,000 range. Buyers who need to stay under $400,000 may find more options in Aliante, while those looking for larger or newer construction homes with more variety will find a deeper inventory in Centennial Hills. ## Community Amenities and Feel Centennial Hills is anchored by its 120 acre park, YMCA, modern library, and the 900,000 square foot Centennial Centre shopping plaza. The community also borders the Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument, adding a natural and historical element that few Las Vegas neighborhoods can match. Aliante's centerpiece is the Aliante Nature Discovery Park, a 20 acre park with trails, playgrounds, and open green space. The community also features the Aliante Golf Club, a well regarded championship course designed by Gary Panks, and the Aliante Casino and Hotel, which provides local dining and entertainment options. Aliante has a more compact, walkable design compared to the sprawling footprint of Centennial Hills. ## Location and Commute Both communities provide solid access to the US 95 and the CC 215 beltway. Centennial Hills generally offers a shorter commute to central Las Vegas and the Strip, estimated at 20 to 30 minutes. Aliante sits slightly further north, which can add a few minutes to southbound commutes but positions residents closer to developing commercial corridors in North Las Vegas. ## Making Your Choice Buyers who value variety in home styles, a larger community footprint, and proximity to Las Vegas city amenities tend to gravitate toward Centennial Hills. Those who prefer a newer, more unified master plan with a lower price point often find Aliante to be the better fit.
