Centennial Hills and Providence are both located in the northwest Las Vegas valley, and buyers often weigh the two against each other when searching for a family friendly home in the area. ## Centennial Hills vs Providence: A Northwest Las Vegas Comparison Providence and Centennial Hills sit near each other in the northwest corner of the Las Vegas valley, sharing access to the same freeways, schools, and commercial corridors. Buyers frequently ask which community offers the better value and lifestyle, and the answer depends on your priorities. Centennial Hills is a large community spanning over 54 square miles with dozens of subdivisions from builders like Lennar, KB Home, Toll Brothers, and D.R. Horton. Homes range from the mid $300,000s to above $800,000. Providence is a more focused master planned community known for its guard gated sections and cohesive design. ## Price and Home Selection Centennial Hills homes range from $375,000 to $800,000 with a 2026 median around $475,000 to $550,000. Providence homes cluster in a similar band, though guard gated sections can command a premium. Providence delivers gated living as its core feature, while Centennial Hills offers gated subdivisions as one option among many. ## Amenities and Community Character Centennial Hills anchors around the 120 acre Centennial Hills Park, a YMCA, a modern library, and the Centennial Centre shopping plaza with over 900,000 square feet of retail. The community also borders Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument and offers mountain views. Providence features community parks, walking paths, and a well maintained streetscape with controlled access in its gated sections. It has a quieter, more uniform feel compared to Centennial Hills. ## Location and Access The two communities are close enough that commute times are nearly identical. Both benefit from the I 11/US 95 and CC 215 interchange, putting the Strip 20 to 30 minutes away and downtown Las Vegas 20 to 25 minutes out. Day to day errands, dining, and entertainment options are shared between the two neighborhoods, so you will not sacrifice convenience by choosing one over the other. ## Which One Fits Your Lifestyle? Providence is a strong choice for buyers who want a gated environment with a more intimate community layout. Centennial Hills is the better option for buyers who want a wider selection of homes, price points, and neighborhood amenities within a larger and more established community.
