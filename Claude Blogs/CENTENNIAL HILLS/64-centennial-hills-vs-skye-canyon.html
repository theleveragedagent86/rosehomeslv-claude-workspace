Centennial Hills and Skye Canyon both occupy the northwest Las Vegas valley, but one is an established community with decades of growth while the other is a newer master plan still building out its vision. ## Centennial Hills vs Skye Canyon: Established vs New Skye Canyon launched in 2015 as a modern master planned community just northwest of Centennial Hills. Centennial Hills has been growing since the late 1990s and was officially named in 2001. The comparison comes down to whether you value an established neighborhood or fresh construction with curated amenities. ## Home Prices and New Construction Centennial Hills homes start in the mid $300,000s and reach above $800,000, with a 2026 median around $475,000 to $550,000. Buyers have access to resale and new construction from builders like Lennar, KB Home, and D.R. Horton, with about 123 new homes available near a $520,000 median. Skye Canyon leans heavily toward new construction, with most homes in the $450,000 to $600,000 range. Buyers can purchase directly from builders and customize floor plans, though resale inventory is more limited. ## Amenities and Community Design Skye Canyon Park features a fitness court, splash pad, sports courts, and event lawn. The neighborhood hosts community events, farmers markets, and seasonal programming that build a tightly knit atmosphere. Centennial Hills offers a larger amenity package: the 120 acre park, YMCA, library, and Centennial Centre shopping plaza with 900,000 square feet of retail. Proximity to Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument and mountain views add a natural dimension. The community also has a 336 bed hospital and established commercial infrastructure that Skye Canyon is still developing. ## Schools and Daily Life Both communities are served by the Clark County School District. Centennial Hills has more established school zones and proximity to charter schools like Coral Academy and Somerset Academy Sky Pointe. Skye Canyon school assignments continue to evolve as the community grows. For daily errands, Centennial Hills has a clear edge thanks to years of commercial development along the US 95 corridor. Skye Canyon residents often drive into Centennial Hills for groceries and dining, though new retail is gradually filling in. ## Choosing Between the Two Skye Canyon is ideal for buyers who want a brand new home in a modern community with a strong social calendar and contemporary design. Centennial Hills is the better fit for buyers who want a wider selection of homes at more varied price points, combined with the convenience of an established neighborhood that already has everything in place.
