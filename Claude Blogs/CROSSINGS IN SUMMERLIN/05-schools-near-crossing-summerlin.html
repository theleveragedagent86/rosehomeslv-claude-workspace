# Schools Near The Crossing Summerlin The Crossing in Summerlin is zoned for some of the highest rated public schools in the Clark County School District, including John W. Bonner Elementary (9/10 GreatSchools), Sig Rogich Middle School (8/10, ranked number one in CCSD), and Palo Verde High School (B+ Niche rating). Private school options are also within a short drive. ## What Elementary School Serves The Crossing? John W. Bonner Elementary School is located within The Crossing itself, making it a true neighborhood school. It carries a GreatSchools rating of 9 out of 10, a Niche grade of A minus, and has earned a 5 Star School designation. The school serves students from pre kindergarten through fifth grade. For families with young children, having a top rated elementary within walking distance is one of the strongest draws of living in this village. ## Which Middle School Is Zoned for The Crossing? Sig Rogich Middle School sits adjacent to The Crossing and serves students in grades 6 through 8. It holds a GreatSchools rating of 8 out of 10 and is ranked as the number one middle school in the entire Clark County School District. Rogich has also received a 5 Star School designation. The school's proximity means most students in The Crossing can walk or bike to campus. ## What High School Do Crossing Students Attend? Palo Verde High School is located adjacent to The Crossing and serves grades 9 through 12. The school holds a Niche rating of B+ and is ranked number 19 in Nevada by U.S. News & World Report. Palo Verde offers a comprehensive academic and athletic program, and its location next to the community eliminates long commutes for high school students. ## Are There Private Schools Near The Crossing? Two highly regarded private schools are located within a few miles. The Meadows School, approximately 4 miles away, is a pre K through 12 institution ranked as the number one private high school in Nevada by Niche with an A+ rating. The Adelson School, roughly 3 miles from The Crossing, is a private Jewish school serving pre K through 12 with a 6 to 1 student to teacher ratio. Both offer rigorous academic programs and smaller class sizes. ## Ryan Rose's Take on Schools in The Crossing Ryan Rose frequently hears from buyers that school quality is the deciding factor when choosing a Summerlin neighborhood.
