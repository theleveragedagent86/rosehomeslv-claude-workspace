# Subdivisions in The Crossing Summerlin The Crossing in Summerlin contains 13 distinct subdivisions offering single family homes, condos, and townhomes built between 1994 and 2002. This established Summerlin village gives buyers a wide range of property types and price points, from entry level condos near $287,000 to spacious single family homes above $600,000. ## What Subdivisions Are in The Crossing? The Crossing includes the following 13 neighborhoods: Aspenglen, Crimson Ridge, Discovery Hills, Discovery Pointe, Highland Hills, Pacific Crest, Pacific Vintage, Red Bluffs, San Marino, Sunset Mesa, Sycamore Ridge, West Hills, and Westridge. Each subdivision operates under the master HOA at $74 per month, with some communities charging an additional sub HOA fee that ranges from $40 to $250 per month depending on amenities. ## Which Subdivisions Are Single Family Homes? Nine subdivisions in The Crossing feature single family detached homes. Aspenglen offers 3 to 4 bedroom layouts between 1,800 and 2,200 square feet with 2 car garages, built by the Howard Hughes Corporation around 1996 to 1997. Discovery Hills is the largest option with 4 to 5 bedroom homes spanning 2,900 to 3,200 square feet and 3 car garages. West Hills dates to approximately 1995, while Westridge was built around 1999. Crimson Ridge, Discovery Pointe, Highland Hills, San Marino, and Sycamore Ridge round out the single family options. ## Which Subdivisions Are Condos and Townhomes? Four subdivisions offer attached housing. Red Bluffs is a 272 unit condo community with 2 to 3 bedroom floor plans from 838 to 1,263 square feet. Pacific Crest is a gated condo complex with attached garages and resort style amenities. Pacific Vintage features 164 townhomes with 2 to 4 bedrooms and 1,204 to 1,859 square feet. Sunset Mesa has 196 townhomes built by Pulte Homes between 1998 and 2000 with sizes from 1,200 to 1,800 square feet. All four of these communities are gated. ## Which Subdivisions Are Gated? Red Bluffs, Pacific Crest, Pacific Vintage, and Sunset Mesa are all gated communities within The Crossing. Red Bluffs provides guard gated access at night and standard gated entry during the day. The single family subdivisions throughout the village are generally non gated, which gives buyers flexibility based on their security preferences. ## A Local Perspective on The Crossing Subdivisions Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, considers The Crossing one of the most versatile villages in Summerlin because of its subdivision variety. First time buyers can start in a Red Bluffs condo, growing families can move into Discovery Hills, and downsizers can find the right fit in Pacific Vintage or Sunset Mesa. Having 13 neighborhoods under one village umbrella creates real options at every price point. ## Ready to Explore The Crossing Subdivisions?
