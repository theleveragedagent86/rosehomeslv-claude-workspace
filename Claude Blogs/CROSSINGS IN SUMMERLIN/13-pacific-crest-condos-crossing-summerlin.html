# Pacific Crest Condos at The Crossing Summerlin Pacific Crest is a gated condominium community within The Crossing village of Summerlin, featuring attached garages, a swimming pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, and a playground. This community offers an amenity rich condo experience in one of Summerlin's most established neighborhoods, with homes built during the late 1990s to early 2000s. ## What Amenities Does Pacific Crest Offer? Pacific Crest stands out among The Crossing's condo communities for its full amenity package. Residents have access to a swimming pool, spa, fitness center, clubhouse, and playground. These private facilities are maintained through the community's sub HOA, which operates alongside The Crossing's master HOA fee of $74 per month. The playground makes Pacific Crest especially appealing to families with young children who want condo living without sacrificing outdoor recreational space. ## Are Pacific Crest Condos Gated? Pacific Crest is a fully gated community, providing controlled access to all residents and their guests. This makes it one of four gated neighborhoods within The Crossing, alongside Red Bluffs, Sunset Mesa, and Pacific Vintage. The gated entry adds a layer of privacy and security that many buyers prioritize when comparing condo options across Summerlin. ## Do Pacific Crest Condos Have Garages? One of Pacific Crest's key selling points is that units come with attached garages. This is a significant advantage over many condo communities in the Las Vegas valley where parking is limited to open lots or detached structures. Having a direct access garage adds convenience, especially during the summer months when Las Vegas temperatures regularly exceed 110 degrees. ## How Does Pacific Crest Compare to Red Bluffs? Both Pacific Crest and Red Bluffs are gated condo communities in The Crossing, but they differ in important ways. Red Bluffs offers 1 car detached garages and guard gated nighttime security across its 272 units. Pacific Crest provides attached garages and includes a playground that Red Bluffs does not. Buyers choosing between the two should weigh attached versus detached garage access, community size, and the specific amenity mix that fits their lifestyle. ## A Local Perspective on Pacific Crest Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, highlights Pacific Crest as one of The Crossing's most well rounded condo options. The attached garage alone sets it apart from many competing communities, and the full amenity package rivals what you would find in newer Summerlin developments. For buyers who want gated condo living with the convenience of a garage and family friendly features, Pacific Crest consistently delivers strong value. ## Interested in Pacific Crest Condos?
