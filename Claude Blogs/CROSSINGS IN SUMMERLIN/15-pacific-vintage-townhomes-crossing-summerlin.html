# Pacific Vintage Townhomes at The Crossing Summerlin Pacific Vintage is a 164 unit gated townhome community in The Crossing village of Summerlin, offering 2 to 4 bedroom floor plans from 1,204 to 1,859 square feet with attached 2 car garages. Built between 1996 and 2002, Pacific Vintage provides townhome living with private amenities and lot sizes ranging from 3,485 to 7,841 square feet. ## How Big Are Pacific Vintage Townhomes? Pacific Vintage townhomes range from 1,204 to 1,859 square feet with 2 to 4 bedrooms and 2 bathrooms. Every unit includes an attached 2 car garage, which distinguishes Pacific Vintage from many townhome communities in the Las Vegas valley. Lot sizes vary from 3,485 to 7,841 square feet, giving some owners more outdoor space than others. The 164 units create a compact, walkable neighborhood within the larger Crossing village. ## Is Pacific Vintage Gated? Pacific Vintage is a fully gated community, making it one of four gated neighborhoods in The Crossing alongside Red Bluffs, Pacific Crest, and Sunset Mesa. Gated access provides controlled entry for residents and guests while maintaining privacy within the community. The village itself is not gated, so Pacific Vintage's gate creates a secure pocket within the broader neighborhood. ## What Amenities Does Pacific Vintage Have? The community features a swimming pool, spa, and a small park. These amenities are maintained through the sub HOA and are exclusive to Pacific Vintage residents. The small park adds green space that many townhome communities lack, creating a spot for relaxation and outdoor activity without leaving the neighborhood. Pacific Vintage's HOA covers access to these facilities on top of The Crossing's master HOA fee of $74 per month. ## How Does Pacific Vintage Compare to Sunset Mesa? Both Pacific Vintage and Sunset Mesa are gated townhome communities in The Crossing, but they differ in key areas. Pacific Vintage offers attached 2 car garages while Sunset Mesa was built by Pulte Homes with slightly different floor plan configurations. Sunset Mesa carries a sub HOA of $250 per month and includes a clubhouse and exercise room. Pacific Vintage's sub HOA tends to run lower, and its lot sizes can extend to nearly 7,900 square feet. Buyers should compare monthly HOA costs, garage access, and community size when deciding between the two. ## A Local Perspective on Pacific Vintage Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, highlights the attached 2 car garage as Pacific Vintage's biggest advantage. In the townhome segment, having direct garage access is a feature that buyers consistently prioritize. Combined with lot sizes that can rival some single family homes, Pacific Vintage offers a townhome experience that feels more spacious than the typical attached home product in Summerlin. ## Interested in Pacific Vintage Townhomes?
