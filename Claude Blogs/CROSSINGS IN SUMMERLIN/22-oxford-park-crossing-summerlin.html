# Oxford Park at The Crossing Summerlin Oxford Park is a fully lighted baseball facility located in The Crossing village of Summerlin, featuring a 90 foot stadium style field with a 60 foot 6 inch pitching rubber and 90 foot base paths. The park also includes a children's playground and open grass areas at 700 Anasazi Dr, Las Vegas, NV 89144. ## What Makes Oxford Park Stand Out? Oxford Park delivers a genuine baseball experience that most neighborhood parks cannot match. The 90 foot base paths and 60 foot 6 inch pitching rubber meet regulation standards, giving players a proper competitive venue. Full stadium lighting extends play into the evening hours, which is essential during the hot Las Vegas summers when families prefer cooler nighttime activities. The park serves as a gathering point for The Crossing's baseball community throughout the year. ## Is Oxford Park Good for Kids? Absolutely. Beyond the baseball diamond, Oxford Park features a dedicated children's playground and open grass play areas. Younger children can play on the equipment while older siblings or parents use the ball field. The open grass sections work well for casual games of catch, frisbee, or simply running around. Families frequently spend entire afternoons at the park, taking advantage of the variety of activities available in one location. ## How Close Is Oxford Park to Homes in The Crossing? Oxford Park sits at 700 Anasazi Dr, centrally positioned within The Crossing village. Most homes in the community are a short walk or bike ride away. This proximity means kids can get to practices and games independently, and parents can easily pop over for a quick visit to the playground. Combined with nearby Crossing Park and its soccer fields, The Crossing offers an unusual density of recreational options for a single Summerlin village. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose Ryan Rose notes that Oxford Park is one of the features that sets The Crossing apart from other Summerlin villages.
