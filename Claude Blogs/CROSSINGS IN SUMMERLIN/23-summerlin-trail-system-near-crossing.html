# Summerlin Trail System Near The Crossing The Summerlin Trail System provides over 200 miles of landscaped and lighted pathways that connect The Crossing to parks, schools, shopping, and neighboring villages throughout the master planned community. Six trail types serve walkers, runners, cyclists, and commuters year round. ## What Types of Trails Are Near The Crossing? Summerlin organizes its trail network into six categories: street side, village, multi modal, regional, urban, and connector trails. Street side trails run alongside main roads and provide safe pedestrian access. Village trails wind through neighborhoods like The Crossing, linking homes to community parks such as Crossing Park and Oxford Park. Multi modal trails accommodate both cyclists and pedestrians on wider paths. Regional trails extend to destinations beyond Summerlin, connecting to the greater Las Vegas trail network. ## Can You Walk or Bike to Daily Destinations from The Crossing? Yes. The trail system was designed to reduce car dependency for short trips. Residents of The Crossing can walk or bike to nearby schools, grocery stores, and restaurants using dedicated pathways separated from vehicle traffic. The trails are landscaped with desert plants and lighted for safe use after dark. This connectivity is one of the features that separates Summerlin from conventional Las Vegas subdivisions, where sidewalks often end abruptly and destinations require a car. ## Are the Trails Good for Exercise and Recreation? The trail system serves both fitness and leisure purposes. Runners log miles on smooth, well maintained paths without dealing with intersections or traffic. Cyclists use the multi modal trails for commuting and weekend rides. Families take evening walks through the village trails that connect The Crossing's green spaces. The 200 plus mile network means residents rarely repeat the same route, and the trail system connects all the way to the edges of Red Rock Canyon for more adventurous outings. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose Ryan Rose sees the Summerlin Trail System as one of the most underappreciated selling points of The Crossing.
