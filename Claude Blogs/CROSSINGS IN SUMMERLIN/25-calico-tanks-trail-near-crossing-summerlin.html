# Calico Tanks Trail Near The Crossing Summerlin Calico Tanks Trail in Red Rock Canyon is a 2.5 mile round trip hike ranked as the number one best hike in Las Vegas, located just 10 to 15 minutes by car from The Crossing in Summerlin. The moderate trail gains over 400 feet in elevation and rewards hikers with panoramic views of the Las Vegas Strip and surrounding desert landscape. ## What Is the Calico Tanks Trail Like? Calico Tanks Trail covers approximately 2.5 miles round trip with a moderate difficulty rating. The path winds through red and cream colored sandstone formations, passing through narrow washes and scrambling sections that keep the hike engaging. The elevation gain of over 400 feet provides a solid workout without being overwhelming. At the top, hikers reach natural rock tanks that collect rainwater and enjoy sweeping views of the Las Vegas Valley. The trail is well traveled and suitable for fit beginners and experienced hikers alike. ## How Far Is Calico Tanks from The Crossing? The trailhead sits within Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, roughly 10 to 15 minutes by car from The Crossing. Residents can reach the 13 mile scenic drive entrance quickly via West Charleston Boulevard. This proximity means a morning hike at Calico Tanks can easily fit into a regular weekend routine. Timed entry reservations are required from October 1 through May 31, so residents should plan ahead during the peak hiking season. ## What Other Hikes Are Near The Crossing? Red Rock Canyon offers over 30 miles of hiking trails beyond Calico Tanks. Turtlehead Peak provides a challenging 5 mile round trip option for experienced hikers. The Summerlin Peaks Loop follows a limestone ridgeline with multiple small summits. Keystone Thrust Trail and Ice Box Canyon offer additional variety.
