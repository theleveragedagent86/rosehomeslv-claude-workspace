The Crossing in Summerlin features Southwestern Craftsman architecture across a mix of single family homes, townhomes, condominiums, and duplex style units. Home sizes range from 700 to 3,200 square feet, giving buyers a wide selection of floor plans and price points within one of Summerlin's most established villages. ## What Home Styles Are Available in The Crossing? The Crossing offers four distinct home types across its thirteen subdivisions. Single family homes come in both single story and two story configurations, with sizes ranging from 1,800 to 3,200 square feet. Townhomes in communities like Sunset Mesa and Pacific Vintage range from 1,200 to 1,859 square feet with two to four bedrooms. Condominiums in Red Bluffs and Pacific Crest start as small as 838 square feet and go up to 1,263 square feet. Duplex style units also exist within the village. Every property type includes attached garages, with two car garages standard and three car garages available in larger communities like Discovery Hills. ## What Is the Architectural Style in The Crossing? The Crossing's homes follow a Southwestern Craftsman design that was popular in Las Vegas during the mid to late 1990s. All homes in the village were built between 1994 and 2002, creating a cohesive architectural look throughout the community. Exterior features typically include stucco finishes, tile roofing, and earth tone color palettes that complement the desert landscape. The consistent build era means the neighborhood has a unified streetscape, and mature trees and landscaping add character that newer Summerlin villages have not yet developed. ## How Do Home Sizes Compare Across The Crossing? The smallest homes in The Crossing are the condos at Red Bluffs, starting at 838 square feet. The largest are the Discovery Hills single family homes, reaching 3,200 square feet with five bedrooms and three car garages. In between, buyers can find Aspenglen single family homes (1,800 to 2,200 square feet), Sunset Mesa townhomes (1,200 to 1,800 square feet), and Pacific Vintage townhomes (1,204 to 1,859 square feet). Prices follow a similar range, from the mid $280,000s for condos up to $600,000 or more for the largest single family homes.
