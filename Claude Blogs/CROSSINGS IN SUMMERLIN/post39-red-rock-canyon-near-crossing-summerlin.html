# Red Rock Canyon Near The Crossing Summerlin Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is located just 10 to 15 minutes from The Crossing in Summerlin, making it one of the most accessible outdoor destinations for residents of this community. The 195,819 acre conservation area offers a 13 mile scenic drive, over 30 miles of hiking trails, rock climbing, horseback riding, and mountain biking against a backdrop of dramatic red sandstone formations. ## What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon? The 13 mile scenic loop drive is the most popular way to experience Red Rock Canyon, winding past towering sandstone cliffs and desert landscapes with multiple pulloff viewpoints along the route. Hikers can choose from more than 30 miles of trails ranging from easy nature walks to strenuous canyon scrambles. Rock climbing draws enthusiasts from around the world to Red Rock's sandstone walls, and equestrian trails offer a unique way to explore the backcountry. Mountain biking is permitted on select trails, adding another option for outdoor recreation just minutes from The Crossing. ## Do You Need a Reservation for Red Rock Canyon? Timed entry reservations are required from October 1 through May 31 each year, covering the peak visitation season when cooler temperatures make the canyon most comfortable. During summer months, reservations are not required, but daytime heat limits most activity to early morning hours. Residents of The Crossing benefit from the short drive, which allows for a quick sunrise hike before the canyon gets crowded and the temperature climbs. Planning ahead and booking a timed entry slot online is the best way to guarantee access during the busy season. ## How Close Is Red Rock Canyon to The Crossing? The drive from The Crossing to the Red Rock Canyon visitor center takes approximately 10 to 15 minutes via West Charleston Boulevard. This proximity means a morning hike at Calico Tanks or an evening scenic drive can easily fit into a weekday schedule without feeling like a major outing.
