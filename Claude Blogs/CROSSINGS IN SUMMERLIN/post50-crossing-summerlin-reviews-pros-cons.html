# The Crossing Summerlin Reviews: Pros and Cons The Crossing in Summerlin earns consistently positive reviews from residents, with Nextdoor feedback highlighting the village as clean, quiet, peaceful, family friendly, and well maintained. Like any community, The Crossing has trade offs worth understanding before you buy. ## What Do Residents Love About The Crossing? The most frequently cited positives center on lifestyle and convenience. Residents praise the village for its parks (Crossing Park spans 12 acres and Oxford Park covers 7 acres), walkability, dog friendliness, and friendly neighbors. The schools serving The Crossing earn strong marks: Bonner Elementary holds a 9 out of 10 rating, Rogich Middle School ranks number one in the Clark County School District, and Palo Verde High School is rated number 19 in Nevada. Having Summerlin Hospital Medical Center located directly within the village provides a level of healthcare access that most Las Vegas neighborhoods simply cannot match. Freeway access via Summerlin Parkway is another frequently mentioned advantage, connecting residents to the rest of the valley quickly. ## What Are the Drawbacks of Living in The Crossing? The Crossing's homes were built between 1994 and 2002, which means there is no new construction available. Buyers looking for modern floor plans, smart home wiring, or energy efficient building standards will need to budget for updates. The village includes 13 subdivisions with a mix of single family homes, condos, and townhomes, so the architectural style leans toward late 1990s design. HOA fees include a $74 per month master association fee plus a sub association fee that varies by subdivision, which can add up depending on where you buy. The median home price of $532,000 puts The Crossing in a moderate range for Summerlin, but rising prices (up 22.2% year over year) may challenge some budgets. ## How Does The Crossing Compare to Other Summerlin Villages? The Crossing holds a unique position as the first Summerlin village built north of Summerlin Parkway, established in 1994. Its maturity means tree lined streets, established landscaping, and a settled neighborhood feel that newer villages cannot replicate. The median household income of $91,765 and a homeownership rate of 66.4% reflect a stable, invested community. The on site Crossing Business Center with 1.2 million square feet of office space and the hospital create a self contained village that reduces the need to leave for work or medical care. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose As a Las Vegas Real Estate Expert, Ryan Rose tells buyers that The Crossing rewards those who value substance over flash. The village may not have the newest kitchens or the trendiest amenities, but the combination of location, schools, healthcare, and community character is difficult to beat at this price point. Ryan Rose recommends touring multiple subdivisions within The Crossing, since the experience can vary significantly between a gated single family enclave and a condo community.
