The Crossing and The Arbors are both established Summerlin villages with homes built in the mid 1990s to early 2000s, but they differ in location, pricing, and community character. Here is how these two popular neighborhoods compare for buyers in 2026. ## Where Are The Crossing and The Arbors Located? The Crossing sits north of Summerlin Parkway and was established in 1994 as the first village built in Summerlin North. The Arbors is located south of Summerlin Parkway in the original Summerlin South section. This geographic split matters because Summerlin Parkway serves as a natural dividing line for school zones, commute routes, and neighborhood identity. The Crossing provides slightly quicker access to the 215 Beltway and northwest Las Vegas, while The Arbors offers easier reach to Downtown Summerlin shopping and the southern portion of the valley. ## How Do Home Prices Compare? The Crossing's median home price sits at $532,000 as of mid 2025, with an overall range from approximately $287,000 to $600,000 or more across its 13 subdivisions. The Arbors falls within a similar price band, though individual home prices vary based on square footage, lot size, and updates. Both villages feature a mix of single family homes, townhomes, and condos, giving buyers entry points at multiple price levels. Neither village offers new construction, so every purchase is a resale with potential for renovation and value appreciation. ## What About Schools and Amenities? The Crossing feeds into Bonner Elementary (rated 9 out of 10), Rogich Middle School (ranked number one in the Clark County School District), and Palo Verde High School. The Arbors also zones to Palo Verde High, though elementary and middle school assignments differ. The Crossing has a distinct advantage with Summerlin Hospital Medical Center located directly within the village. The Arbors counters with The Arbors Tennis and Play Park, a community recreation facility popular with families. Both villages benefit from Summerlin's trail system, parks, and master planned infrastructure. ## How Do HOA Fees Differ? The Crossing charges a $74 per month master association fee through the Summerlin North Homeowners Association, with sub association fees varying by subdivision. The Arbors operates under the Summerlin South master association, with its own fee structure. Buyers should compare the total monthly obligation (master plus sub HOA) when evaluating specific homes in either village. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose As a Las Vegas Real Estate Expert, Ryan Rose advises buyers that choosing between The Crossing and The Arbors often comes down to which side of Summerlin Parkway fits your daily routine.
