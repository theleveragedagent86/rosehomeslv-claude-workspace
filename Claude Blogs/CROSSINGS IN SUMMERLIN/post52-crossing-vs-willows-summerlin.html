The Crossing and The Willows are neighboring villages in Summerlin North, both built in the late 1990s to early 2000s, but they serve different buyer profiles based on size, pricing, and community scope. Here is a side by side look at what each village offers. ## How Close Are The Crossing and The Willows? Both villages sit north of Summerlin Parkway in the Summerlin North section of the master plan. The Willows is a smaller village located adjacent to The Crossing, making these two communities practically next door to each other. Residents of either village share access to the same freeway corridors, shopping centers, and regional parks. The proximity means daily conveniences are nearly identical, so the decision between these two comes down to housing stock, community size, and specific amenities. ## How Do Home Prices and Housing Compare? The Crossing's median home price reached $532,000 as of mid 2025, with prices ranging from approximately $287,000 to $600,000 or more across 13 subdivisions. The Crossing offers single family homes, condos, and townhomes, giving buyers a broad spectrum of options. The Willows is a smaller village with fewer subdivisions and generally comparable pricing for similar home types. Both communities feature resale homes exclusively, with no new construction available. Buyers looking for variety in floor plans and price points will find more options within The Crossing simply due to its larger footprint. ## What About Schools and Community Amenities? The Crossing feeds into Bonner Elementary (9 out of 10 rating), Rogich Middle School (ranked number one in the Clark County School District), and Palo Verde High School. School assignments for The Willows may overlap at the high school level but can differ for elementary and middle schools, so buyers should verify current zoning. The Crossing's standout amenity is Summerlin Hospital Medical Center, a full service facility located within the village boundaries. The Willows features the Willows Community Center, which serves as a gathering point for neighborhood events and activities. ## How Do HOA Structures Compare? Both villages fall under the Summerlin North master association, which charges $74 per month. Individual sub association fees vary by subdivision in both communities. The Crossing's 13 subdivisions include gated and non gated options, each with different sub HOA fees and rules. The Willows' smaller scale means fewer sub associations, which can simplify the buying process for those who prefer a more straightforward HOA structure. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose As a Las Vegas Real Estate Expert, Ryan Rose notes that The Willows appeals to buyers who want a tighter knit, smaller community feel while staying in Summerlin North.
