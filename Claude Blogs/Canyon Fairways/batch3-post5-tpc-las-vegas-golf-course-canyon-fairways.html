# TPC Las Vegas Golf Course at Canyon Fairways TPC Las Vegas is the championship golf course that borders Canyon Fairways, making it one of the defining features of the community. This PGA Tour-affiliated 18-hole course sits directly adjacent to Canyon Fairways' northeastern perimeter, providing golf-course views for many homeowners and convenient tee-time access for residents who play. ## Course Overview TPC Las Vegas is an 18-hole, par-71 championship course originally designed by Bobby Weed with consultation from PGA champion Raymond Floyd. The course opened in 1996 as TPC at The Canyons and has hosted multiple professional events, including PGA Tour and Korn Ferry Tour tournaments. The layout spans over 7,000 yards from the championship tees and features elevation changes, desert wash areas, and mountain views that take full advantage of the Summerlin landscape. ## Public Access Unlike many courses adjacent to luxury communities, TPC Las Vegas is open to the public. Golfers do not need a membership or community residency to play. This public-access model means the course is professionally maintained to TPC standards while remaining accessible to all skill levels. Green fees vary seasonally, with peak-season rates during the cooler months of October through April and discounted rates during the summer. ## Amenities and Facilities The TPC Las Vegas clubhouse includes a pro shop, practice facilities with a driving range and putting greens, and dining options. The course offers professional instruction, league play, and tournament hosting. For Canyon Fairways residents, the proximity means a warm-up session on the range and a round of golf are just minutes from home, with no significant commute required. ## The TPC Brand TPC, which stands for Tournament Players Club, is the PGA Tour's network of premier golf courses. Living adjacent to a TPC course carries recognition that extends nationally and internationally.
