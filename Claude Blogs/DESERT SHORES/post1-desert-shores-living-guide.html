Desert Shores is a 682 acre master planned community in northwest Las Vegas built around four private residents only lakes, with 3,351 homes spanning 22 sub villages and a median sale price of $510,000. ## What is Desert Shores Las Vegas? Desert Shores sits in the 89128 zip code, bounded by Rampart Boulevard, Cheyenne Avenue, Buffalo Drive, and Lake Mead Boulevard. RA Homes founded the community in 1988, making it one of the original lake oriented neighborhoods in the valley. The community predates Lake Las Vegas and remains the only major Las Vegas neighborhood with four privately owned lakes available exclusively to residents. The four lakes (Jacqueline, Sarah, Maddison, and Lindsey) cover roughly 100 acres of total water surface. Lake Jacqueline is the largest at 24.8 acres and stretches over a mile long. All residents 14 and older receive a digital membership ID that grants access to amenities including paddle boats, kayaks, fishing, the lagoon style swimming pool, and a sand beach at Lagoon Beach Park. ## Who lives in Desert Shores? The community has a population of about 7,564 residents with a median age of 50 and a median household income of $70,641. There are no age restrictions, so the neighborhood draws a mix of families, professionals, and empty nesters who want lakefront living without leaving the city. Some sub villages like Mar a Lago feature 24 hour guard gated entries, while other portions of the community remain open access. ## How does Desert Shores compare to nearby neighborhoods? Desert Shores costs roughly one third less than Summerlin West, where the median price climbs to about $840,000. Homes here range from condos starting near $190,000 to lakefront single family homes that exceed $2.5 million. The drive to the Strip takes 12 to 20 minutes, and Centennial Hills Hospital sits just minutes away on Durango Drive. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose helps buyers understand the layered HOA structure, the lakefront premium, and which sub villages offer the best long term value.
