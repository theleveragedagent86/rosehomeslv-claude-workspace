Fishing in Desert Shores is catch and release on four privately stocked lakes featuring bass, bluegill, channel catfish, black crappie, red ear sunfish, white amur, and several baitfish species, available dawn until dusk to DSCA members and up to four guests per household. ## What fish species are stocked in the Desert Shores lakes? The DSCA stocks the four private lakes annually each spring. The current stock list includes largemouth bass, bluegill, channel catfish, black crappie, red ear sunfish, white amur (grass carp used for vegetation control), goldfish, gambusia, and flathead minnows. Bass, bluegill, and catfish are the most commonly caught sport fish. All four lakes (Jacqueline, Sarah, Maddison, and Lindsey) share the same stock and the same rules, so anglers can fish any of them with the same setup. ## What are the Desert Shores fishing rules and hours? Fishing inside Desert Shores is catch and release only. Anglers may use one pole per person and must use non barbed hooks to reduce harm during release. Fishing hours run from dawn until dusk daily. Approved bait includes red worms, night crawlers, mealworms, terrestrial insects (crickets and grasshoppers), salmon eggs, marshmallows, and catfish stink bait. Live freshwater fish as bait are prohibited. Households can bring up to four fishing guests at a time. A Nevada state fishing license is not required as long as the angler is a DSCA member or invited guest with proper credentials.
