Marche Bacchus is a French bistro and wine shop at 2620 Regatta Drive in Desert Shores, with over 950 wine labels and the only lakefront French dining patio in Las Vegas. It has been named a Top 10 Las Vegas restaurant. ## What makes Marche Bacchus special? Marche Bacchus combines a working wine shop with a full service French bistro. You can browse the wine wall, pick any bottle, and drink it at your table for a small corkage fee. The selection covers more than 950 labels, ranging from approachable everyday bottles to rare French and Burgundian finds. The restaurant sits directly on Lake Jacqueline, with a long outdoor patio facing the water. It is the only French restaurant on a lakefront in Las Vegas proper. The setting feels closer to Provence or Sonoma than the Strip. ## What is the menu like? The kitchen runs classic French bistro fare with a California sensibility. Expect escargots, steak frites, mussels, duck confit, and fresh fish. The Sunday brunch is a longtime favorite, with a more casual menu and the same lakefront seating. Pricing sits in the $$$ range, which is fair for the quality and the wine pairing options. Reservations through OpenTable are recommended, especially for weekend evenings and Sunday brunch. ## Why Desert Shores residents love it Many Desert Shores homes are walkable to Marche Bacchus, which is unusual for an upscale Las Vegas restaurant. Residents stroll the lake loop trail, grab dinner, and walk home along the water. It is a small luxury that defines the neighborhood lifestyle.
