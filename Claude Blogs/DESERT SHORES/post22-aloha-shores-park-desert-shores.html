Aloha Shores Park is a 5 acre public park just outside Desert Shores at 7550 Sauer Street, with tennis, pickleball, volleyball, bocce, and a paved walking path. ## What is Aloha Shores Park? Aloha Shores Park is a City of Las Vegas public park located right next to Desert Shores. The address is 7550 Sauer Street, a quick drive or even a short walk from many homes inside the community. Five acres give the park enough room for sport courts, a playground, and shaded picnic space. This is the public companion to Desert Shores' private amenities. Anyone can use it, no membership needed. Families, retirees, and league players all show up here on a regular basis. ## What courts and activities are available? The athletic options are the main draw. Aloha Shores has 2 free, unlit tennis courts and 4 outdoor pickleball courts with permanent lines, all first-come, first-served. There are also volleyball, bocce, and horseshoe areas, plus a playground for younger kids. A paved walking path winds around the perimeter, perfect for an easy lap or pushing a stroller. Picnic tables and a BBQ grill round out the setup. The picnic area can hold up to 100 people, but groups need a reservation. ## How do you reserve the picnic area? Group reservations for the Aloha Shores picnic area go through the City of Las Vegas at 702-229-6011. Drop in play on the courts does not require a reservation.
