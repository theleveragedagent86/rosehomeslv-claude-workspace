Desert Shores is a dog friendly community. The lakes, walking trails, and 4.5 mile Loop draw dog walkers daily. There is no dedicated dog park within Desert Shores itself, but options sit nearby. ## Where do residents walk dogs in Desert Shores? The 8.5 miles of internal trails are the main dog walking network. The 4.5 mile Desert Shores Loop is the most popular path. It circles the lakes and connects most of the neighborhood. Lagoon Beach Park, Aloha Shores Park, and the smaller pocket parks all welcome leashed dogs. Lake access is governed by the master HOA. Dogs cannot enter the water and the lakes themselves are not a swim area. Trails and paved paths around the lakes are the right place for daily walks. Residents often run into the same dogs and owners every morning, which is part of the appeal. ## Where is the nearest dog park? There is no fenced off leash dog park inside Desert Shores. The closest option is Desert Breeze Park in Spring Valley. It is a short drive south and includes separate small and large dog areas. Some residents also use the Pueblo Park trail, a 1.5 mile dog friendly path nearby. Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate specialist, notes that the lack of an internal dog park rarely bothers buyers. Most pick Desert Shores precisely because daily walks happen on the lake loop, not at a fenced park. ## Local Insight from Ryan Rose In Ryan Rose's experience showing Desert Shores homes, dog owners often weigh trail access more heavily than dog park availability. The Loop is the social hub for residents and their dogs. Lakefront streets and Village adjacent homes give the easiest direct trail access.
