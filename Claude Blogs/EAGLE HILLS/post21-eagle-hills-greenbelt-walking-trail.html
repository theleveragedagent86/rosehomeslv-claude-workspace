What Is the Eagle Hills Greenbelt Walking Trail?

The Eagle Hills greenbelt walking trail is a landscaped walking path approximately half a mile long that winds through the heart of this guard gated Summerlin community. It connects homes directly to the neighborhood's 3 acre central park, playground, pavilions, tennis courts, and basketball court.

Where Does the Trail Go?

The greenbelt trail runs through the center of Eagle Hills, following the community's central green corridor. Many of the 160 custom homes back up to this greenbelt, giving residents direct access from their backyards. The path links key amenities together so residents can walk from one end of the neighborhood to the other without ever stepping onto a street.

The trail surface is flat and well maintained, making it accessible for all ages and fitness levels. Mature trees line much of the route, providing shade during warmer months and reinforcing the lush, tree lined character that sets Eagle Hills apart from the surrounding desert landscape.

Who Uses the Greenbelt Trail?

Morning walkers, joggers, dog owners, and families with children all use the greenbelt daily. Because Eagle Hills is guard gated with only 160 homes, the trail never feels crowded. It provides a safe, low traffic loop for seniors staying active, parents pushing strollers, and pet owners enjoying a quick leash walk before or after work.

The greenbelt also serves as a social connector. Neighbors regularly cross paths on the trail, and the adjacent pavilions offer shaded spots for conversation or small gatherings. It creates a walkable, community oriented lifestyle that is rare in Las Vegas.
