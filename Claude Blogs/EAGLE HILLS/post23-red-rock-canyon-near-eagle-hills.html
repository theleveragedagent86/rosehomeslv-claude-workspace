How Far Is Red Rock Canyon from Eagle Hills?

Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is approximately 10 to 15 minutes by car from Eagle Hills in Summerlin, making it one of the closest world class outdoor destinations available to any guard gated community in Las Vegas. The conservation area features a 13 mile scenic drive, dozens of hiking trails, rock climbing routes, and abundant wildlife viewing.

What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon?

The 13 mile scenic loop drive is the most popular starting point, offering pull offs and trailheads along the route. Calico Tanks Trail ranks as one of the most popular hikes in the Las Vegas Valley, with moderate difficulty, hidden seasonal pools, and panoramic views extending to the Las Vegas Strip from the summit. Turtlehead Peak provides a more challenging option for experienced hikers seeking elevation gain and 360 degree views.

Family friendly trails near the visitor center offer shorter, easier options for children and casual walkers. Rock climbing routes attract climbers from around the world, and the canyon's sandstone formations create a dramatic backdrop for photography and nature observation. The area is also dog friendly on leash, so pet owners from Eagle Hills can bring their dogs along on most trails.

Do You Need a Reservation?

Timed entry reservations are required for the scenic drive from October 1 through May 31, between 8 AM and 5 PM. Reservations can be made through Recreation.gov or by calling 877 444 6777. During summer months, reservations are not required, though early morning visits are recommended to avoid peak heat. The proximity of Eagle Hills to the canyon entrance means residents can easily time visits to catch early morning or late afternoon light.
