Is Eagle Hills Dog Friendly?

Eagle Hills is a dog friendly guard gated community in Summerlin with an internal greenbelt walking trail ideal for daily leash walks, plus quick access to several nearby parks and off leash dog parks. Pet owners in Eagle Hills benefit from low traffic streets, shaded tree lined sidewalks, and multiple outdoor options within minutes of the gate.

Where Can You Walk Your Dog Near Eagle Hills?

Inside the community, the half mile greenbelt trail offers a convenient, shaded route for morning and evening dog walks. The path winds through the 3 acre central park and connects homes to pavilions, playgrounds, and sport courts. Because only 160 homes share this space, the trail stays uncrowded and calm for dogs of all temperaments.

Just outside the gate, Bruce Trent Park spans over 50 acres with a dedicated walk and jog track, open grassy areas, and plenty of room for on leash exploration. Willows Park, also nearby, features a 12.7 acre linear park with a trail that provides additional walking options for dogs and their owners.

Are There Off Leash Dog Parks Nearby?

Barkin' Basin Dog Park in Summerlin is the closest dedicated off leash facility. It features three separate fenced areas, benches, trees, drinking fountains, and water spigots for dogs. The park is closed Mondays and Thursdays from 11:30 AM to 4:00 PM for maintenance. Kellogg Zaher Dog Park also serves the area, offering separate sections for small and large dogs along with waste stations and seating.

For a more adventurous outing, Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area allows dogs on leash on most trails. Popular dog friendly hikes include Calico Tanks and Ice Box Canyon, both located about 10 to 15 minutes from Eagle Hills by car.
