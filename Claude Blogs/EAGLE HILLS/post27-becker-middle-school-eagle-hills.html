What Is Ernest Becker Middle School?

Ernest Becker Middle School is the zoned public middle school for Eagle Hills in Summerlin, serving grades 6 through 8 with a GreatSchools rating of 6 out of 10 and a Niche grade of B. Students from William Lummis Elementary transition directly to Becker, keeping Eagle Hills families within a consistent public school pipeline through eighth grade.

How Does Becker Middle School Perform Academically?

Becker Middle School ranks number 70 among Nevada middle schools according to Niche. Current proficiency data shows 24% of students meeting math proficiency standards and 47% meeting reading proficiency standards. While these numbers reflect the broader challenges facing middle school education statewide, the reading scores show a solid foundation for students heading into high school at Palo Verde.

The school operates within the Clark County School District and follows Nevada Academic Content Standards for grades 6 through 8. Becker provides the bridge between Lummis Elementary and Palo Verde High School, both of which earn higher ratings. Families considering Eagle Hills should view Becker as part of a complete K through 12 public school path that ends with one of the strongest high schools in the state.

What Programs and Activities Does Becker Offer?

Becker Middle School provides core academics alongside elective and extracurricular programming typical of Clark County middle schools. The campus serves the broader Summerlin community, drawing students from several well regarded elementary feeder schools. This creates a diverse student body and social environment that prepares students for the larger high school setting at Palo Verde.
