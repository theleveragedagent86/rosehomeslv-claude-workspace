What Is TPC Summerlin?

TPC Summerlin is a private championship golf course adjacent to Eagle Hills, featuring a par 72, 7,243 yard layout designed by Bobby Weed with PGA legend Fuzzy Zoeller. It is the home course of the Shriners Children's Open on the PGA Tour, the first Audubon certified golf course in Nevada, and has been named one of America's best golf courses by Golf Digest.

What Makes TPC Summerlin a Championship Course?

The Bobby Weed design stretches across 7,243 yards of manicured desert terrain, blending strategic bunkering with the natural Summerlin landscape. As a TPC Network property, the course maintains tour level conditioning year round. The Shriners Children's Open brings PGA Tour professionals to this course every fall, giving Eagle Hills residents a front row seat to world class professional golf literally next door.

The Audubon certification distinguishes TPC Summerlin as an environmentally responsible facility, the first course in Nevada to earn that designation. This commitment to sustainable land management aligns with the broader conservation efforts throughout the Summerlin master plan and adds another layer of prestige to the property.

How Close Is TPC Summerlin to Eagle Hills?

TPC Summerlin sits directly adjacent to Eagle Hills, making it one of the closest golf course relationships in all of Summerlin. Some Eagle Hills homes offer views of the course, and residents who hold memberships can access the club without any meaningful commute. This adjacency also means Eagle Hills homeowners enjoy the aesthetic benefit of maintained green space bordering their community year round.
