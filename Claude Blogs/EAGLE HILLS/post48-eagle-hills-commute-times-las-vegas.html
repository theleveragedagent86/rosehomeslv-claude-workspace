What Are the Commute Times from Eagle Hills?

Eagle Hills offers some of the most manageable commute times in the Las Vegas valley, with the Strip reachable in 17 to 20 minutes, downtown Las Vegas in about 15 minutes, and Harry Reid International Airport in roughly 20 minutes. These drive times make Eagle Hills a practical home base for professionals, travelers, and anyone who values both Summerlin quiet and city access.

How Do You Get to the Las Vegas Strip from Eagle Hills?

The most common route runs east on Summerlin Parkway to US 95, then south on I-15 to the Strip. This covers approximately 12 miles and typically takes 17 to 20 minutes outside of rush hour. During peak commute windows between 7:00 and 9:00 a.m. or 4:00 and 6:00 p.m., traffic on I-15 and US 95 can add up to 30 percent more travel time. Even with congestion, most Eagle Hills residents report reaching Strip area workplaces in under 30 minutes.

What About Downtown Las Vegas and the Airport?

Downtown Las Vegas is a quick 15 minute drive from Eagle Hills via Highway 613, which feeds directly into Highway 11 for a nearly straight shot. Harry Reid International Airport sits approximately 15.6 miles away and takes about 20 minutes to reach using I-215 and I-15. These freeway connections also put Red Rock Canyon just 10 to 15 minutes west, giving Eagle Hills residents fast access to outdoor recreation without any freeway driving at all.

How Does Eagle Hills Compare to Other Summerlin Neighborhoods?

Eagle Hills sits in the 89134 zip code on the eastern edge of Summerlin, which actually provides a slight commute advantage over communities deeper in the master plan. Ryan Rose points out that this positioning, combined with direct Summerlin Parkway access, gives Eagle Hills homeowners shorter drive times than many neighboring subdivisions while still enjoying the full benefits of guard gated Summerlin living with 24 hour security and resort style amenities.

The bottom line for commuters is simple. Eagle Hills puts you close enough to work and travel without sacrificing the peaceful, secure residential environment Summerlin is known for.
