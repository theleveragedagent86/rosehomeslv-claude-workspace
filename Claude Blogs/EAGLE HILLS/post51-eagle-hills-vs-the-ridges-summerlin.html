How Does Eagle Hills Compare to The Ridges in Summerlin?

Eagle Hills offers guard gated luxury living at a significantly lower price point than The Ridges, making it the stronger value play for buyers who want custom homes, security, and proximity to TPC Summerlin without crossing into ultra luxury territory. Both communities rank among the most desirable in Summerlin, but they serve different buyer profiles.

What Is the Price Difference Between Eagle Hills and The Ridges?

Eagle Hills homes range from $800,000 to $5,000,000 with most falling between $1M and $3M. The Ridges starts around $1,500,000 and climbs past $15,000,000 for premium estates. Buyers looking for a spacious custom home on a quarter acre to two thirds acre lot will find Eagle Hills delivers that lifestyle at roughly half the entry cost of The Ridges. Both communities include 24 hour guard gated access, but Eagle Hills accomplishes this with a tight knit 160 home layout compared to The Ridges' approximately 900 homesites spread across multiple sub communities.

How Do the Communities Differ in Age and Style?

Eagle Hills was built between 1995 and 2000, with homes ranging from 2,924 to 14,000 square feet. The Ridges features newer construction from 2006 to present, with contemporary and modern desert architecture dominating the landscape. Buyers who prefer established, mature landscaping and traditional custom home designs will gravitate toward Eagle Hills. Those seeking ultra modern finishes and newer builds will lean toward The Ridges. Eagle Hills homes on larger lots offer renovation potential that can bring interiors up to current standards while maintaining the advantage of a lower purchase price.

Which Community Offers Better Amenities and Location?

Eagle Hills includes a private 3 acre park with two tennis courts, a basketball court, playground, pavilions, and greenbelt walking trails all within its gates. The Ridges provides access to hiking trails and sits near Bear's Best Golf Course. Eagle Hills holds a distinct advantage with its direct adjacency to TPC Summerlin, home of the PGA Tour Shriners Children's Open. Ryan Rose frequently advises buyers that Eagle Hills represents one of the best value propositions in all of Summerlin because it pairs guard gated security, generous lot sizes, and country club proximity at a fraction of what comparable features cost in The Ridges.
