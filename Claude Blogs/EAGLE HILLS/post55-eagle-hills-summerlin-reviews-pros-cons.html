What Are the Pros and Cons of Living in Eagle Hills Summerlin?

Eagle Hills earns consistently positive reviews from residents who value guard gated security, spacious custom homes, and a tight knit 160 home community adjacent to TPC Summerlin. Like any neighborhood, it comes with trade offs that buyers should weigh before committing.

What Do Residents Love About Eagle Hills?

The top advantages residents cite include 24 hour guard gated security with controlled access, custom homes ranging from 2,924 to 14,000 square feet on lots up to two thirds of an acre, and a private 3 acre park with tennis courts, basketball, playground, and greenbelt trails. The community's location next to TPC Summerlin gives golf enthusiasts walkable access to a PGA Tour venue. Schools serving Eagle Hills score well, with Lummis Elementary rated 9 out of 10 and Palo Verde High rated 8 out of 10. The 89134 zip code places residents 17 to 20 minutes from the Las Vegas Strip while maintaining a quiet, residential feel. Families, retirees, and professionals all find the community appealing for these reasons.

What Are the Drawbacks of Eagle Hills?

The most common considerations include HOA fees totaling approximately $707 per month when combining the Eagle Hills HOA at $435 to $536 per month with the Summerlin master association fee. Homes were built between 1995 and 2000, meaning some properties may require updates to kitchens, bathrooms, or mechanical systems. The community's 160 home size creates extremely limited inventory, which benefits sellers but makes it challenging for buyers to find available properties. Lot sizes, while generous, vary significantly, so not every home offers the full two thirds acre experience. Buyers should also factor in that the established landscaping, while beautiful, may carry higher maintenance costs than newer desert modern communities.

Is Eagle Hills Worth It Overall?

Ryan Rose consistently sees Eagle Hills attract buyers who have compared it against The Ridges, Canyon Fairways, and other Summerlin luxury options and concluded that Eagle Hills delivers the best combination of value, security, and space. The community's consistent appreciation, low turnover rate, and strong school zoning make it a reliable long term investment. Buyers who prioritize newer construction or lower HOA fees may find better fits elsewhere, but those who want established luxury with proven value retention will find Eagle Hills hard to beat in the Summerlin market.
