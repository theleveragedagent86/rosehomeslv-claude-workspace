Eagle Hills homes were built by various private custom builders between 1995 and 2000, with no single production builder responsible for the community. This approach gave each homeowner full creative control, resulting in 160 individually designed residences that span contemporary, classic, and Mediterranean architectural styles. ## Who Built the Homes in Eagle Hills? Unlike most Summerlin neighborhoods that were developed by one or two production builders, Eagle Hills was established as a custom home community from the start. Each of the 160 lots was sold to individual buyers who then hired their own architects and builders to design and construct their homes. This means the community has no repeating floor plans and no cookie cutter designs. The result is a neighborhood where every home reflects the personal taste and vision of its original owner. ## What Architectural Styles Are Found in Eagle Hills? Three primary styles dominate Eagle Hills: contemporary, classic, and Mediterranean. Contemporary homes in the community often feature clean lines, open floor plans, and walls of glass that connect indoor and outdoor living spaces. Classic designs lean toward traditional proportions with formal living areas and symmetrical facades. Mediterranean style residences showcase stucco exteriors, tile roofing, arched doorways, and courtyard layouts. Ryan Rose notes that this variety of styles is one of Eagle Hills' strongest selling points, as buyers can find a home that matches their aesthetic preferences without leaving the community. ## Are There Any New Homes Being Built in Eagle Hills? No. Eagle Hills is fully built out, and all 160 homes were completed by 2000. There are no vacant lots remaining and no new construction planned. Every purchase in Eagle Hills is a resale transaction, which means buyers are acquiring established custom homes on mature, landscaped lots. The lack of new construction also means the community's character and density are permanently set, with no risk of future development changing the neighborhood's feel.
