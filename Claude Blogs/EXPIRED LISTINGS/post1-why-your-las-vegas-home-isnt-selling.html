Is Your Price Scaring Buyers Away?

Overpricing is the number one reason homes don't sell in Las Vegas. Buyers today are comparing your home against 13,000+ competing listings, and they know what things should cost. Homes priced just 10% above market value take five times longer to sell.

With median home prices sitting around $470,000 and nearly 25% of sellers already dropping their price, the data tells a clear story. If your home isn't attracting showings or offers, the price is the first place to look.

Are Your Listing Photos Costing You Thousands?

Professional photography isn't a luxury. It's a baseline expectation. Homes with professional photos sell 32% faster and for $3,000 to $11,000 more than homes with amateur shots. If your listing featured dark, blurry, or poorly composed images, buyers scrolled right past you.

Video content takes it even further. Listings with video tours generate 403% more inquiries than those without. In a market where 7,502 homes are sitting without offers, standing out visually is everything.

Could Your Agent Be the Problem?

Not every agent markets the same way. Some list your home, put a sign in the yard, and wait. That approach doesn't work when inventory is up 37% year over year and Las Vegas ranks #2 nationally for the biggest swing from a seller's market to a buyer's market.

The right agent brings a strategy that includes pricing analysis, staging recommendations, professional media, and aggressive digital marketing. If your previous agent didn't offer those things, that gap likely cost you the sale.
