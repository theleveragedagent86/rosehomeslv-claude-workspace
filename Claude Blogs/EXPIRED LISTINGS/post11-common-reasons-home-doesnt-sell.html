The Price Problem

Overpricing is the number one reason homes don't sell. Research shows that homes priced just 10% above market value take five times longer to sell and ultimately close for more than 10% below their actual value. In Las Vegas, nearly 25% of sellers have already had to reduce their price. Buyers today are shopping against 13,000+ active listings, and they can spot an inflated price instantly.

Marketing That Misses the Mark

Poor photography, missing video tours, and weak online presence can sink a listing before it even gets a showing. Homes with professional photos sell 32% faster and for $3,000 to $11,000 more. Listings with video content generate 403% more inquiries. If your marketing didn't include these elements, buyers simply never noticed your home.

Condition, Access, and Timing

Deferred maintenance, pet odors, and dated finishes make buyers hesitate. Restrictive showing windows limit the pool of people who can actually walk through your door. And in Las Vegas, listing during July and August means competing against 14% to 15% fewer active buyers due to extreme heat. Even location factors like busy roads or power lines can play a role, though these are harder to change.

The Agent Factor and Market Shifts

An out of area agent or one without a proactive marketing plan can quietly cost you the sale. When inventory is up 37% year over year and Las Vegas ranks second nationally for the biggest swing from a seller's market to a buyer's market, you need an agent who brings strategy, not just a yard sign. Add in mortgage rates sitting between 6.24% and 6.73%, and 1 in 5 deals falling through before closing, and the margin for error is razor thin.
