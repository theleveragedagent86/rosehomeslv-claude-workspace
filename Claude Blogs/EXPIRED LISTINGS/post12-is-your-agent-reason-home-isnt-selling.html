Signs Your Agent Fell Short

Not all agents approach a listing the same way. Some put a sign in the yard, enter the home into the MLS, and wait for results. In a market where inventory is up 37% year over year and over 13,000 homes are competing for buyers, that passive approach doesn't cut it. If your agent was unresponsive to showing requests, slow to return calls, or failed to provide regular updates, those are red flags that directly impact your sale.

Out of Area and Out of Touch

An agent who doesn't specialize in Las Vegas may not understand what local buyers expect. They might price your home based on broad regional data instead of neighborhood specifics. They might not know that July and August see a 14% to 15% drop in buyer activity because of the heat, or that Las Vegas now ranks second nationally for the biggest shift from a seller's market to a buyer's market. Local knowledge isn't a bonus. It's a requirement.

Marketing Makes or Breaks the Sale

The right agent invests in professional photography, video walkthroughs, and targeted digital marketing. Homes with professional photos sell 32% faster and for thousands more. Listings with video generate 403% more inquiries. If your listing went live with phone photos and a generic description, your agent left money on the table. A proactive agent also prepares staging recommendations, pricing analysis, and a plan for the first two weeks on market, which are the most critical window for generating interest.
