Should You Relist Immediately or Wait?

Jumping back on the market the next day might feel productive, but it can actually work against you. A strategic break of 30 to 46 days off the MLS allows your days on market counter to reset. That matters because buyers and their agents pay close attention to how long a home has been listed.

Use that time wisely. Get a fresh comparative market analysis, address any feedback from previous showings, and make targeted improvements that deliver real return on investment.

What Changes Will Actually Make a Difference?

Start with pricing. The Las Vegas market has shifted significantly, with inventory up 37% year over year and 7,502 homes currently sitting without offers. A price that made sense six months ago may be out of touch with today's reality.

Next, look at presentation. Professional staging delivers a 550% return on investment, and fresh paint adds an average of $7,500 in perceived value. Curb appeal improvements can return 238% on every dollar invested. These aren't cosmetic extras. They're the changes that move buyers from browsing to booking a showing.

Do You Need a New Agent?

This is worth honest consideration. If your previous agent didn't provide regular market updates, professional photography, video marketing, or a clear pricing strategy, those gaps likely contributed to the outcome. In a market where 1 in 5 deals are falling through, you need someone with a specific plan for homes that didn't sell the first time.

Ask prospective agents what they would do differently. The right agent will show you data, not just promises.
