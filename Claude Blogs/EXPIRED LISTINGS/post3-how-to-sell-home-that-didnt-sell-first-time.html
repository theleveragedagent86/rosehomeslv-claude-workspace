How Do You Set the Right Price the Second Time?

Pricing is the most common mistake, and fixing it is the most impactful change you can make. Start with a fresh comparative market analysis that reflects current conditions, not what the market looked like three or six months ago. Las Vegas median home prices sit around $470,000, but your neighborhood and property type matter more than the citywide number.

Remember that nearly 25% of Las Vegas sellers have already had to reduce their price. Pricing competitively from day one on your relaunch prevents that cycle from repeating.

What Improvements Give You the Biggest Return?

You don't need a full renovation. Focus on changes with proven returns. Professional staging delivers $23.34 for every $1 invested. Curb appeal upgrades, where $3,500 spent can translate to $12,000 in added value, are another high impact move. Fresh paint, deep cleaning, and decluttering make your home feel larger, brighter, and more inviting.

Professional photography is non negotiable. Homes with pro photos sell 32% faster and for thousands more. If your first listing used phone photos, that alone may have cost you the sale.

Should You Take Your Home Off the Market Before Relisting?

Yes, in most cases. A strategic break of 30 to 46 days off the MLS resets your days on market counter. Buyers and their agents notice when a home has been listed for a long time, and a high day count signals that something might be wrong. Resetting that counter gives your home a fresh start.

Use that break to complete improvements, update your photos, and develop a stronger marketing plan. When you come back on the market, everything should feel new.
