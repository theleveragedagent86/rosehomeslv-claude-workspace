What Counts as a Buyer Incentive

Buyer incentives go beyond dropping your asking price. Common options include offering credits toward closing costs, paying for a mortgage rate buydown, covering the cost of specific repairs, or providing flexible move-in timelines. Each one solves a different problem for a different type of buyer. Closing cost credits help buyers who are cash-strapped after their down payment. A rate buydown reduces their monthly payment for the first few years. Repair credits remove inspection-related anxiety. Flexible timelines appeal to buyers juggling the sale of their own home.

Why Incentives Matter in This Market

Las Vegas is currently ranked as the number two buyer's market in the country. That means buyers have options and leverage. One in five deals is falling through, often over financing issues. In that environment, an incentive that directly addresses a buyer's biggest obstacle can be the difference between a closed sale and another failed attempt. A $5,000 closing cost credit might keep a deal together that would otherwise collapse, while a comparable price cut might not move the needle at all.

Incentives vs. Price Reductions

Here's the key difference. A price reduction lowers your bottom line permanently and affects your comparable sales for the neighborhood. An incentive costs you money at closing but keeps your sale price intact on paper. This matters for appraisals too. If your home appraises at $400,000 and you offer a $7,000 closing cost credit, the recorded sale price stays at $400,000. If you had simply dropped your price by $7,000, that lower number becomes a comp for every home in your area.

Which Incentives Work Best Right Now

Rate buydowns are especially effective when mortgage rates are sitting between 6% and 7%. A seller-funded 2-1 buydown can drop a buyer's rate by two points in the first year and one point in the second year, making the monthly payment significantly more manageable. Closing cost credits remain the most popular incentive because they reduce the cash a buyer needs to bring to the table. Repair credits work well for older homes where buyers are nervous about condition but the home is fundamentally sound.

How Las Vegas Sellers Are Using Incentives

With over 13,000 active listings competing for attention, Las Vegas sellers who offer targeted incentives are separating themselves from the crowd. The most effective approach is identifying why buyers walked away during your first listing and then choosing an incentive that directly addresses that issue. If buyers kept dropping out over inspection items, a repair credit or home warranty solves that. If financing kept falling apart, a rate buydown or closing cost assistance gets deals to the closing table.
