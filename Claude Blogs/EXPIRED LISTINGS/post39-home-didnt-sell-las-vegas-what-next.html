Step One: Diagnose Before You Relist

The worst thing you can do is immediately relist your Las Vegas home with the same price, the same photos, and a different agent. With a median price of $470,000 and homes averaging 55 to 62 days on market, your home has already been seen by most active buyers. If they passed the first time, relisting without changes won't bring them back. Start with an honest assessment. Was the price right? Were the photos professional? Did your agent actively market the property or just wait for the MLS to do the work? These answers determine your next move.

Step Two: Reprice Based on Today's Market

Nearly 25% of Las Vegas sellers have already reduced their price, and the homes that sell are the ones priced competitively from day one of the relist. Las Vegas ranks #2 nationally for the biggest swing from a seller's market to a buyer's market. That means the price that would have worked 12 months ago may be 5% to 10% too high today. A fresh comparative market analysis that accounts for current inventory levels and recent closed sales within your specific neighborhood is essential. Pricing to where the market is heading, not where it was, is the strategy that works.

Step Three: Upgrade Your Marketing

With over 13,000 active listings in the valley, your home needs to stand out in the first three seconds a buyer sees it online. Professional photography, video walkthroughs, and targeted digital advertising aren't extras. They're requirements. Listings with video tours generate 403% more inquiries. If your first listing didn't include these, that's likely a major reason it failed. Consider staging as well, even partial staging of key rooms can make the difference between a scroll-past and a showing request.

Step Four: Time Your Relist Strategically

Don't relist on the same day your listing expires. Take at least two to three weeks off market to reset the days on market counter and give yourself time to make changes. In the Las Vegas market, spring remains the strongest selling season, so if your listing just expired in a slower period, timing your relaunch to catch peak buyer activity can make a real difference. With mortgage rates at 6.24% to 6.73% and 1 in 5 deals falling through, you want every advantage you can get.
