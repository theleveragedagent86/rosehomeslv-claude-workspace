What Questions Should You Ask a New Agent?

Start with this: "What would you do differently?" Any agent worth hiring should be able to review your previous listing and point to specific areas that need improvement. Vague answers like "I'll market it better" aren't enough. You want to hear concrete plans around pricing strategy, professional photography, video marketing, staging, and digital advertising.

Ask about their experience with homes that didn't sell the first time. This is a specialized situation that requires a different approach than a standard listing. An agent who has successfully relisted and sold previously failed listings understands the nuances that matter.

What Red Flags Should You Watch For?

Be cautious of agents who tell you only what you want to hear, especially about price. If a new agent suggests listing at the same price that didn't work before without a compelling reason, that's a warning sign. The Las Vegas market has nearly 25% of sellers reducing their price, and an honest agent will give you the real numbers, even if they're not what you hoped.

Also watch for agents who lack a digital presence. In a market with 13,000+ competing listings, your home needs to be everywhere online. If a prospective agent doesn't use professional photography, video tours, social media marketing, and targeted advertising, keep looking.

How Do You Know When You've Found the Right Agent?

The right agent will present a detailed marketing plan before you sign anything. They'll show you comparable sales data, explain their pricing recommendation with evidence, and walk you through exactly how they'll present your home to buyers. They should also outline a timeline, including whether a strategic market break makes sense for resetting your days on market.

Trust your instincts, but verify with data. A great agent welcomes tough questions and responds with transparency.
