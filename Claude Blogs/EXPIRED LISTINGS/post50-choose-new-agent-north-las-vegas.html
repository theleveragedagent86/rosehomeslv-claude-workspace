Your Agent Must Understand the New Construction Problem

North Las Vegas is ground zero for new home construction in the valley. Lennar, KB Home, and other builders are actively selling in Aliante, Skye Canyon, and the northern corridor with incentives that include rate buydowns, closing cost credits, and design upgrades. With the median home price around $385,000, buyers in this range are stretching their budgets and will choose a brand new home over a resale listing every time if the prices are close. The agent you hire needs a specific plan for positioning your home against these builders. If they can't articulate that plan during your interview, they're not the right fit for North Las Vegas.

Check Their Sales in Your Actual Neighborhood

Selling in Aliante is different from selling in Skye Canyon, which is different from the older neighborhoods near Craig Road and Las Vegas Boulevard. Ask each agent for their closed sales within your specific area over the past six months. How did those homes perform compared to the neighborhood average? What was their list-to-sale price ratio? An agent with strong results in Summerlin or Henderson won't automatically know how to sell in North Las Vegas, where buyer demographics, price sensitivity, and competition from builders create a completely different dynamic.

Insist on a Value-Driven Marketing Strategy

North Las Vegas buyers are value-driven. They want to see exactly what they're getting for every dollar. Your next agent's marketing plan should emphasize the advantages your established home has over new construction: a finished backyard with mature landscaping, no waiting 6 to 8 months for a build, a known neighborhood with established amenities, and no surprise Mello-Roos or special improvement district assessments that new construction areas often carry. Professional photos, virtual tours, and targeted ads reaching first-time buyers and young families should all be part of the plan.

Avoid the Agent Who Tells You What You Want to Hear

In North Las Vegas's price-sensitive market, overpricing by even $10,000 to $15,000 can kill your listing. Be wary of any agent who suggests a price higher than recent comparable sales support. The right agent will show you what similar homes sold for and price your home to compete, not to sit.
