What to Ask Every Agent Before You Sign

The interview process matters more the second time. Ask every prospective agent these questions: How many expired or withdrawn listings have you successfully relisted and sold? What is your specific marketing plan for Summerlin, not Las Vegas in general? Will you provide professional staging, photography, and virtual tours as part of your service? What price would you recommend and how did you arrive at that number? An agent who can't answer these questions with specific, data backed responses isn't the right fit for a Summerlin relisting where the median price sits between $600,000 and $640,000.

Summerlin Requires Local Expertise, Not General Knowledge

Summerlin is a master planned community built by Howard Hughes Corporation, with distinct sub areas that each have their own pricing dynamics. A home in The Ridges attracts a completely different buyer than a home in Summerlin South. Your agent needs to understand these differences and tailor the marketing accordingly. With over 13,000 active listings competing across the valley and luxury homes averaging 68.8 days on market, your agent's knowledge of Summerlin's specific buyer pool is what separates a successful relisting from another expired contract.

Red Flags That Signal the Wrong Agent

Be cautious of agents who suggest the same price your previous agent used without running new comparables. Avoid anyone who doesn't mention professional photography, staging, or virtual tours as part of their standard approach. Watch out for agents who promise a quick sale without explaining how they'll achieve it. Across Las Vegas, more than 40% of sellers are struggling to sell, and one in five deals fall through before closing. In this environment, experience with expired listings and a proven track record in Summerlin's premium market are non negotiable qualifications.
