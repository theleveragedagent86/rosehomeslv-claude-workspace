Why Your Cadence Home Isn't Selling

You picked one of Henderson's most exciting communities. Cadence has the parks, the modern architecture, the walkability, and the lifestyle buyers say they want. So why is your home still sitting on the market with no serious offers? The answer is usually not what you expect, and it almost never has anything to do with the home itself.

Find Out Exactly Why Your Home Didn't Sell

Most homeowners never get a straight answer about what went wrong. Get the honest breakdown here before you make your next move.

New Construction Is Your Biggest Competitor

Cadence is still actively being built out by major builders like Lennar, Toll Brothers, and Richmond American. That means every buyer who walks through your resale home also has the option of buying brand new down the street, often with builder incentives, rate buydowns, and the appeal of untouched finishes. Over 40% of listings in the Las Vegas valley fail to sell, and in communities with heavy new construction, that number can climb even higher. If your listing strategy did not directly address the new build competition, it likely fell flat before it ever had a chance.

Pricing in Cadence Requires Precision

The median home price in Cadence sits roughly between $450,000 and $530,000, but that range covers everything from compact single story plans to larger two story layouts with upgraded lots. With mortgage rates hovering between 6.24% and 6.73%, buyers are extremely price sensitive. Even $10,000 over market value can push your listing to the bottom of the search results while new construction homes with shiny incentive packages steal the spotlight. Across Henderson, the median ranges from $520,000 to $640,000, so Cadence buyers are already stretching to get into the community. Overpricing by even a small margin sends them straight to the builder sales office instead.

Presentation Has to Beat the Model Home

Here is the reality most agents will not tell you. When your competition is a professionally staged model home with designer lighting and zero wear and tear, your resale listing needs to look just as polished. Professional photography sells homes 32% faster, and staging delivers an average return on investment of 550%. If your previous listing relied on phone photos and cluttered rooms, buyers scrolled right past it. In a market with over 13,000 competing listings and average days on market stretching to 55 to 62 days, first impressions are everything.
