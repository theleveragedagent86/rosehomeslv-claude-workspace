How to Choose a New Real Estate Agent in Cadence

Your listing expired, and now you are facing one of the most important decisions in the entire selling process. Choosing your next agent. The temptation is to go with whoever promises the highest price or whoever knocks on your door first. But if your Cadence home already failed to sell once, you need more than promises. You need someone who understands exactly why it did not work and what to do differently.

Look for Cadence Specific Knowledge

Cadence is not a typical Henderson neighborhood. It is a master planned community still under active construction, with major builders like Lennar, Toll Brothers, and Richmond American competing for the same buyer pool you are trying to reach. An agent who does not understand the new construction landscape in Cadence will price your home, market your home, and show your home as if builders do not exist. That is exactly the mistake that leads to expired listings. Your next agent should be able to explain how they plan to position your resale home against brand new inventory, because that is the reality of selling in this community.

Ask the Right Questions

Before you sign with anyone, ask these questions. How many homes have you sold in communities with active new construction? What is your pricing strategy when builders are offering incentives? What does your marketing plan include beyond putting the home on the MLS? How will you handle showing feedback? The answers will tell you everything. With over 40% of valley listings failing to sell and 1 in 5 deals falling through, you cannot afford to work with an agent who wings it. The right agent will have specific, detailed answers, not vague promises about their network or their positive attitude.

Demand a Marketing Plan That Competes

In a market with more than 13,000 competing listings, marketing matters. Professional photography helps homes sell 32% faster. Staging returns 550% on investment. Your next agent should present a written marketing plan that includes professional media, targeted digital advertising, strategic pricing against the $450,000 to $530,000 Cadence median, and a showing strategy designed to highlight your home's advantages over new builds. If the plan sounds the same as what your last agent did, expect the same result.
