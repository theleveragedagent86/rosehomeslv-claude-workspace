Restaurants in Green Valley Henderson

Green Valley Ranch Resort Spa and Casino is a AAA Four Diamond resort located at 2300 Paseo Verde Parkway in Henderson (89052). Opened on December 18, 2001, it was the first locals casino in Las Vegas to earn the Four Diamond designation. Operated by Station Casinos, the resort sits within the Green Valley Ranch residential community and serves as both a neighborhood amenity and a destination property.

Resort Amenities

The resort features 495 rooms and suites, over 110,000 square feet of casino floor with 2,200 slot machines and 55 plus table games, and 10 on site restaurants. The 8 acre backyard includes a walk in sand beach pool, a disappearing edge waterfall, and "The Pond," an adult only pool area. The 40,000 square foot spa and salon has been voted "Best in Las Vegas" and offers full service treatments. A Regal Cinemas movie theater, Kids Quest childcare facility, and Cyber Quest arcade round out the entertainment options. The resort also provides a 24 hour limousine shuttle service to the Las Vegas Strip.

Dining at GVR Resort

The resort's restaurant collection includes Hank's Fine Steaks and Martinis (named after Green Valley founder Hank Greenspun), featuring a piano bar and fine dining atmosphere. Bottiglia Cucina and Enoteca serves Tuscan inspired Italian cuisine with a popular weekend brunch. Blue Ribbon Sushi Bar and Grill offers high quality Japanese fare. Pizza Rock, operated by World Champion Pizza Maker Tony Gemignani, serves artisan pizzas. Borracha Mexican Cantina features a Dia de los Muertos theme with craft cocktails. Lucky Penny Cafe operates 24 hours for casual dining at any time.

Impact on Real Estate

The resort's presence directly benefits Green Valley Ranch property values. Homes within walking distance of the resort and its connected District shopping center enjoy a lifestyle amenity that few Las Vegas valley neighborhoods can match. The resort hosts regular events, live entertainment, and seasonal programming that create a vibrant social atmosphere without residents needing to drive to the Strip.
