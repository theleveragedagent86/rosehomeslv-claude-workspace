Green Valley Henderson has a hot desert climate with 297 sunny days per year, approximately 3,923 hours of annual sunshine, and just 4 to 5 inches of rainfall. The community sits at roughly 1,870 feet of elevation, and the dry heat, mild winters, and extended outdoor season are major factors driving relocation to the area from colder and wetter parts of the country.

Summer Heat

July is the hottest month with an average high of 105 degrees Fahrenheit and an average low of 80 degrees. Temperatures occasionally reach 110 to 112 degrees during peak heat waves, but the extremely low humidity (June averages just 11.7 percent relative humidity) makes the dry heat more tolerable than comparable temperatures in humid climates. Air conditioning is essential from May through September, and most outdoor activity shifts to early morning or evening during the peak summer months. Pool season runs from June through mid September.

Mild Winters

December and January are the coolest months with average highs of 58 to 59 degrees and average lows of 38 to 39 degrees. Temperatures rarely drop below 30 degrees, and snow is essentially nonexistent. February is the wettest month with about 1 inch of rainfall. The winter months offer ideal conditions for outdoor activities, with comfortable temperatures for hiking, golf, and walking the community's 300 plus miles of trails.

Best Months for Outdoor Living

February through April and October through November are considered the prime outdoor months, with daytime highs ranging from 65 to 86 degrees. These months are when Green Valley's parks, trails, golf courses, and outdoor dining areas are at their most enjoyable. The extended outdoor living season of 8 to 9 months is a major reason that covered patios, outdoor kitchens, and pools are among the most valued home features in Green Valley real estate.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose advises buyers from humid climates that the adjustment to desert living is easier than most expect. The low humidity means 100 degree days feel significantly different than they would in Florida or Texas.
