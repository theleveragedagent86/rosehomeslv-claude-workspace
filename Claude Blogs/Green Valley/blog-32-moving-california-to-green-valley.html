Is Green Valley Henderson Safe?

California residents account for a significant portion of relocations to the Las Vegas valley, and Green Valley Henderson is one of the most popular landing spots for California transplants. The combination of no state income tax, dramatically lower home prices, Henderson's top safety rankings, and a suburban lifestyle comparable to the best California suburbs drives the steady migration from the Golden State to Southern Nevada.

Financial Benefits

Nevada has no state income tax, no estate tax, and no inheritance tax. For California residents paying state income tax rates of 9.3 to 13.3 percent, the savings are substantial and immediate. Property taxes in Henderson run approximately 0.53 to 0.55 percent of assessed value annually, compared to California's typical 1 to 1.25 percent or higher. The median home price in Green Valley ($433,000 to $595,000) buys significantly more house than comparable prices in most California metro areas, particularly the Bay Area, Los Angeles, Orange County, and San Diego.

Lifestyle Comparison

Green Valley offers a suburban lifestyle that mirrors many of California's best communities: top rated schools, safe neighborhoods, well maintained parks and trails, quality restaurants, and a strong community feel. The key difference is cost. A family selling a $1.2 million home in a California suburb can often purchase a comparable or larger home in Green Valley for half the price, freeing up capital for savings, investment, or retirement. Henderson's population of 350,020 and its own city services mean this is not a small town; it is a fully developed city with the infrastructure and amenities California transplants expect.

What to Know Before Moving

The biggest adjustment for Californians is the summer heat, with July averages hitting 105 degrees. However, the dry heat at 11 percent humidity is widely reported as more tolerable than humid heat. Most outdoor activity shifts to morning and evening from June through September. Nevada does not have a coast, but Lake Mead is approximately 30 minutes from Green Valley and provides boating, fishing, and water recreation. The drive to California beaches is roughly 4 to 5 hours.
