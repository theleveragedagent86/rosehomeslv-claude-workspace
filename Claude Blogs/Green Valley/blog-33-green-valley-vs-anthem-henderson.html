Where Is Green Valley Located in Henderson?

Green Valley and Anthem are both master planned communities within Henderson, Nevada, making them frequent comparison targets for buyers choosing between Henderson neighborhoods. Green Valley was founded in 1978 as Southern Nevada's first master planned community, while Anthem is newer, developed primarily from the late 1990s through the 2000s in the southern foothills of Henderson.

Location and Setting

Green Valley sits in the central to eastern portion of Henderson at approximately 1,870 feet of elevation. Anthem is located further south in the McCullough Range foothills at higher elevations, providing more dramatic mountain views and a foothill setting. Green Valley is closer to the Strip (10 to 15 miles vs Anthem's 15 to 20 miles) and closer to the airport (5.5 to 7 miles vs Anthem's 10 to 12 miles). Anthem offers quicker access to the Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area for hiking.

Home Prices

Green Valley's pricing spans a wider range due to its size and age diversity: condos from $225,000, single family homes from $395,000, and luxury homes reaching $3 million plus. Anthem's homes are generally newer with median prices slightly higher in comparable size ranges, typically $500,000 to $800,000 for standard single family homes. Sun City Anthem provides a large 55 plus community option within Anthem with 7,144 homes and prices from approximately $350,000 to $2 million.

Community Character

Green Valley feels more established and urban, with mature trees, walkable commercial areas like The District, and the Green Valley Ranch Resort embedded in the neighborhood. Anthem has a more suburban and secluded feel, with quieter streets, foothill terrain, and less commercial development within its boundaries. Anthem Country Club offers a private golf and social membership option. Green Valley's commercial infrastructure is significantly more developed, with The District, Galleria at Sunset, and the GVR Resort all within its boundaries.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose finds that the Green Valley vs Anthem decision often comes down to commute priorities and lifestyle preferences. Buyers who work on the Strip, travel frequently, or want walkable dining and entertainment lean toward Green Valley. Buyers who prioritize newer construction, foothill views, and a quieter suburban atmosphere tend to prefer Anthem.
