Moving from California to Green Valley Henderson

Property taxes in Green Valley Henderson run approximately 0.53 to 0.55 percent of assessed value annually, which is notably lower than the national average of roughly 1.1 percent. For a home with a taxable value of $500,000, annual property taxes would be approximately $2,650 to $2,750. Nevada's property tax structure includes several taxpayer protections that make it particularly favorable compared to many states.

How Nevada Property Tax Works

Nevada assesses property at 35 percent of taxable value (which differs from market value). The state caps annual property tax increases through a tax abatement system: primary residences are limited to a 3 percent annual increase in the tax bill, and all other properties are capped at 8 percent. This means even if your home appreciates significantly in market value, your annual property tax bill cannot increase more than 3 percent per year for your primary residence. The Clark County Assessor's Office handles assessments and can be reached at (702) 455 3882.

Tax Districts and Rates

Green Valley Henderson falls within the Henderson tax district, which includes levies for Clark County, City of Henderson, Clark County School District, Henderson Library District, and various other local districts. The combined tax rate is approximately $3.23 per $100 of assessed value (2024/2025 rate). New homeowners should note that the assessed value on a recently purchased property will be based on the purchase price, while long term homeowners benefit from the 3 percent annual cap that may keep their assessed value well below current market value.

Comparison to Other States

California's effective property tax rate averages approximately 0.71 percent (with much higher assessed values), Texas averages 1.60 percent, and New Jersey averages 2.23 percent. Nevada's roughly 0.53 to 0.55 percent effective rate combined with no state income tax creates a significant overall tax advantage for homeowners, particularly those relocating from high tax states. This is one of the primary financial motivators driving California to Nevada migration.
