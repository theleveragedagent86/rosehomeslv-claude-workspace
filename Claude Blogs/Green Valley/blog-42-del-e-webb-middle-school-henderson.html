Del E. Webb Middle School is located at 2200 Reunion Drive in Henderson (89052), serving students in grades 6 through 8 from the Green Valley Ranch, Seven Hills, and surrounding Henderson communities. Rated 9 out of 10 on GreatSchools, Del E. Webb is one of the highest performing middle schools in the Las Vegas valley and feeds into Coronado High School.

Academic Performance

Del E. Webb Middle School consistently posts test scores and academic metrics that place it among the top tier of Clark County School District middle schools. The school offers honors courses in core subjects, gifted and talented education programming, and accelerated math pathways that prepare students for advanced coursework at the high school level. The 9 out of 10 GreatSchools rating reflects strong performance across math, reading, and science assessments.

Programs and Activities

The school offers band, orchestra, choir, and visual arts programs alongside academic clubs including science olympiad, math league, and student council. Athletic programs cover basketball, flag football, volleyball, soccer, track and field, and cross country. The combination of strong academics and robust extracurricular options creates a well rounded middle school experience that families in the Green Valley Ranch and Seven Hills areas particularly value.

Feeder Pattern

Del E. Webb Middle School draws from several highly rated elementary schools including Elise L. Wolff (10/10), which is adjacent to Seven Hills, and other Green Valley Ranch feeder schools. Graduates continue to Coronado High School (7/10, ranked 13th in Nevada). This K through 12 feeder pattern of consistently high rated schools is one of the strongest educational pipelines in the entire Las Vegas valley.

Ryan Rose considers the Del E. Webb to Coronado feeder pattern one of the most compelling reasons families choose Green Valley Ranch and Seven Hills over competing Henderson communities. The 9 out of 10 middle school rating feeding into a top 15 state ranked high school provides continuity that parents deeply value, and homes zoned for this pathway reflect that demand in their pricing.
