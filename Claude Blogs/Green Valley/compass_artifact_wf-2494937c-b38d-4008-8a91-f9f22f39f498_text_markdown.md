## 5. Community overview and history

### Origins and development
Green Valley is **Southern Nevada's first master-planned community**, predating Summerlin by over a decade. **Hank Greenspun**, publisher of the Las Vegas Sun, began acquiring Henderson land in the 1960s. In **1972**, the City of Henderson sold 4,700 acres of "Section 25" to Greenspun for approximately **$1.2 million** (~$280/acre). His **American Nevada Corporation (ANC)**, founded in 1972, planned and developed the community.

The **grand opening was October 24, 1978**, with Nevada Governor Mike O'Callaghan attending. The community took approximately 20 years to substantially complete across three phases. The Urban Land Institute recognized ANC's Green Valley as a prototype of an outstanding master-planned community.

### Key milestones

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1972 | Greenspun purchases 4,700 acres from Henderson for ~$1.2M |
| 1978 | Grand opening October 24; first homes built |
| ~1985 | Green Valley South construction begins |
| 1989 | Hank Greenspun dies; son Brian takes over |
| 1991 | Green Valley High School opens |
| 1994 | Green Valley Ranch residential construction begins |
| 1996 | Galleria at Sunset opens (Henderson's first indoor mall) |
| 2001 | Green Valley Ranch Resort Spa & Casino opens December 18 |
| 2004 | The District at Green Valley Ranch opens April 15 |

### Size and boundaries
Total acreage: **8,400 acres** (34 km²). Located in the southeast Las Vegas Valley within Henderson city limits. Over **75,000 residents** across **20,000+ homes**. Green Valley Ranch alone has ~4,000 homes and ~17,482 residents across 1,310 acres.

### Zip codes
**89014** (Green Valley North), **89074** (Green Valley South), **89012** (Green Valley South/Ranch/MacDonald Ranch), **89052** (eastern portions of Green Valley Ranch)

## 6. HOA information

### Multi-layered structure
Green Valley operates under a **multi-layered HOA system**: a master/community association plus individual sub-association HOAs. Homeowners typically pay both.

**Green Valley Ranch Community Association (GVRCA)** is the master association for Green Valley Ranch, managing 1,310 acres, 4,000+ homes, and 31 sub-associations (18 gated, 13 non-gated). Website: greenvalleyranch.org.

**Green Valley Homeowners' Recreation Association (GVHRA)** covers 1,048 homes in Green Valley proper, managing pools, clubhouses, and greenbelt areas. Website: gvhra.com.

### Fee ranges
- **Master association fee**: $65–$75/month
- **Total combined (master + sub-association)**: $100–$300/month typical
- Guard-gated luxury communities: higher fees for security and private amenities
- Basic communities with no sub-association: as low as $65–$100/month

### What fees cover
Master fees cover common area maintenance, parks/trails/open space upkeep, pedestrian and bike path maintenance, community events, professional management, insurance, and reserve fund contributions. Sub-association fees cover neighborhood landscaping, gated entry/security, pools/spas, fitness facilities, clubhouse operations, and exterior maintenance (condos/townhomes).

### Management companies
**Terra West Management** (702-362-6262), **CAMCO Nevada**, **FirstService Residential**, **Desert Living** (family-owned Henderson specialist since 2002), **P&G Association Management**, plus self-managed GVRCA and GVHRA with on-site staff.

### Notable rules
All exterior modifications require pre-approval from the Architectural Review Committee (30–45 day review). Exterior paint must match approved palettes. RV/boat street storage is prohibited. Some HOAs require minimum 1-year lease terms. Short-term rental policies vary by sub-association. Green Valley Ranch is rated as having **"relaxed" enforcement** compared to other Henderson communities. Progressive violation fines range from $50 to $1,000+ depending on severity.

## 17. Climate and weather

Henderson has a **hot desert climate** (Köppen BWh) at approximately **1,870 feet elevation** with **297 sunny days per year** (~3,923 sunshine hours).

### Temperature highlights
**July** is the hottest month: average high **105°F**, average low 80°F. **December/January** are coolest: average high 58–59°F, average low 38–39°F. Temperatures rarely drop below 30°F or exceed 112°F.

**Annual rainfall:** Just **4–5 inches** (vs. national average of 38 inches). Wettest month is February (~1 inch). Essentially no snow. Humidity is very low — June averages just **11.7%** relative humidity.

**Best months for outdoor activities:** February, March, April, October, November (65°F–86°F). July–August require caution (100°F+; activity best in early morning/evening). **Best pool months:** June through mid-September.

**Real estate impact:** Pools are extremely popular. Extended outdoor living season (8–9 months). Covered patios, outdoor kitchens, and shade structures are highly valued. A/C is essential May–September. **Solar panels are increasingly popular** given 297 sunny days and Nevada's favorable net metering policies.

## 20. Green Valley Ranch vs. Green Valley — the key distinction

This is a frequent source of confusion. Three distinct entities share the name:

**Green Valley (the broader community)** encompasses the entire **8,400-acre master-planned community** in Henderson — an umbrella term covering Green Valley North, Green Valley South, and Green Valley Ranch. Founded 1978. 75,000+ residents. 20,000+ homes.

**Green Valley Ranch (the residential community)** is a specific **1,310-acre sub-community** within the broader Green Valley, located at higher elevation in the Black Mountain foothills south of I-215. Built 1994–2002. ~4,000 homes across 31 sub-associations (18 gated). More upscale with Strip and mountain views, Spanish/Mediterranean architecture. Has its own master HOA (GVRCA). Home prices $300K–$3M+.

**Green Valley Ranch Resort Spa & Casino** is a **hotel, casino, and resort** — NOT a residential community. Located at 2300 Paseo Verde Pkwy within the Green Valley Ranch residential neighborhood. Opened December 18, 2001. Operated by Station Casinos. 495 rooms, 110,000+ sq ft casino.

All three share the **same Greenspun/ANC developer family** and geographic area, but serve very different purposes.

## Conclusion: from data to content strategy
