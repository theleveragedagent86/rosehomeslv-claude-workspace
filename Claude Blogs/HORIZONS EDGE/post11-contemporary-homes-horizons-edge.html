Contemporary Desert Architecture in Horizon's Edge

Horizon's Edge is one of the few Henderson communities built entirely around contemporary desert architecture, offering a design style that stands apart from the typical stucco subdivisions across the Las Vegas valley. Every home in this gated community was designed by D.R. Horton with clean lines, flat rooflines, and modern materials that complement the surrounding Mojave landscape.

What Makes Horizon's Edge Homes Look Different?

The homes in Horizon's Edge feature a distinctly modern aesthetic with opaque 8' glass panel garage doors, courtyard and atrium entries, and rooftop sky decks. Exterior materials blend desert tones with contemporary finishes, creating a look that feels intentional against the backdrop of Black Mountain. Unlike traditional Nevada subdivisions, these homes were designed to embrace the terrain rather than flatten it, sitting on terraced lots that follow the natural slope of the hillside.

What Contemporary Features Are Inside Horizon's Edge Homes?

Interior finishes match the modern exterior. Homes include Zodiac Quartz countertops, GE stainless steel appliances, and pre-wired surround sound systems. The Tuxedo home automation system and built-in alarm add smart home functionality that was ahead of its time when these homes were built between 2014 and 2017. Loft spaces, casitas, and multiple decks give each floor plan a layered, open feel with plenty of natural light.

How Big Are the Contemporary Homes in Horizon's Edge?

The original Horizon's Edge section offers six floor plans ranging from 1,910 to 3,180 square feet with 3 to 5 bedrooms. The South section features four larger plans from 2,620 to 3,220 square feet with 3 to 4 bedrooms and up to 4.5 bathrooms. Homes are priced from the low $300,000s to the mid $500,000s, making this contemporary style accessible compared to custom modern builds elsewhere in Henderson.
