Mountain View Homes in Horizon's Edge Henderson

Horizon's Edge sits at the base of Black Mountain in Henderson, giving homeowners some of the best mountain and valley views available in a production home community. With over 200 terraced lots and rooftop sky decks, nearly every home in this gated neighborhood was designed to take advantage of the elevated terrain.

Which Homes in Horizon's Edge Have the Best Mountain Views?

The terraced lot design means homes at higher elevations within the community enjoy sweeping views of Black Mountain to the south and the Las Vegas valley to the north. The South section of Horizon's Edge, with its larger floor plans ranging from 2,620 to 3,220 square feet, was specifically designed with rooftop decks and three separate deck levels to maximize the panoramic sightlines. Homes on the upper tiers of the community tend to command the strongest views and slightly higher prices.

Can You See the Las Vegas Strip From Horizon's Edge?

Many homes in Horizon's Edge offer city views that include the Las Vegas Strip skyline, roughly 14 miles to the northwest. The rooftop sky decks provide the best vantage point, especially at night when the valley lights up. These views are a major selling point, particularly for the two-story and loft-style plans that rise above neighboring rooflines.

How Do Terraced Lots Enhance Mountain Views?

Rather than grading the hillside flat, D.R. Horton built Horizon's Edge on terraced lots that step up the slope of Black Mountain. This staggered approach means each row of homes sits slightly above the one in front of it, reducing view obstruction. The result is a community where mountain and city views are not limited to a handful of premium lots but are woven into the overall layout of the neighborhood.

A Local Take on Horizon's Edge Views

Ryan Rose recommends visiting Horizon's Edge in the late afternoon when the light hits Black Mountain and the valley starts to glow. The combination of mountain proximity and city panoramas from a single rooftop deck is something you typically only find in custom hillside homes priced well above this community's range of low $300,000s to mid $500,000s.
