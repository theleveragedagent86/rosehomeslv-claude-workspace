Terraced Lots in Horizon's Edge Henderson

Horizon's Edge features over 200 terraced lots built into the hillside at the base of Black Mountain, making it one of the most distinctive lot layouts in all of Henderson. This design choice gives homeowners elevated perspectives, better privacy, and a connection to the natural desert terrain that flat subdivisions simply cannot match.

What Are Terraced Lots and Why Does Horizon's Edge Use Them?

Terraced lots are graded in a stair-step pattern that follows the natural slope of the land rather than leveling it flat. In Horizon's Edge, D.R. Horton used this approach to preserve the hillside topography while creating buildable home sites. Each row of homes sits at a slightly different elevation, which reduces the feeling of density and allows views to flow over neighboring rooftops. The result is a community that feels more open and spacious than its lot sizes might suggest.

How Do Terraced Lots Affect Home Values in Horizon's Edge?

Terraced lots with unobstructed mountain or valley views tend to hold a premium in resale. In Horizon's Edge, homes are currently priced from the low $300,000s to mid $500,000s, with the higher end typically reflecting better elevation and view positioning. Because terraced lot communities are relatively rare in the Henderson market, this layout contributes to the neighborhood's long-term appeal and differentiation from surrounding subdivisions.

What Is It Like Living on a Terraced Lot?

Residents enjoy greater privacy since each home sits above or below its neighbors rather than at the same level. The elevation changes also create natural separation between yards. Combined with rooftop decks and sky decks included in many Horizon's Edge floor plans, homeowners can take full advantage of their elevated position for entertaining or simply watching the sunset over the valley.

A Local Insight on Terraced Living

Ryan Rose notes that buyers often underestimate how much the terraced layout changes the day-to-day experience of living in Horizon's Edge. Walking through the community, the elevation shifts create a sense of variety that most cookie-cutter neighborhoods lack. It is worth visiting in person to feel the difference that terrain-integrated design makes.
