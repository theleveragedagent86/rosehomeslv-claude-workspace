Buying a Home in Horizon's Edge Henderson

Buying a home in Horizon's Edge means entering a fully built-out gated community in south Henderson with contemporary desert architecture, terraced lots, and mountain views priced from the low $300,000s to mid $500,000s. Since all homes were completed between 2014 and 2017, every purchase here is a resale transaction with established landscaping and a proven track record.

What Should Buyers Know About Horizon's Edge Before Making an Offer?

Horizon's Edge was built exclusively by D.R. Horton across two sections. The original community includes over 200 homes with six floor plans ranging from 1,910 to 3,180 square feet. The South section adds 89 homes with four larger plans from 2,620 to 3,220 square feet. HOA fees are just $50 per month, which is notably low for a gated Henderson community. Buyers should also know that the community is gated per residents, providing an added layer of security and privacy.

What Features Come Standard in Horizon's Edge Homes?

D.R. Horton included above-average standard features in these homes. Expect Zodiac Quartz countertops, GE stainless steel appliances, tankless water heaters, and 16 S.E.E.R. HVAC systems with enhanced R15/R38 insulation. Many plans include casitas, lofts, multiple decks, and courtyard entries. The Tuxedo home automation system and pre-wired surround sound were also included at delivery, so buyers inherit smart home infrastructure without additional investment.

How Does the Horizon's Edge Location Benefit Homebuyers?

The community sits at the base of Black Mountain between I-215 and US-515/I-11, putting residents about 10 minutes from downtown Henderson, 15 to 18 minutes from Harry Reid International Airport, and roughly 20 minutes from the Las Vegas Strip. St. Rose Dominican Siena Campus, ranked number two in Nevada, is nearby. Henderson itself was named the second safest city by SafeWise in 2024, with a violent crime rate of 2.1 per 1,000 compared to the national average of 4.0.

Ryan Rose's Advice for Horizon's Edge Buyers

Ryan Rose suggests paying close attention to lot position when shopping in Horizon's Edge. Because of the terraced layout, the same floor plan can offer dramatically different views depending on its elevation within the community. Touring multiple homes at different tiers is the best way to understand what each price point delivers.
