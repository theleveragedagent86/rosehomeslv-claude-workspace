Foothill High School Henderson NV: What Families Need to Know

Foothill High School is the zoned high school for Horizon's Edge in Henderson, NV, and it boasts a 94% graduation rate with average SAT scores of 1180. For families planning long term in this neighborhood, Foothill provides a solid foundation for college preparation and career readiness.

What Are Foothill High School's Ratings and Test Scores?

Foothill High School holds a B grade from Niche and a 5 out of 10 from GreatSchools. U.S. News ranks it #26 among Nevada high schools, placing it in the top half of the state. The average SAT score is 1180 and the average ACT score is 24, both above national averages. With a 94% graduation rate, the vast majority of students complete their education on time.

What Grades Does Foothill High School Serve?

Foothill serves students in grades 9 through 12. It is the natural continuation of the Horizon's Edge school pipeline, following Ulis Newton Elementary and Mannion Middle School. This K through 12 feeder pattern keeps students within a familiar Henderson community throughout their entire public school experience.

How Does Foothill High School Prepare Students for College?

The combination of above average standardized test scores and a near perfect graduation rate signals that Foothill is effectively preparing students for postsecondary education. Students benefit from a range of academic programs, and the school's location in Henderson provides access to extracurricular and community resources. Ryan Rose often highlights this complete school pipeline as one of the reasons families choose Horizon's Edge over other Henderson neighborhoods.
