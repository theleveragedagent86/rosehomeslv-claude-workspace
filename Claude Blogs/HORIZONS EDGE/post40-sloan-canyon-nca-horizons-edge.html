Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area Near Horizon's Edge

Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area sits adjacent to Horizon's Edge, offering residents direct access to one of Southern Nevada's most significant archaeological and outdoor recreation sites. The conservation area features over 1,700 individual petroglyph design elements and miles of desert hiking trails.

What Are the Petroglyphs at Sloan Canyon?

Sloan Canyon's petroglyph site is one of the most important rock art locations in Southern Nevada. The site contains over 1,700 individual design elements carved into the canyon walls by Native peoples over thousands of years. These petroglyphs are protected as a nationally significant cultural resource, and visitors can view them along designated trail routes within the conservation area.

What Hiking Trails Are in Sloan Canyon?

The conservation area offers multiple hiking trails that wind through desert terrain with views of the surrounding mountain landscape. Trails vary in difficulty, making Sloan Canyon accessible to casual walkers and experienced hikers alike. The proximity to Horizon's Edge means residents can start a morning hike and be back home before the afternoon heat sets in, a practical advantage that Ryan Rose often highlights to outdoor enthusiasts considering the neighborhood.
