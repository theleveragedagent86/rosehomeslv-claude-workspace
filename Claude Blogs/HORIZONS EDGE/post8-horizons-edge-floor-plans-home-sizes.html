Horizon's Edge Floor Plans and Home Sizes

Horizon's Edge in Henderson, NV offers ten distinct floor plans ranging from 1,910 to 3,220 square feet. Built exclusively by D.R. Horton between 2014 and 2017, the community features single family homes in both one and two story configurations with contemporary desert architecture.

What Floor Plans Are Available in the Original Horizon's Edge?

The original Horizon's Edge section includes six floor plans. Plan 1 offers 1,910 square feet with 4 bedrooms and 3 bathrooms. Plan 2 provides 1,955 square feet with 3 bedrooms and 2 bathrooms. Plan 3 steps up to 2,580 square feet with 3 bedrooms and 2.5 bathrooms. Plan 4 delivers 2,780 square feet with 4 bedrooms and 3 bathrooms. Plan 5 is the largest at 2,980 square feet with 5 bedrooms and 3 bathrooms, while Plan 6 tops out at 3,180 square feet with 4 bedrooms and 3.5 bathrooms.

What About the Horizon's Edge South Floor Plans?

Horizon's Edge South added four more floor plans with slightly larger footprints. The 2620 plan offers 2,620 square feet with 3 bedrooms and 3.5 bathrooms. The 2820 plan provides 2,820 square feet with 3 bedrooms and 3.5 bathrooms. The 3020 plan includes 3,020 square feet with 4 bedrooms and 4.5 bathrooms. The 3220 plan rounds out the collection at 3,220 square feet with 4 bedrooms and 3.5 bathrooms.

What Features Come Standard in These Homes?

Homes in Horizon's Edge include 2 car garages, GE stainless steel appliances, and Zodiac Quartz countertops. Energy efficient features include tankless water heaters, 16 S.E.E.R. HVAC systems, and R15/R38 insulation. Many plans feature rooftop decks, casitas, courtyards, and loft spaces. The Horizon's Edge South section also includes Tuxedo home automation systems and pre wired surround sound.

Local Insight

Ryan Rose recommends paying close attention to the lot position when comparing floor plans, because the terraced layout of Horizon's Edge means two identical plans can have very different views. A Plan 3 on a higher terrace might offer better mountain and valley sightlines than a larger Plan 5 on a lower lot, so the view premium is worth factoring into your decision.
