What Are the Pros and Cons of Living in Henderson NV?

Henderson offers a strong combination of safety, schools, and proximity to Las Vegas, but it also comes with desert climate challenges and a rising cost of living. Here is an honest breakdown of what to expect.

What Are the Biggest Pros of Living in Henderson?

Safety tops the list. Henderson ranks as the #2 safest large city in the United States according to AdvisorSmith using FBI NIBRS data. The city's public schools include multiple 4 and 5 star rated campuses, and master planned communities like Inspirada offer access to National Blue Ribbon schools such as Pinecrest Academy.

Location is another major advantage. Henderson sits 15 minutes from the Las Vegas Strip, 15 to 18 minutes from Harry Reid International Airport, and provides direct highway access via I-15, I-215, and I-515. Nevada has no state income tax, which puts more money in residents' pockets compared to neighboring California or other high tax states.

Outdoor recreation is abundant. Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area borders Henderson's southern edge. Master planned communities offer trails, parks, and sports facilities. The growing local economy continues to attract employers and new businesses to the area.

What Are the Drawbacks of Living in Henderson?

Summer heat is the most common complaint. Temperatures regularly exceed 100 degrees from June through September, and outdoor activity during midday becomes impractical for months at a time. Public transit options are limited compared to major metro areas, making car ownership essentially required.

HOA rules in master planned communities can be strict. Landscaping, exterior paint colors, and parking are all subject to community guidelines. Lot sizes in newer Henderson neighborhoods tend to be smaller than those found in older parts of the Las Vegas valley. Water restrictions apply year round due to the ongoing drought conditions affecting the Colorado River and Lake Mead. The cost of living in Henderson has been rising steadily, with home prices and daily expenses trending upward over the past several years.
