How Does Inspirada Compare to Cadence in Henderson NV?

Inspirada and Cadence are both master planned communities in Henderson, but they differ in age, layout, and amenities. Here is a side by side comparison to help you decide which community fits your lifestyle.

What Are the Key Differences Between Inspirada and Cadence?

Inspirada opened in 2007 and spans 1,940 acres across 7 villages and 39 neighborhoods. Cadence is newer, opening in 2015 on a slightly larger 2,200 acre footprint. Both communities are still actively building, which means new construction options are available in each. Inspirada currently has 9,600+ residents with a projected buildout of 22,000+.

Pricing in both communities is competitive. Inspirada homes range from $419K to over $1.1M with a median of $549K as of February 2026. Cadence offers a similar price range, making the two communities direct competitors for buyers in this segment. Builders in Inspirada include Toll Brothers, KB Home, Tri Pointe, Beazer, and Century Communities.

Which Community Has Better Amenities?

Inspirada offers 85 acres of parkland across 7 parks, 35+ miles of trails, and multiple heated pools with splash pads. The community borders Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area, providing direct access to over 1,700 petroglyphs and desert hiking. Cadence features a central 50 acre park and its own trail system, plus proximity to Galleria at Sunset for shopping and dining.

Schools are a significant differentiator. Inspirada is served by Pinecrest Academy, ranked the #1 elementary school in Nevada and a National Blue Ribbon School. Both communities benefit from Henderson's #2 safest large city ranking in the U.S.

Which Community Is Right for You?

Ryan Rose works with buyers exploring both communities regularly. Inspirada appeals to families who prioritize school quality, extensive trail systems, and proximity to protected open space. Cadence may appeal to buyers who prefer a newer community with convenient access to established retail at Galleria at Sunset. Inspirada's base HOA of $95 per month is worth comparing to Cadence's fees when budgeting. The upcoming Centurion Town Center opening in December 2026 will add Sprouts, retail, and 600 apartments to the Inspirada area.
