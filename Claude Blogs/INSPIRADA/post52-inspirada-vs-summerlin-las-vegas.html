How Does Inspirada Compare to Summerlin Las Vegas?

Inspirada in Henderson and Summerlin on the west side of Las Vegas are two of southern Nevada's most popular master planned communities, but they serve different buyers. Here is what sets them apart.

What Is the Biggest Difference Between Inspirada and Summerlin?

Scale is the most obvious distinction. Summerlin spans 22,500 acres and opened in 1990, making it one of the largest and most established master planned communities in the country. Inspirada covers 1,940 acres and opened in 2007, with active new construction still underway. Summerlin is largely built out in its older villages while Inspirada projects a buildout to 22,000+ residents from its current 9,600+.

Location separates the two as well. Inspirada sits in southeast Henderson, 15 minutes from the Strip and 15 to 18 minutes from Harry Reid International Airport. Summerlin is on the far west side, roughly 20 to 25 minutes from the Strip depending on traffic. Summerlin offers direct access to Red Rock Canyon, while Inspirada borders Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area with over 1,700 petroglyphs.

How Do Home Prices Compare?

Inspirada's median home price is $549K as of February 2026, with a range of $419K to over $1.1M. Summerlin generally carries higher price points, particularly in its newer villages and guard gated enclaves. Buyers looking for more home per dollar often find stronger value in Inspirada. Both communities offer new construction, but Inspirada's active builders include Toll Brothers, KB Home, Tri Pointe, Beazer, and Century Communities.

Amenity access also differs. Summerlin features Downtown Summerlin with major retail and dining, multiple golf courses, and over 150 miles of trails. Inspirada offers 85 acres of parks, 35+ miles of trails, multiple heated pools, and will gain significant retail with Centurion Town Center opening December 2026. Inspirada's base HOA is $95 per month, while Summerlin HOA costs vary widely depending on the sub association.
