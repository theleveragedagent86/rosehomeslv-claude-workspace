Madeira Canyon HOA Fees Explained: What You Pay and What You Get

Madeira Canyon HOA fees vary by neighborhood, ranging from approximately $135 per quarter in some sections to $820 per quarter in others, with The Club at Madeira Canyon charging around $245 per month for its 55+ residents. Understanding what these fees cover helps buyers make informed decisions about which neighborhood fits their budget.

Master Association Fees

All Madeira Canyon homeowners pay into the master homeowners association, which is managed by Associa Nevada South. These fees cover maintenance of common areas, community landscaping, and shared amenities throughout the development. The master association maintains the community's water smart desert landscaping, walking paths, and general upkeep that keeps property values strong.

Neighborhood Specific Fees

Beyond the master association, individual neighborhoods may have additional fees based on their amenities and services. Guard gated communities like The Club at Madeira Canyon and Videiras include security costs in their HOA dues. The Club's approximately $245 monthly fee covers 24 hour gate guards, nighttime security patrols, exclusive clubhouse access, pool and fitness center maintenance, and a full time activities director.

Castillo, one of the more affordable neighborhoods, charges around $135 per quarter, reflecting its non gated status and fewer exclusive amenities. This makes Castillo an attractive entry point for buyers who want to live in Madeira Canyon without higher carrying costs.

What Do HOA Fees Cover?

Typical Madeira Canyon HOA fees include association management and administration, grounds and common area maintenance, community recreation facilities, and reserve funds for future repairs. Gated neighborhoods add security services and private amenity maintenance to their fee structure.

Financial Health of the HOA

The Madeira Canyon Homeowners Association maintains strong financial standing according to third party analysis. The association carries low special assessment risk, meaning homeowners are unlikely to face unexpected large payments for community repairs or improvements.

Verifying Current Fees

Ryan Rose recommends that all buyers verify current HOA fees directly with Associa Nevada South at (702) 795 3344 before making an offer. Fees can change annually, and specific neighborhoods may have updated their dues since this information was published.
