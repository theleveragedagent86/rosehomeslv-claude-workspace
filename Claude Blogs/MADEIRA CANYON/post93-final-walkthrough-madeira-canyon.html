Final Walkthrough Before Closing in Madeira Canyon Henderson

The final walkthrough is your last opportunity to verify property condition before taking ownership. Conducted within 24 to 48 hours of closing, this inspection confirms that agreed upon repairs were completed, no new damage occurred, and the home is in expected condition. Taking this step seriously protects your investment.

Purpose of the Walkthrough

Final walkthroughs are not repeat home inspections but rather verification visits. You are confirming that the property remains in substantially the same condition as when you agreed to purchase it, that sellers completed any negotiated repairs, and that all items included in the sale remain present.

The walkthrough also ensures sellers have vacated completely and not left behind items requiring your disposal. Moving out sometimes causes damage that was not present during earlier visits, making this final check essential.

Timing and Scheduling

Schedule walkthroughs as close to closing as practical, ideally the day before or morning of closing. This timing minimizes opportunity for problems to develop between your inspection and taking ownership. Your agent coordinates walkthrough scheduling with listing agents and sellers.

Allow adequate time for thorough inspection rather than rushing. Most walkthroughs take 30 to 60 minutes depending on home size and complexity. Bring your inspection report for reference if repairs were negotiated.

Walkthrough Checklist

Test all major systems during your walkthrough. Turn on the HVAC system and verify heating and cooling operation. Run water in every sink, tub, and shower checking for leaks and proper drainage. Flush all toilets. Test the garage door opener and all remotes. Verify appliances function including dishwasher, garbage disposal, oven, and range.

Check that all light fixtures work and switches operate correctly. Test doorbell and any security systems. Confirm pool and spa equipment operates if applicable. Open and close all doors and windows checking for proper operation.

Verify Repairs

If sellers agreed to repairs during negotiation, verify each item was completed satisfactorily. Review repair receipts or invoices if provided. Do not assume repairs were done properly without checking. If professional repairs were required, confirm licensed contractors performed the work.

Issues discovered during walkthroughs that should have been repaired can delay closing until resolved or result in escrow holdbacks ensuring completion. Address discrepancies before signing closing documents.

Check for Damage

Moving out can damage walls, floors, and fixtures. Look for holes, scratches, dents, and stains that were not present previously. Check areas where furniture was positioned for hidden damage. Examine door frames and hallways where large items may have been moved.

Outdoor areas deserve attention too. Verify landscaping was not damaged, fences remain intact, and pool equipment was not affected by moving activities.

Confirm Inclusions

Your contract specifies items conveying with the property. Common inclusions are appliances, window coverings, lighting fixtures, and sometimes furniture or outdoor items. Verify all included items remain present. Substitutions or removals violate contract terms.

Also confirm that items excluded from the sale (perhaps a chandelier or specific appliances sellers were keeping) have been removed and replaced with acceptable alternatives if required.

What If Problems Are Found?

Minor issues may be addressed through closing credits or repair escrows allowing closing to proceed. Significant problems may require postponing closing until resolved. Your agent and the listing agent work to find solutions preserving the transaction while protecting your interests.

Never close on a property with unresolved significant issues assuming they will be fixed afterward. Once you own the home, leveraging seller cooperation becomes much more difficult.

Documentation

Photograph any concerns discovered during walkthrough. Documentation provides evidence if disputes arise and helps track items needing attention after closing. Note the date and time of your walkthrough for records.
