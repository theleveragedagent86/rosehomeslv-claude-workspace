Mesquite Municipal Airport (FAA identifier 67L) is a general aviation facility serving private pilots and charter flights in Mesquite NV, with no scheduled commercial service. It supports light aircraft, business jets, and recreational flyers across the Virgin River Valley. ## What Is Mesquite Municipal Airport (67L)? Mesquite Municipal Airport (67L) is a public use general aviation airport owned and operated by the City of Mesquite. It serves single engine planes, twin engine aircraft, and small business jets, but does not host any scheduled commercial airlines. Pilots use 67L for recreational flying, flight training, and private charter arrivals from regional hubs. The airport's designation as a general aviation field means Mesquite residents flying commercially still rely on St. George Regional Airport (roughly 42 miles north) or Harry Reid International (roughly 80 miles south) for scheduled service. ## What Services Does 67L Offer Pilots? Mesquite Municipal Airport 67L provides the standard amenities expected at a general aviation field, including fuel services, tie down spaces, and hangar availability. The airport accommodates both based aircraft and transient pilots flying in for golf trips, casino weekends, or business visits. Its location near the CasaBlanca, Virgin River, and Eureka resorts makes it popular with pilots who fly themselves in for Mesquite's championship golf courses. For pilots flying charter or private, 67L offers a faster alternative to driving from Las Vegas or St. George. Flight plans can typically be filed through standard FAA channels, and the airport is tracked on FlightAware for inbound and outbound traffic. ## Is Mesquite Municipal Used for Commercial Flights? No, Mesquite Municipal Airport (67L) does not offer commercial passenger service. Residents and visitors needing commercial flights fly out of St. George Regional (SGU) for regional service or Harry Reid International (LAS) for major domestic and international routes.
