Median Household Income & Cost of Living in Mesquite NV

The median household income in Mesquite NV is $75,227, with an average household income of $92,019, and Nevada's lack of state income tax keeps the overall cost of living below Las Vegas levels. Understanding financial metrics is critical when evaluating whether a community aligns with your budget and economic goals.

What Is the Median Household Income in Mesquite?

Mesquite's median household income sits at $75,227, reflecting a healthy and growing economic base. The average household income reaches $92,019, indicating income diversity across the community. Year-over-year, median household income has grown 1.1%, demonstrating steady economic progress. These figures suggest that Mesquite attracts working families, retirees with investment income, and professionals seeking an affordable lifestyle without sacrificing quality.

How Does Nevada's Tax Structure Impact Your Bottom Line?

Nevada has no state income tax, a feature that fundamentally changes financial planning for residents. Compared to California, Oregon, and other high-tax states, the absence of state income tax translates to thousands of dollars remaining in your pocket annually. This advantage particularly benefits retirees living on fixed incomes and business owners whose earnings exceed employment-based income. When combined with Mesquite's below-Vegas cost of living, the tax advantage becomes even more pronounced. Property taxes in Mesquite average approximately 1% of home value, among the lowest rates in the nation and significantly lower than property tax burdens in other states.

What Is the Cost of Living in Mesquite Versus Las Vegas?

Mesquite's cost of living runs generally lower than the Las Vegas metropolitan area, making it attractive to budget-conscious homebuyers and investors. Home list prices reflect this affordability, with a median home list price of $372,500. However, the market spans widely, from homes priced at $49,990 to luxury properties reaching $1,899,000, accommodating virtually every budget level. While Mesquite offers affordability, it's important to acknowledge tradeoffs. Some services are more limited than Las Vegas; the job market is smaller, and certain healthcare specialists may require travel to larger cities. St. George, Utah, approximately 30 to 35 minutes away, serves as the nearest major hub for specialty shopping and services.

Is Mesquite's Growth Sustainable?

Mesquite demonstrates strong demographic momentum with a current population of 25,094, growing at 3.07% annually. Since 2020, the community has experienced 21.77% growth, indicating sustained interest from new residents. This growth trajectory suggests continued economic development and property value appreciation. Is Mesquite Affordable for Your Situation?

Affordability is relative. Mesquite appeals to retirees seeking lower living costs, families prioritizing financial security, and investors searching for appreciation potential in a growing market. The combination of no state income tax, low property taxes, and below-Vegas prices creates a compelling financial picture. However, the trade-off involves services and job market limitations. For remote workers, retirees, and business owners, these limitations are irrelevant. For job-seekers, careful consideration is warranted.
