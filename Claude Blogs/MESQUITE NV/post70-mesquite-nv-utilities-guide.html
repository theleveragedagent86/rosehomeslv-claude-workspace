Utilities in Mesquite NV are delivered by a mix of public and private providers: Overton Power District No. 5 for electric, Virgin Valley Water District for water, Southwest Gas for natural gas, and the City of Mesquite for sewer service. ## Who Provides Electricity in Mesquite NV? Electric service across Mesquite NV comes from Overton Power District No. 5 (OPD5), a public utility serving the region since 1935. OPD5 operates a local office at 731 Turtleback Road and can be reached at 702-346-5710. Average residential rates run about 11.5 cents per kilowatt hour, and the typical monthly bill sits near $165 depending on home size and summer cooling demand. In 2024, a new power purchase agreement drove a roughly 6.37 percent rate increase, adding about $8.40 to the average monthly bill. OPD5 remains one of the more affordable utility districts in southern Nevada thanks to its public ownership structure. ## What Utility Handles Water and Sewer in Mesquite? Water service comes from the Virgin Valley Water District (VVWD) at 500 Riverside Road; the main phone line is 702-346-5731. VVWD pulls from nine wells in Basin 222 and serves roughly 18,000 residents across Mesquite and Bunkerville. Rates are tiered by meter size, bills are due by the 15th of each month, and a 10 percent late fee applies after that date. A three year rate increase was approved in November 2023 to fund infrastructure upgrades. Sewer service is handled directly by the City of Mesquite and billed alongside municipal services. Natural gas for cooking, heating, and water heaters comes from Southwest Gas, the regional provider across Nevada and parts of Arizona and California. ## What About Trash, Internet, and Other Services? Most Mesquite neighborhoods bundle trash pickup into HOA fees or city service agreements, depending on the community. Internet is covered separately with multiple providers competing in the market, a topic addressed in its own dedicated guide. New residents typically set up OPD5, VVWD, and Southwest Gas accounts before closing, and many HOAs coordinate sewer and trash transitions through the title company. ## Ryan Rose on Setting Up Utilities in Mesquite Ryan Rose, who has walked hundreds of buyers through their Mesquite NV closings, notes that the smartest move is calling OPD5 and VVWD about three business days before the closing date. Public utilities process account transfers quickly, but setting them up too late can leave a new home without power or water on move in day. He also reminds buyers to ask whether the HOA includes trash or landscape water. Planning a move to Mesquite?
