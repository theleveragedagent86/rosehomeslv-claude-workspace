The Mesquite Hot Air Balloon Festival is a free annual event scheduled January 23 through 25, 2026, featuring more than 30 balloons in a mass ascension launched from the Oasis parking lot directly across from CasaBlanca Resort in Mesquite NV. Spectators watch sunrise liftoffs, an evening Night Glow, and entertainment in the CasaBlanca Showroom throughout the weekend. ## When Is the Mesquite Hot Air Balloon Festival? The Mesquite Hot Air Balloon Festival runs January 23 to 25, 2026, with admission free to the public at all balloon field events. More than 30 colorful hot air balloons gather for the mass ascension, which takes off from the Oasis parking lot across from CasaBlanca Resort on Mesquite Boulevard. The sunrise launches are the visual highlight of the festival, drawing photographers from across southern Nevada, Utah, and Arizona. Friday and Saturday evenings feature the Night Glow from 6 to 7 pm, where tethered balloons fire their burners in unison against the darkening sky. At 8 pm, live entertainment takes over the CasaBlanca Showroom for the 21 and older crowd, extending the festival from morning through night. ## Where Do the Balloons Launch in Mesquite? Balloons launch from the Oasis parking lot, positioned directly across from CasaBlanca Resort at 950 W Mesquite Blvd. The location sits just off Interstate 15 Exit 120, making it easy to reach from St. George to the north or Las Vegas to the south. Spectators typically arrive before dawn to watch crews inflate the envelopes and catch the ascension at first light. Viewing is free and open to all ages, with no tickets required for the field events. Local vendors set up food and coffee service around the launch site, and the CasaBlanca casino, hotel, and restaurants remain active throughout the weekend for visitors who want to make a full getaway of it. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose frequently fields questions about Mesquite NV winter events and explains that the Hot Air Balloon Festival falls just a week after Motor Mania, creating back to back weekends that showcase why retirees and snowbirds flock here in January.
