Mesquite NV hosts two farmers markets: the Downtown Mesquite Farmers Market at Front Street Station, open Saturdays 9 am to 1 pm from April through November, and the Mesquite Plaza Friday Night Farmers Markets, which run year round. Both gather local produce, crafts, and prepared foods in central Mesquite. ## Where Are the Mesquite NV Farmers Markets? The primary weekend option, the Downtown Mesquite Farmers Market, sets up at Front Street Station and operates Saturdays from 9 am to 1 pm during the April through November growing season. Shoppers find seasonal produce, baked goods, jams, handcrafted gifts, and prepared foods from regional vendors. The downtown location puts the market within walking distance of Mesquite's original city center and main street businesses. The second option, the Mesquite Plaza Friday Night Farmers Markets, runs year round in the evening, giving residents and visitors a weekly after work destination that operates even during the hot summer months when Saturday morning markets close. Together the two farmers markets in Mesquite NV keep local shopping active across every season. ## What Do Mesquite Farmers Markets Sell? Vendors at both markets typically offer southern Nevada and Utah regional produce including tomatoes, peppers, melons, peaches, and leafy greens in peak season. Local bakers bring breads and pastries, and honey producers, jam makers, and small batch food artisans round out the food offerings. Craft booths add handmade jewelry, candles, soaps, and art. Food trucks and prepared food vendors often join the Friday Night market, giving it more of a community gathering feel alongside the shopping. Because Mesquite sits in the Virgin River valley with a long growing season and mild winters, the farmers market calendar stays active far longer here than in most of Nevada. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose, who has toured hundreds of Mesquite NV homes, notes that proximity to Front Street and Mesquite Plaza is a quiet differentiator when buyers compare neighborhoods.
