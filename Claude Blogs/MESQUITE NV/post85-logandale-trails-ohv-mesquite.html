Logandale Trails sits about 35 miles southwest of Mesquite NV and offers more than 200 miles of OHV, ATV, horseback, and hiking trails across BLM-managed red rock terrain, making it one of the premier off-road destinations in southern Nevada. ## Where Is Logandale Trails OHV Riding Near Mesquite? Logandale Trails near Mesquite is located in the Moapa Valley, roughly a 45 minute drive from town via I-15 south to Exit 93. The trail system is managed by the Bureau of Land Management and features hundreds of miles of interconnected routes that wind through sandstone washes, narrow canyons, and elevated ridgelines with views toward Valley of Fire State Park. Trails are rated across a wide range of difficulty, from beginner friendly sand washes to technical rock crawls that challenge experienced drivers. Riders will encounter staging areas with vault toilets but no potable water, so plan to pack in all supplies. ## What Kind of OHVs Can You Ride at Logandale Trails? The system welcomes ATVs, UTVs, dirt bikes, Jeeps, side by sides, and full size 4WD trucks. Horseback riders and mountain bikers share many of the routes, and the terrain accommodates everything from a casual afternoon loop to a full weekend of exploration. The loose sand and slickrock surfaces favor aggressive tires and properly aired down pressures. Riders under 18 must wear DOT approved helmets under Nevada law, and all OHVs must display current Nevada registration. Glow plug start times vary by season, but early mornings in cooler months bring the best combination of traction and temperature. ## When Is the Best Season to Ride Logandale Trails? Winter and spring deliver the ideal riding conditions, with daytime temperatures typically ranging from the 50s to the 70s between November and April. Summer rides carry real risk of heat exhaustion, and afternoon monsoon storms can flood the washes without warning.
