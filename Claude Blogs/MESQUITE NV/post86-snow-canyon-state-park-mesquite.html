Snow Canyon State Park sits about 50 miles northeast of Mesquite NV, roughly a one hour drive north on I-15, and offers red Navajo sandstone cliffs, ancient lava tubes, and more than a dozen trails inside Utah's Red Cliffs Desert Reserve. ## How Far Is Snow Canyon State Park from Mesquite NV? Snow Canyon State Park from Mesquite NV is a straightforward 50 mile drive, taking about one hour via I-15 north to St. George, then west on Snow Canyon Parkway to the park entrance near Ivins, Utah. Day use fees apply per vehicle, and the park opens at sunrise and closes at 10 pm. Gates, restrooms, and a paved scenic drive make it one of the most accessible state parks in the region for a day trip from Mesquite. Snow Canyon covers 7,400 acres of striking geology: frozen lava flows, black basalt ridges, and red and white sandstone domes that contrast sharply with the surrounding desert. ## What Are the Best Trails at Snow Canyon? Jenny's Canyon is the shortest signature hike, a half mile round trip into a cool slot canyon. Whiptail Trail is a paved 3 mile round trip suitable for strollers and bikes, running along the park's main corridor. Hidden Pinyon winds 1.5 miles through sandstone formations to a scenic overlook, while Johnson Canyon leads to a seasonal arch. More demanding options include Cinder Cone, a steep climb up an extinct volcano with panoramic views, and Padre Canyon, a 6.3 mile route favored by experienced hikers. Lava tubes near the Whiptail area require a flashlight and a willingness to scramble. ## When Should You Visit Snow Canyon State Park? Spring and fall deliver ideal temperatures, with March through May and September through November offering highs in the 60s and 70s. Summer visitors should hike at sunrise because afternoon surface temperatures on the slickrock routinely exceed 120 degrees.
