Fishing near Mesquite NV centers on three main waters: Mesquite Urban Pond at Hafen Lane Park (stocked seasonally with rainbow trout and channel catfish by the Nevada Department of Wildlife), the Virgin River (largemouth bass and Utah chub), and Lake Mead about 1.5 hours south for striped bass. ## Where Is the Best Fishing Near Mesquite NV? The best fishing near Mesquite NV for quick outings is the Mesquite Urban Pond inside Hafen Lane Park at 450 Hafen Ln. The Nevada Department of Wildlife (NDOW) stocks the pond seasonally with rainbow trout in the cooler months and channel catfish during the warmer season. The pond sits alongside a playground, BBQ area, and pickleball courts, making it ideal for family outings. The Virgin River runs right through Mesquite and holds largemouth bass, rainbow trout, and Utah chub. Access points are free along the river corridor, though flow levels vary significantly by season and recent runoff. ## What Are the Fishing Limits in Mesquite NV? Clark County fishing limits allow up to 10 trout and 15 warmwater game fish per day, with a sub-limit of 10 black bass within the warmwater count. A Nevada fishing license is required for anglers age 12 and up, and licenses can be purchased online through NDOW or at local sporting goods retailers. Lake Mead, about 90 minutes south via I-15 and US-93, is the region's big-water destination. Echo Bay Marina and the Boulder Beach Pier are two of the most productive spots for striped bass, which often run large during spring and fall feeding windows. Largemouth bass, crappie, and channel catfish round out the Lake Mead fishery. ## When Is the Best Time to Fish Near Mesquite? Cool months from October through April deliver the best trout action at Mesquite Urban Pond, matching NDOW's seasonal stocking schedule. Striped bass fishing at Lake Mead peaks in spring and fall.
