Moapa Valley near Mesquite is an unincorporated Clark County community about 35 miles southwest of Mesquite, formed when Logandale and Overton merged in 1981, and anchored by Mormon pioneer heritage dating to the 1860s, the Lost City Museum in Overton, and Valley of Fire State Park on its eastern edge. ## Where Is Moapa Valley Located? Moapa Valley near Mesquite sits along I-15 and SR-169 in northeastern Clark County. Logandale is roughly 35 miles southwest of Mesquite via I-15, and Overton is about 5 miles further southeast. The two towns officially merged as the unincorporated town of Moapa Valley in 1981, though both retain distinct identities and post offices. The valley is ringed by dramatic desert scenery, including the red sandstone formations of Valley of Fire State Park and the recreation areas around Lake Mead's Overton Arm. Farming, ranching, and outdoor tourism drive the local economy. ## What Is There to Do in Logandale and Overton? Logandale is best known for the Logandale Trails System, a BLM-managed network of more than 200 miles of trails for OHV riding, horseback, and hiking. Winter and spring are peak seasons thanks to mild temperatures. The Clark County Fairgrounds in Logandale host the annual Clark County Fair each April, one of the largest community events in southern Nevada. Overton is home to the Lost City Museum, which preserves Ancestral Puebloan artifacts recovered before the Virgin River valley was flooded to create Lake Mead. Echo Bay Marina on Lake Mead, accessible via Overton, is a launch point for striped bass fishing and boating. ## Is Moapa Valley a Good Place to Live?
