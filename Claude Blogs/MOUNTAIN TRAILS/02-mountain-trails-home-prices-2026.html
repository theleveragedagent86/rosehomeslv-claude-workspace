Mountain Trails Summerlin Home Prices 2026

Mountain Trails home prices in 2026 range from approximately $1,000,000 to over $5,000,000, with a current median price around $2,640,000. This guard gated Summerlin community remains one of the most sought after luxury neighborhoods in Las Vegas.

What Do Homes in Mountain Trails Cost Right Now?

The wide price range reflects the custom nature of every home in Mountain Trails. Entry level properties, typically smaller or older builds on half acre lots, start near the $1,000,000 mark. Fully renovated or larger estates on premium lots can exceed $5,000,000. The $2,640,000 median gives buyers a realistic benchmark for what most transactions look like in this community.

Mountain Trails consists of just 103 homes, so inventory is always limited. When a property does come to market, it tends to attract serious buyers quickly. The combination of half acre to full acre lots, custom architecture, and guard gated security creates consistent demand from luxury homebuyers relocating to Las Vegas.

Why Are Mountain Trails Homes Priced at a Premium?

Several factors drive Mountain Trails pricing. Every home was custom built between 1996 and 2004, meaning each property has a unique floor plan, architectural style, and finish level. Lot sizes of half an acre or more are exceptionally rare in Summerlin. The 24 hour guard gate, private park, tennis courts, and access to The Trails Community Center add further value.

Location plays a significant role as well. Mountain Trails sits within The Trails Village, just minutes from Downtown Summerlin shopping, Red Rock Canyon, top rated schools, and Summerlin Hospital Medical Center. The Las Vegas Strip is only a 15 to 20 minute drive away.

A Local Perspective on Mountain Trails Pricing

Ryan Rose notes that Mountain Trails offers a compelling value compared to newer guard gated communities in Summerlin. Buyers get significantly larger lots and more established landscaping, often at a lower price per square foot than comparable properties in communities built more recently. For those who prioritize space, privacy, and a mature neighborhood feel, the numbers make strong sense.
