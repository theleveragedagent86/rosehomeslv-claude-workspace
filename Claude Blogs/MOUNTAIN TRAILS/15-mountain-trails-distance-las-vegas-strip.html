How Far Is Mountain Trails from the Las Vegas Strip?

One of the most common questions from buyers considering Mountain Trails is about distance to the Strip. The answer: approximately 10 miles and a 15 to 20 minute drive, giving residents quick access to world class entertainment while living in a quiet, guard gated neighborhood.

What Is the Drive Time from Mountain Trails to the Las Vegas Strip?

Mountain Trails sits in the western part of Summerlin, roughly 10 miles from the heart of the Las Vegas Strip. The most direct routes use Summerlin Parkway east to US 95 or the 215 Beltway south to I-15. During normal traffic conditions, the drive takes 15 to 20 minutes.

Rush hour can add 5 to 10 minutes depending on the specific destination along the Strip. Evening drives for dinner or shows typically run on the shorter end since traffic flows outbound from the valley during those hours.

How Far Is Mountain Trails from Other Key Destinations?

The Strip is just one piece of the location picture. Harry Reid International Airport is approximately 12.4 miles away, reachable in 25 to 30 minutes. Downtown Las Vegas takes about 20 to 25 minutes. Closer to home, Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is roughly two miles away, and Downtown Summerlin's shopping and dining district sits just three miles from the community.

For outdoor recreation, Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is a 15 minute drive west. TPC Summerlin, home to the PGA Tour's Shriners Children's Open, is also minutes away. This central positioning within Summerlin means Mountain Trails residents reach nearly every major Las Vegas destination in under 30 minutes.

A Local Perspective on the Mountain Trails Location

Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate specialist, emphasizes that Mountain Trails occupies a sweet spot in the valley. Residents enjoy the seclusion of a guard gated community with half acre lots and mature landscaping, yet they are never more than 20 minutes from the Strip, the airport, or world class dining. Buyers relocating from other cities are often pleasantly surprised by how compact Las Vegas feels once they settle into Summerlin.
