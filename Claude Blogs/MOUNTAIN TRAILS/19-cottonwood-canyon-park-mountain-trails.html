Cottonwood Canyon Park Near Mountain Trails: Desert Trails and Scenic Views

Cottonwood Canyon Park brings the natural beauty of the Mojave right to the edge of Summerlin's residential villages. For Mountain Trails residents who want to hike or bike without driving to Red Rock Canyon, this park delivers desert landscape views just minutes from home.

What Makes Cottonwood Canyon Park Unique?

Unlike the manicured sports parks found throughout Summerlin, Cottonwood Canyon Park leans into its desert setting. The park features scenic walking and biking trails that wind through natural terrain, offering views of the surrounding canyon landscape. It sits near TPC Las Vegas, placing it in the western corridor of Summerlin where the community meets the foothills. For residents who prefer a trail that feels more like a hike than a neighborhood stroll, Cottonwood Canyon delivers.

Is Cottonwood Canyon Park Good for Biking?

Yes. The trails at Cottonwood Canyon Park accommodate both walkers and cyclists, making it a popular destination for morning rides and after work loops. The terrain provides more variety than Summerlin's flat paved pathways, so riders looking for gentle elevation changes and desert scenery will find it here. The park also connects to Summerlin's broader 200 plus mile trail system, allowing cyclists to extend their route in any direction.

How Far Is Cottonwood Canyon Park from Mountain Trails?

Cottonwood Canyon Park is a short drive from Mountain Trails, located in the western portion of Summerlin near TPC Las Vegas. Residents can also reach the park via Summerlin's interconnected trail network, making it accessible by bike without ever hitting a main road. Its position on the edge of developed Summerlin means the views feel more open and natural compared to parks deeper within the community.
