Ernest A. Becker Sr. Middle School: The Zoned Option for Mountain Trails Students

When Mountain Trails students finish elementary school, their next stop is Ernest A. Becker Sr. Middle School. As the zoned middle school for the 89134 zip code, Becker serves as the bridge between elementary and high school for families in this guard gated Summerlin community.

What Grades Does Becker Middle School Serve?

Ernest A. Becker Sr. Middle School is a public school in the Clark County School District serving students in grades 6 through 8. The campus handles the critical transition years when students move from a single classroom teacher model to a subject specific rotation. Becker is the designated zoned middle school for Mountain Trails and the broader Trails Village area, meaning families in 89134 have guaranteed enrollment.

What Academic Programs Are Available at Becker Middle School?

Becker Middle School offers the standard CCSD curriculum along with elective options that prepare students for high school. The school provides honors level coursework in core subjects, giving advanced students the opportunity to stay challenged. Extracurricular activities, athletic programs, and student organizations round out the experience, keeping students engaged beyond the classroom.

Middle school is where students begin building the academic foundation for high school success. Becker's position as a neighborhood school means Mountain Trails families can stay involved in their children's education without long commutes or complicated logistics. The school also feeds directly into Palo Verde High School, creating a clear and consistent academic pathway for Summerlin students.

How Does Becker Middle School Fit into the Mountain Trails School Pipeline?

Mountain Trails families benefit from a well defined school pipeline: Lummis Elementary or Bryan Elementary for grades K through 5, Becker Middle School for grades 6 through 8, and Palo Verde High School for grades 9 through 12. This continuity means students grow up alongside the same peers, building friendships and community connections that last through graduation. The entire pathway stays within the Summerlin corridor, keeping commutes short and parent involvement high.
