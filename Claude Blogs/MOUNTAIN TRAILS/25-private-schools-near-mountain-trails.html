Private Schools Near Mountain Trails: Top Options in Summerlin

Mountain Trails families who prefer a private school education have two nationally recognized campuses within a short drive. Both the Adelson Educational Campus and The Meadows School offer rigorous academics, small class sizes, and college preparatory programs that rank among the best in Nevada.

What Is the Adelson Educational Campus Near Mountain Trails?

The Adelson Educational Campus is a private school serving grades K through 12, located roughly two miles from Mountain Trails. The school holds a Niche grade of A+ and ranks as the #2 Best Private K through 12 school in Nevada. Enrollment sits at approximately 510 students with an impressive 8:1 student to teacher ratio and an average class size of just 12 students.

Adelson offers 14 AP and advanced courses across English, History, Math, and Science. The school maintains a 75% acceptance rate, and 40% of students receive financial aid. While the campus is rooted in Jewish community values, it welcomes students of all faiths and affiliations. Tuition ranges from approximately $19,000 to $31,200 depending on grade level. Faculty credentials are strong: 85% of teachers hold advanced degrees.

What Does The Meadows School Offer Families?

The Meadows School serves students in Pre K through 12 on a 40 acre campus approximately three miles from Mountain Trails. With a Niche grade of A+ and recognition as a top ranked school in Nevada, The Meadows provides one of the most comprehensive private educations in the state. The school enrolls roughly 930 students and maintains a 7:1 student to teacher ratio with an average class size of 11.

Academic rigor is a hallmark: The Meadows offers 30 AP courses spanning subjects from Biology and Calculus to Computer Science and Studio Art. The school boasts 100% college placement, and 90% of its faculty hold advanced degrees. Tuition ranges from $21,660 to $33,180 by division. With 15 interscholastic sports and membership in the National Association of Independent Schools (NAIS), The Meadows combines academics with a full campus experience.

How Do These Private Schools Compare?

Both schools deliver elite academics, but they differ in important ways. Adelson offers smaller enrollment and a more intimate campus experience with a faith based foundation. The Meadows provides a larger student body, more AP course options, and a 40 acre campus with extensive athletic facilities. Tuition at Adelson starts lower, while The Meadows' broader course catalog may appeal to families seeking maximum academic variety. Both schools place graduates in top universities nationwide.
