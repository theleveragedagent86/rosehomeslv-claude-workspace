Trails Village Center Restaurants: Dining Steps from Mountain Trails

Mountain Trails residents have a full lineup of casual restaurants and cafes less than one mile away at Trails Village Center. This neighborhood retail hub at the corner of Far Hills Avenue and Town Center Drive serves as the go to dining spot for The Trails Village in Summerlin.

What Restaurants Are at Trails Village Center?

Trails Village Center offers a mix of quick service and fast casual options designed for everyday convenience. Current tenants include Chipotle Mexican Grill, Mountain Mike's Pizza, Domino's Pizza, Einstein Bros. Bagels, Port of Subs, McDonald's, and Nekter Juice Bar. A Starbucks anchors the center's coffee scene, and Foxtail Coffee is expected to open soon, adding a second specialty coffee option to the plaza.

How Far Is Trails Village Center from Mountain Trails?

The center sits less than one mile from Mountain Trails' guard gate, making it the closest dining and retail destination for residents. Most trips take under three minutes by car or roughly 10 minutes on foot. The proximity makes Trails Village Center the default stop for morning coffee, a quick lunch, or grabbing dinner when cooking is not on the agenda. The shopping center also includes everyday retail and services, so residents can combine errands with a meal in a single trip.

Is Trails Village Center Enough for Everyday Dining?

For quick meals and casual bites, Trails Village Center covers the essentials. The mix leans toward fast casual and chain restaurants rather than sit down dining. Residents looking for a broader selection of full service restaurants will find dozens of options three to five miles away at Downtown Summerlin, where upscale steakhouses, farm to table concepts, and international cuisine round out the dining picture.
