Red Rock Canyon Near Mountain Trails Summerlin

Living in Mountain Trails puts one of the most spectacular natural landmarks in the American Southwest practically in your backyard. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area sits roughly 15 minutes from the community, offering world class scenery, hiking, and rock climbing year round.

What Makes Red Rock Canyon Special?

Red Rock Canyon spans over 195,000 acres of protected desert landscape west of Las Vegas. The centerpiece is a 13 mile scenic drive that loops through towering sandstone formations with striking red, orange, and cream colored rock faces. Visitors can drive the loop in about 30 minutes or stop at multiple pullouts and trailheads along the way. The area draws nearly three million visitors annually, and Mountain Trails residents can access it before the crowds arrive on any given morning.

For hikers, the canyon offers more than 30 miles of trails ranging from easy nature walks to strenuous scrambles. Popular routes include Calico Tanks, Keystone Thrust, and Ice Box Canyon. The area also ranks as a world class rock climbing destination, attracting climbers from across the globe to scale sandstone walls with routes for every skill level.

How Close Is Red Rock Canyon to Mountain Trails?

The drive from Mountain Trails to the Red Rock Canyon visitor center takes approximately 15 minutes via West Charleston Boulevard. That proximity means residents can fit a morning hike into a weekday schedule or enjoy a sunset drive through the scenic loop on a whim. Few master planned communities in the Las Vegas Valley offer this kind of access to protected wilderness while still sitting within minutes of shopping, dining, and top rated schools.
