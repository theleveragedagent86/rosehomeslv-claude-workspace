Mountain Trails Summerlin Safety and Crime Rates

Safety is the first question most buyers ask before choosing a neighborhood. Mountain Trails in Summerlin delivers some of the strongest safety numbers in the Las Vegas valley, backed by both public law enforcement and private security layers that few communities can match.

How Safe Is Summerlin Compared to the National Average?

Summerlin's violent crime rate sits at 15.9 per 1,000 residents, well below the national average of 22.7. Property crime follows the same trend at 30 per 1,000, compared to 35.4 nationally. These figures place Summerlin among the safest large master planned communities in the western United States and reflect a consistent pattern of low crime that has held for years.

Mountain Trails benefits from an additional security layer beyond these already favorable numbers. The community operates a 24 hour guard gate that controls vehicle access into the neighborhood. This means only residents, their guests, and authorized visitors pass through, significantly reducing opportunistic crime and unwanted solicitation.

What Security Resources Protect Mountain Trails?

Two distinct forces patrol the area. The Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department's Summerlin Area Command provides public law enforcement with officers who know the local streets and respond quickly to calls. On top of that, the Summerlin Council maintains its own private security team that patrols community common areas, parks, and roadways throughout the master plan.

Inside Mountain Trails, the guard gate adds a third tier. The combination of LVMPD, private Summerlin patrols, and a staffed gate creates overlapping coverage that most Las Vegas neighborhoods simply do not offer. With only 103 custom homes behind the gate, the community is small enough that neighbors recognize unfamiliar vehicles and faces quickly.
