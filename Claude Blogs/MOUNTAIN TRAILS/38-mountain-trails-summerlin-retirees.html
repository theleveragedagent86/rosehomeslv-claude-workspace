Mountain Trails Summerlin for Retirees

Retirement in Las Vegas does not have to mean living on the Strip or settling for a cookie cutter 55 plus community. Mountain Trails in Summerlin offers retirees a guard gated neighborhood with custom homes, low crime, and proximity to healthcare that checks every box on the retirement wish list.

Is Mountain Trails a Good Neighborhood for Retirees?

Mountain Trails is not a designated 55 plus community, which many retirees actually prefer. There are no age restrictions, meaning residents enjoy a multigenerational neighborhood without the limitations that come with age restricted developments. The community contains 103 custom homes, and single story floor plans are available for buyers who want to avoid stairs.

The guard gated entrance provides 24 hour controlled access, creating a quiet and secure environment. Summerlin's violent crime rate of 15.9 per 1,000 residents falls well below the national average of 22.7, giving retirees added peace of mind. Streets inside the gate see minimal traffic, making daily walks safe and enjoyable.

How Close Is Mountain Trails to Healthcare?

Summerlin Hospital Medical Center sits approximately two miles from Mountain Trails. This full service hospital provides emergency care, surgical services, and specialty clinics, eliminating the long drives that retirees in more remote communities often face. Additional medical offices, urgent care centers, and pharmacies are clustered along the Summerlin Parkway corridor within minutes of the neighborhood.

Downtown Summerlin, located about three miles away, offers shopping, dining, and entertainment options that keep daily life convenient without requiring a trip across town. Red Rock Canyon is roughly a 15 minute drive, providing scenic excursions for active retirees who enjoy hiking and nature.
