Mountain Trails vs The Ridges in Summerlin

Mountain Trails and The Ridges represent two generations of Summerlin luxury living, each with its own character, price point, and appeal. Choosing between them comes down to what you value most in a custom home community.

How Do Mountain Trails and The Ridges Compare on Price and Construction?

Mountain Trails features 103 custom homes built between 1996 and 2004 on half acre to full acre lots. Home sizes range from 3,758 to 13,106 square feet, with prices spanning $1,000,000 to over $5,000,000 and a median around $2,640,000. Architectural styles include Mediterranean, contemporary, and desert modern.

The Ridges is Summerlin's premier luxury community, with custom homes built from 2006 to the present on lots ranging from half an acre to over one acre. Prices in The Ridges typically start around $2,000,000 and climb past $10,000,000, reflecting newer construction, modern floor plans, and high end finishes. Buyers seeking the latest smart home technology and contemporary design often gravitate toward The Ridges.

What Lifestyle and Location Differences Should Buyers Consider?

Mountain Trails sits in The Trails Village with access to Trails Village Center, a private community park, and Summerlin's extensive trail system. The neighborhood's mature trees and established landscaping create a warm, settled environment that newer communities have not yet developed.

The Ridges offers proximity to Bear's Best golf course and dramatic views of Red Rock Canyon from many homesites. The community feels more expansive, with wider streets and a more modern architectural palette. Both are guard gated with 24 hour security, but The Ridges carries a higher price of entry and typically higher HOA fees to match its newer amenities.

Ryan Rose's Perspective on These Two Communities

Ryan Rose points out that Mountain Trails offers an exceptional value proposition for buyers who want guard gated custom living without The Ridges' premium pricing. The mature landscaping, generous lot sizes, and tight knit community of just 103 homes create something that new construction cannot replicate overnight. For buyers with a larger budget who prioritize the newest finishes and contemporary architecture, The Ridges remains the benchmark.
