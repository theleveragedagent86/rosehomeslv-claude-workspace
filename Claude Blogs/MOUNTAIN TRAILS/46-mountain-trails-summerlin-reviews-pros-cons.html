Mountain Trails Summerlin Reviews: Pros and Cons

Every luxury neighborhood has tradeoffs. Mountain Trails in Summerlin consistently ranks among the most desirable guard gated communities in Las Vegas, but understanding both sides helps buyers make confident decisions before writing an offer.

What Do Residents Love About Mountain Trails?

Privacy and exclusivity top the list. Mountain Trails contains only 103 custom homes on half acre to full acre lots, making it one of the smallest and most private enclaves in Summerlin. A 24 hour guard gate controls all vehicle access, and entry requires an appointment. Residents describe the atmosphere as quiet, safe, and neighborly without feeling crowded.

Location is the second major advantage. The community sits within The Trails Village, placing residents minutes from Downtown Summerlin shopping, TPC Summerlin golf, and Red Rock Canyon hiking. The Las Vegas Strip is 15 to 20 minutes away, and McCarran International Airport is reachable in 25 to 30 minutes. Summerlin Hospital is roughly two miles from the neighborhood entrance.

School access rounds out the positives. Lummis Elementary, Becker Middle School, and Palo Verde High School serve the area through the Clark County School District. Private options like The Meadows School and Adelson Educational Campus sit within three miles. Summerlin's violent crime rate of 15.9 per 1,000 residents falls well below the national average of 22.7, reinforcing the safety factor that families value.

What Are the Drawbacks of Mountain Trails?

Price is the most obvious barrier. Homes range from $1 million to over $5 million, with a median around $2.64 million. HOA fees of $100 to $200 per month add to the ongoing cost. Construction dates span 1996 to 2004, so some homes may need kitchen, bathroom, or mechanical system updates to meet current standards. Inventory is also extremely limited. With only 103 total homes, listings appear infrequently, and competition among buyers can be intense when one does hit the market.

Ryan Rose's Local Insight

Ryan Rose has walked through dozens of Mountain Trails homes and consistently sees buyers weigh the same question: is the premium worth it? The answer depends on priorities. Buyers who value space, privacy, and a true residential feel find that Mountain Trails delivers something no newer Summerlin community replicates. The lots are larger, the streets are quieter, and the guard gate creates genuine separation from the surrounding area. For those who want turnkey finishes, newer construction in The Ridges or Reverence may be a better fit.
