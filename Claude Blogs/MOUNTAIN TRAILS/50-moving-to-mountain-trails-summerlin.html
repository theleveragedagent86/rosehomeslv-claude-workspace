Moving to Mountain Trails Summerlin

Relocating to a guard gated luxury community requires more planning than a typical move. Mountain Trails in Summerlin has specific access protocols, limited inventory, and a buying process that rewards preparation over impulse.

What Should You Know Before Buying in Mountain Trails?

Mountain Trails contains only 103 custom homes, which means listings are rare. When a property does become available, it typically attracts serious buyers quickly. Homes range from $1 million to over $5 million, with sizes spanning 3,758 to 13,106 square feet on half acre to full acre lots. Architectural styles include Mediterranean, contemporary, and desert modern. Because construction dates range from 1996 to 2004, buyers should budget for potential updates to kitchens, bathrooms, or mechanical systems depending on the specific home.

The community operates a 24 hour guard gate, and all visits require an appointment. This applies during the home search phase as well. Buyers cannot simply drive through to browse. Working with an agent who has existing relationships within the community makes the touring process smoother and faster.

What Are the Costs of Living in Mountain Trails?

Beyond the purchase price, HOA fees run approximately $100 to $200 per month. These fees cover guard gate staffing, common area maintenance, and community upkeep. Property taxes in the 89134 zip code follow Clark County assessment rates. Buyers relocating from states with higher property tax burdens, such as California or New York, often find Nevada's lack of state income tax and comparatively lower property taxes to be a meaningful financial benefit.

What Is Nearby After You Move In?

Daily life in Mountain Trails is convenient. Summerlin Hospital is roughly two miles away. Grocery stores, restaurants, and retail at Trails Village Center sit within minutes. Downtown Summerlin provides expanded shopping and dining. The Las Vegas Strip is 15 to 20 minutes by car, Harry Reid International Airport takes 25 to 30 minutes, and Red Rock Canyon is a short drive west. Public schools include Lummis Elementary, Becker Middle School, and Palo Verde High School, while private options like The Meadows School and Adelson Educational Campus are within three miles.
