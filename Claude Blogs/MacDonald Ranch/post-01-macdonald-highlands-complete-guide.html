MacDonald Highlands Henderson NV: Complete Guide to Las Vegas's Most Exclusive Hilltop Community

MacDonald Highlands is a 1,320 acre guard gated luxury community perched at 2,500 to 2,700 feet of elevation in Henderson's McCullough Mountains, offering some of the most dramatic Las Vegas Strip views in the entire valley. With home prices ranging from roughly $475,000 to $25 million (and higher for custom builds), this hilltop enclave has earned its reputation as the Beverly Hills of Las Vegas.

Where Is MacDonald Highlands?

MacDonald Highlands sits in the southeastern corner of Henderson, Nevada, within the highly coveted 89012 ZIP code, which ranks as the 7th highest per capita income ZIP in the United States. The community is approximately 15 miles from the Las Vegas Strip (about a 17 to 20 minute drive), 12 miles from Harry Reid International Airport, and just 8 minutes from The District at Green Valley Ranch for shopping and dining.

History and Development

The MacDonald family began acquiring land in the Las Vegas Valley in the early 1970s, eventually becoming the third largest private landowners behind Howard Hughes and the Greenspun family. When Henderson dismissed the terrain as "too rough to develop," Rich MacDonald saw opportunity. Development began in the late 1990s, with DragonRidge Country Club opening in 2000. Rich MacDonald still lives in the community today, a rarity among developers that speaks to his confidence in what he created.

What Makes MacDonald Highlands Different

MacDonald Highlands holds the distinction of being the lowest density master planned community in Southern Nevada, meaning larger lots, more privacy, and fewer neighbors. It was the first eco friendly hillside community in the Las Vegas Valley, with deeds that prohibit altering natural rock formations. Two 24 hour manned guard gates (with a third planned) plus secondary interior gates protect sub neighborhoods like Lairmont, Dragon Rock, and Vu Pointe. Henderson itself ranks as the 2nd safest large city in America.

The private DragonRidge Country Club anchors the lifestyle with an 18 hole championship course, a 44,000 square foot clubhouse, four dining venues, tennis and pickleball courts, a fitness center, spa, and resort style pool. Membership is available exclusively to MacDonald Highlands residents.

Neighborhoods and New Development

MacDonald Highlands contains more than 16 distinct neighborhoods ranging from intimate custom enclaves like Lairmont (just 16 lots behind a private second gate) to builder communities such as SkyVu by Christopher Homes, Dragon Rock by Blue Heron, and the groundbreaking NEO by LIVV Homes, the world's first net zero luxury community. The most anticipated addition is the Four Seasons Private Residences Las Vegas: two towers with 171 residences and six standalone villas, priced from $3.5 million to $30 million, set to welcome residents in late 2026.
