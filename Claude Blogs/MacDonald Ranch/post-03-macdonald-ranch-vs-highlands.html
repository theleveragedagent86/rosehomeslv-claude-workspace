MacDonald Ranch vs. MacDonald Highlands: What's the Difference?

MacDonald Ranch and MacDonald Highlands are related but very different communities in Henderson, Nevada, and confusing them is one of the most common mistakes homebuyers make. MacDonald Ranch is the 3,200 acre master planned community that encompasses four distinct villages. MacDonald Highlands is Village III within that larger master plan, the guard gated luxury portion with DragonRidge Country Club. Understanding the distinction matters because prices range from under $300,000 in some MacDonald Ranch villages to over $25 million in MacDonald Highlands.

The Four Villages of MacDonald Ranch

When the MacDonald family developed their sprawling property between Eastern Avenue and Gibson Street, they divided it into four villages, each serving a completely different market. Village I was sold to Del Webb and became Sun City MacDonald Ranch, a 564 acre active adult community with 2,513 homes and the Desert Willow Golf Course. Village II became Sunridge at MacDonald Ranch, a family oriented community with mid range pricing and access to top rated schools including Coral Academy of Science and Pinecrest Academy.

Village III is MacDonald Highlands, the 1,320 acre guard gated luxury community perched at 2,500 to 2,700 feet of elevation in the McCullough Mountains. This is where you'll find custom estates from $475,000 to $25 million plus, DragonRidge Country Club, 24 hour manned guard gates, and 16 plus distinct neighborhoods. Village IV became The Canyons at MacDonald Ranch, which was entitled and sold to Lennar, then built out as a gated (but not guard gated) family community with homes generally ranging from $600,000 to $2.1 million.

Key Differences at a Glance

The most immediate difference is access: MacDonald Highlands has two 24 hour manned guard gates with a third planned, while the other villages use standard community gating or are ungated. Elevation is another major differentiator. MacDonald Highlands sits at the highest points in the McCullough Mountains with dramatic Las Vegas Strip views, while Sun City MacDonald Ranch sits at 2,100 to 2,400 feet and the other villages occupy lower terrain. Only MacDonald Highlands residents can join DragonRidge Country Club, the private 18 hole championship course that anchors the luxury lifestyle.
