Should You Buy or Build in MacDonald Highlands? A Cost Comparison

MacDonald Highlands offers both move in ready luxury homes and vacant lots for custom construction. Choosing between buying a resale or new spec home versus building from the ground up involves significantly different costs, timelines, and levels of customization. Here is how the numbers compare in 2026.

Custom Construction Costs

Building a custom home in MacDonald Highlands costs substantially more per square foot than standard Las Vegas construction. Where a typical Las Vegas build runs $130 to $200 per square foot, MacDonald Highlands custom homes operate at a two to four times premium due to hillside grading, premium materials, and the community's Desert Elegance architectural standards.

Mid luxury custom builds (quality finishes but not ultra high end) run approximately $300 to $400 per square foot for construction alone. High luxury builds comparable to Blue Heron's semi custom Dragon Rock homes cost $500 to $700 or more per square foot. Ultra luxury estates with features like disappearing glass walls, infinity edge pools, imported stone, and 10 car garages can reach $700 to $1,000 or more per square foot in construction costs alone.

Land Costs Add Up Quickly

Vacant lots in MacDonald Highlands currently range from approximately $700,000 to over $20 million. The median asking price for listed parcels sits around $3.99 million according to Redfin. Premium lots with full Strip views, golf course frontage, or ridgeline positions command the highest prices. Lot sizes range from approximately one third of an acre for standard home sites to over 14 acres for the largest available parcels.

Total Build Estimates

A 5,000 square foot mid range custom home on a moderately priced lot could total $3.8 million to $5.6 million including land, construction, soft costs, site work, and pool. A 7,000 square foot high end build runs approximately $6.5 million to $9.6 million. For a 10,000 square foot ultra luxury estate, expect a total investment of $10.5 million to $15.7 million or more. These estimates include architecture, engineering, permitting, landscaping, and typical hillside site preparation.

Buying a Resale or Spec Home

Purchasing a move in ready home eliminates construction risk and the 12 to 24 month (or longer) timeline of a custom build. Resale closings typically complete in 30 to 60 days. Semi custom options through builders like Christopher Homes and LIVV Homes offer some personalization while still delivering faster than a fully custom project. The trade off is less control over floor plan, finishes, and site orientation compared to a ground up build.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose works with both buyers purchasing existing homes and those building custom. His advice: if you find a resale home that checks 80 percent or more of your boxes, buying will almost always be more cost effective than building. Custom construction makes sense for buyers with very specific requirements (a particular view angle, an exact floor plan, specialized features) who are willing to invest the additional time and capital. MacDonald Highlands has approximately 11 approved builders to choose from, including Blue Heron, Christopher Homes, Sun West Custom Homes, and others.
