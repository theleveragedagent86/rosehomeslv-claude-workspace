MacDonald Highlands Vacant Lots for Sale: Prices, Sizes, and What's Still Available in 2026

MacDonald Highlands currently has 25 to 28 vacant lots listed for sale, with asking prices ranging from approximately $699,000 to $20.5 million. The median asking price sits around $3.99 million according to Redfin. For buyers who want to build a custom estate from the ground up in Henderson's most exclusive guard gated community, these parcels represent a finite and shrinking supply of buildable land.

Price Range and Lot Sizes

The least expensive lots start near $699,000 for approximately 1.92 acres near newer development areas. Mid range parcels with solid views and good positioning typically ask between $2 million and $5 million. Premium ridgeline lots with full Las Vegas Strip panoramas, golf course frontage, or mountaintop positioning can command $5 million to $20 million or more. The most expensive currently listed parcel sits at 677 Boulder Summit Drive, asking $20.5 million.

Standard lot sizes start at approximately one third of an acre for builder parcels, while custom home sites range from half an acre to over five acres. The largest available tract is roughly 14 acres associated with the Obsidian Rock development, which is planned for only 8 custom homesites across that entire acreage.

Where the Available Lots Are

Current inventory is spread across several areas within the community. Dragon Peak and The Peak area feature some of the highest elevation positions with sweeping views. Scenic Rim Drive offers parcels like 616/620 Scenic Rim Drive (approximately 0.91 acres, listed around $3.65 million) with panoramic views. Additional lots are available on Dragon Mountain Court, in Glenbrook Canyon, within the Equinox enclave (where 2 of 7 original lots remain), in the Vu Pointe area, and along Tangiers Drive.

View Premiums Are Significant

The view from a lot is the single biggest price driver. Parcels with full Las Vegas Strip views command two to three times the price of mountain only view lots. Golf course frontage adds an additional 50 to 100 percent premium. The ultimate combination of Strip views, golf course frontage, and mountain backdrop can push pricing to $5 million and well beyond for the lot alone before any construction begins.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose emphasizes that available lots in MacDonald Highlands are a finite resource. The community's 1,320 acres are not expanding, and every lot sold is one fewer option for future custom builders. Buyers serious about building should evaluate current lot inventory carefully, especially parcels with protected view corridors that cannot be obstructed by future development. MacDonald Highlands has approximately 11 approved builders, and all construction must meet the community's Desert Elegance architectural standards through the Architectural Review Committee.
