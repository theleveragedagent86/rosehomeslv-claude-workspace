MacDonald Highlands HOA Fees Explained: What $330 Per Month Actually Covers

The MacDonald Highlands master HOA fee is $330 per month ($3,960 per year), and the official community website notes this amount has remained the same for years. Some neighborhoods within the community carry additional sub association dues, bringing total monthly fees to between $330 and $829 depending on where you live. Here is exactly what you are paying for.

What the Master HOA Covers

The $330 monthly master fee funds the core services that define the MacDonald Highlands experience. The most visible benefit is 24 hour security: two manned guard gates (the Valle Verde Gate off Horizon Ridge Parkway and the Stephanie Gate off Stephanie Street) plus roving security patrols throughout the community around the clock. All guests must be pre registered, and visitors receive windshield passes at entry.

The fee also covers maintenance of common areas and landscaping, road maintenance throughout the community, upkeep of three neighborhood parks, maintenance of approximately five miles of paved walking and fitness trails that connect to the McCullough Mountain foothills, and the community's tennis, basketball, and volleyball courts. The Architectural Review Committee, which reviews all exterior modifications to maintain Desert Elegance design standards, operates under the HOA umbrella as well.

Sub Association Fees

Many neighborhoods within MacDonald Highlands carry a separate sub association fee on top of the $330 master dues. These sub HOA fees range from $0 to approximately $499 per month depending on the neighborhood. Double gated neighborhoods like Dragon Rock, Lairmont, and Vu Pointe typically carry sub association dues to fund their secondary gate security and additional common area maintenance. NEO by LIVV Homes markets itself as having "no HOA" for its sub association, but the $330 master fee still applies to all MacDonald Highlands residents.

Four Seasons Residences: A Different Structure

The upcoming Four Seasons Private Residences will operate under a different fee model. Residents will pay the $330 monthly master HOA plus approximately $1 per square foot per month for Four Seasons branded amenities and services. For a 3,000 square foot residence, that means roughly $3,330 per month in combined fees, reflecting the resort level staffing, concierge services, Wolfgang Puck dining, spa, and pool amenities exclusive to the towers.

HOA Management

The master association, formally named The Foothills at MacDonald Ranch Master Association, is managed by RPMG, Inc. A five person Board of Directors governs the community through quarterly meetings. Additional fees include transponder charges ($25 to $75) for gate access, park reservation deposits, and design review application fees for exterior modifications.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose points out that $330 per month for the level of security, maintenance, and amenities MacDonald Highlands provides is remarkably competitive compared to other guard gated luxury communities in the Las Vegas valley. The fee stability over multiple years is another positive signal, suggesting the association is well managed and adequately funded without the need for special assessments that can surprise homeowners in other communities.
