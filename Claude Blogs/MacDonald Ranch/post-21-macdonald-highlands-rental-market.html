MacDonald Highlands Rental Market: Can You Rent a Home and What Does It Cost?

Yes, you can rent a home in MacDonald Highlands, and long term lease prices currently range from approximately $1,400 to $28,000 per month depending on size, location, and finishes. However, there is one critical rule every prospective renter needs to understand before searching: short term and vacation rentals are strictly prohibited in this community.

Long Term Rental Availability

MacDonald Highlands maintains an owner occupancy rate of approximately 72.7 percent, meaning roughly 27 percent of homes are either rented out or held as secondary residences. At any given time, a handful of properties are available for long term lease, though inventory stays limited because most homeowners in this guard gated community either live in their homes full time or use them as personal second residences rather than income producing rentals.

What Rental Prices Look Like

The $1,400 to $28,000 monthly range reflects the extreme diversity of housing within MacDonald Highlands. At the lower end, you may find smaller attached Vu homes or older builds with more modest finishes. Mid range rentals between $4,000 and $8,000 per month typically offer 2,500 to 4,000 square feet with community views and standard luxury appointments. Premium rentals above $10,000 per month deliver custom home experiences with Strip views, resort style pools, and the full MacDonald Highlands lifestyle.

The Short Term Rental Ban

MacDonald Highlands HOA rules explicitly prohibit short term and vacation rentals, including platforms like Airbnb and VRBO. This ban is strictly enforced through the community's 24 hour guard gates, where all guests must be registered and visitor passes are monitored. For residents and buyers, this policy is actually a significant benefit: it preserves the quiet, residential character of the community and prevents the revolving door of strangers that short term rentals create in other luxury neighborhoods.

What Renters Should Know About Guard Gate Access

Renters in MacDonald Highlands receive the same guard gate access and community privileges as homeowners, including use of the six community parks, walking trails, dog parks, and recreational facilities. DragonRidge Country Club membership is a separate matter: it is available only to property owners, so renters would not have access to the golf course, clubhouse dining, fitness center, or athletic facilities unless the property owner maintains an active membership and extends guest privileges.
