Is MacDonald Highlands a Good Investment? Appreciation Data and ROI Analysis

MacDonald Highlands has delivered exceptional returns for homeowners in recent years, with median sale prices rising 27 to 48 percent year over year depending on the tracking source and time period. The community set the all time Las Vegas Valley sale record at $25.25 million in 2025, and multiple data sources report overall median price growth exceeding 90 percent over broader windows. For investors and homebuyers evaluating the long term case, the fundamentals supporting continued appreciation are compelling.

What the Numbers Show

The trailing 12 month median sale price sits between $2.7 million and $3.8 million, representing dramatic gains from levels just a few years prior. In 2025 alone, the community recorded sales at $25.25 million, $15 million, $14.25 million, $13.4 million, $12.5 million, and $10.25 million, demonstrating that the ultra luxury tier is actively trading at record levels. The 89012 ZIP code ranks as the 7th highest per capita income ZIP code in the United States, underscoring the economic profile of the buyer pool that sustains these valuations.

Why MacDonald Highlands Appreciates

Several structural factors drive long term value that do not exist in most luxury communities. First, MacDonald Highlands spans 1,320 acres with finite developable land. As the lowest density master planned community in Southern Nevada, new supply is inherently constrained. There is no adjacent parcel where a competitor can build 500 homes and dilute values.

Second, the Four Seasons Private Residences (priced from $3.5 million to $30 million, with over $725 million in sales already secured) will add a globally recognized luxury brand to the community when it opens. Branded residences historically elevate surrounding property values by associating the entire neighborhood with world class hospitality standards.

Third, Nevada's zero state income tax continues to pull high net worth individuals from California, New York, and other high tax states. This migration pipeline shows no signs of slowing, and MacDonald Highlands is the premier destination for relocators seeking hilltop luxury with Strip views.

The Investment Risks

No real estate investment is without risk. MacDonald Highlands properties carry high holding costs: $330 per month in master HOA fees (plus sub association fees in some neighborhoods), DragonRidge Country Club membership dues for those who join, property taxes, insurance, and maintenance on homes that often exceed 5,000 square feet. Luxury markets are also more sensitive to economic downturns and interest rate environments than entry level housing. Properties priced above $10 million can sit on the market for six months or longer, and the buyer pool at that level is inherently small.
