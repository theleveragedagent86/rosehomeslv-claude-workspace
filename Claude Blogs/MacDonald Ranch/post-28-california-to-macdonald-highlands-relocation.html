The Tax Math That Drives the Move

California's state income tax tops out at 13.3 percent on ordinary income and applies the same rate to capital gains. Nevada charges zero state income tax on any form of income. For a household earning $2 million annually, this difference alone represents approximately $266,000 in annual tax savings. Add the sale of a California property with $2 million in capital gains (avoiding roughly $266,000 in state capital gains tax), and the first year financial benefit of relocating can exceed $500,000.

Over a five year period, the cumulative savings for a high earning household easily surpasses $1.3 million, often enough to cover a significant portion of a MacDonald Highlands home purchase. These are not theoretical projections; they represent actual savings that California relocators realize immediately upon establishing Nevada residency.

The Practical Transition

Harry Reid International Airport is approximately 12 miles from MacDonald Highlands with direct flights to Los Angeles (1 hour), San Francisco (1.5 hours), San Diego (1 hour), and every other major California city. Many relocators maintain California business operations while living in Nevada, flying back for meetings as needed. The drive from MacDonald Highlands to Los Angeles takes approximately 4 hours via I-15.

Establishing Nevada residency requires changing your driver's license, vehicle registration, and voter registration, plus spending more than 183 days per year in Nevada. Working with a tax professional who specializes in California to Nevada transitions is strongly recommended, as California's Franchise Tax Board actively audits high income departures.
