Nevada's 0% State Income Tax: How MacDonald Highlands Residents Save $500K Plus Annually

Nevada imposes zero state income tax on any form of personal income: wages, business income, investment income, retirement distributions, and capital gains. For the high net worth individuals who call MacDonald Highlands home, this single policy represents the largest ongoing financial benefit of living in Henderson, often saving residents $500,000 or more every single year compared to what they would pay in states like California, New York, or New Jersey.

The Savings at Different Income Levels

California's top marginal state income tax rate is 13.3 percent, and it applies to both ordinary income and capital gains. Here is what that means in real dollar terms for MacDonald Highlands residents who relocated from California. At $1 million in annual income, the Nevada advantage is approximately $133,000 per year. At $2 million, the savings reach approximately $266,000. At $5 million, residents keep roughly $665,000 more annually. Over a decade, a household earning $3 million per year saves approximately $4 million in state income taxes alone by living in Nevada instead of California.

Capital Gains: The Hidden Multiplier

The income tax savings are significant on their own, but the capital gains treatment is where the math becomes extraordinary. Nevada charges zero tax on capital gains from stock sales, business sales, real estate transactions, and investment liquidations. California taxes all capital gains as ordinary income at rates up to 13.3 percent. A MacDonald Highlands resident who sells a business for $10 million in profit avoids approximately $1.33 million in California state capital gains tax. A $20 million exit saves $2.66 million. This is why entrepreneurs and executives time their major liquidity events to occur after establishing Nevada residency.

What Nevada Does Tax

Nevada is not entirely tax free. The state generates revenue through sales tax (Clark County's combined rate is 8.375 percent), gaming taxes, mining taxes, and a modified business tax on larger employers. Property taxes also apply, though Henderson's effective rate of approximately 0.53 to 0.77 percent is well below the national average of 1.02 percent. For MacDonald Highlands homeowners, these taxes are modest compared to the income and capital gains taxes eliminated by living in Nevada.

Residency Requirements

To claim Nevada residency and its tax benefits, individuals must spend more than 183 days per year in the state, obtain a Nevada driver's license, register vehicles in Nevada, and register to vote in Nevada. For those relocating from California specifically, the California Franchise Tax Board is known for aggressive auditing of high income departures. Maintaining clear documentation of your physical presence in Nevada, including guard gate entry records, club membership usage logs, and local spending patterns, is essential.
