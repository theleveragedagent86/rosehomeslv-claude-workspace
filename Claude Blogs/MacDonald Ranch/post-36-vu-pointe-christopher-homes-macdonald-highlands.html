Vu Pointe by Christopher Homes: Elevated Living in MacDonald Highlands

Vu Pointe is the newest addition to Christopher Homes' trio of luxury neighborhoods within MacDonald Highlands. Building on the success of the original Vu and SkyVu collections, Vu Pointe features elevated homesites with commanding views of the Las Vegas Strip and surrounding mountains. The neighborhood represents Christopher Homes' latest design evolution, incorporating lessons from over 30 years of luxury homebuilding in the Las Vegas Valley into a refined product that meets the expectations of today's high net worth buyers.

Design and Architecture

Vu Pointe homes showcase Christopher Homes' contemporary desert aesthetic with clean architectural lines, flat rooflines, and expansive glass walls that blur the boundary between indoor and outdoor spaces. The signature multi slide glass wall systems remain a centerpiece of the design, opening entire living areas to covered patios and pool decks. Homes typically range from 3,200 to 5,500 square feet with both single story and multi level configurations available. Christopher Homes offers semi custom options within Vu Pointe, allowing buyers to select finishes, flooring, cabinetry, and fixture packages that personalize the home without the 18 to 24 month timeline of a fully custom build.

Pricing and Positioning

Vu Pointe homes are priced in the $2.5 million to $5 million range, slotting between the original Vu collection and SkyVu in terms of pricing. The elevated lot positions and newer construction help justify the premium over the original Vu, while the semi custom (rather than fully custom) build approach keeps prices below the most expensive SkyVu offerings. For buyers who want newer construction with the latest Christopher Homes design features, Vu Pointe represents the best combination of modern finishes and relative value within the builder's MacDonald Highlands portfolio.

How Vu Pointe Compares to SkyVu and Vu

Christopher Homes operates three distinct neighborhoods within MacDonald Highlands, each targeting a slightly different buyer. The original Vu emphasizes single story simplicity at the most accessible price point ($1.8 million to $3.5 million). SkyVu offers larger lots and 360 degree views at higher elevations ($2.5 million to $6 million plus). Vu Pointe occupies the middle ground with newer builds, elevated positions, and semi custom flexibility ($2.5 million to $5 million). All three share the same build quality, architectural vision, and access to MacDonald Highlands amenities; the differences come down to lot size, elevation, customization level, and price.

What Buyers Should Know

Vu Pointe inventory moves quickly. Because Christopher Homes builds in limited releases, new homesites become available in small batches rather than large phases. This controlled release strategy maintains exclusivity and supports resale values, but it also means buyers need to act decisively when a homesite or completed home becomes available. Working with an agent who has direct relationships with the Christopher Homes sales team provides an advantage in learning about upcoming releases before they hit the open market.
