Dragon Peak Drive by Blue Heron: MacDonald Highlands' Summit Homesites

Dragon Peak Drive represents Blue Heron's most exclusive collection within MacDonald Highlands, featuring a limited number of custom homesites at the community's highest elevations. If Dragon Rock is Blue Heron's flagship neighborhood, Dragon Peak Drive is the penthouse above it: fewer lots, higher positions, and some of the most dramatic residential views in the entire state of Nevada. Homes on Dragon Peak Drive have sold for $10 million to $25 million, placing this enclave among the most expensive residential addresses in the Las Vegas Valley.

Location and Elevation Advantage

Dragon Peak Drive sits at the upper reaches of MacDonald Highlands, above even the elevated Dragon Rock homesites. The additional elevation creates a measurable difference in the view experience. From Dragon Peak, homeowners look out over the entire Las Vegas Valley with unobstructed 360 degree panoramas that include the Strip, Downtown Las Vegas, Red Rock Canyon, Mount Charleston (often snow capped in winter), and the Spring Mountains. On clear days, visibility extends well beyond the valley floor. The elevation also provides slightly cooler temperatures during summer months and a sense of physical separation from the city below that is difficult to describe until you experience it in person.

Blue Heron Custom Process

Dragon Peak Drive homes are built through Blue Heron's fully custom process, which typically spans 18 to 30 months from initial design to completion. The process begins with lot selection, where the topography, sun orientation, view corridors, and access are carefully evaluated before any design work starts. Blue Heron's in house design team then creates a home that is specifically engineered for that lot, taking advantage of grade changes, rock formations, and natural drainage patterns to create architecture that appears to grow out of the hillside rather than sit on top of it.

Home Specifications

Dragon Peak Drive homes are among the largest in MacDonald Highlands, typically ranging from 8,000 to 15,000 square feet with 5 to 8 bedrooms. At this scale, homes routinely include features like private motor courts with 6 to 10 car garages, multiple primary suites for multi generational families or guests, commercial grade kitchens, resort pools with swim up bars, dedicated spa wings, home gyms with sauna and steam rooms, home theaters, wine grottos, and rooftop entertainment decks. The material selections at this tier include imported stone, custom millwork, and bespoke fixtures that are specified individually rather than selected from catalogs.

The Record Setting Sales

MacDonald Highlands' record $25.25 million sale in July 2025 was on Dragon Ridge Peak Drive, a Blue Heron property. This sale confirmed what the market had been signaling: Dragon Peak Drive commands prices that compete with luxury residential addresses in Beverly Hills, Aspen, and Scottsdale. The combination of Blue Heron's design reputation, the irreplaceable elevation and views, and the broader MacDonald Highlands community infrastructure creates a product that has no direct comparison in the Las Vegas market.
