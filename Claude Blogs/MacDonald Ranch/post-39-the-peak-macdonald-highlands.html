The Peak at MacDonald Highlands: Blue Heron's $13.5 Million to $20 Million Collection

The Peak is Blue Heron's newest and most ambitious residential collection within MacDonald Highlands, featuring a very limited number of fully custom homes priced from $13.5 million to $20 million. These homes occupy the absolute highest buildable positions in the community, offering views and privacy that no other MacDonald Highlands neighborhood can match. The Peak represents the culmination of Blue Heron founder Tyler Jones' vision for what luxury residential living can be when design talent, world class views, and virtually unlimited budgets converge.

Why The Peak Commands $13.5 Million to $20 Million

The pricing at The Peak reflects several factors that separate it from every other neighborhood in MacDonald Highlands and, arguably, from every other residential address in Nevada. First, the lots: The Peak occupies the summit positions of MacDonald Highlands, above Dragon Rock and Dragon Peak Drive, with completely unobstructed views in every direction. These are the last premium lots available at these elevations, and once they are built out, no comparable positions exist within the community.

Second, the build quality: Blue Heron's custom process at The Peak tier involves 24 to 36 months of design and construction with specifications that rival the finest homes in Beverly Hills and Miami Beach. Every material, fixture, and finish is individually selected and often custom fabricated. The homes are designed to function as private resorts, with amenity packages that include infinity edge pools with Strip view swim lanes, commercial grade home gyms, private spas, motor courts designed for exotic car collections, and entertainment spaces scaled for hosting large events.

The Blue Heron Experience at This Level

At The Peak price tier, the Blue Heron experience extends well beyond homebuilding. The design process is deeply collaborative, with Blue Heron's architects, interior designers, and landscape architects working as an integrated team over months of concept development before construction begins. Buyers at this level typically have highly specific requirements, whether that is a particular type of imported stone for the exterior cladding, a theater room designed to specific acoustic standards, or outdoor living spaces configured for entertaining 200 guests.

Who Is Buying at The Peak

The Peak attracts the same buyer profile that drives $15 million to $30 million sales in Malibu, Scottsdale's Paradise Valley, and Aspen: ultra high net worth individuals including tech entrepreneurs, professional athletes, entertainment industry figures, and corporate executives. Many are relocating from California or other high tax states and are making a deliberate choice to establish their primary residence in Nevada. At this price point, the $400,000 to $600,000 in annual state income tax savings compared to California becomes a significant financial consideration that can effectively pay for a substantial portion of the home's carrying costs.
