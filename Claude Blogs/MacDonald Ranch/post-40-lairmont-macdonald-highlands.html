Lairmont: Established Custom Homes in MacDonald Highlands

Lairmont is one of MacDonald Highlands' original custom home neighborhoods, featuring spacious lots and homes built during the community's earlier development phases. For buyers who want the full MacDonald Highlands experience, including guard gated security, DragonRidge Country Club access, and the community's panoramic mountain and valley views, but prefer an established home at a more accessible price point than the newer Blue Heron and Christopher Homes collections, Lairmont offers compelling value within one of Henderson's most exclusive addresses.

Homes and Lot Sizes

Lairmont homes were built by various custom builders during MacDonald Highlands' initial development phases and reflect a range of architectural styles from Mediterranean and Tuscan influenced designs to transitional and contemporary desert aesthetics. Homes typically range from 3,500 to 7,000 square feet with 3 to 6 bedrooms, and many sit on lots of 0.25 to 0.50 acres or larger. The generous lot sizes are a significant advantage of Lairmont: compared to newer subdivisions within MacDonald Highlands where lots trend smaller to maximize density, Lairmont offers the kind of yard space, setbacks, and privacy between neighbors that is increasingly rare in the community's newer neighborhoods.

Pricing and Value Proposition

Lairmont homes typically trade in the $1.5 million to $4 million range, with pricing driven primarily by lot size, view quality, home condition, and whether the home has been renovated or maintained in its original state. At the lower end of this range, Lairmont represents one of the most affordable ways to live inside MacDonald Highlands' guard gates. For renovation minded buyers, purchasing an older Lairmont home and investing in a modern update can create a home that rivals newer construction at a total cost below what a comparable new build would require.

The Renovation Opportunity

Many Lairmont homes were built in the 2000s and early 2010s, which means some properties show dated finishes, older floor plan layouts, and efficiency levels that do not match current building standards. For buyers who are comfortable with a renovation project, this creates an opportunity. A Lairmont home purchased at $2 million with $500,000 to $800,000 invested in a comprehensive renovation (kitchen, bathrooms, flooring, smart home systems, energy upgrades, pool renovation) can yield a home that competes with $3.5 million to $4.5 million new construction, effectively building instant equity through the improvement process.

Community and Location

Lairmont residents enjoy the same community amenities as all MacDonald Highlands homeowners, including the six community parks, walking trails, dog parks, and eligibility for DragonRidge Country Club membership. The neighborhood's position within MacDonald Highlands provides good access to both the Valle Verde and Stephanie Street guard gates, and many Lairmont homesites offer views of the DragonRidge golf course, the Strip, or the surrounding mountain terrain. Lairmont's established tree canopy and mature landscaping give the neighborhood a settled, residential feel that newer communities within MacDonald Highlands have not yet achieved.
