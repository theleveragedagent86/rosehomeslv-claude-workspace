The Palisades and Stone Mountain Ridge: Golf Course Living in MacDonald Highlands

The Palisades and Stone Mountain Ridge are two of MacDonald Highlands' established neighborhoods that offer custom homes with proximity to the DragonRidge Country Club golf course. Both neighborhoods feature homes built during earlier phases of the community's development on lots that back up to or overlook the championship layout, providing the kind of daily golf course views that many buyers moving to MacDonald Highlands specifically seek. For those who want to wake up to fairway views and walk to the first tee, these neighborhoods deliver.

The Palisades

The Palisades features custom homes ranging from approximately 3,500 to 6,500 square feet with 3 to 5 bedrooms on lots that offer views of the DragonRidge golf course and the surrounding mountain terrain. Homes in The Palisades were built by various custom builders and represent a mix of architectural styles from Mediterranean and Tuscan to transitional designs. Lot sizes are generally generous, ranging from 0.25 to 0.45 acres, providing comfortable spacing between homes and room for expanded outdoor living. Pricing typically falls in the $1.5 million to $4 million range depending on lot position, golf course frontage, and home condition.

Stone Mountain Ridge

Stone Mountain Ridge sits at slightly higher elevations within MacDonald Highlands, providing homes with views that extend beyond the golf course to include the Las Vegas Valley and surrounding mountain ranges. Homes here range from approximately 3,800 to 7,000 square feet with 4 to 6 bedrooms. The neighborhood's elevation advantage means that many Stone Mountain Ridge properties capture both the intimate golf course views below and the broader valley panorama beyond. Pricing in Stone Mountain Ridge typically ranges from $1.8 million to $4.5 million, with the premium over The Palisades reflecting the enhanced view corridors and slightly larger home sizes.

The Golf Course Advantage

Living on or near the DragonRidge golf course provides benefits beyond the view. Homes with golf course frontage are insulated from future development on at least one side: the fairways and greens will never be built on, guaranteeing that the open space and views are permanent. This development protection is a meaningful long term value consideration. Golf course lots also tend to appreciate at a premium to non golf lots within the same community, as the supply of golf fronting properties is fixed while demand from golf enthusiasts remains consistent.

Community Access

Both The Palisades and Stone Mountain Ridge benefit from their positions near the heart of MacDonald Highlands. The DragonRidge Country Club clubhouse, home to the Montrose Steakhouse, Onyx Bar, fitness center, tennis and pickleball courts, and spa, is minutes away. Community parks and walking trails are easily accessible, and the neighborhoods are well positioned relative to both the Valle Verde and Stephanie Street guard gates. For daily errands and commuting, the centralized location means shorter drives to either gate and quick access to the Henderson retail and dining corridors along Valle Verde Drive and Eastern Avenue.

Renovation and Modernization

As established neighborhoods, both The Palisades and Stone Mountain Ridge include homes that may benefit from renovation. Properties built in the early 2000s through 2010 era may have dated kitchen and bathroom finishes, older HVAC systems, and floor plans that predate the current preference for open concept living. For renovation minded buyers, this creates opportunity: golf course lots with permanent view protection, purchased at $1.5 million to $2.5 million, can be updated into homes that rival $3 million to $4 million new construction, with the added benefit of mature landscaping and established neighborhood character.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose recommends The Palisades and Stone Mountain Ridge to buyers who want the golf lifestyle integrated into their daily living. There is a meaningful difference between belonging to a country club and actually living on the golf course, and these neighborhoods deliver that experience within the security and prestige of MacDonald Highlands. For buyers who will use DragonRidge regularly, the convenience of walking from your back patio to the clubhouse or practice green is a lifestyle upgrade that does not carry a proportional price premium.
