Four Seasons MacDonald Highlands Construction Update 2026: Timeline and Progress

The Four Seasons Private Residences Las Vegas is actively under construction within MacDonald Highlands, with the $1.3 billion project targeting completion in late 2026. The two residential towers (24 and 25 stories) plus 6 standalone villas represent the first high rise residential project built in the Las Vegas Valley since the Great Recession, and the construction progress is closely watched by both prospective buyers and the broader MacDonald Highlands community. Here is what we know about the timeline, milestones, and what to expect as the project moves toward completion.

Current Construction Status

As of early 2026, the Four Seasons project has secured $781 million in financing and moved past the foundation and structural phases into vertical construction. The twin towers are rising from the MacDonald Highlands hillside with the distinctive contemporary glass and steel design that was previewed in architectural renderings. Site preparation for this project was significant given the hillside terrain: grading, reinforcing, and preparing the elevated building pad required extensive work before vertical construction could begin. The project's hilltop position within MacDonald Highlands, while providing the panoramic views that make the residences so desirable, also presented engineering challenges that the development team addressed during the earlier phases.

Project Timeline

The Four Seasons Private Residences Las Vegas has followed a development timeline that began with the initial announcement and sales launch. Over $725 million in sales were secured during the pre construction sales period, with approximately 70 percent of the 171 residences and 6 villas sold before the towers reached their full height. The target for initial resident move ins is late 2026, though luxury high rise projects of this scale and complexity can experience timeline adjustments based on construction conditions, material deliveries, and the extensive finishing work required for residences at this price point.

The Development Team

The Four Seasons project is being developed by Azure Resorts and Hotels in partnership with Luxus Development, with Four Seasons Hotels and Resorts providing the branded hospitality management that will define the resident experience. Discovery Land Company's involvement brings decades of experience developing America's most exclusive residential communities. The construction team includes general contractors and specialty subcontractors experienced with luxury high rise construction in the Las Vegas market. Four Seasons' brand standards require exacting quality control throughout the construction process, from structural systems to interior finishes, which contributes to both the quality and the timeline of the project.

What Buyers Should Know About the Timeline

Prospective buyers evaluating the Four Seasons should understand several timeline considerations. First, the 70 percent sold figure means the remaining 30 percent of units will likely see pricing adjustments as completion approaches and the project transitions from pre construction sales to finished inventory sales. Historically, branded residence projects see price increases as construction progresses and the finished product becomes visible and tangible. Second, buyers who purchased early in the pre construction phase have locked in pricing that may represent significant value relative to what finished units will command. Third, the Wolfgang Puck dining venues, resort pools, spa, fitness center, and other amenities will open concurrent with or shortly after the first resident move ins, meaning early residents will experience the full Four Seasons lifestyle from day one.

Impact on MacDonald Highlands During Construction

Large scale construction projects within a residential community inevitably create some disruption. MacDonald Highlands residents have experienced increased construction traffic, noise during building hours, and visual changes to the community's skyline as the towers have risen. The community's HOA and the development team have worked to minimize impact through designated construction vehicle routes, adherence to Henderson's construction hour restrictions, and communication with residents about project milestones and any temporary disruptions. Once complete, the construction disruption will be replaced by the permanent benefits the Four Seasons brings: enhanced community prestige, new dining and amenity options, and the positive impact on property values throughout MacDonald Highlands.
