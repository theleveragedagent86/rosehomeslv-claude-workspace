DragonRidge Country Club: The Complete Guide to MacDonald Highlands' Private Club

DragonRidge Country Club is the private golf and social club at the heart of MacDonald Highlands, offering members an 18 hole championship golf course, four dining venues, a 15,000 square foot fitness and athletic center, tennis and pickleball courts, a resort style pool, and a full calendar of social events. Open exclusively to MacDonald Highlands residents, DragonRidge is more than a golf club; it is the community's social center, the place where neighbors become friends and where the daily rhythm of MacDonald Highlands life plays out across fairways, dining rooms, and poolside terraces.

The Clubhouse

The DragonRidge clubhouse spans approximately 40,000 to 44,000 square feet and sits atop the mountain with 360 degree views of the Las Vegas Valley, the Strip, and the surrounding McCullough Range. The clubhouse is the architectural centerpiece of MacDonald Highlands, and its elevated position means that dining at Montrose Steakhouse or having cocktails at the Onyx Bar comes with a panoramic backdrop that rivals any restaurant on the Strip. The clubhouse houses four distinct dining venues (Montrose Steakhouse, Dragon Grille, Onyx Bar, and Red Dragon Sushi Bar), private event spaces including the 5,000 square foot Grand Highlands Ballroom, member lounges, the pro shop, and club administrative offices.

Golf

The DragonRidge golf course is an 18 hole, par 72 championship layout designed by Jay Morrish and David Druzisky. Stretching 6,975 yards from the tips with a slope rating of 138 to 143, the course snakes through the McCullough Mountains with dramatic elevation changes, volcanic rock formations, and views that make it one of the most visually stunning courses in the western United States. The course is strictly private: only MacDonald Highlands residents with active golf memberships can play, and the membership cap ensures the course is never overcrowded. Golf Digest has recognized DragonRidge as a "Best in State" course, ranking it 12th in Nevada for 2025 to 2026.

Dining

DragonRidge's four dining venues cover the full spectrum from fine dining to casual fare. Montrose Steakhouse delivers premium steaks and seafood with Strip views in an elegant setting. Dragon Grille serves family friendly casual fare. Onyx Bar is the social hub, offering craft cocktails, small plates, and live music on Thursday evenings. Red Dragon Sushi Bar provides fresh Japanese cuisine. Additional dining at the Center Court Cafe (Starbucks coffee) and the Poolside Snack Bar rounds out the options. For many members, the dining program alone justifies the club membership: having four quality restaurants minutes from home eliminates the need to drive to the Strip for a proper dinner.

Fitness, Tennis, and Pool

The 15,000 square foot Tennis and Athletic Center houses state of the art fitness equipment, two group exercise studios, a private Pilates room, and full locker rooms with sauna, steam room, and whirlpool. The Lapis and Oak Spa offers massage and fascial stretch therapy. Five lighted championship tennis courts (one with stadium seating) host USTA leagues, tournaments, and daily clinics, and dedicated pickleball courts accommodate the sport's growing popularity. The custom lounge style swimming pool can accommodate up to 150 for events and serves as a resort style retreat for daily use. A full size outdoor basketball court and volleyball court complete the athletic offerings.

Events and Social Life

DragonRidge's event calendar keeps the club active year round. Signature events include the Fourth of July fireworks extravaganza (2,000 plus attendees on the driving range with bounce houses and water slides), New Year's Eve celebrations, Breakfast with Santa, champagne socials, wine dinners featuring prestigious vintners, monthly live music at Onyx Bar, golf tournaments and pro ams, ladies' group activities, summer camps for kids, junior tennis and golf programs, poolside BBQs on holiday weekends, and tennis mixers. The club is not just a place to play golf; it is where MacDonald Highlands residents build their social lives.
