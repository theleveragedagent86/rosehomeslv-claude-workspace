DragonRidge Golf Course Review: 18 Holes Through the McCullough Mountains

DragonRidge Country Club's 18 hole championship course is a par 72, 6,975 yard layout designed by Jay Morrish and David Druzisky that winds through the volcanic rock formations and dramatic elevation changes of the McCullough Mountains. With a slope rating of 138 to 143 depending on the tees, this is a challenging course that rewards precision and course management while delivering some of the most spectacular scenery of any golf course in the western United States. Golf Digest has ranked DragonRidge 12th in Nevada in its "Best in State" rankings for 2025 to 2026, and the Las Vegas Review Journal awarded it Gold Winner for Best Private Golf Course in 2021.

The Course Layout

What makes DragonRidge unique is how the course integrates with the natural terrain rather than fighting against it. The McCullough Mountains provide natural elevation changes that create dramatic uphill and downhill holes, with volcanic rock outcroppings framing fairways and guarding greens. Several holes play along the ridge with panoramic Las Vegas Strip views that can distract even focused players. The course routing takes players through different micro environments within MacDonald Highlands: some holes play through residential corridors with custom homes flanking the fairways, while others feel completely isolated in mountain terrain where the only visible structures are the distant Strip skyline.

Standout Holes

While every hole on DragonRidge offers something memorable, several stand out for their combination of challenge and visual impact. The par 3 holes are particularly noteworthy, as the elevation changes create dramatic downhill tee shots where club selection is critical and the mountain backdrop frames each green. The par 5s offer risk reward opportunities where aggressive players can take advantage of the elevation drops to reach greens in two, while conservative players face challenging approach shots into well protected greens. The finishing holes build to a crescendo as the course climbs back toward the clubhouse, with the final approach shots playing uphill toward the mountaintop clubhouse and its 360 degree views.

Course Conditions and Maintenance

DragonRidge invested over $10.5 million in capital improvements since 2020, including a comprehensive course renovation in 2023 focused on water conservation and playing surface quality. The renovation addressed tee boxes, fairways, and green surrounds while implementing water saving technology that aligns with Southern Nevada's water conservation mandates. Despite the desert climate and water restrictions, the course maintains excellent playing conditions year round, with the lower winter humidity and mild temperatures (highs in the 50s to 60s) providing what many golfers consider the best playing conditions of the year. Summer rounds are typically played in the early morning to avoid peak heat, though MacDonald Highlands' 2,500 to 2,700 foot elevation means temperatures run 5 to 10 degrees cooler than the valley floor.

The Practice Facility

DragonRidge's 60,000 square foot practice facility is one of the most comprehensive in the Las Vegas Valley. It features an all grass driving range (not mats), two practice sand bunkers, and two bent grass putting greens that mirror the speed and break of the course's on course greens. The all grass range is a significant differentiator from many Las Vegas practice facilities that use artificial turf hitting areas, and it allows players to practice from lies that replicate actual course conditions. The planned Jack Nicklaus Academy of Golf, set to be Nevada's first location, will add world class instruction to the already impressive practice infrastructure.

Playing Experience and Pace

Because DragonRidge is strictly private with a capped membership available only to MacDonald Highlands residents, the course avoids the overcrowding that plagues many Las Vegas golf experiences. Tee times are readily available, rounds move at a comfortable pace, and the course never feels rushed or backed up. This exclusivity is one of the primary reasons golfers choose MacDonald Highlands over other Las Vegas golf communities where the courses are semi private or open to outside play. At DragonRidge, you will never be stuck behind a group of tourists who have never played the course before.
