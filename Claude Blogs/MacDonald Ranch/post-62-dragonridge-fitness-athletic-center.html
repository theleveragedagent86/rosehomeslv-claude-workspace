Is DragonRidge Membership Worth It?

DragonRidge Fitness and Athletic Center: 15,000 Square Feet of Luxury Wellness

The DragonRidge Tennis and Athletic Center spans 15,000 square feet and provides MacDonald Highlands members with a comprehensive fitness, wellness, and recovery facility that eliminates the need for an outside gym membership. Featuring state of the art cardiovascular and strength equipment, two group exercise studios, a private Pilates room, full locker rooms with sauna, steam room, and whirlpool, and the Lapis and Oak Spa, the athletic center delivers a fitness experience on par with Las Vegas' best standalone luxury gyms but with the convenience and exclusivity of a private club.

Fitness Equipment and Floor Layout

The main fitness floor houses a full complement of cardiovascular machines (treadmills, ellipticals, stationary bikes, rowing machines) and a comprehensive strength training area with free weights, weight machines, cable systems, and functional training equipment. The equipment is regularly updated to maintain current generation machines, and the floor layout provides ample spacing between stations. Unlike commercial gyms where machines are packed tightly to maximize membership revenue, DragonRidge's fitness floor feels spacious and uncrowded, a direct benefit of the capped membership that prevents the facility from exceeding its comfortable capacity. Members consistently have access to the equipment they want, when they want it, without waiting for machines or competing for floor space during peak hours.

Group Exercise Studios

Two dedicated group exercise studios host a rotating schedule of classes that cater to different fitness levels and interests. Class offerings typically include yoga (multiple styles), high intensity interval training, cycling/spin, barre, strength and conditioning, and seasonal specialty classes. The studios are purpose built with appropriate flooring, mirrors, sound systems, and ventilation for group exercise, and class sizes are kept small enough to allow instructors to provide individual attention. For members who prefer guided workouts over solo training, the group class schedule provides daily options without requiring a drive to an off site studio.

Private Pilates Room

A dedicated private Pilates room houses reformer machines and other Pilates specific equipment, offering both group Pilates classes and private instruction sessions. Pilates has become one of the most popular fitness modalities among luxury community residents, and DragonRidge's private room provides the focused environment and professional instruction that Pilates practice requires. The private setting means that members doing Pilates are not competing for space or attention with the general fitness floor, and the dedicated equipment is maintained to the standards that serious Pilates practitioners expect.

Lapis and Oak Spa

The Lapis and Oak Spa provides massage therapy and fascial stretch therapy with pricing from $50 to $750 per session depending on the treatment type and duration. The spa offers a recovery and relaxation complement to the fitness programming, allowing members to address muscle tension, stress, and mobility within the same facility where they train. Having spa services within the athletic center means members can incorporate regular massage or stretch therapy into their fitness routine without the logistical complexity of scheduling at a separate spa location. The integration of fitness and recovery under one roof reflects the modern wellness approach that treats exercise and recovery as equally important components of physical health.

Locker Rooms and Recovery Amenities

The men's and ladies' locker rooms provide the full private club experience: individual lockers, shower facilities, vanity areas, and the recovery amenities that distinguish a club facility from a commercial gym. The sauna, steam room, and whirlpool are available to all athletic center members and serve as both post workout recovery tools and standalone relaxation amenities. These heat and water based recovery options are particularly valued in the desert climate, where the dry air and physical demands of an active lifestyle make regular sauna and steam room use genuinely beneficial rather than merely indulgent.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose points out that the DragonRidge athletic center represents a meaningful quality of life benefit that non golfers sometimes overlook when evaluating MacDonald Highlands. Even residents who never pick up a golf club can get substantial daily value from the fitness center, group classes, spa, and pool. For buyers who currently pay $200 to $500 per month for a premium gym plus additional costs for yoga studios, Pilates, and spa services, the DragonRidge membership can consolidate all of those expenses into a single club membership while adding the social and convenience benefits of having everything within the community gates.
