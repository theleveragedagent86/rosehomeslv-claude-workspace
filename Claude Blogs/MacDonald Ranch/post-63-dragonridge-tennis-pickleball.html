Tennis and Pickleball at DragonRidge Country Club: Courts, Programs, and Leagues

DragonRidge Country Club offers five lighted championship tennis courts (including one with stadium seating) and dedicated pickleball courts, supported by a comprehensive program of daily clinics, USTA leagues, tournaments, private instruction, and junior development programs. For MacDonald Highlands residents who play racquet sports, DragonRidge provides a facility and programming structure that matches or exceeds what standalone tennis clubs offer, integrated within the broader country club experience.

The Tennis Facility

The five championship tennis courts are lighted for evening play, which is particularly valuable in the Las Vegas climate where summer daytime temperatures make outdoor play impractical during peak heat hours. The courts are maintained to tournament standards with consistent surfaces and professional net systems. One court features stadium seating, accommodating spectators for tournaments and competitive matches. The courts are located adjacent to the Tennis and Athletic Center, giving players immediate access to the fitness floor, locker rooms, and recovery amenities before and after play. This integration means you can warm up in the fitness center, play a match, cool down in the steam room, and grab lunch at the Center Court Cafe without getting in your car.

Tennis Programming

DragonRidge's tennis program runs year round, taking advantage of the Las Vegas climate that allows outdoor play roughly 300 days per year. Daily clinics provide structured group instruction for players at all levels, from beginners working on fundamentals to advanced players refining competitive skills. USTA league play connects DragonRidge members with the broader Southern Nevada tennis community through organized competitive teams. In house tournaments provide opportunities for friendly competition among members, and social mixers combine tennis with the club's dining and bar offerings for events that are as much about socializing as they are about sport.

Private Instruction

For players seeking accelerated improvement, DragonRidge offers private lessons with the club's tennis professionals. Private instruction is available for individuals, couples, and small groups, with lesson structures tailored to specific goals whether that is preparing for USTA league competition, developing a new serve technique, or learning the game from scratch. The private lesson program benefits from the uncrowded court environment: unlike public facilities where court time is limited and expensive, DragonRidge pros can schedule lessons with flexible timing and without the pressure of a tight hourly court rental window.

Pickleball

Responding to pickleball's explosive growth nationally, DragonRidge has added dedicated pickleball courts to its racquet sports offerings. Pickleball has become one of the fastest growing sports among the demographic that lives in luxury communities: active adults who enjoy competitive but lower impact racquet sports that are easier on aging joints than tennis. The dedicated pickleball courts (rather than converted tennis courts with temporary lines) provide the proper dimensions and net heights that serious pickleball players expect. Organized play sessions and social events around pickleball have quickly become some of the club's most popular programming, attracting members who might not play tennis but are enthusiastic pickleball participants.

Junior Tennis Programs

DragonRidge's junior tennis programs serve MacDonald Highlands families with children who want to learn and develop in the sport. Junior clinics are structured by age and skill level, providing appropriate instruction for young beginners through competitive junior tournament players. Summer camps add intensive multi day tennis programming during school breaks. The junior programs serve a dual purpose: they develop young players' skills and they create a social community for MacDonald Highlands kids, who form friendships on the courts that extend into school and neighborhood life. For families relocating to MacDonald Highlands, enrolling children in the junior tennis program is one of the fastest ways to integrate into the community social fabric.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose notes that the tennis and pickleball facilities are an often underappreciated part of the DragonRidge value proposition. Many buyers initially focus on the golf course and dining when evaluating the club, but members who play racquet sports consistently rank the courts and programming among their most used amenities. The combination of quality courts, year round outdoor play weather, organized leagues and clinics, and the social community that forms around regular play creates a daily engagement with the club that goes beyond what occasional golf rounds provide.
