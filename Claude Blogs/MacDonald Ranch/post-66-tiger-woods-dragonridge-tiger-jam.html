Tiger Woods at DragonRidge: When Tiger Jam Came to MacDonald Highlands

DragonRidge Country Club has hosted some of golf's most notable events, including Tiger Woods' Tiger Jam III and Tiger Jam IV charity tournaments and the Wendy's 3 Tour Challenge. These events brought the world's most famous golfer and national television cameras to MacDonald Highlands, validating DragonRidge as a course worthy of the game's highest level of competition and charity exhibition. The Tiger Jam history is more than a piece of club trivia; it speaks to the quality and prestige of the course that MacDonald Highlands residents play every day.

Tiger Jam at DragonRidge

The Tiger Jam is Tiger Woods' annual charity event benefiting the Tiger Woods Foundation (now the TGR Foundation), which supports education programs for underserved youth. The event combines a celebrity golf tournament with an evening concert and gala, attracting a mix of professional athletes, entertainment celebrities, business leaders, and high net worth supporters. Tiger Jam III and IV were held at DragonRidge Country Club, bringing Tiger Woods, his fellow PGA Tour professionals, and the celebrity participants to MacDonald Highlands for multi day events that showcased the course and clubhouse to a national audience. The events raised millions for the foundation while putting DragonRidge on the map as a venue capable of hosting the highest profile golf events in the Las Vegas market.

The Wendy's 3 Tour Challenge

DragonRidge also hosted the Wendy's 3 Tour Challenge, a made for television event that pitted two player teams from the PGA Tour, LPGA Tour, and Champions Tour against each other in a team competition format. The event brought some of the biggest names in professional golf to MacDonald Highlands and was broadcast to a national television audience, further establishing DragonRidge as a premier private course with the infrastructure and quality to support televised professional competition. The 3 Tour Challenge format was particularly well suited to DragonRidge's dramatic mountain course, as the elevation changes and volcanic rock formations provided visually compelling television content that flat, traditional courses cannot match.

What This Means for the Course

Hosting Tiger Woods and televised professional events is a distinction that only a small number of private clubs in America have earned. For a course to be selected for events of this caliber, it must meet exacting standards for course condition, layout quality, infrastructure (practice facilities, clubhouse, media accommodations), and visual presentation. The fact that DragonRidge passed these tests confirms what members experience every round: this is a course built and maintained to a standard that the game's best players consider worthy of competition. For MacDonald Highlands residents, the Tiger Jam and 3 Tour Challenge history means their home course has been vetted and approved at the highest level of professional golf.

The Celebrity Connection

The Tiger Jam events also established DragonRidge and MacDonald Highlands as familiar venues for celebrities, professional athletes, and business leaders who participate in the Las Vegas charity golf circuit. Several MacDonald Highlands residents are themselves notable figures (Oscar De La Hoya, Andrew Cherng, Steve Aoki, among others), and the community's association with events like Tiger Jam reinforces its position as a place where high profile individuals feel comfortable living and socializing.
