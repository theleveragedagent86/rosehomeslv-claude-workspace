LIVV Homes NEO: Net Zero Luxury Living with Tesla Powerwalls in MacDonald Highlands

NEO by LIVV Homes is the world's first net zero energy luxury community, and it sits inside the gates of MacDonald Highlands. With 89 oversized lots and homes priced from $3.1 million to $4.5 million, NEO proves that environmental sustainability and ultra luxury living are not competing priorities. Every NEO home is engineered to produce as much energy as it consumes over the course of a year, which means residents enjoy near zero utility costs while living in homes that rival any custom estate in the community for quality, design, and finish level.

What Net Zero Means at This Price Point

Net zero energy means the home's energy production (primarily through solar panels) equals or exceeds its total energy consumption over an annual cycle. At a standard home, net zero is achievable but often involves design compromises: smaller windows to reduce heat gain, simpler systems, and utilitarian aesthetics. LIVV Homes' achievement with NEO is delivering net zero performance in homes with the expansive glass walls, large open floor plans, professional grade kitchen appliances, and resort style outdoor living that luxury buyers expect. This is accomplished through a combination of Tesla Powerwall battery storage, high efficiency solar panel arrays, advanced insulation and building envelope technology, smart home energy management systems, and Sub Zero and Wolf appliances that combine premium performance with energy efficiency.

Floor Plans and Models

NEO offers three primary floor plan models that cover a range of sizes and configurations. The Velour model at 3,036 square feet provides a more efficient luxury footprint for buyers who want net zero living without managing an oversized estate. The Thia model ranges from 3,692 to 4,053 square feet, offering the mid range sweet spot that accommodates families and entertainers with additional bedrooms and living spaces. The Paragon at 5,372 square feet is the flagship model, delivering a full scale luxury estate experience with extensive indoor outdoor living, multiple entertaining zones, and the kind of architectural presence that makes a statement on the MacDonald Highlands streetscape. All three models feature LIVV Homes' contemporary design language with clean lines, open concepts, and the integration of sustainable technology throughout.

The Technology Package

Every NEO home comes equipped with a technology package that goes well beyond basic solar panels. Tesla Powerwall battery storage systems allow homes to store excess solar energy for use during evening hours and peak demand periods, reducing reliance on the grid even when the sun is not shining. Smart home energy management systems monitor and optimize energy use in real time, adjusting climate control, lighting, and other systems to minimize waste. The solar arrays are designed and positioned for maximum production given MacDonald Highlands' elevation, orientation, and the Las Vegas Valley's approximately 297 sunny days per year, which is among the highest solar resource indexes in the nation.

Cost of Ownership Advantage

The near zero utility cost profile of NEO homes creates a tangible financial advantage that compounds over time. In a conventional MacDonald Highlands luxury home of 4,000 to 6,000 square feet, annual electricity costs in the Las Vegas climate can easily reach $6,000 to $12,000 or more, particularly during the summer cooling season. Over a 10 year ownership period, NEO homeowners may save $60,000 to $120,000 in utility costs relative to comparable non net zero homes, a meaningful return that partially offsets the higher initial purchase price and reinforces the investment case for sustainable luxury construction.

MacDonald Highlands' Eco Friendly Heritage

NEO's net zero achievement builds on MacDonald Highlands' established environmental credentials. The community was the first eco friendly hillside community in the Las Vegas Valley, with deed restrictions that prohibit altering natural rock formations and design guidelines that emphasize harmony with the desert landscape. DragonRidge Country Club invested $10.5 million in course renovations focused partly on water conservation. NEO represents the next evolution of this environmental commitment, proving that MacDonald Highlands continues to lead on sustainability while maintaining its position at the top of the Las Vegas luxury market.
