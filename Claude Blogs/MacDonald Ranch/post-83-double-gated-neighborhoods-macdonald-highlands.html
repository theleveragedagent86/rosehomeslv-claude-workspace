Double Gated Neighborhoods in MacDonald Highlands: The Communities Behind Secondary Gates

MacDonald Highlands is already a guard gated community with 24 hour manned entrances, but several neighborhoods within the community add a second layer of gated access, creating a "gated within gated" security model that represents the highest level of residential privacy available in the Las Vegas Valley. These double gated enclaves are the most exclusive addresses within an already exclusive community, offering residents an additional barrier between themselves and the outside world. For buyers who prioritize privacy above all other considerations, these neighborhoods are where MacDonald Highlands reaches its peak.

Lairmont: The Ultimate Private Enclave

Lairmont is arguably MacDonald Highlands' most exclusive neighborhood: a 12 acre enclave with only 16 golf course lots behind its own private secondary gate. The extreme limitation of just 16 homes creates a level of intimacy and exclusivity that is virtually impossible to find in any other Las Vegas community. Every Lairmont home sits on the DragonRidge golf course with direct course frontage, and the secondary gate means that even other MacDonald Highlands residents cannot drive through Lairmont unless visiting a resident. Prices in Lairmont start at approximately $1.2 million and go significantly higher for custom estates. The combination of the 16 home cap, golf course frontage, and double gated access makes Lairmont one of the most tightly held neighborhoods in Henderson.

Dragon Rock and Dragon Peak Drive

Blue Heron's Dragon Rock (51 properties on 0.27 to 1.23 acre lots) and Dragon Peak Drive (31 custom homesites on 0.49 to 2.46 acre lots) both sit behind a shared secondary gate that separates them from the broader MacDonald Highlands community. These neighborhoods contain some of the most valuable homes in MacDonald Highlands, including properties at The Peak that list at $13.5 million to $20 million. The secondary gate adds another layer of screening: visitors must pass through the main MacDonald Highlands guard gate and then through the Dragon Rock/Dragon Peak gate, with access controlled at both points. For residents who are high profile individuals, this double verification provides meaningful additional privacy.

Vu Pointe

Christopher Homes' Vu Pointe neighborhood of 74 single story properties above the DragonRidge Country Club clubhouse sits behind its own secondary gate. While the security benefit is consistent with other double gated neighborhoods, Vu Pointe's secondary gate also serves a lifestyle function: it creates a quiet, low traffic residential area directly above the clubhouse, allowing residents to walk to dinner, fitness, or social events at DragonRidge without the street traffic that might otherwise pass through a neighborhood adjacent to a busy club facility. The gate preserves the residential tranquility while maintaining walkable proximity to amenities.

Glenbrook Canyon

Glenbrook Canyon is MacDonald Highlands' most secluded neighborhood, with only 14 mountainous custom home sites on lots ranging from 0.99 to 1.76 acres behind its own secondary gate. The combination of large lots, extreme terrain, limited homes, and secondary gated access creates an environment that feels more like a private mountain retreat than a suburban community. Glenbrook Canyon's topography provides natural privacy that supplements the gated access: the lots are positioned within canyons and on ridges where homes are visually separated from neighbors by terrain, vegetation, and distance. Prices start at approximately $1.1 million for lots, with custom homes commanding significantly more.

The Toll Brothers Area

The former Toll Brothers neighborhood, now available only as resale properties, also sits behind a secondary gate. These homes along Enchanted River Drive, River Spey Avenue, and River Dee Place benefit from the same double gated security model as the community's custom home enclaves, providing secondary gate protection at a potentially lower price point than the Blue Heron or Lairmont neighborhoods.

What Double Gating Means for Daily Life

Living behind a secondary gate within MacDonald Highlands adds a small amount of time to the entry process (a few seconds at the secondary gate after clearing the main guard gate) but provides meaningful benefits. Traffic through the neighborhood is reduced to only residents of that specific enclave and their verified guests. Noise from the broader community is further buffered. The sense of privacy is palpable: standing in Lairmont or Glenbrook Canyon, it is easy to forget that there are 2,000 other households within the broader MacDonald Highlands community, because the secondary gate creates its own micro community of a few dozen homes maximum.
