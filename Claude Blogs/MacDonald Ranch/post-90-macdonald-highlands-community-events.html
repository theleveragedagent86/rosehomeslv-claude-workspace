What It's Really Like Living in MacDonald Highlands

MacDonald Highlands Community Events: From Fourth of July Fireworks to Champagne Socials

MacDonald Highlands is not just a collection of luxury homes behind guard gates. It is an active community with a social calendar that brings residents together year round through a combination of HOA organized events and DragonRidge Country Club programming. For buyers who value community connection alongside privacy and exclusivity, the events scene at MacDonald Highlands delivers both.

Community Wide Events (All Residents)

The HOA organizes events throughout the year that are open to all MacDonald Highlands residents regardless of club membership. Yappy Hours at the community dog parks have become signature gatherings, featuring pop up Bloody Mary bars, wine and cheese stations, and seasonal refreshments while dogs play in the enclosed park. The Easter Egg Hunt draws families with young children to the community parks each spring. Walking trail fitness activities and park gatherings provide additional opportunities for residents to connect in casual settings. The Highlander Magazine, the community's own publication, keeps residents informed about upcoming events, community news, and neighborhood updates.

DragonRidge Country Club Events (Members)

DragonRidge Country Club maintains an extensive social calendar that goes far beyond golf. The Fourth of July fireworks extravaganza is the community's marquee annual event, drawing over 2,000 attendees to the driving range for a celebration with bounce houses, water slides, live entertainment, and a professional fireworks show visible across the valley. New Year's Eve celebrations at the clubhouse offer formal dining and champagne toasts with panoramic Strip views. Breakfast with Santa brings holiday spirit for families during the December season.

Throughout the year, the club hosts champagne socials, wine dinners featuring prestigious vintners, golf tournaments and pro ams, live music Thursday nights at the Onyx Bar, business networking events, ladies' group activities, poolside BBQs on holiday weekends, tennis mixers and USTA league play, wellness hiking outings, and summer camps for kids. Junior tennis and golf programs give younger residents structured athletic development within the club. The breadth of programming means there is something on the calendar virtually every week for members who want to stay socially active.

The Social Fabric

What makes MacDonald Highlands' events meaningful is the intimacy of the audience. With approximately 2,000 households in the community and a capped membership at the club, these are not massive anonymous gatherings. They are neighborhood events where residents recognize faces and build genuine relationships over time. The combination of community wide events (accessible to all homeowners) and club events (for members) creates two tiers of social engagement, ensuring that residents can participate at whatever level of involvement suits their lifestyle.
