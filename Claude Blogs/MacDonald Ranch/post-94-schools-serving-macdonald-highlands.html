Schools Serving MacDonald Highlands: Elementary, Middle, and High School Zone Guide

MacDonald Highlands falls within attendance zones for some of the highest performing public schools in the Clark County School District. For families considering a move to Henderson's premier luxury community, school quality is often a decisive factor, and the zoned options here consistently rank among the best in Nevada.

Elementary Schools

Two 5 star rated elementary schools serve the MacDonald Highlands area. John C. Vanderburg Elementary carries the highest rating from the Nevada School Performance Framework and ranks among the top 10 to 15 elementary schools in the state. Proficiency rates at Vanderburg run 30 to 40% above Clark County School District averages in both math and reading, reflecting a combination of strong teaching, involved families, and a student body drawn from the affluent neighborhoods surrounding MacDonald Highlands.

James E. and A. Rae Smalley Elementary also holds a 5 star NSPF rating, with 73% math proficiency and 80% reading proficiency among its approximately 707 students. Both schools offer the academic foundation that families in MacDonald Highlands expect, and the small community feel of each campus creates an environment where teachers know students individually and parent involvement runs high.

Middle School

Bob Miller Middle School is the primary middle school serving MacDonald Highlands. Rated 9 out of 10 by GreatSchools and ranked 17th in Nevada, Bob Miller enrolls approximately 1,449 students and posts proficiency rates of 53% in math and 67% in reading, both well above district averages. The school is named after former Nevada Governor Bob Miller and offers a range of academic and extracurricular programs that prepare students for Henderson's competitive high schools. Greenspun Junior High (rated 6 out of 10) also serves some addresses in the broader area.

High School

Foothill High School is the zoned high school for MacDonald Highlands. Ranked 26th in Nevada by U.S. News, Foothill enrolls approximately 2,362 students and posts a 94% graduation rate. Average standardized test scores include SAT averages of 1180 and ACT averages of 24, both above national medians. The school offers Advanced Placement courses, competitive athletics, and a range of extracurricular programs. Coronado High School is also nearby for families in certain sections of the broader area. Nevada State High School Henderson, rated 9 out of 10 by GreatSchools, provides an additional option for students seeking an accelerated academic environment.

School Quality and Property Values

The connection between top rated schools and property values is well documented, and MacDonald Highlands benefits from this relationship. Families willing to invest in luxury real estate also prioritize educational quality, and the strong zoned schools create a self reinforcing cycle: high performing schools attract affluent families, whose involvement and resources further strengthen the schools, which in turn supports property demand and values in the community.
