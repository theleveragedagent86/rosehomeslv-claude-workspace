# Bob Baskin Park Las Vegas Bob Baskin Park is a 7am to 11pm public park at 2801 W. Oakey Blvd. in the Naked City neighborhood, featuring playgrounds, tennis courts, a water play area, and a paved jogging track. ## What Is Bob Baskin Park in Las Vegas? Bob Baskin Park is a City of Las Vegas park located on West Oakey Boulevard in the 89102 zip code, right in the heart of the Naked City neighborhood. The park offers a playground, water play area, picnic area, tennis courts, a fitness court, and a paved jogging and walking track that loops approximately half a mile. Ample shade structures and open green space make it one of the more versatile parks in this part of the valley. The park is open daily from 7am to 11pm. ## Is Bob Baskin Park Good for Families and Exercise? Families with children will find the playground and water play area useful during the warmer months, which in Las Vegas means most of the year. The picnic area provides covered spots to sit while kids play, and the open space is large enough for casual sports or group activities. For adults focused on fitness, the half mile paved loop is flat and easy to navigate, making it suitable for jogging, walking, or pushing a stroller. The fitness court adds bodyweight exercise stations along the route, and the tennis courts are available on a first come, first served basis. ## Are There Hiking Trails Near Naked City? There are no dedicated hiking trails within the Naked City neighborhood itself. Bob Baskin Park's walking track is the closest option for a quick outdoor loop. For actual trail hiking, Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is roughly 20 miles west, and the Clark County Wetlands Park is about 12 miles southeast. Both offer a range of trail difficulties and are popular weekend destinations for Las Vegas residents looking to get out of the city. Ryan Rose, who has toured hundreds of homes in this area, notes that Bob Baskin Park is often an underrated selling point for Naked City properties. He finds that buyers who visit the park in person are pleasantly surprised by the amount of shade and the quality of the walking track, especially compared to smaller pocket parks in surrounding neighborhoods.
