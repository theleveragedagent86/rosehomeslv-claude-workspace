# Stupak Park and Community Center Las Vegas Stupak Park and Community Center at 300 W. Boston Ave. in Naked City offers a synthetic turf soccer field, two playgrounds, a water play feature, a gymnasium, weight room, and library. ## What Is Stupak Park in Las Vegas? Chester Stupak Community Park is a public park in the Naked City neighborhood of Las Vegas, located at 300 W. Boston Ave. in the 89102 zip code. The park includes a small synthetic turf soccer field with goals, netting, and lights for evening play. Two playground structures serve different age groups, with one designed for toddlers ages 2 to 5 and another built for youth ages 5 to 12. A swing set and water play feature round out the children's amenities. Four shade structures provide relief from the sun across the park grounds. Hours run daily from 7am to 11pm. ## What Does the Stupak Community Center Offer? The Stupak Community Center sits adjacent to the park and provides indoor recreation options for the neighborhood. The center houses a full gymnasium used primarily for basketball, a weight room with fitness equipment, and a small library. Hours at the community center vary by season and programming, so checking with the City of Las Vegas for current schedules is recommended. The indoor facilities make this location useful year round, which matters in a city where summer temperatures regularly exceed 110 degrees. ## How Does Stupak Park Compare to Bob Baskin Park? Both parks serve the Naked City neighborhood, but they fill different roles. Bob Baskin Park is the larger of the two, with tennis courts, a fitness court, and a paved half mile jogging track. Stupak Park is more compact and youth focused, with its lighted soccer field and age specific playgrounds. The community center attached to Stupak gives it an advantage for indoor activities like basketball and weightlifting. Families in the area often use both parks depending on what their children need on a given day. One detail Ryan Rose highlights for families considering Naked City is that Stupak Park's lighted soccer field is one of the few synthetic turf fields in this part of the valley available at no cost. He points out that access to both Stupak and Bob Baskin within walking distance is a practical advantage that many buyers overlook when evaluating the neighborhood.
