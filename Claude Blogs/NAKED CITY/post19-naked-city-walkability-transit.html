# Walk Score and Transit in Naked City Las Vegas Naked City Las Vegas has a Walk Score of 54 out of 100, a Transit Score of 40 out of 100, and a Bike Score of 52 out of 100, making it a somewhat walkable neighborhood with bus access along Sahara Avenue. ## What Is the Walk Score for Naked City Las Vegas? Naked City earns a Walk Score of 54 out of 100, which places it in the "Somewhat Walkable" category. This means that some errands can be completed on foot, but a car is still useful for most daily tasks. Grocery stores, restaurants, and convenience shops are present along the Sahara Avenue and Las Vegas Boulevard corridors, but the neighborhood's layout and block spacing make walking less efficient than driving for longer trips. The score reflects the area's mixed use character, where residential blocks sit close to commercial strips but are not always connected by comfortable pedestrian routes. ## What Transit Options Exist in Naked City? The neighborhood scores 40 out of 100 for transit, classified as "Some Transit." Two bus routes serve the area directly. The SX Sahara Express stops within the neighborhood boundary, and Route 119 runs approximately 0.1 miles from the center of the area. The SAHARA Las Vegas hotel, about 0.5 miles from Naked City, has a Las Vegas Monorail station that connects riders to properties along the east side of the Strip, ending at the Convention Center and MGM Grand. This monorail access is a meaningful addition for residents who work on the Strip and want to avoid parking costs. ## Is Naked City Bikeable? Naked City scores 52 out of 100 for bikeability, placing it in the "Bikeable" range. Flat terrain throughout the Las Vegas valley makes cycling physically easy, and the short distances to the Strip, downtown, and nearby shopping corridors are practical by bike. However, dedicated bike lanes are limited in this part of the city, and riders should expect to share roads with vehicle traffic. The area's grid street layout does make navigation straightforward for cyclists familiar with the neighborhood. He notes that the combination of bus service on Sahara Avenue and monorail access at the SAHARA hotel gives Naked City residents transit options that most neighborhoods at this price point simply do not offer.
