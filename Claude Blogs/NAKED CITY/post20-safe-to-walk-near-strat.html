# Is It Safe to Walk Near the Stratosphere Las Vegas? Walking near the STRAT in Las Vegas requires awareness of the surrounding Naked City neighborhood, which has a high crime rate at 75.39 per 1,000 residents and an F safety grade from CrimeGrade.org for the 89102 zip code. ## What Is the Crime Rate Near the Stratosphere? The area surrounding the STRAT falls within the 89102 zip code, which receives an F grade from CrimeGrade.org, placing it in the 3rd percentile for safety nationwide. The overall crime rate is 75.39 per 1,000 residents, and the violent crime rate of 71.9 is significantly higher than the national average of 22.7. The Naked City neighborhood immediately west of the STRAT has historically been associated with gang activity, drug trafficking, and a visible homeless population. These statistics reflect the broader zip code and not every block equally, but they are important to understand before walking through the area. ## When Is It Safest to Walk Near the STRAT? Daytime hours generally present fewer risks than nighttime, especially along Las Vegas Boulevard where foot traffic from tourists and resort guests provides a level of natural surveillance. The STRAT property itself, including its entrance, parking areas, and the stretch of the Boulevard directly in front, is monitored by casino security and well lit at night. Moving west off Las Vegas Boulevard into the residential streets of Naked City is where conditions change noticeably, with less lighting, fewer pedestrians, and more open lots. Sticking to main roads and walking with purpose during daylight hours reduces risk considerably. ## What Precautions Should Walkers Take? Standard urban safety practices apply in this area. Avoid displaying expensive electronics or jewelry while walking. Stay on well lit, well traveled streets, particularly Las Vegas Boulevard and Sahara Avenue. Walking in groups is preferable to walking alone, especially after dark. Rideshare services are widely available and inexpensive for short trips between the STRAT and other parts of the Strip or downtown. Valley Hospital is approximately 1.5 miles away and UMC, the region's Level I Trauma Center, is roughly 2 miles from the neighborhood. Based on Ryan Rose's experience walking this neighborhood, he advises buyers and visitors to spend time in the area during both day and evening hours before making any decisions. He notes that the blocks closest to Las Vegas Boulevard feel different from the interior residential streets, and understanding that contrast is essential for anyone considering a home purchase or investment nearby.
