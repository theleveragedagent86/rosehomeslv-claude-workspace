# Top of the World Restaurant at The STRAT Top of the World is an award winning fine dining restaurant on the 106th floor of The STRAT Tower in Las Vegas, featuring a 360 degree revolving dining room roughly 0.3 miles from the Naked City neighborhood. ## What Is Top of the World Restaurant at The STRAT? Top of the World is a fine dining restaurant located at Level 106 of The STRAT Tower, one of the tallest freestanding observation towers in the United States. The dining room completes a full 360 degree rotation approximately every 80 minutes, giving diners continuously shifting views of the Las Vegas Strip, surrounding mountains, and the broader valley. The restaurant sits at the very northern edge of the Strip, making it one of the closest upscale dining options for residents of Naked City, just 0.3 miles to the west. ## What Kind of Food Does Top of the World Serve? The menu at Top of the World focuses on contemporary American fine dining with an emphasis on steak, seafood, and seasonal ingredients. The restaurant has earned multiple awards for both its cuisine and its wine program. Pricing falls in the four dollar sign range, making it a destination for celebrations, anniversaries, and special occasions rather than everyday dining. Reservations are strongly recommended, especially for window seating during sunset hours when demand is highest. ## Is Top of the World Worth the Price? For the combination of food quality and the experience of dining 800 feet above the Strip, Top of the World delivers something that few restaurants in Las Vegas can match. The rotating view alone sets it apart from ground level fine dining options. Visitors should be aware that The STRAT Tower charges an observation deck fee for general visitors, but diners at Top of the World receive complimentary elevator access. Dress code is upscale casual, and the atmosphere is quieter and more refined than most Strip restaurants. One experience Ryan Rose recommends for visitors and residents alike is a sunset dinner at Top of the World, where the transition from daylight to the glow of the Strip creates a uniquely Las Vegas moment that never gets old, even for locals.
