Is Naked City a good place to live? For residents who value affordability, central location, and proximity to the Las Vegas Strip, Naked City offers a combination that few other neighborhoods in the valley can match. ## Is Naked City Las Vegas a Good Place to Live in 2026? Naked City sits just west of the north end of the Strip, placing residents within a two to five minute drive of major resorts, entertainment, and employment centers. The neighborhood falls within the 89102 ZIP code, where the typical home value is $335,074 and condos start around $150,000. For buyers and renters working in hospitality, healthcare, or the service industry, the commute savings alone can be significant. The area has no HOA, no age restrictions, and no gated entry, which appeals to people seeking flexibility and lower monthly costs. Public transit options include the SX Sahara Express and Route 119 bus lines. Bob Baskin Park and Stupak Park provide green space for recreation, and Valley Hospital Medical Center is about 1.5 miles away. ## What Are the Drawbacks of Living in Naked City? Transparency matters when evaluating any neighborhood. The 89102 ZIP code receives an F grade from CrimeGrade.org, with a crime rate of 75.39 per 1,000 residents annually. The violent crime rate is well above the national average. Much of the housing stock dates from the 1950s through the 1980s, so buyers should budget for potential repairs or updates. The Walk Score of 54 and Transit Score of 40 indicate that while some errands are walkable, most residents will still rely on a car. The neighborhood is also predominantly renter occupied (about 70%), which can affect community stability and long term property maintenance in some blocks. Prospective residents should visit in person, at different times of day, to get an accurate feel for the area. ## What About Naked City's Future? Revitalization efforts are underway. The "Philly at 215" project marks the first new residential construction in over 65 years, and City Councilwoman Olivia Diaz has championed broader improvements. The adjacent Gateway District is attracting commercial investment, which could have a positive ripple effect on Naked City's livability over time. ## Insight from a Local Expert Ryan Rose, who has toured hundreds of Naked City homes, notes that the neighborhood rewards buyers who do their homework.
