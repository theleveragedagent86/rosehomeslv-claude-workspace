# Naked City vs Gateway District Las Vegas Naked City and the Gateway District refer to overlapping areas near the north end of the Las Vegas Strip, with Naked City being the historic residential neighborhood and the Gateway District representing the evolving commercial and cultural identity of the same zone. ## Are Naked City and the Gateway District the Same Place? The two names describe different aspects of the same general area. Naked City is the longstanding residential neighborhood bounded by Sahara Avenue, Valley View Boulevard, Desert Inn Road, and Rancho Drive. The Gateway District is a newer designation that emerged as the area began attracting indie shops, vintage stores, and antique dealers. City Councilwoman Olivia Diaz has described the area as "the gateway into Las Vegas," reflecting the vision of a neighborhood that serves as an entry point between downtown and the Strip. While Naked City carries decades of residential history, the Gateway District name signals a forward looking commercial and creative identity. ## How Do Real Estate Markets Compare? Because the two areas overlap geographically, the real estate fundamentals are largely shared. The 89102 zip code reports a typical home value of $335,074 with values up 25.1% over the past year. Condos start around $150,000 while single family homes can reach $500,000 or more. Approximately 62% of housing is multi family and about 70% of residents are renters. The median days on market sits at 39. Properties marketed under the Gateway District branding may carry slightly different perceptions, but pricing reflects the same underlying market conditions. New construction like Philly at 215, with its 87 units, benefits both identities equally. ## Which Name Should Buyers Search For? Buyers searching for property in this area should use both terms. Listings may reference Naked City, the Gateway District, or simply the 89102 zip code. Developers envisioning the future of this area tend to favor the Gateway District label, which emphasizes the mixed income, working class neighborhood concept they are building toward. Longtime residents and local media more commonly use Naked City. Understanding both terms ensures that buyers do not miss relevant listings or overlook the full scope of activity in this part of Las Vegas.
