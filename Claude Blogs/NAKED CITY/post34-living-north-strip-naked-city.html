# Living Near the North Strip in Las Vegas Living near the north Strip in Naked City puts residents within a two to five minute drive of major casinos, entertainment, and dining while offering some of the most affordable home prices in the Las Vegas Valley. ## What Is Daily Life Like Near the North Strip? Naked City sits at the north end of the Las Vegas Strip, placing residents within walking distance of The STRAT at 0.3 miles and the SAHARA Las Vegas at 0.5 miles. The drive to the airport takes 10 to 15 minutes, and downtown is just five to seven minutes away. The neighborhood earns a Walk Score of 54, a Transit Score of 40, and a Bike Score of 52, reflecting moderate walkability with solid access to public transportation along major corridors. Valley Hospital is 1.5 miles from the neighborhood and UMC is two miles away, providing quick access to medical care. Atomic Golf sits adjacent to the area, adding a modern entertainment option to the immediate surroundings. ## How Affordable Is It to Live Near the North Strip? Naked City is one of the few neighborhoods where proximity to the Strip does not come with a luxury price tag. Condos start around $150,000 and single family homes range up to $500,000 or more, with a typical home value of $335,074. The median listed price reached $389,000 as of February 2026. Roughly 70% of residents are renters paying a median rent of $1,284, which remains well below the average for Strip adjacent areas. With no HOA fees and no gated communities, ongoing housing costs stay lower than comparable neighborhoods. The 89102 zip code has seen home values increase 25.1% over the past year, reflecting growing demand for this location. ## What Are the Trade Offs of North Strip Living? Proximity to the Strip brings certain realities. The neighborhood carries an F safety grade from CrimeGrade.org with a crime rate of 75.39 per 1,000 residents, and violent crime sits higher than the national average. The population density of 228,860 per square mile makes it one of the most compact neighborhoods in Las Vegas. However, ongoing redevelopment through projects like Philly at 215 and the support of City Councilwoman Olivia Diaz signal that conditions are actively improving. Buyers who enter during this transitional period may benefit from both current affordability and future appreciation. Ryan Rose, who specializes in north Strip real estate, notes that the combination of a five minute Strip commute, rising property values, and active redevelopment creates a window of opportunity that will not last indefinitely as the Gateway District vision takes shape.
