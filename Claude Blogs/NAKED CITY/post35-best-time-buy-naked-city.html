# Best Time to Buy in Naked City Las Vegas The best time to buy in Naked City Las Vegas is during the current redevelopment phase, when home values are rising but prices remain well below comparable Strip adjacent neighborhoods across the valley. ## Why Is Now a Good Time to Buy in Naked City? Home values in the 89102 zip code have increased 25.1% over the past year, with the typical home value reaching $335,074. Despite this growth, entry points remain accessible with condos starting around $150,000 and single family homes available up to $500,000 or more. The median listed price of $389,000 as of February 2026 still sits below many neighborhoods with similar proximity to the Strip. The Philly at 215 development brought 87 new residential units to the area, representing the first new construction in over 65 years. This kind of development activity typically signals the early stages of a longer appreciation cycle, making current pricing attractive for buyers who plan to hold. ## What Seasonal Trends Affect Naked City Home Prices? Las Vegas real estate generally follows predictable seasonal patterns. Spring and early summer bring the highest buyer activity and competition, which can push prices upward. Fall and winter months tend to see fewer buyers in the market, creating more negotiating leverage for those willing to purchase during the slower season. The median days on market in the 89102 zip code currently sits at 39, suggesting that well priced homes move at a moderate pace rather than selling instantly. Buyers who monitor new listings closely and act within the first two weeks of a property hitting the market tend to secure better terms than those who wait. ## What Should First Time Buyers Know About Naked City? Naked City's housing stock is heavily weighted toward multi family properties, with approximately 62% of units in that category and about 35% single family. Roughly 70% of current residents are renters, which creates opportunities for investors as well as owner occupants looking to build equity in an appreciating market. There are no HOA fees or gated communities in the neighborhood, keeping monthly costs predictable. The area's ongoing transformation under the Gateway District vision, championed by City Councilwoman Olivia Diaz, adds a layer of long term upside that newer neighborhoods cannot offer.
