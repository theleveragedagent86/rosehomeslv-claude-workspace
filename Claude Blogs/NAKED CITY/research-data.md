## Lifestyle & Location
- Drive to Strip: ~2-5 minutes (Naked City is immediately adjacent to the north end of the Strip near The STRAT/SAHARA)
- Drive to Airport: ~10-15 minutes (approx. 5-6 miles to Harry Reid International Airport)
- Drive to Downtown (Fremont Street): ~5-7 minutes (approx. 1.5-2 miles north)
- Walk Score: 54/100 "Somewhat Walkable" (at 2400 W Sahara Ave reference point)
- Transit Score: 40/100 "Some Transit" (bus lines: SX Sahara Express at 0.0 mi, Route 119 Simmons/Koval at 0.1 mi)
- Bike Score: 52/100 "Bikeable"
- Nearest Hospital: Valley Hospital Medical Center, 620 Shadow Lane, Las Vegas NV 89106 (~1.5 miles south, ~5 min drive); University Medical Center (UMC), 1800 W Charleston Blvd (~2 miles south, ~7 min drive; Level I Trauma Center, 564 beds)
- Safety: HIGH CRIME AREA. 89102 receives an F grade from CrimeGrade.org (3rd percentile for safety -- safer than only 3% of US ZIP codes). Crime rate: 75.39 per 1,000 residents annually. Violent crime rate 71.9 vs. national avg 22.7. Naked City specifically identified as "one of the roughest parts of Las Vegas" with violent crime common; historically associated with gangs, drug trafficking, and homelessness since the 1980s. Cost of crime per resident: $936/year ($472 above national avg).
- Pet Friendly: Limited -- no dedicated dog parks within the immediate neighborhood; Bob Baskin Park and Stupak Park are nearby for leashed walks; nearest off-leash dog parks are further out (Desert Breeze Dog Park ~5 mi). Urban environment not ideal for pets.
- Retirement Friendly: Not recommended for retirees seeking quiet, low-crime living. However, proximity to medical facilities (Valley Hospital 1.5 mi, UMC 2 mi) and the Strip's amenities could appeal to some active retirees. No 55+ age-restricted sections. High renter population (70%) suggests transient demographics.
- Notable: Naked City is one of Las Vegas's most historically significant and rapidly evolving neighborhoods. It sits in a prime location immediately adjacent to the north end of the Strip, making it a target for redevelopment and gentrification. The "Philly at 215" project marks the first new residential construction in 65+ years. City Councilwoman Olivia Diaz has championed revitalization, stating "It's the gateway into Las Vegas and we want that same transformation to happen." Developers Plantone and Raanan envision a mixed-income working-class neighborhood for Strip employees. The area also saw controversy over a proposed parolee transitional housing facility (3 Keys Communities, 2024), which developers opposed. Downtown Spaces & Naked City Studios at 1800 Industrial Rd represents the arts/culture side of the neighborhood's evolution.
