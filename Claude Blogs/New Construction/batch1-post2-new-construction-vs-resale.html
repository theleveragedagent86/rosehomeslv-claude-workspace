# New Construction vs Resale Homes in Las Vegas: Pros and Cons Choosing between a new construction home and a resale in Las Vegas comes down to your priorities around price, customization, timing, and location. Both options have genuine advantages depending on where you are in life and what you value most in a home. ## The Case for New Construction New construction homes in Las Vegas come with a builder warranty, modern energy efficiency, and the ability to personalize finishes at the design center. Nevada law requires builders to provide a minimum one-year warranty on workmanship, two years on mechanical systems, and up to ten years on structural components. For buyers who want predictability and zero deferred maintenance, that warranty coverage alone can justify the premium over a resale. Energy efficiency is another major advantage. Homes built in 2025 and 2026 comply with Nevada's updated 2024 International Energy Conservation Code, which means better insulation, more efficient HVAC systems, and lower utility bills. In a Las Vegas summer where air conditioning runs 10 to 11 months a year, that efficiency gap between a new home and a 15-year-old resale can translate to hundreds of dollars in monthly savings. ## The Case for Resale Homes Resale homes in Las Vegas are often located in more established areas with mature landscaping, shorter commutes to employment centers, and price points that reflect more square footage for the dollar. In 2026, the new construction median sits around $530,000, while resale inventory can offer comparable size in desirable zip codes for less. Resale buyers also avoid certain new construction costs that catch buyers off guard. SID and LID assessments, design center upgrade markups, and lengthy construction timelines are not factors in a resale transaction. A resale home purchased today can close in 30 to 45 days, while a to-be-built new home might take 6 to 12 months to complete. ## Where New Construction Wins Outright Builder incentives in 2026 have made new construction financing more attractive than resale in many cases. DR Horton offered a 3.99% rate buydown used by nearly three quarters of their buyers. Taylor Morrison offered tiered rates starting at 2.99% in year one. Resale sellers rarely offer anything close to that level of financing assistance. For buyers who qualify, a builder-subsidized interest rate can lower monthly payments enough to offset a higher purchase price. New construction also wins for buyers who want a brand-new home in a master-planned community. Summerlin, Cadence in Henderson, and emerging areas like Skye Summit offer amenities, trails, and community infrastructure that older resale neighborhoods simply cannot replicate. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose has helped buyers work through this exact decision dozens of times.
