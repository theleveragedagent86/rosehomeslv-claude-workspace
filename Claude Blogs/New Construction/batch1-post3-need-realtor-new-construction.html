# Do You Need a Realtor When Buying New Construction in Las Vegas? Yes, you absolutely should have a realtor when buying new construction in Las Vegas, and the good news is that it costs you nothing. The builder pays the buyer's agent commission, so you get professional representation at no expense to you. ## The Builder's Agent Works for the Builder Every new construction sales office in Las Vegas has an on-site agent. That agent is friendly, knowledgeable, and paid by the builder. Their job is to represent the builder's interests, not yours. They will answer your questions, walk you through the model, and help you complete the purchase contract. What they will not do is point out unfavorable contract terms, flag communities with high SID assessments, or recommend you get an independent inspection. This is not a criticism of those agents. It is simply the structure of the transaction. When you visit a new home sales office without your own realtor, you are the only person in that room without professional representation. ## What a Buyer's Agent Actually Does in New Construction An experienced buyer's agent who works frequently with Las Vegas builders can help you negotiate upgrades, push for closing cost credits, and identify which design center selections offer the best resale value. Builders do not typically negotiate base price, but they do negotiate on incentives, and having an agent who knows what is currently available across multiple communities gives you real leverage. Your agent can also review the purchase contract before you sign it. Builder contracts in Las Vegas are written by the builder's legal team and heavily favor the builder. Key provisions around construction delays, cancellation rights, and warranty exclusions deserve a careful read. An agent familiar with these contracts knows which clauses to flag and when to ask for modifications. ## The One Critical Timing Rule You must bring your realtor with you on your first visit to the sales office or register them before that first visit. Las Vegas builders require agent registration at first contact. If you tour a community without your realtor and then try to add them to the transaction later, the builder will almost always deny it. Your agent loses their commission, and you lose your representation for the rest of the purchase. This rule catches buyers off guard constantly. Even if you are just planning to browse or gather information, bring your realtor along or call them first so they can register online before you walk in the door. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose has accompanied buyers to sales offices across Summerlin, Henderson, North Las Vegas, and the southwest valley. He consistently finds that represented buyers receive better incentive packages than unrepresented buyers, not because builders are penalizing unrepresented buyers, but because an experienced agent knows what to ask for. Buyers who walk in alone often leave with whatever the standard offer happens to be that week.
