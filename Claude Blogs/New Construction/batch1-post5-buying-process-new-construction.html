# New Construction Home Buying Process in Las Vegas: Step by Step Buying a new construction home in Las Vegas follows a different process than buying a resale, and knowing what to expect at each stage will help you avoid costly mistakes. The process typically runs 6 to 12 months from contract to keys, depending on where the home is in the build cycle. ## Step 1: Get Pre-Approved and Register Your Realtor First Before visiting any model home, get pre-approved with a lender and contact your realtor. Your realtor must be registered with the builder before or during your first visit to the sales office. Las Vegas builders strictly enforce this rule and will not add an agent to the transaction after the fact. Your pre-approval letter tells the sales agent you are a serious buyer and prevents you from falling in love with a home outside your budget. ## Step 2: Choose Your Community and Lot Once you select a community and builder, you will choose a specific lot and floorplan. Lot premiums for corner lots, cul de sac positions, and premium views add to the base price and must be factored into your budget upfront. Popular communities like Cadence and Summerlin villages can have wait lists for specific lots, so moving quickly when a desirable lot opens matters. ## Step 3: Sign the Purchase Contract Builder contracts in Las Vegas are written to protect the builder, not the buyer. Key terms cover the earnest money deposit (typically 1% to 3% of the purchase price), construction timeline and delay provisions, and cancellation rights. Most builder contracts have very limited cancellation rights after signing. Review the contract carefully with your realtor before signing. If anything is unclear, ask for clarification in writing. ## Step 4: Design Center Selections Within a few weeks of signing your contract, you will visit the builder's design center to select flooring, cabinets, countertops, fixtures, and upgrades. This appointment is exciting and expensive. Builders mark up design center upgrades significantly, so prioritize structural and hard-to-change options like extended garages, extra bathrooms, or pre-plumbing for a pool. Cosmetic items like flooring and fixtures can often be upgraded more affordably after closing. ## Step 5: Construction Phase and Inspections Construction typically takes 6 to 9 months for a standard production home. Schedule a pre-drywall inspection with a licensed third-party inspector when framing, plumbing, and electrical are visible but before the walls close. This inspection runs $200 to $400 and is one of the most valuable steps in the process. Nevada law does not require builders to correct every cosmetic issue flagged by your inspector, but structural and mechanical findings carry more weight. ## Step 6: Final Walkthrough and Closing Before closing, you will complete a blue tape walkthrough where you and the builder's representative identify items needing correction. These are noted and the builder schedules repairs before closing or shortly after. Your lender will order a final appraisal and you will receive a closing disclosure three business days before your closing date. New construction closing costs in Las Vegas typically run 2% to 3% of the purchase price, though builder incentives often cover part or all of these costs in 2026. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose walks buyers through this process from pre-approval to keys and beyond.
