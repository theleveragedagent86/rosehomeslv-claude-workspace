# Upgrades You Should Never Buy from the Builder in Las Vegas Builder design centers are beautifully staged to make every upgrade look essential, but some of them are dramatically overpriced compared to what you can get on the open market after closing. Knowing which upgrades to skip at the design center can free up tens of thousands of dollars for better uses. ## Flooring Is Almost Always Cheaper Aftermarket Flooring is the most common area where builders mark up significantly above market rates. Builders typically charge $8 to $12 per square foot installed for luxury vinyl plank or tile, including labor. Independent flooring companies in Las Vegas regularly install comparable or superior products for $4 to $7 per square foot. On a 2,000 square foot home, that difference adds up to $8,000 to $10,000 or more. The builder's selection is also limited to what their supply partner provides. Aftermarket flooring companies offer a far wider range of products, styles, and quality tiers. Unless you want to move into a finished floor on day one, this is one of the easiest upgrades to defer and handle independently after closing. ## Appliance Packages Offer Little Value Through the Builder Appliance upgrades are another area where builder pricing consistently loses to the open market. Builders source appliances through commercial agreements that do not pass savings to the buyer. A builder kitchen appliance package that costs $6,000 as an upgrade can typically be purchased and installed through retailers like Best Buy, Costco, or local appliance stores for $3,500 to $4,500 with equivalent or better brands. Builders also tend to offer limited brand choices. If you have a preference for a specific brand or configuration, you are unlikely to find it in the builder's catalog. Deferring appliance upgrades allows you to shop sales, open-box deals, and floor model discounts that simply do not exist in the design center environment. ## Lighting Fixtures and Ceiling Fans Light fixtures and ceiling fans offered at the design center are typically marked up 40 to 60 percent above retail. A ceiling fan that costs $80 at a home improvement store might appear as a $180 upgrade in the design center. Lighting throughout a home represents hundreds of individual fixtures over time, and the cumulative markup adds up quickly. The exception here is recessed lighting and electrical rough-in work. Adding new electrical boxes, running wiring, and installing recessed cans after construction is expensive and disruptive. If you want additional recessed lighting in specific rooms, that electrical work is worth doing during construction. The fixtures themselves can be swapped out any time. ## Countertops Deserve Careful Comparison Countertop upgrades from the builder can be reasonable or wildly overpriced depending on the specific builder and tier. Always ask for the price per square foot on the exact material being quoted, then compare to local stone fabricators in Las Vegas. Companies like Bedrosians, The Countertop Factory, and local independent fabricators often supply and install granite or quartz at 20 to 40 percent less than what a builder charges for the same or similar material. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose advises clients to treat the design center like a first draft, not a final decision.
