# Structural Upgrades That Cannot Be Changed Later in Las Vegas Some builder upgrades are simply cosmetic preferences, and others are permanent decisions baked into the physical structure of the home. Understanding which upgrades fall into the structural category is one of the most important things a new construction buyer can do before signing. ## What Makes an Upgrade Structural A structural upgrade modifies the framing, foundation, plumbing rough-in, or electrical layout of a home. Once drywall is up and the home is closed in, these elements become inaccessible without significant demolition. Builders in Las Vegas allow buyers to select structural options during a dedicated structural appointment that typically happens early in the purchase process, often before a design center appointment. Common structural upgrades in Las Vegas communities include extended garages, tandem third-car bays, casitas or attached guest suites, multi-generational living spaces with private entries, loft conversions, bedroom-to-office conversions, and covered patio extensions. Each of these modifies the physical floor plan in ways that cannot be altered after the frame is built without tearing into existing structure. ## Extended Garages and Casitas The extended garage is one of the most practical structural upgrades available in Las Vegas. A standard two-car garage in a production home is often sized to fit two vehicles with minimal clearance. An extension of two to four feet adds room for storage, a workbench, a golf cart, or recreational equipment. Builders typically price garage extensions at $3,000 to $8,000. Adding length to a finished garage later requires demo, slab work, and reconstruction that can cost $25,000 to $40,000. Casitas and detached guest suites are among the most requested structural options in Las Vegas communities. These separate living spaces with private entries serve as in-law quarters, rental opportunities, or home offices. A casita added during construction typically costs $30,000 to $60,000 depending on size and builder. Adding equivalent square footage as an addition after closing in Las Vegas routinely costs $150,000 or more when all trades, permits, and finishes are factored in. ## Rough-In Plumbing and Electrical Some structural upgrades do not add visible space but position the home for future improvements. Pool pre-plumb packages install the plumbing lines from the home to the backyard pad during construction. Without this option, adding a pool later requires trenching through the slab and yard, adding $4,000 to $8,000 in rough-in costs alone before the pool is even started. A water softener loop installs a bypass in the main water supply line that allows a softener to be connected without cutting into finished walls. A gas loop or stub for an outdoor kitchen performs the same function for future gas appliances. These plumbing rough-ins cost a few hundred to a few thousand dollars during construction but require major disruption to add after the home is complete. ## Ceiling Height and Window Configurations Ceiling height is a structural element that cannot be changed once the roof is framed. Many Las Vegas builders offer an upgrade from standard nine-foot ceilings to ten-foot or even eleven-foot ceilings in single-story homes. This upgrade affects how open and livable a home feels and is not replicable without full reconstruction. Window count, size, and placement are also structural. Adding a window to an exterior wall after the home is complete involves structural modification, weatherproofing, stucco repair, and interior finishing work that typically costs $2,500 to $5,000 per window. Selecting the right window configurations at the time of purchase eliminates this expense entirely. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose advises every new construction client to treat the structural appointment as the single most consequential meeting of the entire purchase process.
