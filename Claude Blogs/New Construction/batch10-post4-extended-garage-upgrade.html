# The Extended Garage: The Most Underrated Upgrade in Las Vegas If there is one builder upgrade that Las Vegas homeowners wish they had selected more often than any other, it is the extended garage. It is quiet, unsexy, and often overlooked in favor of countertop or flooring upgrades at the design center, but it delivers more practical value per dollar than almost any other option on the list. ## Why Standard Las Vegas Garages Fall Short Production builders in Las Vegas typically include a two-car garage that measures approximately 20 feet deep by 20 feet wide. This is the minimum footprint needed to fit two standard sedans with the garage door closed. It leaves almost no room for storage, a water heater, a water softener, a workbench, recreational gear, or any of the other items that realistically accumulate in a Las Vegas household. Las Vegas residents also tend to use their garages heavily as utility spaces. Summers in the valley push 115 degrees Fahrenheit, which means the garage often becomes the staging area for tools, bikes, holiday items, sporting equipment, and anything that cannot tolerate attic heat. A standard garage fills up quickly, and once it does, the overflow ends up in living spaces or paid storage units. ## What a Garage Extension Actually Costs Builder garage extensions in Las Vegas typically add two to four feet of depth to the standard footprint. A two-foot extension on a two-car garage adds roughly 40 square feet of floor space and costs approximately $3,000 to $5,000 at the time of construction. A four-foot extension adds approximately 80 square feet and typically runs $6,000 to $10,000 depending on the builder. Adding garage depth after the home is complete is a fundamentally different project. It requires demolishing the existing rear wall, extending the foundation, reframing, reroofing, restuccoing, and refinishing the interior. When all trades are included, a four-foot garage extension added post-construction in Las Vegas typically costs $25,000 to $40,000. The math on doing it at the time of construction is not close. ## The Tandem Bay Option Many Las Vegas builders also offer a tandem third-car bay as a structural option. This configuration adds a third parking space accessed by driving through the second bay, creating a tandem arrangement. Tandem bays are excellent for storing a third vehicle, a golf cart, an RV or boat (where permitted by the HOA), or as a dedicated workshop or gym space. Tandem bays typically cost $15,000 to $25,000 as a builder option and essentially cannot be added after construction without a full addition project. In resale, homes with tandem garages consistently attract more interest from buyers in Las Vegas, particularly those coming from California where garage space is a perpetual premium. ## The Resale Argument for Extended Garages Buyers in the Las Vegas market actively filter for garage size when searching for resale properties. A home with an extended or three-car garage commands a measurable premium over an otherwise identical home with a standard two-car configuration. Depending on the community and price point, that premium ranges from $10,000 to $30,000 at resale. When the upgrade cost at the time of construction was $5,000 to $10,000, the return on investment is clear. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose recommends the garage extension to nearly every buyer considering a production home in Las Vegas, regardless of how many cars they own. The additional square footage is useful day one and becomes more useful every year.
