# Flooring Upgrades: Tile vs LVP vs Carpet in Las Vegas New Construction Choosing the right flooring upgrade in a Las Vegas new construction home matters more than most buyers realize. The desert climate, hard water, and heavy AC use all affect how flooring holds up over time, and the choice you make at the builder's design center will follow you for years. ## What Builders Offer as Standard Most Las Vegas production builders include a base-grade carpet throughout the home as standard, with tile or vinyl plank limited to wet areas like bathrooms and laundry rooms. The carpet is typically a low-pile, 20-ounce or lighter commercial grade that compresses quickly under foot traffic. Builders like Lennar, DR Horton, and KB Home each have their own design center packages, and the jump from base carpet to upgraded flooring usually starts around $3,000 to $6,000 for a 2,000-square-foot home depending on the material selected. At the design center, builders typically offer three main flooring categories: ceramic or porcelain tile, luxury vinyl plank (LVP), and carpet in upgraded grades. Each has a different price tier and a very different performance profile in the Las Vegas climate. ## Tile: The Desert Standard Porcelain tile is the most popular flooring choice in Las Vegas new homes, and for good reason. It handles extreme heat, hard water, and daily cleaning without warping, fading, or retaining odors. In a climate where barefoot mornings and scorpion checks are realities, smooth tile is easy to inspect and easier to clean. Builder tile packages at the design center typically run $5 to $10 per square foot installed, while aftermarket tile installation through a local flooring company can cost $4 to $8 per square foot for comparable material. The savings after closing on tile can be meaningful, but many buyers prefer the convenience of having it done before move-in. One important note: large-format tile (24x24 or larger) is far more labor-intensive to install correctly and almost always cheaper and better executed by a specialized tile contractor after closing than through a builder's subcontractor working on a production schedule. ## LVP: The Practical Middle Ground Luxury vinyl plank has become extremely popular in Las Vegas new construction because it is waterproof, warm underfoot, and significantly softer than tile for households with young children or pets. Good-quality LVP runs 6mm to 12mm thick with an attached pad, and the difference between builder-grade and aftermarket LVP is mostly in that thickness and the quality of the wear layer. Builder LVP packages typically cost $4 to $7 per square foot at the design center. Aftermarket installation through a flooring retailer often lands in the $3 to $6 range for comparable or better product, particularly when buying during a sale period. The one concern with LVP in Las Vegas is heat. In a home that sits vacant during summer or has areas that receive intense direct sunlight, cheap LVP can warp. Choosing a product rated for temperatures above 140 degrees Fahrenheit is important in this climate. ## Carpet: Where It Still Makes Sense Carpet is not disappearing from Las Vegas homes, but buyers are increasingly limiting it to bedrooms only. An upgraded carpet with a thick pad significantly improves comfort in sleeping areas and adds a layer of sound insulation between floors. Builder carpet upgrades for bedrooms only are often a reasonable value at the design center because installation pricing is bundled. For main living areas, skipping carpet entirely and doing tile or LVP is the more durable and resale-friendly choice. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose consistently advises buyers to do tile or LVP on the main floor at the builder's design center if budget allows, and to skip the builder's carpet upgrade in favor of aftermarket installation post-closing, where you get better material at lower cost. Spending design center credits on structural items like extended garages or insulation upgrades tends to deliver more long-term value than premium carpet throughout.
