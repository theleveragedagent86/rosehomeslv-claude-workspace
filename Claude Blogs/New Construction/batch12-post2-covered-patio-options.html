# Covered Patio Options for Las Vegas: Builder vs Custom After Closing A covered patio is one of the most used spaces in a Las Vegas home, and the decision to add one through the builder or after closing has significant financial and quality implications. In a city where summer temperatures regularly exceed 115 degrees, outdoor shade is not a luxury. ## What Builders Typically Offer Most Las Vegas production builders offer covered patio extensions as a structural upgrade at the design center. These are typically aluminum patio covers, sometimes called "Alumawood" or similar composite systems, and they are priced as flat-rate structural add-ons. A builder-installed covered patio extension of 12 to 16 feet often costs $8,000 to $18,000 through the design center depending on the builder and the size. DR Horton, Lennar, and KB Home each have different patio packages, and what is included varies widely. Some builders offer only a partial overhang extension while others provide a fully covered rear patio. The appeal of doing it through the builder is that it is rolled into your mortgage financing. Instead of paying $15,000 cash after closing, you finance it at your mortgage rate. For buyers who plan to use the outdoor space immediately, this can be the right move financially even if the builder's markup is significant. ## The Case for Going Custom After Closing Las Vegas has a thriving patio cover industry, and local contractors who specialize in aluminum patio systems, wood pergolas, and full patio enclosures typically deliver better results at lower cost than builder design center packages. A professionally installed aluminum lattice cover or solid patio cover of 300 to 400 square feet typically costs $6,000 to $14,000 through a local contractor, often with more material and customization options than a builder provides. Custom patio builders can also match your home's exterior color, install ceiling fans and lighting, and add misting systems, which most builder packages do not include. The main drawback is timing. After closing, you are paying cash or financing through a personal loan or HELOC. If you are tight on liquidity post-closing, waiting 6 to 12 months to add a patio cover is a real consideration in a city where summer arrives quickly. ## Structural vs Non-Structural: A Critical Distinction One factor that favors doing a covered patio through the builder is when the builder's plans include structural changes to the home itself, such as raising the roofline or adding a full room extension over the patio area. Those changes cannot be made after the slab is poured and the framing is complete. A simple patio cover does not require structural changes and can be installed any time after closing. A room addition or extended covered living space that integrates with the home's roof line absolutely must be done through the builder. Always ask the builder what is included and whether the structure connects to the home's roofline or is a freestanding or attached cover. That one question determines whether you have any flexibility to wait. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose recommends that buyers ask specifically what the builder includes as a standard rear covered area before purchasing any patio upgrade at the design center. Many floor plans already include a partial covered patio standard, and buyers sometimes pay for an extension they assumed was not there. For buyers who want a more elaborate outdoor entertainment space with misting, lighting, and custom finishes, waiting and hiring a local specialty contractor after closing almost always delivers more value per dollar.
