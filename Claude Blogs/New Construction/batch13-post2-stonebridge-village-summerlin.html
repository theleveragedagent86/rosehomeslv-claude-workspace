# Stonebridge Village Summerlin: New Homes, Builders and Prices Stonebridge Village sits in the northern reaches of Summerlin and is one of the most visually dramatic settings for new construction in all of Las Vegas, offering sweeping views of Red Rock Canyon and elevated terrain that sets it apart from flatter valley communities. ## Where Stonebridge Village Is Located Stonebridge Village is situated in the northern section of Summerlin, generally accessed via the Town Center Drive and Queensridge Drive corridors. The elevation here gives many homes a true view lot experience, with ridgeline and canyon vistas that builders in lower-elevation parts of Summerlin simply cannot offer. Proximity to the 215 Beltway makes commuting to the rest of the Las Vegas Valley straightforward, and residents are a short drive from Downtown Summerlin for shopping, dining, and entertainment. The village is one of the more established northern Summerlin communities, which means infrastructure and amenities are fully in place. Residents have access to Summerlin's extensive trail system, community parks, and the broader network of recreational facilities that make Summerlin consistently rank among the best master-planned communities in the country. ## Builders and Pricing in Stonebridge Village Lennar operates one of its 55-plus Heritage communities within Stonebridge Village, with pricing ranging from the mid-$400s to the mid-$800s. This Heritage at Stonebridge community is one of the more affordable entry points into active adult living in a Summerlin address. For buyers who are not restricted to 55-plus product, other builders have offered single-family homes in Stonebridge at price points generally starting in the $600s and climbing well above $1 million for larger lots with premium views. Toll Brothers has maintained a presence in the elevated view-lot sections of northern Summerlin, and buyers seeking larger luxury homes should inquire about current availability directly with the builder's sales office. Inventory turns quickly in this part of Summerlin due to limited buildable lots and strong demand from both local move-up buyers and out-of-state relocators. ## SID Assessments and HOA Structure Like most newer Summerlin villages, Stonebridge carries a Special Improvement District assessment in addition to the standard HOA fees. Buyers should request the current SID balance and annual assessment amount from any builder before signing a contract. Depending on the specific community within Stonebridge, this assessment can run from several hundred to over a thousand dollars per year on top of HOA dues. The community structure in Stonebridge includes both a sub-association HOA for each neighborhood and the overarching Summerlin master HOA. Combined monthly fees vary by community but typically fall in the $100 to $300 range. Both HOAs enforce architectural standards that protect the visual consistency and long-term value of the village. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose notes that Stonebridge is one of the few Summerlin villages where the lot premium for a view site is genuinely worth the cost. Homes positioned on the ridgeline above Las Vegas retain exceptional resale appeal because those canyon-facing lots are finite and cannot be replicated anywhere else in the valley. Ryan recommends visiting on a clear winter morning when Red Rock Canyon views are at their most vivid before making any offer.
