The 215 Beltway serves as the eastern boundary for this part of Summerlin, providing easy freeway access without putting residents directly adjacent to highway noise. West Sahara Avenue and Town Center Drive are the main surface roads connecting Kestrel Village to the rest of the Las Vegas Valley, including Downtown Summerlin, which is within a few minutes' drive. This central positioning within the Summerlin master plan means Kestrel Village residents are close to an extensive network of parks, recreational trails, and community gathering spaces. Tri Pointe Homes consistently earns top customer satisfaction ratings in the Las Vegas market and brings thoughtful design standards to their Kestrel Village communities. Buyers in Kestrel should compare design center allowances and standard features across builders carefully, as what is included at base price varies considerably from one builder to the next. ## HOA Fees and SID Considerations Like all newer Summerlin villages, Kestrel Village carries both a sub-community HOA and the Summerlin master HOA. Buyers should budget for combined monthly fees that typically fall somewhere between $100 and $300, depending on the specific neighborhood.
