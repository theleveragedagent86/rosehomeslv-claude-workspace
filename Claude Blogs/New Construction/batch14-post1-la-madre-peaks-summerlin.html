# La Madre Peaks: Summerlin's Newest New Construction Area in 2026 La Madre Peaks is the newest village opening in Summerlin, and buyers who want to get in early on a master-planned community are paying very close attention. Located along the northwestern edge of Summerlin near the Spring Mountains foothills, La Madre Peaks sits at one of the most scenic addresses in all of Las Vegas. ## What Is La Madre Peaks and Where Is It? La Madre Peaks is a planned village within the greater Summerlin master-planned community, developed by The Howard Hughes Corporation. The village takes its name from La Madre Mountain, part of the Spring Mountain Range that frames the western horizon from this neighborhood. Positioned near the 215 Beltway in the northwest, La Madre Peaks offers direct access to Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, which sits just minutes away. Homes here enjoy mountain and canyon views that few Las Vegas addresses can match. The village is designed as a premier residential area within Summerlin, with lot sizing and home placement intended to maximize the surrounding natural scenery. Streets and community planning follow the same high standards that have made Summerlin the top-selling master-planned community in the United States for multiple consecutive years. ## Who Is Building in La Madre Peaks? Toll Brothers has been among the builders staking out homes in the La Madre Peaks area, offering luxury single-family homes consistent with the premium positioning of this village. Richmond American Homes and Taylor Morrison have also shown interest in the northwest Summerlin expansion zones. Pricing in this area starts in the upper $600,000s and climbs into the $1 million-plus range for larger lots and premium view positions. Buyers considering La Madre Peaks should expect finished square footages ranging from approximately 2,500 to over 4,000 square feet. Because this is a newer village, the selection of available home sites changes quickly. Early buyers often secure the most desirable lots before models are even fully open, which is why working with a local agent early in the process matters. ## The SID Factor in La Madre Peaks Like other Summerlin villages, La Madre Peaks homes are subject to a Special Improvement District assessment. SID fees in Summerlin typically run between $500 and $3,000 annually depending on the specific community and lot. These fees fund the infrastructure, trails, and parks that define the Summerlin lifestyle, and they transfer to new owners on resale. Understanding the full monthly cost of ownership, including the SID, is essential before making an offer. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose recommends La Madre Peaks to buyers who prioritize views and long-term community quality over getting the lowest possible price per square foot.
