# Cadence Henderson: Inside the Top-Ranked Master-Planned Community Cadence in Henderson, Nevada holds the number one ranking among all master-planned communities in the United States according to RCLCO, the most widely cited research organization in residential real estate. For buyers who want a proven, amenity-rich community with real estate from the mid-$300s to over $600,000, Cadence deserves a close look in 2026. ## What Makes Cadence the Nation's Top Community Cadence is a 2,200-acre master-planned community located in the heart of Henderson, just east of US-95 along the Galleria at Sunset corridor. The community was designed around a central park system, extensive walking and biking trails, multiple community pools, a fitness center, and a social clubhouse. With 12,250 total planned homes across multiple neighborhoods, Cadence is large enough to support its own commercial ecosystem. A major milestone for the community in 2026 is the opening of Cadence Crossing, a casino and entertainment complex that will further establish the area as a self-contained destination. ## Builders and Prices Inside Cadence Five major builders operate inside Cadence: Lennar, KB Home, Century Communities, Beazer Homes, and Toll Brothers. That mix covers the full price spectrum within the community. Lennar leads with its "Everything's Included" model, where features like smart home technology and upgraded appliances come standard rather than as costly upgrades. Beazer offers its Choice Plans customization program, giving buyers flexibility on floorplan configurations before construction begins. Toll Brothers anchors the luxury end with larger homes priced from the upper $500s and above. Entry-level buyers will find the most competitive options through Century Communities, which also operates an online purchasing platform for buyers who prefer a streamlined process. ## Timeline and Urgency in 2026 Cadence has approximately two to three years of buildout remaining. That window sounds comfortable, but it narrows quickly as each new neighborhood phase opens and closes. The best lots, including those with community views, park adjacency, or cul-de-sac positions, go early in each phase release. Buyers who wait for a "better time" often find the specific floorplan or location they wanted is already gone. Henderson LID assessments apply in Cadence, and the annual amount varies by neighborhood and lot size.
