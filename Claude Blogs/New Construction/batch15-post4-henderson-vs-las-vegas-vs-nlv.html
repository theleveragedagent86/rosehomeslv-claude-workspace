# Henderson vs Las Vegas vs North Las Vegas for New Construction Choosing between Henderson, Las Vegas, and North Las Vegas for new construction is one of the most common decisions buyers face in the valley, and the right answer depends almost entirely on your priorities around budget, schools, commute, and lifestyle. ## Henderson: Best Schools and Safety, Less Land Remaining Henderson holds the strongest credentials for families. The city ranks among the top two in the nation for low violent crime, with just 2.1 violent incidents per 1,000 residents. Schools in Henderson feed into Coronado High School, Liberty High School, and Green Valley High School, all of which rank well within Clark County. New construction entry points in Henderson start around $450,000 to $500,000 for production homes, with prices rising into the $700s and beyond in communities like Lake Las Vegas and MacDonald Highlands. The trade-off is inventory: Cadence and Inspirada, the two flagship master-planned communities, are approaching final buildout. Buyers who want new construction in Henderson have a narrowing window before the supply dries up entirely. ## Las Vegas Proper: Summerlin, the Southwest, and Northwest Options The city of Las Vegas encompasses the largest share of new construction activity in the valley, including Summerlin to the northwest, Mountains Edge and Southern Highlands to the southwest, and a growing pipeline downtown. Summerlin commands the highest prices in the region, with entry points around $400,000 for smaller townhomes and prices rising to $2 million and above for custom luxury. The southwest corridor offers family-friendly communities from the low $400s with good access to the 215 beltway. Skye Canyon in the northwest is an outdoor-lifestyle-focused community at a similar price point. Las Vegas proper does not match Henderson on school rankings or crime statistics at a citywide level, but specific communities and school zones within Las Vegas can be highly competitive. ## North Las Vegas: Most Value Per Dollar, Highest Growth Trajectory North Las Vegas delivers more square footage per dollar than anywhere else in the valley. A $400,000 budget in North Las Vegas typically buys a home in the range of 2,000 to 2,400 square feet; the same budget in Henderson yields closer to 1,600 to 1,900 square feet. Entry-level pricing in North Las Vegas starts in the high $300s, the lowest threshold in the valley for new construction. The city is also the fastest-growing in Nevada, with major infrastructure investments underway including a planned UNLV North Campus on 5,000 acres of former federal land. School quality and safety rankings in North Las Vegas do not match Henderson on average, but the value proposition is compelling for buyers prioritizing budget and long-term appreciation tied to growth. ## How to Choose Between the Three The clearest framework is this: choose Henderson if schools and safety are your top priorities and you can move quickly before inventory disappears. Choose Las Vegas proper if you want Summerlin prestige, Southwest access, or a wider range of lifestyle communities. Choose North Las Vegas if you want the most home for your money and believe in the long-term growth story of a city that is transforming rapidly. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose consults with buyers on exactly this decision regularly, and the most important advice he offers is to anchor your choice in your true daily life priorities rather than general reputation.
