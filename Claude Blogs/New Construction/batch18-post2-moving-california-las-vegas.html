# Moving from California to Las Vegas New Construction Guide California residents are relocating to Las Vegas at a rate that reshapes the city every year, and in 2026 new construction is the top choice for buyers making the move. The financial case is overwhelming, and the lifestyle upgrade surprises nearly everyone who makes the switch. ## The Numbers Behind California to Las Vegas Relocation Approximately 47 percent of all Las Vegas-bound moves originate in California, representing roughly 50,000 people per year making the journey. The financial motivation is straightforward. The average California household that relocates to Las Vegas saves approximately $585,000 in housing value over their ownership period while also eliminating $20,000 to $40,000 in annual state income tax, since Nevada has no state income tax. A home that costs $1.1 million in Southern California often has a Las Vegas equivalent in the $500,000 to $600,000 range, and that equivalent is frequently brand new. This gap is why so many California buyers go directly to new construction rather than resale. They arrive with significant equity from their California sale, can often pay cash or put down 30 to 50 percent, and end up with a lower mortgage payment than they had on their California home. ## Which Communities California Buyers Choose Most Often California relocators tend to gravitate toward Summerlin and Henderson because both areas feel familiar. Summerlin, on the western edge of Las Vegas near Red Rock Canyon, offers walkable village centers, top-rated schools, and a master-planned environment that resembles Irvine or Valencia in layout. New construction in Summerlin runs from the mid-$600,000s to well over $1 million in communities like Stonebridge Village and Kestrel Village. Henderson draws buyers from the San Diego and Orange County areas in particular, with communities like Cadence offering a master-planned experience that was ranked number one nationally by RCLCO. Cadence spans 12,250 total homes and still has active new construction in 2026. The Henderson area also offers the best school ratings in the Las Vegas Valley, which matters greatly to families relocating with children. ## What California Buyers Need to Know About Nevada Taxes The property tax situation in Nevada will feel dramatically different. Nevada assesses property at 35 percent of taxable value, and primary residence increases are capped at just 3 percent annually regardless of market appreciation. The effective property tax rate runs roughly 0.48 to 0.55 percent. A $600,000 home in Las Vegas will cost approximately $2,900 to $3,300 per year in property taxes, compared to the far higher base rates plus Mello-Roos and special assessments that California buyers are accustomed to paying. Nevada does have Special Improvement Districts (SIDs) in Summerlin and Local Improvement Districts (LIDs) in Henderson communities, which add $500 to $3,000 or more annually and run for 10 to 20 years. These are worth factoring into your comparison, but even with SID and LID costs included, Nevada property holding costs remain dramatically lower than California. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose works with California relocators regularly and consistently sees one pattern: buyers underestimate how quickly they can close the gap between their California lifestyle and their new Las Vegas life.
