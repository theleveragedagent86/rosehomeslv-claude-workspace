# Multi Generational Homes in Las Vegas: NextGen, Casitas and Dual Suites Las Vegas is one of the strongest markets in the country for multigenerational new construction, and buyers have more purpose-built options here than in most comparable cities. Whether you are looking for a connected casita, a private NextGen suite, or a true dual primary floor plan, builders in the valley are ready to deliver. ## What Makes a Home Truly Multigenerational A genuinely multigenerational home goes beyond a simple in-law suite. The best floor plans include a separate entrance, a kitchenette or full kitchen, a private living area, and a dedicated bedroom with its own bathroom. This separation gives extended family members real independence while keeping them close. In the Las Vegas market, several builders have developed proprietary multigenerational programs that offer this level of separation as a standard or structural option, not an afterthought. Lennar's NextGen program is the most recognized multigenerational product in Las Vegas. NextGen homes feature an attached private suite with its own front entrance, kitchenette, laundry hookups, bedroom, and bathroom. The suite connects to the main home through an interior door, allowing families to live together while maintaining separate daily routines. Lennar offers NextGen plans across multiple Las Vegas communities in the mid $500,000s and above, with some larger plans exceeding $700,000. The program was designed specifically for families who want to house adult children, aging parents, or both under one roof. ## Other Builders Offering Multigenerational Options KB Home builds homes with optional casita suites in several Henderson and North Las Vegas communities. Richmond American offers a "Next Gen" equivalent through its structural options program, allowing buyers to add a detached or attached guest suite at the design center stage. Pulte Homes carries multigenerational floor plans in its Summerlin collections, including dual primary bedroom plans that place two full owner suites on opposite ends of the home for maximum privacy. Toll Brothers offers some of the most spacious multigenerational options in the Las Vegas luxury segment, with plans that include attached casitas exceeding 500 square feet. These are particularly popular in communities near Henderson and the west side of the valley where lot sizes support larger footprints. Beazer Homes also offers flex suite options through its Choice Plans program, which lets buyers reconfigure rooms before framing begins. ## HOA Rules and Casita Considerations Buyers considering a multigenerational home should review the community's CC&Rs before purchasing. Some HOA rules in Las Vegas restrict short term rental use of casitas and secondary suites, though long term family use is almost universally permitted. Buyers should also confirm whether the casita has a separate address or meter, which can matter for utility billing and insurance purposes. Clark County does not count an attached NextGen suite as a separate unit for property tax purposes, which is a meaningful financial advantage. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose has worked with many families who chose Las Vegas specifically because of the multigenerational options available here.
