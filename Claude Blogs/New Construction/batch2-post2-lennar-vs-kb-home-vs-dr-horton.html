# Lennar vs KB Home vs DR Horton Las Vegas: Builder Comparison If you are shopping new construction in Las Vegas in 2026, you will encounter Lennar, KB Home, and DR Horton in almost every master-planned community. These three builders dominate the volume segment of the market, but they each take a different approach to pricing, upgrades, and the buying experience. Here is what you actually need to know before signing anything. ## Lennar: The All-In Approach Lennar is the number one builder in Las Vegas by closings, moving 1,796 homes in the most recent reporting period at a median price of $519,000. Their signature selling point is the "Everything's Included" program, which means the price you see at the model home already includes features other builders charge extra for, things like quartz countertops, smart home packages, and upgraded flooring. This approach simplifies budgeting considerably. Lennar also runs some of the most aggressive lender incentives in the market, including promotional FHA rates as low as 2.75% in the first year on select communities. The tradeoff is limited personalization since you choose from defined packages rather than building a custom selections list. ## DR Horton: Volume Pricing with Rate Buydowns DR Horton ranks second in Las Vegas with 1,663 closings and the lowest median price among these three at $413,000. That price point puts them in reach of a wider pool of buyers, and they have doubled down on that accessibility through rate buydown programs. In recent months, 73% of DR Horton buyers in Las Vegas used the builder's 3.99% rate buydown, which is one of the strongest financing incentives in the market. The homes themselves are production builds with standard finishes, and the design center options carry meaningful markups. DR Horton makes the most sense for buyers who are focused on keeping the monthly payment low and do not need heavy customization. ## KB Home: Energy Efficiency and Personalization KB Home sits fourth in overall Las Vegas closings with 1,337 homes at a median of $539,000. What distinguishes them from Lennar and DR Horton is their focus on energy efficiency and buyer personalization. Every KB Home sold in Las Vegas carries ENERGY STAR certification, which matters in a desert climate where cooling costs can run 10 to 11 months out of the year. KB Home also uses a studio-based personalization model where buyers make design selections earlier in the process, allowing for more meaningful customization than the package-based approach Lennar uses. Their price per square foot tends to run slightly higher, but the energy savings and selection flexibility attract buyers who plan to stay long term. ## How to Compare on Equal Footing The most important thing to understand when comparing these three builders is that the base price does not tell the full story. Lennar's base includes more, while DR Horton and KB Home build from a lower starting point but add to it through design center upgrades and lot premiums.
