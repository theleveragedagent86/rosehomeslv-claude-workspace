# Scorpions and New Construction in Las Vegas: Why Building Disturbs Habitat New construction activity in Las Vegas does not just change the landscape above ground. It also displaces the wildlife living beneath it, and in the Mojave Desert, that includes scorpions. ## There Are 25 Species in Greater Las Vegas The greater Las Vegas area is home to 25 scorpion species, and the most dangerous of these is the Arizona bark scorpion, which is the only scorpion in North America whose sting can be life-threatening. Bark scorpions are light tan, slender, and typically between two and three inches long. They are climbers, which means they move up walls, into gaps, and through any opening they can find, including electrical conduit, pipe chases, and cracks in block walls. Scorpions are native to the desert scrub habitat that covers much of the Las Vegas Valley. When a builder clears land for a new community, the existing scorpion population does not simply disappear. It disperses into adjacent neighborhoods. This is why scorpion activity frequently spikes in established communities that border active construction zones. Residents who had never seen a scorpion before can suddenly find them appearing in homes. ## How Scorpions Enter New Construction Homes New construction in Las Vegas is almost entirely built on concrete slab foundations with concrete masonry unit block exterior walls. These materials have characteristics that can work in a scorpion's favor. The hollow cores inside concrete block walls provide shelter, and the expansion joints used to allow for thermal movement create gaps that scorpions can enter with ease. Any gap wide enough to slide a credit card through is wide enough for a scorpion. Scorpions can also enter through gaps around plumbing penetrations, utility boxes, poorly sealed door sweeps, and weep holes in block walls. In new construction, these entry points are sometimes left unaddressed at close of escrow because they fall outside what builders consider a workmanship defect. A pest control professional who specializes in scorpion proofing, sometimes called sealing or exclusion, can close off these entry points systematically. ## What to Do Before and After You Move In The most effective time to address scorpions in a new home is before you move your furniture in. An empty house is far easier to inspect and treat than a furnished one. Many pest control companies in Las Vegas offer new construction scorpion proofing packages that include UV light inspections at night, sealing of wall penetrations and expansion joints, and an initial pesticide treatment around the foundation perimeter. Bark scorpions glow under ultraviolet light, which makes UV inspection a reliable detection method. Keeping a UV flashlight in your home and doing a sweep of garage walls, closet corners, and baseboards on a regular basis is a simple precaution that many experienced Las Vegas homeowners consider part of normal desert living. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose always makes sure families with young children or pets understand the scorpion situation before buying in a new community adjacent to undeveloped desert.
