# Landscaping Your New Build in Las Vegas: Water Rules and Xeriscape Ideas Every new construction home in Las Vegas comes with a backyard of bare compacted dirt and a front yard that legally cannot have grass. Understanding Nevada's water rules and your xeriscape options is not optional — it is the foundation of homeownership in the Mojave Desert. ## What the Law Actually Says About Grass Since April 5, 2022, Nevada state law prohibits grass and spray irrigation systems in all new residential construction. This is not an HOA policy you can appeal or a local ordinance with exceptions. It applies statewide, and it means your new home can never have a traditional lawn in the front yard or any landscaped common areas. The Southern Nevada Water Authority (SNWA) also offers a $3 per square foot rebate for homeowners who remove existing turf from properties built before the ban. If you are moving from an older home where you had grass, that rebate can add up quickly. For new construction buyers, the mandate is simply built into the rules from day one. ## What Xeriscape Actually Looks Like in 2026 The word "xeriscape" brings to mind rocks and cacti, but Las Vegas xeriscape in 2026 is far more sophisticated. A well-designed desert landscape uses decomposed granite in multiple colors, boulders of varying sizes, flowering desert perennials like desert marigold and salvia, and drought-tolerant shrubs like Texas sage and lantana that bloom through the spring and fall. Drip irrigation is required by law in place of spray systems. A properly designed drip system waters directly at the root zone, reducing water use by up to 50 percent compared to spray heads. The result is a yard that looks intentional and clean, not abandoned. ## Your HOA Landscaping Timeline and Approval Process Most Las Vegas HOAs require front yard landscaping to be completed within 6 to 12 months of your closing date. You must submit a landscape plan to the HOA architectural review committee before you break ground. The plan needs to show plant placement, hardscape materials, and drip irrigation routing. Failure to submit or complete landscaping on time results in fines, and many HOAs will not grant extensions without documented financial hardship. The backyard is typically held to less strict timelines but must also comply with SNWA rules against spray irrigation once you install anything. ## Cost Estimates for Desert Landscaping in Las Vegas A basic front yard xeriscape installation for a standard new construction lot runs from $4,000 to $9,000, depending on lot size, plant selection, and how much bouldering or hardscape you include. If you want a more designed look with larger specimen plants, decorative boulders, and a detailed drip system, expect $10,000 to $20,000 or more. Getting multiple bids is essential. Las Vegas has dozens of desert landscaping contractors, and pricing varies significantly. Ask specifically for contractors who have experience with HOA approval submissions, since a plan that gets rejected adds weeks to your timeline. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose recommends that buyers ask about backyard landscaping plans before they even close.
