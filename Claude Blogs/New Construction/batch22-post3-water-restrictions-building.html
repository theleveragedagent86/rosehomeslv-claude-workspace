# Water Restrictions and Las Vegas New Construction: How Conservation Shapes Building Las Vegas has the strictest water conservation rules of any major American city, and those rules directly shape what new construction homes look like, how yards are designed, and what every new homeowner inherits when they take possession of a newly built property. Understanding the restrictions before you buy is not optional: it is part of knowing what you are purchasing. ## The Grass Ban and What Replaced It The Southern Nevada Water Authority prohibited grass installation on all new residential properties effective April 2022. This was not a suggestion or a design trend. It was a legal prohibition backed by state legislation, and it applies to every home built in the Las Vegas valley from that date forward. If you are purchasing any new construction home built since April 2022, there is no traditional lawn on the property and there will not be one. What builders deliver instead is a desert-appropriate front yard with decomposed granite, native plants, and approved drought-tolerant landscaping. Drip irrigation systems are required for any planted material, replacing the spray heads that characterized older Las Vegas neighborhoods. The SNWA actively enforces these rules and offers rebates of $3 per square foot for homeowners who remove existing grass on older properties, a separate program that reflects the same conservation mandate. ## How Water Rules Shape Building Design Conservation compliance is built into the construction process itself. Builders submit water-efficient landscape plans for permit approval before grading begins. Pool pre-plumb and pool construction remain permitted, as pools in the desert climate actually lose less water than an equivalent area of grass when properly covered. Interior plumbing in new homes must meet efficiency standards governing flow rates on fixtures, showerheads, and toilets. The Clark County 2024 building codes, effective January 11, 2026, incorporate the latest International Residential Code standards which include water efficiency provisions that apply uniformly to all new construction. Builders cannot opt out of these requirements, which means every new home in the valley is being built to a higher conservation standard than anything constructed before 2022. ## What Buyers Need to Know Before Closing New construction buyers sometimes arrive at their model home tour expecting to select grass as a landscaping option and are surprised to learn it is not available. The good news is that modern xeriscape landscaping in Las Vegas has evolved substantially. Builders now offer desert landscaping packages with real visual appeal, layered plant materials, colored rock, and lighting options that make the no-grass restriction feel like a design feature rather than a limitation. Buyers should also understand HOA landscaping rules, which run alongside state water law. Most new construction communities have CC and Rs specifying approved plant lists and design standards that already align with SNWA requirements, so compliance is built into the community itself. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose points out that buyers relocating from California or other states with lush landscaping traditions sometimes feel uncertain about desert yards until they see what well-executed xeriscape actually looks like in a finished new construction neighborhood.
