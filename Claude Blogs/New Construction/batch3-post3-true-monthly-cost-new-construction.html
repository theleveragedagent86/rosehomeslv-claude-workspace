# True Monthly Cost of Owning a New Construction Home in Las Vegas Your mortgage payment is only one piece of what you will pay each month to own a new construction home in Las Vegas. Adding up every line item before you buy gives you a far more accurate picture of whether a specific home fits your budget. ## Principal, Interest, and Mortgage Insurance On a $530,000 home with a 10 percent down payment at 6.0 percent interest, the principal and interest payment comes to approximately $2,868 per month. With a 20 percent down payment, that drops to roughly $2,549. If you put down less than 20 percent with a conventional loan, add private mortgage insurance of approximately $150 to $250 per month until you reach 20 percent equity. FHA borrowers pay a monthly mortgage insurance premium of 0.55 percent of the loan balance annually, which on a $480,000 loan adds about $220 per month. Builder rate buydown programs in 2026 can reduce this payment substantially in the first one to three years. DR Horton's 3.99 percent program and Taylor Morrison's tiered 2.99 to 4.99 percent buydown have helped buyers reduce their first-year payment by $300 to $500 per month compared to a market-rate loan. ## Property Tax, HOA, and Special Assessments Nevada assesses property at 35 percent of taxable value and caps annual increases at 3 percent for a primary residence. On a $530,000 home, effective property taxes run approximately $2,800 to $3,200 per year, or $233 to $267 per month. If your community carries a SID or LID assessment, add another $42 to $250 per month depending on district and remaining balance. HOA fees in Las Vegas new construction communities typically range from $50 to $200 per month for a standard single-family community. Some master-planned areas like Summerlin carry multiple HOA layers, with a master HOA plus a sub-association, which can push combined fees to $250 to $350 per month. ## Utilities and Maintenance in the Desert Nevada Energy bills run high in Las Vegas given that air conditioning operates 10 to 11 months per year. A new construction home in the 2,000 to 2,500 square foot range can expect summer electricity bills of $200 to $350 per month even with modern energy-efficient builds. Gas costs are modest, typically $30 to $60 per month. Water averages $60 to $100 per month for a desert-landscaped lot. Maintenance on a new construction home is lower in the first few years but is not zero. Setting aside 0.5 to 1 percent of the home's value annually for repairs and maintenance is a sound baseline, which on a $530,000 home equals $220 to $440 per month in savings. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose typically walks clients through a complete monthly payment stack before they fall in love with a floor plan.
