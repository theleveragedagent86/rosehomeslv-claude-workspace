The benefits are significant, but using a VA loan on new construction does require some additional planning. ## How VA Loans Work on New Construction The VA guarantees loans made by private lenders to eligible borrowers, and that guarantee allows lenders to offer 0% down with no private mortgage insurance requirement. With full entitlement, there is no loan limit on how much you can borrow, which means even a $700,000 or $800,000 home in Summerlin can qualify for zero down financing if your lender approves the loan amount. The funding fee for first-time VA borrowers without a disability rating is 2.15% of the loan amount, though it can be rolled into the loan rather than paid at closing. New construction adds a layer of complexity because the VA requires the home to be built to VA minimum property requirements, and the builder must be a VA-approved builder. The good news is that major Las Vegas builders including Lennar, KB Home, DR Horton, and Pulte are all familiar with VA construction requirements and typically maintain VA-approved status. You will want to confirm this early in the process, as some smaller builders or niche custom builders may not have pursued VA approval. ## Extended Rate Locks Are Critical One of the biggest challenges with VA loans on new construction is the rate lock. Standard rate locks run 30 to 60 days, but a new Las Vegas home can take 6 to 12 months to build. You will need an extended rate lock, which typically costs an upfront fee ranging from 0.25% to 0.75% of the loan amount, or you will need a lender offering a float-down option that lets you capture a lower rate if rates drop before closing. Given that mortgage rates in 2026 are hovering between 5.9% and 6.15%, locking early can protect you from any upward movement during the construction period. The VA appraisal process on new construction also differs from a standard purchase appraisal. The VA will typically perform the appraisal once the home is near completion, so you do not get a full value confirmation until late in the build.
