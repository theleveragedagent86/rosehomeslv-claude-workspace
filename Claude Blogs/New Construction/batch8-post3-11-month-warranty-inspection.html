# The 11-Month Warranty Inspection in Las Vegas: Your Last Chance The 11-month warranty inspection is one of the most valuable and most overlooked steps a new construction homeowner can take in Las Vegas. It is your last opportunity to submit a formal workmanship warranty claim before the first year of builder coverage expires, and most homeowners have no idea it exists. ## Why 11 Months and Not 12 Your builder's one-year workmanship warranty begins on your closing date. The reason you schedule the inspection at month 11 rather than waiting until the end of month 12 is simple: you need time. After the inspection report is complete, you need several weeks to review the findings, compile your warranty submission, send it to the builder in writing, and allow the builder time to respond before your coverage window closes. Waiting until month 12 puts you at serious risk of missing the deadline entirely. The 11-month inspection is sometimes called a "warranty inspection" to distinguish it from the pre-purchase inspections you had done before closing. The scope is similar to a standard home inspection, covering all visible systems and components, but the focus shifts to identifying defects that have revealed themselves through a full year of daily use and seasonal changes. ## What the Inspection Covers A thorough 11-month inspection in Las Vegas examines the same categories as a standard home inspection: roofing and attic ventilation, HVAC performance and ductwork connections, plumbing fixtures and water heater, electrical systems, windows and doors, interior finishes including drywall cracks and flooring separation, and the exterior including stucco, caulking, and any concrete flatwork like the driveway and walkways. Las Vegas homes go through significant thermal stress in the first year. Summer heat pushes attic temperatures to 140 degrees or more, and that expansion and contraction cycle can reveal issues with roofing materials, attic ventilation, and window seals that were not apparent at closing. The 11-month inspection catches problems that literally could not have been seen before because they had not yet developed. ## How to Prepare Before the Inspector Arrives Before your inspector visits, do a walkthrough of your own and take notes on anything you have noticed in the past year: doors that have started sticking, grout lines that have cracked, caulk that has pulled away from fixtures, any staining on ceilings or walls, and any unusual sounds from the HVAC system. Your list is valuable input for the inspector and ensures that items you have observed firsthand are included in the formal report. Keep a dedicated folder or digital file with photographs of any concerns you noticed throughout the year. Many experienced new construction owners in Las Vegas start a running photo log the day they move in, making the 11-month process much more efficient and thorough. ## What to Do with the Report Once you receive the inspection report, submit all covered items to your builder's warranty department in writing. Include the inspector's report, your own photos, and a clear list of each item you are requesting to have repaired. Send this via email or the builder's official warranty portal so you have a documented record with timestamps. Verbal requests carry no weight when it comes to warranty claims. Most major Las Vegas builders including Lennar, DR Horton, and KB Home have dedicated warranty service teams with online portals for submitting requests. Response times and quality vary, but having everything in writing protects your rights under Nevada law. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose has seen many buyers skip the 11-month inspection and then find themselves outside the warranty window when problems surface.
