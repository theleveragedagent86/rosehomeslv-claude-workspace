# How to File a Warranty Claim with Your Las Vegas Builder Filing a warranty claim with your Las Vegas builder is a straightforward process when you know the steps, but it requires documentation and persistence. The most important thing to understand is that nothing in the warranty process should be handled verbally: every communication needs to be in writing. ## Step One: Document the Defect Thoroughly Before submitting anything to your builder, document the problem completely. Take clear photographs from multiple angles and note the date you first observed the issue. If the problem is intermittent, like a door that only sticks in summer or an HVAC noise that only happens at startup, write down when it occurs and under what conditions. This documentation protects you if the builder later claims the defect was not observed within the warranty period or does not match your description. Check your warranty agreement documents from closing to confirm that the issue you are reporting falls within the current coverage period. Year one covers workmanship, year two covers mechanical systems, and years three through ten cover structural components. Submitting a mechanical claim in year three rather than year two, for example, would fall outside the mechanical coverage window. ## Step Two: Submit Through Official Channels Major Las Vegas builders including Lennar, DR Horton, KB Home, and Pulte all have dedicated warranty service departments with online portals. Use the official portal whenever possible because it automatically creates a timestamped record of your submission. If your builder does not have an online system, send your claim via email to the warranty department address listed in your closing documents. Your written submission should include your name and address, your closing date, the specific item or area of concern, your photographs, and a clear statement requesting repair under warranty. Avoid combining unrelated issues into one submission; separate claims submitted individually are easier to track and harder for the builder to dismiss. ## Step Three: Follow Up in Writing Nevada law under NRS Chapter 40 requires builders to respond to construction defect notices within 90 days. If your builder acknowledges your claim but then goes silent on scheduling a repair, follow up in writing and reference your original submission date. Keep copies of all correspondence in a single organized folder, whether physical or digital. If the builder's repair technician comes to your home, do not sign any paperwork stating the repair is complete unless you have personally verified the fix. Get the repair described in writing and, for significant issues, consider having your independent inspector verify the work before you sign off. ## Step Four: Escalate If Necessary If your builder fails to respond within the required timeframe or refuses to honor a legitimate warranty claim, you have several options in Nevada. You can file a complaint with the Nevada State Contractors Board, which licenses all residential builders and has authority to investigate and discipline contractors. You can also pursue your rights under NRS Chapter 40, which requires a formal notice of defect before legal action but provides a meaningful path to resolution including mediation. Structural warranty claims that exceed $40,000 may warrant consulting with a construction defect attorney in Nevada. Many attorneys in this space offer free initial consultations and can quickly tell you whether your situation warrants legal action. ## Local Insight As a Las Vegas real estate specialist, Ryan Rose has helped many clients navigate the builder warranty process, and the consistent pattern among successful claims is thorough documentation from day one.
