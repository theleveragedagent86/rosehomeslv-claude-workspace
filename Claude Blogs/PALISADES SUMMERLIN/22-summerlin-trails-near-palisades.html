The Summerlin Trail System spans more than 150 miles of paved and natural surface paths connecting villages, parks, and open spaces throughout the master planned community. Residents of the Palisades Summerlin enjoy direct access to this extensive network, which supports walking, jogging, and cycling just steps from their front doors. ## How Large Is the Summerlin Trail System? Summerlin's trail network currently includes over 150 miles of interconnected paths that weave through residential villages, commercial areas, and natural open spaces. Ten miles of trail run along the 215 Beltway, creating a regional corridor for commuters and recreational users alike. The system is still expanding, with planned connections that will eventually link Summerlin directly to Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area. Nearly one third of Summerlin's total 22,500 acres is designated open space, giving the trail system room to grow. ## Which Trails Are Closest to the Palisades? The Palisades sits in The Canyons Village of Summerlin North, positioning residents near several key trail segments. The 215 Beltway Trail runs along the southern edge of the Summerlin area, offering a flat, paved route ideal for long distance walks and bike rides. The Trails Park at 9550 Trails Center Drive is also nearby, providing a trailhead with parking, restrooms, and connections to surrounding path networks. From The Canyons Village, residents can pick up multiple trail segments without driving. ## What Activities Does the Trail System Support? The Summerlin trails accommodate a wide range of activities.
