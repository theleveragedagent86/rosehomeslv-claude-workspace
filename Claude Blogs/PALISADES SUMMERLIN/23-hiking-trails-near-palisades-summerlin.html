The Palisades Summerlin sits within minutes of some of the best hiking trails in the Las Vegas valley. From the limestone ridgelines of the Summerlin Peak Loop to the red sandstone canyons of Red Rock Canyon, residents of this guard gated community can reach trailheads for every skill level without a long drive. ## What Are the Best Hiking Trails Near the Palisades? Several standout trails are accessible within a short drive from the Palisades. The Summerlin Peak Loop is a moderate distance hike that starts from Buckskin Cliff Shadows Park and follows a limestone ridgeline with solid vertical gain and sweeping views of the valley. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, roughly 15 to 20 minutes west, offers more than 30 miles of trails ranging from flat interpretive walks to strenuous canyon scrambles. The Calico Basin area on Red Rock's eastern boundary provides additional options for hikers who prefer a quieter, less trafficked experience. ## Are There Trails for Beginners Near the Palisades? Absolutely. The Summerlin Trail System includes over 150 miles of paved and natural surface paths that work well for casual walkers and those building up to longer hikes. The 215 Beltway Trail offers 10 miles of flat, paved surface ideal for a low intensity outing. Inside Red Rock Canyon, several short loop trails near the visitor center provide easy terrain with interpretive signs about local geology and wildlife. These beginner friendly options make the area appealing to families and retirees alike. ## What Makes Hiking from the Palisades So Convenient? Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert who specializes in the Palisades, often emphasizes that the community's location in The Canyons Village of Summerlin North places residents at the western edge of the Las Vegas valley. That positioning means shorter drives to trailheads and less traffic compared to neighborhoods farther east. The Palisades is surrounded by designated open space, and future trail connections are planned to link Summerlin directly to Red Rock Canyon, which will make trailhead access even easier. ## Local Insight Hiking culture in western Summerlin is not seasonal. While spring and fall bring the most comfortable temperatures, early morning hikes in summer and mild winter days keep trails active year round. Many Palisades residents build daily walks and weekend hikes into their routines, taking advantage of the cooler temperatures that come with the slightly higher elevation near the Spring Mountains. The proximity of world class hiking to a luxury guard gated community is a lifestyle combination that continues to attract buyers to this part of Las Vegas.
