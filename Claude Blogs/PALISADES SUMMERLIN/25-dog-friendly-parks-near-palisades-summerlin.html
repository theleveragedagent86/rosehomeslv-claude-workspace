The Palisades Summerlin is surrounded by dog friendly trails, parks, and open spaces that make it one of the best Las Vegas neighborhoods for pet owners. With over 150 miles of walking paths in the Summerlin Trail System and Red Rock Canyon just 15 minutes away, dogs and their owners have no shortage of places to explore together. ## What Dog Friendly Trails Are Near the Palisades? The Summerlin Trail System offers more than 150 miles of paved and natural surface paths that welcome leashed dogs. These trails connect villages, parks, and open spaces throughout the community, giving Palisades residents a variety of routes right outside the gates. The 215 Beltway Trail provides 10 miles of flat, paved walking surface that works well for dogs of all sizes and energy levels. For more adventurous outings, Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area allows leashed dogs on many of its trails, offering desert terrain and canyon scenery roughly 15 to 20 minutes from the Palisades. ## Which Parks Near the Palisades Welcome Dogs? The Trails Park at 9550 Trails Center Drive is a popular destination for dog owners in the area. Its large meadow provides open space for leashed walks, and the park connects to surrounding trail segments for longer outings. The Palisades community park and green space at the neighborhood entrance also offers room for quick walks. Throughout Summerlin, community parks with walking paths and grassy areas accommodate leashed dogs, though leash requirements are enforced in all public spaces. ## Is the Palisades a Good Neighborhood for Dog Owners?
