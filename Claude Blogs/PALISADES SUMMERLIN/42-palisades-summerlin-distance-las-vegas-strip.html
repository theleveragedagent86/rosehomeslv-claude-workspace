One of the most common questions buyers ask about Summerlin is how far it sits from the Las Vegas Strip. The Palisades Summerlin offers the best of both worlds: a quiet, guard gated neighborhood tucked against the foothills with the energy of the Strip just a short drive away. ## How Far Is the Palisades Summerlin From the Las Vegas Strip? The Palisades is approximately 12 miles from the Las Vegas Strip, and the drive typically takes between 17 and 25 minutes depending on traffic and your specific destination along Las Vegas Boulevard. The southern end of the Strip near Mandalay Bay is slightly farther, while the central portion near The Venetian and Caesars Palace tends to be the quickest route via Summerlin Parkway to I-15. ## What Is the Best Route From the Palisades to the Strip? Most residents take Summerlin Parkway east to US 95, then connect to I-15 south toward the Strip. This route avoids surface street congestion and provides a smooth, highway speed commute for the majority of the trip. During off peak hours, the drive can be as short as 17 minutes. Weekend evenings and major event nights may push travel times closer to 25 minutes, but the route remains reliable year round. ## How Far Is the Palisades From Downtown Summerlin? Before heading to the Strip, it is worth noting that Downtown Summerlin is only about two minutes by car from the Palisades. This open air shopping and dining center features more than 125 stores and restaurants, a multiplex cinema, and regular community events. Many Palisades residents find that Downtown Summerlin satisfies most of their entertainment and dining needs without requiring a trip to the Strip at all. ## How Far Is the Palisades From the Airport? Harry Reid International Airport is about 16 miles from the Palisades, with a typical drive time of 24 to 25 minutes. The 215 Beltway south to I-15 provides the most direct path. This makes the Palisades a practical home base for frequent flyers and business travelers who need consistent airport access. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert who specializes in Summerlin luxury properties, emphasizes that the Palisades' proximity to the Strip is one of its most underrated advantages.
