# Pittman Wash Trail from Quail Ridge Estates The Pittman Wash Trail runs 8.5 miles out and back through Green Valley North, giving Quail Ridge Estates residents a flat, paved option for walking, running, and biking close to home. ## How Long Is the Pittman Wash Trail Near Quail Ridge Estates? The Pittman Wash Trail is 8.5 miles out and back. The grade stays at 5 percent or less, so the entire route is rated easy. The surface is paved concrete and asphalt and the trail is more than 8 feet wide, which means strollers, wheelchairs, and bikes share the path comfortably. The trail runs through Green Valley North and connects to the Arroyo Grande Sports Complex. Quail Ridge Estates residents can access the trail through several Green Valley North entry points within a short drive. ## What Amenities Does the Pittman Wash Trail Have? The trail is built for daily use. Benches, picnic tables, and water fountains appear along the route, so you can rest, refill, or take a break with kids. Restrooms are located at Pecos Legacy Park, one of the trail's anchor stops. Because the path is wide and paved, runners and cyclists tend to share the route well. Families bring scooters and strollers, and dog walkers use the trail in early-morning and evening hours. ## Is the Pittman Wash Trail Good for Biking? Yes. The flat grade, paved surface, and wide path make Pittman Wash one of the better paved bike options in Henderson. Riders can do short out-and-back segments or the full 8.5-mile loop. Connecting to the Arroyo Grande Sports Complex extends the ride, and benches make rest stops easy. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose, who has toured many Quail Ridge Estates homes, notes that buyers who exercise outdoors often pair the neighborhood with Pittman Wash for daily fitness.
