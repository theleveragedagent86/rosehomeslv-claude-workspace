# Quail Ridge Estates School Zone Guide Quail Ridge Estates families are zoned to three Clark County School District schools in Henderson: Nate Mack Elementary (PK-5), Thurman White Academy of the Performing Arts (6-8), and Green Valley High School (9-12). The three schools provide K-12 continuity within the same area. ## What is the Quail Ridge Estates school zone? The Quail Ridge Estates school zone is set by the Clark County School District (CCSD) and covers three feeder schools. Elementary students attend Nate Mack Elementary. Middle school students attend Thurman White Academy of the Performing Arts. High school students attend Green Valley High School. All three are public schools, all are within Henderson, and all are assigned automatically based on a Quail Ridge Estates address. ## How are the zoned schools rated? Each level of the zone earns above-average marks within CCSD. Nate Mack Elementary holds a 7 out of 10 GreatSchools rating, a 5-star Nevada Department of Education designation, and a US News rank of 93 in Nevada. Thurman White Academy is rated 8 out of 10 by GreatSchools and ranks number 39 in Nevada by US News. Green Valley High School ranks 33 in Nevada with a B+ grade on Niche and offers AP plus IB programs. Henderson schools generally test higher than the Las Vegas average within CCSD. ## Are there charter or private alternatives? Yes. Families inside the Quail Ridge Estates zone can also choose private and charter schools. Common alternatives in greater Henderson include the Adelson Educational Campus, Faith Lutheran Henderson Campus, and Pinecrest Academy charter schools. These options require their own admission steps and, in the case of private schools, tuition. They are not part of the zoned assignment. ## What about higher education? After high school, Quail Ridge Estates families have several college options nearby. Nevada State University is right next to Henderson. The College of Southern Nevada Henderson Campus is also close. UNLV sits about 15 minutes away via the 215 Beltway. That combination puts public four-year, community college, and research university campuses inside an easy drive of the neighborhood.
