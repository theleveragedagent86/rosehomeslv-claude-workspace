Queensridge residents with dogs have several off leash and on leash options within a short drive of the community. While the guard gated grounds of Queensridge allow leashed dog walking on the community's internal trails, nearby parks and facilities expand the options for exercise, socialization, and off leash play. This guide covers the best dog parks and dog runs accessible from the 800 acre community in western Las Vegas. ## Kellogg and Zaher Sports Complex Dog Runs The closest and most developed off leash option near Queensridge is the Charlie Kellogg and Joe Zaher Sports Complex. The complex includes three dedicated dog runs, fenced and separated by dog size to ensure safe play for both small and large breeds. The surfaces are maintained for comfort, and the runs are open during regular park hours. Many Queensridge dog owners consider this their primary off leash destination due to the short drive and quality of the facilities. ## Arbors Play Park Arbors Play Park in the Summerlin area sits near Queensridge and features walking trails, basketball courts, and a playground. While not a dedicated dog park, the trails at Arbors Play Park provide a leashed walking option with a different setting than the Queensridge community paths. The park is well maintained and offers open space for families and dog owners to enjoy together. ## Firefighters Memorial Park Firefighters Memorial Park is another nearby option in western Las Vegas, offering multi use recreation space and open areas. Leashed walking is permitted, and the park provides a quieter setting compared to the larger sports complex. For residents looking for a low key outing with their dog, this park offers a straightforward, accessible choice. ## Dog Walking on Queensridge Community Trails Within Queensridge itself, residents frequently walk their dogs along the community's internal trail network. The trails wind through landscaped grounds, mature trees, and open green spaces. Dogs must remain leashed on community property, and residents are expected to clean up after their pets. The private, quiet nature of these trails makes them a preferred option for daily walks. ## Red Rock Canyon Pet Policies Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, roughly 10 to 15 minutes west, permits leashed dogs on select trails. Regulations vary by trailhead, so checking current posted rules before heading out is essential. For Queensridge dog owners who enjoy hiking, Red Rock Canyon provides a dramatic natural setting for a leashed trail outing with their pets. ## Choosing the Right Option For off leash play, the three dog runs at the Kellogg and Zaher Sports Complex are the top choice near Queensridge. For leashed walking in a private setting, the community trails within Queensridge itself are the most convenient. For variety and scenery, the trails at Red Rock Canyon and the parks in the surrounding Summerlin area round out a strong selection.
