Queensridge's position in western Las Vegas places residents within minutes of some of the best hiking trails in the region. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, the primary hiking destination, is roughly 10 to 15 minutes west via Charleston Boulevard. Combined with the Summerlin Trail System and community walking paths, Queensridge offers direct access to hiking options ranging from easy flat walks to strenuous summit climbs. ## Calico Tanks Trail Calico Tanks Trail is rated the number one hike in Las Vegas and sits within Red Rock Canyon. The trail covers approximately 2.5 miles round trip with over 400 feet of elevation gain. The route passes through narrow sandstone corridors and scrambling sections before reaching a natural rock tank with panoramic views of the Las Vegas Valley. The difficulty level is moderate, making it accessible for most hikers with basic fitness. ## Calico Hills Trail Also within Red Rock Canyon, the Calico Hills Trail stretches 6.4 miles through some of the most visually striking terrain in southern Nevada. The red sandstone formations along this trail are among the most photographed features in the conservation area. The trail is relatively flat compared to others in the canyon, making it a strong option for longer distance hikers who prefer moderate terrain. ## Ice Box Canyon Trail Ice Box Canyon Trail runs 2.3 miles and leads to a seasonal waterfall at the canyon terminus. The trail earns its name from the cooler temperatures inside the narrow canyon walls, providing relief during warmer months. The terrain includes rocky sections and some boulder navigation, placing it in the moderate difficulty range. This trail is a favorite among Queensridge residents looking for a shorter hike with a rewarding destination. ## Turtlehead Peak For experienced hikers seeking a challenge, Turtlehead Peak covers roughly 5 miles round trip with significant elevation gain and exposed scrambling sections. The summit delivers 360 degree panoramic views of Red Rock Canyon, the Spring Mountains, and the Las Vegas Valley. This trail is rated advanced and is best attempted during cooler months by hikers comfortable with steep, rocky terrain. ## Summerlin Trail System Closer to home, the Summerlin Trail System offers over 150 miles of connected walking and biking paths. These trails link through the Peccole Ranch and greater Summerlin area, providing a flatter, paved alternative for daily exercise. Queensridge residents can access this network from the western Las Vegas area without driving to Red Rock Canyon. ## Queensridge Community Trails Within the guard gated community itself, Queensridge maintains internal walking trails through landscaped grounds, mature trees, and open spaces. While not hiking trails in the traditional sense, they serve as a daily exercise option and a starting point for residents building up to longer routes at Red Rock Canyon.
