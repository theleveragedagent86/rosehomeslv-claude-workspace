Fox Hill Park in Summerlin West is one of the most exciting adventure playgrounds in Las Vegas, located just a short drive from Red Rock Country Club in the Paseos Village. ## What Makes Fox Hill Park Different From Other Summerlin Parks? Fox Hill Park is built around an adventure and climbing theme that sets it apart from every other park in the valley. The centerpiece is a 32 foot climbing tower surrounded by 20 distinct play structures, including two zip lines, embankment slides, a massive climbing apparatus called the Orange Beast, custom swings, and a spinning machine. The entire 18 acre park is designed to challenge kids physically while keeping them engaged for hours. The park also features the Biggo Solo, Tornado Agility Course, Asteroid frame net, and Genesis XL mast net. These aren't standard playground fixtures; they were engineered by Dynamo Playgrounds specifically for Fox Hill Park. One of the best surprises is the view from the top of the slides, where you can see a panoramic sweep of the Las Vegas Strip and the entire valley below. ## Is Fox Hill Park Good for Families With Young Children? Fox Hill Park is best suited for children ages five and older who enjoy climbing and physical challenges. The structures are large and require a level of coordination that younger toddlers may not have yet. That said, paved pathways and smooth trails make the park accessible for strollers and wheelchairs, so families with mixed age groups can still enjoy the visit. Additional amenities include four covered picnic ramadas, an 18 hole disc golf course, and ample parking at the base of the park. The walk uphill to the main play area is short but noticeable, so wearing comfortable shoes is a good idea. ## A Local Perspective on Fox Hill Park Red Rock Country Club residents are fortunate to have Fox Hill Park roughly ten minutes away.
