Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area sits directly west of Red Rock Country Club, putting nearly 200,000 acres of protected desert landscape within a five to ten minute drive from the community gates. ## What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area? Red Rock Canyon spans 195,819 acres of federally protected land and offers 26 distinct hiking trails, a 13 mile scenic drive, rock climbing routes, mountain biking, camping, and horseback riding. The scenic drive is open year round, with hours shifting by season: 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM in March and October, 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM from April through September, and 6:00 AM to 5:00 PM from November through February. Popular trails include Calico Tanks (2.5 miles round trip with panoramic views), Pine Creek Canyon (3 miles round trip, family friendly with seasonal waterfalls), and Lost Creek Trail (0.7 miles, easy and shaded). For experienced hikers, Turtlehead Peak delivers a strenuous 5 mile round trip with sweeping mountain vistas. A visitor center operates daily from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM and provides maps, exhibits, and ranger information. ## Do You Need Reservations for the Red Rock Canyon Scenic Drive? Yes, timed entry reservations are required from October 1 through May 31 for entry between 8:00 AM and 5:00 PM. Reservations can be made through Recreation.gov or by calling (877) 444 6777. During the summer months from June 1 through September 30, reservations are not required, and passes can be purchased directly at the entrance station. This reservation system keeps the scenic drive manageable and reduces overcrowding at trailheads. Red Rock Country Club residents who use the area frequently often purchase an annual pass, which pays for itself after just a few visits. ## Living Next to a National Conservation Area Proximity to Red Rock Canyon is one of the most compelling reasons buyers choose Red Rock Country Club over other luxury communities in Las Vegas.
