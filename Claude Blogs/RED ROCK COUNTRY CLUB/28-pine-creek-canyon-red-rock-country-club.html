Pine Creek Canyon Trail is a family friendly three mile round trip hike inside Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, located just five to ten minutes west of Red Rock Country Club in Summerlin, Las Vegas. ## What Makes Pine Creek Canyon a Great Family Hike? Pine Creek Canyon offers a mix of easy walking and light scrambling that keeps the trail interesting for adults while remaining accessible for children. The three mile out and back route passes through a shaded canyon with seasonal creek crossings, mountain views, and the ruins of a historic homestead built in the early 1900s. After periods of rainfall, small waterfalls appear along the canyon walls, adding a surprising desert oasis element to the experience. The trail surface is a mix of packed dirt and rock, with a few sections requiring simple hand over rock navigation. These bouldering spots are optional and easy to bypass, making Pine Creek Canyon suitable for hikers of varying skill levels. Most families complete the full route in one to two hours at a comfortable pace. ## When Should You Hike Pine Creek Canyon? Spring and fall deliver the best conditions, with mild temperatures and the highest likelihood of water flowing through the creek. Late winter visits after recent rain offer the best chance of seeing the canyon's small waterfalls. Summer hiking is possible but requires an early start, as temperatures climb quickly once the sun rises above the canyon walls. The Pine Creek Canyon trailhead is accessible from the 13 mile scenic drive inside Red Rock Canyon. Timed entry reservations are required from October through May and can be booked through Recreation.gov. Weekend mornings fill up fast, so weekday visits or early arrivals give families a quieter experience on the trail. ## A Canyon Hike Your Family Can Do Every Weekend Red Rock Country Club families have Pine Creek Canyon practically in their backyard.
