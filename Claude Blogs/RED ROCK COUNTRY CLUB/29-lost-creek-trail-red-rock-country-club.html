Lost Creek Trail is an easy 0.7 mile round trip nature walk inside Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, located just five to ten minutes west of Red Rock Country Club in Summerlin, Las Vegas. ## What Is Lost Creek Trail Like? Lost Creek Trail is one of the shortest and most accessible paths in Red Rock Canyon, making it ideal for visitors of all ages and fitness levels. The 0.7 mile out and back route follows a shaded path through a canyon with lush vegetation, a running creek, and a wooden bridge crossing. Heavy tree cover and rock overhangs keep the trail cool even on warm days, which is unusual for a desert hike. The first portion of the trail is wheelchair accessible with a relatively flat, compacted surface. Beyond that section, the path narrows and includes some uneven ground as it follows the creek deeper into the canyon. The entire walk typically takes 20 to 30 minutes at a leisurely pace, making it easy to pair with a visit to nearby Willow Springs picnic area. ## Who Is Lost Creek Trail Best For? This trail suits families with young children, older adults, and anyone looking for a quick outdoor experience without significant physical effort. The shaded environment and running water make it especially appealing during warmer months when other Red Rock Canyon trails become uncomfortably hot. Dogs are not permitted on the trail, so pet owners should plan accordingly. Lost Creek Trail is accessed from the scenic drive inside Red Rock Canyon. Timed entry reservations are required from October through May through Recreation.gov. Because the trail is short and the parking area is small, it rarely feels crowded even on busy weekends. Pairing it with Calico Tanks or Pine Creek Canyon on the same visit makes for a full morning of hiking variety. ## The Easiest Outdoor Escape Near Red Rock Country Club Not every outing needs to be a summit push or a five mile trek.
