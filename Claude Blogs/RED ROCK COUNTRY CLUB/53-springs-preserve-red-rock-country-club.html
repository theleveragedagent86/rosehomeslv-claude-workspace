Springs Preserve is a 180 acre cultural and nature attraction located at 333 S Valley View Blvd, approximately 15 minutes from Red Rock Country Club in Las Vegas. This unique destination combines museums, botanical gardens, trails, and interactive exhibits that celebrate the natural history of the Mojave Desert and the story of Las Vegas itself. ## What Can You See and Do at Springs Preserve? Springs Preserve houses two major museums on its grounds. The Origen Museum features more than 75 permanent exhibits, an indoor theater, and rotating traveling exhibit space. The Nevada State Museum showcases a 20,000 year old Columbian Mammoth skeleton, a recreated stalactite cave, Nevada mining history, and original Las Vegas showgirl costumes. Beyond the museums, the botanical gardens display thousands of Mojave Desert and desert adapted plants across themed sections including cactus gardens and herb gardens. The seasonal Butterfly Habitat lets visitors walk among free flying butterflies in a lush, enclosed environment. The Flash Flood Exhibit recreates the power of a desert storm using 5,000 gallons of recycled water, giving visitors a visceral sense of the forces that shaped this landscape. More than three miles of trails wind through the property, passing desert wetlands, cottonwood groves, and interpretive sites. ## How Far Is Springs Preserve from Red Rock Country Club? The drive from Red Rock Country Club to Springs Preserve takes about 15 minutes heading east on West Charleston Boulevard. The route is straightforward, following Charleston directly to Valley View Boulevard. General admission is $18.95 for adults and $10.95 for children ages 3 to 17, with children 2 and under admitted free.
