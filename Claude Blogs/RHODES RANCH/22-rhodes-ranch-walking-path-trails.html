The paved path is one of the most used amenities in this 1,330 acre master planned community in southwest Las Vegas. ## What Is the Rhodes Ranch Walking Path Like? The main walking path is a two mile paved loop that winds past landscaped ponds where ducks and geese gather, alongside fairways of the Rhodes Ranch Golf Club, and through several of the community's 18 subdivisions. The path is wide enough for joggers, walkers, and cyclists to share comfortably, and it stays well maintained year round by the HOA. Benches and shaded rest areas are positioned at intervals along the route, making it accessible for residents of all fitness levels. Because the path stays entirely within the guard gated community, residents can walk or jog at dawn, dusk, or after dark with an added layer of security. The flat, paved surface is stroller friendly and wheelchair accessible, which makes it popular with families, seniors, and pet owners alike. Many residents use the loop as a daily fitness routine, logging laps before or after work. ## What Other Trails Are Near Rhodes Ranch? For residents who want more distance or elevation, several parks and trail systems sit within a short drive. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is roughly 20 to 25 minutes west and provides more than 30 miles of hiking and biking trails ranging from easy to strenuous.
