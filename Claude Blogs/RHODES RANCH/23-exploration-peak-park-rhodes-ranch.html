Exploration Peak Park is an 80 acre regional park located at 9275 South Buffalo Drive, just five minutes south of Rhodes Ranch in the Mountain's Edge community. The park centers on a 2,846 foot natural peak with summit trails, an Old West themed playground, a water play area, and an outdoor amphitheater, making it one of the most popular outdoor destinations for Rhodes Ranch families. ## What Trails and Views Does Exploration Peak Park Offer? The signature Exploration Peak Trail is a one mile out and back hike that climbs to the 2,846 foot summit, taking roughly 30 minutes to complete at a moderate pace. The summit rewards hikers with panoramic views of the entire Las Vegas Valley, the Spring Mountains to the west, and the Mountain's Edge community below. The trail is unpaved but well marked, and it is suitable for hikers, trail runners, and mountain bikers of moderate fitness. Additional walking paths loop through the lower sections of the park, offering flat terrain for strollers, wheelchairs, and casual walkers. The park is open daily from 5:00 AM to 9:30 PM, giving early risers and evening walkers plenty of time to enjoy the trails. Of the 80 total acres, 24 are fully developed with amenities while the remaining acreage is preserved as open space and natural trail corridors. ## What Playground and Amenities Are at Exploration Peak Park? The playground is one of the largest themed play areas in the Las Vegas Valley. It features an Old West town with building replicas and a covered wagon play structure, an Indian village section with a web teepee climber, and an archaeology dig site where kids can pretend to excavate artifacts. Additional play equipment includes multiple slides, enclosed elevated walkways, swings, seesaws, and a sand play area. Beyond the playground, the park offers a water play area with spray jets and a tipping water bucket, a horseshoe pit, and an outdoor amphitheater that hosts community events and performances.
