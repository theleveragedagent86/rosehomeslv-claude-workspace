Las Vegas is home to some of the best golf course communities in the country, with options ranging from ultra exclusive private clubs to accessible guard gated neighborhoods where championship golf is part of everyday life. Rhodes Ranch, Southern Highlands, Red Rock Country Club, and several other communities each offer a distinct combination of course quality, home prices, and lifestyle amenities. ## What Are the Top Golf Course Communities in Las Vegas? Southern Highlands anchors the luxury end of the market with an 18 hole private course designed by Robert Trent Jones Jr. and home prices that regularly exceed $1 million. Red Rock Country Club in Summerlin features two 18 hole Arnold Palmer designed courses set against the Red Rock Canyon backdrop, with guard gated access and a large clubhouse with dining and fitness facilities. Both communities cater to buyers seeking an exclusive, high end golf lifestyle. TPC Summerlin, home to the PGA Tour's Shriners Children's Open, sits adjacent to The Ridges and Tournament Hills, where custom estates overlook one of the most recognizable courses in Nevada. DragonRidge Country Club in Henderson's MacDonald Highlands offers dramatic elevation changes and Strip views from a private Jack Nicklaus inspired layout. Anthem Country Club features a Keith Foster designed course in a family friendly Henderson setting. ## How Does Rhodes Ranch Compare on Value? Rhodes Ranch stands out as the most affordable guard gated golf community in the Las Vegas valley. With a median home price around $466,536, homes here cost 30 to 50 percent less than comparable properties in Southern Highlands or Red Rock Country Club. The Ted Robinson Sr. designed par 71 course carries a 4 star Golf Digest rating and offers 6,909 yards of championship golf with water features on seven holes and a par 3 collection the architect called the finest he ever designed. Unlike private club communities that require mandatory memberships costing thousands annually, Rhodes Ranch operates on a semi private, pay per play model. Green fees range from $25 to $85, and residents receive preferred rates. HOA dues of $155 to $225 per month cover access to the R Club recreation center, Fun Zone Water Park, tennis courts, and a two mile walking path, but golf is separate. ## Which Golf Community Is Best for Families and Retirees? For families, Rhodes Ranch offers a rare combination of a championship course, guard gated security, top rated schools in the area, and resort style amenities at a price point that leaves room in the budget. Siena Golf Club is another option for older buyers, operating as a guard gated 55 plus community with its own course. Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, works with buyers across all of these communities and notes that Rhodes Ranch consistently attracts golfers who want the full lifestyle package without the financial commitment of a private club. ## Choosing the Right Golf Community Canyon Gate Country Club, also designed by Ted Robinson, and Bear's Best, a public Jack Nicklaus design, round out the options for buyers who want to live near quality golf in Las Vegas. The right choice depends on your budget, whether you prefer private or semi private access, and which part of the valley fits your commute and lifestyle needs.
