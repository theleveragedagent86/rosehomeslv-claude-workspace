Desert Oasis High School is one of the primary public high schools serving Rhodes Ranch families in southwest Las Vegas. Located at 6600 West Erie Avenue, this grades 9 through 12 campus opened in 2008 and carries an 89% graduation rate, which exceeds the Nevada state average of 82%. ## What Are the Ratings for Desert Oasis High School? Desert Oasis High School holds a B minus overall grade on Niche and a 5 out of 10 on GreatSchools. SchoolDigger ranks it 48th out of 103 Nevada high schools with a 3 star rating. The school enrolls approximately 3,183 students, making it one of the larger high school campuses in the Clark County School District. Reading proficiency at Desert Oasis sits at 43%, which is nearly in line with the 44% Nevada state average. Math proficiency comes in at 20%, which falls below the 30% state benchmark. However, the school's 89% graduation rate tells an important story about student retention and completion that test score snapshots alone do not capture. That graduation rate has remained consistently strong, ranging between 81% and 90% over recent years. ## What Programs Does Desert Oasis High School Offer? Desert Oasis provides Advanced Placement courses for students seeking college level academics. The school also features Career and Technical Education pathways, including a Barber and Beauty CTE program that gives students hands on vocational training before graduation. Athletics are a significant part of campus life, with competitive teams in football, basketball, soccer, track and field, and other sports. The school opened in August 2008 as part of Clark County's expansion to serve the rapidly growing southwest Las Vegas corridor.
