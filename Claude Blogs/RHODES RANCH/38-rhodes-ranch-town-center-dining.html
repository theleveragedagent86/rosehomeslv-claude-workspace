Rhodes Ranch Town Center is a 130,000 square foot shopping and dining plaza located at the intersection of Durango Drive and Warm Springs Road, directly adjacent to the Rhodes Ranch community. The center puts everyday restaurants and quick service favorites within a short drive or even walking distance for many residents of this southwest Las Vegas neighborhood. ## What Restaurants Are in Rhodes Ranch Town Center? Buffalo Wild Wings is the center's largest restaurant, offering wings, burgers, and a full bar in a sports focused setting with wall to wall screens for game days. Tropical Smoothie Cafe serves smoothies, wraps, flatbreads, and bowls that lean toward the healthier end of the fast casual spectrum. Chop Stop rounds out the health conscious options with customizable salads, wraps, and grain bowls made to order. For pizza, Little Caesars provides a budget friendly grab and go option, while Subway handles the sandwich category with its standard build your own format. Coldstone Creamery is the go to dessert stop, mixing custom ice cream creations on a frozen granite stone. Together, these tenants cover the most common everyday cravings without requiring a longer trip. ## Is Rhodes Ranch Town Center Walkable from the Community? The center sits just outside the Rhodes Ranch gates at Durango and Warm Springs, which places it within roughly a mile of many homes in the community's western sections. Residents in neighborhoods closest to the Durango side can walk or bike to the center in under 15 minutes, though most drive the two to three minute trip. The plaza is anchored by a Vons grocery store, so many residents combine a grocery run with a quick meal. The convenience factor is a genuine lifestyle benefit. Having a full service grocery store, multiple restaurants, and everyday retail within a mile of your front door means fewer long drives for routine errands. Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert who specializes in the Rhodes Ranch area, notes that this kind of neighborhood level walkability is uncommon in master planned communities across the Las Vegas valley. ## How Does Town Center Compare to Other Nearby Dining? Rhodes Ranch Town Center fills a specific role as the neighborhood's casual, everyday dining hub. It is not competing with the upscale chef driven restaurants at UnCommons or the specialty options along the Durango corridor. Instead, it handles the weeknight wing run, the Saturday lunch smoothie, and the post practice pizza stop that families rely on week after week. For residents who want both convenience and culinary variety, the combination of Town Center for daily needs and UnCommons for date nights and special occasions covers the full spectrum. The Ranch House Grille inside the gates at the golf club adds a third layer with its American bar and grill menu and daily happy hour.
