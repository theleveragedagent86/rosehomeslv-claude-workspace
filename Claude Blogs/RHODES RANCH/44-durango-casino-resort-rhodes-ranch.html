Durango Casino and Resort is a $780 million Station Casinos property located at Durango Drive and the I-215 beltway, directly adjacent to the Rhodes Ranch community in southwest Las Vegas. The resort opened on December 5, 2023, and has quickly become the go to entertainment and dining destination for residents who want a high end experience without driving to the Strip. ## What Does Durango Casino and Resort Offer? The property includes 209 guest rooms, an 83,000 square foot casino floor with 2,200 slot machines and more than 60 table games, a full sportsbook, and a seasonal outdoor pool with cabanas. The dining lineup rivals many Strip resorts. Eat Your Heart Out is a 25,000 square foot elevated food hall featuring 11 stalls including Irv's Burgers, Prince Street Pizza, Shang Artisan Noodle, and Marc Vetri's Fiorella. Signature restaurants include Mijo Modern Mexican with its speakeasy style Wax Rabbit lounge, Summer House by Lettuce Entertain You Restaurants, and Nicco's Prime Cuts and Fresh Fish. A major expansion announced in late 2025 will add a $385 million, 275,000 square foot wing that includes a 36 lane bowling alley, luxury movie theater, 400 new slot machines, and multiple entertainment venues. A new Meril restaurant from chef Emeril Lagasse is also in development. ## How Close Is Durango Casino to Rhodes Ranch? The resort sits less than one mile from the Rhodes Ranch guard gates. Most residents reach the property in under three minutes by car, and some neighborhoods within the community are close enough that walking or biking is practical. That proximity means Rhodes Ranch homeowners can enjoy a casual dinner at Eat Your Heart Out or catch a game at the sportsbook on a weeknight without any planning or commute.
