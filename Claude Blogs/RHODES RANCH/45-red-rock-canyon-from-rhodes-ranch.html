Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is roughly 20 to 25 minutes west of Rhodes Ranch, making this southwest Las Vegas community one of the closest residential neighborhoods to one of Nevada's premier outdoor destinations. The 195,819 acre conservation area features a 13 mile scenic drive, more than 30 miles of hiking trails, world class rock climbing, and dramatic sandstone formations that draw over three million visitors each year. ## How Do You Get to Red Rock Canyon From Rhodes Ranch? The most direct route heads west on Warm Springs Road or Charleston Boulevard (State Route 159) toward the visitor center at 3205 State Highway 159. From the Rhodes Ranch guard gates, the drive covers roughly 17 miles and takes 20 to 25 minutes in normal traffic. GPS navigation to "Red Rock Canyon Visitor Center" will pull up the correct destination. Timed entry reservations are required for the scenic drive from October through May for entry between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m. Reservations can be booked at Recreation.gov or by calling 877 444 6777. During summer months, June through September, no reservation is needed. The visitor center is open daily from 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. and provides maps, trail information, and exhibits on the area's geology and wildlife. ## What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon? The 13 mile one way scenic loop is the main attraction, offering pullouts with views of the Keystone Thrust, Calico Hills, and towering red sandstone walls. Popular hiking trails include Calico Tanks (2.5 miles round trip), Ice Box Canyon (2.6 miles round trip), and Turtlehead Peak (5 miles round trip with 2,000 feet of elevation gain). The area is also a globally recognized rock climbing destination with thousands of bolted and traditional routes.
