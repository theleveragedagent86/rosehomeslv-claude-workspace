Rhodes Ranch and Mountains Edge are neighboring communities in southwest Las Vegas that attract similar buyers, but Rhodes Ranch is guard gated with a golf course and water park while Mountains Edge offers newer construction and a massive regional park without gated entry. The right choice depends on whether you prioritize security and resort style amenities or newer homes and lower HOA costs. ## How Do Home Prices and HOA Fees Compare? Rhodes Ranch has a median home price near $466,000 with HOA fees between $155 and $225 per month. The community was built starting in 1997 and includes approximately 3,795 homes across 1,330 acres, with styles ranging from modest single story homes to custom estates. HOA fees at Rhodes Ranch cover guard gate staffing, golf course grounds, the R Club Recreation Center, the Fun Zone Water Park, and community patrol. Mountains Edge began construction in 2004, making it one of the newer master planned communities in the southwest valley. Home prices are similar to or slightly below Rhodes Ranch, and HOA fees run lower because there is no guard gate or private golf course to maintain. Buyers looking for newer builds with modern energy efficiency and open floor plans will find more options in Mountains Edge, while Rhodes Ranch offers a wider range of home sizes and price points within a gated perimeter. ## What Are the Biggest Lifestyle Differences? Rhodes Ranch is a guard gated community with 24/7 staffed entrances, an 18 hole par 71 golf course designed by Ted Robinson Sr., the Fun Zone Water Park for residents, and the 35,000 square foot R Club Recreation Center. Daily life centers around these private amenities, and the gated environment keeps through traffic out of the neighborhood. Walking paths loop past ponds and golf course scenery, creating a resort like setting for morning exercise. Mountains Edge is an open community without gates, but it anchors around the 220 acre Mountains Edge Regional Park, one of the largest parks in Clark County. Exploration Peak Park, with its hilltop summit trail and panoramic views, is another standout feature. Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, points out that families who prioritize large outdoor public spaces and newer construction often lean toward Mountains Edge, while buyers who want gated security and private club amenities consistently choose Rhodes Ranch. ## Which Community Fits Your Priorities? Both communities sit in the desirable 89148 and 89178 zip codes with easy access to Durango Drive, Blue Diamond Road, and the 215 Beltway. School zones overlap in several areas, and shopping centers like The Uncommons and Rhodes Ranch Town Center serve both neighborhoods. The decision often comes down to gated versus open living and private amenities versus public parkland. If you value guard gated security, a private golf course, and a built in water park, Rhodes Ranch is the stronger match.
