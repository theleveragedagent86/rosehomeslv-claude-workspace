Sunset Ridge is the only subdivision in Ridgebrook built by two different homebuilders: Pulte Homes and KB Homes. With 114 homes, nine floor plans, and sizes from 1,423 to 2,325 square feet, Sunset Ridge offers the most design variety of any pocket within this Summerlin community. ## What Floor Plans Does Sunset Ridge Offer? The nine floor plans in Sunset Ridge represent the combined catalogs of Pulte Homes and KB Homes, giving buyers more layout options than any other Ridgebrook subdivision. The smallest plan starts at just 1,423 square feet, making it the most compact home available anywhere in Ridgebrook. The largest reaches 2,325 square feet, providing ample space for families who need multiple bedrooms and flexible living areas. All homes were constructed between 2001 and 2003. Because two builders worked side by side, Sunset Ridge has a distinctive streetscape. Pulte Homes and KB Homes each brought their own design sensibilities to the subdivision, creating a varied mix of facades and rooflines along each block. Stucco exteriors in desert tones and lush green landscaped yards tie the different designs together into a cohesive neighborhood feel. ## How Does Sunset Ridge Compare to Ridgebrook's Other Subdivisions? Sunset Ridge occupies a unique middle ground within Ridgebrook. Its smallest homes are more compact than anything in Echo Ridge, Granite Peaks, or Southstar, while its larger plans compete well with the mid range options in those subdivisions. This range makes Sunset Ridge attractive to a broad spectrum of buyers, from first time purchasers to growing families. Prices follow the community's overall range of the mid $300,000s to the upper $400,000s, with smaller floor plans generally priced at the lower end. Ryan Rose considers Sunset Ridge one of the most versatile pockets in Ridgebrook for buyers who have not yet narrowed down their ideal square footage. Touring nine different floor plans within a single subdivision allows buyers to compare layouts efficiently without driving across town.
