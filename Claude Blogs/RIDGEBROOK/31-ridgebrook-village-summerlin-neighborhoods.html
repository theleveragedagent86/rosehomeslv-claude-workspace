Ridgebrook is a 100 acre village within Summerlin South that brings together four distinct neighborhoods around a shared 6.3 acre park. This compact community in the 89135 zip code holds 486 single family homes built by three different builders, giving buyers a range of styles and price points in one of Summerlin's most walkable settings. ## What Are the Four Neighborhoods in Ridgebrook Village? Ridgebrook contains four subdivisions, each with its own builder and architectural character. Echo Ridge was built by Pulte Homes and includes 121 single story homes ranging from 1,663 to 2,633 square feet. Granite Peaks, built by KB Homes, offers 136 homes across six floor plans spanning 1,717 to 2,550 square feet. Southstar by RS Development adds 115 homes with four floor plans between 1,553 and 2,050 square feet. Sunset Ridge rounds out the village with 114 homes built by both Pulte Homes and KB Homes. Floor plans in Sunset Ridge range from 1,423 to 2,325 square feet across nine different layouts. All four neighborhoods feature stucco exteriors, desert landscaping, and one or two story designs that reflect Summerlin's southwestern aesthetic. ## How Is Ridgebrook Village Organized Around Its Park? Ridgebrook Park sits at the center of the community, surrounded by all four subdivisions. This 6.3 acre green space includes a lighted tennis court, basketball court, children's playground, interactive water play area, picnic pavilion, and open fields. The layout means most residents live within a short walk of the park, which creates a true neighborhood gathering point. Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas Realtor who specializes in Summerlin communities, notes that this park centered design is one of the features that sets Ridgebrook apart from larger Summerlin villages. Judy and John L. Goolsby Elementary School also sits within the community, making school commutes simple for families with young children. ## What Makes Ridgebrook Village a Standout in Summerlin? Ridgebrook's smaller scale gives it a quieter, more connected feel compared to sprawling Summerlin villages with thousands of homes. The community is bordered by Desert Inn Road to the north, Hualapai Way to the east, and West Flamingo Road to the south and west. Neighbors include the luxury guard gated community of The Ridges and Red Rock Country Club directly to the north.
