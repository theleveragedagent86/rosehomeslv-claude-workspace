# Is Reverence Summerlin a Good Place to Live? Reverence is one of the most desirable neighborhoods in Summerlin for buyers who want guard gated security, newer construction, and direct access to outdoor recreation. The community consistently ranks among the top choices for families, professionals, and retirees looking for a complete lifestyle package in the Las Vegas valley. ## Security and Privacy Reverence is a 24 hour guard gated community, meaning every vehicle entering the neighborhood passes through a staffed gate. This provides a level of security and privacy that open neighborhoods cannot match. Summerlin as a whole has crime rates well below the Las Vegas city average, and the guard gate adds another layer on top of that. ## Location and Convenience The community sits in Summerlin West along the base of the Spring Mountains, giving residents mountain views and cooler temperatures at 3,200 feet elevation. Downtown Summerlin is a short drive south, providing access to over 125 shops, restaurants, and entertainment options including a movie theater and seasonal events. The 215 Beltway runs along the eastern edge of the community, connecting residents to the rest of the valley. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is just minutes west, making it one of the closest residential communities to world class hiking and outdoor recreation. ## Community Feel With only 655 homes on 300 acres, Reverence has a smaller, more connected feel than many Summerlin villages. The clubhouse programming includes regular social events, and the shared amenities (pool, fitness, pickleball, parks) create natural gathering points for neighbors. The HOA runs approximately $200 per month plus a $50 Summerlin master association fee, covering landscaping, gate staffing, and amenity maintenance. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose has helped multiple buyers relocate to Reverence and consistently hears the same feedback: the combination of security, mountain proximity, and modern construction is hard to find in one place. Buyers relocating from California and other states are especially drawn to Reverence because it feels like a resort community without the resort pricing found in places like The Ridges.
