# New Construction in Reverence Summerlin (2026) Monument at Reverence is the newest neighborhood within the Reverence village, offering new construction townhomes by Pulte Homes. It is the first Reverence neighborhood designed around a low maintenance, lock and leave lifestyle, and it represents the most affordable entry point into this guard gated Summerlin community. ## Monument Floor Plans Monument features three distinct floor plans, all two story with contemporary architectural styling and open concept living: The Branton offers 1,654 square feet with a practical layout designed for everyday living. The Cornwall comes in at 1,736 square feet with additional flex space. The Danbury is the largest at 1,869 square feet, providing the most room for families or buyers who want a home office. All three plans include modern finishes, energy efficient construction, and Pulte's Life Tested design approach, which focuses on how people actually use their spaces rather than maximizing raw square footage. ## Pricing and Value New construction at Monument starts in the high $400,000s, making it significantly more accessible than the single family homes in The Heights, where resale prices typically range from $650,000 to over $1.1 million. Monument buyers still get access to the Reverence guard gate and community trails, though the private Heights Clubhouse is exclusive to Heights residents. For buyers comparing new construction across Summerlin, Monument offers a rare opportunity to buy new within a guard gated community at a price point that is competitive with non gated neighborhoods elsewhere in the master plan. ## Why New Construction in Reverence Pulte Homes is the sole builder in Reverence, which means every home in the community shares a consistent design standard and build quality. New construction buyers benefit from current building codes, modern insulation and HVAC systems, and Pulte's structural warranty. The townhome format at Monument also means reduced exterior maintenance compared to a detached single family home. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose has walked the Monument models and notes that Pulte has done a good job maximizing usable space in each plan.
