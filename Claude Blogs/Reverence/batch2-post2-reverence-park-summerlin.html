# Reverence Park in Summerlin Reverence Park is an eight acre public park located at 2901 Reverence Parkway within the Reverence village in Summerlin West. It is one of the larger parks in the Summerlin system and serves as the primary outdoor recreation hub for all Reverence residents. ## Courts and Sports Facilities The park features two lighted tennis courts, a lighted full sized basketball court, and a lighted sand volleyball court. The lighting extends usability well into the evening, with the park open daily until 10:30 PM and tennis courts available from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. This makes it practical for residents who work during the day and want to play after dark, which is especially valuable during Las Vegas summers when cooler evening hours are preferred. ## Walking Track and Playground A dedicated walking track loops through the park, giving residents a flat, maintained surface for walking or jogging without leaving the community. The track connects to the broader Summerlin trail network, which spans over 200 miles across the master plan. The playground area is designed for younger children and includes shaded structures. Covered picnic areas with seating are spread throughout the park, and community restrooms are available on site. ## Amphitheater Reverence Park includes an amphitheater space that hosts community events and gatherings throughout the year. The setting takes advantage of the mountain backdrop to the west, making it a popular spot for residents during cooler months. ## Park Rules Dogs are welcome but must be on a leash at all times. No alcoholic beverages or glass containers are permitted in the park. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose highlights Reverence Park as one of the features that sets this community apart from other guard gated neighborhoods in Summerlin. Many gated communities have pools and clubhouses, but an eight acre park with lighted courts and a walking track within the community itself is uncommon. Families with active kids and adults who want outdoor recreation without driving will get daily use out of this park.
