# The Heights at Reverence Floor Plans and Home Styles The Heights at Reverence is the luxury guard gated section within the Reverence village in Summerlin, featuring approximately 480 homes across seven neighborhoods. Developed exclusively by Pulte Homes, The Heights offers four collections of single family homes ranging from roughly 2,156 to 4,815 square feet. ## Collections Overview Pulte organized The Heights into four distinct collections, each targeting a different buyer profile. The smaller collections start around 2,156 square feet and work well for couples, downsizers, or buyers who want a single story layout with manageable square footage. The larger collections reach up to 4,815 square feet and include two story plans with four to five bedrooms, bonus rooms, and multi car garages. All collections share the same guard gated access and exclusive use of the 16,000 square foot Reverence Clubhouse, Skye Fitness center, resort pool, and pickleball courts. ## Architectural Styles Homes in The Heights draw from five architectural influences: Craftsman, Prairie, Hacienda, Farmhouse, and Desert Contemporary. This variety gives the streetscape a diverse look rather than the uniform appearance found in some builder communities. Exteriors feature a mix of stucco, stone, and wood accents depending on the style, with color palettes designed to complement the desert landscape. ## Design Philosophy Pulte uses what they call a Life Tested design approach, which prioritizes how residents actually use space over raw square footage. This shows up in details like oversized pantries, dedicated drop zones near the garage entry, and flexible spaces that can serve as offices, guest rooms, or hobby areas depending on the buyer's needs. Floor plans emphasize open concept living with kitchen, dining, and family areas flowing together. Many plans position the primary suite on the ground floor, which appeals to buyers who want single level living for daily use with additional bedrooms upstairs for guests or family. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose has toured every collection in The Heights and recommends that buyers spend time comparing the lot positions, not just the floor plans. Homes on the western edge of The Heights tend to have the best mountain views, while interior lots offer more privacy and less road noise.
