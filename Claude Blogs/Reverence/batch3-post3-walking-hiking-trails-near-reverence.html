# Walking and Hiking Trails Near Reverence Summerlin Reverence in Summerlin sits at the intersection of two trail systems: the community's internal walking paths and the broader Summerlin trail network, which spans over 200 miles and continues to expand. Combined with the proximity to Red Rock Canyon, this gives residents one of the best trail access points in the Las Vegas valley. ## Trails Within Reverence Inside the community, paved walking paths connect neighborhoods to the eight acre Reverence Park and the Reverence Clubhouse. These internal trails are flat, well maintained, and provide a safe loop for daily walks, jogs, or dog walking without leaving the guard gate. The walking track at Reverence Park offers a dedicated loop separate from the neighborhood sidewalks. ## Summerlin Trail System The Summerlin trail system is one of the largest in any master planned community in the country. Over 200 miles of interconnected trails weave through the community, connecting villages, parks, and commercial areas. From Reverence, residents can access the trail network heading south along the 215 Beltway corridor. A 10 mile completed section runs along the 215 Beltway with plans for continued expansion. The trail surfaces include both paved paths suitable for walking and cycling and natural surface trails for a more rugged experience. ## Red Rock Canyon Legacy Trails The Clark County Red Rock Canyon Legacy Trails project will eventually connect the Summerlin trail system directly to Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area. A planned 5.5 mile section beginning near Sky Vista Drive in Summerlin will link to the Red Rock Visitors Center. Once complete, Reverence residents will be able to reach Red Rock Canyon trails on foot or by bike without driving. Currently, Red Rock Canyon is approximately a 5 minute drive from Reverence and offers over 30 miles of hiking trails within the conservation area. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose recommends the trail access as one of Reverence's most underrated features. Many buyers focus on the clubhouse and guard gate, but the ability to step outside and walk for miles on maintained trails, with eventual connectivity to Red Rock Canyon, adds a daily quality of life benefit that is hard to replicate in other parts of the valley.
