# Is Reverence Summerlin Safe? Crime Rates and Security Reverence is one of the safest residential communities in the Las Vegas valley. The combination of a 24 hour staffed guard gate, Summerlin's overall low crime rates, and the community's design all contribute to a security profile that is well above the city average. ## Guard Gate Security Every vehicle entering Reverence passes through a staffed guard gate, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Visitors must be approved by residents before entering, and the gate staff maintains a log of all traffic. This single point of access eliminates drive through traffic and significantly reduces opportunistic crime. Research in criminology has consistently found that gated communities experience fewer residential burglaries, less vandalism, and lower rates of opportunistic property crime compared to open neighborhoods. The controlled access point is the primary factor. ## Summerlin Crime Rates Summerlin as a whole reports crime rates well below the Las Vegas city average. The master planned community's design, active HOA oversight, well maintained common areas, and residential demographics all contribute to a lower crime environment. Summerlin is not an independent city, so it falls under Las Vegas Metro Police Department jurisdiction, but it consistently ranks among the safest areas in the valley. ## Community Design Factors Beyond the guard gate, Reverence's layout contributes to safety in less obvious ways. The community has 655 homes on 300 acres, which is a lower density than many Summerlin neighborhoods. The streets are designed with limited through routes, discouraging non resident traffic. Well lit sidewalks, maintained landscaping, and the regular presence of residents using the parks and trails create natural surveillance throughout the day and evening. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose frequently works with buyers who prioritize safety, particularly families with children and retirees. He notes that the guard gate at Reverence provides peace of mind that goes beyond crime statistics. Knowing that every vehicle is screened before entering the community changes how residents use outdoor spaces, with children playing in front yards and adults walking the trails after dark in ways that feel less comfortable in open neighborhoods.
