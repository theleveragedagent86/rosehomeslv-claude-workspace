# Reverence Summerlin Architectural Styles Homes in Reverence draw from five distinct architectural influences: Craftsman, Prairie, Hacienda, Farmhouse, and Desert Contemporary. Pulte Homes designed each floor plan to be available in multiple exterior styles, giving the streetscape a varied, curated look rather than the uniform appearance common in single builder communities. ## Craftsman Craftsman exteriors feature exposed rafter tails, stone accents, and prominent front porches or entry details. The style emphasizes natural materials and handcrafted textures, with earth tone color palettes that blend with the desert surroundings. Craftsman plans in Reverence tend to have warm, substantial curb appeal. ## Prairie Prairie style homes take inspiration from Frank Lloyd Wright's horizontal emphasis. These exteriors use low pitched rooflines, overhanging eaves, and horizontal banding to create a grounded, wide profile. The style works particularly well on Reverence's elevated terrain, where the horizontal lines complement the mountain backdrop. ## Hacienda Hacienda exteriors reference Southwestern and Spanish Colonial traditions with arched entries, clay tile roof accents, and textured stucco walls. This style is popular across the Las Vegas valley and feels natural in the desert landscape. Hacienda plans in Reverence incorporate courtyards or covered patios that extend living space outdoors. ## Farmhouse The Farmhouse style brings board and batten siding, metal roof accents, and clean vertical lines to Reverence's streetscape. It is the most modern interpretation of a traditional style in the community, blending rustic elements with contemporary proportions. Farmhouse exteriors tend to stand out against the more muted Craftsman and Hacienda neighbors. ## Desert Contemporary Desert Contemporary is the most modern option, featuring flat or low slope rooflines, large windows, and minimal ornamentation. The style prioritizes clean geometry and maximizes natural light. Desert Contemporary homes in Reverence often have the most glass facing west, taking full advantage of the mountain views. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose advises buyers to consider how architectural style affects resale value. In Reverence, Desert Contemporary and Craftsman tend to generate the strongest buyer interest on resale because they appeal to the widest range of tastes. He also notes that the mix of styles on each street keeps the neighborhood visually interesting, which is a quality buyers notice immediately during a first visit.
