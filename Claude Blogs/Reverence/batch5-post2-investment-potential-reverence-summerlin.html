# Investment Potential in Reverence Summerlin Reverence offers several characteristics that make it attractive for real estate investment: guard gated exclusivity, limited inventory, newer construction, and strong amenities. These factors have supported consistent demand and above average appreciation since the community opened in 2017. ## Limited Supply Drives Value Reverence is capped at 655 homes on 300 acres, and Pulte Homes is the sole builder. There is no undeveloped land remaining for future expansion beyond the current Monument townhome phase. This fixed supply means that as demand for guard gated Summerlin living continues, Reverence inventory stays constrained. Limited supply with steady demand is a fundamental driver of price appreciation. ## Price Appreciation Trends The broader Summerlin market has seen consistent year over year appreciation, with a median sale price around $682,000 as of 2025. Reverence has tracked above that average, with per square foot pricing around $530 in early 2026. The guard gate premium, newer construction, and clubhouse amenities all contribute to Reverence holding its value better than older, non gated Summerlin neighborhoods during market fluctuations. ## Rental Demand For investors considering the rental market, Reverence's guard gated address, modern construction, and proximity to Red Rock Canyon and Downtown Summerlin create appeal for higher income tenants. The Monument townhomes, priced in the high $400,000s, offer the most practical rental investment entry point in the community. However, investors should verify current HOA rules regarding rental restrictions before purchasing, as some guard gated communities limit short term or long term rentals. ## Nevada Tax Advantages Nevada's lack of state income tax benefits investors by keeping more rental income and capital gains in their pocket. Property taxes in Clark County are also well below national averages, which improves cash flow for rental properties and reduces carrying costs for long term holds. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose advises investors considering Reverence to focus on the long term hold rather than short term flips.
