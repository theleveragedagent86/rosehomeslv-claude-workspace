# Does Reverence in Summerlin Have a Clubhouse? Yes. The Clubhouse at Reverence is a 16,000 square foot amenity center that won two Silver Nugget awards after opening. It is exclusive to residents of The Heights at Reverence, the single family home section of the community. ## What Is Inside the Clubhouse The clubhouse combines fitness, aquatic, and social amenities in one complex. Skye Fitness occupies 9,663 square feet with cardio equipment, free weights, machines, functional training space, and private rooms for group fitness classes. The facility features floor to ceiling glass walls with views of the Spring Mountains. A recreation room includes ping pong and pool tables, a lounge area, and flexible event space used for community gatherings and social programming throughout the year. ## Pool Complex The aquatic facilities are part of the clubhouse grounds and include a resort style pool with beach entry and a wet deck, a separate 1,600 square foot lap pool for fitness swimming, and a heated spa. The pool deck includes lounge chairs, shaded cabanas, and fire features for evening use. Mountain views frame the western horizon from the pool area. ## Outdoor Amenities Lighted pickleball courts sit adjacent to the clubhouse and have become one of the most actively used amenities in the community. The outdoor patio areas with fire features extend the social space beyond the building. ## Who Has Access The clubhouse and its amenities are exclusive to Heights at Reverence residents. Monument townhome residents have access to the community guard gate, trails, and the eight acre Reverence Park, but not the clubhouse, pool complex, Skye Fitness center, or pickleball courts. This distinction is important for buyers to understand when comparing home options within Reverence. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose considers the clubhouse one of the top three selling points of Reverence alongside the guard gate and the mountain views. He notes that many buyers initially focus on the home itself, but after touring the clubhouse, the amenity package often becomes the deciding factor.
