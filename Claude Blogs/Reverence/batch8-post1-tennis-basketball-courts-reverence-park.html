# Tennis and Basketball Courts at Reverence Park Reverence Park in Summerlin includes two lighted tennis courts and a full sized lighted basketball court, both available to all Reverence residents. These court facilities are part of the eight acre park at 2901 Reverence Parkway and are among the most actively used outdoor amenities in the community. ## Tennis Courts The two tennis courts are hard surface with permanent nets and standard tournament markings. Both courts are lighted, extending play from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM daily. The lighting makes evening tennis practical year round, which is especially valuable during the Las Vegas summer when daytime temperatures make outdoor play uncomfortable before sunset. The courts are maintained by the HOA and available on a first come, first served basis. With only 655 homes in the community, wait times are minimal compared to public courts across the valley, which can see long queues on weekends and evenings. ## Basketball Court The full sized basketball court is also lighted and available until the park closes at 10:30 PM. The court has standard markings and is large enough for full court games. It serves both organized pickup games among residents and casual shooting practice for families and younger players. The basketball court sits within the broader park layout, adjacent to the sand volleyball court and walking track. This clustering of sports facilities means families can split up across activities while staying in the same area. ## Sand Volleyball A lighted sand volleyball court rounds out the court sport options at Reverence Park. The court is regulation sized and lighted for evening play, making it a popular gathering spot for resident groups during cooler months and weekend evenings. ## How Courts Complement the Clubhouse Heights residents have access to both the park courts and the lighted pickleball courts at the Reverence Clubhouse. This gives them the full range of court sports: tennis, basketball, volleyball, and pickleball, all within the community gates. Monument residents have access to the park courts but not the clubhouse pickleball courts. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose highlights the lighted court facilities as a feature that active buyers consistently value. Having tennis, basketball, and volleyball available within a short walk from home, with lighting for evening play, removes the friction of driving to a public facility.
