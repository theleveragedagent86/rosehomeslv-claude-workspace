# How Much Do Homes Cost in Reverence Summerlin? Homes in Reverence range from the high $400,000s for townhomes to over $1.1 million for the largest single family homes. The wide price range reflects the two distinct sections of the community: Monument townhomes and The Heights single family residences. ## Monument Townhomes (High $400,000s) Monument at Reverence is the most affordable entry point. These two story townhomes by Pulte Homes offer three floor plans between 1,654 and 1,869 square feet. Prices start in the high $400,000s for new construction, making Monument competitive with non gated neighborhoods elsewhere in Summerlin. Monument buyers get access to the guard gate, community trails, and Reverence Park, but not the private Heights Clubhouse. ## The Heights Single Family Homes ($650,000 to $1.1 Million Plus) The Heights at Reverence includes approximately 480 single family homes across four collections. Floor plans range from roughly 2,156 to 4,815 square feet. Resale homes in The Heights generally list between $650,000 and $1.1 million, with pricing driven by square footage, lot position, and view orientation. Homes with unobstructed western mountain views or eastern Strip views trade at a premium of $50,000 to $100,000 over comparable interior lots. The largest floor plans (4,500 plus square feet) with premium lots reach the top of the price range. ## Price Per Square Foot As of early 2026, the average price per square foot in Reverence is approximately $530. This is above the broader Summerlin median, reflecting the guard gate premium, newer construction (2017 and later), and the community's amenity package. ## Monthly Costs Beyond the Mortgage Buyers should budget for approximately $250 per month in total HOA fees ($200 Reverence HOA plus $50 Summerlin master association). Clark County property taxes are below the national average but vary by assessed value. Heights residents pay no additional fees for clubhouse, pool, or fitness center access, as these are included in the HOA. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose advises buyers to think about total monthly cost rather than just the purchase price. The HOA fee covers amenities that would cost hundreds per month if accessed through private memberships. He also notes that Reverence's limited inventory (655 total homes) means well priced listings do not sit on the market long, particularly in the $650,000 to $800,000 range where demand is strongest.
