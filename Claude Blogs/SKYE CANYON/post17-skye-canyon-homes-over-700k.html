Skye Canyon homes over $700K are concentrated in the Toll Brothers Vista Rossa Collection, larger Toll Montrose homes, and upgraded Century Northglenn or Cantaro II plans. These sit at the top end of the Skye Canyon market in northwest Las Vegas.

What Skye Canyon homes are priced over $700K?

Toll Brothers Vista Rossa is the main two-story anchor above $700K, with plans from 2,880 to 3,249 square feet. The Montoro and Loma floor plans offer up to six bedrooms plus a loft, and pricing climbs quickly with structural options like extended great rooms and multi-generational suites. Toll Brothers Montrose Collection, a single-story line at 2,031 to 2,370 square feet with 10-foot ceilings, also reaches into the $700s once buyers add upgrades.

Century Northglenn delivers 2,396 to 2,605 square feet on two stories, with four to five bedrooms and an optional casita that frequently pushes finished pricing past $700K. Century Cantaro II plans at 2,581 to 2,947 square feet land in the same range when buyers choose premium homesites and design center selections.

Who buys Skye Canyon homes above $700K?

Move-up families relocating from California, established Las Vegas professionals, and remote workers consolidating a primary residence drive most of the demand at this price point. When clients ask Ryan Rose about luxury Skye Canyon options, the conversation usually starts with Toll Brothers because the brand controls the upper tier of new construction in the village. Buyers in this band also weigh lot size, view orientation toward the Sheep and Spring Mountains, and proximity to the Skye Center amenity hub.

The Skye Canyon median sale price was $586,500 in February 2026, so $700K-plus buyers are paying a premium for square footage, ceiling height, and design upgrades. Most homes at this level include three-car garages, gourmet kitchens, and primary suites positioned for privacy.
