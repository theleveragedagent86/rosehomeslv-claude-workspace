Single-story homes in Skye Canyon are available through Toll Brothers Montrose, Century Madera, Century Aviano, and most of the Beazer Sage Reserve plans. Skye Canyon, in northwest Las Vegas, has one of the deepest single-story new-construction selections in the valley.

Which Skye Canyon communities offer single-story homes?

Toll Brothers Montrose is the luxury single-story collection at 2,031 to 2,370 square feet with 10-foot ceilings throughout. Century Madera is a gated all-single-story enclave at 1,636 to 1,816 square feet, with 10-foot ceilings, quartz baths, and optional courtyards. Century Aviano is another gated single-story village offering three plans from 1,805 to 2,029 square feet, plus a private community pool exclusive to residents.

Beazer Sage Reserve at Skye Hills launched five plans, four of which are single-story, with square footage between 1,750 and 3,179. That gives buyers a single-level option across a wide footprint range, including some that compete on size with two-story homes elsewhere in the master plan.

Why are single-story homes in demand at Skye Canyon?

Ryan Rose frequently recommends single-story Skye Canyon homes to downsizers, retirees, and parents of young children who want everything on one level. Skye Canyon stays an all-ages community, not a 55-plus restricted plan, so single-story stock here works for buyers who do not qualify for or do not want an age-restricted neighborhood. Demand stays consistent because the topography of northwest Las Vegas makes single-story builds practical on most homesites.

Single-story homes also tend to hold value well in Las Vegas resale because the buyer pool is broader than two-story-only listings. Operating costs run lower in many cases because conditioned air does not have to climb a second floor.
