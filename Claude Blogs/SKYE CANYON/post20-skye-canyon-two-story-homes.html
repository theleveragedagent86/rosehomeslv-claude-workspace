Two-story homes in Skye Canyon are offered by Toll Brothers Vista Rossa, Lennar Belterra I and II, Pulte Pinewood, and several Century Communities lines including Aspen, Northglenn, Cantaro I and II, and Eaglepointe. Skye Canyon, in northwest Las Vegas, has more two-story options than most master plans in the valley.

Which Skye Canyon builders offer two-story homes?

Toll Brothers Vista Rossa is the luxury two-story collection at 2,880 to 3,249 square feet, with up to six bedrooms and a loft on the Montoro and Loma plans. Lennar Belterra I and II run from 1,560 to 2,270 square feet and include smart-home integration through the Lennar Everything's Included program. Pulte Pinewood is a gated enclave with six plans across one- and two-story configurations, including the Parklane plan.

Century Communities runs the widest two-story lineup in Skye Canyon. Aspen covers 1,700 to 2,300 square feet, Northglenn delivers 2,396 to 2,605 with four to five bedrooms and an optional casita, Cantaro I spans 1,792 to 2,119, Cantaro II reaches 2,581 to 2,947, and Eaglepointe sits at 2,126 to 2,328 with up to five bedrooms plus loft and courtyard options.

Why choose a two-story home in Skye Canyon?

Based on Ryan Rose's experience with Skye Canyon two-story plans, families pick them for the bedroom count, the separation of primary suite from secondary bedrooms, and the loft as a flex space. Two-story homes also typically deliver more square footage per dollar than single-story equivalents on a similar lot. That matters in Skye Canyon, where homesites are sized for new-construction efficiency rather than half-acre estates.

Buyers should check stair placement, second-floor laundry availability, and primary suite location before committing. Some two-story plans put the primary on the main level, which works for buyers who want one-level living with extra rooms for guests or kids upstairs.
