Quick move-in homes in Skye Canyon typically close within 30 to 90 days, compared to 6 to 10 months for a dirt-to-finish new build. Every active builder in the community maintains some level of spec inventory for buyers who need to move sooner.

What counts as a quick move-in home?

A quick move-in, often called a QMI or spec home, is a new build that the builder has already started or finished without a buyer in contract. Skye Canyon builders like Toll Brothers, Lennar, Pulte, Century Communities, Beazer, and LGI Homes all keep spec inventory rolling so buyers do not have to wait through the full construction cycle.

The exact timeline depends on the stage. A QMI at drywall might close in 60 to 90 days, while a fully finished spec can close in as little as 30 days once financing clears. Each builder posts its current quick move-in list directly on its website.

What features come with Skye Canyon quick move-ins?

When clients ask Ryan Rose about quick move-in homes, the first thing to check is what level of design selections the builder has already locked in. With a spec home, the cabinets, countertops, flooring, and fixtures are already chosen, so buyers trade some customization for speed.

Energy-efficient construction is standard across Skye Canyon builders. Lennar's Everything's Included program ships with smart home integration, and Beazer's Sage Reserve homes were built to DOE Zero Energy Ready standards. Tesla Solar Roof was also offered historically in the Skye Hills section, although availability changes by phase.
