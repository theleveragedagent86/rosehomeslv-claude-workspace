Choosing between new construction and resale in Skye Canyon comes down to trade-offs between warranty and customization on one side, and mature landscaping plus established neighborhoods on the other. Both options sit in a similar price range, with the median sale price at $586,500 as of February 2026.

What does each option cost in Skye Canyon?

New construction in Skye Canyon ranges from the $380s for LGI Topaz townhomes up to over $900,000 for Toll Brothers luxury homes. Resale homes cover a similar range, with most activity clustered around the $586,500 median and roughly 100 to 150 homes typically active at any time.

Skye Canyon is still young, with the oldest homes built starting in 2014. That means even resale homes are only 10 to 12 years old at most, so the gap in age between new and resale is much smaller than in older Las Vegas neighborhoods.

What do you actually get with each path?

Ryan Rose frequently helps buyers decide between new and resale, and the answer usually depends on lifestyle, not just price. New construction comes with builder warranties, current code, and energy-efficient systems, plus the ability to pick finishes if you buy early enough in the phase. Resale homes typically include landscaping, window coverings, appliances, and sometimes a pool, all of which add real dollars after closing.

The community has a 92 percent satisfaction rating from a developer survey, so both new and resale buyers tend to be happy with their decision. The deeper question is whether you want to wait for construction or move into a home where the backyard is already finished.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

For first-time Skye Canyon buyers, the hidden cost of new construction often shows up in the backyard. Builders typically deliver a graded dirt lot, and finishing landscaping, paint, blinds, and a side yard can run $30,000 to $60,000. A resale home in the same price range may already have all of that included.
