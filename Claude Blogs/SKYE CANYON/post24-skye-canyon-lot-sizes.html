Most single-family detached lots in Skye Canyon fall between roughly 4,500 and 7,500 square feet, with premium lots in luxury sections reaching 8,000 square feet or more. Townhome lots in newer attached product like LGI Topaz are smaller.

What is the typical lot size in Skye Canyon?

The Skye Canyon master plan covers 1,700 acres, and the bulk of single-family detached homes sit on lots in the 4,500 to 7,500 square foot range. That puts the community in line with most modern Las Vegas master plans, where efficient lot layouts help keep new home pricing in reach.

Larger lots are concentrated in the Toll Brothers neighborhoods, especially Vista Rossa and Montrose. These sections include more luxury floor plans, larger setbacks, and a few premium homesites that exceed 8,000 square feet.

Are there any tight-spaced neighborhoods?

Resident feedback notes that some sub-villages have tight lot spacing, with relatively little distance between homes. This is most common in the entry-level and mid-tier detached neighborhoods, where lot widths are narrower to keep pricing competitive.

Ryan Rose, who has walked dozens of Skye Canyon spec homes, recommends checking the actual side-yard width during a model tour. The difference between a 5-foot and a 10-foot side yard changes how usable the backyard feels, especially for homes with single-story floor plans where every foot of outdoor space matters.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

If lot size is a priority, plan to focus your search on the luxury and premium sections, or look at corner lots and end-of-cul-de-sac homesites in any neighborhood. Builders typically charge lot premiums of $10,000 to $40,000 for these positions, but the extra square footage is hard to add later.
