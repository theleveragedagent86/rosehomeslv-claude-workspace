Skye Canyon is widely considered a good place to live in Las Vegas, with a 92 percent resident satisfaction rating, a top 10 national master plan ranking, a CrimeGrade B+ safety score, and a median household income near $106,060 in the 89166 ZIP.

Is Skye Canyon a safe and desirable neighborhood?

Safety data places Skye Canyon among the more secure pockets of Las Vegas. CrimeGrade.org assigns the area a B+ rating, with roughly 2.9 crimes per 1,000 residents, well below the citywide average. The 89166 ZIP code ranks as one of the safer Las Vegas ZIPs overall.

The lifestyle layer reinforces the desirability. Skye Canyon's median age is 36 and the broader 89166 population is 27,060, with around 5,108 residing inside Skye Canyon proper. Household income in the ZIP averaged $106,060 in 2023, which supports the community's mix of family friendly amenities and modern home product.

What makes Skye Canyon stand out for daily living?

The community offers Skye Center, an 8,000 square foot clubhouse, plus Skye Fitness, a 10,000 square foot gym with a Junior Olympic pool, spa, yoga room, and juice bar. Five parks, miles of trails, and a community garden round out the outdoor menu. One detail Ryan Rose highlights for Skye Canyon buyers is that Mt. Charleston sits just 20 to 30 minutes away, giving residents real hiking, picnicking, and snow access without leaving the metro.

Commutes also work in residents' favor. The Strip is 23 to 40 minutes via US 95, the airport is 28 to 40 minutes, downtown Las Vegas is around 19 minutes, and Downtown Summerlin is about 16 minutes. Centennial Hills Hospital, a 262 bed facility on North Durango Drive, sits close by for medical needs.

You get newer construction, a strong amenity package, low crime, and quick access to both city jobs and mountain recreation.
