Beazer Ravenna at Skye Canyon

Beazer Ravenna at Skye Canyon was Beazer Homes' first neighborhood inside the Skye Canyon master plan, a gated community with floor plans from 1,916 to 2,532 square feet. Historic pricing started in the low $300s when Ravenna debuted, and resale values today reflect Skye Canyon's broader appreciation.

What is Beazer Ravenna at Skye Canyon?

Ravenna launched in October 2019 as Beazer's debut neighborhood at Skye Canyon, unveiled at a first-look event with new floor plan designs. The community is gated, which separates it from much of the master plan since Skye Canyon overall is not gated. Square footage runs 1,916 to 2,532 across the plan lineup.

Beazer built Ravenna with its standard energy-conscious construction approach, focused on better envelope sealing, efficient HVAC, and low-flow fixtures. That matters at resale because energy costs and Las Vegas summer cooling bills are usually the first thing buyers ask about.

What does Ravenna resale look like today?

Ryan Rose, who has walked Beazer Ravenna model homes, notes that the low $300s pricing from 2019 is long gone. Skye Canyon's February 2026 median sat at $586,500, and Ravenna's gated status plus newer construction date keep its resale values competitive within the master plan. Specific pricing depends on plan, lot, upgrades, and how the original buyer cared for the home.

Buyers shopping Ravenna today are usually trading off gated security and a newer build year against the older, larger Skye Canyon sub-villages. Master HOA dues are $83 per month, and Ravenna's sub-community fee covers the gate, common area landscaping, and shared services unique to the neighborhood.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

Ravenna gets attention from buyers who specifically want a gated address inside Skye Canyon. The plan footprints between 1,916 and 2,532 square feet hit a useful range for move-up buyers without pushing them into the larger Cantaro II or Eaglepointe square footage.
