How much are Skye Canyon HOA dues in 2026?

The Skye Canyon master HOA charges $83 per month per home. On top of that, each sub village inside the master plan operates its own sub association, with monthly dues ranging from $50 to $120 depending on the section, amenities, and whether the sub village is gated. That puts total monthly HOA exposure for most homes between $133 and $203.

Master dues fund Skye Center, the 8,000 square foot clubhouse with Aspire Coffee House, plus Skye Fitness, the 10,000 square foot gym with Junior Olympic pool, spa, yoga room, and juice bar. The five community parks, trail system, and community garden also fall under master responsibility. Resident events like Chalktober Fest and the farmers market are paid out of the same budget.

What do the sub association fees cover?

Sub association fees vary because each village offers different services. Some sub villages are gated and pay for guard service and gate maintenance. Others include private pocket parks, landscaped entries, or extra street lighting. Ryan Rose frequently fields questions about Skye Canyon and explains that buyers should always ask for the sub HOA disclosure before writing an offer, because the sub fee can swing the monthly carry by $70 or more.

For comparison, a $150 combined monthly HOA buys access to a master plan amenity package that mirrors what many private clubs charge separately. The Aspire Coffee House inside Skye Center is open to residents, the fitness center is staffed, and the events calendar runs year round. That bundling is one reason resident satisfaction sits at 92 percent.

Ryan Rose tells buyers comparing Skye Canyon to other northwest master plans that the all in HOA number is what matters, not the headline master rate. Two homes on the same street can have different sub HOA dues depending on the village line. Before you fall in love with a floor plan, confirm the total dues so the monthly payment math lines up with your budget.
