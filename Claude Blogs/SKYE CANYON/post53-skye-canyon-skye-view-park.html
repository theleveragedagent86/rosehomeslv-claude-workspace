Skye View Park is the Skye Canyon neighborhood park built around pickleball and dog owners. It includes a covered play area, 2 pickleball courts, a tennis court, 2 separate dog parks for small and large dogs, and a splash pad, and it is open dawn to dusk.

What Is Skye View Park?

Skye View Park is one of four neighborhood parks inside Skye Canyon and the only one that combines pickleball, tennis, and two dog parks on the same site. The two dog parks are separated into a small-dog area and a large-dog area, which is the layout most owners prefer. A covered play area and splash pad sit nearby for families.

Skye Canyon's dog park rules apply at Skye View: 2 dogs maximum per visitor, current vaccinations required, and dogs must be leashed entering and leaving the gated areas. The park is open dawn to dusk and is included in the master HOA's $83 per month coverage.

What Activities Does It Support?

Based on Ryan Rose's experience with dog-owner buyers, Skye View Park is the park they ask about first. The split small-dog and large-dog areas mean owners do not have to gamble on mixed-size play, and the proximity to the splash pad and covered play area lets parents bring dogs and kids on the same trip. Pickleball players also gravitate here because 2 dedicated courts are easier to find than a single shared court.

The tennis court rounds out the racquet sport options for residents who do not want to share court time with pickleball groups. Combined, the amenities make Skye View one of the busier neighborhood parks in the community.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

Ryan Rose recommends Skye View Park to families with dogs because the dual dog parks solve the most common complaint at single-park setups. Mornings before 9 a.m. and evenings after 6 p.m. tend to be the easiest times for both pickleball and the dog runs. If a dog-friendly park is on your must-have list, Skye Canyon should be high on your tour. Contact Ryan Rose to map homes near Skye View.
