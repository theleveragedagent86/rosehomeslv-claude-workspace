Skye Canyon Fit Fest: 5K and 8K Races

Fit Fest is Skye Canyon's annual family fitness event. The day combines a 5K road race and an 8K trail run with yoga, group workouts, dance classes, a marketplace, and food vendors. It's built around the community's "active healthy living" identity.

What races are part of Fit Fest?

Fit Fest features two timed races. The 5K Road Run takes participants through Skye Canyon's neighborhood streets. The 8K Trail Mix is a longer dirt-trail race that uses the community's open-space paths and natural terrain. Both routes are open to runners and walkers, and the 5K is approachable for first-time racers or families with kids who want to participate.

The 8K trail option is the more distinctive feature. Skye Canyon sits at the edge of Las Vegas with direct access to undeveloped desert and elevation, so a dirt-trail race here actually feels different from a typical city 5K.

What else happens at Fit Fest?

When clients ask Ryan Rose about Fit Fest, he describes it as more than just a race day. The event includes yoga sessions, Bootcamp Blast workouts, and DanceWorkz classes throughout the morning. There's an artisanal marketplace with local vendors, food vendors on site, and a kids rock climbing wall to keep younger family members busy while parents run or work out.

The format works for households at different fitness levels. One parent can run the 8K trail race while the other does yoga and the kids hit the climbing wall. That kind of programming is exactly why Skye Canyon brands itself around active healthy living, and Fit Fest is one of the clearest expressions of that positioning.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

Fit Fest tends to fall on a Saturday morning at Skye Canyon Park and draws residents plus people from across northwest Las Vegas.
