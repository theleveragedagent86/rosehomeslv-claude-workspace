Skye Serves Community Service Program

Skye Serves is the community service arm of Skye Canyon. The program organizes residents around volunteer projects, with a longstanding partnership benefiting Project 150 and proceeds from signature events like Chalktober Fest directed to the charity.

What is the Skye Serves program?

Skye Serves is Skye Canyon's resident-led community service initiative. The program builds on the master plan's active healthy living brand by adding a giving-back component to the event calendar. Residents contribute time, resources, and event participation, and the master association coordinates the larger volunteer efforts. The partnership with Project 150, a Las Vegas nonprofit that supports homeless and at-risk youth, is the program's most visible relationship.

The connection runs through community events as well as direct volunteer work. Proceeds from Chalktober Fest, one of Skye Canyon's biggest annual gatherings, benefit Project 150. That setup turns a community festival into a fundraising engine without changing the family-friendly format of the event itself.

Why does Skye Serves matter to buyers?

Ryan Rose highlights Skye Serves to buyers who value living in a neighborhood where the community brand goes beyond amenities. Pools, parks, and trails are easy to copy from one master plan to another. A structured service program with a named charity partner is harder to replicate, and it is part of why Skye Canyon ranks among the top 50 master plans in the country with a 92% resident satisfaction score.

For families moving from out of state, Skye Serves also gives kids and parents a built-in way to plug into Las Vegas charitable work without having to start from scratch. The events are local, the cause is local, and the participation is already organized.
