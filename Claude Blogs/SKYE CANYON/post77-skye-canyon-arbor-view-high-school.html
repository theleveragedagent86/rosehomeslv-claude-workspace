Arbor View High School: STEM Magnet for Skye Canyon Teens

Arbor View High School is the zoned CCSD high school for most Skye Canyon addresses. About 10 minutes from the community, it carries a STEM magnet designation and offers AP courses.

Why is Arbor View a strong choice for Skye Canyon families?

Arbor View serves grades 9 through 12 as part of the Clark County School District. Its STEM magnet designation means students can pursue focused tracks in engineering, science, and math alongside the standard high school curriculum. AP courses give college bound students a chance to earn credit before graduation.

The 10 minute drive from Skye Canyon makes daily commutes manageable, and the school pulls from several northwest Las Vegas neighborhoods, which gives kids a wider peer group than a small zoned school would.

What should parents know before enrolling?

STEM magnet seats can require a separate application even though Arbor View is the zoned high school for many Skye Canyon homes. Families who want the magnet track should check timelines early in eighth grade. General enrollment follows standard CCSD zoning, so verifying your specific address at ccsd.net is the safest first step.

When clients ask Ryan Rose about Arbor View, the answer is usually that it pairs well with Somerset Academy as a K-8 feeder. Kids can do K-8 at the charter, then move to Arbor View for high school without leaving the northwest.
