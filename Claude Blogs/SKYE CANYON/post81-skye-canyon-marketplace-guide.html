Skye Canyon Marketplace is the retail and dining hub built inside the master plan along Skye Canyon Park Drive. Anchored by a 125,000 square foot Smith's Marketplace, it brings groceries, restaurants, services, and pet care within walking distance of many homes.

What is inside Skye Canyon Marketplace?

Skye Canyon Marketplace is a community shopping center designed so residents can handle daily errands without leaving the neighborhood. The anchor is Smith's Marketplace, a full size Kroger owned grocery store with pharmacy, bakery, deli, floral, and fuel center. An in store Starbucks runs inside the Smith's lobby.

The surrounding storefronts include Chase Bank, Domino's, Sally Beauty, Menchie's Frozen Yogurt, Tropical Smoothie Cafe, Woof Gang Bakery and Grooming, Subway, Burger King, Island Flavor, and Cafe Rio. Sit down options nearby include Sicilian Guys Trattoria, Sakura Ya, and El Pollo Loco at 9550 W. Skye Canyon Park Dr.

Why is the Marketplace central to the Skye Canyon lifestyle?

Ryan Rose points out that Skye Canyon Marketplace is the single biggest reason the community feels self sufficient. Many master plans require a 10 to 15 minute drive for groceries and weeknight dinners. Skye Canyon residents can walk or bike to the same errand mix from large pockets of the community thanks to the Skye Canyon Park Drive trail system.

The tenant mix is also balanced. Quick service food, a bank branch, frozen yogurt, smoothies, pet grooming, beauty supply, and a national pizza chain cover the daily routine. Independent restaurants like Sicilian Guys and Sakura Ya raise the dining floor above the typical suburban strip.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

Ryan Rose tells buyers that the Marketplace changes how Skye Canyon feels day to day. Errand trips that used to mean a 20 minute round trip become a 5 minute walk. That is rare for a Las Vegas master plan and one of the reasons resale values inside Skye Canyon have held up.
