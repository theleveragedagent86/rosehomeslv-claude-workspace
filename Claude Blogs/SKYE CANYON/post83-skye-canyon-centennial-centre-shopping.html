Centennial Centre is a 900,000 square foot regional shopping center about 10 minutes south of Skye Canyon. With 100 plus stores including Walmart Supercenter, Sam's Club, and Home Depot, it catches everything the Skye Canyon Marketplace does not carry.

What is Centennial Centre?

Centennial Centre is one of the largest shopping centers in northwest Las Vegas. The complex covers roughly 900,000 square feet and hosts more than 100 retailers, restaurants, banks, and service businesses. Major anchors include Walmart Supercenter, Sam's Club, and Home Depot, which together cover groceries, bulk goods, and home improvement.

The center sits about 10 minutes south of Skye Canyon by car. That distance keeps it close enough for a weekly run while leaving Skye Canyon's own Marketplace as the daily errand stop. Restaurants, pet stores, banks, beauty, and apparel fill in the rest of the tenant mix.

How do Skye Canyon residents use Centennial Centre?

Ryan Rose, who has shopped at Centennial Centre with clients touring nearby homes, sees a clear pattern. Skye Canyon residents use the local Marketplace for daily groceries, dinner pickups, and pet care. They drive to Centennial Centre for bulk Sam's Club runs, home projects at Home Depot, and items Walmart carries that smaller stores do not stock.

The combination works well. Most weeks a Skye Canyon homeowner does not need to leave the master plan. When a bigger errand list builds up, a single 10 minute drive to Centennial Centre handles the rest. It is a more efficient setup than communities that depend on Centennial Centre for everything.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

Ryan Rose tells buyers that the Centennial Centre proximity is a quiet selling point for Skye Canyon. Many buyers focus on the in community Marketplace and miss the second layer of retail just down the 215. Between the two centers, Skye Canyon residents almost never need to drive to Summerlin or the Strip for routine shopping.
