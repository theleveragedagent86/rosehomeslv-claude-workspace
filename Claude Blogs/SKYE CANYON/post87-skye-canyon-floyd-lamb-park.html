Floyd Lamb Park From Skye Canyon

Floyd Lamb Park at Tule Springs is a 2,040 acre City of Las Vegas park sitting just 5 to 10 minutes from Skye Canyon. Four fishing lakes, picnic areas, historic ranch buildings, and roaming peacocks make it a local favorite.

What is Floyd Lamb Park?

Floyd Lamb Park covers 2,040 acres at 9200 Tule Springs Road. The land includes the historic Tule Springs Ranch, which gives the park a working ranch feel with old stone buildings, shade trees, and grassy lawns that look out toward the mountains. The park has four stocked fishing lakes, picnic areas, gazebos for events, and trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

The roaming peacocks are the park's most famous feature. They wander the lawns, perch on fences, and add a relaxed, almost rural feel that you do not expect in Las Vegas. Hours run from 8am to dusk on a seasonal schedule, and a small entry fee applies.

Why do Skye Canyon residents go there?

When clients ask Ryan Rose about Floyd Lamb Park, the short answer is that it is the closest big green space to Skye Canyon. The drive is short, parking is easy, and the park works for everything from a quick walk to a full afternoon with kids. Families come for the lakes, walking groups use the trails, and photographers book the gazebos and historic ranch buildings for portraits and events.

The picnic areas are well shaded, which matters in Las Vegas summers. Fishing is catch-and-release for some species, and a Nevada fishing license is required for adults. The trail network is flat enough for casual riders and walkers, and equestrian use is allowed in designated areas.
