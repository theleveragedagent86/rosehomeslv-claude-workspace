Lee Canyon Ski Resort From Skye Canyon

Lee Canyon Ski Resort sits about 50 minutes from Skye Canyon via US-95 North and SR-156. It is one of the southernmost ski areas in the United States, with winter skiing, year-round chairlifts, and a summer mountain bike park.

What is Lee Canyon?

Lee Canyon is the only ski resort in southern Nevada. It runs downhill skiing and snowboarding from December through March, with multiple terrain levels for beginners through advanced riders. A dedicated tubing area gives families a snow-play option without lift tickets. The resort sits at elevation in the Spring Mountains, so snow conditions can hold longer than valley weather suggests.

Lifts stay open year round. In summer, the resort runs a mountain bike park with lift-served downhill trails. Scenic chairlift rides are available outside the ski season for visitors who want the view without the activity.

How does the trip from Skye Canyon work?

Ryan Rose recommends Lee Canyon to families who want a winter sport option without flying out of town. From Skye Canyon, the drive heads north on US-95 and then west on SR-156. Most days the trip runs about 50 minutes, which makes a day trip very realistic. Skye Canyon residents can leave after breakfast, ski or board for several hours, and be home by dinner.

Winter driving requires attention. Snow and ice can form on SR-156, and chains may be required during storms. Most weekends draw steady traffic, so an early start helps. The resort website lists current conditions, lift status, and chain requirements before each visit.
