Skye Canyon was built with active living in mind, and that includes the four-legged residents. The community offers more dedicated dog space than most Las Vegas master plans, plus a pet bakery and trails that welcome leashed dogs.

Where can dogs play in Skye Canyon?

Skye Canyon has four dedicated dog parks across two locations. Skye View Park includes a small dog area and a large dog area. Big Skye Park also features a small dog area and a large dog area. Each park is fenced and separated by size, which keeps play safe and predictable.

The community rules are simple. Visitors may bring up to two dogs at a time. Dogs must be current on vaccinations. Leashes are required on entry and exit. Once inside the fenced run, dogs can play off-leash within their size group.

What pet services and trails are nearby?

Ryan Rose, a dog owner himself, recommends Skye Canyon for buyers who want trail access and on-site pet services without leaving the community. Woof Gang Bakery and Grooming sits inside the Skye Canyon Marketplace and handles grooming appointments, fresh-baked treats, and pet supplies in one stop.

Pet-friendly trails wind throughout Skye Canyon, and leashed dogs are welcome at the general community parks as well. For weekend outings, Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument and Floyd Lamb Park sit minutes from the community gates. Both allow leashed pets and offer wide-open space.
