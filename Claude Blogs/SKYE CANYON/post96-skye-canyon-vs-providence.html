Skye Canyon vs Providence Las Vegas

Skye Canyon and Providence sit just minutes apart in northwest Las Vegas, yet they feel different the moment you drive in. One is newer and amenity-heavy. The other is older and more established.

How do Skye Canyon and Providence compare on price and homes?

Skye Canyon spans 1,700 acres in ZIP 89166 with a median sale price around $586,500 as of February 2026. Inventory ranges from LGI townhomes in the $380s to Toll Brothers homes above $900,000, with six active builders still selling new construction.

Providence is older, built mostly in the late 1990s and 2000s, and sits west of Skye Canyon. Prices land in a similar range, but most inventory is resale, not new build. Providence has mature trees and established landscaping that Skye Canyon will not match for another decade.

Which community has better amenities and schools?

Skye Canyon wins on amenities. The Skye Center clubhouse, 10,000 square foot Skye Fitness gym, junior Olympic pool, five community parks, and 4,000-plus trees are part of the master plan. Smith's Marketplace anchors daily shopping at the entrance.

Providence has parks and a community center, but no 10,000 square foot gym and a smaller clubhouse footprint. Both feed into CCSD, with Skye Canyon zoned for Arbor View High School and home to Somerset Academy charter K-8 (ranked top 18 in Nevada). Ryan Rose points out that buyers choosing between Skye Canyon and Providence often pick Skye Canyon for the gym and pool access and Providence for the established neighborhood feel and mature lots.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

The honest answer depends on what stage of life you are in. Active families and buyers who want new construction lean Skye Canyon. Buyers who want shade trees, settled streets, and resale flexibility lean Providence. Both are safe, both are NW Vegas, both put you 25 to 40 minutes from the Strip.
