Moving to Skye Canyon: Complete Buyer Guide

Skye Canyon ranks among the top 50 master plans in the country, with 92 percent resident satisfaction and a healthy living brand. If you are relocating to northwest Las Vegas, here is what to know before you write an offer.

What does it cost to buy in Skye Canyon?

The February 2026 median sale price in Skye Canyon sits at $586,500. Entry-level townhomes from LGI start in the $380s. Mid-range single-family from Lennar, Pulte, Beazer, and Century Communities runs $500,000 to $750,000. Toll Brothers homes in Montrose and Vista Rossa push past $900,000.

HOA dues are dual: a master HOA at $83 per month, plus a sub-village HOA between $50 and $120 depending on the section. Most homes are zoned for the Las Vegas city portion of CCSD and have access to Somerset Academy charter K-8 (ranked top 18 in Nevada) and Arbor View High School with its STEM magnet program.

What is daily life like in Skye Canyon?

The lifestyle is built around health and outdoor recreation. Residents use the 10,000 square foot Skye Fitness gym, the junior Olympic pool, five community parks, miles of trails, and four dog parks. Smith's Marketplace at the entrance covers groceries, and the Skye Canyon Marketplace adds Skye Bar and Grill, Sicilian Guys, Sakura Ya, and Aspire Coffee on-site.

Centennial Hills Hospital (262 beds) is 5 to 10 minutes away. The Strip is 23 to 40 minutes depending on time of day. Mt. Charleston, Lee Canyon ski area, Tule Springs Fossil Beds, and Floyd Lamb Park are all within a short drive for weekend recreation. Ryan Rose recommends Skye Canyon to relocators because the combination of new construction, top schools, and the lowest-crime ZIP cluster in northwest Las Vegas is hard to match.
