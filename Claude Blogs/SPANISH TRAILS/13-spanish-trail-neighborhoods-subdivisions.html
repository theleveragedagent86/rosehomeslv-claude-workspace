Spanish Trail is made up of 11 distinct neighborhoods spread across 640 acres, each offering a different style of living within the same guard gated community. From luxury estates on half acre lots to townhome style villas, the subdivisions within Spanish Trail cover a remarkably wide range of home sizes, price points, and lifestyles. ## What Are the Neighborhoods Inside Spanish Trail? The confirmed subdivisions within Spanish Trail include The Estates, The Villas, Carmel, Links, The Gardens, The Courtyards, The Springs, and The Islands. Each neighborhood has its own architectural character and lot configuration, but all share access to the community's amenities, including the 27 hole golf course, 50,000 square foot clubhouse, 12 tennis courts, and two aquatic centers. The Estates section features the largest properties in the community, with lots of half an acre or more and custom built luxury homes that can reach $8,000,000. On the other end of the spectrum, The Villas offers townhome style residences on smaller lots starting at approximately 5,900 square feet, with prices ranging from around $200,000 to the mid $600,000s. This variety is one of the reasons Spanish Trail appeals to such a broad range of buyers. ## How Do Spanish Trail's Subdivisions Differ in Price and Size? Home sizes across Spanish Trail range from 1,157 square feet in the smaller villa and townhome sections to 16,649 square feet in the custom estate neighborhoods. The overall community price range stretches from approximately $360,000 for entry level townhomes to $8,000,000 for the most expansive estate properties, with a median sale price of $894,000 as of January 2026. Neighborhoods like Carmel and Links tend to offer single family homes in the mid range, while The Gardens, The Courtyards, and The Springs each provide their own distinct lot sizes and home styles within the community's overall framework. ## Which Spanish Trail Neighborhood Is Right for You? Choosing the right subdivision comes down to your priorities. If you want maximum space, privacy, and a custom built home, The Estates is the clear choice. If you prefer lower maintenance living with access to the same world class amenities, The Villas provides an affordable entry point into the community. Families often gravitate toward the mid range neighborhoods that offer single family homes with yards, while retirees appreciate the convenience and simplicity of the townhome sections.
