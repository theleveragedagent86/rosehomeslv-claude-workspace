Spanish Trail Country Club is a private, member owned club at the heart of the Spanish Trail community in Las Vegas, featuring a 27 hole Robert Trent Jones Jr. championship golf course, a 50,000 square foot clubhouse, 12 illuminated tennis courts, two aquatic centers, and a full service restaurant open seven days a week. ## What Does Spanish Trail Country Club Offer Members? The centerpiece of Spanish Trail Country Club is its 27 hole golf course, split into three distinct nine hole layouts: Canyon, Lakes, and Sunrise. Designed by Robert Trent Jones Jr., the course first opened in 1983 as the first new golf course Las Vegas had seen in fifteen years. The PGA Las Vegas Invitational was hosted at Spanish Trail five times, and the course currently serves as the home course for the UNLV Women's Golf Team. Beyond golf, the club's 50,000 square foot clubhouse anchors daily life for members. The facility includes a renovated fitness center, event spaces for private parties and corporate functions, and a restaurant serving breakfast, lunch, and dinner throughout the week. Members also have access to 12 illuminated tennis courts and two separate aquatic centers with pools and spas. ## What Types of Memberships Are Available? Spanish Trail Country Club offers several membership tiers to match different lifestyles. Full golf memberships provide unrestricted access to the 27 hole course along with all clubhouse amenities. Social memberships grant access to dining, fitness, tennis, and aquatic facilities without golf privileges. Corporate and national memberships are also available for businesses and out of state residents.
