The Spanish Trail golf course is a 27 hole championship layout designed by Robert Trent Jones Jr., one of the most celebrated golf course architects in the world. When it opened in 1983, it was the first new golf course Las Vegas had seen in fifteen years. ## What Makes the Robert Trent Jones Jr. Design Special? Robert Trent Jones Jr. designed the Spanish Trail course with three distinct nine hole layouts: Canyon, Lakes, and Sunrise. Each nine plays with a different character, giving members variety every time they step onto the course. The Canyon nine features elevation changes and desert terrain, the Lakes nine incorporates water features throughout, and the Sunrise nine offers a blend of both elements with wider fairways and strategic bunkering. Jones Jr. is known for designing courses that challenge low handicap players while remaining playable for higher handicaps. His portfolio includes over 270 courses worldwide, and the Spanish Trail layout reflects his philosophy of integrating natural landscape features into the design rather than imposing artificial shaping on the terrain. ## What Is the Tournament History at Spanish Trail? Spanish Trail's golf course hosted the PGA Las Vegas Invitational five times, attracting top professional talent to the community. The tournament history adds a level of prestige that few private courses in the Las Vegas valley can claim. The course also serves as the home course for the UNLV Women's Golf Team, providing a consistent collegiate level proving ground.
