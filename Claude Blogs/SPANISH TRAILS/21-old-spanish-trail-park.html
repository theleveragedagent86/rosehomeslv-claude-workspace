Old Spanish Trail Park is a 9.9-acre community park located at 8150 Tara Ave, just outside the Spanish Trail guard gates, offering a 1.2-mile walking trail, covered play structures, picnic gazebos, and educational displays about the historic Old Spanish Trail trade route. ## What Amenities Does Old Spanish Trail Park Offer? Old Spanish Trail Park is one of the most well-rounded neighborhood parks in the Spring Valley area. The park features a desert-themed playground with a dinosaur ribcage climbing structure and soft rubber safety flooring, making it a favorite for families with young children. Restrooms are conveniently located near the playground, and water fountains are available throughout the park. Four covered gazebos with built-in BBQ pits provide excellent picnic and gathering spots. The park's fenced perimeter gives parents peace of mind, allowing children to play freely without the worry of nearby traffic. Grassy open areas offer additional space for casual recreation and relaxation. ## Is Old Spanish Trail Park Good for Walking and Running? The 1.2-mile walking trail that loops through the park is flat, well-marked, and suitable for walkers and runners of all fitness levels. Ryan Rose often recommends this park to buyers exploring the Spanish Trail neighborhood because its proximity to the community makes daily outdoor activity effortless. The trail takes roughly 23 minutes at a moderate walking pace, making it ideal for a quick morning or evening loop. ## What Makes This Park Unique for Spanish Trail Residents? Beyond recreation, Old Spanish Trail Park preserves a piece of regional history. Interpretive signs throughout the park explain the significance of the Old Spanish Trail, a trade route that once connected northern New Mexico with Los Angeles. Displays cover topics ranging from early indigenous settlers to Captain John C. Fremont's charting of the trail in 1845. For Spanish Trail residents who value outdoor access without a long commute, this park delivers exactly that.
