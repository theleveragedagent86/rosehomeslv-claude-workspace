Spanish Trail and Summerlin are both popular luxury communities in Las Vegas, but they serve different types of buyers. Spanish Trail offers an intimate, fully guard gated environment with 1,200 homes on 640 acres, while Summerlin is a massive master planned community spanning 22,500 acres with tens of thousands of homes. ## How Does Spanish Trail Compare to Summerlin in Size and Exclusivity? Spanish Trail is a single guard gated community with 11 neighborhoods, all protected behind staffed entry points. The community was built between 1984 and 2005 and is fully built out, meaning every home is a resale. This creates a stable, established feel with mature landscaping, wide streets, and a consistent neighborhood character throughout. Summerlin covers roughly 35 times the land area of Spanish Trail and includes multiple villages with varying price points, home styles, and levels of gating. Some Summerlin neighborhoods are guard gated, such as The Ridges, but the majority of the community is open access. Summerlin also continues to add new construction, giving buyers more options for modern floor plans and builder incentives. ## Which Community Is Better for Golf and Country Club Living? Ryan Rose works with many buyers who prioritize golf course living, and Spanish Trail stands out in this category. The community features a private 27 hole Robert Trent Jones Jr. golf course that winds through the neighborhoods, giving many homes direct fairway or green views. The 50,000 square foot clubhouse, tennis courts, pools, and dining are all on site and accessible within the community gates. Summerlin has access to several golf courses, including TPC Las Vegas and Bear's Best, but these courses are spread across the broader community rather than integrated into a single gated environment. For buyers who want to walk or cart to the first tee from their front door, Spanish Trail provides a more connected golf lifestyle. ## Price Comparison and Home Values Spanish Trail homes range from approximately $360,000 for townhomes to $8 million for custom estates, with a median price of $894,000 as of January 2026. Summerlin offers a wider price range from the $300,000s to over $10 million, with significantly more inventory at any given time. Spanish Trail's resale only market tends to hold value well, with a 5.5 percent year over year appreciation rate.
