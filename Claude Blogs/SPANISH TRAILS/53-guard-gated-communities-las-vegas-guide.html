Las Vegas is home to more than a dozen guard gated communities, each offering a different combination of price points, amenities, and lifestyle. Whether you prioritize golf, modern architecture, or proximity to the Strip, there is a guard gated neighborhood in the Las Vegas valley that fits your criteria. ## What Are the Top Guard Gated Communities in Las Vegas? The most well known guard gated communities in Las Vegas include Spanish Trail, The Ridges at Summerlin, MacDonald Highlands, Queensridge, Canyon Gate, Eagle Hills, Anthem Country Club, Tournament Hills, Southern Highlands (gated sections), and Las Vegas Country Club. Each of these communities provides staffed entry gates, controlled access, and an added layer of privacy that standard neighborhoods do not offer. Spanish Trail is one of the oldest and most established guard gated communities in the valley. Built between 1984 and 2005 on 640 acres, it features 1,200 homes, a 27 hole Robert Trent Jones Jr. golf course, and a 50,000 square foot clubhouse. The Ridges at Summerlin is a newer luxury enclave with modern custom homes starting above $1 million, while MacDonald Highlands in Henderson offers hillside estates with elevated Strip views. ## How Do Guard Gated Communities Differ from Standard Gated Neighborhoods? Ryan Rose explains this distinction frequently to buyers who are new to the Las Vegas market. A guard gated community has staffed entry points where security personnel verify every visitor before granting access. A standard gated community may use a keypad, remote, or call box system without a live guard present. The difference in security level is significant, particularly for buyers who value privacy and want to control who enters their neighborhood. Guard gated communities in Las Vegas typically carry higher home values and HOA fees to support 24 hour staffing. Spanish Trail HOA fees range from $180 to $425 per month, which covers guard gate operations, common area maintenance, and community landscaping. This cost structure is consistent across most guard gated communities in the valley. ## Choosing the Right Guard Gated Community The right community depends on your priorities. Spanish Trail appeals to buyers who want an established, golf centered neighborhood with mature landscaping and a central southwest location. MacDonald Highlands and The Ridges attract buyers seeking newer construction and dramatic views. Queensridge and Las Vegas Country Club offer proximity to the west side of the valley with more traditional luxury.
