Spring Valley Las Vegas is served by the Clark County School District and offers families a solid selection of public schools, including one of the valley's stronger high schools, three middle school options, and nine or more elementary schools spread across the community. Families moving to Spring Valley will find academic programs ranging from International Baccalaureate to dual enrollment college courses. ## What High School Serves Spring Valley Las Vegas? Spring Valley High School is the community's primary high school, located at 3750 South Buffalo Drive. The school holds a B+ rating from Niche and a 7 out of 10 score from GreatSchools, placing it in the top half of Nevada public high schools. SchoolDigger ranks it 40th out of 103 high schools statewide. The school serves approximately 2,677 students in grades 9 through 12 and maintains a 94% graduation rate. Academic offerings include Advanced Placement courses, the International Baccalaureate program, and dual enrollment options that let students earn college credits before graduation. Spring Valley High also fields 32 varsity sports teams, making it one of the more athletically comprehensive schools in the district. ## What Middle Schools Are in Spring Valley? Three middle schools serve the Spring Valley area, each feeding into Spring Valley High School or nearby high schools. Clifford J. Lawrence Junior High School sits in the northern portion of the community and serves students in grades 6 through 8. Faiss Middle School offers another strong option in the central part of Spring Valley. Victoria Fertitta Middle School rounds out the middle school choices and is one of the newer facilities in the area. All three schools operate under CCSD and offer standard academic programs along with elective courses in arts, technology, and physical education. Parents should verify current attendance zone boundaries through the CCSD website, as zones can shift from year to year. ## What Elementary Schools Are Available in Spring Valley? Spring Valley's elementary school options include Bendorf, Bryan, Christensen, Diskin, Forbuss, Frias, Hayes, Piggott, and Reedom elementary schools. These schools serve students in kindergarten through fifth grade and are distributed across the community's 33.4 square miles, ensuring most families have a neighborhood school within a short drive. Several of these elementary schools offer magnet or specialized programs.
