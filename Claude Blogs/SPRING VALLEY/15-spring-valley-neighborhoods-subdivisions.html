Spring Valley is home to dozens of distinct neighborhoods and subdivisions, each offering a different lifestyle, price point, and community feel across its 33.4 square miles in the western Las Vegas valley. ## What Are the Main Neighborhoods in Spring Valley Las Vegas? Spring Valley's neighborhoods range from guard gated luxury enclaves like Spanish Trail and Spanish Hills to well established family friendly subdivisions built by major homebuilders. Spanish Trail alone contains 11 individual neighborhoods within its 640 acre footprint, offering everything from patio homes to custom estates. Outside the gated communities, buyers will find a wide selection of single family subdivisions developed by Pardee Homes, Beazer Homes, D.R. Horton, and LGI Homes. The community was first established in 1981 and has grown steadily over the decades. Older subdivisions near Decatur Boulevard and Jones Boulevard feature homes from the mid 1970s through the 1990s, while newer developments near the 215 Beltway and Hualapai Way include construction from the 2010s and 2020s. ## How Do Spring Valley Subdivisions Compare? Subdivisions in the northern portion of Spring Valley, closer to Sahara Avenue, tend to feature smaller lot sizes and more affordable price points. As you move south and west toward the 215 Beltway and Red Rock Canyon, lot sizes increase and home values climb. The southern sections in zip codes 89113 and 89148 are popular with families seeking newer builds and proximity to parks and trails.
