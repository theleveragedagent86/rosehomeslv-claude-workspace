The Springs Preserve is a 180 acre cultural and natural attraction located at 333 South Valley View Boulevard, just a short drive from Spring Valley. This unique destination combines botanical gardens, museums, nature trails, and interactive exhibits that celebrate the history and ecology of the Las Vegas Valley. ## What Is There to See at the Springs Preserve? The Springs Preserve offers a variety of attractions spread across its expansive grounds. The Origen Museum features more than 75 permanent exhibits, an indoor theater, and a traveling exhibit gallery. The Nevada State Museum on site houses a 20,000 year old Columbian Mammoth skeleton, a recreated stalactite cave, and exhibits on Nevada mining history and Las Vegas showgirl culture. At Boomtown 1905, visitors can walk through a recreated historical streetscape complete with railroad cottages, a train depot, and a saloon that tells the story of early Las Vegas. The botanical gardens showcase thousands of Mojave Desert and desert adapted plants, and a seasonal butterfly habitat opens in the spring and fall. Over three miles of trails wind through desert wetlands, cottonwood groves, and interpretive sites. ## How Far Is the Springs Preserve from Spring Valley? The Springs Preserve sits approximately three miles west of downtown Las Vegas, making it an easy 10 to 15 minute drive from most Spring Valley neighborhoods. The preserve is open Thursday through Monday from 9 AM to 4 PM. Admission for Nevada residents is $9.95 for adults and $4.95 for children ages 3 to 17, with discounts available for seniors and military.
