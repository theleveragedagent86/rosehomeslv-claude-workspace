Spring Valley offers residents a variety of walking paths and trails that make it easy to stay active without leaving the neighborhood. From paved park loops to desert summit hikes, Spring Valley has options for every fitness level. ## What Are the Best Walking Paths in Spring Valley? Desert Breeze Park features one of the most popular walking paths in the area. The paved loop stretches over a mile through the 119 acre park, passing playgrounds, sports fields, and shaded rest areas along the way. The flat, well maintained surface makes it a favorite for joggers, dog walkers, and families with strollers. Spring Valley Community Park on Flamingo Road also provides a quiet walking and jogging path that encircles the 33 acre green space. The path is well maintained with open space and picnic areas nearby, offering a more relaxed setting for residents who prefer a less crowded environment. ## Are There Hiking Trails Near Spring Valley? For residents looking for more elevation and scenery, Exploration Peak Park delivers a rewarding 1.0 mile out and back trail that climbs to a 2,846 foot summit. The hike takes roughly 30 minutes and rewards visitors with 360 degree panoramic views of the Las Vegas Valley, surrounding mountains, and the distant Strip skyline. Sturdy footwear is recommended due to loose rocks on certain sections. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area sits just west of Spring Valley, roughly a 20 minute drive via Charleston Boulevard. The conservation area features dozens of trails ranging from easy desert walks to challenging canyon scrambles, all set against dramatic sandstone formations. Ryan Rose often recommends Red Rock Canyon as one of the top lifestyle perks of living in Spring Valley. ## A Local Perspective on Spring Valley Trails What makes Spring Valley stand out is the range of trail options within a short distance.
