Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is one of the top natural attractions near Spring Valley, located just west of the neighborhood with a drive time of roughly 20 minutes. Spring Valley residents enjoy some of the closest and most convenient access to this iconic desert landscape in the Las Vegas Valley. ## How Do You Get to Red Rock Canyon From Spring Valley? The most direct route from Spring Valley follows Charleston Boulevard (State Route 159) west toward the mountains. The drive covers approximately 15 to 20 miles depending on your starting point within the neighborhood and typically takes 20 to 25 minutes in normal traffic. An alternate route follows Blue Diamond Road (SR 160) west to the junction with SR 159 for those coming from the southern end of Spring Valley. The conservation area entrance leads to a one way, 13 mile scenic loop drive with pullouts for trailheads, overlooks, and picnic areas. The visitor center near the entrance offers maps, exhibits, and information about current trail conditions. ## Do You Need Reservations for Red Rock Canyon? Timed entry reservations are required from October 1 through May 31 between 8 AM and 5 PM. Reservations can be purchased in advance through Recreation.gov. From June 1 through September 30, no reservation is needed. The standard entry fee is $20 per vehicle, $10 for motorcycles, and $5 for pedestrians. America the Beautiful annual passes are accepted. Ryan Rose notes that the proximity to Red Rock Canyon is one of the most underrated perks of living in Spring Valley. While many Las Vegas neighborhoods require 30 to 45 minutes to reach the canyon, Spring Valley residents can be on the scenic drive in under half an hour. ## What to Expect at Red Rock Canyon The conservation area features dozens of hiking trails ranging from easy walks to challenging scrambles through sandstone canyons. Popular trails include Calico Tanks, Keystone Thrust, and Ice Box Canyon. Rock climbing, mountain biking, and wildlife viewing are also available throughout the area. The dramatic red and cream colored rock formations rise over 3,000 feet above the valley floor.
