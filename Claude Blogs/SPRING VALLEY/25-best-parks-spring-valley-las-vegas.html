Spring Valley is home to some of the best parks in the Las Vegas Valley, offering everything from sports fields and aquatic centers to hiking trails and dog parks. The neighborhood's park system is a major reason families and active residents choose to live here. ## What Are the Top Parks in Spring Valley? Desert Breeze Park is the standout, covering 119 developed acres at 8275 Spring Mountain Road. The park features soccer, baseball, football, and softball fields along with basketball courts, roller hockey rinks, a 20,000 square foot skate park, playgrounds, a walking path, and a dedicated dog park. The on site community center includes an indoor gym, and the aquatic center offers pools for lap swimming and family recreation. Desert Breeze also hosts annual events including the Extreme Thing Sports and Music Festival and the Bite of Las Vegas. Exploration Peak Park covers 80 acres and features a hiking trail that leads to a 2,846 foot summit with panoramic views of the Las Vegas Valley. The park also includes picnic areas, playgrounds, a replica western town, and an outdoor amphitheater. It is a popular destination for families, hikers, and dog owners. ## What Parks Are Nearby Spring Valley? Springs Preserve is a 180 acre cultural and natural attraction located just east of Spring Valley. The preserve features botanical gardens, museums, nature trails, and exhibits focused on the history and ecology of the Las Vegas Valley. It offers educational programming for all ages and serves as one of the most unique green spaces in the metro area.
