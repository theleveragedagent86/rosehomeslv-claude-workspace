Clifford J. Lawrence Junior High School is a public middle school serving students in grades 6 through 8 in the northern portion of Spring Valley, Las Vegas. The school operates under the Clark County School District and feeds into Spring Valley High School. ## What Grades and Programs Does Lawrence Junior High Offer? Lawrence Junior High serves students in grades 6 through 8 and provides the standard CCSD middle school curriculum along with elective courses in areas such as art, music, technology, and physical education. The school prepares students for the academic rigor of Spring Valley High School, which offers IB, AP, and dual enrollment programs at the high school level. Class offerings at the junior high level are designed to build foundational skills in math, science, English language arts, and social studies. Students can also participate in extracurricular activities including student council, academic competitions, and school sports. The transition from elementary to middle school is a key period, and Lawrence provides orientation programs to help incoming sixth graders adjust. ## Where Is Lawrence Junior High Located? Lawrence Junior High is situated in the northern section of Spring Valley, making it accessible to families living in the neighborhoods north of West Flamingo Road. The school serves a defined attendance zone within the CCSD boundary system, and families should verify their zoned school through the district's online tool before making a home purchase. Ryan Rose points out that the northern Spring Valley neighborhoods zoned for Lawrence Junior High tend to offer a mix of single family homes and townhomes at a range of price points. Proximity to this school is a factor for families with children approaching middle school age who want to avoid lengthy commutes. ## What High School Does Lawrence Junior High Feed Into? Lawrence Junior High feeds into Spring Valley High School, which holds a B+ rating from Niche and a 94% graduation rate. This feeder pattern is important for families planning ahead, since choosing a home zoned for Lawrence means the student will likely continue at Spring Valley High for grades 9 through 12. The continuity of the feeder pattern allows students to build peer relationships that carry through to high school.
