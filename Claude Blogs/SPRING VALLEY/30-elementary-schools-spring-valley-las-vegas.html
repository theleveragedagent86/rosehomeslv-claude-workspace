Spring Valley Las Vegas is home to more than a dozen public elementary schools operated by the Clark County School District, giving families multiple options for kindergarten through fifth grade education. The schools are distributed across the community's 33.4 square miles, so most families have a neighborhood campus within a short drive. ## What Elementary Schools Are in Spring Valley? Spring Valley's elementary schools include Bendorf Elementary, Roger M. Bryan Elementary, M.J. Christensen Elementary, P.A. Diskin Elementary, Keith C. and Karen W. Hayes Elementary, Charles and Phyllis Frias Elementary, Clarence Piggott Elementary, Robert L. Forbuss Elementary, and Carolyn S. Reedom Elementary. All operate under CCSD and serve students in kindergarten through fifth grade. Each school follows the CCSD core curriculum in reading, math, science, and social studies. Several campuses offer additional programs such as gifted and talented education, Title I services, and English language learner support. Class sizes, staffing levels, and available resources can vary by campus, so visiting individual schools is the best way for families to evaluate fit. ## How Do Elementary School Zones Work in Spring Valley? CCSD assigns students to elementary schools based on home address, and attendance zone boundaries can shift from year to year. A home on one side of a street may be zoned for a different school than the home across from it. Ryan Rose emphasizes that confirming the current attendance zone through the CCSD zoning tool is an essential step before purchasing a home in Spring Valley. The elementary schools in Spring Valley feed into three middle school options: Clifford J. Lawrence Junior High, Faiss Middle School, and Victoria Fertitta Middle School. From there, students typically continue to Spring Valley High School. Understanding this full pipeline from elementary through high school helps families plan for the long term. ## Which Elementary Schools Feed Into Spring Valley High School? Bendorf, Bryan, Christensen, Diskin, and Hayes elementary schools are among the campuses that feed into the Spring Valley High School pipeline. Other elementary schools in the area, including Frias, Piggott, Forbuss, and Reedom, serve Spring Valley neighborhoods but may feed into different middle and high school combinations depending on their specific zone.
