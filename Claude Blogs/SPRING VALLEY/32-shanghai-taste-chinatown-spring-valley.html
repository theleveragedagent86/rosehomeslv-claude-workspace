ShangHai Taste is widely considered the best soup dumpling restaurant in Las Vegas, drawing locals and visitors alike to its original location at 4266 West Spring Mountain Road in the Spring Valley Chinatown corridor. Chef Jimmy Li and his team produce over 3,000 hand folded xiao long bao daily, earning a James Beard Award semifinalist nomination for Best Chef: Southwest. ## What Makes ShangHai Taste Special? ShangHai Taste specializes in xiao long bao (soup dumplings) and sheng jian bao (pan fried soup dumplings) prepared in an open display kitchen where diners can watch each dumpling being folded by hand. Every xiao long bao features the correct number of twists at the top, Shanghai style, with a slightly thicker wrapper designed to hold a generous amount of hot pork broth inside. The restaurant serves these dumplings at a pace that few competitors can match. Beyond dumplings, the menu covers a full range of Shanghainese cuisine including scallion oil noodles, lion's head meatballs, and braised pork belly. Chef Jimmy Li opened the original Chinatown location in late 2019 alongside business partner Joe Muscaglione, and the restaurant quickly earned a national reputation. A second Las Vegas location opened near Rainbow and Windmill in 2024, expanding access for southwest valley residents. ## Is ShangHai Taste Worth the Wait? Lines at the original Chinatown location are common, especially on weekends when waits can stretch past 60 minutes during peak hours. The restaurant does not accept reservations at the Spring Mountain Road location, so plan to arrive early or visit during weekday lunch for shorter waits. Most dishes fall in the $12 to $20 range, making it one of the best value dining experiences in the Las Vegas Valley. The Infatuation calls ShangHai Taste "an undisputed local legend" and "a portal into soup dumpling heaven." That reputation continues to grow as Chef Li expands his restaurant group, which now includes locations in Texas alongside the two Las Vegas shops. ## Why Spring Valley Residents Love ShangHai Taste Ryan Rose points to ShangHai Taste as a prime example of why Spring Valley's Chinatown corridor adds real lifestyle value for residents. Having a nationally recognized, James Beard caliber restaurant within a short drive of your home is a perk that most suburban communities simply cannot offer. The ongoing expansion of the restaurant speaks to the strength of the Chinatown dining scene.
