Chengdu Taste brings authentic Sichuan cuisine to the Spring Valley Chinatown corridor, earning a reputation as one of the best Sichuan restaurants in the United States. Located at 3950 Schiff Drive just off Spring Mountain Road, the restaurant specializes in the bold, layered flavors of Chengdu style cooking that balance heat, numbing spice, and aromatic depth. ## What Should You Order at Chengdu Taste? The menu at Chengdu Taste spans over 100 dishes rooted in traditional Sichuan cooking techniques. The Wonton in Spicy Chili Sauce is widely considered one of the best renditions available in America, delivering a precise balance of spicy, sweet, and tingly sensations in every bite. Other standout dishes include Mung Bean Jelly Noodle in Chili Sauce, Dan Dan Noodles, and the Diced Rabbit with Younger Sister's Secret Recipe. Chengdu style cooking differs from other Sichuan regional styles in its cleaner, lighter approach to heat. Peppers are used to highlight and enhance flavors rather than overwhelm them, creating dishes that showcase complexity rather than pure fire. Portions are generous, and most entrees fall between $13 and $18, making it easy to order several dishes for the table and share family style. ## How Spicy Is Chengdu Taste? The restaurant offers varying spice levels across its menu, so diners who prefer milder dishes can find options alongside the fiery specialties. Dishes marked with chili symbols indicate increasing heat levels, and the staff is generally helpful in guiding newcomers toward appropriate choices. The Sichuan peppercorn produces a distinctive numbing sensation called "ma la" that is central to the cuisine and different from the sharp heat of standard chili peppers. First time visitors should plan for an adventurous meal and consider ordering one milder dish alongside spicier selections. The Spring Onion Chicken in Pepper Sauce offers a good entry point, while the Boiled Fish in Chili Sauce delivers a more intense experience for heat seekers. ## Why Ryan Rose Highlights Chengdu Taste Ryan Rose sees Chengdu Taste as another example of the nationally recognized dining that Spring Valley residents enjoy at their doorstep. The restaurant draws visitors from across the valley and beyond, yet it sits in a modest strip mall setting that keeps prices accessible for everyday dining.
