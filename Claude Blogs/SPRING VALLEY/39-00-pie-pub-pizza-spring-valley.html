00 Pie and Pub, also known as Double Zero, is a Neo Neapolitan pizzeria and pub at 3853 Spring Mountain Road in Spring Valley's Chinatown corridor. Led by World Pizza Champion Michael Vakneen, the restaurant earned a spot on the 50 Top Pizza list as one of the best pizzerias in the United States. ## What Makes 00 Pie and Pub Stand Out? Chef Michael Vakneen competed at the World Pizza Games at the Las Vegas Pizza Expo and took first place for the United States and second in the world in the non traditional category. He brought that competition caliber precision to 00 Pie and Pub when it opened in 2023. The restaurant uses "00" flour, an Italian classification known for producing a light, airy crust with the signature char of a high temperature oven. The menu features standout pies like the Short Rib All'Arancia, Salami Rossa, Burrata E Pesto, and Funghi. Beyond pizza, guests praise the Tokyo Wings and Wagyu Carpaccio as top appetizer picks. The concept blends elevated Italian cooking with a Japanese izakaya inspired atmosphere, creating a dining room that feels both polished and laid back. ## Where Does 00 Pie and Pub Rank Nationally? 50 Top Pizza, the respected global pizza guide based in Italy, placed Double Zero Pie and Pub at number 43 on its list of the top 50 pizzerias in the United States for 2025. The restaurant also appeared at number 45 on the 2024 list, marking back to back recognition. Chef Vakneen is the youngest chef ever selected for the 50 Top Pizza honors, reinforcing the restaurant's standing as a serious destination rather than a casual neighborhood spot. Ryan Rose notes that 00 Pie and Pub is part of a growing trend along Spring Mountain Road, where world class culinary talent is choosing the Chinatown corridor over Strip locations. The lower overhead and loyal local customer base make it an attractive fit for ambitious chefs. ## A Pizza Worth the Drive The lively atmosphere can get loud during peak hours, and the pub side of the concept delivers with a solid drink selection that pairs well with the food. Portions are generous and prices sit in the moderate range for the quality level. For Spring Valley residents, having a nationally ranked pizzeria within the neighborhood is another example of what makes the Chinatown corridor such a distinctive place to live.
