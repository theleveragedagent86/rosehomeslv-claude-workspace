Spring Valley is home to one of the most impressive concentrations of Asian restaurants in the western United States, anchored by the Chinatown corridor along Spring Mountain Road. With more than 200 Asian and international restaurants spanning over three miles and 20 plus strip malls, the selection here rivals major Chinatowns in cities like Los Angeles and San Francisco. ## What Are the Top Asian Restaurants in Spring Valley? ShangHai Taste on Spring Mountain Road has earned a reputation for serving the best soup dumplings in Las Vegas, with hand folded xiao long bao that draw long lines on weekends. Chengdu Taste, originally from the San Gabriel Valley in Los Angeles, brings fiery Sichuan cooking to Spring Valley with dishes like boiled fish in chili oil and dan dan noodles that have won national acclaim. Raku, a Japanese izakaya on Spring Mountain Road, has been one of the most critically celebrated restaurants in Las Vegas for over a decade. The late night charcoal grilled small plates and handmade tofu attract both local chefs and visiting food writers. Pho Kim Long rounds out the top tier with authentic Vietnamese pho served until 4:30 AM on weekends, while Izakaya Go provides a more casual Japanese dining option along the same corridor. ## What Cuisines Are Represented in Spring Valley's Chinatown? The diversity of the corridor extends well beyond Chinese and Japanese fare. HANU Korean BBQ offers an all you can eat menu with around 50 options where diners grill their own selections tableside. Hot N Juicy Crawfish brings Cajun style seafood boils to the mix. Mas Por Favor operates a quick serve Mexican counter in the front with a speakeasy cocktail bar hidden in the back. Ryan Rose highlights this culinary diversity as one of the strongest lifestyle advantages for Spring Valley homeowners. The corridor is nationally recognized as a dining destination, and residents benefit from having access to this range of cuisines within a short drive or even a walk from many Spring Valley neighborhoods. ## Why the Chinatown Corridor Keeps Growing New restaurants continue to open along Spring Mountain Road, drawn by the corridor's built in foot traffic and reputation among food enthusiasts. Chef driven concepts like 00 Pie and Pub and Partage have chosen Chinatown over Strip locations, signaling a shift in where top culinary talent wants to operate. For residents, this growth means the dining options only improve over time.
