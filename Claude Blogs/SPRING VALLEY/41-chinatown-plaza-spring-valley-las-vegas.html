Chinatown Plaza is a landmark shopping destination located on Spring Mountain Road in the heart of Spring Valley. The plaza anchors one of the most culturally vibrant corridors in Las Vegas, blending Asian retail, dining, and services under one roof. ## What Is Chinatown Plaza in Spring Valley? Chinatown Plaza opened in 1995 at 4205 Spring Mountain Road and spans roughly 85,000 square feet of retail space. The plaza is distinguished by its Tang Dynasty inspired architecture, including a prominent gateway that has become one of the most recognizable structures along the Spring Mountain corridor. Clark County officially designated the surrounding area as an Asian Pacific American Cultural Center in 1996, cementing its role as a cultural hub. The plaza is anchored by 99 Ranch Market, one of the largest Asian supermarket chains in the country. Shoppers can find a wide selection of fresh produce, seafood, meats, and specialty ingredients that serve the cooking traditions of Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean, Japanese, Filipino, and Thai cuisines. The market draws customers from across the Las Vegas Valley, not just Spring Valley residents. ## What Stores and Restaurants Are at Chinatown Plaza? Beyond 99 Ranch Market, Chinatown Plaza houses a mix of restaurants, bakeries, and specialty retailers. Notable tenants include Paris Baguette, Capital Seafood, 888 Korean BBQ, Diamond Bakery, and Noodle Pot. The plaza also features a variety of service businesses including travel agencies, herbal medicine shops, and beauty salons that cater to the local Asian community.
