Spring Valley offers one of the most diverse shopping landscapes in the Las Vegas Valley, with everything from international retail plazas to large scale power centers within minutes of most homes. The neighborhood's central location makes it easy to reach major retail destinations in every direction. ## What Are the Best Shopping Centers in Spring Valley? The Chinatown corridor along Spring Mountain Road is the most distinctive shopping area in Spring Valley. More than 20 strip malls line a three mile stretch, offering Asian grocery stores, specialty retailers, bakeries, restaurants, and service businesses. Chinatown Plaza at 4205 Spring Mountain Road anchors the district with its 85,000 square foot footprint and 99 Ranch Market. The Arroyo Market Square at 7200 Arroyo Crossing Parkway is a 940,000 square foot power center on the southern edge of Spring Valley. Anchored by Walmart, Sam's Club, Best Buy, Home Depot, and Marshalls, the center covers nearly every big box shopping need. Seafood City Supermarket adds an international grocery option, and dozens of restaurants line the perimeter. ## What Shopping Is Near Spring Valley? Town Square Las Vegas sits just east of Spring Valley on Las Vegas Boulevard at the I 15 and 215 Beltway interchange. The 100 acre open air center features over 115 stores and restaurants, an 18 screen AMC movie theater, and a children's park. Retailers include H&M, Whole Foods Market, and The Container Store, with dining options ranging from Brio Italian Grille to Lazy Dog Restaurant and Bar. Spring Valley Town Center at 4240 South Rainbow Boulevard provides a neighborhood scale shopping experience with restaurants, a UPS Store, and local service businesses. Downtown Summerlin, roughly five to ten minutes northwest, offers a premium open air retail and entertainment experience with national brands, local restaurants, and seasonal events. ## How Does Shopping Access Benefit Spring Valley Homebuyers?
