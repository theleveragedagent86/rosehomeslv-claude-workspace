Summer temperatures in Spring Valley routinely reach 100 degrees Fahrenheit and above from June through September. July and August are the peak months, with highs occasionally climbing past 110 degrees. Low humidity keeps the heat from feeling as oppressive as humid climates, but residents still plan outdoor activities for early morning or evening hours. Air conditioning is essential in every Spring Valley home, and most properties are equipped with central HVAC systems built for desert conditions. Pool homes are especially popular for families and individuals who want relief during the hottest months. Energy costs tend to spike in summer, so buyers should factor utility bills into their monthly budget. ## What Are Winters Like in Spring Valley Las Vegas? Winters in Spring Valley are mild by national standards, with daytime highs in the 50s and 60s and overnight lows dipping into the 30s and 40s. Snow is extremely rare in the valley floor, though residents can see snow capped mountains from their neighborhoods on colder days. The winter months from November through February are considered the best time for outdoor recreation. Annual rainfall in Spring Valley averages roughly 4 inches, spread across a handful of brief storm events.
