Spring Valley is one of the most ethnically diverse communities in the entire Las Vegas valley, earning an A+ diversity grade from Niche and reflecting a population where nearly one in three residents was born outside the United States. ## How Diverse Is Spring Valley Las Vegas? Spring Valley's demographic makeup sets it apart from most Las Vegas neighborhoods. Approximately 29% of residents are foreign born, contributing to a rich mix of cultures, languages, and traditions that shape the community's character. Niche rates Spring Valley with an A+ for diversity, placing it among the top ranked communities in Nevada for ethnic and cultural representation. This diversity is visible in everyday life, from the businesses along Spring Mountain Road to the variety of cultural events and community organizations throughout the area. Chinatown Plaza, which opened in 1995, anchored the growth of one of the most celebrated Asian dining and shopping corridors in the western United States. The neighborhood's cultural depth extends well beyond Asian cuisine, with Latin American, Middle Eastern, and European influences woven throughout local commerce. ## What Is the Population of Spring Valley? Spring Valley's population sits between 215,000 and 220,000 residents, making it one of the largest census designated places in the country. As an unincorporated community within Clark County, Spring Valley does not have its own city government, but its size rivals that of many midsize American cities. The population has grown steadily since Pardee Homes began developing the area in the mid 1970s.
