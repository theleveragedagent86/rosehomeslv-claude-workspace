# Relocating to Summerlin from California Relocating to Summerlin from California gives you access to a master planned community with no state income tax, significantly lower home prices than most California metros, and 294 sunny days per year in the western Las Vegas Valley. ## Why Are So Many Californians Moving to Summerlin? The combination of tax savings and housing affordability continues to drive California residents toward Summerlin and greater Las Vegas. Nevada has no state income tax, which translates to immediate savings for families and professionals who were paying California's rates of up to 13.3 percent. The median home price in Summerlin sits at $686,000 in 2026, which buys a spacious single family home with a yard, a considerable upgrade from what that budget delivers in Los Angeles, San Diego, or the Bay Area. ## How Does the Cost of Living Compare to California? Beyond the tax advantage, day to day expenses tend to be lower across the board. Average rent in Summerlin runs about $2,550 per month, and HOA fees in the community typically total $100 to $200 monthly. Groceries, utilities, and services are generally 10 to 20 percent less expensive than coastal California markets. For buyers looking at premium neighborhoods, Summerlin West offers newer construction at a median of $800,000, while guard gated communities like The Ridges and Red Rock Country Club range from $1 million to $5 million and above. ## What About Schools, Healthcare, and Daily Life? Summerlin's 26 schools consistently outperform Nevada state averages, with reading proficiency at 63 percent versus the state's 43 percent. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is located within the community for convenient healthcare access. The lifestyle transitions smoothly for Californians accustomed to outdoor living, with over 300 parks, 200 miles of trails, and Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area just 10 to 15 minutes away. ## Is the Commute and Location Convenient? One detail Ryan Rose highlights for buyers considering Summerlin is the community's strategic location. The Strip is a 20 minute drive, the airport is 25 to 30 minutes away, and the higher elevation on the western valley edge keeps summer temperatures 5 to 10 degrees cooler than central Las Vegas.
