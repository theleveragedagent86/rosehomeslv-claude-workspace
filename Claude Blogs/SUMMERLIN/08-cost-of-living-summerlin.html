# Cost of Living in Summerlin 2026 The cost of living in Summerlin in 2026 centers around a median home price of $686,000, average rent of $2,550 per month, and HOA fees typically ranging from $100 to $200 monthly, all supported by Nevada's zero state income tax. ## How Much Does Housing Cost in Summerlin? Housing is the largest expense for Summerlin residents, with options spanning a wide range. Condos and townhomes start between $300,000 and $500,000, making them the most accessible entry point. Single family homes fall in the $500,000 to $800,000 range, while premium properties run from $800,000 to $1.5 million. Guard gated luxury communities like Summit Club and The Ridges can exceed $5 million. Summerlin West, the newest section, carries a median of $800,000 reflecting newer construction and modern floor plans. ## What Are the Monthly Expenses Beyond a Mortgage? HOA fees in Summerlin include a master association fee of $55 to $65 per month plus a sub association fee of $50 to $150 per month, bringing the typical total to $100 to $200 monthly. These fees cover community trail maintenance, parks, and shared amenities. Renters can expect to pay around $2,550 per month on average. Utilities, groceries, and transportation costs remain lower than comparable communities in California or the Pacific Northwest, partly due to the absence of state income tax in Nevada. ## How Does Summerlin Compare to Other Las Vegas Communities? Summerlin's median sale price of $686,000 places it above the Las Vegas metro average but reflects the premium amenities and school quality that come with a Howard Hughes master planned community. Schools here score 45 percent math proficiency versus the state average of 31 percent.
