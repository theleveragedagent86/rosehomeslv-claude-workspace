# Pros and Cons of Living in Summerlin The pros of living in Summerlin include top rated schools, no state income tax, over 300 parks, and a master planned lifestyle, while the cons include desert heat that exceeds 100 degrees in summer and a median home price of $686,000 that sits above the Las Vegas average. ## What Are the Biggest Advantages of Living in Summerlin? Summerlin delivers a well organized community experience that few neighborhoods in Las Vegas can match. The 22,500 acre master plan includes 19 villages, over 200 miles of trails, 10 golf courses, and Downtown Summerlin for shopping and dining. Schools consistently outperform state averages with math proficiency at 45 percent versus 31 percent statewide. Nevada's lack of state income tax is a significant financial benefit, and the community sits just 10 to 15 minutes from Red Rock Canyon for hiking and outdoor recreation. ## What Are the Downsides of Living in Summerlin? Summer temperatures regularly reach 100 to 115 degrees from June through September, which limits outdoor activity during peak hours. The median home price of $686,000 puts Summerlin out of reach for some buyers, and HOA fees of $100 to $200 per month add to the ongoing cost. The drive to the Las Vegas Strip takes about 20 minutes, and airport access requires 25 to 30 minutes, which can feel long for frequent travelers. Water conservation restrictions are also part of desert living, and landscaping rules within HOA communities can feel restrictive to some homeowners. ## Is Summerlin Worth It Despite the Drawbacks? For most residents, the pros significantly outweigh the cons. Property values have appreciated 9.8 percent year over year, making homeownership here a strong investment. The community's higher elevation on the western valley edge keeps temperatures 5 to 10 degrees cooler than central Las Vegas. Healthcare is convenient with Summerlin Hospital Medical Center located within the community, and the area offers options for every life stage from family neighborhoods to 55 plus communities like Sun City Summerlin. Based on Ryan Rose's experience working with Summerlin buyers and sellers, the most common regret he hears is not moving sooner. The combination of safety, schools, and outdoor access creates a quality of life that keeps the community's population growing past 130,000 residents.
