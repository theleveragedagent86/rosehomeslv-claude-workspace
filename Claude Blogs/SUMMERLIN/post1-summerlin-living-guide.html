Summerlin is a 22,500 acre master planned community on the western edge of the Las Vegas Valley, offering over 130,000 residents an unmatched combination of parks, trails, top rated schools, and proximity to Red Rock Canyon. ## What Makes Living in Summerlin So Popular? Developed by the Howard Hughes Corporation since 1990, Summerlin consistently ranks among the best selling master planned communities in the country. The community spans three main sections: Summerlin North, Summerlin South, and Summerlin West, each containing distinct villages with their own character. Homes range from condos starting around $300,000 to ultra luxury estates in The Ridges and Summit Club exceeding $20 million. With roughly 5,000 acres of developable land remaining, Summerlin continues to grow while maintaining its reputation for quality. ## What Amenities Does Summerlin Offer Residents? Summerlin delivers more than 300 parks and over 200 miles of interconnected trails for walking, running, and cycling. Ten golf courses serve the community, including TPC Summerlin, home to the PGA Tour's Shriners Children's Open. Downtown Summerlin provides open air shopping, dining, and entertainment anchored by the Las Vegas Ballpark. Residents enjoy 294 sunny days per year and sit just 20 minutes from the Las Vegas Strip and roughly 25 to 30 minutes from Harry Reid International Airport. ## How Are Summerlin's Schools and Safety? Families choose Summerlin in large part for its schools. The area is served by 26 schools, with math proficiency at 45% compared to the 31% state average and reading proficiency at 63% versus 43% statewide. Vassiliadis Elementary ranks number one in the Clark County School District, while Sig Rogich Middle School holds a perfect 10 out of 10 on GreatSchools. Private options include The Meadows School, rated the top private K through 12 in Nevada with a 100% college acceptance rate. ## How Is Living in Summerlin Structured with HOAs? Summerlin operates on a two tier HOA system. Four Master Associations (North, South, West, and Centre) cover parks, trails, monuments, and community programming for roughly $55 to $65 per month. Sub associations handle neighborhood pools, architectural review, and CC&R enforcement for an additional $50 to $150 monthly. Ryan Rose frequently fields questions about Summerlin's HOA structure and explains that the total typically falls between $100 and $200 per month, which funds the high level of community maintenance residents expect. ## What Should You Know Before Choosing Summerlin? One detail that surprises newcomers is how different the villages feel from one another. Sun City Summerlin and Siena cater exclusively to the 55 plus crowd with resort style amenities, while Summerlin West villages like Reverence and Stonebridge attract younger families with newer construction and modern floor plans.
