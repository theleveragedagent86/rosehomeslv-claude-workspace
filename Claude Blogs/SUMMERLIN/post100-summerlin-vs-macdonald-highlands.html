## Summerlin vs MacDonald Highlands: Luxury Showdown When luxury buyers narrow their Las Vegas search to the top tier, Summerlin and MacDonald Highlands consistently make the short list. Both communities offer guard gated living, custom estate homesites, and access to world class amenities. But the scale, setting, and character of each are fundamentally different. Here is how they compare for buyers shopping at the highest end of the market. ## What Is MacDonald Highlands? MacDonald Highlands is an exclusive luxury community in Henderson, built into the McCullough Range foothills at elevations that provide panoramic views of the Las Vegas Strip, the surrounding mountains, and the desert floor. The centerpiece is DragonRidge Country Club, which features a Tom Fazio designed golf course, a full service clubhouse, fitness facilities, tennis courts, and resort style pools. Custom estates in MacDonald Highlands range from approximately $1 million to well over $10 million, with some of the most architecturally striking homes in the entire valley. ## How Does Summerlin's Luxury Compare? Summerlin's luxury inventory is concentrated in communities like The Ridges, the highest elevation guard gated enclave in Summerlin. The Ridges features ultra modern glass and steel architecture alongside contemporary desert designs, with custom homes starting around $2 million and reaching $10 million plus. Other Summerlin luxury options include The Summit, Olympia Ridge, and several Howard Hughes Corporation developed enclaves. The key difference is scale. Summerlin is a 22,500 acre master planned community with dozens of neighborhoods, extensive commercial infrastructure, and over 200 miles of trails. MacDonald Highlands is a boutique community of roughly 1,200 homesites. Buyers who want exclusivity and a smaller, more intimate residential enclave tend to gravitate toward MacDonald Highlands. Buyers who want luxury living within a larger community that has everything from grocery stores to medical offices within minutes tend to prefer Summerlin. ## How Do the Views and Settings Differ? MacDonald Highlands has a genuine edge in panoramic Strip views. The elevated positioning along the McCullough Range gives many homesites direct sightlines to the Las Vegas skyline, especially dramatic at night. Summerlin's luxury homes in The Ridges offer mountain and canyon views oriented toward Red Rock Canyon rather than the Strip. Both are stunning, but the view profiles are quite different. ## What About Location and Access? Summerlin is closer to Red Rock Canyon (10 to 20 minutes) and offers quicker access to the extensive retail and dining options along the 215 beltway corridor. MacDonald Highlands is closer to Harry Reid International Airport and Lake Mead, with Henderson's dining and shopping scene along St. Rose Parkway and the Galleria at Sunset nearby. ## What Is the Community Experience Like? MacDonald Highlands revolves around DragonRidge Country Club. Social life, fitness, dining, and golf all center on the club. It is an intimate, country club driven lifestyle. Summerlin's luxury residents have access to the broader Summerlin community amenities, including Downtown Summerlin, TPC Las Vegas, and the full network of parks and recreation facilities. As Ryan Rose with Rose Homes LV describes it, MacDonald Highlands is for the buyer who wants a private hilltop estate with Strip views and a country club at the center of daily life. Summerlin's luxury enclaves are for the buyer who wants that same caliber of home inside a full scale community with everything within reach. ## Exploring Luxury Options?
