La Strega Summerlin: Full Restaurant Review

Finding genuinely excellent Italian food outside the Strip corridor is not always easy in Las Vegas. La Strega, tucked into the Town Center area of Summerlin, has been proving since 2019 that it belongs in any conversation about the best Italian dining in the valley. The restaurant brings a coastal Italian perspective to a neighborhood that had been underserved in that category for years.

The Chef Behind La Strega

Chef Gina Marinelli is the creative force driving La Strega's kitchen. Her training includes Le Cordon Bleu along with time under some of the most respected names in the industry, including Michael Mina, Shawn McClain, and Scott Conant. That combination of classical technique and modern mentorship shows up clearly on the plate. Marinelli's approach leans heavily on fresh seafood and handmade pastas, drawing from coastal Italian traditions while incorporating her own perspective.

The result is a menu that feels personal rather than formulaic. Every dish reflects real skill and intentional sourcing, which is why diners keep coming back.

What to Order at La Strega

The Maine Lobster Spaghetti is the signature dish and the one that shows up most frequently in reviews. It delivers exactly what it promises: generous portions of lobster over perfectly cooked pasta with a sauce that balances richness and brightness. The Cacio e Pepe is another standout, executing a deceptively simple Roman classic with precision that separates it from lesser versions around town.

Beyond pasta, the Grilled Octopus appetizer is consistently excellent, and the Spicy Pig pizza offers something unexpected for guests who want to explore beyond the Italian fine dining side of the menu. The wine list complements the food well without overwhelming with too many choices.

The Dining Experience

La Strega sits at 3555 S Town Center Drive, Suite 105, in the 89135 zip code. The atmosphere strikes a balance between polished and approachable. It works equally well for a date night or a celebratory dinner with family. With over 2,127 OpenTable reviews, the restaurant has built a loyal following that extends well beyond Summerlin itself.

Pricing falls in the moderate range for the quality delivered. It is not a casual weeknight spot for most budgets, but it offers strong value compared to comparable Italian restaurants on the Strip.
