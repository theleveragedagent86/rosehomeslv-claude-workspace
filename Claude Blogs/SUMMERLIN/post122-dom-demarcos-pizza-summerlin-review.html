Dom DeMarco's Pizzeria Summerlin Review

Brooklyn pizza in the middle of the Mojave Desert sounds unlikely, but Dom DeMarco's Pizzeria has been pulling it off since 2011. Located at 9785 W Charleston Blvd, this Summerlin restaurant carries the legacy of Di Fara Pizza, the Brooklyn institution where Domenico DeMarco hand crafted pies for more than 55 years. That lineage is not just marketing. It shows up in every slice.

When clients ask Ryan Rose about the food scene in Summerlin, Dom DeMarco's consistently comes up as a neighborhood favorite that locals are genuinely passionate about.

The Di Fara Legacy

Domenico DeMarco opened Di Fara Pizza in Brooklyn in 1965 and built it into one of the most respected pizzerias in the country. The Las Vegas location was established through a partnership that brought those original Brooklyn recipes to the west side. The connection is authentic, and the recipes have been preserved rather than watered down for a different market.

That commitment to the original approach is what separates Dom DeMarco's from the many pizza spots that claim New York roots without delivering New York quality. The dough, the sauce, and the attention to fundamentals all trace back to the Di Fara kitchen.

Source: domdemarcos.com

The Pizza and Menu Highlights

Dom DeMarco's offers three distinct pizza styles: Neapolitan, New York, and Sicilian square. Having all three under one roof is unusual and lets diners compare styles side by side. The New York slices deliver the foldable, crispy edged experience that transplants from the East Coast spend years searching for in Las Vegas. The Sicilian square pies bring a thicker, focaccia style base with a satisfying crunch.

Beyond pizza, the menu covers Italian American staples including pasta, wings, and salads. The wings and pasta have both earned local recognition, and the daily happy hour makes the already reasonable $$ price point even more accessible. Dom DeMarco's has been voted best pizza, best pasta, best wings, best happy hour, and best outdoor dining by local publications and polls.

Source: domdemarcos.com, Yelp, OpenTable

The Experience and Reputation

With 1,537 Yelp reviews and a 4.6 star rating on OpenTable, Dom DeMarco's has built one of the stronger review profiles of any restaurant in the Summerlin area. That kind of volume and consistency over more than a decade speaks to genuine staying power rather than initial hype.

The space works for families, casual dinners, and takeout nights equally well. Outdoor dining adds an option for cooler evenings, and the daily happy hour pulls in a regular crowd of locals who treat the place as a neighborhood gathering spot.
