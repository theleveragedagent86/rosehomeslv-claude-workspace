Harlo Steakhouse Summerlin Review

Summerlin has had strong steakhouse options for years, but most of them have lived inside casino properties. Harlo Steakhouse changes that equation. Located in Downtown Summerlin and led by Chef Gina Marinelli, the same chef behind La Strega, Harlo brings a standalone, chef driven steakhouse to the west side that competes with anything the Strip has to offer.

For buyers evaluating Summerlin as a place to live, restaurants like Harlo signal that the neighborhood's dining scene has matured well beyond the chain restaurant phase. Ryan Rose regularly highlights this evolution when working with clients who value culinary quality close to home.

Chef Gina Marinelli's Second Act

Gina Marinelli already proved herself with La Strega, building one of the most acclaimed Italian restaurants in the Las Vegas valley. With Harlo, she applies the same philosophy of meticulous sourcing and precise execution to the steakhouse format. The transition makes sense when you understand her background: classical training at Le Cordon Bleu combined with mentorship under chefs like Michael Mina and Scott Conant gave her the range to work across cuisines with confidence.

The result is a steakhouse that feels intentional rather than formulaic. Where many steakhouses rely on a predictable template of dark wood, massive portions, and a perfunctory wine list, Harlo takes a more considered approach to every element of the experience.

The Beef and the Menu

The beef program at Harlo is built on meticulous sourcing, with cuts selected for quality and consistency rather than simply checking the usual boxes. The menu treats steak as the centerpiece without neglecting the supporting cast. Craft cocktails are a genuine strength, designed to complement the richness of the food rather than compete with it.

Pricing falls in the $$$ to $$$$ range, positioning Harlo as a celebration and special occasion restaurant. That said, the cocktail program and appetizer menu create options for guests who want to experience the restaurant without committing to a full steak dinner. The 4.6 star rating on OpenTable reflects a dining room that is delivering consistently at a high level.

Source: harlosteak.com, OpenTable

The Downtown Summerlin Setting

Harlo's location within Downtown Summerlin places it in the center of the area's most active retail and dining district. The neighborhood setting means you can pair dinner with shopping, a movie, or a walk through the plaza before or after your meal. For Summerlin residents, it removes the need to drive to the Strip or a casino property for an upscale steak dinner.

The atmosphere leans upscale without crossing into stuffy territory. It reads as polished and adult, making it a natural fit for anniversaries, business dinners, and milestone celebrations.

Where Harlo Fits in the Summerlin Steakhouse Landscape

Harlo competes directly with the established steakhouse options at Red Rock Casino, including T Bones Chophouse, and with Echo and Rig in nearby Tivoli Village. What sets Harlo apart is the chef driven identity and the standalone neighborhood location. It adds real depth to the best steakhouses in Summerlin conversation.
