## Nittaya's Secret Kitchen Near Summerlin: Thai Restaurant Review Summerlin residents looking for authentic Thai food beyond the usual takeout spots should know about Nittaya's Secret Kitchen, a local favorite tucked into the Rampart Commons shopping area just minutes from the heart of Summerlin. ## What Makes Nittaya's Secret Kitchen Stand Out? Nittaya's has built a loyal following among west side locals by focusing on dishes that go well beyond pad thai and green curry. The menu leans into regional Thai recipes that most restaurants in Las Vegas simply do not offer. Nem khao, a crispy rice salad that originated in Laos and northeastern Thailand, is one of the standout appetizers. The dish combines crumbled fried rice balls with fresh herbs, peanuts, lime, and a tangy dressing that balances texture and flavor in a way that keeps regulars coming back. Siamese corn fritters are another signature item. These golden bites deliver a sweet and savory crunch that works perfectly as a shared starter. The stuffed chicken wings, deboned and packed with a seasoned filling, showcase the kind of careful preparation that sets Nittaya's apart from more casual Thai spots in the valley. ## How Is the Overall Dining Experience? The atmosphere at Nittaya's leans casual and comfortable rather than flashy. This is a neighborhood restaurant where the focus stays on the food, not the decor. Pricing falls in the moderate range, making it an easy weeknight option for Summerlin families or a solid pick for a relaxed dinner with friends. The restaurant carries a 4.5 star average across major review platforms, which speaks to the consistency of the kitchen. Service tends to be friendly and knowledgeable, especially when it comes to guiding first time visitors through the more adventurous parts of the menu. If you are new to Thai cuisine beyond the basics, the staff can steer you toward dishes that match your comfort level while still introducing something new. ## Where Is Nittaya's Located Relative to Summerlin? Nittaya's Secret Kitchen sits in the Rampart Commons area, which puts it right along the border of Summerlin near Rampart Boulevard. For residents in Summerlin South villages like The Willows, The Trails, and The Arbors, it is a quick drive. Even from Summerlin West and the newer communities, you are looking at roughly 10 to 15 minutes depending on traffic. ## Why Ryan Rose Recommends This Spot As a Summerlin real estate agent, Ryan Rose with Rose Homes LV frequently tells clients that the dining scene is one of the underrated perks of living on the west side.
