## The Gardens Village Summerlin: Neighborhood Guide The Gardens is one of the smaller, quieter villages in Summerlin South, offering an established residential feel with tree lined streets and a more intimate sense of community. For buyers who want the benefits of the Summerlin master plan without the scale of the larger villages, The Gardens delivers a neighborhood experience that feels personal and well maintained. ## What Sub Communities Are in The Gardens? The Gardens is made up of eight sub communities: Chelsea Gardens, Country Gardens, Garden Glen, Garden Terrace, Glenleigh Gardens, Ivy Glen, Rosemont, and Wisteria Hills. The smaller number of neighborhoods compared to villages like The Willows or The Arbors contributes to the quieter, more residential atmosphere that defines The Gardens. Each sub community offers a slightly different mix of home styles, but the overall character of the village leans toward single family residences on well kept lots. Mature trees and established landscaping are consistent throughout, giving the area a sense of permanence that newer construction phases in Summerlin West have not yet developed. ## What Is the Lifestyle Like in The Gardens? Living in The Gardens means living in one of the more relaxed corners of Summerlin. The streets are quieter, the lots tend to be generous, and the pace feels a step removed from the busier commercial corridors. This makes The Gardens particularly attractive to families, retirees, and anyone who values a peaceful residential setting over proximity to nightlife or high energy retail. Residents have access to the Summerlin trail system, nearby community parks, and the broader Summerlin amenity package. The village sits within comfortable reach of shopping and dining along Charleston Boulevard and Town Center Drive, so daily errands and restaurant options are never far away. ## Where Is The Gardens Within Summerlin? The Gardens is located in the Summerlin South portion of the master plan. It neighbors other established villages and benefits from the infrastructure and mature development that characterize this part of Summerlin. Access to the 215 Beltway and major cross streets like Charleston and Flamingo makes commuting and getting around the valley straightforward. The proximity to Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is another advantage. Weekend hikes, scenic drives, and outdoor recreation are all just a few minutes from the front door. ## What Should Buyers Consider? Like other established Summerlin villages, homes in The Gardens were primarily built in the late 1990s and early 2000s. Buyers should plan for inspections that focus on the age of roofing, HVAC systems, and plumbing. The upside is that you are buying into a proven neighborhood with a track record of strong property values and well managed HOA oversight. The combination of the sub community HOA and the Summerlin master association fee applies here, just as it does throughout the master plan.
