The Crossing Village Summerlin: Neighborhood Guide

The Crossing sits at the heart of Summerlin North, a village defined by its central location and family friendly orientation. Positioned within the 89144 zip code, The Crossing provides the kind of balanced living that appeals to households looking for quality homes, strong schools, and convenient access to everything Summerlin offers. As Ryan Rose, a Summerlin focused Realtor, notes, The Crossing is one of those villages that consistently attracts families because the location and the housing stock both deliver on what matters most to them.

13 Sub Communities Across The Crossing

The Crossing is composed of 13 sub communities: Aspen Glen, Crimson Ridge, Discovery Hills, Discovery Pointe, Highland Hills, Pacific Crest, Red Bluffs, San Marino, Sunset Mesa, Sycamore Ridge, Vintage, West Hills, and Westridge. The names alone suggest the variety of terrain and positioning within the village. Some neighborhoods sit at slightly higher elevations with views, while others occupy the more level interior portions of the village.

The 89144 zip code carries a median home price of approximately $575,000, placing The Crossing in the mid to upper range of Summerlin pricing. This reflects the quality of the housing stock, the desirability of the location, and the strong demand from families and professionals who want to live in a well established but not aging part of Summerlin North. Homes here range from traditional single family residences in neighborhoods like Highland Hills and West Hills to updated properties in Pacific Crest and Sycamore Ridge.

A Village Built for Families

The Crossing earned its reputation as a family village through a combination of factors that go beyond just the homes. The schools serving this area are among Summerlin's most sought after, and the village's central positioning means that students at various grade levels have access to quality campuses without long commutes. Parks, walking paths, and the broader Summerlin trail network are woven through and around the village, giving residents outdoor recreation options right outside their doors.

The family orientation also shows up in the way the neighborhoods are designed. Streets are laid out for residential living rather than through traffic. Cul de sacs are common. The overall density feels comfortable without being cramped, and the homes offer the three, four, and five bedroom configurations that growing families need.

Source: teamcarver.com

Central Location Benefits

One of The Crossing's greatest assets is its position within Summerlin. The village is not on the far edge of the community, which means that access to Downtown Summerlin, major retail corridors, medical facilities, and the 215 Beltway is straightforward. Commuters heading to the Strip, downtown Las Vegas, or the Henderson business corridor can reach those destinations without the extended drive times that come with living in Summerlin's western reaches.

Who Thrives in The Crossing

The Crossing serves families, professionals, and buyers who want a centrally located Summerlin North address with strong housing options in the $575,000 median range. It is a village that rewards buyers who prioritize location and lifestyle over novelty.
