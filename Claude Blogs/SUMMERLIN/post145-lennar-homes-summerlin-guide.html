Lennar Homes in Summerlin: Builder Guide

Lennar brings four active communities to Summerlin's new home market with two features that no other builder in the community can match: the Everything's Included pricing model and the Next Gen home within a home concept. These two programs address the biggest frustrations buyers typically encounter during new construction purchases, namely surprise upgrade costs and the challenge of housing extended family members under one roof.

Las Vegas Realtor Ryan Rose works with buyers across every Summerlin builder and considers Lennar the strongest option for families navigating multi generational housing needs.

Everything's Included

Lennar's Everything's Included model means that features other builders list as upgrades come standard in the base price. Granite or quartz countertops, stainless steel appliances, upgraded flooring, smart home technology, and enhanced fixture packages are included from the start. The result is a purchase experience where the price you see at the model home is much closer to the price you pay at closing.

This approach eliminates one of the most common sources of buyer stress in new construction: the design center visit where a $500,000 base price quietly becomes $580,000 after selecting the finishes that made the model home look appealing. With Lennar, those finishes are already in the number. For buyers comparing Summerlin home prices across builders, the Everything's Included model makes Lennar's pricing more transparent than competitors whose base prices exclude desirable features.

Next Gen: The Home Within a Home

Lennar's Next Gen floor plans include a fully self contained living suite attached to the main home. This suite has its own separate entrance, kitchenette, living area, bedroom, and full bathroom. It functions as an independent apartment while remaining connected to the primary residence through an interior door that can be opened or closed depending on the family's preference.

The Next Gen concept was designed for multi generational families, and it solves a problem that conventional floor plans cannot. Parents or in laws who move in can maintain their independence, prepare their own meals, receive their own guests, and keep their own schedule without sharing the main home's common spaces. At the same time, family members are steps away rather than across town.

This product type has become increasingly popular in Las Vegas, where families relocating from states with higher costs of living often bring parents along rather than maintaining two separate households. The economics make sense, and the Next Gen layout ensures the arrangement works socially as well as financially.

Source: lennar.com, neighborhoodsinlasvegas.com

Who Should Consider Lennar

Lennar is the right builder for buyers who want pricing clarity and families who need space for parents or adult children. The Everything's Included model particularly benefits first time buyers who may not have the budget flexibility to absorb tens of thousands in design center upgrades. The Next Gen layout suits families with aging parents, boomerang adult children, or anyone who wants rental income potential from a purpose built suite within their primary residence.

Buyers who prioritize deep customization over included features may find Lennar's approach limiting, since the everything included model reduces the number of individual choices available. That tradeoff is intentional and is part of what keeps the pricing straightforward.
