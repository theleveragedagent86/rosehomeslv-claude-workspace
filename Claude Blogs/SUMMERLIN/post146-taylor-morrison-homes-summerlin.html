Taylor Morrison has entered the Summerlin market with Esplanade at Red Rock, a community that brings one of the nation's largest homebuilders into the master planned community's western expansion. With approximately 400 homes planned and pricing starting from $800K, this is a significant addition to the 2026 new construction landscape in Summerlin.

Esplanade at Red Rock: What to Expect

Sales for Esplanade at Red Rock are expected to open in Q2 2026, making this a timely opportunity for buyers who want to get in early. The community is positioned in Summerlin's western corridor, which offers proximity to Red Rock Canyon and the natural desert landscape that draws so many buyers to this side of the valley.

Taylor Morrison is known nationally for two things that matter in the Summerlin market: design flexibility and energy efficiency. Their approach to new construction gives buyers more choices in layout, finishes, and personalization than many production builders offer. For buyers who have felt limited by the standard option sheets at other Summerlin communities, that flexibility is a meaningful differentiator.

The 400 home buildout means this will be a sizable community, not a boutique enclave. Buyers should expect a phased release of homesites with multiple floor plan options at various price points above the $800K entry.

Energy Efficiency and Construction Quality

Taylor Morrison has built its reputation in part on energy performance. Their homes typically include advanced insulation packages, high efficiency HVAC systems, and construction techniques designed to minimize energy waste. In a climate like Las Vegas, where summer cooling costs can push electric bills well above $300 per month in older homes, that efficiency translates to real savings.

The builder also incorporates solar ready design into many of their floor plans, which aligns with the broader trend toward solar adoption in Summerlin. Even if buyers do not install panels immediately, having the infrastructure in place reduces the cost and complexity of adding them later.

For buyers weighing new construction against resale inventory, the energy performance of a Taylor Morrison home is worth factoring into the total cost of ownership. A newer, tighter building envelope paired with modern mechanical systems can offset hundreds of dollars per month in utility costs compared to homes built in the early 2000s.

Pricing and Market Position

Starting from $800K, Esplanade at Red Rock sits in the upper middle of the Summerlin pricing spectrum. This positions it above entry level new construction but below the guard gated luxury tier. Buyers in this range are typically move up purchasers, relocating professionals, or families who want new construction without crossing into the seven figure range.

Keep in mind that base pricing does not include lot premiums, structural options, or design center upgrades. It is common for the final purchase price to land 10 to 20 percent above the advertised starting point once buyers make their selections. Factor in HOA fees and potential SID/LID assessments when building your budget.
