SNWA Water Restrictions and What They Mean for Your Yard

The Southern Nevada Water Authority enforces mandatory watering schedules that every Summerlin homeowner needs to understand. Outdoor watering is limited to assigned days based on your address, typically one to three days per week depending on the season. Watering on Sundays is prohibited year round. Violations can result in fines, and the SNWA actively monitors compliance through their water waste investigation program.

These restrictions are the primary reason desert adapted landscaping has become the standard in Summerlin. Lush grass lawns that require daily watering are increasingly impractical and expensive to maintain. Many homeowners are converting existing grass areas to xeriscaping or artificial turf, and the SNWA offers rebates for grass removal that can offset a significant portion of the conversion cost.

Source: Southern Nevada Water Authority (snwa.com), SNWA Water Smart Landscapes program

Popular Landscaping Services in Summerlin

Desert landscaping specialists in the Summerlin area typically offer several core services that address the unique needs of the local climate. Xeriscaping design and installation is the most requested service, involving the use of drought tolerant plants, decorative rock, and efficient drip irrigation systems to create attractive yards that use minimal water.

Artificial turf installation has surged in popularity, especially for backyards and pet areas. Modern synthetic turf looks remarkably natural and eliminates watering entirely. Quality installation with proper drainage and infill typically runs between $8 and $14 per square foot, and the long term savings on water bills make it a sound investment for many homeowners.

Drip irrigation and tree trimming round out the most requested services. Drip systems deliver water directly to plant roots with minimal waste, while regular tree pruning keeps desert species like mesquite and palo verde healthy and reduces storm damage risk.

HOA Landscaping Standards

Summerlin HOAs enforce landscaping standards that vary by village and sub association. Common requirements include maintaining front yard plantings in healthy condition, keeping rock and gravel beds free of weeds, and ensuring that any dead or dying plant material is replaced promptly. Some communities have specific approved plant lists or restrictions on the types of ground cover allowed.

A landscaping company familiar with Summerlin HOA requirements can help you avoid violation notices and keep your property in good standing. Many landscapers offer monthly or quarterly maintenance plans that include weed control, irrigation checks, pruning, and seasonal plant replacement.

Choosing the Right Landscaper

When selecting a landscaping company in Summerlin, verify that they hold a valid Nevada contractor's license for landscaping work. Ask for references from other Summerlin homeowners and review their portfolio of completed desert landscaping projects. A company that primarily works in desert environments will deliver better results than a general contractor unfamiliar with local climate challenges.
