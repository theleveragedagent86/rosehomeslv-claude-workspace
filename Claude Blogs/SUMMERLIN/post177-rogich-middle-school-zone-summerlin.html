Sig Rogich Middle School Zone: Which Summerlin Neighborhoods Feed In

Sig Rogich Middle School holds the top spot among all middle schools in Clark County and ranks number 12 in the state of Nevada. For families already living in Summerlin or planning a move, getting into the Rogich zone means access to a caliber of public education that is difficult to find anywhere else in the Las Vegas valley.

As a Summerlin real estate agent, Ryan Rose regularly helps families map out which neighborhoods feed into Rogich. The zone covers several of Summerlin's most popular villages, and understanding how the elementary feeder pattern works is the key to landing in the right spot.

Which Elementary Schools Feed Into Rogich

Three elementary schools serve as primary feeders into Sig Rogich Middle School. The most notable is Vassiliadis Elementary, which holds the number one ranking in Clark County. Students from Vassiliadis make up a significant portion of the Rogich student body, and the academic standards established at the elementary level carry through into middle school.

Givens Elementary and Staton Elementary also feed into Rogich. Givens serves portions of The Vistas and surrounding areas, while Staton draws from parts of The Arbors and adjacent neighborhoods. All three feeder schools are well regarded within the CCSD system, which contributes to the overall strength of the Rogich student population.

Source: ccsd.net/schools/zoning/search/, publicschoolreview.com, billysellsvegas.com

Summerlin Villages in the Rogich Zone

The Paseos is the village most directly associated with the Rogich zone. Families who buy in The Paseos benefit from the full premium feeder pattern: Vassiliadis Elementary to Rogich Middle to Palo Verde High. This three school sequence is widely recognized as the strongest public school pipeline in Southern Nevada.

Parts of The Arbors also fall within the Rogich attendance boundary. The Arbors is one of Summerlin's most established villages, with mature landscaping, larger lots, and a range of home styles from the 1990s through the 2000s. Homes in The Arbors that are zoned for Staton Elementary feed into Rogich at the middle school level.

Sections of Summerlin West round out the Rogich zone. These areas include newer construction and developing subdivisions along the western corridor of the community. As always, exact boundaries can shift at the street level, so checking the CCSD zoning lookup tool before making an offer is a step that should never be skipped.

The Rogich to Palo Verde Connection

After completing middle school at Rogich, students feed into Palo Verde High School, ranked in the top 20 in Nevada and in the top 20 percent nationally. This continuity from a top ranked middle school into a strong high school is a major factor in why the Rogich zone commands a price premium. The relationship between school quality and home values is especially pronounced in these neighborhoods.
