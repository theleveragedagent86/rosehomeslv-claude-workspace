Kitchen and Bathroom Remodel Costs in Summerlin

Kitchens and bathrooms drive more purchasing decisions than almost any other feature in a home. In Summerlin, where many properties were built in the late 1990s through the 2010s, a growing number of homeowners are updating these spaces to match current design standards and improve functionality. Whether you are remodeling to enjoy the upgrades yourself or preparing a home for sale, understanding realistic costs helps you plan smartly and avoid budget surprises.

Kitchen Remodel Costs

A kitchen remodel in the Summerlin area ranges from $20,000 to $55,000 depending on scope and material selections. At the lower end, that budget covers cabinet refacing, new countertops, updated hardware, a fresh backsplash, and replacement of dated appliances. At the higher end, you are looking at a full gut renovation with custom cabinetry, quartz or natural stone countertops, new flooring, upgraded lighting, and premium appliances.

The most common mid range kitchen remodel in Summerlin falls between $30,000 and $40,000. This typically includes new shaker style cabinets, quartz countertops, a tile backsplash, stainless steel appliances, and updated lighting. That level of investment brings a dated kitchen fully into the current market standard without venturing into luxury territory.

Labor costs in Las Vegas have increased in recent years, and contractor availability can affect pricing and timelines. Getting multiple bids and checking references is especially important in a market where demand for skilled tradespeople remains high.

Bathroom Remodel Costs

Bathroom remodels in Summerlin range from $3,500 for a basic refresh to $30,000 or more for a full primary bathroom renovation. A basic update includes new fixtures, a fresh vanity, updated lighting, and paint. Moving into the $10,000 to $20,000 range adds tile work, a new shower enclosure, upgraded plumbing fixtures, and potentially a layout reconfiguration.

Primary bathroom renovations at the higher end often include features like a freestanding tub, frameless glass shower, dual vanities with stone countertops, heated flooring, and custom tilework. These upgrades are increasingly common in Summerlin homes priced above $600,000, where buyers expect a finished, move in ready primary suite.

Guest and secondary bathrooms cost less to update and often deliver strong visual impact for a modest investment. Replacing a dated vanity, adding a framed mirror, and installing new tile flooring can refresh a secondary bathroom for $5,000 to $8,000.

Remodeling and Resale Value

Kitchen and bathroom remodels consistently rank among the highest return on investment improvements for homes in the Las Vegas market. A well executed mid range kitchen remodel can recover 60 to 80 percent of its cost at resale, while a bathroom update can return 50 to 70 percent. The actual return depends on the neighborhood, the quality of the work, and how the finished product compares to competing listings.

Overimproving is a real risk. Spending $55,000 on a kitchen in a home valued at $450,000 may not pencil out at resale. The remodel should be proportional to the home's overall value and consistent with what buyers in that price range expect. In Summerlin, where home prices vary significantly by village and lot, this calibration matters.
