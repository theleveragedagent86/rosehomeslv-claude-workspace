Available NV Energy Rebates

NV Energy provides rebates on a range of home improvements. The largest incentive is for heat pump HVAC systems, which qualify for up to $3,200 back. Heat pumps are particularly effective in the Las Vegas climate because they handle both heating and cooling with significantly less energy than traditional systems. For homeowners replacing an aging AC unit, a standard central air conditioning upgrade qualifies for up to $1,500.

Heat pump water heaters offer another opportunity, with rebates of up to $800. These units use a fraction of the energy that conventional electric water heaters consume and are well suited for the warm ambient temperatures in Summerlin garages. Insulation upgrades qualify for up to $1,200, which is especially relevant for older homes in 89128 and other established Summerlin zip codes where original insulation may have degraded over the decades.

Smart thermostats come with a $75 to $150 rebate. As covered in the smart home upgrades guide, these devices pay for themselves quickly through automated temperature management.

Free Home Energy Audit

One of the most underused benefits NV Energy provides is a free home energy audit. A technician visits your property, evaluates insulation, ductwork, windows, and equipment, and delivers a customized report identifying where you are losing energy and what upgrades will produce the greatest savings. This audit is available at no cost to NV Energy customers and provides a clear roadmap before you spend anything on improvements.

For new homeowners who just completed setting up utilities in Summerlin, scheduling this audit early is a smart first step.

Time of Use Rate Plans

Beyond equipment rebates, NV Energy offers time of use rate plans that can save Summerlin homeowners 10 to 20 percent on summer electric bills. These plans charge lower rates during off peak hours, typically late evening through early morning, and higher rates during peak afternoon demand. If you can shift laundry, dishwashing, and other heavy usage to off peak times, the savings add up quickly across an entire cooling season.

Pairing a time of use plan with a solar panel system creates an even more powerful combination. Solar production peaks during the same afternoon hours when time of use rates are highest, offsetting the most expensive electricity on your bill.
