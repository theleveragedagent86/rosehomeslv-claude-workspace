Monsoon Season Prep Guide for Summerlin Homeowners

Monsoon season in Las Vegas runs from June through September, bringing intense but brief storms that can produce heavy rain, strong winds, and flash flooding in a matter of minutes. For Summerlin homeowners, a few hours of preparation before the season starts can prevent thousands of dollars in damage. Understanding the specific risks in this part of the valley is especially important for newer residents who may not have experienced a desert monsoon before.

Las Vegas Realtor Ryan Rose advises every buyer he works with to build monsoon prep into their annual homeownership routine, particularly those purchasing in western Summerlin neighborhoods.

Why Western Summerlin Faces Higher Flood Risk

Summerlin's western neighborhoods sit at higher elevations closer to Red Rock Canyon, which means stormwater flows downhill from the mountains through natural washes and engineered flood channels. Clark County maintains flood control infrastructure throughout the area, but during extreme storms, washes near Red Rock can carry significant volumes of water. Some parcels in western Summerlin fall within or adjacent to designated Clark County flood zones, which is a factor buyers should review during the purchase process.

Understanding Summerlin's climate patterns helps homeowners anticipate when the heaviest storms typically arrive and plan accordingly.

Home Preparation Checklist for Monsoon Season

Start with gutters and downspouts. Clear all debris so water flows freely away from the foundation. Clogged gutters during a heavy downpour can lead to water intrusion along rooflines and into walls. Inspect and clean these before June and again midway through the season.

Check all exterior drainage. Walk the perimeter of the home and confirm that grading directs water away from the foundation. Fill any low spots where water might pool against the structure. If the property has a sump pump, test it to confirm it activates properly.

Seal windows and doors. Desert heat causes weatherstripping and caulking to deteriorate faster than in milder climates. Inspect seals around all windows, sliding doors, and garage doors. Replace any cracked or missing material before the first storm.

Trim trees and secure outdoor items. High winds during monsoon storms can turn loose branches into projectiles. Trim back any dead or overhanging limbs, and bring in or anchor patio furniture, potted plants, and decorative items that could become airborne.

Flood Insurance Considerations

Standard homeowners insurance policies do not cover flood damage. Flood insurance is a separate policy, typically purchased through the National Flood Insurance Program or a private carrier. Even if a Summerlin property is not in a designated high risk flood zone, coverage is still available and worth considering given the intensity of monsoon events. Properties near washes or in lower elevation areas of the community carry more risk and may benefit the most from this additional protection.
