Why Most Summerlin HOAs Restrict Short Term Rentals

Summerlin is a master planned community managed by The Howard Hughes Corporation, and the vast majority of its sub associations prohibit rentals shorter than 30 days. These restrictions exist to preserve the residential character of the neighborhoods. Homeowners who violate the policy risk fines, legal action from the HOA, and revocation of rental privileges altogether.

Before purchasing any property with the intention of renting it on platforms like Airbnb or Vrbo, investors must review the specific CC&Rs for that community. The short term rental rules in Summerlin are not universal, and making assumptions based on one neighborhood can lead to expensive surprises in another.

Neighborhoods with More Rental Flexibility

A small number of Summerlin adjacent properties operate under different rental guidelines. The C2 Lofts and certain condo developments near Summerlin Centre have historically allowed more flexible lease terms, though each building's HOA still sets its own policy. These properties tend to attract investors specifically because of the relaxed rental language in their governing documents.

Condos and townhomes in mixed use areas near Downtown Summerlin may also have different rules than the single family neighborhoods deeper into the community. Investors should request the full HOA document package during due diligence and have it reviewed before finalizing an offer. The best rental neighborhoods in Summerlin require this level of homework upfront.

Clark County STR Licensing Requirements

Beyond HOA rules, Clark County requires a short term rental business license for any property rented for fewer than 31 consecutive days. The annual fee is $250, and the property must be located within 1,000 feet of a licensed short term rental manager. Applicants also need a valid Clark County business license and must comply with occupancy limits, noise ordinances, and parking requirements. Failure to obtain the proper license can result in fines of $1,000 or more per violation.

The Corporate Housing Alternative

Many savvy Summerlin investors have shifted toward furnished rentals with 30 day or longer minimum stays. This model avoids both the HOA short term rental restrictions and the Clark County STR licensing requirements. The target tenants include traveling healthcare professionals, corporate relocators, insurance claim displaced families, and remote workers who need month to month flexibility.

Furnished monthly rentals in Summerlin can command significantly higher per month rates than traditional unfurnished leases. A three bedroom home that might rent unfurnished for $2,200 per month could bring $3,500 to $4,500 furnished on a 30 day minimum basis, depending on the village and the quality of furnishings.
