## Las Vegas Ballpark and City National Arena at Downtown Summerlin Las Vegas Ballpark is a world class Triple A baseball stadium and City National Arena is the Vegas Golden Knights practice facility, both located at Downtown Summerlin and offering year round sports and entertainment for residents. ## What Is the Las Vegas Ballpark? Las Vegas Ballpark is the home stadium of the Las Vegas Aviators, the Triple A affiliate of the Oakland Athletics. Opened as a state of the art facility, the ballpark seats approximately 10,000 fans and features modern amenities that rival many major league venues. The design emphasizes an intimate fan experience with sight lines that put you close to the action from every seat. Game nights at the Ballpark have become a staple of the Summerlin social calendar. The atmosphere blends competitive baseball with family entertainment, including fireworks nights, themed promotions, and a concession lineup that goes well beyond standard stadium fare. With a season that runs from spring through early fall, the Ballpark provides months of affordable, accessible entertainment just minutes from home for Summerlin residents. ## What Is City National Arena? City National Arena serves as the official practice facility of the Vegas Golden Knights, the NHL franchise that electrified Las Vegas when it debuted in 2017. The arena hosts Golden Knights practices that are open to the public, giving fans a free, up close look at their favorite players. Beyond practices, the venue hosts youth hockey leagues, figure skating events, and community programming. The arena also includes the official Golden Knights team store and dining options, making it a destination even on non practice days. For hockey fans living in Summerlin, having the team's practice home in the neighborhood is a unique perk that no other master planned community in Las Vegas can match. ## How Do These Venues Fit into the Downtown Summerlin Experience? Both venues sit within the broader Downtown Summerlin entertainment district, which means attending a game or catching a practice session naturally pairs with shopping, dining, or simply enjoying the open air promenade. Families often plan an entire evening around a Ballpark game, arriving early for dinner at a nearby restaurant and staying after for the post game atmosphere. ## Are Events at These Venues Affordable and Accessible? Yes. Aviators tickets are priced well below major league levels, making baseball games a budget friendly outing for families. Golden Knights practices are free and open to the public on most practice days. Both venues offer ample free parking, which is a significant advantage over attending events on the Las Vegas Strip. ## A Local Perspective According to Summerlin real estate specialist Ryan Rose, the Ballpark and City National Arena add a layer of lifestyle value that buyers consistently appreciate.
