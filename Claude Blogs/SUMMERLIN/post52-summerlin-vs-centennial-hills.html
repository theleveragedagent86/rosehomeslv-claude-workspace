## Summerlin vs Centennial Hills: Full Comparison for Las Vegas Buyers Summerlin and Centennial Hills both sit on the northwest side of the Las Vegas Valley, but they differ significantly in price, maturity, and amenities. Here is what you should know before choosing. ## How Do Home Prices Stack Up? Summerlin's median home price hovers around $686,000 in 2026, while Centennial Hills offers a more accessible entry point between $450,000 and $550,000. That price gap reflects Summerlin's three decades of development, its guard gated enclaves, and the prestige tied to the Howard Hughes Corporation brand. Centennial Hills, built primarily in the 2000s, delivers newer construction at a lower cost per square foot. For buyers who want a northwest Las Vegas address without the Summerlin premium, Centennial Hills can stretch the budget considerably further. ## Which Has Better Infrastructure and Amenities? Summerlin holds a clear advantage in established infrastructure. Downtown Summerlin provides a walkable retail and entertainment district, and the community includes more than 300 parks, 10 golf courses, and over 200 miles of trails. Centennial Hills is still growing into its identity. Centennial Hills Park stands out as a popular gathering spot, and the area's retail and dining scene continues to expand along the US 95 corridor. Grocery stores, medical offices, and everyday conveniences are well represented in both communities, though Summerlin offers a deeper and more polished selection. ## What About Schools and Family Life? Families in Summerlin benefit from 26 schools within the community, including highly rated options like Vassiliadis Elementary, Sig Rogich Middle, and Palo Verde High. Centennial Hills also feeds into solid Clark County School District campuses and has attracted newer charter and magnet programs. Both neighborhoods deliver safe, family friendly environments with parks and youth programming. Summerlin's larger trail network and variety of recreational facilities give it an edge for active families who prioritize outdoor time. ## How Far Is the Commute? Both communities sit along the US 95 corridor, making commute patterns similar for anyone working in the northwest valley or downtown Las Vegas. Summerlin Parkway gives Summerlin residents a dedicated route to the Strip in roughly 20 minutes. Centennial Hills residents reach the same areas via US 95, with comparable drive times. Neither community offers a particularly short airport commute, as both sit on the opposite side of the valley from Harry Reid International. ## Insight from a Local Agent According to Ryan Rose of Rose Homes LV, the decision between Summerlin and Centennial Hills often comes down to budget versus brand. Buyers who want a nationally recognized master plan with mature landscaping and premium amenities lean toward Summerlin. Those seeking newer homes at a friendlier price point find Centennial Hills delivers strong value. ## Want Help Choosing the Right Community?
