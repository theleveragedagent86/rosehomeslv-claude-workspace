## Best Coffee Shops in Summerlin: Local Roasters and Cafe Favorites Summerlin's coffee scene has grown well beyond chain drive throughs. From indie roasters with on site roasting to Latin inspired cafes, these are the coffee shops that Summerlin locals trust for their daily cup. ## Mothership Coffee Roasters Mothership is Las Vegas's longest running independent coffee roaster, and their Downtown Summerlin location is a community hub. The space features tiered seating that works equally well for remote work sessions and casual meetups. They prioritize ethical sourcing and roast their beans in house, which translates to a noticeably fresher cup than what you will find at most chains. If you are exploring the area, pair a visit with a walk through the [Downtown Summerlin shopping and dining district](/blogs/downtown-summerlin-shopping-dining). ## Makers and Finders Located at 120 Festival Plaza, Makers and Finders brings Latin American coffee culture to Summerlin. Their menu features specialty drinks with flavors drawn from South and Central American traditions, and the food menu is just as thoughtful. The interior has a warm, modern feel, and the staff is known for being genuinely knowledgeable about their beans and brewing methods. Pricing is reasonable for the quality. ## Sambalatte Sambalatte has multiple locations in the Summerlin area and has earned recognition from both Zagat and the Las Vegas Review Journal. Their espresso program is precise, and the pastry case is consistently well stocked. It is a reliable go to when you want a well made latte without hunting for a new spot. The atmosphere is clean and comfortable, leaning more polished cafe than cozy corner shop. ## Vesta Coffee Roasters Vesta roasts on site, and the difference is apparent from the first sip. Their rotating single origin offerings give regulars a reason to keep coming back, and the baristas are happy to walk you through tasting notes. If you care about the craft side of coffee and want to support a small batch roaster, Vesta is the pick. ## Teaspoon Teaspoon, located at 1990 Village Center Circle, draws from Vietnamese coffee traditions to create a menu that stands apart from the typical Summerlin cafe. Their Vietnamese iced coffee is a standout, and the tea menu is equally extensive. The shop has a bright, inviting interior that skews younger in design and energy. ## Alchemy Coffee Alchemy has built a loyal local following by keeping things simple and doing them well. The menu covers espresso classics without unnecessary complexity, and the atmosphere is relaxed enough for a long sit. It is the kind of neighborhood shop where the baristas know the regulars by name. ## Ryan Rose on Living Near Great Coffee As a Realtor focused on the Summerlin market, Ryan Rose often points out that walkability to quality coffee and dining is a real factor in neighborhood selection.
