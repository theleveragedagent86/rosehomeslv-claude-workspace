## Moving from California to Summerlin: Tax Savings Guide California residents relocating to Summerlin often cite tax savings as the single biggest motivator behind their move. Nevada has no state income tax, and the difference in annual take home pay can be substantial depending on your income bracket. ## How Much Will You Actually Save on Income Taxes? California's state income tax ranges from 1% to 13.3% at the highest bracket, making it one of the most expensive states in the country for earners. Nevada charges zero state income tax. That gap translates to real numbers quickly. A household earning $50,000 saves roughly $1,200 per year. At $200,000, the savings jump to approximately $12,000 annually. High earners pulling in $500,000 or more can expect to keep an additional $45,000 or more each year simply by changing their state of residence. Retirement income is also completely untaxed in Nevada. If you are drawing Social Security, pensions, or 401(k) distributions, none of it will be subject to state taxation once you establish residency. ## What About Property Taxes? Nevada's effective property tax rate runs between 0.50% and 0.70%, with a 3% annual cap on increases for owner occupied homes. California's rate sits around 1% with a 2% cap under Proposition 13. For a $500,000 home, that difference can mean paying $2,500 to $3,500 in Nevada versus $5,000 in California each year. Over the life of a mortgage, this savings alone adds up to tens of thousands of dollars. Nevada also has no estate tax, no inheritance tax, and no corporate income tax, which matters for business owners and families doing long term wealth planning. ## How Do You Establish Nevada Residency Correctly? California is known for aggressively auditing high income earners who claim to have moved out of state. To protect yourself, you need to establish clear domicile in Nevada. That means spending fewer than 183 days per year in California, obtaining a Nevada driver's license, registering to vote in Nevada, and transferring your banking relationships to local institutions. Keeping detailed records of your physical presence in Nevada is strongly recommended, especially in the first two years after your move. ## Is Sales Tax Higher in Nevada? Clark County's combined sales tax rate is 8.375%, which is comparable to most California counties that average 8.5% or higher. The difference is marginal and generally offset many times over by the income and property tax savings. ## What Does This Mean for Summerlin Buyers?
