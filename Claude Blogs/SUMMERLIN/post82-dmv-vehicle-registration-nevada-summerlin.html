## DMV and Vehicle Registration for New Nevada Residents One of the first tasks after relocating to Summerlin is getting your Nevada driver's license and registering your vehicle. The state requires both within 30 days of establishing residency, and handling them early avoids potential fines or complications. Here is everything you need to know to get through the process efficiently. ## Getting Your Nevada Driver's License Nevada law requires new residents to obtain a state driver's license within 30 days of moving. You will need to visit a DMV office in person and bring proof of identity (passport, birth certificate, or similar government issued document) along with proof of your Nevada residential address. Acceptable address documents include a utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement. If you hold a valid driver's license from another U.S. state, you will surrender it at the DMV counter. Nevada does not allow you to hold licenses from two states simultaneously. The fee for a standard license is $41.25, and it remains valid for eight years. The closest DMV offices to Summerlin are the Sahara West location and the Flamingo office, both of which accept walk ins and appointments. Booking an appointment online through the Nevada DMV website will save you significant wait time, especially during peak hours. ## Registering Your Vehicle in Nevada You can transfer your vehicle registration to Nevada during the same DMV visit where you get your license. If your vehicle has never been registered in Nevada before, it must pass a vehicle inspection. Cars model year 1968 and newer also require a smog check from a certified emissions testing station before you can complete registration. You do not need to surrender your out of state title at the time of registration, but you will need to provide it for the title transfer process. Bring your current registration, proof of insurance from a Nevada approved provider, and the vehicle's odometer reading. ## What About Smog and Emissions? Clark County requires emissions testing for most gasoline powered vehicles. Testing stations are located throughout the Las Vegas Valley, and the process usually takes less than 30 minutes. If your vehicle fails, you will need to address the issue and retest before the DMV will process your registration. ## How Much Does It All Cost? Between the license fee, registration, and emissions testing, new residents should budget roughly $200 to $400 depending on the vehicle. Registration fees in Nevada are based on the vehicle's value and decrease over time. Newer and higher value vehicles will pay more. Government services tax and supplemental fees are included in the total at the DMV counter. ## Tips for a Smooth DMV Visit Arrive with all your documents organized and ready. Missing even one piece of required paperwork means a return trip. Get your smog check completed before visiting the DMV so you can handle everything in a single appointment. And if possible, schedule your visit midweek in the morning when wait times tend to be shortest.
