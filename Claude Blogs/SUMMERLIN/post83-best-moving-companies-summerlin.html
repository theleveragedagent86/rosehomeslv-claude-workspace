Make sure each estimate is based on an in home or virtual walkthrough rather than a rough phone estimate. Ask whether the quote includes fuel surcharges, stair fees, and long carry charges. Confirm that the company is licensed and insured through the Nevada Transportation Authority for local moves or the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration for interstate relocations. ## What Should You Budget? For a local Summerlin move, expect to spend between $400 and $1,500 depending on home size. A two bedroom apartment or condo typically falls in the $400 to $700 range, while a four or five bedroom home can run $1,000 to $1,500 or more. Long distance moves from states like California or Arizona will vary widely based on distance and volume.
