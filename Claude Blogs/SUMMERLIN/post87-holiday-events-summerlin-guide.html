## Summerlin Holiday Events: Halloween and Christmas Guide Summerlin goes all in on holiday events, turning Downtown Summerlin and the surrounding community into a seasonal destination from October through January. Between Halloween festivals, holiday parades, ice skating, and Santa photos, there is something happening nearly every weekend during the fall and winter months. ## What Happens During Halloween in Summerlin? Vegas Halloween Town takes over Downtown Summerlin from October 25 through October 31. The event brings together vendors, a Haunted Trail, and carnival attractions that draw crowds from across the valley. It is a ticketed event and tends to sell out on peak nights, so planning ahead is a good idea. Before Vegas Halloween Town kicks off, the Parade of Mischief runs every Friday and Saturday evening from October 3 through October 25. The parade starts at 6pm on Park Centre Drive and is completely free. Costumed characters, floats, and performers walk the route while families line the sidewalks. It is one of the best free family events in all of Las Vegas during the fall season. The Summerlin Library also hosts an Outdoor Fall Festival each year at no cost. The event is geared toward younger children and families looking for a low key alternative to the larger Halloween productions. ## What Are the Christmas and Holiday Events? The Holiday Parade at Downtown Summerlin is the anchor event of the season. Running every Friday and Saturday at 6pm from November 21 through December 20, the parade features snow machines, a Santa appearance, and more than 100 youth performers from local dance studios and schools. The parade is free and has become one of Summerlin's most beloved traditions. Santa's Chalet opens daily from November 14 through December 24. Photo packages start at $39.99, and pet photo sessions are available on Tuesdays and Thursdays for families who want their dogs in the holiday picture. The Chalet tends to get busier as Christmas approaches, so earlier visits mean shorter waits. Rock Rink Ice Skating operates from November 14 through January 19, giving Summerlin a seasonal outdoor skating experience that you would not expect in the desert. Admission with skate rentals runs $18 per person. The Jupiter Express Train is another family favorite at $5 per person for a ride through the holiday displays. Summerlin also hosts a Hanukkah Celebration on December 21 from 4pm to 6pm, featuring a public menorah lighting ceremony and family activities. ## Why Do the Holidays Matter for Homebuyers? The holiday programming speaks to the kind of community Summerlin has built. These are not one off events. They are recurring traditions that families look forward to year after year. For anyone evaluating whether Summerlin is the right fit, experiencing a holiday season here often seals the deal.
