## Daycare and Preschools in Summerlin Finding reliable childcare is one of the first things parents tackle when moving to a new community. Summerlin has a solid selection of daycare centers and preschools that serve children from as young as six weeks through elementary school age. Programs range from nationally accredited early education to flexible full day care designed around working parents' schedules. ## Creative Kids Learning Center Creative Kids Learning Center is located at 10650 Alta Drive and accepts children from 6 weeks through 12 years old. The center operates Monday through Friday from 6:30am to 6pm, making it a practical option for families with standard work schedules. The age range is one of the broadest in the area, which means parents with multiple children at different stages can keep everyone in one location. ## Merryhill Preschool Summerlin Merryhill Preschool is NAEYC accredited, a distinction earned by programs that meet nationally recognized standards for early childhood education. The school accepts children starting at 6 weeks and serves families throughout the Ridges, Tournament Hills, and Red Rock Country Club areas. NAEYC accreditation requires ongoing quality assessments, so it is a meaningful benchmark for parents who prioritize structured early learning. ## La Petite Academy La Petite Academy at 8451 Boseck Drive serves children from 6 weeks through 6 years old. One of the notable features is the experience level of the teaching staff, with teachers averaging more than 10 years in early childhood education. For parents who value consistency and experienced caregivers, La Petite is worth a visit. The curriculum balances structured learning with play based activities. ## Summerlin KinderCare Summerlin KinderCare is a state licensed childcare center that is part of the KinderCare national network. The advantage of a KinderCare facility is the standardized curriculum and consistent quality benchmarks across locations. For families relocating to Summerlin from another state, KinderCare's national presence means the program may already be familiar. The center offers infant care, toddler programs, and preschool education. ## What Should Parents Look For? Licensing, accreditation, staff to child ratios, and hours of operation are the core factors. NAEYC accreditation and state licensing are not the same thing. State licensing sets a minimum standard, while NAEYC accreditation reflects a higher level of program quality. Visiting in person, asking about staff turnover, and observing the classroom environment during drop off hours will give you a better sense of fit than any website or brochure. Proximity to home or work also matters. Summerlin's layout keeps most of these centers within a short drive from the major residential villages, which reduces commute stress for parents managing tight morning routines.
