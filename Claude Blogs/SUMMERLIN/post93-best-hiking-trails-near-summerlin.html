Best Hiking Trails Near Summerlin for All Levels

One of the biggest draws of living in Summerlin is immediate access to some of the finest hiking in the American Southwest. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area sits just 10 to 15 minutes from most Summerlin neighborhoods, and the community itself features over 200 miles of interconnected trails. Whether you are a seasoned hiker looking for challenging scrambles or a beginner wanting gentle paved paths, Summerlin puts it all within reach.

Ryan Rose helps buyers relocate to Summerlin regularly and finds that trail access is one of the top lifestyle factors driving people toward this community.

Red Rock Canyon: World Class Hiking Minutes Away

Red Rock Canyon offers over 30 miles of trails ranging from easy strolls to strenuous climbs. The 13 mile scenic drive provides access to most trailheads and serves as a stunning introduction to the area's geology and desert landscapes.

The most popular trail in the area is Calico Tanks, consistently rated the number one hike in Las Vegas. This moderate trail winds through colorful sandstone formations and rewards hikers with panoramic views of the valley from the top. It is manageable for most fitness levels and takes roughly 60 to 90 minutes to complete.

Important note for planning: Red Rock Canyon requires timed entry reservations from October 1 through May 31. Reservations are available through Recreation.gov and tend to fill up on weekends, so booking in advance is strongly recommended during peak season.

Summerlin's Own Trail Network

You do not need to leave the community to find excellent walking and hiking options. Summerlin maintains over 200 miles of interconnected trails that link neighborhoods, parks, shopping areas, and schools. Ten miles of these trails run along the 215 Beltway, providing long, uninterrupted stretches perfect for distance runners and cyclists.

These paved and natural surface paths are ideal for daily exercise, dog walking, and family outings. Many residents use the trail system as their primary mode of getting around the community, especially for trips to Downtown Summerlin and nearby parks.

Source: summerlin.com

Trail Recommendations by Skill Level

For beginners, the Summerlin community trails and the Moenkopi Loop at Red Rock Canyon offer flat, accessible routes with beautiful desert scenery. These require no special gear and work well for families with young children.

Intermediate hikers should target Calico Tanks and the Keystone Thrust Trail, both of which involve moderate elevation gain and some light scrambling. These trails showcase Red Rock's most dramatic geological features without demanding expert level fitness.

Advanced hikers will find plenty of challenge in Turtlehead Peak and the longer canyon routes that branch off the scenic drive. These trails involve significant elevation change and require proper footwear, water, and sun protection.

Making the Most of Trail Access

The Summerlin climate supports year round hiking, though summer months call for early morning starts to avoid peak heat. Fall through spring is the prime hiking season, which aligns with the timed entry period at Red Rock Canyon.

For anyone considering a move to Las Vegas, few communities anywhere in the country can match what Summerlin offers in terms of outdoor recreation access. The combination of a world class national conservation area next door and hundreds of miles of community trails creates a lifestyle that active residents genuinely appreciate every day.
