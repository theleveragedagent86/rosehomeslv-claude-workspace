## Summerlin vs Anthem Henderson: Detailed Comparison Summerlin and Anthem are both premium master planned communities in the Las Vegas Valley, but they serve different types of buyers. Summerlin dominates the west side with sheer scale and commercial depth. Anthem occupies a desirable stretch of southeast Henderson with strong schools, guard gated neighborhoods, and a quieter pace of life. Here is how the two compare across the categories that matter most. ## Where Are They and How Far from Key Destinations? Summerlin sits along the western edge of Las Vegas, bordered by Red Rock Canyon to the west. Anthem is located in Henderson, roughly 15 miles southeast of the Strip. Both communities fall in the 20 to 30 minute range for reaching the Strip, though specific drive times depend on which neighborhood you are starting from. Anthem has the clear advantage for airport proximity. Henderson's position puts Anthem residents about 20 minutes from Harry Reid International Airport, compared to 25 to 35 minutes from most Summerlin neighborhoods. For business travelers and frequent flyers, that difference matters. Summerlin wins for outdoor recreation access. Red Rock Canyon trailheads are 10 to 20 minutes away, with over 200 miles of community trails connecting parks and neighborhoods. Anthem has its own trail network and is closer to Lake Mead and Sloan Canyon, but Red Rock Canyon is a hard asset to match. ## How Do Prices Compare? Anthem is generally more affordable than comparable Summerlin neighborhoods. Buyers looking for a well maintained home in a guard gated community will often find lower per square foot pricing in Anthem than in Summerlin's equivalent enclaves. Summerlin's price range is broader overall, stretching from entry level condos in the mid $300s to custom estates exceeding $10 million in The Ridges. Anthem's neighborhoods include Anthem Hills (guard gated single family homes), Anthem Country Club (golf course community with custom lots), and Sun City Anthem (a popular 55 plus active adult community). Each serves a distinct buyer profile, but the price ceiling is lower than Summerlin's top tier. ## What About Schools? Both communities are known for strong schools, and this is one area where the comparison is genuinely close. Anthem feeds into several of Henderson's highest rated public schools, and the area has excellent private school access as well. Summerlin has more schools by volume simply because of its size, but Anthem's school quality is consistently among the best in the valley. ## How Does the Lifestyle Differ? The biggest lifestyle gap is commercial development. Summerlin's Downtown Summerlin district and surrounding retail centers offer an extensive selection of dining, shopping, and entertainment. Anthem's retail options are more limited, centered along Eastern Avenue and Anthem Parkway. Residents who want a wide variety of restaurants and shops within a short drive will find Summerlin has considerably more to offer. Anthem compensates with a tighter knit residential feel. The community is smaller, the streets are quieter, and the overall atmosphere leans more toward privacy and family life than nightlife and dining. ## Which Should You Choose? Ryan Rose, team lead at Rose Homes LV, sees buyers choose Anthem when they want guard gated living at a lower price point with excellent schools and easy airport access. Summerlin wins when buyers prioritize Red Rock proximity, retail variety, and the widest range of housing options in the valley. ## Want to See Both Communities?
