### Monsoon Season & Flash Flood Tips

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Monsoon season | June–September |
| Flash flood season | July–September (peak) |
| Rainfall intensity | 1 inch in 15–30 minutes possible |
| Flood damage cost | 1 inch of water = $25,000+ in damage |
| Standard home insurance | Does NOT cover flooding |
| Flood insurance wait period | 30 days to take effect |

**Homeowner Preparation Checklist**:
1. Purchase flood insurance before June (30-day wait period)
2. Review warranty/insurance documents — many require proof of regular maintenance
3. Check FEMA Flood Map Service Center for your area's risk level
4. Stock emergency supplies: bottled water, canned food, flashlights
5. Tie down or bring in outdoor objects (furniture, toys, trash cans)
6. Elevate items in garages/storage areas
7. Test sump pump if applicable
8. Check roof for loose shingles or wind damage before monsoon season
9. During storms: shelter in place, avoid washes and drainage areas
10. After flooding: shut off electricity at main breaker, turn off gas if you smell it, avoid standing water

### Summer Home Maintenance Checklist (Las Vegas)

**HVAC (Critical — #1 Priority)**:
- Schedule tune-up in March or early April (wait times increase after May)
- Check/replace air filters every 2–3 weeks during summer
- Set daytime temperature to 77–79°F
- Raise a few degrees when away; avoid drastic changes
- AC runs 12–16 hours daily when temps exceed 110°F
- Clear outdoor unit: remove weeds, leaves, debris for airflow

**Energy Efficiency**:
- Open windows before 8 AM for cool air, then close
- Close curtains/blinds during peak heat (10 AM–4 PM)
- Smart thermostats learn patterns and reduce costs

**Exterior**:
- Inspect roof for loose shingles or UV damage
- Check stucco for cracks from heat expansion
- Inspect weatherstripping on doors/windows
- Clean pool equipment; increase chemical treatment

**Electrical Safety**:
- Check for overloaded circuits (AC + appliances)
- Ensure outdoor outlets have GFCI protection
- Inspect wiring for heat damage
