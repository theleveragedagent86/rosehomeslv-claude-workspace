# Summerlin, Las Vegas -- Complete Research Data

*Research compiled: April 5, 2026*

## 1. Community Overview

- **Full Name:** Summerlin (master-planned community)
- **Location:** Western edge of the Las Vegas Valley, Nevada
- **Developer:** Howard Hughes Corporation (formerly Summa Corporation)
- **Total Acreage:** 22,500 acres
- **Population:** Approximately 130,000+ residents
- **Year Founded:** 1990 (first residents moved in 1991)
- **Named After:** Jean Amelia Summerlin, Howard Hughes' paternal grandmother
- **Remaining Developable Land:** ~5,000 gross acres reserved for future growth (infrastructure, open space, commercial, and residential)
- **Recognition:** One of the nation's top-selling master-planned communities for more than three decades

---

## 2. History & Development Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1952 | Howard Robard Hughes Jr. purchased 30,000 acres along the western rim of the Las Vegas Valley for approximately $3 per acre. Originally envisioned as a future aviation center. |
| Early 1980s | Summa Corporation recognized the residential development potential of the land. |
| 1980s | Summa Corporation traded 5,000 acres of environmentally sensitive land to the Bureau of Land Management in exchange for 3,000 acres better suited for development. |
| 1990 | Summerlin Parkway -- a six-lane highway costing $22 million -- opened, connecting Rainbow Boulevard to Town Center Drive via U.S. Route 95. First residential area, park, and school completed. |
| 1991 | First family moved into Summerlin. |
| 1994 | Summa Corporation rebranded as the Howard Hughes Corporation and continued expanding Summerlin. |
| 2014 | Downtown Summerlin retail center opened. |
| 2019 | Las Vegas Ballpark opened (home of the Las Vegas Aviators, Triple-A affiliate). |
| Present | 130,000+ residents; still actively building new villages and neighborhoods in Summerlin West. |

## 4. Community Structure & Villages

### Master Association Structure
Summerlin has a **two-tier HOA governance system:**
1. **Master Community Associations** (4 total): Summerlin North, Summerlin South, Summerlin West, and Summerlin Centre
2. **Sub-Associations** (neighborhood-level): Manage day-to-day rules for specific villages, gated enclaves, condos, and townhomes

## 7. HOA Fees & Governance

### Fee Structure
| Fee Type | Monthly Cost |
|----------|-------------|
| Master Association Fee | ~$55-$65/month |
| Neighborhood Sub-Association Fee | $50-$150/month |
| **Total (typical single-family)** | **$100-$200/month** |
| Gated/Luxury Communities | Additional assessments may apply |

### 2026 Fee Update
- Effective January 1, 2026: Summerlin Council's portion of the assessment increased by $7/month, bringing its share to $37 per household
- Funds community parks, pools, events, and recreational programming

### What Fees Cover
**Master Association:**
- Major parks and trails
- Entry monuments
- Streetscape landscaping
- Community-wide standards
- Community programming

**Sub-Associations:**
- Local CC&R enforcement
- Neighborhood amenities (pools, small parks)
- Architectural review for exterior changes
- Some restrict short-term rentals
- Many require tenant registration or specific lease language

## 8. Guard-Gated & Luxury Communities

### The Ridges
- One of the most exclusive residential communities in Summerlin
- Custom and semi-custom estates with contemporary architecture
- Elevated home sites with valley and mountain views
- Homes typically start over $1 million (many $2M-$10M+)
- **Club Ridges:** Private 9,000 sq ft facility with full fitness center, resort and lap pools, steam rooms, lighted tennis courts
- Adjacent to Bear's Best Golf Course (Jack Nicklaus design)

### The Summit Club
- Ultra-luxury guard-gated community next to The Ridges
- Lots starting in the 7-figures; homes exceeding $10-$20 million
- **Tom Fazio-designed** members-only golf course
- $90 million, 110,000 sq ft clubhouse
- Panoramic mountain, golf, and Strip views
- Attracts high-profile residents seeking privacy

### Red Rock Country Club
- Guard-gated golf community
- Two Arnold Palmer Signature golf courses
- Resort-style amenities

### Other Guard-Gated Communities
- Ascension
- Regency at Summerlin
- Tournament Hills
- Canyon Fairways
- Promontory Ridge

## 9. 55+ Active Adult Communities

### Sun City Summerlin
- **Developer:** Del Webb
- **Size:** ~8,000 single-family homes on 2,400 acres
- **Largest** 55+ community in Summerlin
- **Golf:** 3 Billy Casper/Greg Nash designed courses (Palm Valley, Highland Falls, Eagle Crest)
- **Amenities:** 4 social/recreation centers, pools, spas, tennis, bocce, racquetball, 312-seat theater
- **Home Prices:** Median ~$499,900 (Feb 2026)

### Siena
- **Developer:** Sunrise Colony Company (built 2000-2006)
- **Size:** 2,001 single-family and attached homes
- **Distinction:** Only GUARD-GATED active adult community in Summerlin
- **Golf:** Siena Golf Club (Lee Schmidt/Brian Curley design, 18 holes)
- **Amenities:** Community center, fitness center, Italian-themed buildings, lake with waterfall
- **Floor Plans:** 18 floor plans available

### Regency at Summerlin
- **Builder:** Toll Brothers (construction began 2015)
- **Size:** 434 single-family homes (gated community)
- **Home Sizes:** 1,665 - 2,659 sq ft
- **Bedrooms/Baths:** 2 bedrooms, 2-2.5 bathrooms, 2-car garages
- **Style:** Luxurious homes with stunning landscapes

## 10. Schools & Education

### School Statistics
- **Total Schools:** 26 public, private, and charter schools
- **Math Proficiency:** 45% (vs. 31% Nevada state average)

- **Sig Rogich Middle School** -- Ranked #1 in Clark County, #12 in Nevada; Five-Star Nevada School Performance Framework rating; 10/10 GreatSchools rating

**High Schools:**
- **Palo Verde High School** -- Well-regarded comprehensive high school

## 14. Proximity to Red Rock Canyon

- **Distance:** 10-15 minutes from most Summerlin neighborhoods; ~15 miles / 20 minutes from Downtown Summerlin
- **Red Rock Canyon NCA Features:**
  - 13-mile scenic drive
  - 30+ miles of hiking trails
  - World-class rock climbing
  - Horseback riding, mountain biking, road biking
  - Picnic areas and visitor center
- **Top Trail:** Calico Tanks Trail -- ranked #1 best hike in Las Vegas
- **Timed Entry:** Reservations required October 1 - May 31, 8 AM - 5 PM (via Recreation.gov)
- **Access:** No public transportation -- Summerlin's proximity makes it the most convenient residential base

## 16. Lifestyle & Key Selling Points

### Why Buyers Choose Summerlin
1. **Master-Planned Excellence** -- 35+ years of thoughtful planning with integrated amenities
2. **Schools** -- Top-rated public and private schools; significantly above state averages
3. **Outdoor Lifestyle** -- 300+ parks, 200+ miles of trails, adjacent to Red Rock Canyon
4. **Safety** -- Guard-gated options; well-maintained community standards
5. **Diverse Housing** -- From $300K townhomes to $20M+ estates; 55+ communities to young family neighborhoods
6. **Appreciation** -- Consistent home value growth; median up 9.8% YoY
7. **Amenities** -- Downtown Summerlin shopping/dining, 10 golf courses, sports facilities
8. **Location** -- 20 minutes to the Las Vegas Strip; easy freeway access via Summerlin Parkway and I-215
9. **Healthcare** -- Summerlin Hospital Medical Center within the community
10. **Cultural Amenities** -- Public library, performing arts center, community events year-round

### Climate
- **Avg. Sunny Days:** 294 per year
- **Summer Highs:** 100-115 degrees F
- **Winter Lows:** 30-45 degrees F
- **Annual Rainfall:** ~4.2 inches
- **Elevation:** Higher than central Las Vegas Valley (cooler by 5-10 degrees in summer)

## 17. Transportation & Access

- **Summerlin Parkway:** Direct freeway connection to US-95 and downtown Las Vegas
- **I-215 Beltway:** Rings the western and southern edges of Summerlin
- **Distance to Las Vegas Strip:** ~20 minutes
- **Distance to McCarran/Harry Reid International Airport:** ~25-30 minutes
- **Distance to Downtown Las Vegas:** ~20-25 minutes
- **Distance to Red Rock Canyon:** 10-15 minutes
- **Public Transit:** RTC bus routes serve major corridors; limited compared to central valley
