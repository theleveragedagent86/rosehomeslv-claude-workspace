Allegro Park is one of the four major parks in Seven Hills Henderson, covering approximately 4.5 acres at 1023 Seven Hills Drive. Open daily from 6 AM to midnight, Allegro Park is primarily a soccer and open field park located adjacent to Wolff Elementary School, making it a popular after school and weekend destination for families in the community.

Amenities and Recreation

Allegro Park features a large soccer field that hosts youth games on weekends, a playground, picnic tables, barbecue facilities, restrooms, and open grass areas for casual play. The park's proximity to Wolff Elementary School makes it a natural extension of the school's outdoor space, and many families use the park for after school activities, birthday gatherings, and community events.

Paved walking trails within Allegro Park connect to the Seven Hills Trail system, linking all four of the community's major parks: Allegro, Sonata, Vivaldi, and Puccini. The 2.6 mile Seven Hills Trail loop provides a continuous path between these parks, allowing residents to walk or jog through the community without crossing vehicle traffic in most sections.

Water Conservation and Views

The City of Henderson completed a significant water conservation project at Allegro Park, removing 8,600 square feet and converting 68,700 square feet of turf landscaping to drought adapted desert plantings. This project saves an estimated 1,847,000 gallons of water annually while preserving the park's core recreational areas including the soccer fields, playground, and picnic areas. The park's position within Seven Hills provides views of the surrounding valley and mountain ranges, adding a scenic backdrop to everyday recreation.

Ryan Rose highlights Allegro Park as a key amenity for families with school age children.
