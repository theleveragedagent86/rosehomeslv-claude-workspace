Seven Hills Henderson HOA Fees and What They Cover

Seven Hills Henderson Home Prices 2026

The cost of living in Seven Hills Henderson is shaped by Nevada's tax advantages, moderate property taxes, and the community's HOA structure. Nevada has no state income tax, which means wages, investment income, Social Security benefits, pension distributions, and retirement account withdrawals are all free from state income taxation. This single factor can save residents thousands of dollars annually compared to living in states with income taxes.

Property Taxes and Housing Costs

Clark County's effective property tax rate is approximately 0.77 percent, which is below the national median of 1.02 percent. The county assesses property at 35 percent of market value, and Nevada's Assembly Bill 489 caps annual property tax increases at three percent on primary residences, providing predictable long term costs. For a home valued at $700,000, annual property taxes would be approximately $2,100 to $2,500 depending on the specific tax district.

HOA fees in Seven Hills range from approximately $60 to $558 per month depending on the neighborhood, with guard gated communities at the higher end and production home neighborhoods at the lower end. These fees cover common area maintenance, park upkeep, security patrols, and in some neighborhoods, front yard landscaping and additional amenities.

Utilities and Daily Expenses

Electricity in the Henderson area averages approximately 13 cents per kilowatt hour, which is eight percent below the Nevada state average. Monthly utility costs including electricity, water, and garbage typically run $200 to $250 for a single family home. Water costs include a base charge plus usage fees, with the City of Henderson managing water and wastewater services directly. Henderson's overall cost of living runs approximately four to 18 percent above the national average, with housing and transportation costs driving most of the difference while food costs run about 7.5 percent below average and healthcare costs run approximately 13 percent below the national average.
