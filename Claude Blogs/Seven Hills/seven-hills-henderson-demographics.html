Is Seven Hills Henderson a Good Place to Live?

Seven Hills Henderson is a suburban community with approximately 19,000 residents across its 1,300 acres and 25 neighborhoods. The community's demographics reflect a mature, middle to upper income population with strong educational attainment and a stable residential character that has developed over more than two decades since the first homes were built in the late 1990s.

Income and Age

The median household income in the Seven Hills area is approximately $75,000, placing the community above the national median and higher than roughly 52 percent of American neighborhoods. The median age of residents is 43 to 46 years, reflecting a population that skews toward established professionals, move up buyers, and early retirees rather than first time homebuyers or young renters. The average household size is approximately 2.47 people with an average family size of three.

Education and Employment

Seven Hills' population demonstrates strong educational attainment, consistent with the community's income levels and professional character. The majority of homes in Seven Hills were built after 1999, meaning most original buyers entered the community as working age adults and many have remained through retirement. The community's proximity to the Las Vegas Strip, Henderson's corporate corridor, and the broader Clark County employment base makes it practical for professionals in hospitality, healthcare, technology, and professional services.

Community Character

The demographic profile of Seven Hills supports a quiet, well maintained residential atmosphere. The relatively mature median age, above average incomes, and homeowner dominated housing stock create a neighborhood where properties are consistently maintained and community standards are upheld. The mix of young families attracted by the school ratings, working professionals drawn to the location and amenities, and retirees who appreciate the golf, parks, and quality of life gives Seven Hills a multigenerational character that avoids feeling exclusively oriented toward any single age group.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose observes that Seven Hills' demographic stability is one of the factors that supports its long term property values. A community with above average incomes, strong homeownership rates, and a mature resident base tends to maintain its appearance, fund its HOA obligations consistently, and attract similar quality buyers over time. For buyers evaluating Seven Hills as a long term investment, the demographic fundamentals reinforce the community's position as one of Henderson's most desirable residential addresses.
