What Is Seven Hills Henderson?

More than one third of Seven Hills Henderson's 1,300 acres is dedicated to open space, a ratio that is uncommon among master planned communities in the Las Vegas valley. This commitment to open space preservation gives Seven Hills a low density, retreat like character that distinguishes it from newer, more densely developed communities where maximum lot yield takes priority over natural landscape preservation.

What the Open Space Includes

Seven Hills' open space encompasses the community's four major parks (Sonata, Vivaldi, Puccini, and Allegro), pocket parks, the connected trail system, landscaped medians and common areas, natural desert hillside areas, and the Serket Golf Club course. The golf course alone accounts for a significant portion of the open space, providing a green, manicured buffer between residential neighborhoods that enhances views and reduces the visual density of the community.

The natural desert hillside areas that define Seven Hills' terrain are preserved as open space rather than developed, maintaining the rolling topography that gives the community its name and its character. These undeveloped hillsides provide natural habitat, visual interest, and a connection to the surrounding desert landscape that complements the manicured parks and streetscapes within the developed portions of the community.

Impact on Property Values and Lifestyle

The generous open space allocation directly benefits property values by ensuring that Seven Hills never feels overcrowded or overdeveloped. Homes adjacent to open space parcels, trail corridors, and the golf course enjoy permanent view protection and privacy buffers that cannot be built on. The trail system that threads through the open space connects all four major parks, giving residents a car free network of walking and jogging routes that enhance daily quality of life.
