What Is Seven Hills Henderson?

Property taxes in Seven Hills Henderson are governed by Clark County's tax rate structure, which is lower than the national median. Nevada's property tax system assesses homes at 35 percent of market value and applies the district tax rate to that assessed value. For Seven Hills homeowners, this results in effective tax rates of approximately 0.77 percent of market value, compared to the national median effective rate of 1.02 percent.

How Property Taxes Are Calculated

Clark County calculates property taxes by multiplying the assessed value (35 percent of the home's taxable value) by the district tax rate of approximately $2.96 per $100 of assessed value. For example, a Seven Hills home with a taxable value of $700,000 would have an assessed value of $245,000, resulting in an annual property tax of approximately $7,252 before any exemptions. The actual tax bill may vary based on the specific tax district and any applicable exemptions.

Nevada's Assembly Bill 489 provides important taxpayer protection by capping annual property tax increases at three percent for primary residences, regardless of how much the assessed value increases. This means that even during periods of rapid home price appreciation, the actual tax bill increase is limited, providing predictability for homeowners budgeting their housing costs over time.

Exemptions and Savings

Nevada offers a primary residence exemption that reduces the taxable value for homeowners who occupy their property as a primary residence. Veterans with service connected disabilities may qualify for additional property tax exemptions. Senior citizens aged 62 and older with household incomes below certain thresholds may qualify for a property tax assistance program. These exemptions can meaningfully reduce the annual tax bill for qualifying homeowners.
