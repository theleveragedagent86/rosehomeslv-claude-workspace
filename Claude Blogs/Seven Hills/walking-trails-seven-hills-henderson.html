What Is Seven Hills Henderson?

Parks in Seven Hills Henderson

Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area Near Seven Hills

Seven Hills Henderson features a paved internal trail system that connects the community's parks and neighborhoods, along with easy access to several longer trails in the surrounding Henderson foothills. The centerpiece is the Seven Hills Trail, a 2.6 mile paved loop rated as easy on AllTrails, making it accessible for walkers, joggers, and families with strollers.

The Seven Hills Trail

The Seven Hills Trail is a 2.6 mile loop that begins near Puccini Park and winds through the community with approximately 196 feet of elevation gain. The trail is paved, wide, and classified as an urban multi use path. Dogs are welcome on leash. The gentle rolling terrain provides varied scenery with views of the surrounding mountains and the Las Vegas Strip from elevated sections. Most residents complete the loop in 45 minutes to an hour at a comfortable walking pace.

Beyond the main loop, additional paved paths run through the community connecting all four major parks (Sonata, Vivaldi, Puccini, and Allegro) and winding through greenbelts between neighborhoods. These paths allow residents to walk or bike to parks, schools, and common areas without using roads.

Trails Near Seven Hills

The St. Rose Parkway Trail runs approximately 5.1 miles along St. Rose Parkway on the northern boundary of Seven Hills, providing a multi use path for jogging and cycling with connections to the broader Henderson trail network. The Amargosa Trail, a 3.7 mile paved loop, connects Discovery Park to Paseo Vista Park and is popular with cyclists and runners.

For more adventurous hikers, Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area sits approximately 4 miles from Seven Hills with access to the McCullough Hills Trail, a challenging dirt and gravel route through desert terrain with significant elevation gain. Black Mountain Trail offers a strenuous ridgeline hike with scrambling sections and panoramic views of the entire Las Vegas valley. These trails provide a stark contrast to the paved community paths and appeal to experienced hikers seeking rugged desert landscape.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose considers Seven Hills' trail connectivity one of its most underrated features. The ability to step out your front door and walk a paved loop through parks and greenbelts is a quality of life advantage that many Las Vegas area communities simply do not offer. For buyers who enjoy morning walks or evening jogs, the combination of internal community paths and nearby desert trails means Seven Hills delivers outdoor recreation for casual and serious hikers alike.
