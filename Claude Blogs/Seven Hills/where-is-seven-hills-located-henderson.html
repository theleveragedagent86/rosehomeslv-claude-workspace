What Is Seven Hills Henderson?

Seven Hills Henderson Zip Code 89052

Seven Hills Henderson Distance to the Las Vegas Strip

Seven Hills is located in the southern foothills of Henderson, Nevada, approximately seven miles south of the Las Vegas Strip and eight miles from Harry Reid International Airport. The community sits in the 89052 zip code along the McCullough Mountain Range foothills, bordered by Eastern Avenue to the east and accessible via State Route 146 (Lake Mead Parkway) and Interstate 215.

Henderson, Not Las Vegas

Seven Hills is in the city of Henderson, which is a separate incorporated city from Las Vegas. Henderson is the second largest city in Nevada and consistently ranks among the safest large cities in the country. While the two cities share the Las Vegas valley, Henderson has its own city government, police department, parks system, and municipal services. This distinction matters for property taxes, city services, and community identity, as Henderson residents benefit from the city's strong safety record and well funded public infrastructure.

Nearby Landmarks and Access Points

Seven Hills' position in southern Henderson places it near several key landmarks. The Serket Golf Club (formerly Rio Secco Golf Club) is located within the community itself. Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area borders the community to the south and west. Henderson Executive Airport sits less than a mile away. The M Resort Spa Casino is a short drive south on Las Vegas Boulevard. Major grocery shopping and dining options are concentrated along the Eastern Avenue and St. Rose Parkway corridors east of the community.

Local Expert Insight

Ryan Rose points out that Seven Hills' location delivers a rare combination of suburban quiet and metropolitan access. Living seven miles from the Strip means residents can reach world class dining, entertainment, and employment in 15 to 20 minutes while returning home to a community that feels removed from the energy of the Las Vegas corridor. The southern Henderson position also means less traffic congestion than communities located closer to the Strip or along the busy I-15 corridor.
