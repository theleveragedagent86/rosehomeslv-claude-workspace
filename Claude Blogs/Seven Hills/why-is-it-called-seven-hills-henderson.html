When Was Seven Hills Henderson Built?

Neighborhoods in Seven Hills Henderson

Seven Hills Henderson gets its name from the seven prominent hills and ridgelines that define the community's terrain in the southern foothills of Henderson. The community was developed on approximately 1,300 acres of rolling desert hillside, and the distinct topographic features that give the area its character also inspired the name that the developers chose when planning the master planned community in the mid 1990s.

Italian Inspiration

The Seven Hills name also draws a parallel to the famous Seven Hills of Rome, reflecting the Italian and Tuscan design theme that runs throughout the community. Neighborhood names like Venezia, Rapallo, Terracina, Palazzo Monte, Crescendo, Puccini, Vivaldi, Sonata, and Allegro all reference Italian culture, music, and geography. The community's architectural standards emphasize Italian and Mediterranean design elements including stucco exteriors, tile roofs, and wrought iron details, creating a visual connection to the Tuscan countryside that the developers envisioned.

The Terrain Advantage

The hilly terrain that gives Seven Hills its name is also one of the community's greatest assets. The elevation changes across the 1,300 acre footprint create natural viewpoints throughout the community, with many homes enjoying panoramic views of the Las Vegas Strip, the surrounding mountain ranges, and the Serket Golf Club fairways. The varied topography also allowed developers to create distinct neighborhoods at different elevations, giving each section of the community its own character while maintaining the cohesive Italian design language.
