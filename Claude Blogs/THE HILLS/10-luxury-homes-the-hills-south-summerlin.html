Luxury homes in The Hills South Summerlin include custom estates ranging from 2,924 to over 20,000 square feet, situated on lots up to two thirds of an acre within four guard gated communities. This 725 acre village in Summerlin delivers some of the most prestigious residential properties in all of Las Vegas. ## What Types of Luxury Homes Are Available? The Hills South offers three tiers of luxury living. At the entry level, Corta Bella provides 126 production luxury homes built by Coleman Homes (now Toll Brothers) with 24 hour guard gate access. These homes feature consistent architectural quality and are ideal for buyers who want gated security without a custom build price tag. The mid tier includes custom homes in Eagle Hills and Tournament Hills. Eagle Hills contains 160 custom residences on lots of one third to two thirds of an acre, with home sizes spanning 2,924 to over 14,000 square feet. Tournament Hills offers approximately 132 custom homes with direct proximity to TPC Summerlin's championship fairways. Architectural styles across both communities range from contemporary and Mediterranean to classic desert modern. At the pinnacle, Country Club Hills houses roughly 175 homes including "The Enclave," known locally as Billionaire's Row. This section contains seven of the largest and most expensive homes in Las Vegas, with estates exceeding 20,000 square feet and price tags well above $10 million. ## What Features Define Hills South Luxury Estates? Custom homes in The Hills South were built between 1991 and 2000, and many have undergone extensive renovations to match current luxury standards. Common features include resort style pools, home theaters, wine cellars, multi car garages, and chef's kitchens. Homes backing TPC Summerlin offer sweeping golf course views, and properties along the western edges enjoy Red Rock Canyon sightlines. Every guard gated community provides 24 hour staffed security with controlled visitor access and roving patrols. Eagle Hills also includes a private three acre community park, adding green space that most luxury enclaves lack. The combination of large lots, mature landscaping, and mountain views creates a sense of privacy that is rare this close to the Las Vegas Strip. ## Why Do Luxury Buyers Choose The Hills South? Location drives much of the appeal. TPC Summerlin, the Bobby Weed designed course where Tiger Woods won his first PGA Tour event in 1996, anchors the community. The annual Shriners Children's Open brings national attention to the neighborhood.
