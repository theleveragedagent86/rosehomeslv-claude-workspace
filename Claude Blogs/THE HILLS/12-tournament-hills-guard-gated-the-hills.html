Tournament Hills is a guard-gated enclave of approximately 132 custom luxury homes within The Hills South village of Summerlin, prized for its direct adjacency to TPC Summerlin golf course. Residents here enjoy some of the most coveted golf course views in all of Las Vegas. ## What Sets Tournament Hills Apart from Other Guard-Gated Communities? Location is the defining advantage. Tournament Hills sits directly alongside TPC Summerlin, the championship course that hosts the annual Shriners Children's Open and where Tiger Woods captured his first PGA Tour victory in 1996. Many homes in the community back directly onto the fairways, offering unobstructed views of manicured greens and the surrounding mountain landscape. The community features roughly 132 custom built residences, each reflecting the individual tastes of their original owners. You'll find a mix of architectural styles, from sleek contemporary designs to classic Mediterranean estates, all set on generous lots that provide genuine separation between neighbors. The 24 hour staffed guard gate controls all entry points, ensuring privacy and security around the clock. ## How Much Does It Cost to Live in Tournament Hills? Tournament Hills commands premium pricing, which reflects both the guard gated security and the golf course proximity. The sub HOA runs approximately $480 per month, with an additional $47 per month for the Summerlin master planned community assessment. These fees cover guard gate staffing, common area maintenance, and access to Summerlin's broader amenity network. Home prices in Tournament Hills vary significantly based on lot position, square footage, and whether the property offers direct golf course frontage. Golf course facing homes consistently command a premium over interior lots. Buyers should expect pricing that places Tournament Hills among the upper tier of Summerlin's luxury market. ## What Is TPC Summerlin and Why Does It Matter? TPC Summerlin is a par 72, 7,243 yard course designed by Bobby Weed in collaboration with Fuzzy Zoeller. It opened in 1991 and quickly became one of Nevada's most recognized golf venues. The course earned distinction as the first in Nevada to receive Audubon Cooperative Sanctuary certification, reflecting its commitment to environmental stewardship. Living next to a course of this caliber adds lasting value to Tournament Hills properties. ## A Local Perspective on Tournament Hills Ryan Rose frequently works with buyers drawn to Tournament Hills for its rare combination of golf course living and guard gated exclusivity.
