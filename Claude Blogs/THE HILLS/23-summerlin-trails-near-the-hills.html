Residents of The Hills in Summerlin have direct access to one of the most extensive trail systems in the Las Vegas Valley. Summerlin's 200+ mile interconnected network of walking, biking, and jogging trails connects The Hills to parks, shopping centers, schools, and neighboring communities without ever requiring a car. ## How Big Is the Summerlin Trail System Near The Hills? The Summerlin trail system spans over 200 miles and continues to grow as new sections of the master planned community develop. Three distinct trail types serve different needs. Village trails run eight feet wide through natural arroyos and open space corridors, providing a more scenic, nature focused experience. Street side trails measure five to eight feet wide, set back from roads with professional landscaping. Dedicated bike routes run four to six feet wide along arterial and collector roads for cycling commuters and fitness riders. From The Hills, residents can step onto village trails that wind through washes and green belts, connecting to The Hills Park and eventually reaching Summerlin's commercial centers and other village parks. ## What Can You Do on the Trails Near The Hills? The trail system supports walking, jogging, cycling, and dog walking. In a community survey, 89% of Summerlin residents cited walking trails as the most important amenity, which speaks to how central these paths are to daily life. Morning joggers, after dinner walkers, and weekend cyclists all share the network comfortably thanks to its generous width and multiple route options. The trails also serve a practical transportation function. Families in The Hills can walk or bike to nearby schools, and the connected network reaches retail and dining options without navigating busy streets. ## Are the Trails Well Maintained? Summerlin's trail system is maintained by the Summerlin Council, which oversees landscaping, surface quality, and signage throughout the network. The street side trails feature desert adapted plantings and consistent upkeep that keeps paths clear year round. Trail maps and wayfinding markers help newcomers navigate the system with confidence. ## A Local Perspective on Summerlin Trails Ryan Rose frequently recommends The Hills to buyers who prioritize an active outdoor lifestyle, and the trail system is a major reason why.
