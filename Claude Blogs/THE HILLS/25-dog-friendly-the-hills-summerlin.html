The Hills in Summerlin is one of the most dog friendly neighborhoods in the Las Vegas Valley, thanks to over 200 miles of connected walking trails, multiple parks with open green spaces, and a community culture that embraces pet ownership. Dog owners here enjoy daily walking options that most Las Vegas neighborhoods simply cannot provide. ## What Makes The Hills Summerlin Dog Friendly? The single biggest advantage for dog owners in The Hills is direct access to Summerlin's 200+ mile trail system. These eight foot wide village trails wind through natural arroyos and landscaped corridors, providing varied routes for daily walks that never get repetitive. Street side trails add even more options, with five to eight foot wide paths set back from traffic for safer walks with pets. The Hills Park, a seven acre community park on Hillpointe Drive, offers open lawn areas where dogs can stretch their legs. Multiple picnic ramadas throughout the park give owners shaded spots to rest during warmer months. Inside the guard gated Eagle Hills enclave, a private three acre park provides an even quieter option for residents of that community. ## Are There Good Walking Routes for Dogs Near The Hills? The trail system connects The Hills to surrounding Summerlin villages, parks, and commercial areas. Dog owners can build routes of any length, from a quick 15 minute loop to a multi mile morning trek. The connected network means you can vary your path daily, which keeps walks engaging for both owner and pet. In a Summerlin community survey, 89% of residents named walking trails as their most important amenity, and dog owners are a significant part of that number. Morning and evening walks are most popular during summer, when temperatures are more comfortable. The landscaped street side trails offer partial shade from mature desert plantings along many stretches. ## What Should Dog Owners Know Before Moving to The Hills? Summerlin's HOA communities, including The Hills, typically have pet policies that specify leash requirements and breed or size restrictions. Prospective buyers should review the specific CC&Rs for their target subdivision within The Hills before purchasing. Most areas require dogs to be leashed on trails and in common areas. ## A Local Perspective on Dog Friendly Living Ryan Rose works with many buyers who consider pet friendliness a top priority when choosing a neighborhood.
