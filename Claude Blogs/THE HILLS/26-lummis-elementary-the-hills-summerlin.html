Lummis Elementary School sits at the heart of The Hills in Summerlin, giving families in this established village a top rated neighborhood school within walking distance of home. ## What Makes Lummis Elementary a Strong School in Summerlin? Lummis Elementary earned a 9 out of 10 test score rating on GreatSchools, with 67.6% of students scoring proficient in English and 63.1% proficient in math. Both figures significantly outpace Nevada state averages of 45.6% and 35.4%, respectively. The school serves roughly 500 students in grades Pre K through 5, maintaining a manageable 19:1 student to teacher ratio that allows for more individualized attention. Located at 9000 Hillpointe Road in the 89134 zip code, Lummis was the first public school built in all of Summerlin when it opened in 1993. That history means deep roots in the community, and the campus has evolved alongside The Hills village for over three decades. ## What Programs Does Lummis Elementary Offer? Beyond standard academics, Lummis offers a Gifted and Talented program for advanced learners along with Project Lead the Way (PLTW) curriculum that introduces students to STEM concepts through hands on learning. Vocal music instruction rounds out the elective offerings. The school also maintains exceptionally low discipline issues, with an out of school suspension rate under 1% compared to the 6% state average. ## How Close Is Lummis Elementary to Homes in The Hills? The campus sits directly on Hillpointe Road, adjacent to The Hills Park and just steps from Becker Middle School. This cluster of schools and green space creates a family friendly corridor where children can walk or bike to class.
