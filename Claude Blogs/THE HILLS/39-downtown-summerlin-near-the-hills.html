Downtown Summerlin is a 400 acre open air shopping, dining, and entertainment destination with over 125 stores and restaurants. Located roughly 10 minutes by car from The Hills (about seven miles), it serves as the largest retail hub in the Summerlin master planned community and one of the most visited shopping destinations in the Las Vegas Valley. ## What Stores and Shopping Are at Downtown Summerlin? Downtown Summerlin's 125 plus tenant lineup spans major department stores, national brands, and local boutiques. The open air layout organizes retail along pedestrian friendly streets with landscaped walkways, fountains, and gathering spaces. Unlike enclosed malls, the design takes advantage of southern Nevada's 300 plus days of sunshine and keeps the shopping experience feeling open and relaxed. The scale of Downtown Summerlin means that most shopping needs can be handled in a single trip. Apparel, electronics, home goods, sporting goods, and specialty retailers all have a presence here. Seasonal pop up shops and rotating vendors add variety throughout the year. ## What Restaurants Are at Downtown Summerlin? The dining options at Downtown Summerlin go well beyond typical food court fare. True Food Kitchen serves a seasonal, health conscious menu that changes quarterly based on ingredient availability. Meraki Greek Grill offers family friendly Greek cuisine with fresh, made to order dishes. These sit alongside dozens of other restaurants spanning casual, fast casual, and full service dining. The restaurant patios along the main promenade create a social atmosphere, especially on weekend evenings. During cooler months, outdoor heaters extend patio season well into December and January. ## What Entertainment Options Does Downtown Summerlin Offer? Beyond retail and dining, Downtown Summerlin functions as a community gathering space. The center hosts concerts, holiday events, a weekly farmers market, and seasonal festivals that draw visitors from across the valley. A multiplex cinema provides movie options without driving to other parts of town.
