TPC Summerlin is a private championship golf course within The Hills South village in Summerlin. Designed by Bobby Weed with Fuzzy Zoeller as player consultant, this par 72, 7,243 yard course serves as the permanent home of the PGA Tour's Shriners Children's Open. ## What Makes TPC Summerlin One of Nevada's Top Golf Courses? TPC Summerlin opened in 1991 and quickly established itself as a premier desert golf experience. The course features bentgrass greens, natural desert washes, honey mesquite plantings, and water features woven through rugged Mojave terrain. Five sets of tees on each hole make the layout accessible to recreational golfers while still challenging touring professionals. Golf Digest named TPC Summerlin one of America's best golf courses, and Nevada Business Magazine ranked it the number one private golf course in Nevada in 2006. The club also holds the distinction of being the first golf course in Nevada certified as an Audubon Cooperative Sanctuary, reflecting its commitment to environmental stewardship across the property's desert landscape. ## What Amenities Does TPC Summerlin Offer Members? Beyond championship golf, TPC Summerlin operates as a full lifestyle club. The facility includes a state of the art lifestyle center with fitness and wellness programs, tennis courts, and a swimming pool. The TPC Grille offers Southwestern inspired dining with panoramic views of Red Rock Canyon and the 18th fairway, creating one of the most scenic dining experiences in Summerlin. Members also gain access to the TPC Passport program, which provides playing privileges at TPC courses nationwide. The club hosts corporate outings, charity events, and members only social gatherings throughout the year. ## How Does TPC Summerlin Affect The Hills South Real Estate?
