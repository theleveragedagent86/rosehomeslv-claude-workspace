Tiger Woods won his first PGA Tour victory at TPC Summerlin on October 6, 1996, during the Las Vegas Invitational. That moment put the course, located in The Hills South village of Summerlin, on the map as the place where golf's most dominant career began. ## How Did Tiger Woods Win His First PGA Tour Event at TPC Summerlin? The 1996 Las Vegas Invitational was a 90 hole tournament played across three courses, with the final rounds held at TPC Summerlin. Woods, just 20 years old and only weeks into his professional career, entered the final round trailing leader Davis Love III. He fired a closing round 64 to force a sudden death playoff, then birdied the first extra hole to claim victory. The win was Woods' first of 82 career PGA Tour victories, tying Sam Snead's all time record. He earned $297,000 for the victory, a modest sum compared to modern purses but a defining payday for the player who would reshape professional golf. ## Why Is the 1996 Las Vegas Invitational Historically Significant? Woods had turned professional just seven weeks before the Las Vegas Invitational and had already generated enormous buzz with strong finishes in his first few events. The victory at TPC Summerlin confirmed that the hype was justified. Within months, he would win the 1997 Masters by 12 strokes, but Las Vegas was where the winning started. The tournament also demonstrated that TPC Summerlin could produce dramatic, world class golf. The course's challenging desert layout provided the ideal stage for a playoff finish that remains one of the most replayed moments in PGA Tour history. ## What Is TPC Summerlin Like Today? TPC Summerlin continues to host the PGA Tour annually through the Shriners Children's Open. The par 72, 7,243 yard course designed by Bobby Weed maintains the same championship caliber that tested Woods and his competitors in 1996.
