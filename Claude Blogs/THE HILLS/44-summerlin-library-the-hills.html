The Summerlin Library and Performing Arts Center is located at 1771 Inner Circle Drive in the heart of The Hills village. This dual purpose facility combines a full service public library with a 287 seat performing arts center, offering residents convenient access to education, entertainment, and community events within walking distance of home. ## What Does the Summerlin Library Offer? The library provides a comprehensive range of services for all ages. Study rooms, public computers, free Wi Fi, and a bookstore are available throughout the facility. Specialized offerings include a Digital Memories Preservation Lab for converting personal photos and videos, 3D printing services, and K through 12 homework help programs. Regular storytimes serve younger children, while adult programming covers a variety of educational and cultural topics. The facility also features art galleries that rotate exhibitions from local and regional artists. Four study rooms in the Adult Services Department give students and professionals quiet workspace, and two larger reservable meeting spaces accommodate groups of various sizes. The library is open Monday through Thursday from 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. and Friday through Sunday from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. ## What Is the Summerlin Performing Arts Center? The 287 seat Performing Arts Center within the library building hosts live performances, lectures, film screenings, and community events throughout the year. Nevada Ballet Theatre has used this venue for performances and programming, bringing professional dance to a neighborhood setting. The intimate size of the theater creates a close connection between performers and audiences that larger venues cannot replicate. The combination of library resources and performing arts programming under one roof makes this facility unique among Las Vegas community centers. ## How Does the Library Benefit The Hills Residents? Located on Inner Circle Drive at the center of The Hills village, the library sits within easy reach of nearly every home in the community. ADA accessible parking is available directly in front of the main entrance, with additional parking on the north side. The cross streets of Summerlin Parkway and Town Center Drive provide quick access from surrounding villages. ## A Local Perspective on Community Culture in The Hills Ryan Rose highlights the Summerlin Library and Performing Arts Center as one of the defining features of The Hills village, where residents enjoy walkable access to cultural programming that most Las Vegas neighborhoods lack.
