Nevada Ballet Theatre operates its studios and administrative offices at 1651 Inner Circle in The Hills village of Summerlin. Founded in 1972, NBT has grown from a small group of Las Vegas Strip performers into a nationally recognized dance organization with deep roots in the Summerlin community. ## What Is Nevada Ballet Theatre? Nevada Ballet Theatre was founded in 1972 when Vassili Sulich, a principal dancer with the Tropicana Hotel's Folies Bergere, assembled ballet dancers from Las Vegas Boulevard's entertainment industry. They began presenting dance concerts at UNLV's Judy Bayley Theatre, and enthusiastic audience response quickly led to the formation of a volunteer board under founding chairman Nancy Houssels. Within a few years, NBT developed into a fully professional ballet company. Today, NBT serves as the resident ballet company of The Smith Center for the Performing Arts, where it stages major productions including George Balanchine's The Nutcracker, Romeo and Juliet, and original works. The company has collaborated with Cirque du Soleil on "A Choreographers' Showcase" and performs under the artistic direction of Roy Kaiser, who was appointed in 2017. ## What Programs Does NBT Offer at Its Summerlin Location? The School of NBT, headquartered at the Inner Circle facility in The Hills, provides dance training for students ranging from 18 months through adults. Programs include recreational classes, a pre professional track, a competition team, and summer intensives. NBT II serves as a bridge program for advanced students preparing for professional careers. The organization's mission centers on educating and inspiring audiences while providing premier dance training and access to professional performances. ## Why Does NBT's Presence Matter for The Hills? Having a professional arts organization headquartered in the neighborhood adds a cultural dimension that sets The Hills apart from other Summerlin villages. The Summerlin Library and Performing Arts Center at 1771 Inner Circle, just steps from NBT's studios, has hosted ballet performances and programming, creating a walkable arts corridor. This combination of professional dance and library arts programming gives The Hills a cultural identity unique in Las Vegas. ## A Local Perspective on Arts and Culture in The Hills Ryan Rose points to Nevada Ballet Theatre's presence as one of the features that give The Hills its distinctive neighborhood character.
