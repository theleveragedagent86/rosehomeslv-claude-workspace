Vassiliadis Elementary School in The Paseos, Summerlin

When a school ranks #5 among all elementary schools in Nevada, people pay attention. Billy and Rosemary Vassiliadis Elementary sits right inside The Paseos village, and it has quickly become one of the top reasons families want to live here.

What Makes Vassiliadis Elementary One of the Best Schools in Nevada?

The numbers speak for themselves. Vassiliadis earns an A minus from Niche, an 8 out of 10 from GreatSchools, and the #5 ranking among Nevada elementary schools from U.S. News. The school places in the top 1% statewide for both math and reading proficiency. That is not a typo. Top 1%.

Math proficiency reaches 82%, and reading proficiency hits 76%. These figures put Vassiliadis well ahead of district and state averages. The school opened in 2017, so the facilities are modern and purpose built for today's learning environment. With roughly 1,083 students and an 18 to 1 student teacher ratio, class sizes stay manageable.

Where Is Vassiliadis Elementary Located in The Paseos?

The school sits at 215 Antelope Ridge Drive, directly within The Paseos village. It borders Fox Hill Park, which gives students easy access to green space and athletic fields. Many families in the surrounding sub neighborhoods walk their kids to school each morning.

That location next to Fox Hill Park also means after school activities flow naturally into outdoor time. The park features playgrounds, open fields, and walking paths that families use year round.

How Does Vassiliadis Compare to Other Summerlin Elementary Schools?

Vassiliadis stands out even in a district known for strong schools. Its top 1% proficiency scores and recent construction give it advantages that older campuses simply cannot match. The Paseos spans 768 acres with nearly 30 sub neighborhoods, and Vassiliadis serves as the primary Pre K through 5 option for a large portion of those residents. The school feeds into Sig Rogich Middle School and Palo Verde High School, creating a strong academic pipeline.
