Staton Elementary School Near The Paseos in Summerlin

Families in The Paseos have access to several excellent public schools, and Ethel W. Staton Elementary is one of the nearby options that serves parts of this popular Summerlin village. Here is what parents should know before choosing a home in the area.

Which Paseos Neighborhoods Does Staton Elementary Serve?

Staton Elementary is a public K through 5 school that primarily serves the Barcelona neighborhood area within The Paseos. School zone boundaries in Clark County can shift, so confirming your specific address with the district before purchasing a home is always a smart move.

The Paseos covers 768 acres with nearly 30 sub neighborhoods, and different sections feed into different elementary schools. Staton handles the southeastern portions of the village while Givens and Vassiliadis serve other areas. Knowing which school your street is zoned for matters more than which village you technically live in.

What Is the School Pipeline from Staton Elementary?

Students at Staton Elementary typically feed into Sig Rogich Middle School, which ranks #1 among Clark County's 59 middle schools. From there, they move on to Palo Verde High School, ranked #19 in Nevada with a 96% graduation rate. That K through 12 pipeline gives families in The Paseos a consistent, high quality educational path.

This progression through strong schools is one of the biggest selling points for The Paseos. Parents can plan their children's entire public school experience within the same community, from kindergarten through senior year.

Why Do Families Consider School Zones Before Buying in The Paseos?

School assignments directly affect home values and family life. A home zoned for a top rated school will hold its value differently than one that is not. In The Paseos, the combination of Staton, Givens, and Vassiliadis at the elementary level gives buyers multiple strong options depending on which sub neighborhood they choose.
