Palo Verde High School Near The Paseos in Summerlin

For families in The Paseos, the public school journey culminates at Palo Verde High School. With a 96% graduation rate and strong academic metrics, this Summerlin campus gives students a solid foundation for college and careers.

What Are the Academics Like at Palo Verde High School?

Palo Verde earns a B+ from Niche and an 8 out of 10 from GreatSchools. U.S. News ranks it #19 among all high schools in Nevada. The average SAT score is 1240, and the average GPA sits at 3.47. That 96% graduation rate puts Palo Verde well above both state and national averages.

The school serves approximately 3,141 students across grades 9 through 12. Built in 1996, the campus has had decades to establish strong programs in academics, athletics, and extracurriculars. Students from The Paseos typically attend Palo Verde after completing middle school at Sig Rogich.

How Far Is Palo Verde High School from The Paseos?

Palo Verde sits at 333 Pavilion Center Drive, just a short drive from The Paseos village. The campus is right next to Sig Rogich Middle School, so families in the area benefit from having both schools on the same corridor. Most Paseos residents can reach the campus in under 10 minutes.

The location along Pavilion Center Drive also puts students close to shopping, dining, and recreation options in Downtown Summerlin. That convenience matters for teens who are starting to drive and build their own schedules.

What Is the Full K Through 12 School Pipeline in The Paseos?

The Paseos offers one of the most complete public school pipelines in Summerlin. Elementary students attend Givens, Vassiliadis, or Staton. They feed into Sig Rogich Middle School, which ranks #1 in Clark County. Then they finish at Palo Verde High School. Every step of that pipeline holds an 8 out of 10 GreatSchools rating or higher.

That consistency across all grade levels is rare. It gives families the confidence to invest in a home knowing their children will attend strong public schools from kindergarten through graduation without ever needing to consider a transfer or private school alternative.
