Faith Lutheran Middle and High School Near The Paseos

Faith Lutheran Middle and High School is one of the closest private school options for families in The Paseos, serving grades 6 through 12 with a faith based college preparatory curriculum. The campus is a short drive from the community, making it a convenient choice for Summerlin families seeking an alternative to public schools.

What Grades Does Faith Lutheran Serve?

Faith Lutheran covers grades 6 through 12, combining middle school and high school on a single campus. This structure allows students to transition seamlessly from 6th grade through graduation without switching schools. The school follows a Christian educational philosophy while maintaining rigorous academic standards. Students benefit from a consistent learning environment through some of the most formative years of their education.

What Makes Faith Lutheran a Popular Choice for Paseos Families?

Location plays a significant role. Faith Lutheran sits just a short drive from The Paseos, keeping daily commutes quick for parents and students. The school offers smaller class sizes compared to most Clark County public schools, giving students more individualized attention from teachers. Faith Lutheran also fields competitive athletic programs and provides a variety of extracurricular activities. The combination of academics, athletics, and a values based environment appeals to many Summerlin families.

How Does Faith Lutheran Compare to Other Private Schools Near The Paseos?

Faith Lutheran is one of five private schools within easy reach of The Paseos. It competes with Bishop Gorman High School for high school students, though Faith Lutheran also captures the middle school years starting in 6th grade. For families wanting a single campus from middle school through graduation, Faith Lutheran fills a unique niche. Adelson Educational Campus and The Meadows School offer broader grade ranges starting at kindergarten and pre kindergarten, while The Alexander Dawson School serves pre kindergarten through 8th grade.
