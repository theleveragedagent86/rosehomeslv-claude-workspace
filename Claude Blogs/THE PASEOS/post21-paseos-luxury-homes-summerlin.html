The Paseos Luxury Homes Over $1 Million

The Paseos offers some of Summerlin's most impressive luxury homes, with estate style properties ranging from just over $1 million to more than $2.6 million. These homes sit in premium neighborhoods with oversized lots, custom finishes, and access to guard gated privacy.

What Luxury Neighborhoods Are in The Paseos?

Two guard gated communities anchor the luxury market in The Paseos: Traccia and Santaluz. Both offer controlled access, enhanced privacy, and a higher caliber of construction. Los Altos is another standout, featuring homes between 3,296 and 3,821 square feet on lots that can exceed 23,000 square feet. Barcelona rounds out the top tier with homes averaging 3,500 to 4,000 square feet, built with the kind of open layouts and premium materials luxury buyers expect.

Who Built the Luxury Homes in The Paseos?

Toll Brothers and William Lyon Homes are the primary builders behind the luxury inventory in The Paseos. Toll Brothers is nationally recognized for its semi custom and fully custom estate homes, and their presence here reflects the caliber of the neighborhood. William Lyon Homes contributed elegant floor plans with upscale standard features. Both builders designed homes that take advantage of The Paseos' western views toward Red Rock Canyon and the village's roughly 3,000 foot elevation.

What Do Luxury Buyers Get in The Paseos?

Beyond square footage and lot size, luxury buyers in The Paseos gain access to a location that is difficult to replicate. The village sits at the western edge of Summerlin, backing up to Red Rock Canyon with trail access and dramatic desert scenery. The elevation provides temperatures roughly 10 degrees cooler than the Las Vegas valley floor. Top rated schools, proximity to Downtown Summerlin shopping, and Summerlin's consistent ranking as one of America's best master planned communities add lasting value to every purchase.

Local Insight

Luxury properties in The Paseos move differently than the broader market. Guard gated homes in Traccia and Santaluz tend to have longer days on market but command stronger per square foot pricing. Buyers in this tier should plan on patience and precision rather than speed.
