The Paseos Lot Sizes and Home Sizes

Lot sizes in The Paseos range from compact townhome footprints to estate lots exceeding 23,000 square feet, while home sizes span from 1,305 square feet to nearly 4,000 square feet. This variety across 30 plus sub communities gives buyers options at almost every scale.

What Are the Standard Lot Sizes in The Paseos?

Most single family lots in The Paseos fall between 8,000 and 10,000 square feet. These standard lots accommodate the village's most common home styles, typically three to four bedroom layouts with two car garages and private backyards. For buyers seeking larger properties, Los Altos offers lots ranging from approximately 9,000 to more than 23,000 square feet. These oversized parcels allow for expanded outdoor living, pools, and landscaping that standard lots cannot support.

What Home Sizes Are Available?

The smallest homes in The Paseos are Coronado condos at approximately 1,305 square feet. From there, the range expands through Santa Rosa townhomes at 1,488 to 2,220 square feet, mid range single family homes, and up to the Barcelona neighborhood where homes average 3,500 to 4,000 square feet. Los Altos single family homes range from 3,296 to 3,821 square feet. In total, more than 50 different home designs exist across The Paseos, built by seven major builders over two decades of development.

How Do Lot and Home Sizes Affect Value?

Lot size is one of the strongest value drivers in The Paseos. Homes on larger lots, particularly those with western exposure toward Red Rock Canyon, consistently command higher prices per square foot. Corner lots and cul de sac positions also carry premiums. The village's 3,000 foot elevation and backing to protected desert land mean that many of these lots offer views and open space that cannot be built over, providing long term value protection for homeowners.

Local Insight

When I advise buyers in The Paseos, lot size is often where the real value conversation begins. Two homes with identical square footage can differ by $100,000 or more based on lot dimensions, orientation, and neighboring views. Always look beyond the house itself and evaluate what the land offers.
