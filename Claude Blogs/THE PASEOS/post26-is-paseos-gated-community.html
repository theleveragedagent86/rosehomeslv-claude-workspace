Is The Paseos a Gated Community?

The Paseos village itself is not fully gated or guard gated. However, several individual neighborhoods within The Paseos are gated, and two of them, Traccia and Santaluz, feature full guard gate service with 24 hour staffed entry.

Does The Paseos Have Gates at Every Entrance?

No. The Paseos is an open access village within Summerlin, meaning the main streets and common areas are accessible to everyone. This is typical of most Summerlin villages, where the perimeter is open but individual neighborhoods can add their own gating. The village spans 768 acres with over 30 sub neighborhoods, and each community sets its own access controls through its sub association. You can drive through The Paseos freely, but entering a gated neighborhood requires authorization.

Which Neighborhoods in The Paseos Are Guard Gated?

Traccia and Santaluz are the two guard gated communities in The Paseos. Both feature staffed entry points where a guard verifies every visitor before allowing access. These neighborhoods sit at the luxury tier of the village, with homes typically priced well above the village median of $725,000 to $840,000. Guard gated communities offer the highest level of security and privacy available in The Paseos, making them popular with buyers who prioritize controlled access.

Are There Other Gated Neighborhoods Without Guard Gates?

Yes. Multiple neighborhoods in The Paseos use automatic gated entry systems that require a remote, key card, or code to enter. These communities offer controlled access without the expense of staffed guards. Gated neighborhoods typically carry slightly higher HOA dues to cover gate maintenance and repair. Buyers who want security without the premium of guard gate living often find these communities to be the right balance.
