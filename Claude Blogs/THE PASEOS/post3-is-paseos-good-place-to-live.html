Is The Paseos a Good Place to Live?

Yes, The Paseos is an excellent place to live, offering top rated schools, two exceptional parks, trail access to over 150 miles of paths, and a wide range of home styles within a well managed Summerlin master planned community. The village consistently ranks among the most popular neighborhoods in Summerlin West for families, professionals, and retirees.

What Makes The Paseos Stand Out From Other Summerlin Villages?

The Paseos spans 768 acres and includes more than 28 sub neighborhoods, giving residents a level of variety that few Summerlin villages can match. Homes range from affordable condos near $400,000 to luxury estates over $2.6 million, all within walking distance of community parks and trail connections. The village sits at approximately 3,000 feet of elevation near the base of Red Rock Canyon, which keeps summer temperatures about 10 degrees cooler than the valley floor. This combination of diversity, location, and natural beauty sets The Paseos apart.

Are The Schools Near The Paseos Any Good?

The Paseos is zoned for some of the highest rated public schools in Nevada. Vassiliadis Elementary holds an A minus rating and ranks number 5 in the state, with 82% of students proficient in math. Givens Elementary earns a B plus and sits in the top 5% statewide. Sig Rogich Middle School carries a B plus rating and holds the number 1 ranking in the Clark County School District. Palo Verde High School boasts a 96% graduation rate and an average SAT score of 1,240. Private options like Faith Lutheran, Bishop Gorman, and The Meadows School are also nearby.

What Amenities Are Available to Paseos Residents?

Fox Hill Park is an 18 acre destination park featuring a 32 foot climbing tower, zip line, disc golf course, and more than 20 play structures. The Paseos Park offers 12.5 acres of sports fields, basketball courts, a splash pad, and shaded picnic areas. The Paseos Trail System runs roughly 2 miles through the village and connects to the broader Summerlin Trail System. Downtown Summerlin, with its dining, shopping, and entertainment, is just a short drive away. Local Insight

What makes The Paseos truly livable is the neighborhood feel. Despite its size, the village has a cohesive community atmosphere. Families walk to parks after school, runners share the trail system in the early mornings, and the western mountain views remind you daily why you chose this corner of Las Vegas.
