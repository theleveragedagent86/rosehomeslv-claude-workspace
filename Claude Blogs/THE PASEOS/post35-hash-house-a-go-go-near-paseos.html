Hash House a Go Go Near The Paseos

Hash House a Go Go is a Las Vegas favorite known for massive portions, creative farm themed dishes, and some of the biggest pancakes in the city. The location near The Paseos in the Canyon Pointe area makes it one of the most convenient breakfast and brunch spots for Summerlin residents.

What Is Hash House a Go Go Known For?

Hash House a Go Go calls its menu "twisted farm food," and the name fits. Portions are enormous, with single plates easily feeding two people. The restaurant is famous for its oversized pancakes, which arrive larger than the plate itself and stacked high with toppings. Chicken and waffles, sage fried chicken, and farmhouse eggs are among the most ordered items. The menu extends beyond breakfast into lunch and dinner, but the morning offerings are what draw the biggest crowds.

How Far Is Hash House a Go Go from The Paseos?

The nearest Hash House a Go Go location sits in the Canyon Pointe shopping area along W Charleston Boulevard, roughly five to seven minutes by car from The Paseos. The drive is straightforward, heading east on Charleston with plenty of parking available at the center. Canyon Pointe also houses BJ's Restaurant and Brewhouse and other casual dining options, making it easy to combine errands with a meal.

Is Hash House a Go Go Good for Families?

Families from The Paseos make up a big part of the weekend crowd. The oversized portions work well for sharing between parents and kids, and the playful farm themed presentation keeps younger diners entertained. The atmosphere is casual and loud in the best way, so parents do not need to worry about keeping kids perfectly quiet. Weekend wait times can stretch past 30 minutes during peak brunch hours, so arriving early or on a weekday is the best strategy.

Local Insight

The Canyon Pointe location is close enough that it feels like a neighborhood spot rather than a destination.
