Canyon Pointe Shopping Near The Paseos

Canyon Pointe is the big box retail hub closest to The Paseos, covering 600,000 square feet across 62 acres at West Charleston Boulevard and the 215 Beltway. If you need bulk groceries, home improvement supplies, or electronics, this is where Paseos residents go.

What Stores Are at Canyon Pointe?

Canyon Pointe is built around major anchor tenants that handle large purchases and weekly stock up trips. Costco draws the heaviest traffic, serving as both a grocery source and a general merchandise warehouse. Best Buy covers electronics and appliances. Lowe's handles home improvement and building materials, which is especially useful for Paseos homeowners maintaining or upgrading their properties. Marshalls and Ross offer off price clothing and home goods. Office Depot serves home office and school supply needs. PetSmart rounds out the tenant list for pet owners in the area.

Why Do Paseos Residents Shop at Canyon Pointe?

The center fills the practical, high volume side of the shopping equation. While Downtown Summerlin, Tivoli Village, and Boca Park handle fashion, dining, and lifestyle, Canyon Pointe is where the work gets done. A single trip can cover groceries at Costco, a hardware run at Lowe's, and a tech purchase at Best Buy. The 62 acre layout provides ample parking, which matters when loading large items. The location at Charleston and the 215 offers quick access from The Paseos without fighting traffic deeper into the valley.

How Far Is Canyon Pointe from The Paseos?

Canyon Pointe is a short drive from The Paseos via the 215 Beltway or West Charleston Boulevard. Most residents reach it in under ten minutes. The center's position near the beltway interchange also makes it a convenient stop for commuters heading to and from work. Its location complements the Rampart Boulevard shopping corridor, giving Paseos residents a complete retail network within a tight radius.

Local Insight

Canyon Pointe is one of those centers that every Paseos household visits regularly, even if it does not get the same attention as Downtown Summerlin. Having Costco and Lowe's this close is a practical advantage that saves time and fuel costs over the course of a year. I always point it out to buyers who are focused on day to day convenience.
