Commute Times From The Paseos Summerlin

The Paseos offers some of the most convenient commute times in the Las Vegas Valley. Located in Summerlin West with direct access to the 215 Beltway, residents can reach the Strip in 20 to 25 minutes, the airport in 25 minutes, and downtown Las Vegas in about 15 minutes.

How Long Is the Commute to the Las Vegas Strip?

Most residents reach the Las Vegas Strip in 20 to 25 minutes via the 215 Beltway to Interstate 15 South. This route is reliable during standard commute hours, though the I 15 interchange near Spring Mountain Road can slow down between 4 and 6 PM on weekdays. Leaving before 7:30 AM or after 6:30 PM typically shaves five minutes off the trip. The Paseos location on the western edge of Summerlin puts residents on the 215 quickly, avoiding the surface street congestion that affects neighborhoods farther east.

What About Commutes to Henderson and Other Areas?

Henderson sits roughly 30 to 35 minutes from The Paseos, depending on traffic and the specific part of Henderson. The 215 Beltway provides a direct loop connecting Summerlin West to the southeast valley. North Las Vegas runs about 25 to 30 minutes via US 95. For residents working in the Summerlin business corridor along Town Center Drive, the commute drops to just 5 to 10 minutes, making local employment incredibly accessible.

Is The Paseos Good for Remote Workers and Retirees?

The Paseos serves remote workers and retirees particularly well. Quiet residential streets, minimal through traffic, and proximity to coffee shops and coworking spaces at Downtown Summerlin make daily routines easy. Errands to major shopping, medical offices at Summerlin Hospital, and dining options along the 215 corridor rarely take more than 10 minutes. The neighborhood layout means you spend less time in your car overall, even when you do need to leave the community.

Local Insight

What I appreciate most about The Paseos location is how rarely you sit in traffic. The western Summerlin position means you are driving against the flow during morning and evening rush hours. Most of my clients who work on the Strip or near the airport report consistent 25 minute commutes with very few surprises. That reliability makes a real difference in daily quality of life.
