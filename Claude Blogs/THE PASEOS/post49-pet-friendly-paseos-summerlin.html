Pet Friendly Living in The Paseos Summerlin

The Paseos is one of the most pet friendly neighborhoods in Summerlin, with over 150 miles of trails, multiple parks, and open spaces that make daily walks and outdoor time easy for dogs and their owners. The community layout was designed with an active, outdoor lifestyle in mind.

Where Can You Walk Dogs in The Paseos?

The Paseos connects to the Summerlin Trail System, which spans over 150 miles of paved and unpaved paths throughout the master planned community. These trails wind through neighborhoods, along washes, and into open desert areas, giving dog owners plenty of route variety. Within The Paseos itself, walking paths connect sub neighborhoods, parks, and common areas. The Paseos Park and Fox Hill Park both offer open grassy areas where dogs can stretch out on a leash. Morning and evening walks along the trail system are popular with residents year round.

Are There Dog Friendly Parks and Businesses Nearby?

Fox Hill Park features open green spaces and walking paths that work well for dogs. The Paseos Park provides additional room for leashed walks and outdoor play. Beyond the neighborhood, Tivoli Village has designated dog friendly areas where pets are welcome at outdoor patios and seating areas. Downtown Summerlin, located about one mile away, also accommodates leashed dogs in its outdoor walkways. Several veterinary clinics and pet supply stores serve the 89138 zip code, keeping essential pet services close to home.

What Are the HOA Pet Policies in The Paseos?

The Paseos does not have breed specific restrictions at the community level. However, individual sub neighborhoods within The Paseos may have their own HOA guidelines regarding the number of pets, weight limits, or leash requirements in common areas. Buyers should review the specific CC&Rs for their target sub community before purchasing. Most neighborhoods in The Paseos are welcoming to standard household pets, and the overall community culture is noticeably dog friendly based on the number of residents who walk their pets daily.

Local Insight

I walk The Paseos trails regularly and always see dozens of residents out with their dogs, especially in the cooler morning and evening hours. The trail connectivity here is a genuine lifestyle advantage for pet owners. You can walk a different route every day for weeks without repeating yourself. That kind of variety keeps both owners and pets engaged, and it builds a real sense of community among neighbors who share the paths.
