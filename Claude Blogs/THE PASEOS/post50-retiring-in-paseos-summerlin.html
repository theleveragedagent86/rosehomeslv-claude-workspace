Retiring in The Paseos Summerlin

The Paseos is an excellent choice for retirees seeking a quiet, well connected neighborhood in Las Vegas. With single story home options, walkable trails, proximity to medical care, and easy access to shopping and dining, it checks every box for comfortable retirement living.

What Housing Options Work Best for Retirees?

The Paseos offers several housing styles that suit retirees well. Coronado condos provide single story, low maintenance living with smaller floor plans ideal for downsizers. Single story detached homes are available in multiple sub neighborhoods for those who prefer a private yard without stairs. The variety of home sizes means retirees can choose based on their space needs, whether that means a compact condo or a larger single story home with room for visiting family. Lock and leave options in the attached home communities also appeal to snowbirds and frequent travelers.

How Close Is The Paseos to Medical Care?

Summerlin Hospital Medical Center sits just minutes from The Paseos at 657 N Town Center Drive. This 485 bed facility offers emergency services, surgical care, cardiac care, and a full range of outpatient services. Multiple medical office buildings along the Summerlin corridor provide primary care, dental, and specialist appointments within a short drive. The proximity to quality healthcare is a major factor for retirees evaluating neighborhoods, and The Paseos delivers on this front without question.

What Lifestyle Amenities Make The Paseos Good for Retirement?

The Paseos trail system connects to over 150 miles of paths across Summerlin, giving retirees safe, flat walking routes for daily exercise. Downtown Summerlin is roughly one mile away, offering shopping, dining, entertainment, and a weekly farmers market. TPC Summerlin and other golf courses are nearby for avid golfers. The neighborhood sits at approximately 3,000 feet elevation, which means summer temperatures run about 10 degrees cooler than lower parts of the valley. Red Rock Canyon is minutes away for scenic drives and light hiking.

Local Insight

I work with many retirees who choose The Paseos specifically for its balance of activity and calm. The trails keep you active, the medical campus is close enough to feel secure, and the shopping at Downtown Summerlin handles every errand without a long drive. Several of my retired clients tell me their social lives improved after moving here because the walkable community naturally brings neighbors together. The Paseos is not a 55 plus community, which means a healthy mix of families and retirees that keeps the neighborhood lively.
