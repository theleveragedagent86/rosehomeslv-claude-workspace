Best Time to Buy in The Paseos Summerlin

The best time to buy a home in The Paseos is typically during the winter months from November through January. Less competition, motivated sellers, and greater negotiation leverage make this window the smartest entry point for buyers in Summerlin West.

Why Is Winter the Best Season to Buy?

The Las Vegas housing market follows consistent seasonal patterns. Spring (March through May) brings peak activity with the most listings and the most competition, which drives prices higher. Summer slows down as extreme heat reduces showings and buyer urgency. Fall sees a modest pickup before tapering around Thanksgiving. Winter, particularly December and January, is when serious buyers gain the upper hand. Sellers listing during the holidays are often highly motivated, and fewer competing offers mean you can negotiate on price, closing costs, or repairs without the bidding wars common in spring.

What Does the 2026 Market Look Like for The Paseos?

The current Las Vegas market favors buyers more than it has in recent years. Inventory has loosened and negotiation power is returning to the buyer side. The Paseos median home price sits around $725K to $840K in the 89138 zip code, with 72% of homes owner occupied. This high owner occupancy rate means less investor driven volatility and more stable pricing. Interest rates remain a factor, but buyers who prioritize negotiation leverage over waiting for a specific rate often come out ahead in total cost.

Should You Wait or Buy Now?

Waiting for the "perfect" market conditions is a common trap. In The Paseos, desirable homes in guard gated communities like Traccia and Santaluz move quickly regardless of season. Unique floor plans and premium lot positions rarely sit on the market long. If you find the right home at a fair price, the seasonal discount of buying in winter may not apply because well priced homes attract offers year round. The strategic approach is to start your search in fall, stay active through winter, and be prepared to move quickly when the right property appears.

Local Insight

I have closed deals in The Paseos during every season, and the data backs up the winter advantage. My buyers who purchased between November and January consistently secured better terms than those competing in the spring rush. That said, I also tell clients not to pass on a great home just because it is April. The Paseos has limited turnover in its best sub neighborhoods, so a home in the right location at the right price is worth acting on regardless of the calendar.
