The Paseos Park: A 12.5 Acre Community Park in Summerlin

The Paseos Park is a 12.5 acre community park located at 12122 Desert Moon Road in the heart of The Paseos village, offering residents everything from splash pads to lighted basketball courts. It serves as a central gathering spot for families, athletes, and anyone who wants green space just steps from home.

What Amenities Does The Paseos Park Have?

The Paseos Park packs a surprising number of features into its 12.5 acres. A lighted full sized basketball court with six hoops gives pickup games room to run well into the evening. The sand volleyball court draws its own crowd, and junior soccer and tee ball fields keep younger athletes busy on weekends.

For families with small children, the interactive water play area (splash pad) is a standout during Summerlin's warm months. A dedicated playground sits nearby, and a motorized toy area gives kids space to ride battery powered cars without competing for sidewalk room. Covered picnic pavilions and open grass areas round out the park's offerings.

Does The Paseos Park Connect to the Summerlin Trail System?

It does. Paved trail connections at the park link directly into the greater Summerlin Trail System, which spans over 150 miles across the master planned community. Joggers, cyclists, and dog walkers can start at The Paseos Park and travel north or south through the village on dedicated paths. The Paseos Paseo Trail runs roughly two miles from one end of the village to the other, making the park a natural starting point for a morning loop.

Why Do Residents Love The Paseos Park?

With 56 reviews on Yelp and consistent praise, the park resonates with residents because it covers every age group. Young families gravitate toward the splash pad and playground. Teens and adults use the basketball and volleyball courts. Weekend picnics under the covered pavilions are a staple, especially in spring and fall when temperatures at The Paseos' 3,000 foot elevation sit about 10 degrees cooler than the valley floor.

Local Insight

Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert who works extensively in Summerlin, points out that The Paseos Park is one of those amenities buyers underestimate until they live near it. The combination of trail access, a splash pad, and lighted courts means families use it almost daily rather than driving across town for recreation.
