Fox Hill Park: The Adventure Park in The Paseos, Summerlin

Fox Hill Park is an 18 acre adventure park in The Paseos village of Summerlin, featuring over 20 play structures, a 32 foot climbing tower, and a zip line that make it one of the most unique parks in the Las Vegas valley. Located at 231 Antelope Ridge Drive and adjacent to Vassiliadis Elementary School, it draws families from across the city.

What Makes Fox Hill Park Different From Other Las Vegas Parks?

Fox Hill Park is not a typical neighborhood park with a swing set and a bench. The centerpiece is a 32 foot climbing tower surrounded by rope courses, parkour challenges, and a structure locals call "The Orange Beast," a giant climbing frame built for older kids and adventurous adults. A "live wire" zip line adds another element most valley parks simply do not offer.

The equipment list reads more like an outdoor fitness course: a Genesis XL Mast Net, Biggo Solo, Tornado Agility Course, Asteroid frame net, custom climbing fort, spinning machine, grass mounds, and giant slides. With 111 reviews on Yelp, Fox Hill Park consistently earns praise for keeping kids engaged for hours rather than minutes.

Is Fox Hill Park Good for Families With Young Children?

Absolutely. While the climbing tower and parkour elements challenge older kids, the park also includes custom swings, smaller play structures, and open grass areas for toddlers. Four covered picnic ramadas give parents shaded seating with clear sightlines to the play areas. Families regularly pack lunches and spend full mornings here, especially on cooler days when The Paseos' 3,000 foot elevation keeps temperatures comfortable.

Can You See the Las Vegas Strip From Fox Hill Park?

Yes. Fox Hill Park sits at an elevation that provides panoramic views of the Las Vegas Strip and the entire valley to the east. To the west, The Paseos backs up to Red Rock Canyon, so the backdrop includes red rock formations and the Spring Mountains. It is one of the best vantage points in all of Summerlin for scenery.
