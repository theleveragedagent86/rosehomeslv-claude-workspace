Fox Hill Park Disc Golf Course in The Paseos, Summerlin

Fox Hill Park in The Paseos features a free 18 hole disc golf course that winds through one of Summerlin's most scenic parks. Located at 231 Antelope Ridge Drive in Las Vegas, the course offers valley views, elevation changes, and a surprisingly challenging layout for a public park.

How Many Holes Does the Fox Hill Park Disc Golf Course Have?

The course has 18 holes spread across Fox Hill Park's 18 acres. The layout uses the park's natural terrain, including grass mounds and elevation changes, to create varied throwing lines. Players navigate around play structures, open fields, and landscaped areas, which keeps each hole distinct. The course is free to play and open during regular park hours.

Is the Fox Hill Park Disc Golf Course Good for Beginners?

The course works well for all skill levels. Beginners will find the open fairways forgiving, while experienced players can challenge themselves with longer throws and tighter lines on several holes. The park's 3,000 foot elevation in The Paseos means the air is slightly thinner, which can add a few feet of distance to drives. Bring water, especially during warmer months, though four covered picnic ramadas provide shaded rest stops between holes.

Where Can You Play Disc Golf Near Summerlin?

Fox Hill Park is one of the most accessible disc golf options on the west side of the Las Vegas valley. Its location inside The Paseos village means players who live in Summerlin can walk or bike to the course using the connected trail system. The park sits adjacent to Vassiliadis Elementary School and is surrounded by residential streets, so parking is straightforward. With panoramic views of the Las Vegas Strip to the east and Red Rock Canyon to the west, the scenery alone makes it worth the visit.
