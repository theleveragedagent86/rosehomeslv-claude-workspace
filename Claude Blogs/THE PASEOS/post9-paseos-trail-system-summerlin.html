The Paseos Trail System and Connections in Summerlin

The Paseos village in Summerlin has its own dedicated trail system spanning approximately two miles from north to south, with paved paths that connect directly into Summerlin's 150+ mile trail network. Whether you walk, jog, or bike, the trails start right outside your front door.

How Long Is The Paseos Trail System?

The Paseos Paseo Trail runs roughly two miles through the village on paved, multi use paths designed for walking, jogging, and cycling. The trail connects the village's two major parks: The Paseos Park (12.5 acres at 12122 Desert Moon Road) and Fox Hill Park (18 acres at 231 Antelope Ridge Drive). A popular route listed on AllTrails, the Paseos and Fox Hill Parks Path, covers 2.8 miles out and back and is rated easy with an estimated completion time of about 59 minutes.

Where Do The Paseos Trails Connect?

The village trails feed directly into the greater Summerlin Trail System, which covers over 150 miles across the entire master planned community. From The Paseos, you can head east toward the Summerlin Parkway corridor or south toward other Summerlin West villages without ever leaving a dedicated path. Trail connections at both The Paseos Park and Fox Hill Park serve as entry points to the broader network, making it easy to extend a short neighborhood walk into a longer workout.

Are The Paseos Trails Paved or Dirt?

The trails within The Paseos are fully paved, which makes them accessible for strollers, wheelchairs, and road bikes. The flat to gently rolling terrain suits all fitness levels. At 3,000 feet of elevation, The Paseos sits about 10 degrees cooler than the valley floor during summer, so early morning and evening walks remain comfortable even in July and August. The western edge of the village backs up to Red Rock Canyon, giving trail users mountain views throughout their route.

Local Insight

Ryan Rose considers The Paseos trail system one of the village's strongest selling points for active buyers. Many residents use the trails daily for commuting between parks, walking dogs, or fitting in a quick jog before work. The fact that you can step onto a paved path from nearly any street in the village and reach either park or the greater Summerlin network within minutes is a lifestyle advantage that shows up in resale value.
