Bruce Trent Park and Farmers Market Near The Pueblos

Bruce Trent Park, located off Vegas Drive just minutes from The Pueblos of Summerlin, hosts a popular Wednesday evening farmers market that brings local produce and artisan vendors to the neighborhood. This combination of park recreation and community market culture makes the area surrounding The Pueblos especially appealing for residents who value a connected lifestyle.

What Is the Bruce Trent Park Farmers Market?

The Wednesday evening farmers market at Bruce Trent Park draws vendors from across the Las Vegas valley who offer fresh produce, prepared foods, and locally made goods. The evening timing works well for residents who want to stop by after work or enjoy a casual outing as temperatures cool down. The market has become a weekly gathering point for families and neighbors in the Summerlin North area, adding a social element that goes beyond simple grocery shopping.

How Close Is Bruce Trent Park to The Pueblos of Summerlin?

Bruce Trent Park sits off Vegas Drive, placing it within a short drive of The Pueblos of Summerlin. The proximity means residents can make the farmers market part of their regular weekly routine without any significant commute. For those who already enjoy the 60+ acre Pueblo Park and its 3.7 mile trail system, Bruce Trent Park offers an additional outdoor destination with a distinctly different character and community focus.

What Else Does Bruce Trent Park Offer?

Beyond the farmers market, Bruce Trent Park provides standard recreational amenities including open green spaces, playground areas, and shaded picnic spots. The park serves as a complement to the larger Pueblo Park, giving residents of The Pueblos multiple nearby options for outdoor activity. Between the two parks, families in this part of Summerlin have access to a diverse range of recreation without driving across town.
