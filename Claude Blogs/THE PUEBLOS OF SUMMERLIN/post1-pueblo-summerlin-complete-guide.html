What Is The Pueblos of Summerlin?

The Pueblos of Summerlin is the original village of the Summerlin master plan, a 568 acre residential community in northwest Las Vegas that has anchored the area since 1992. As the first village ever built in Summerlin, The Pueblos set the standard for the neighborhoods that followed, and it remains one of the most established and sought after places to call home in the valley.

How Many Subdivisions Are in The Pueblos of Summerlin?

The Pueblos contains 18 distinct subdivisions, each with its own character and housing style. These include Alta Mira, Bonita Canyon, Buena Vista Townhomes, Hermosa, La Mancha, La Posada Condos, Mirada, Monterrey, Monterrey Este, The Plateau, Red Hills Condos, Santa Fe, Sedona, Sonoma, Tamarisk, Taos Estates, Valencia, and Vista Del Oro. Housing options range from condos starting around 900 square feet to single family homes exceeding 3,500 square feet. Architectural styles lean heavily into Spanish hacienda and desert contemporary design, with stucco exteriors, flat rooflines, and deep overhangs that complement the Mojave landscape.

What Amenities Does The Pueblos of Summerlin Offer?

One of the community's crown jewels is Pueblo Park, a 60 plus acre green space featuring playgrounds, basketball courts, ramadas, BBQ areas, a desert garden, and a 3.7 mile trail loop. The village is managed by the Summerlin North Community Association and sits as the easternmost village in Summerlin, providing quick access to both the 215 Beltway and US 95. Residents enjoy proximity to Downtown Summerlin, Tivoli Village, Pueblo Place Shopping Center, and a wide selection of local restaurants. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center, a 485 bed facility, is also nearby.

Who Lives in The Pueblos of Summerlin?

The Pueblos attracts a diverse mix of residents, from young professionals and growing families to retirees. The Destinations Pueblo 55 plus community caters specifically to active adults, while families benefit from in village schools like Bryan Elementary, The Meadows School, and St. Elizabeth Ann Seton. Two subdivisions, La Posada and Red Hills, offer gated living for those who prefer added privacy. Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert specializing in Summerlin communities, often points to The Pueblos as one of the best values in the master plan for buyers who want established landscaping, mature trees, and a true neighborhood feel.

Local Insight

What many buyers overlook about The Pueblos is its location advantage. As the easternmost village in Summerlin, commute times to Downtown Las Vegas run about 15 minutes, and you can reach the Strip in 20 to 25 minutes. That is noticeably faster than communities deeper into Summerlin. The mature landscaping throughout the village also gives it a warmth and character that newer builds simply cannot replicate.
