Richard H. Bryan Elementary School: A Top Choice for Families in The Pueblos of Summerlin

Richard H. Bryan Elementary School sits right inside The Pueblos village of Summerlin, giving families a walkable, well regarded public school option for grades K through 5. Since opening in 1996, Bryan Elementary has served as a cornerstone of this tightly knit community in the 89128 zip code.

What Grade Levels Does Bryan Elementary School Serve?

Bryan Elementary is a Clark County School District (CCSD) campus serving students in kindergarten through fifth grade. The school opened in 1996 to meet growing demand as Summerlin North expanded westward. Its location within The Pueblos village means many students live just minutes from campus, reducing commute times and making after school activities more accessible. Families in surrounding subdivisions regularly walk or bike to drop off.

Why Do Pueblo Families Choose Bryan Elementary?

Convenience is a major factor; Bryan Elementary is one of only three schools located within The Pueblos village boundaries. That proximity fosters strong parent involvement and a genuine neighborhood school atmosphere. The broader Summerlin area offers access to 16 public and 11 private schools, but having a quality elementary campus right in the neighborhood is a standout benefit. Ryan Rose often highlights Bryan Elementary when advising families who are relocating to The Pueblos because walkability to school is a quality of life upgrade that never gets old.

What Other Schools Are Near The Pueblos of Summerlin?

Beyond Bryan Elementary, The Pueblos village is also home to The Meadows School (a private PK through 12 college preparatory campus) and St. Elizabeth Ann Seton Catholic School (a private K through 8 program). For middle school, students typically feed into Ernest A. Becker Sr. Middle School, and Cimarron Memorial High School serves the area for grades 9 through 12. This range of options means families can stay in one neighborhood from preschool all the way through graduation.
