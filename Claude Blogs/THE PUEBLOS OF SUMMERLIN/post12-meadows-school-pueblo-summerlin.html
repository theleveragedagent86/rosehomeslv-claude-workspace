The Meadows School: Private College Prep Education in The Pueblos of Summerlin

The Meadows School is a private, non sectarian, college preparatory campus located right inside The Pueblos village of Summerlin, serving students from pre kindergarten through 12th grade. Founded in 1988, it remains one of the most respected independent schools in the Las Vegas valley.

What Makes The Meadows School Stand Out in Summerlin?

The Meadows School is a nonprofit institution with a rigorous college preparatory curriculum that spans PK through 12. Its campus sits within The Pueblos village boundaries, making it one of only three schools physically located inside this Summerlin North neighborhood. The school predates much of the surrounding residential development, having opened four years before Summerlin's master plan community began its major expansion in the early 1990s. That long track record gives families confidence in both academic quality and institutional stability.

Is The Meadows School a Good Fit for Pueblo Families?

For families who want a single campus experience from preschool through high school graduation, The Meadows School eliminates the transitions that come with switching between elementary, middle, and high school. The non sectarian approach welcomes students of all backgrounds, and the college prep focus prepares graduates for competitive university admissions. Ryan Rose frequently works with families relocating to The Pueblos specifically because The Meadows School is within walking distance, a convenience that simplifies daily routines and adds real value to nearby homes.

How Does The Meadows Compare to Other Schools Near The Pueblos?

The Pueblos of Summerlin offers an unusual concentration of school choices. In addition to The Meadows School, the village is home to Richard H. Bryan Elementary (CCSD, K through 5) and St. Elizabeth Ann Seton Catholic School (private, K through 8). Public middle and high school students are served by Becker Middle School and Cimarron Memorial High School nearby. The broader area provides access to 16 public and 11 private campuses, but few neighborhoods can match having a PK through 12 private school within their own borders.
