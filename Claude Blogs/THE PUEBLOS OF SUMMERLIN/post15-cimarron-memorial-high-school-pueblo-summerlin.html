Cimarron Memorial High School: The Public High School for The Pueblos of Summerlin

Cimarron Memorial High School is the CCSD campus serving The Pueblos of Summerlin for grades 9 through 12, giving families a nearby public high school option that completes the neighborhood's K through 12 school pipeline. Located close to the village in Summerlin North, Cimarron Memorial anchors the secondary education pathway for the 89128 zip code.

What Grades Does Cimarron Memorial High School Serve?

Cimarron Memorial is a Clark County School District high school enrolling students in ninth through twelfth grade. The campus serves multiple Summerlin North neighborhoods, with The Pueblos village being one of its primary feeder areas. Students from Becker Middle School typically transition to Cimarron Memorial, maintaining friendships and social connections that developed during middle school. The school offers a full range of academic programs, extracurriculars, and athletics.

How Does Cimarron Memorial Connect to The Pueblos School Pipeline?

The Pueblos of Summerlin benefits from a straightforward public school progression: Bryan Elementary (K through 5), Becker Middle School (6 through 8), and Cimarron Memorial High School (9 through 12). This clear feeder pattern means families who buy in The Pueblos can plan their children's entire public school experience without relocating. Ryan Rose notes that this continuity is a deciding factor for many families who choose The Pueblos over other Summerlin villages where school assignments may be less predictable.

Are There Other High School Options Near The Pueblos?

Families seeking alternatives to Cimarron Memorial have several choices. The Meadows School, located within The Pueblos village itself, is a private PK through 12 campus with a college preparatory focus. Palo Verde High School is another CCSD option in the broader Summerlin area. With 16 public and 11 private schools accessible from The Pueblos, families are not limited to a single path. The variety of choices across both public and private sectors is one of the reasons the neighborhood appeals to education focused buyers.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

High school zoning is often the first question I get from families with teenagers who are considering The Pueblos. Cimarron Memorial's proximity and the seamless feeder pattern from Bryan Elementary through Becker Middle School give parents peace of mind. That school stability translates directly into neighborhood stability, and it is one of the reasons The Pueblos continues to attract long term residents rather than short term investors.
