The Pueblos of Summerlin Home Prices 2026

Home prices in The Pueblos of Summerlin currently range from the upper $300Ks for condos and townhomes to $900K and above for larger single family residences, with a median price hovering around $430,000. As the first village built in the Summerlin master plan, The Pueblos offers some of the most competitive pricing in the community while still delivering the full Summerlin lifestyle.

What Do Single Family Homes Cost in The Pueblos of Summerlin?

Single family homes in The Pueblos typically list between $600,000 and $850,000 in early 2026. These homes range from approximately 1,003 to 3,538 square feet and sit on lots averaging 0.12 to 0.18 acres. Subdivisions like Taos Estates and Monterrey tend to command higher prices due to larger floor plans and premium lot positions. Architectural styles throughout the village feature stucco construction with Spanish hacienda and desert contemporary design elements.

What Are Condo and Townhome Prices in The Pueblos?

Buyers looking for more affordable entry points will find condos in La Posada and Red Hills starting in the upper $300Ks, with units ranging from 900 to 1,300 square feet. Both of these subdivisions are gated, adding a layer of privacy and security. Buena Vista Townhomes offer a middle ground, with floor plans between 1,200 and 1,800 square feet. These attached options make The Pueblos accessible to first time buyers, downsizers, and investors alike.

How Do The Pueblos Prices Compare to Other Summerlin Villages?

Because all homes in The Pueblos are resale properties built in the early 1990s, pricing tends to sit below newer Summerlin villages like Stonebridge or The Paseos. However, the trade off comes with mature landscaping, established neighborhoods, and larger lot sizes that newer construction rarely offers. Ryan Rose frequently works with buyers who discover that The Pueblos delivers more square footage per dollar than many of its Summerlin neighbors.

Local Insight

The key pricing factor most buyers miss in The Pueblos is the sub association fee structure. Beyond the $74 per month master HOA, many subdivisions charge an additional $50 to $150 per month. When comparing home values, always factor in total monthly costs. That said, the overall cost of ownership here remains very competitive for Summerlin, especially considering the village's prime eastside location and access to Pueblo Park's 60 plus acres.
