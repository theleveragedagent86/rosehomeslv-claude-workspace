Gated Communities in The Pueblos: La Posada and Red Hills

If you want gated living within The Pueblos of Summerlin, La Posada and Red Hills are your two options, and both deliver resort style amenities in a secure, low maintenance setting. These condo communities sit within the original Summerlin village and offer a lifestyle that appeals to buyers seeking privacy without sacrificing convenience.

What Makes La Posada a Standout Gated Community?

La Posada is a gated condominium community within The Pueblos that includes a pool, clubhouse, exercise room, and dedicated guest parking. Units feature the same Southwestern architectural character found throughout the village: adobe colored stucco, flat rooflines, and desert landscaping. Condos here typically range from 900 to 1,300 square feet, making them well suited for singles, couples, or downsizers who want a manageable footprint. The gated entry provides controlled access while the shared amenities create a built in social atmosphere.

What Does Red Hills Offer Residents?

Red Hills is the second gated condo community in The Pueblos, and it takes the amenity package a step further with a resort style pool, hot tub, exercise room, clubhouse, and guest parking. Like La Posada, Red Hills condos fall in the 900 to 1,300 square foot range with two to three bedrooms. The community shares the same Spanish hacienda aesthetic with thick walls, covered porches, and drought resistant landscaping. For buyers comparing the two, Red Hills tends to appeal to those who prioritize outdoor amenity space, particularly the hot tub and larger pool area.

How Do Prices Compare in La Posada and Red Hills?

Both communities fall on the more affordable end of The Pueblos pricing spectrum, with units generally starting in the upper $300Ks. This positions them well below the median price of roughly $430,000 for the broader neighborhood, making them an accessible entry point into Summerlin. Ryan Rose frequently works with buyers exploring these gated options, noting that the combination of controlled access, resort amenities, and Summerlin location creates strong long term value.

Is Gated Living in The Pueblos Worth It?

For buyers who value security, community amenities, and lower maintenance responsibilities, La Posada and Red Hills check every box. The gated format means less yard work, shared upkeep costs, and a quieter day to day environment. Both communities are all resale, with homes dating to the early 1990s, so expect mature landscaping and an established neighborhood feel that newer builds cannot match.
