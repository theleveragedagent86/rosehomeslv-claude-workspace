Condos and Townhomes in The Pueblos of Summerlin

The Pueblos of Summerlin offers some of the most affordable attached housing in all of Summerlin, with condos in La Posada and Red Hills plus townhomes in Buena Vista giving buyers three distinct options. These communities combine Southwestern architecture with resort style amenities at price points well below the neighborhood median.

What Condos Are Available in The Pueblos of Summerlin?

Two gated condo communities serve The Pueblos: La Posada and Red Hills. Both feature units ranging from 900 to 1,300 square feet with two to three bedrooms and two bathrooms. La Posada includes a pool, clubhouse, exercise room, and guest parking behind its gated entry. Red Hills matches those amenities and adds a resort style pool, hot tub, and larger clubhouse space. Condos in both communities feature the signature Pueblos aesthetic: adobe colored stucco exteriors, flat rooflines, and drought resistant landscaping that blends with the surrounding desert.

What Are Buena Vista Townhomes Like?

Buena Vista is The Pueblos' dedicated townhome subdivision, offering attached units between 1,200 and 1,800 square feet. These townhomes come with attached garages, a feature that distinguishes them from the condo communities. Buena Vista residents also enjoy a community clubhouse, pool, spa, and exercise room. The additional square footage and garage access make Buena Vista a popular choice for buyers who want more space than a condo provides while still avoiding the full maintenance responsibilities of a detached single family home.

How Much Do Condos and Townhomes Cost in The Pueblos?

Attached housing in The Pueblos generally starts in the upper $300Ks, positioning these properties as some of the most accessible options in Summerlin. The neighborhood median sits around $430,000, and many condo and townhome units fall right at or below that mark. Ryan Rose often recommends these communities to first time buyers and downsizers who want the Summerlin lifestyle, established landscaping, and community amenities without stretching into higher price brackets.

Are Condos and Townhomes in The Pueblos a Good Investment?

All properties in The Pueblos are resale, dating back to the early 1990s. That maturity is actually an advantage: buyers get proven community infrastructure, established HOA management through the Summerlin North Community Association, and a neighborhood track record spanning more than three decades. The combination of gated security (in La Posada and Red Hills), resort amenities, and a prime Summerlin location continues to support steady demand for attached housing in the village.
