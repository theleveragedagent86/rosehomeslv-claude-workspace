Is The Pueblos of Summerlin a Good Place to Live?

Yes, The Pueblos of Summerlin is an excellent place to live, offering one of the most established and well rounded residential experiences in all of Las Vegas. As the first village built in the Summerlin master plan in 1992, this 568 acre community has had over three decades to mature into a neighborhood with real character, strong schools, and enviable amenities.

What Makes The Pueblos of Summerlin a Great Neighborhood?

Safety is one of the top reasons residents love living here. The Pueblos sits in an area that ranks in the 82nd percentile nationally for safety, with violent crime rates running 70 to 85 percent below national averages. The community also benefits from its location as the easternmost village in Summerlin, which means shorter commute times than most Summerlin neighborhoods. Downtown Las Vegas is roughly 15 minutes away, and the Strip is a 20 to 25 minute drive.

What Amenities Are Available to Residents?

Pueblo Park anchors the community with over 60 acres of green space, including playgrounds, basketball courts, a desert garden, ramadas, BBQ areas, and a popular 3.7 mile trail loop. Everyday conveniences are within minutes, with Pueblo Place Shopping Center, Downtown Summerlin, and Tivoli Village all nearby. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center, a 485 bed facility, provides major medical care just a short drive away. The village's 18 subdivisions offer everything from gated condos to spacious single family homes, so there is a fit for nearly every lifestyle and budget.

Are the Schools Near The Pueblos of Summerlin Good?

Families benefit from multiple school options right within the village. Bryan Elementary serves the community as a public K through 5 school. The Meadows School, a well regarded private institution, covers pre K through 12th grade. St. Elizabeth Ann Seton provides a private Catholic education for grades K through 8. For older students, Becker Middle School, Cimarron Memorial High School, and Palo Verde High School are all nearby. Ryan Rose notes that this concentration of quality schools within walking or short driving distance is one of the features that consistently draws families to The Pueblos.

Local Insight

One thing that sets The Pueblos apart from newer Summerlin villages is the tree canopy. After 30 plus years of growth, the mature landscaping creates shaded streets and a neighborhood atmosphere that new construction simply cannot match. Residents also appreciate the sense of community here; neighbors know each other, and the parks are genuinely used rather than just decorative.
