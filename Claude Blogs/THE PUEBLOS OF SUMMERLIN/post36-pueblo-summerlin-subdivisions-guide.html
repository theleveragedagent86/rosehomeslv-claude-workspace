All 18 Subdivisions in The Pueblos of Summerlin

The Pueblos of Summerlin contains 18 distinct subdivisions spread across 568 acres, making it one of the most diverse villages in all of Summerlin. Whether you want a single family home, a townhome, or a gated condo, this original Summerlin village has a subdivision that fits.

What Are the 18 Subdivisions in The Pueblos?

The complete list includes Alta Mira, Bonita Canyon, Buena Vista Townhomes, Hermosa, La Mancha, La Posada Condos, Mirada, Monterrey, Monterrey Este, The Plateau, Red Hills Condos, Santa Fe, Sedona, Sonoma, Tamarisk, Taos Estates, Valencia, and Vista Del Oro. Each carries the ancestral pueblo architectural theme with adobe stucco exteriors, flat rooflines, deep overhangs, and covered porches. Homes date back to 1992, when The Pueblos became the first village developed in Summerlin by The Howard Hughes Corporation.

Which Subdivisions Offer Condos and Townhomes?

Buyers seeking attached housing have three options. Buena Vista is a townhome community with a clubhouse, pool, and spa. La Posada is a gated condo community that includes a pool, clubhouse, and controlled access. Red Hills is the second gated condo option, featuring a resort style pool, hot tub, exercise room, and guest parking. These three communities fall on the more affordable side of The Pueblos, with pricing starting in the upper $300Ks.

How Do Single Family Subdivisions Differ?

The remaining 15 subdivisions consist of single family homes that range from the upper $300Ks to over $900K. Taos Estates sits at the top of the price spectrum, offering larger lots and custom finishes. Neighborhoods like Valencia, Sedona, and Santa Fe provide mid range options with strong resale appeal. Ryan Rose often points out that the variety across these 15 communities means buyers can stay within The Pueblos as their needs change, moving up or downsizing without leaving the village.

What Ties All 18 Subdivisions Together?

Every subdivision falls under the Summerlin North Community Association, managed through the Summerlink portal. The master HOA is $74 per month as of January 2026, which includes a $37 Summerlin Council portion covering trails, parks, and community events. Sub association fees range from $50 to $150 per month depending on the subdivision and its amenities. The easternmost village in Summerlin, The Pueblos sits adjacent to Desert Shores and provides easy access to the Summerlin Trail System.
