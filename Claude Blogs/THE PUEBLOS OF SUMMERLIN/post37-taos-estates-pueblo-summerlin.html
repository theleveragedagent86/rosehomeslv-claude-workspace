Taos Estates in The Pueblos of Summerlin

Taos Estates is the premier single family subdivision in The Pueblos of Summerlin, offering larger lots, custom finishes, and prices that reach well above $900K. For buyers who want the character of this original Summerlin village paired with upscale living, Taos Estates delivers on both fronts.

What Makes Taos Estates Different From Other Pueblos Subdivisions?

Taos Estates sits at the top of the pricing spectrum within The Pueblos, and for good reason. Homes here feature generous lot sizes, upgraded floor plans, and the kind of custom detail that separates this subdivision from its 17 neighbors. The architecture stays true to the village's ancestral pueblo theme: adobe stucco exteriors, flat rooflines, thick walls, and deep overhangs designed for the desert climate. Mature landscaping throughout the community reflects its early 1990s roots, giving streets a settled, established feel that newer Summerlin villages simply cannot replicate.

How Much Do Homes in Taos Estates Cost?

Taos Estates homes generally price from the mid $600Ks to over $900K, placing them at the high end of The Pueblos market. By comparison, the broader village median hovers around $430,000. The premium reflects larger square footage, wider lots, and upgrades that many homeowners have added over three decades of ownership. Ryan Rose notes that Taos Estates consistently attracts buyers moving up from smaller Pueblos subdivisions who want more space without leaving the neighborhood they already know.

What Are the HOA Costs for Taos Estates?

Like all Pueblos subdivisions, Taos Estates falls under the Summerlin North Community Association with a master HOA of $74 per month as of January 2026. This includes a $37 Summerlin Council portion that funds trails, parks, and community programming. Sub association fees for Taos Estates are separate and vary, so prospective buyers should confirm the current amount before making an offer. All HOA management runs through the Summerlink portal.

Is Taos Estates a Good Long Term Investment?

The combination of limited inventory, premium lot sizes, and a location in Summerlin's first village makes Taos Estates a strong hold for long term value. The Pueblos sits on the eastern edge of Summerlin, adjacent to Desert Shores, with direct access to the Summerlin Trail System and proximity to Downtown Summerlin shopping and dining. Established communities with this kind of location advantage rarely lose their appeal.
