Valencia in The Pueblos of Summerlin

Valencia is one of the most sought after single family subdivisions in The Pueblos of Summerlin, offering mid range pricing, solid floor plans, and the established Southwestern character that defines this original Summerlin village. Buyers looking for value in a proven location consistently put Valencia on their shortlist.

What Type of Homes Does Valencia Offer?

Valencia consists entirely of single family detached homes built in the early to mid 1990s as part of Summerlin's first village development by The Howard Hughes Corporation. Homes follow the ancestral pueblo architectural style: adobe stucco facades, flat rooflines, deep overhangs, thick walls, and covered porches designed for desert living. Most properties feature two to four bedrooms with open floor plans that were progressive for their era. Mature desert landscaping lines the streets, giving Valencia the shade and privacy that only three decades of growth can provide.

How Much Do Valencia Homes Cost?

Valencia homes generally fall in the mid range of The Pueblos pricing spectrum, typically landing between the upper $300Ks and the mid $500Ks. This positions Valencia below premium subdivisions like Taos Estates while offering more space and privacy than the attached condo and townhome communities. For buyers who want a single family home in Summerlin without crossing into the $600K plus range, Valencia delivers strong square footage per dollar.

What Does Valencia's Location Offer?

Sitting within The Pueblos on the eastern edge of Summerlin, Valencia residents benefit from adjacency to Desert Shores and quick access to the Summerlin Trail System. Ryan Rose frequently highlights the commute advantage here: Valencia's position makes it one of the closest Summerlin subdivisions to the 95 freeway, cutting drive times to downtown Las Vegas and the Strip. Grocery, dining, and retail are minutes away at Pueblo Place Shopping Center and Summerhill Plaza.

What Are the HOA Details for Valencia?

Valencia falls under the Summerlin North Community Association, with a master HOA of $74 per month as of January 2026. That includes a $37 Summerlin Council fee that funds community trails, parks, and events. Valencia may carry an additional sub association fee ranging from $50 to $150 per month depending on community upkeep obligations. All HOA management is handled through the Summerlink resident portal.
