Summerlin North Community Association Guide for The Pueblos

Every homeowner in The Pueblos of Summerlin is a member of the Summerlin North Community Association, and understanding how it works will save you time, money, and frustration from day one. This guide covers fees, management, and what your HOA dollars actually pay for.

What Is the Summerlin North Community Association?

The Summerlin North Community Association (SNCA) is the master association that governs The Pueblos along with other villages in the northern section of Summerlin. It operates under the umbrella of The Howard Hughes Corporation's community framework and handles common area maintenance, architectural review, and community standards enforcement. All SNCA business is managed through the Summerlink online portal, where residents can pay dues, submit architectural requests, access governing documents, and contact management directly.

How Much Are HOA Fees in The Pueblos?

The master HOA fee is $74 per month as of January 2026. This breaks down into two parts: $37 goes to the Summerlin North Community Association for local village upkeep, and $37 goes to the Summerlin Council, which funds the broader trail system, community parks, events, and shared amenities across Summerlin. Most subdivisions in The Pueblos also carry a sub association fee ranging from $50 to $150 per month, depending on the community's private amenities. Gated communities like La Posada and Red Hills tend to sit at the higher end of that range due to pool, clubhouse, and gate maintenance.

What Do Your HOA Dues Cover?

The master association portion covers common area landscaping, street lighting in shared spaces, community signage, and architectural compliance oversight. The Summerlin Council portion funds the Summerlin Trail System, neighborhood parks, seasonal community events, and recreational programming. Ryan Rose encourages buyers to request the full HOA budget breakdown before closing, as the combined master and sub association fees represent a meaningful monthly expense that varies by subdivision.

How Do You Access Summerlink as a Resident?

Summerlink is the digital hub for all Summerlin North Community Association services. New homeowners receive login credentials after closing, typically within two to three weeks of recorded ownership transfer. Through the portal, residents can pay monthly dues, submit exterior modification requests, review CC&Rs and bylaws, report maintenance issues in common areas, and access meeting minutes. The portal is available at summerlink.com and replaces most of the phone and mail interactions that older HOA systems still require.
