TPC Summerlin and the Shriners Open: Championship Golf Near The Pueblos

TPC Summerlin, home of the annual Shriners Children's Open on the PGA Tour, sits so close to The Pueblos of Summerlin that residents describe it as a "golf cart ride" away. This private championship golf club brings world class professional golf to the neighborhood every fall.

What Is TPC Summerlin?

TPC Summerlin is a private golf club featuring a championship course that hosts PGA Tour professionals each October during the Shriners Children's Open. The tournament draws top ranked players from around the world to compete in Summerlin, giving local residents front row access to elite professional golf. Members enjoy the same fairways and greens that PGA Tour players compete on, along with premium dining and practice facilities.

How Close Is TPC Summerlin to The Pueblos?

The proximity between The Pueblos and TPC Summerlin is one of the most notable perks of living in this part of Summerlin. Residents can reach the club in minutes, making it practical for members to fit in a round before or after work. During tournament week, Pueblos residents can walk or take a short drive to watch PGA Tour professionals compete without dealing with the traffic and logistics that spectators from other parts of Las Vegas face.

What Is the Shriners Children's Open?

The Shriners Children's Open is a PGA Tour FedExCup event held annually at TPC Summerlin. The tournament raises funds for Shriners Children's hospitals while showcasing Las Vegas as a premier golf destination. For Pueblos residents, tournament week transforms the neighborhood into a hub of professional sports activity, with increased dining, entertainment, and community energy throughout western Summerlin.
