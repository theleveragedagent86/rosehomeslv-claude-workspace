Buying a Home in The Pueblos of Summerlin

The Pueblos of Summerlin offers one of the best entry points into Summerlin's west Las Vegas lifestyle, with resale homes ranging from the upper $300Ks to $900K and above. As the first village built in the Summerlin master plan, this 568 acre community delivers mature landscaping, established infrastructure, and a proven track record of holding value.

What Should Buyers Expect When Shopping in The Pueblos?

Every home in The Pueblos is a resale property, with construction dating back to 1992. Buyers will find single family homes between 1,003 and 3,538 square feet, townhomes from 1,200 to 1,800 square feet, and condos from 900 to 1,300 square feet. Most single family homes include three to five bedrooms, two to three or more bathrooms, and two to three car garages. Current single family listings typically fall between $600K and $850K, while the community's median price sits near $430,000 when factoring in all property types.

How Are HOA Costs Structured for Buyers?

Buyers should budget for two layers of homeowner association fees. The Summerlin master association costs $74 per month, and each of the 18 subdivisions within The Pueblos charges an additional $50 to $150 per month depending on the community and its amenities. These fees cover common area maintenance, landscaping in shared spaces, and access to Summerlin's broader network of parks and trails. Gated subdivisions such as La Posada and Red Hills tend to carry slightly higher sub association dues.

What Makes The Pueblos a Smart Purchase in Summerlin?

Summerlin consistently ranks among the top Las Vegas communities for long term appreciation, and The Pueblos benefits directly from that trend. The neighborhood sits just 20 to 25 minutes from the Strip, giving homeowners easy access to employment centers and entertainment without the noise of the corridor. Ryan Rose frequently guides buyers through the 18 subdivisions here, helping them match lot sizes, floor plans, and gated versus non gated options to their specific needs.

Are There Gated Communities Within The Pueblos?

Buyers seeking added privacy can look at La Posada and Red Hills, which are the two gated options within The Pueblos. Both offer condo style living with controlled access. For those who prefer single family homes, subdivisions like Alta Mira, Bonita Canyon, Hermosa, Sedona, and Taos Estates provide a range of sizes and price points across the community's non gated sections.
