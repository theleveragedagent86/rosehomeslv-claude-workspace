The Pueblos of Summerlin Lot Sizes and Yard Space

Lot sizes in The Pueblos of Summerlin typically range from 0.12 to 0.18 acres for single family homes, giving homeowners enough outdoor space for patios, landscaping, and small play areas while keeping maintenance manageable. Across 18 subdivisions and 568 acres, lot configurations vary enough to match different lifestyle priorities.

How Big Are the Lots in The Pueblos of Summerlin?

Most single family lots fall between 0.12 and 0.18 acres, which translates to roughly 5,200 to 7,800 square feet of total lot area. This range is consistent with Summerlin's master planned community standards. Subdivisions such as Taos Estates and Bonita Canyon tend to sit at the larger end of the spectrum, while communities like Hermosa and Sedona offer more compact footprints. Townhomes in Buena Vista and condos in La Posada and Red Hills have shared exterior spaces rather than private lots.

What Can You Do with Yard Space in The Pueblos?

Desert landscaping dominates front yards throughout The Pueblos, with HOA guidelines requiring drought resistant plants and materials that complement the Southwestern aesthetic. Backyards offer more flexibility. Many homeowners have added covered patios, built in barbecues, or small pools within the available footprint. Since homes range from 1,003 to 3,538 square feet on these lots, the ratio of home to yard varies significantly. Smaller homes on average sized lots leave room for more generous outdoor living spaces.

How Do Lot Sizes Compare to Other Summerlin Villages?

The Pueblos was the first village completed in Summerlin, with construction starting in 1992. Lot sizes here reflect the planning standards of that era, which favored slightly more generous spacing than some of the newer, higher density villages built in the 2000s and 2010s. Ryan Rose often points out that buyers who prioritize yard space and separation from neighbors will find The Pueblos more appealing than recently developed Summerlin communities, where lot widths have tightened to accommodate more homes per acre.

Do Lot Sizes Affect Home Values in The Pueblos?

Larger lots within The Pueblos do command higher prices, particularly in subdivisions where homes approach the upper end of the 0.18 acre range. Current single family listings price between $600K and $850K, with lot size being one of several factors alongside square footage, bedroom count, and renovation status. The community's median price sits near $430,000 across all property types. Since The Pueblos is fully built out with no new construction, the existing lot inventory is fixed, which supports long term value for properties with above average outdoor space.
