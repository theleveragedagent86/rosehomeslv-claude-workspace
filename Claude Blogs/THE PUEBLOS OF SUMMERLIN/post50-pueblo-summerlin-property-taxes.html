Property Taxes in The Pueblos of Summerlin

Property taxes in The Pueblos of Summerlin follow Clark County's assessment structure, where homes are taxed at approximately 3.2782% of their assessed value, with assessed value calculated at 35% of taxable value. Combined with Nevada's zero state income tax, this creates a favorable overall tax environment for homeowners in this west Las Vegas community.

How Are Property Taxes Calculated in The Pueblos?

Clark County assesses residential property at 35% of its taxable value, then applies a tax rate of approximately 3.2782%. For a home with a taxable value of $430,000 (near The Pueblos' median), the assessed value would be $150,500, resulting in an annual property tax of roughly $4,934. Actual bills may vary based on the county's determination of taxable value, which can differ from market price. Nevada law also caps annual property tax increases, providing homeowners with predictability in their carrying costs.

How Do Property Taxes Compare Across Property Types?

The Pueblos offers homes ranging from the upper $300Ks to $900K and above, so tax bills vary significantly. Condos in La Posada and Red Hills, which start at the lower end of that range, carry proportionally smaller tax obligations. Single family homes currently listing between $600K and $850K will see higher annual assessments. Townhomes in Buena Vista fall in between. Ryan Rose recommends reviewing the specific tax history of any property before making an offer, since assessed values and market values do not always move in lockstep.

What Other Costs Should Homeowners Factor In?

Beyond property taxes, homeowners in The Pueblos pay two layers of HOA dues: $74 per month for the Summerlin master association and $50 to $150 per month for the individual subdivision's association. When budgeting total housing costs, combining property taxes with HOA fees, insurance, and mortgage payments gives the most accurate picture. The 18 subdivisions across the community's 568 acres each carry different sub association fees, so total costs depend on which neighborhood you choose.

Does Nevada's Tax Structure Benefit Pueblos Homeowners?

Nevada charges no state income tax, which is a significant advantage for homeowners compared to neighboring California and other high tax states. This means more of your household income stays available for mortgage payments, home improvements, or savings. Summerlin consistently ranks among the top Las Vegas communities for appreciation, and the combination of moderate property taxes with zero income tax makes The Pueblos especially attractive to buyers relocating from states with heavier tax burdens.
