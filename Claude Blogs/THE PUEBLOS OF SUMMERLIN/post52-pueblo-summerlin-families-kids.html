Families in The Pueblos of Summerlin

The Pueblos of Summerlin is one of the most family friendly neighborhoods in Las Vegas, with multiple schools inside the village, a 60 plus acre park, and a safety rating in the 82nd percentile nationally. Families here benefit from a master planned environment designed with kids in mind.

What Schools Are Available for Families in The Pueblos?

Families in The Pueblos have exceptional school access without leaving the village. Bryan Elementary serves grades K through 5 as the local public option, while The Meadows School offers private education from pre K through 12th grade. St. Elizabeth Ann Seton provides a private Catholic education for grades K through 8. For older students, Becker Middle School is nearby, and both Cimarron Memorial High School and Palo Verde High School serve the area. This concentration of public and private options within a single neighborhood is uncommon in Las Vegas.

What Parks and Activities Are Available for Kids?

Pueblo Park spans more than 60 acres and includes playgrounds, a basketball court, and walking trails that connect to Summerlin's broader trail system. Children and teens have space to ride bikes, play sports, and explore outdoors in a safe, well maintained setting. The park serves as the social hub of the community, where families gather on weekends and after school. Beyond the park, Downtown Summerlin and Tivoli Village are a short drive away, offering family dining, seasonal events, and entertainment options.

How Safe Is The Pueblos for Raising a Family?

Safety is a top priority for families choosing a neighborhood, and The Pueblos ranks in the 82nd percentile nationally for low crime. The master planned community structure maintained by the Howard Hughes Corporation ensures consistent upkeep, well lit streets, and active HOA oversight. Several subdivisions within The Pueblos are gated, adding another layer of security. The combination of low crime statistics, community management, and gated options gives parents confidence about their children's safety.

Why Do Families Choose The Pueblos Over Other Summerlin Villages?

Ryan Rose frequently helps families relocate to The Pueblos because of its unique combination of school density, park space, and established character. As Summerlin's first village, built in 1992 across 568 acres with 18 subdivisions, The Pueblos has a mature landscape and a settled neighborhood feel that newer villages are still developing. Homes range from the upper $300Ks to $900K and above, giving families options at multiple budget levels. The diversity of property types, from condos to single family homes, means growing families can upsize without leaving the community they know.
