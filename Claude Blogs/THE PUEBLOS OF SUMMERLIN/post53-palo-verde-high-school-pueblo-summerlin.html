Palo Verde High School Near The Pueblos of Summerlin

Palo Verde High School is a public high school in the Clark County School District that serves portions of The Pueblos of Summerlin, giving families in this west Las Vegas neighborhood a strong secondary education option close to home.

What Does Palo Verde High School Offer Students?

Palo Verde High School operates as a comprehensive public high school serving grades 9 through 12 within CCSD. The school provides a full range of academic programs, extracurricular activities, and athletic teams that are standard across Clark County's larger high schools. Its location in the Summerlin corridor gives students access to a well maintained campus in a safe suburban setting. Families in The Pueblos who are zoned for Palo Verde benefit from the short commute, as the school sits within easy driving distance of the village.

How Does Palo Verde Compare to Cimarron Memorial?

Families in The Pueblos often weigh Palo Verde against Cimarron Memorial High School, the other nearby CCSD high school serving parts of the community. Both schools offer comprehensive programs, but they draw from slightly different attendance zones, so your specific address within The Pueblos' 18 subdivisions determines your zoned school. Some families choose between them based on specific programs, athletic strengths, or campus culture. Ryan Rose recommends verifying your zoning directly with CCSD before making a home purchase, since attendance boundaries can shift.

What Private School Alternatives Exist in The Pueblos?

Beyond the public high school options, The Pueblos is home to The Meadows School, a private institution offering education from pre K through 12th grade. This gives families an alternative path for students who may benefit from smaller class sizes and a private school curriculum. St. Elizabeth Ann Seton, also within the village, covers grades K through 8. The concentration of schooling options, both public and private, within and near The Pueblos is one of the strongest selling points for families evaluating the neighborhood.

Why Does School Access Matter for Pueblos Home Values?

Proximity to quality schools consistently influences home values in Las Vegas, and The Pueblos benefits from its access to Palo Verde, Cimarron Memorial, and several private institutions. Buyers with school age children prioritize neighborhoods where commutes to campus are short and options are varied. The Pueblos' 568 acres across 18 subdivisions offer homes from the upper $300Ks to $900K and above, and properties zoned for preferred schools often attract competitive offers. This school density contributes to the strong demand that has characterized The Pueblos since it became Summerlin's first village in 1992.
