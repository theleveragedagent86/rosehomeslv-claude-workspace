The Pueblos vs The Paseos of Summerlin: Which Village Is Right for You?

The Pueblos and The Paseos are two of the most established villages in Summerlin, and buyers frequently compare them. Both offer access to Summerlin's amenities and schools, but they differ in age, home styles, pricing, and neighborhood character. Here is how they stack up.

How Do Home Prices Compare?

The Pueblos, built starting in 1992 as Summerlin's first village, generally offers lower price points than The Paseos. Homes in The Pueblos range from the upper $300Ks to over $900K, with most sales falling between $400K and $650K. The Paseos, developed later with newer construction, typically commands higher prices, with most homes ranging from $500K to over $1 million. For buyers seeking value in an established Summerlin location, The Pueblos often provides more home for the money.

What About Home Styles and Lot Sizes?

The Pueblos features a mix of home styles across its 18 subdivisions, including single-story ranch homes, two-story traditional designs, condos, and townhomes. Lot sizes vary but tend to be generous for Summerlin, especially in subdivisions like Taos Estates. The Paseos offers newer construction with more contemporary floor plans, open-concept layouts, and modern finishes. Lot sizes in The Paseos are often more compact, reflecting the trend toward maximizing interior square footage over yard space. Ryan Rose advises buyers to consider whether they prioritize newer finishes or larger lots when choosing between the two.

How Do the Locations Differ?

The Pueblos sits in Summerlin North with quick access to the 215 Beltway, Lake Mead Boulevard, and Summerlin Parkway. It is closer to Red Rock Canyon and the JW Marriott Resort. The Paseos is located more centrally within Summerlin, with easy access to Downtown Summerlin shopping and dining. Both villages connect to the Summerlin Trail System, but The Pueblos offers a more direct path to the western mountain trails.

Which Village Is Better for Families?

Both villages serve families well with access to quality CCSD schools and Summerlin parks. The Pueblos feeds into Bryan Elementary, Becker Middle School, and Cimarron-Memorial High School. The Paseos is zoned for different schools within the Summerlin area. The Pueblos' larger lots and established landscaping give it a more mature neighborhood feel with shade trees and wide streets. The Paseos appeals to families who prefer newer homes with modern kitchens and energy-efficient features.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose The Pueblos if you want more space, lower prices, mature landscaping, and proximity to Red Rock Canyon. Choose The Paseos if you prioritize newer construction, modern floor plans, and walkability to Downtown Summerlin. Both are excellent Summerlin villages with strong resale value and community amenities.
