Buying your first luxury home in The Ridges at Summerlin is an exciting step, and the process differs from a standard home purchase in several important ways. This guide walks first time luxury buyers through pricing, financing, HOA requirements, and what to expect inside this 800 acre guard gated community. ## What Should First Time Luxury Buyers Know About The Ridges? The Ridges contains 15 distinct neighborhoods with home prices ranging from approximately $1,099,000 in the Fairway Hills townhome section to above $8,999,000 for custom estates. The current median sale price is around $3.49 million. First time luxury buyers should understand that properties at this level often involve longer negotiation timelines, more detailed inspections, and higher earnest money deposits than entry level purchases. Financing a luxury home also looks different. Jumbo loan products are standard for anything above conforming loan limits, and many Ridges buyers opt for portfolio loans through private banking relationships. Cash purchases are common in this market as well, and having proof of funds ready before scheduling showings will position you as a serious buyer. ## How Does the HOA Work in The Ridges? The Ridges operates under a master HOA that covers guard gate security, common area maintenance, and access to Club Ridges, a 9,000 square foot private amenity center. Individual neighborhoods within The Ridges may have their own sub association fees on top of the master HOA. In Fairway Hills, for example, HOA fees run approximately $1,109 per month. Architectural review is another key element. If you purchase a custom lot and plan to build, your home design must meet community guidelines for materials, height, setbacks, and landscaping. Ryan Rose can walk you through the approval process so there are no surprises after you close on a lot. ## What Amenities Come With a Ridges Home? Beyond Club Ridges, residents will soon have access to the Amara Golf Club, a $300 million redevelopment of the former Bear's Best course designed by Jack Nicklaus. The club is set to reopen in October 2026 and will add villa residences starting in 2027. Membership details and fee structures are still being finalized. The community's elevation, ranging from 3,200 to 4,000 feet, delivers cooler temperatures and dramatic views of Red Rock Canyon and the Las Vegas Strip. These views are a core part of what makes The Ridges one of the most recognized luxury addresses in the Las Vegas Valley. ## Ryan Rose's Advice for First Time Luxury Buyers Ryan Rose recommends that first time luxury buyers start with a clear picture of their must haves versus nice to haves. The Ridges offers everything from 2,000 square foot townhomes to 22,000 square foot custom estates, so narrowing your target early saves time and keeps your search focused. He also suggests touring multiple neighborhoods within The Ridges before making a decision. The feel of Promontory is different from Azure, which is different from The Pointe.
