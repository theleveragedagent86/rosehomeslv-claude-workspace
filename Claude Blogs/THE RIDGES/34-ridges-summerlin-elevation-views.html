The Ridges in Summerlin sits at the highest elevation of any residential community in the Las Vegas valley, ranging from 3,200 to 4,000 feet above sea level. This elevated position delivers two benefits that no other luxury community can replicate: panoramic views of Red Rock Canyon and the Las Vegas Strip, and temperatures that run noticeably cooler than the valley floor below. ## How High Is The Ridges in Summerlin? The Ridges spans an elevation range of approximately 3,200 feet at its lower neighborhoods to 4,000 feet at the highest custom home sites. To put this in perspective, the Las Vegas Strip sits at roughly 2,000 feet above sea level, meaning homes in The Ridges are positioned 1,200 to 2,000 feet higher than the city center. This difference in altitude translates to measurably cooler temperatures, particularly during the intense summer months when even a few degrees of relief makes a meaningful difference in daily comfort. Ryan Rose points out that buyers relocating from other markets are often surprised by how much the temperature drops as they drive up into The Ridges. The western orientation, combined with the rising terrain of the Spring Mountains just beyond, creates natural airflow patterns that further cool the community in the evenings. ## What Views Can You See From The Ridges? The views from The Ridges extend in two primary directions. Looking west, residents see the dramatic red and gold sandstone formations of Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, one of Southern Nevada's most treasured natural landmarks. The canyon walls change color throughout the day as sunlight shifts, providing a living backdrop that never looks the same twice. Looking east and south, the Las Vegas valley unfolds below with the city skyline, the Strip, and the surrounding mountain ranges visible on clear days. Many homes in the upper custom neighborhoods like Promontory, Arrowhead, and Azure were specifically designed with expansive glass walls and elevated terraces to capture these sightlines. The combination of western canyon views and eastern valley panoramas is unique to The Ridges and cannot be found at lower elevation communities. ## How Does Elevation Affect Daily Life? Beyond the views and cooler temperatures, the elevation of The Ridges shapes the overall atmosphere of the community. The 800 acre setting feels removed from the density of the valley, even though residents are only minutes from Summerlin's shopping, dining, and medical facilities along the 215 beltway. The higher terrain also means lower light pollution, making evenings on a backyard terrace noticeably more tranquil than comparable properties closer to the city center. Ryan Rose considers the elevation a defining feature when showing homes in The Ridges. Many luxury communities in Las Vegas offer guard gates and high end finishes, but the physical setting of The Ridges at the base of the Spring Mountains is something that cannot be engineered or replicated elsewhere in the valley. ## Why Views Drive Home Values Homes with unobstructed Red Rock Canyon or Strip views consistently command premium prices within The Ridges. The custom neighborhoods at the highest elevations, including Promontory, Arrowhead, and portions of Azure, tend to carry the highest price points in the community. Ryan Rose advises buyers to think of view orientation as a long term investment, since the protected status of Red Rock Canyon means the western views will never be blocked by future development.
