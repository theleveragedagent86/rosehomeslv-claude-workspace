Turtlehead Peak is one of the most challenging and rewarding hikes near The Ridges in Summerlin. Located within Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, this roughly 5 mile round trip trail delivers significant elevation gain, rugged terrain, and panoramic summit views that stretch across the entire Las Vegas valley. It is a bucket list hike for experienced outdoor enthusiasts living in the area. ## What Is the Turtlehead Peak Trail? Turtlehead Peak is a prominent summit within Red Rock Canyon that earned its name from the turtle shaped rock formation at the top. The trail covers approximately 5 miles round trip and involves substantial elevation gain as hikers climb from the desert floor to the peak. The route includes loose rock, steep scrambling sections, and exposed ridgelines that demand solid hiking experience and physical fitness. This is not a casual morning walk. Turtlehead Peak is designed for experienced hikers who are comfortable navigating uneven terrain, using their hands for balance on steep sections, and managing exposure at higher elevations. The effort is rewarded with some of the most expansive views available anywhere near Las Vegas. ## How Close Is Turtlehead Peak to The Ridges? The trailhead is located within Red Rock Canyon, roughly 15 to 20 minutes by car from The Ridges. Despite the challenging nature of the hike, the proximity means that experienced hikers can complete the trail and return home within a half day, making it accessible as a regular weekend activity. ## What to Expect on the Hike The trail begins with a relatively gentle approach through desert terrain before the grade increases sharply. The middle and upper portions of the route involve scrambling over large rocks and navigating sections where the path is not always clearly defined. Cairns mark the way in some areas, but hikers should have solid navigation awareness. The final push to the summit is the steepest section and often involves hands on rock contact to maintain stability. At the top, hikers are rewarded with 360 degree views of Red Rock Canyon, the Spring Mountains, and the Las Vegas Strip in the distance. On clear days, visibility extends for miles in every direction. ## Who Should Hike Turtlehead Peak? This trail is best suited for experienced hikers with a good level of cardiovascular fitness and comfort on exposed, rocky terrain. It is not recommended for young children, casual walkers, or anyone unfamiliar with desert hiking conditions. Hikers should bring plenty of water, wear sturdy boots with ankle support, and start early to avoid afternoon heat and sun exposure. ## Why Turtlehead Peak Matters to Ridges Homeowners For residents of The Ridges who take their outdoor lifestyle seriously, having a challenging summit hike this close to home is a rare advantage.
