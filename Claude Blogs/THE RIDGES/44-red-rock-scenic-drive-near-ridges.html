Red Rock Canyon's 13 mile Scenic Drive is one of the most iconic outdoor experiences in southern Nevada, and it starts just 15 minutes from The Ridges in Summerlin. For residents of this guard gated luxury community, the Scenic Drive offers a world class natural attraction that most people travel hours to reach but Ridges homeowners can enjoy on any given afternoon. ## What Is the Red Rock Canyon Scenic Drive? The Red Rock Canyon Scenic Drive is a one way, 13 mile loop road that winds through the heart of the Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area. The route passes dramatic sandstone formations, desert washes, and panoramic viewpoints that showcase the geological diversity of the Spring Mountains and Mojave Desert. The drive is open to passenger vehicles, bicycles, and motorcycles, and it includes multiple pullouts, trailheads, and picnic areas along the way. The conservation area itself encompasses 200,000 acres of protected desert and mountain terrain. The Scenic Drive serves as the primary way for visitors to access the most popular hiking trails, rock climbing areas, and scenic overlooks within the park. A visitor center near the entrance provides information on current trail conditions, wildlife activity, and seasonal programming. ## Timed Entry Reservations From October 1 through May 31, timed entry reservations are required to access the Scenic Drive. This reservation system was implemented to manage the high volume of visitors during the cooler months, which is peak season for outdoor recreation in the Las Vegas area. Reservations can be made online in advance and help ensure a less crowded and more enjoyable experience on the drive. Ryan Rose advises Ridges residents and prospective buyers that the reservation system actually works in their favor. Because The Ridges is so close to Red Rock Canyon, residents can plan visits with greater flexibility and take advantage of early morning or late afternoon time slots when lighting conditions are best for photography and the temperatures are most comfortable. ## Activities Along the Scenic Drive The 13 mile loop provides access to over 30 miles of hiking trails, ranging from easy walks to strenuous summit routes. Popular trailheads along the drive include Calico Tanks, Turtlehead Peak, Keystone Thrust, and Ice Box Canyon. Rock climbing areas draw visitors from around the world, and designated picnic areas offer shaded spots for a break with mountain views. Cycling the Scenic Drive is a popular activity for road cyclists, as the loop provides a challenging and scenic route with elevation changes and minimal vehicle conflict due to the one way design. Mountain biking and horseback riding are available on designated trails within the conservation area as well. ## Why Proximity to Red Rock Matters Ryan Rose considers the proximity to Red Rock Canyon one of the defining lifestyle advantages of living in The Ridges.
