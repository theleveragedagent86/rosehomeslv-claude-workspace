The Ridges at Summerlin and MacDonald Highlands in Henderson are two of the most prestigious luxury communities in the Las Vegas valley. Both offer guard gated security, custom home options, and resort caliber amenities. However, they differ significantly in location, architectural style, views, and overall lifestyle. This comparison breaks down the key differences to help buyers decide which community fits their priorities. ## Location The Ridges is located in western Las Vegas within the Summerlin master planned community. It sits at the base of Red Rock Canyon at elevations between 3,200 and 4,000 feet. MacDonald Highlands is situated in Henderson on the southeastern side of the valley, perched in the McCullough Range at approximately 3,000 feet of elevation. The two communities are on opposite sides of the valley, and the choice between them often comes down to which part of Las Vegas a buyer prefers for daily commuting and lifestyle. ## Community Size and Homes The Ridges spans 800 acres and includes 15 distinct neighborhoods with approximately 957 homes. The median home price is around $3.49 million. MacDonald Highlands features primarily custom estate lots and finished homes, with prices typically ranging from $2 million to over $10 million. MacDonald Highlands tends to attract buyers seeking larger lots and fully custom architectural design, while The Ridges offers a mix of production luxury, semi custom, and full custom homes across its neighborhoods. ## Views and Setting The Ridges offers dramatic westward views of Red Rock Canyon's sandstone formations and the Spring Mountains. The natural desert landscape is preserved throughout the community, and many homes sit on elevated lots with panoramic mountain backdrops. MacDonald Highlands provides sweeping views of the Las Vegas Strip from its eastern facing lots, along with views of the surrounding McCullough Range. Buyers who prioritize mountain and canyon scenery typically gravitate toward The Ridges, while those who want city skyline and Strip views often prefer MacDonald Highlands. ## Golf and Recreation The Ridges is home to the Amara Golf Club, a private 18 hole course designed within the natural terrain of the community. Club Ridges provides additional amenities including pools, tennis courts, and a fitness center. MacDonald Highlands features the DragonRidge Country Club, anchored by the Jack Nicklaus designed DragonRidge Golf Course. DragonRidge offers a full country club experience with dining, spa services, and social events. Both communities deliver world class golf, but the club models and membership structures differ. ## HOA Fees HOA fees at The Ridges range from $522 to $1,272 per month depending on the specific neighborhood. MacDonald Highlands HOA fees vary by neighborhood and lot type, with some custom estate areas carrying different fee structures. Buyers should compare the specific services included in each HOA, as both communities wrap security, landscaping, and common area maintenance into their fees. ## Security Both communities are guard gated with 24/7 staffed entry points. The Ridges employs physical human guards and private roving patrols. MacDonald Highlands also maintains guard gated access with security monitoring. Security is a shared strength of both communities and is not typically a differentiating factor between them. ## How Ryan Rose Helps Buyers Compare Ryan Rose works with luxury buyers who are evaluating both The Ridges and MacDonald Highlands.
