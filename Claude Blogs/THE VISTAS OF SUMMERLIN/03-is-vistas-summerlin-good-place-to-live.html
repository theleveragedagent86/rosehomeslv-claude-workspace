Is The Vistas Summerlin a Good Place to Live?

Yes, The Vistas is one of the most desirable villages in all of Summerlin, combining top rated schools, low crime rates, resort caliber amenities, and a prime location west of the 215 Beltway. The community ranks in the 97th percentile for safety in Las Vegas, with a violent crime rate of just 1.597 per 1,000 residents.

What Are the Best Features of Living in The Vistas?

The amenity package alone sets The Vistas apart. Residents have access to the 23 acre Vistas Community Park, a 7,900 square foot community center, a resident only swimming pool with a large slide and children's lagoon, two lighted tennis courts, a basketball court with six hoops, two softball fields, and multiple play areas. North Tower Park and South Tower Park add another 14 acres of green space.

The trail system connects to over 150 miles of Summerlin walking, jogging, and biking paths. For families, the school zoning is exceptional: Linda Rankin Givens Elementary holds an 8 out of 10 on GreatSchools with 70% reading proficiency, Sig Rogich Middle School earned both a Nevada Five Star designation and 2015 Blue Ribbon School award, and Palo Verde High School boasts a 96% graduation rate.

What About Commute Times and Convenience?

The Vistas sits in a sweet spot for daily logistics. The Las Vegas Strip is a 20 to 25 minute drive via the 215 Beltway, Harry Reid International Airport takes 20 to 30 minutes, and Downtown Summerlin with its shopping and dining is just minutes away. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is roughly 3 to 4 miles from the community.

Grocery stores, restaurants, and retail are clustered nearby at Vista Commons Shopping Center and Downtown Summerlin. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is a short drive west, giving residents weekend hiking and outdoor recreation without a long commute.

Are There Any Drawbacks?

The Vistas is a resale only community with no new construction, so buyers need to be prepared to move quickly when inventory appears. HOA fees total between $100 and $184 per month when combining the master and sub association dues. Some buyers may also find the European inspired architecture less varied than newer Summerlin villages, though many consider the cohesive aesthetic a strength.
