Schools Near The Vistas Summerlin

The Vistas Summerlin is zoned for three of the highest rated public schools in the Clark County School District: Linda Rankin Givens Elementary, Sig Rogich Middle School, and Palo Verde High School. All three hold an 8 out of 10 rating on GreatSchools, making The Vistas one of the strongest school zones in the entire Las Vegas valley.

Which Elementary School Serves The Vistas?

Linda Rankin Givens Elementary School sits within The Vistas community itself, meaning many students can walk to school. Opened in 2004, the school serves approximately 725 students in grades PK through 5. It ranks in the top 5% of all Nevada schools, with 66% math proficiency and 70% reading proficiency. Parents consistently praise the engaged teaching staff and active PTA community.

What About Middle School and High School?

Sig Rogich Middle School is located adjacent to The Vistas and serves grades 6 through 8. The school has earned both the Nevada Five Star designation and was named a 2015 National Blue Ribbon School. It holds the number one ranking among all 59 CCSD middle schools, a distinction that directly supports home values in The Vistas.

Palo Verde High School sits roughly 1 to 2 miles from the community and serves grades 9 through 12. Ranked 19th in Nevada, the school boasts a 96% graduation rate and an average SAT score of 1240. The campus offers a wide range of Advanced Placement courses, competitive athletics, and performing arts programs.

Are There Private School Options Nearby?

Several respected private schools are within a short drive of The Vistas. Bishop Gorman High School, a Catholic college preparatory school, is approximately 5 miles away and is widely considered one of the top high schools in Nevada. Faith Lutheran Middle and High School is about 4 miles from the community. The Meadows School, an independent college prep campus serving PK through 12, is roughly 5 miles away. Adelson Educational Campus, a Jewish day school for PK through 12, sits about 4 miles from The Vistas.
