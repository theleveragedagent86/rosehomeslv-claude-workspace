Summerlin Trail System Near The Vistas: Your Complete Guide

The Summerlin Trail System is a 150+ mile network of walking, jogging, and biking paths that connects neighborhoods across the entire Summerlin master planned community. For residents of The Vistas, this trail system is accessible right from the neighborhood, making outdoor exercise a daily convenience rather than a planned event.

How Big Is the Summerlin Trail System?

The trail network spans over 150 miles and continues to grow as Summerlin expands. Trails are paved, well maintained, and designed to connect residential villages to parks, schools, shopping centers, and open spaces. The system is one of the largest community trail networks in Nevada and a major reason Summerlin consistently ranks among the best master planned communities in the country.

From The Vistas, residents can access the trail system through multiple connection points within the 815 acre village. Internal walking paths link all three community parks and feed directly into the broader Summerlin network.

What Can You Do on the Summerlin Trails?

The trails accommodate walking, jogging, cycling, and dog walking. Many sections run along landscaped corridors that offer shade and scenic views of the surrounding desert and Spring Mountains. The flat, paved surfaces make the trails accessible for all fitness levels, from casual strollers to serious runners training for distance.

For those seeking something more rugged, the Red Rock Legacy Trail (Phase 1) begins at Sky Vista Drive in Summerlin and connects to the natural landscape west of the community. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, with its 30+ miles of hiking and biking trails, sits approximately 10 miles west along Charleston Boulevard.

Why Do Homebuyers Care About Trail Access?

Proximity to outdoor recreation adds measurable value to a home, and The Vistas delivers on that front with both internal walking paths and seamless connections to the larger Summerlin system.
