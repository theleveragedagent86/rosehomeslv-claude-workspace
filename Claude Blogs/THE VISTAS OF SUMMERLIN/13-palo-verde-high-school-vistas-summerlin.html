Is Palo Verde High School a Good School?

Palo Verde High School is the zoned public high school for students living in The Vistas of Summerlin, located at 333 S Pavilion Center Drive, just one to two miles from the neighborhood. Rated 8 out of 10 on GreatSchools and B+ on Niche, Palo Verde ranks 19th among all high schools in Nevada and delivers a 96% graduation rate.

Students at Palo Verde carry an average GPA of 3.47 and average SAT score of 1240, both strong indicators of a college ready student body. The school serves grades 9 through 12 and draws from several of Summerlin's top performing elementary and middle schools, including Givens Elementary and Sig Rogich Middle, which feed directly from The Vistas.

What Academics and Activities Does Palo Verde Offer?

Palo Verde provides a broad range of Advanced Placement and honors courses that allow students to earn college credit before graduation. The school is also known for competitive athletics, award winning performing arts programs, and active student organizations that build leadership and community involvement.

The combination of strong academics and extracurricular depth makes Palo Verde a destination school for families throughout the Summerlin area. For students coming from Rogich Middle School, the transition is smooth because many of their peers follow the same path, creating social continuity alongside academic consistency.

How Close Is Palo Verde High School to The Vistas?

Palo Verde sits roughly one to two miles south of The Vistas along Pavilion Center Drive. Older students can drive in minutes, and the school is also accessible by bike or a short bus ride. This proximity means parents spend less time on school commutes and students have more time for homework, activities, and part time work.

Having all three zoned public schools within walking or short driving distance is one of The Vistas' most compelling advantages for families. The K through 12 pipeline of Givens Elementary, Sig Rogich Middle, and Palo Verde High, each rated 8 out of 10, is one of the strongest in Clark County.
