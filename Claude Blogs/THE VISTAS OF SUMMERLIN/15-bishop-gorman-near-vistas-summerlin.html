Why Do Families in The Vistas Choose Bishop Gorman High School?

Bishop Gorman High School is the premier private high school in the Las Vegas valley, located approximately 5 miles from The Vistas of Summerlin. As a Catholic college preparatory school serving grades 9 through 12, Gorman has built a national reputation for academic excellence, championship athletics, and college placement rates that rival top prep schools across the country.

For families in The Vistas who want a private high school option, Bishop Gorman is the most sought after choice. The commute takes roughly 10 to 15 minutes, making it practical for daily school runs. Many Vistas families enroll their children at Givens Elementary and Sig Rogich Middle in the public system, then transition to Gorman for high school.

What Are the Academics Like at Bishop Gorman?

Bishop Gorman offers a rigorous college preparatory curriculum with a wide selection of Advanced Placement courses, honors tracks, and electives. The school emphasizes critical thinking, service, and faith formation as part of its Catholic mission. Graduates consistently gain acceptance to competitive universities, including Ivy League and top tier programs nationwide.

Class sizes are smaller than public school averages, allowing for more individualized instruction and mentorship. The campus itself features modern facilities, including dedicated STEM labs, performing arts spaces, and a media center that support a well rounded educational experience.

How Strong Is Bishop Gorman's Athletics Program?

Bishop Gorman is widely considered one of the top high school athletic programs in the United States. The football program alone has produced dozens of Division I athletes and multiple national rankings. Beyond football, the school competes at elite levels in basketball, baseball, soccer, swimming, tennis, and numerous other sports.

For student athletes in The Vistas, attending Gorman means access to world class coaching, training facilities, and exposure to college recruiters. The school's athletic success also builds a strong sense of community and school pride that extends well beyond the playing field.
