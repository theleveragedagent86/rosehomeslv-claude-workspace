Single-Story Homes in The Vistas Summerlin

Single story living is one of the most requested features among buyers in Summerlin, and The Vistas delivers with multiple subcommunities dedicated entirely to one level floor plans. Whether you are downsizing, planning for accessibility, or simply prefer life without stairs, this village has strong options.

Which Subcommunities Offer Single-Story Homes?

Several subcommunities in The Vistas were designed exclusively with single story floor plans. Talaverde, built by Woodside Homes in 2004, is a gated community featuring large single story homes from 2,058 to 2,537 square feet. Cara Vella, developed by Pulte Homes between 2003 and 2004, offers all single story layouts ranging from 2,200 to 2,800 square feet across 72 homes.

Santalina by William Lyon Homes also features all single story plans from 2,000 to 3,000 square feet. Hillstone, another Pulte community with 145 homes, provides more modestly sized single story options between 1,500 and 1,900 square feet with two to three bedrooms. Beyond these dedicated communities, you will find individual single story floor plans scattered throughout other subcommunities like Portofino and Encanto that offer both one and two story options.

What Price Range Should Single-Story Buyers Expect?

Single story homes in The Vistas generally range from the low $500,000s in Hillstone to well over $1,000,000 in Talaverde and the larger Santalina and Cara Vella floor plans. Pricing depends heavily on square footage, lot position, upgrades, and whether the subcommunity is gated. Talaverde, as a gated single story community, tends to command a premium.

All homes in The Vistas are resale, built between 2000 and 2008. Many owners have renovated kitchens, bathrooms, and flooring over the years, so updated single story homes are available for buyers who want move in ready conditions.

Why Are Single-Story Homes So Popular Here?

The Vistas attracts a broad demographic, from retirees who appreciate the accessibility of one level living to families who simply prefer open, connected floor plans. Single story homes also tend to hold strong resale value in Las Vegas because demand consistently outpaces supply. The European inspired architecture throughout the village adds character and curb appeal that you will not find in cookie cutter subdivisions.

Local Insight: Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, sees consistent demand for single story properties in The Vistas. "Cara Vella and Talaverde are two of my favorite recommendations for single story buyers," Ryan says. "You get generous square footage, quality construction, and that distinct European aesthetic all on one level. They do not last long on the market."
