Downtown Summerlin Dining: Upscale Eats Minutes From The Vistas

Downtown Summerlin is the premier open air shopping and dining destination in the west valley, and it sits just two to three miles from The Vistas. With over 125 shops and restaurants spread across 106 acres, this center gives Vistas residents access to some of the best dining in Summerlin without a long drive.

What Are the Best Restaurants at Downtown Summerlin?

Downtown Summerlin offers a range of dining that goes well beyond typical suburban fare. True Food Kitchen leads the way with its health conscious, seasonal menu that appeals to anyone looking for fresh, thoughtfully prepared meals. Harlo Steak and Bar delivers a modern steakhouse experience with quality seafood and an upscale atmosphere, perfect for date nights or celebrations.

Public School 702 brings a gastropub twist with creative dishes and craft cocktails in a trendy setting. BJ's Restaurant and Brewhouse rounds out the options with a family friendly brewpub atmosphere, house brewed beers, and a menu that has something for everyone. Whether you want a light, healthy lunch or a full evening out, Downtown Summerlin covers it.

How Far Is Downtown Summerlin From The Vistas?

The drive from The Vistas to Downtown Summerlin takes roughly five to ten minutes, depending on traffic. That short distance means Vistas residents can easily pop over for dinner, catch a movie at Regal Cinemas, and still be home early. Living this close to a 1.6 million square foot dining and entertainment hub is a major advantage that sets The Vistas apart from more isolated Summerlin communities.

Can You Make a Full Evening Out of Downtown Summerlin?

Without question. Start with dinner at one of the restaurants mentioned above, then browse the shops or catch a film at the movie theater. The open air layout makes it pleasant to walk around, especially during cooler months. Seasonal events and live entertainment add even more reasons to visit throughout the year. For Vistas residents, Downtown Summerlin functions like an extension of the neighborhood.
