The Vistas Summerlin Subcommunities Guide: 26+ Neighborhoods Inside One Village

The Vistas of Summerlin is not a single neighborhood; it is an 815 acre village made up of 26 to 28 distinct subcommunities. Each one has its own character, builder, and home style, giving buyers an impressive range of options within one cohesive master planned setting.

What Subcommunities Are Inside The Vistas?

The lineup includes Altessa (apartments), Ashton Park, Barrington by Toll Brothers, Bella Vista by Pulte, Canterra, Capri, Cara Vella by Pulte (all single story), Casa Rosa by Kimball, Encanto by William Lyon, and Estancia by Woodside with homes ranging from 2,700 to 3,900 square feet. You will also find Hillstone by Pulte (single story), Kingwood, Miraleste, Miramonte by Pulte, and Monterossa by Toll Brothers (gated).

Additional subcommunities include Palmilla by Woodside (gated), Paradiso by Pulte (gated), Portofino by KB Homes with 213 homes, Sage Hills by Pulte (gated townhomes), San Marcos by Kimball, Santalina by William Lyon (all single story), Solano, Somerset by William Lyon, Sonesta, Summerfield, Talaverde by Woodside (gated, single story), Talega, and Vista Verde by William Lyon.

Are Any Subcommunities Gated?

Yes. Several subcommunities offer gated access for added privacy and security. Monterossa, Palmilla, Paradiso, Sage Hills, and Talaverde all feature gated entrances. These range from luxury single family homes to townhome layouts, so there is a gated option for a variety of budgets and lifestyles.

Which Builders Developed The Vistas?

Nine major builders shaped The Vistas: KB Homes, Kimball Hill Homes, Pulte Homes, R/S Homes, Richmond American Homes, Ryland Homes, Toll Brothers, William Lyon Homes, and Woodside Homes. That builder diversity is a big reason why the village offers such a wide variety of floor plans, price points, and architectural styles.
