The Vistas Summerlin Clock Towers and Landmarks: Icons of the Village

Every great neighborhood has a signature feature, and in The Vistas of Summerlin, it is the twin clock towers. These 48 foot tall structures greet residents and visitors at the village's main entrances and have become some of the most recognizable landmarks in all of Summerlin.

Where Are The Vistas Clock Towers Located?

The two clock towers stand at the north and south entrances to The Vistas. They serve as gateway markers, letting you know the moment you have arrived in the village. Their positioning at opposite ends of the community creates a sense of symmetry and grandeur that reinforces the European inspired design found throughout the neighborhood.

What Do the Clock Towers Look Like?

Each tower rises 48 feet and features the same European architectural details found on homes throughout The Vistas: ornate stonework, arched openings, and a timeless design that echoes Old World craftsmanship. They are visible from the surrounding streets and serve as both functional landmarks and visual anchors for the entire village. For many residents, the towers are the defining image of their community.

Are There Other Landmarks in The Vistas?

Beyond the clock towers, The Vistas is home to several notable features. The 7,900 square foot community center at 11312 Parkside Way serves as a social hub. The resort style pool with its slide and children's lagoon is another landmark in its own right. The village's parks, walking paths, and European styled streetscapes all contribute to a landscape that feels curated rather than accidental.
