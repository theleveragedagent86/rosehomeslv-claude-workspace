Is The Vistas of Summerlin Safe?

Yes. The Vistas of Summerlin ranks in the 97th percentile for safety in Las Vegas, making it one of the safest communities in the entire valley. With a violent crime rate of just 1.597 per 1,000 residents, this 815 acre Summerlin village offers the kind of security that families, retirees, and professionals look for when choosing a neighborhood.

What Are the Crime Statistics for The Vistas?

The Vistas posts a violent crime rate of 1.597 per 1,000 residents, which places it well above average for safety compared to both Nevada state averages and national benchmarks. Vehicle theft in The Vistas falls in the 79th percentile, meaning it is safer than most areas across the state and the country for that category as well. These numbers reflect a community where residents can feel confident about their personal safety and property security.

Why Is The Vistas Considered So Safe?

Several factors contribute to the low crime rates in The Vistas. The community sits within the broader Summerlin master plan, which has long been recognized as one of the safest large scale developments in Southern Nevada. Many of the 26 to 28 subcommunities within The Vistas are gated, adding an extra layer of controlled access. The neighborhood design itself, with well lit streets, active parks, and a strong sense of community, creates an environment where residents know their neighbors and unusual activity gets noticed quickly.

Is The Vistas Safe for Families and Evening Activities?

The Vistas is particularly well suited for families who want their kids to play outside, walk to school, or use the community parks after hours. Linda Rankin Givens Elementary School sits within the community, and Sig Rogich Middle School is adjacent, so children do not need to travel far. The three community parks, including the 23 acre Vistas Community Park, feature lighted sports courts and play areas that stay active into the evening hours. Residents regularly use the walking paths and trail connections throughout the day and after dark.
