Retiring in The Vistas of Summerlin

The Vistas of Summerlin offers retirees a quiet, low crime suburban setting with single story home options, proximity to medical care, and access to over 150 miles of trails. While it is not an age restricted community, The Vistas provides many of the features that make Summerlin one of the most popular retirement destinations in Southern Nevada.

Are There Single Story Homes Available in The Vistas?

Yes. Several subcommunities within The Vistas offer single story floor plans that appeal to retirees looking to avoid stairs. Cara Vella, Hillstone, Santalina, and Talaverde all include single story options. With median home prices in the $600,000 to $650,000 range across The Vistas, retirees can find well maintained homes with modern layouts in a community that feels established and settled. The 815 acre village includes 26 to 28 subcommunities, so there is variety in home styles and price points.

How Close Is Medical Care to The Vistas?

Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is approximately three to four miles from The Vistas at 657 N. Town Center Drive. That translates to a five to ten minute drive, which gives retirees peace of mind knowing that quality medical care is close. The broader Summerlin area also offers a range of specialist offices, urgent care facilities, and pharmacies within a short radius of the community.

Is The Vistas a Good Fit for Active Retirees?

The Vistas connects to over 150 miles of Summerlin trails, making it ideal for retirees who enjoy walking, hiking, or cycling. The community's three parks total more than 37 acres and include lighted tennis courts, a basketball court, and walking paths. Red Rock Canyon is a short drive west for those who want more rugged outdoor recreation. The 7,900 square foot Vistas Community Center and the resident only swimming pool add social and recreational options without leaving the neighborhood.

What About Nearby 55+ Communities?

The Vistas itself does not have age restricted sections, but retirees who prefer a dedicated 55 plus environment can explore Siena and Sun City Summerlin, both located nearby within the greater Summerlin area. Many retirees, however, choose The Vistas specifically because it offers a multigenerational setting with the safety, quiet, and amenities they want without the restrictions of a seniors only community.

Local Insight From Ryan Rose

According to Ryan Rose, who has worked extensively in Summerlin, The Vistas attracts retirees who want the best of both worlds: a peaceful, safe neighborhood with enough activity and amenities to stay engaged. The single story homes in Cara Vella and Talaverde are particularly popular with his retired clients because they combine single level living with well designed floor plans. The proximity to Summerlin Hospital is a factor that comes up in nearly every conversation with buyers approaching retirement.
