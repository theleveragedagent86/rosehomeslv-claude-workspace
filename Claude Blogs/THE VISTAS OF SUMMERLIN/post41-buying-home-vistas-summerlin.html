Buying a Home in The Vistas of Summerlin

The Vistas of Summerlin is one of the most sought after villages in the entire Summerlin master plan, and for good reason. With 26 to 28 subcommunities spread across 815 acres, this established neighborhood offers a wide range of home styles at various price points. If you are considering buying here, there are a few things you should know before making your move.

What Types of Homes Are Available in The Vistas?

The Vistas features single story homes, two story homes, townhomes, and luxury estates built between 2000 and 2008 by respected builders like Toll Brothers, Pulte Homes, KB Homes, and Richmond American Homes. Prices currently range from around $400,000 for townhomes in Sage Hills to over $2,500,000 for luxury properties in gated subcommunities like Monterossa and Paradiso. The median home price sits in the $600,000 to $650,000 range, which places most of the neighborhood squarely in the mid to upper tier of the Las Vegas market.

What Should Buyers Know About Costs Beyond the Purchase Price?

Beyond the sale price, buyers in The Vistas should budget for HOA fees and a yearly Special Improvement District (SID) assessment. These additional costs fund the community's parks, trails, and common area maintenance. The Vistas has three parks totaling over 37 acres, a community pool, tennis courts, basketball courts, and access to over 150 miles of trails. Schools in the area are all rated 8 out of 10 on GreatSchools, which adds lasting value to any home purchase.

Is The Vistas a Resale Only Market?

Yes. The Vistas is fully built out with no new construction available. Every transaction is a resale, which means buyers are purchasing established homes in mature, landscaped neighborhoods. This also means inventory can be limited at times, so working with an agent who knows the subcommunities and their pricing trends is essential. The European inspired architecture throughout The Vistas holds up well, and most homes have been well maintained by long term owners.

Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate specialist, recommends that buyers interested in The Vistas get familiar with the individual subcommunities before scheduling tours.
