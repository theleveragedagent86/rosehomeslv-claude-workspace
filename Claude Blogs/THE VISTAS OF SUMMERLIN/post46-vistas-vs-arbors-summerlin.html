The Vistas vs The Arbors Summerlin

Two of Summerlin's most popular villages, The Vistas and The Arbors, attract buyers for different reasons. Both sit within the master planned community of Summerlin, but they differ in age, location, pricing, and overall feel. Here is how they compare so you can decide which one fits your lifestyle best.

How Do Home Prices Compare Between The Vistas and The Arbors?

The Vistas features homes ranging from about $400,000 to over $2,500,000, with a median price around $600,000 to $650,000. The Arbors typically comes in slightly lower on the median, which makes sense given its older construction. Most homes in The Arbors were built in the mid 1990s through the early 2000s, while The Vistas was primarily built between 2002 and 2006. That newer construction in The Vistas often translates to more modern floor plans and updated finishes.

Which Village Has a Better Location?

The Arbors sits in Summerlin South, closer to the Las Vegas Strip at roughly 15 to 20 minutes by car. The Vistas is the first village west of the 215 Beltway, placing it closer to Red Rock Canyon and the open desert landscape. If your priority is proximity to the Strip, The Arbors has the edge. If you want that western mountain backdrop with easy trail access across 150 plus miles of paths, The Vistas wins that category.

What About Community Features and Safety?

The Vistas spans 815 acres across 26 to 28 subcommunities, offering 37 plus acres of parks, a community pool, tennis courts, basketball courts, and softball fields. The Arbors also has its own parks and amenities, though the overall footprint is smaller. Both villages include a mix of gated and non gated subcommunities. Safety is a standout for The Vistas, which ranks in the 97th percentile for safety in Las Vegas. Schools serving The Vistas, including Givens Elementary, Rogich Middle, and Palo Verde High School, all carry 8 out of 10 ratings.

Local Insight From Ryan Rose

Ryan Rose often tells buyers that choosing between The Vistas and The Arbors comes down to priorities. The Arbors works well for people who want established landscaping, a slightly lower entry price, and a shorter drive to the Strip. The Vistas appeals to buyers who value newer construction, European inspired architecture, and that elevated western Summerlin setting near Red Rock Canyon. Both are strong communities; it is really about which lifestyle details matter most to you.
