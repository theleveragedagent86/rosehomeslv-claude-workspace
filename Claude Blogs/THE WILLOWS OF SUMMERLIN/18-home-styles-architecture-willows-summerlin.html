# Home Styles and Architecture in The Willows Summerlin The Willows in Summerlin features an unusually diverse range of home styles across its 20 subdivisions, from compact single story condos starting around $400,000 to sprawling custom estates exceeding $2.8 million. Built between 1998 and 2007 by Toll Brothers, Pulte Homes, and William Lyon Homes, this community showcases the architectural variety that defined Summerlin's golden era of development. ## What Architectural Styles Are Found in The Willows? Most homes in The Willows reflect the desert contemporary and Mediterranean revival styles popular in Las Vegas during the late 1990s and early 2000s. Stucco exteriors in earth tones, clay tile roofs, and arched entryways appear throughout the community. Some custom homes in Willow Falls incorporate Tuscan influences with stone accents and courtyard entries. The overall aesthetic is cohesive without being repetitive, thanks to the involvement of three different builders across 20 neighborhoods. ## What Home Types Are Available? The Willows offers nearly every residential format. Condos and townhomes provide entry level options in more compact floor plans. Single family homes make up the majority of the community, with single story and two story configurations available. Subdivisions like Castlewood (1,158 to 1,667 sq ft) and Autumn Ridge (1,432 to 1,628 sq ft) serve buyers seeking smaller, mostly single story homes. Heritage Glen (3,364 to 5,085 sq ft) and Eden Ridge (3,091 to 5,757 sq ft) deliver larger floor plans on lots up to 0.69 acres. Willow Creek tops the scale with homes reaching 9,476 square feet. ## How Do Lot Sizes Vary Across Subdivisions? Lot sizes in The Willows range from 0.17 to 0.69 acres, a spread that is significant by Summerlin standards. Wood Glen offers lots between 0.17 and 0.3 acres, while Eden Ridge provides the largest lots at up to 0.69 acres. Bella Veranda, a gated William Lyon Homes subdivision, features 80 homes between 2,578 and 3,001 square feet in mostly single story configurations. This variety means buyers can find everything from a low maintenance courtyard home to a full estate property within the same village. ## A Local Perspective on Architecture in The Willows Ryan Rose, a Las Vegas real estate expert, appreciates the architectural diversity that sets The Willows apart from newer Summerlin communities. Modern developments tend to offer two or three floor plans repeated throughout. The Willows, with its 20 subdivisions and three builders, gives buyers genuine choices in style, size, and lot configuration. Mature landscaping throughout the community adds character that new construction cannot match. ## Searching for the Right Home Style?
