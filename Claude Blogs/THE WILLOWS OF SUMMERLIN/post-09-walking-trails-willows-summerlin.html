Walking Trails in The Willows Summerlin: A Complete Guide

The Willows village in Summerlin offers multiple walking trails, including the Willows Park Trail (0.5 to 1.4 miles), the Willows Paseo Linear Trail running the length of the village, and connections to Summerlin's 200 plus mile trail system. Whether you walk for fitness, relaxation, or simply getting from point A to point B, this neighborhood delivers.

What Trails Are Available in The Willows?

The primary walking route is the Willows Park Trail, which starts at Willows Park and ends at Town Center Drive, covering between 0.5 and 1.4 miles depending on your route. This trail is especially popular with dog walkers and families looking for a safe, scenic path. The Willows Paseo Linear Trail provides an additional option, meandering through the village and connecting the east and west sides of the community through an 11 acre green corridor.

Can You Connect to the Larger Summerlin Trail System?

Yes, The Willows trails link directly into the broader Summerlin Trail System, which spans more than 200 miles across the master planned community. These connected paths reach parks, shopping centers, and schools, making it possible to run daily errands on foot or by bike. For residents who value a walkable lifestyle, this connectivity is a major draw.

Are The Willows Trails Good for Families and Dog Walkers?

The Willows Park Trail is specifically noted as a favorite among dog walkers and families with children. The trails pass through 34 plus acres of parkland spread across three community parks, offering shade, play structures, and open space along the way. The flat, well maintained paths are stroller and wheelchair accessible, making them usable for residents of all ages and ability levels.
