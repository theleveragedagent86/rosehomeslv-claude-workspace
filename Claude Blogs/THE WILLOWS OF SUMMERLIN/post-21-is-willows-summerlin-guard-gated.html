Is The Willows a Guard-Gated Community in Summerlin?

Buyers searching for gated living in Summerlin often ask whether The Willows is a guard gated community. The answer is nuanced: The Willows itself is not entirely guard gated, but it contains neighborhoods that offer both guard gated and standard gated access.

Which Parts of The Willows Are Guard Gated?

Willow Creek is the premier guard gated enclave within The Willows. Developed by Toll Brothers, Willow Creek features a staffed guard gate controlling entry to its collection of luxury neighborhoods. Within Willow Creek, you will find Wood Glen, Willow Falls, Heritage Glen, and Eden Ridge. Homes in Willow Creek range from 2,670 to 9,476 square feet on lots spanning 0.17 to 0.69 acres, making it the most exclusive section of the village.

Willow Falls takes security a step further with a second gate inside Willow Creek. This double gated pocket contains 80 custom luxury homes on lots measuring 14,000 to 18,000 square feet. For buyers who prioritize maximum privacy and controlled access, Willow Falls delivers a level of seclusion that few Summerlin neighborhoods can match.

What About Gated (Non Guard Gated) Neighborhoods?

Two additional neighborhoods in The Willows use automated gates without a staffed guard booth. Bella Veranda, built by William Lyon Homes, is a gated community of 80 homes ranging from 2,578 to 3,001 square feet. Heather Glen is also gated, offering homes between 2,000 and 4,000 plus square feet. Both provide a layer of controlled access at a lower HOA cost than the guard gated Willow Creek section.

Are the Remaining Neighborhoods Open Access?

The majority of The Willows' 20 subdivisions operate without gates. Neighborhoods like Autumn Ridge, Castlewood, Glenbrook, and Sierra Woods offer open street access while still benefiting from The Willows' master association amenities. Residents in these neighborhoods pay the $76 per month master HOA fee and enjoy full access to the Willows Park, pool, community center, and 34 plus acres of parkland.
