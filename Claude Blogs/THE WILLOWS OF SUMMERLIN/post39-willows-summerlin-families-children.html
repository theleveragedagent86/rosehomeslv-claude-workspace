The Willows Summerlin for Families with Children

Few neighborhoods in Las Vegas can match the combination of parks, schools, and safety that The Willows delivers for families. Spanning 731 acres with 20 subdivisions, this Summerlin village was built with children in mind from the ground up.

Three Parks Covering 34+ Acres

The Willows gives families access to three dedicated parks that offer something for every age group. The Willows Park covers 12.7 acres and features a playground, resident only pool with water slides, softball fields, and basketball courts. Spotted Leaf Park adds another 10.5 acres with soccer fields, tennis courts, and its own playground. Willows Paseo Park rounds out the options at 11 acres, offering baseball diamonds, basketball and tennis courts, plus the beloved Aesop's Fables sculpture garden that younger children love to explore.

With more than 34 acres of parkland spread across the community, families never need to drive far to find outdoor recreation. The parks stay well maintained year round, and the resident only pool ensures a quieter, more controlled swimming experience compared to public facilities.

Top Rated Schools Within Reach

Parents in The Willows have strong public school options at every level. Ober Elementary (K through 5) sits within the village itself, allowing many students to walk or bike to class. Goolsby Elementary (K through 5) serves as an additional option nearby. Fertitta Middle School handles grades 6 through 8, and Palo Verde High School covers the high school years for grades 9 through 12.

Families seeking private education also have excellent choices close by. Faith Lutheran, Bishop Gorman, and The Meadows School all sit within a short drive. This range of educational options means families can choose the best fit for each child without long commutes.

Safety and Walkability

The Willows falls within Summerlin South, which reports a crime rate of 27.44 per 1,000 residents. That number sits below both the Nevada state average and the national average, giving parents extra confidence about letting kids play outside. Walking trails throughout the community connect to the broader Summerlin trail system, which stretches over 200 miles. Families regularly use these paths for evening walks, bike rides, and weekend outings.

Walking distance to Downtown Summerlin adds another layer of convenience. Families can grab dinner, catch a movie, or enjoy seasonal events without fighting traffic across town.

Local Insight from Ryan Rose

Ryan Rose consistently recommends The Willows to families relocating to Las Vegas. He points out that having three full sized parks, a resident only pool with water slides, and a walkable elementary school within the village creates a daily quality of life that most neighborhoods simply cannot replicate. The combination of strong schools, low crime, and trail connectivity makes The Willows one of the most complete family neighborhoods in all of Summerlin.
