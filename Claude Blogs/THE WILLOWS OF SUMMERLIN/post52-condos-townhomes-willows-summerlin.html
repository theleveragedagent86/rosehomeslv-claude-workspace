Condos and Townhomes in The Willows Summerlin

Looking for attached housing in one of Summerlin's most desirable neighborhoods? The Willows offers a limited selection of condos and townhomes that provide access to this premier community at a more accessible price point than single family homes.

What to Expect From Attached Homes Here

The Willows is primarily a single family community, so condos and townhomes represent a smaller portion of the overall inventory. That limited supply works in buyers' favor when it comes to long term value. When attached units do hit the market, they tend to move quickly because demand consistently outpaces availability.

Homes throughout The Willows were built between 1998 and 2007 by builders including Toll Brothers, Pulte Homes, and William Lyon Homes. The construction quality across the community reflects the high standards these builders brought to Summerlin during that era. Even attached units benefit from the thoughtful design and durable materials that define this neighborhood.

Pricing and Value Proposition

The overall price range in The Willows spans from approximately $400,000 to over $2,850,000. Condos and townhomes typically fall on the lower end of that spectrum, making them an ideal entry point for first time buyers or those looking to downsize. The master HOA fee of $76 per month covers community amenities and common area maintenance.

Nevada's lack of state income tax adds another financial advantage for homeowners here. Clark County's property tax rate of approximately 0.53% of assessed value ranks among the lowest in the country. Those savings add up significantly over the life of your ownership.

Location Benefits

The Willows sits within walking distance of Downtown Summerlin, giving residents easy access to shopping, dining, and entertainment. Red Rock Canyon is just minutes away for outdoor recreation. The community covers 731 acres across 20 subdivisions, offering a spacious feel despite its convenient location.

Because the community is fully built out with no new construction planned, the character of the neighborhood is well established. Buyers can walk the streets, meet neighbors, and get a true sense of daily life before making a decision. There are no surprises from future development changing the landscape.
