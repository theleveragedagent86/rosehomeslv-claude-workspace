Moving to The Willows From Out of State

Relocating to Las Vegas from another state is one of the biggest lifestyle shifts you can make. The Willows in Summerlin offers newcomers a well established village with strong amenities, excellent schools, and financial advantages that make the transition rewarding.

Tax Advantages That Matter Immediately

Nevada collects no state income tax. That single fact drives thousands of relocations each year from high tax states like California, New York, and Illinois. Your paycheck, retirement withdrawals, and investment income stay intact. Clark County's property tax rate sits at approximately 0.53%, which is significantly lower than most metropolitan areas nationwide.

For a home priced at $600,000 in The Willows, annual property taxes would run roughly $3,180. Compare that to states where effective rates exceed 1.5% or 2%, and the savings become substantial over time. The Summerlin master HOA adds $76 per month, with additional sub HOA fees varying by subdivision.

What The Willows Offers New Residents

The Willows spans 731 acres and contains 20 subdivisions with home prices ranging from $400,000 to $2,850,000 or more. This range gives relocating buyers options at nearly every budget level. The village was built between 1998 and 2007 by respected builders including Toll Brothers, Pulte Homes, and William Lyon Homes.

Families will find top rated schools zoned to the village, including Ober Elementary, Goolsby Elementary, and Palo Verde High School. Over 34 acres of parks, a resident only pool with water slides, and connections to Summerlin's 200 plus mile trail system create a neighborhood that feels complete from day one. Downtown Summerlin's 125 plus shops and restaurants sit within walking distance.

Getting Around and Getting Settled

Harry Reid International Airport is approximately 20 minutes from The Willows, which simplifies travel back to visit family or for business. The Las Vegas Strip is about 25 minutes by car. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center sits just 2 to 3 miles away, making healthcare access convenient for the entire family.

The desert climate delivers 300 plus days of sunshine each year with mild winters and hot summers. New arrivals from colder climates appreciate the lack of snow removal, ice, and seasonal road hazards. Summers do bring intense heat, so homes with updated HVAC systems and energy efficient windows are worth prioritizing.

Planning Your Relocation

Out of state buyers benefit from working with a local agent who understands how different Willows subdivisions compare. Guard gated Willow Creek appeals to luxury buyers, while communities like Autumn Ridge and Bella Veranda offer more accessible pricing. Having someone on the ground to tour homes, evaluate condition, and negotiate on your behalf streamlines the entire process.
