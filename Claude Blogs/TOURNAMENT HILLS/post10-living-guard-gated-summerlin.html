Living in a Guard Gated Community in Summerlin

Living behind a guard gate in Summerlin means enjoying daily privacy, enhanced security, and a quieter pace of life that is difficult to find elsewhere in Las Vegas. Communities like Tournament Hills, The Ridges, and Queensridge offer this lifestyle alongside luxury amenities and custom homes.

What Is Daily Life Like Behind a Guard Gate?

The most immediate difference is the quiet. Streets inside guard gated communities carry almost no through traffic, making them noticeably calmer than even the nicest non gated neighborhoods. Residents in communities like Tournament Hills enjoy amenities such as tennis courts, a basketball court, a playground, a shaded picnic pavilion, and a dog park, all within the security perimeter. The average household income in Tournament Hills is approximately $160,911, reflecting the established, professional demographic these communities attract.

How Does Guard Gated Living Affect Property Values?

Guard gated communities in Summerlin consistently command a premium over comparable non gated neighborhoods. In Tournament Hills, home prices range from $1,500,000 to over $13,900,000, with custom estates sitting on lots between 0.36 and 2.52 acres. The exclusivity of a staffed gate, combined with limited inventory (Tournament Hills has only 132 homes), creates strong demand and supports long term property appreciation. Buyers view the guard gate as both a lifestyle feature and a financial safeguard.

What Should Buyers Know Before Choosing a Guard Gated Community?

HOA fees in guard gated communities are higher than standard neighborhoods because they fund 24 hour security staffing and premium amenities. In Tournament Hills, monthly fees total approximately $527, covering the community HOA and the Summerlin master plan assessment. Ryan Rose advises buyers to compare not just the fees but also what those fees include, since the scope of security services and community amenities varies significantly from one guard gated neighborhood to another.

A Local Perspective

The lifestyle shift after moving behind a guard gate is something buyers often underestimate. It is not just about safety; it changes the way you use your own neighborhood. Kids ride bikes without supervision, evening walks feel relaxed, and there is a sense of community that develops naturally when everyone shares the same private space. Tournament Hills, with its small footprint of 132 homes, exemplifies this better than most.
