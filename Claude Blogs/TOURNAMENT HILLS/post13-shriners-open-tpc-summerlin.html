Shriners Children's Open at TPC Summerlin

The Shriners Children's Open is an official PGA TOUR event held annually at TPC Summerlin in the Tournament Hills neighborhood of Las Vegas. This tournament brings the world's top professional golfers to the western Las Vegas valley each fall, putting Tournament Hills on the national stage.

What Is the Shriners Children's Open?

The Shriners Children's Open is a PGA TOUR regular season event that raises funds for Shriners Children's hospitals across North America. The tournament takes place on TPC Summerlin's par 72, 7,243 yard championship course, designed by Bobby Weed with Fuzzy Zoeller as consultant. Each year, the event attracts large crowds to the Tournament Hills area, bringing national television coverage and significant economic activity to the community.

What Is It Like Living Near a PGA TOUR Event?

For Tournament Hills residents, the Shriners Children's Open is a unique neighborhood experience. Homeowners with golf course frontage can watch PGA TOUR professionals compete from their own backyards. The 132 custom homes in Tournament Hills surround TPC Summerlin, giving residents a front row seat to one of the most exciting weeks on the PGA TOUR calendar. Tournament week also brings heightened visibility to the neighborhood, which has historically supported strong property values.

How Does the Tournament Benefit Las Vegas?

Beyond the golf, the Shriners Children's Open generates millions of dollars for pediatric healthcare while showcasing Las Vegas as a world class sports destination. The event is part of a broader trend that includes the Las Vegas Super Bowl, Formula 1, and multiple professional sports franchises now calling the valley home. TPC Summerlin's role as host course has cemented Tournament Hills as one of the most recognized luxury neighborhoods in Nevada.
