Tiger Woods' First PGA Win at TPC Summerlin in 1996

Tiger Woods won his first PGA TOUR victory at TPC Summerlin in 1996, launching one of the most legendary careers in golf history right in the heart of Tournament Hills. That single moment transformed this Las Vegas course into hallowed ground for golf fans worldwide.

What Happened at TPC Summerlin in 1996?

In October 1996, a 20 year old Tiger Woods captured his first professional PGA TOUR win at the Las Vegas Invitational, played on TPC Summerlin's par 72 championship layout. Woods had turned professional just weeks earlier, and this victory signaled the arrival of a generational talent. The win took place on the same fairways and greens that Tournament Hills homeowners see from their backyards today.

Why Does Tiger Woods' Win Matter to Tournament Hills?

That 1996 victory permanently linked TPC Summerlin to one of the most iconic moments in golf history. The course, designed by Bobby Weed with Fuzzy Zoeller as consultant, had already earned recognition as one of Nevada's finest private clubs since its 1991 opening. Tiger Woods' breakthrough win added a layer of historical significance that continues to elevate the prestige of both the course and the surrounding Tournament Hills neighborhood.

How Has TPC Summerlin Built on That Legacy?

Since Woods' historic win, TPC Summerlin has continued to host PGA TOUR events, now known as the Shriners Children's Open. The course has been named "#1 Private Golf Course in Nevada" by Nevada Business Magazine and recognized by Golf Digest as "One of America's Best Golf Courses." For the 132 homeowners in Tournament Hills, living alongside a course with this caliber of history and ongoing professional play is a defining feature of the community.
