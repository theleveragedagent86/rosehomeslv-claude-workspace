Buying a Home in Tournament Hills

Buying a home in Tournament Hills requires proof of funds or a pre qualification letter before you can even tour a property. This guard gated Summerlin community of just 132 custom estates operates on a different level than typical Las Vegas real estate transactions, and preparation is everything.

What Do You Need Before Touring Tournament Hills Homes?

Tournament Hills enforces strict access requirements for prospective buyers. You must present a verified proof of funds statement or a mortgage pre qualification letter to your agent before scheduling any showing. The community's 24 hour guard gate controls entry, and unqualified visitors are turned away. Working with an experienced luxury agent who understands these protocols will save time and prevent frustration.

How Much Does It Cost to Buy in Tournament Hills?

Home prices in Tournament Hills range from approximately $1.5 million to $13.9 million, with a median sale price around $2.375 million. The community average approaches $5 million, reflecting the premium placed on custom construction, oversized lots, and proximity to TPC Summerlin. Monthly HOA fees run about $527 and cover guard gate staffing, common area landscaping, and community maintenance. Buyers should also factor in property taxes and potential renovation costs for older builds from the 1993 to 2001 construction period.

What Should Buyers Know About Inventory in Tournament Hills?

With only 132 homes in the entire community and a population of roughly 230 residents, available listings are scarce at any given time. Many transactions happen off market through private networking rather than public MLS listings. Some properties have been fully demolished and rebuilt, so buyers may find modern construction behind the gates despite the community being fully built out. Patience and strong agent relationships are essential for accessing the best opportunities.
