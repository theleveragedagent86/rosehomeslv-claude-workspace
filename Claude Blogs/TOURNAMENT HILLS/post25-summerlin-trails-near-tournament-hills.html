Summerlin Trail System Near Tournament Hills

Tournament Hills connects to the Summerlin Trail System, a network of over 150 miles of walking, biking, and jogging paths that wind through the master planned community and link neighborhoods, parks, and shopping areas.

How Big Is the Summerlin Trail System?

The Summerlin Trail System spans more than 150 miles of paved and natural surface paths. The trails are designed as a connected community network, meaning you can walk or bike from one end of Summerlin to another without using major roads. For Tournament Hills residents, this creates an outdoor recreation resource that extends far beyond the neighborhood boundaries. Runners, cyclists, and casual walkers all use the system daily.

Can You Access the Trails from Tournament Hills?

Yes. Tournament Hills sits within the Hills South village of Summerlin, and the trail network runs through and around the surrounding neighborhoods. Residents can step out of the community and join the trail system for a morning jog, an evening walk, or a weekend bike ride. The trails also connect to nearby parks like Bruce Trent Park and The Hills Park, making it easy to combine trail time with park visits in a single outing.

Are the Trails Good for Biking and Running?

The Summerlin trails accommodate multiple activities including walking, jogging, and biking. The paved sections are smooth enough for road bikes and strollers, while some natural surface areas offer a more rugged experience. For residents who want even more adventure, Red Rock Canyon is just 15 to 20 minutes by car and offers hiking trails ranging from 2 to 14 plus miles. The combination of Summerlin's urban trails and Red Rock's desert terrain gives Tournament Hills homeowners access to both everyday fitness routes and weekend adventure destinations.

What Makes the Trail System a Selling Point for Tournament Hills?

Ryan Rose considers the Summerlin Trail System one of the most underrated features for buyers exploring Tournament Hills. While the guard gated entry, 132 custom homes, and TPC Summerlin adjacency get the most attention, the trail system is what residents use every single day. It transforms the community from a luxury neighborhood into an active outdoor lifestyle, and that daily usability is what keeps homeowners happy long after closing day.
