Palo Verde High School and Tournament Hills

Palo Verde High School is the zoned public high school for Tournament Hills, serving grades 9 through 12 in the Clark County School District. Ranked in the top 20% of all public schools in Nevada, Palo Verde is one of the strongest high schools in the Las Vegas valley.

What Are Palo Verde High School's Academic Results?

Palo Verde High School delivers strong numbers across the board. Math proficiency reaches 40%, well above the Nevada state average of 28%. Reading proficiency stands at 61%, significantly higher than the state average of 44%. The graduation rate is 96%, outpacing the state average of 82%. The school offers Advanced Placement courses with a 35% AP participation rate, plus International Baccalaureate program pathways and a UNLV College Pathway for students looking to start earning college credit early.

What Programs and Activities Does Palo Verde Offer?

Palo Verde enrolls approximately 3,141 students and provides a wide range of academic and extracurricular programs. The AP and IB programs attract motivated students seeking college level coursework, while the UNLV College Pathway creates a direct bridge to higher education. Beyond academics, the school is known for active student life including clubs, athletics, and spirit events. Students and parents frequently highlight the strong sense of community during pep rallies, football games, and spirit weeks.

How Does Palo Verde Compare to Other Las Vegas High Schools?

Palo Verde ranks in the top 20% of Nevada's 692 schools and holds a Niche grade of B+. Its test scores, graduation rate, and program offerings place it ahead of most CCSD high schools. Ryan Rose notes that families moving to Tournament Hills from out of state often compare Palo Verde favorably to the top public high schools in their previous cities. The combination of IB, AP, and dual enrollment options gives students multiple paths to college preparation without requiring private school tuition.

Why Does Palo Verde Matter for Tournament Hills Homeowners?

Being zoned for a top tier public high school adds measurable value to homes in Tournament Hills. Buyers with teenagers or younger children planning ahead want to know their neighborhood feeds into a school with strong academics and a high graduation rate. The full public school pipeline from Lummis Elementary through Becker Middle School to Palo Verde High School gives families in this guard gated community of 132 custom homes a seamless K through 12 experience without leaving the Summerlin area.
