Red Rock Canyon Access from Tournament Hills

Tournament Hills residents can reach Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area in roughly 15 to 20 minutes by car, making it one of the closest luxury communities in Summerlin to this world class outdoor destination.

How Far Is Red Rock Canyon from Tournament Hills?

Red Rock Canyon sits just west of the Las Vegas Valley, and the drive from Tournament Hills takes approximately 15 to 20 minutes along West Charleston Boulevard. The conservation area spans nearly 200,000 acres of Mojave Desert landscape, offering hiking, rock climbing, mountain biking, and horseback riding. The centerpiece is a 13 mile scenic drive that loops through towering sandstone formations with colors ranging from cream to deep crimson. For Tournament Hills homeowners, this level of proximity to protected desert terrain is a genuine lifestyle advantage that few neighborhoods in the valley can match.

What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon?

The scenic drive is open year round, with seasonal hours running from 6:00 AM to 5:00 PM in winter and extending to 8:00 PM in summer. Timed entry reservations are required from October through May for vehicles entering between 8:00 AM and 5:00 PM. Over 30 miles of hiking trails range from easy interpretive walks to strenuous summit routes. Rock climbers travel from around the world to scale the sandstone walls, and the calico hills area draws photographers at sunrise and sunset. The visitor center is open daily from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM and provides trail maps, wildlife exhibits, and ranger programs.

Why Does Red Rock Canyon Matter for Tournament Hills Homeowners?

The proximity transforms daily life in Tournament Hills. Morning hikes before work, weekend family outings on the scenic drive, and sunset trail runs are all realistic options rather than occasional day trips. Combined with the Summerlin Trail System that connects directly to the neighborhood, Tournament Hills offers a true indoor outdoor lifestyle. Residents also benefit from the scenic corridor along West Charleston, where desert views remain unobstructed by development.
