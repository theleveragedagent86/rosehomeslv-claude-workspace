Las Vegas Ballpark at Downtown Summerlin Near Tournament Hills

Las Vegas Ballpark sits roughly five minutes from Tournament Hills in Downtown Summerlin, giving residents one of the shortest commutes to professional baseball in the Las Vegas Valley.

What Is the Las Vegas Ballpark?

Las Vegas Ballpark is a 10,000 seat stadium located on eight acres in Downtown Summerlin at 1650 South Pavilion Center Drive. It serves as the home field for the Las Vegas Aviators, the Triple A affiliate of the Athletics in the Pacific Coast League. The ballpark opened in 2019 and features 22 suites, club seating, berm seating, party decks, a kids zone, multiple bars, and a pool beyond the outfield wall. The facility hosts Aviators games from spring through early fall, along with concerts, festivals, and community events throughout the year.

Will MLB Games Be Played at Las Vegas Ballpark in 2026?

Yes. The Athletics will play regular season MLB games at Las Vegas Ballpark in 2026, hosting the Milwaukee Brewers from June 8 through 10 and the Colorado Rockies from June 12 through 14. This marks a historic moment for Las Vegas baseball and for Tournament Hills residents who can walk into a major league game just minutes from home. The Aviators also open their 2026 season in late March, meaning live baseball is available from spring through the fall months.

How Close Is Tournament Hills to the Ballpark?

Tournament Hills is located in the Hills South village of Summerlin, zip code 89134, and the drive to Las Vegas Ballpark takes approximately five minutes via Rampart Boulevard or Town Center Drive. The ballpark shares the Downtown Summerlin campus with City National Arena and over 125 shops and restaurants, so game nights can easily include dinner, shopping, or entertainment before or after the first pitch.
