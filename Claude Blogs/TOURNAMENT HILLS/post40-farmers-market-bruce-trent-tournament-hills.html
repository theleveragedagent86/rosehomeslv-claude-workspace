Las Vegas Farmers Market at Bruce Trent Park Near Tournament Hills

The Las Vegas Farmers Market at Bruce Trent Park runs every Wednesday just one minute from Tournament Hills, making it the closest weekly market to any guard gated community in Summerlin.

When Is the Farmers Market at Bruce Trent Park?

The Las Vegas Farmers Market operates every Wednesday from 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM at Bruce Trent Park, located at 1600 North Rampart Boulevard in Las Vegas, Nevada 89128. The market runs year round and features certified farmers, regional growers, and local producers selling fresh fruits, vegetables, baked goods, prepared foods, and artisan products. Vendors rotate seasonally, so the selection changes throughout the year. Parking is free and readily available at the park.

How Close Is the Farmers Market to Tournament Hills?

Bruce Trent Park sits approximately one minute from Tournament Hills by car and is also accessible on foot or by bike via the Summerlin Trail System. The 28 acre park borders the neighborhood, meaning Tournament Hills residents can walk to the market, browse the vendors, and return home with fresh produce in under an hour. This level of proximity turns a weekly farmers market from an occasional errand into a regular part of the neighborhood routine.

What Else Does Bruce Trent Park Offer?

Beyond the Wednesday market, Bruce Trent Park includes sports fields, tennis courts, a fitness course, walking paths, and shaded picnic areas. The park is named after the first Las Vegas parks superintendent and spans 28 acres of developed recreational space. For Tournament Hills families, the park functions as an extension of the neighborhood itself. Kids can play on the fields while parents shop the market, and evening walks through the park are a daily occurrence for many residents in the community.

What Does Ryan Rose Say About the Farmers Market?

Ryan Rose considers the Wednesday farmers market one of the hidden gems for Tournament Hills homeowners. Buyers often focus on the guard gate, the 132 custom homes, and the TPC Summerlin adjacency, but the ability to walk to a weekly farmers market at Bruce Trent Park creates a sense of neighborhood life and community connection that is hard to find in other luxury enclaves across the valley.
