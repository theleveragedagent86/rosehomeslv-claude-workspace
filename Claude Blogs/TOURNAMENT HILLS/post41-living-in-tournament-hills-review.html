What Is It Like Living in Tournament Hills?

Living in Tournament Hills means residing in one of Summerlin's most exclusive guard gated enclaves, where just 132 custom homes sit on oversized lots bordering TPC Summerlin golf course. This neighborhood delivers privacy, security, and a quiet luxury lifestyle that few Las Vegas communities can replicate.

What Makes Daily Life in Tournament Hills Different?

Tournament Hills operates at a pace that feels removed from the Las Vegas strip, even though the drive is only 20 to 25 minutes. The community is part of The Hills South village within Summerlin, and its population of roughly 230 people creates an atmosphere closer to a private estate compound than a typical subdivision. Guard gated entry with 24 hour roaming security means residents rarely think about safety, and the low density ensures streets stay quiet throughout the day.

What Amenities Do Residents Use Most?

Inside the community, residents have access to tennis courts, a basketball court, a playground, a shaded picnic pavilion, and a private dog park. TPC Summerlin club members also enjoy an Olympic sized swimming pool, additional tennis courts, and a full clubhouse. Beyond the gates, Downtown Summerlin sits five minutes away with dining, shopping, and entertainment. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is also five to ten minutes from the neighborhood, adding practical convenience to the luxury setting.

Who Lives in Tournament Hills?

The median age in Tournament Hills is 57, and the average household income is $160,911. The community attracts established professionals, retirees, and reportedly several celebrity homeowners. Homes range from $1.5 million to over $13.9 million, with architectural styles spanning classic, Mediterranean, and contemporary designs on lots between 0.36 and 2.52 acres. This price range and lot size attract buyers who prioritize space and privacy over proximity to nightlife.

Is Tournament Hills Worth the Investment?

Ryan Rose works with buyers evaluating Tournament Hills against other guard gated Summerlin communities, and the consistent feedback centers on the combination of TPC Summerlin adjacency, legitimate security, and lot sizes that are difficult to find elsewhere in the valley. The community's small footprint of 132 homes means resale inventory is always limited, which has historically supported strong property values.
