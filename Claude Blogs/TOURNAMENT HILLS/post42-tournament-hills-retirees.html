Tournament Hills for Retirees and Empty Nesters

Tournament Hills is one of the most appealing guard gated communities in Las Vegas for retirees, with a median resident age of 57, 24 hour security, and a quiet neighborhood of only 132 custom homes in the Hills South village of Summerlin.

Why Do Retirees Choose Tournament Hills?

The community's small scale and guard gated entry create the kind of controlled, low traffic environment that retirees value. With a population of roughly 230 people, Tournament Hills operates more like a private residential compound than a busy subdivision. Streets stay calm, noise is minimal, and the 24 hour roaming security patrol means residents can travel without worrying about their property. The average household income of $160,911 reflects an established, financially secure community of peers.

What Healthcare and Conveniences Are Nearby?

Summerlin Hospital Medical Center is five to ten minutes from Tournament Hills at 657 N. Town Center Drive, providing immediate access to emergency and specialty care. Downtown Summerlin, with its pharmacies, grocery stores, restaurants, and shopping, sits just five minutes away. Harry Reid International Airport is a 25 to 30 minute drive, making travel straightforward for retirees who split time between Las Vegas and other locations.

What Recreation Options Suit Active Retirees?

Tournament Hills borders TPC Summerlin, giving golf enthusiasts proximity to one of the top courses in the Southwest. Club members gain access to an Olympic sized swimming pool, tennis courts, and a clubhouse. Within the neighborhood itself, tennis courts, a shaded picnic pavilion, and a private dog park provide daily activity without leaving the gates. The Summerlin Trail System connects over 150 miles of walking and biking paths, perfect for retirees who want low impact outdoor exercise close to home.

Is Tournament Hills the Right Fit for Empty Nesters?

Ryan Rose regularly advises empty nesters considering a move to Tournament Hills, and the conversation often centers on the same advantages: oversized lots between 0.36 and 2.52 acres deliver space and privacy, the guard gate eliminates unwanted solicitation, and the community's mature demographic means neighbors share similar lifestyles. Homes built between 1993 and 2001 in classic, Mediterranean, and contemporary styles offer single story and flexible layouts that suit aging in place.
