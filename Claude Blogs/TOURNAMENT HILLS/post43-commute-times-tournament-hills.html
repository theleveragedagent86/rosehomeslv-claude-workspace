Commute Times from Tournament Hills Las Vegas

Tournament Hills in Summerlin offers commute times of 20 to 25 minutes to the Las Vegas Strip, 25 to 30 minutes to Harry Reid International Airport, and just 5 minutes to Downtown Summerlin, making it one of the most conveniently located guard gated communities in the valley.

How Far Is Tournament Hills from the Las Vegas Strip?

The drive from Tournament Hills to the Las Vegas Strip covers roughly 16 miles and takes 20 to 25 minutes via Summerlin Parkway. During off peak hours, the commute can drop closer to 18 minutes. Summerlin Parkway connects directly to US 95, providing a straightforward route without navigating surface streets through congested corridors. Residents who work on or near the Strip benefit from a reverse commute pattern, since most traffic flows into Summerlin during evening hours.

How Long Does It Take to Reach the Airport?

Harry Reid International Airport is approximately 16 miles from Tournament Hills, with a typical drive time of 25 to 30 minutes depending on traffic and time of day. The route follows Summerlin Parkway to US 95 South, then connects to I 15 or surface streets near the airport. For frequent travelers, this commute is manageable and predictable, especially when departing in the early morning or midday window before rush hour builds.

What About Downtown Las Vegas and Everyday Errands?

Downtown Las Vegas sits 20 to 25 minutes from Tournament Hills. More relevant for daily life, Downtown Summerlin is a five minute drive and serves as the primary hub for groceries, dining, retail, and entertainment. Summerlin Hospital Medical Center at 657 N. Town Center Drive is five to ten minutes away. Schools, fitness centers, and professional offices throughout Summerlin are all reachable in under 15 minutes, keeping most errands within a tight radius of the neighborhood.

Does Location Affect Tournament Hills Home Values?

Ryan Rose points out that Tournament Hills occupies a sweet spot in the Summerlin market: far enough from the Strip to feel residential and peaceful, but close enough that working professionals and business owners face a manageable commute. The five minute proximity to Downtown Summerlin eliminates the isolation that some remote luxury communities experience, and the direct Summerlin Parkway access keeps drive times consistent even as the valley's population grows.
