Tournament Hills Community: Only 132 Custom Homes

Tournament Hills contains exactly 132 custom built homes on lots ranging from 0.36 to 2.52 acres, making it one of the smallest and most exclusive guard gated communities in all of Summerlin and the Las Vegas valley.

Why Does the 132 Home Count Matter?

A community of 132 homes operates fundamentally differently than neighborhoods with 500 or 1,000 plus residences. Shared amenities like the tennis courts, basketball court, playground, and dog park serve a population of roughly 230 people, meaning facilities stay uncrowded and well maintained. HOA decisions involve fewer homeowners, which typically leads to faster action on maintenance, landscaping, and community improvements. The guard gate manages a known set of residents and guests, strengthening security through familiarity.

What Types of Custom Homes Were Built Here?

Every home in Tournament Hills was custom built between 1993 and 2001, with architectural styles spanning classic, Mediterranean, and contemporary designs. Unlike production home communities where a handful of floor plans repeat throughout the neighborhood, each Tournament Hills residence reflects individual design choices by its original owner. Lot sizes between 0.36 and 2.52 acres give homes significant separation from neighbors, and many properties face TPC Summerlin golf course with unobstructed views of the surrounding mountains.

How Does Limited Inventory Affect the Market?

With only 132 total homes, Tournament Hills rarely has more than a few properties listed for sale at any given time. Current prices range from $1.5 million to over $13.9 million, with the average hovering around $5 million. This scarcity creates a market dynamic where desirable listings attract serious buyers quickly. Homes in Tournament Hills do not sit on the market the way properties in larger communities often do, because the supply is permanently fixed and demand from luxury buyers in Summerlin remains strong.

What Makes 132 Homes the Ideal Community Size?

Ryan Rose considers Tournament Hills a case study in how community size shapes the ownership experience. At 132 homes, the neighborhood is large enough to fund quality amenities and 24 hour guard gate security through HOA dues, but small enough that every resident recognizes their neighbors. Celebrity homeowners have reportedly chosen Tournament Hills precisely because the compact size limits foot traffic and outside exposure. For buyers who want a true neighborhood feel within a luxury, guard gated setting, the 132 home footprint is difficult to replicate anywhere else in Las Vegas.
