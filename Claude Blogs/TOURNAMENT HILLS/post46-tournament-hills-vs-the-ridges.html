Tournament Hills vs The Ridges Summerlin

Tournament Hills and The Ridges are two of Summerlin's most prestigious guard gated communities, but they differ in age, architecture, and overall character. Choosing between them depends on whether you prioritize classic estate living or modern contemporary design.

How Do Tournament Hills and The Ridges Compare on Price?

Both communities sit in the ultra luxury tier. Tournament Hills homes range from $1.5 million to $13.9 million, with a community average near $5 million. The Ridges offers a similar spread, with homes typically starting around $2 million and reaching above $15 million. Lot sizes in Tournament Hills range from 0.36 to 2.52 acres, giving buyers some of the largest homesites in all of Summerlin. The Ridges generally features smaller lots, though its newer construction and modern finishes often command premium pricing per square foot.

What Are the Architectural Differences?

This is where the two communities diverge most. Tournament Hills was built between 1993 and 2001, with 132 custom homes reflecting Mediterranean, Tuscan, and traditional desert estate styles. The Ridges was developed primarily in the 2000s and 2010s, and modern contemporary architecture dominates the streetscape. Buyers drawn to clean lines, walls of glass, and minimalist design tend to favor The Ridges. Those who prefer timeless estate architecture with mature landscaping often gravitate toward Tournament Hills.

Which Community Has Better Golf Access?

Tournament Hills sits directly adjacent to TPC Summerlin, home of the annual PGA TOUR Shriners Children's Open. Residents live steps from a championship course that hosts professional events each fall. The Ridges borders Bear's Best Golf Club, a Tom Fazio designed course featuring replicas of 18 celebrated holes from across the country. Both offer outstanding golf proximity, but TPC Summerlin's status as a PGA TOUR venue gives Tournament Hills a distinct edge in prestige and national visibility.

Ryan Rose's Local Insight

Ryan Rose finds that buyers comparing these two communities often come down to a preference for established legacy or contemporary newness. Tournament Hills offers irreplaceable lot sizes, a fully built out and mature neighborhood, and the cachet of TPC Summerlin next door. The Ridges delivers newer construction and a modern aesthetic. Both are exceptional, but Tournament Hills' fixed supply of just 132 homes creates a scarcity factor that The Ridges, with its larger footprint, simply cannot replicate.
