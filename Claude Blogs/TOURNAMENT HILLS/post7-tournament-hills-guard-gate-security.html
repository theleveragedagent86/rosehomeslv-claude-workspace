Tournament Hills Guard Gate and 24-Hour Security

Tournament Hills in Summerlin, Las Vegas provides round the clock protection through a staffed guard gate and 24 hour roaming security patrols. For the 132 families living inside this exclusive community, security is not an afterthought; it is built into the foundation of daily life.

How Does the Tournament Hills Guard Gate Work?

The guard gate at Tournament Hills is staffed 24 hours a day, seven days a week. Every vehicle entering the community is stopped and verified by on site security personnel. Residents are given seamless access, while guests and service providers must be pre approved or cleared at the gate. This system ensures that only authorized individuals enter the neighborhood at any time.

What About Security Beyond the Gate?

In addition to the staffed entry point, Tournament Hills employs roaming security guards who patrol the community throughout the day and night. These patrols cover the neighborhood's private streets, common areas, and perimeter. The combination of a controlled entry gate and active patrols creates a layered security approach that few communities in Las Vegas can match.

Why Is This Level of Security Important for Tournament Hills?

With home prices ranging from $1,500,000 to over $13,900,000, Tournament Hills attracts high net worth buyers who prioritize privacy and safety. The community is located in zip code 89134, which is already recognized as a low crime area within Las Vegas. Celebrity homeowners have reportedly chosen Tournament Hills in part because of these security measures. Ryan Rose notes that the guard gate is consistently one of the top reasons buyers choose this community over other luxury options in Summerlin.

A Local Perspective

What stands out about Tournament Hills security is the personal touch. Because the community has only 132 homes, the guards know residents by name and by vehicle. It creates a level of comfort that goes beyond what you experience in larger guard gated neighborhoods where turnover is higher and interactions feel transactional.
