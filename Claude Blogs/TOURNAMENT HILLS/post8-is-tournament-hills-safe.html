Is Tournament Hills Safe?

Tournament Hills is one of the safest neighborhoods in Las Vegas, protected by a 24 hour staffed guard gate, roaming security patrols, and its location in zip code 89134, a recognized low crime area. With only 132 homes behind the gate, this Summerlin community offers an exceptional level of security and privacy.

What Security Features Keep Tournament Hills Safe?

The community's primary safety feature is its staffed guard gate, which operates around the clock. Every visitor must be verified before entering, and roaming security guards patrol the neighborhood day and night. These layered measures mean residents rarely encounter uninvited foot traffic or solicitors. The small size of the community, just 132 custom homes, also means that unfamiliar faces are quickly noticed.

How Safe Is the Surrounding Area?

Tournament Hills sits within zip code 89134 in Summerlin, which consistently ranks as one of the lower crime areas in the greater Las Vegas metro. The community is part of The Hills South village, surrounded by other established Summerlin neighborhoods and the TPC Summerlin golf course. Downtown Summerlin is roughly a five minute drive away, while the Las Vegas Strip is about 20 to 25 minutes by car, keeping the neighborhood removed from high traffic tourist corridors.

Who Lives in Tournament Hills?

The median age in Tournament Hills is 57, with an average household income of approximately $160,911. The community attracts established professionals and reportedly includes celebrity homeowners. Ryan Rose, who works closely with buyers in this market, confirms that the combination of guard gated security and a tight knit, small community is a deciding factor for most families who choose Tournament Hills over other luxury options.

A Local Perspective

Safety in Tournament Hills goes beyond crime statistics. The streets are quiet enough for evening walks, neighbors recognize one another, and the guard gate creates a genuine sense of separation from the busy valley below. It is the kind of neighborhood where residents leave packages on their doorsteps without a second thought.
