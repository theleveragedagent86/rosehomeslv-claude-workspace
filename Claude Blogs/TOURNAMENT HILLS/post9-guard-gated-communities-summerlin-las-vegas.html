Guard Gated Communities in Summerlin Las Vegas

Summerlin is home to some of the most prestigious guard gated communities in the Las Vegas valley, each offering 24 hour security, luxury homes, and a private lifestyle. From golf course estates to custom hilltop properties, these neighborhoods represent the highest tier of Summerlin living.

Which Communities in Summerlin Are Guard Gated?

Five standout guard gated communities define luxury living in Summerlin. Tournament Hills, with only 132 custom homes along the TPC Summerlin golf course, offers prices from $1.5 million to over $13.9 million. The Ridges is known for its modern, ultra luxury custom estates with dramatic red rock canyon views. Canyon Fairways provides a golf course lifestyle within an intimate guard gated setting. Queensridge features both custom homes and the iconic luxury high rise towers. Spanish Hills delivers expansive custom properties with sweeping views of the Las Vegas Strip.

What Are the Benefits of Living Behind a Guard Gate?

Guard gated communities provide a level of security that standard gated neighborhoods cannot match. A staffed gate means every visitor is verified by a live person, not just a keypad or call box. Many of these communities also employ roaming security patrols, adding another layer of protection. For high net worth homeowners and public figures, this combination of controlled access and active monitoring is essential.

How Do You Choose the Right Guard Gated Community?

Each community has a distinct personality. Tournament Hills appeals to buyers who want a small, tight knit neighborhood near world class golf. The Ridges attracts those seeking contemporary architecture and canyon views. Queensridge suits buyers who prefer a mix of high rise and estate living. Ryan Rose, a Summerlin luxury real estate specialist, recommends visiting multiple communities to compare the guard gate experience, lot sizes, architectural styles, and HOA structures before making a decision.

A Local Perspective

Not all guard gates are created equal. Some communities have hundreds of homes, which means more traffic through the gate and a less personal experience. The smaller communities, like Tournament Hills with its 132 homes, tend to provide a noticeably more private and attentive security presence. That distinction matters to buyers who value true exclusivity.
