# Tree Top Park in The Arbors Summerlin Tree Top Park is a 5.2-acre neighborhood park located at 600 Sageberry Drive within The Arbors village in Summerlin. The park serves as one of the primary family recreation spaces in the community, with facilities designed for younger children, casual sports, and gatherings. ## Park Amenities Tree Top Park features a T-ball field with bleachers, making it a home base for youth baseball activities in The Arbors. The park also includes an open grass area suitable for pickup games, catch, and informal sports, a picnic pavilion for group gatherings, a children's playground, and community restrooms. The open grass areas are well maintained and provide a flat, usable space that families appreciate for everything from kite flying to weekend picnics. The combination of structured amenities (the T-ball field and playground) and open space gives the park flexibility for different uses throughout the day. ## Park Hours and Rules Tree Top Park is open daily until 10:30 PM, allowing evening use during the warmer months when outdoor activity is more comfortable after sunset. Standard Summerlin park rules apply: dogs must be on a leash, no alcoholic beverages or glass containers, and no camping or lodging. ## Connection to Arbors Paseo Park Tree Top Park connects to the Arbors Paseo Park, a 7.6-acre linear park with walkways and grass areas that links Tree Top Park to the Arbors Tennis and Play Park. This connection creates a continuous green corridor through the heart of The Arbors, allowing residents to walk or jog between parks without crossing major streets. The connected park system is one of the design elements that makes The Arbors feel more cohesive than many other Summerlin villages. The green corridors provide shade, visual relief from the desert landscape, and practical exercise routes. ## Location Within The Arbors Tree Top Park sits in the central portion of The Arbors village, within walking distance of multiple residential neighborhoods. Its location on Sageberry Drive places it near the Arbors Tennis and Play Park, the Veterans Memorial Leisure Center, and several of the community's single-family home neighborhoods. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose notes that Tree Top Park is the go-to spot for families with young children in The Arbors. The T-ball field and playground draw parents on weekday afternoons and weekend mornings, creating a natural meeting point where neighbors connect.
