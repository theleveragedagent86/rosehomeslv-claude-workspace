# Arbors Tennis and Play Park Summerlin The Arbors Tennis and Play Park is one of the most unique community parks in the Summerlin master plan, combining competitive tennis facilities with family-friendly recreation at 610 Sageberry Drive. The park is home to the Summerlin Tennis Club and features amenities that cater to all ages, from toddlers to adult athletes. ## Tennis Facilities The park features five outdoor lighted tennis courts, including a stadium court designed for organized matches and tournaments. The courts are well maintained and available for both recreational play and competitive use. Lighting extends court availability into the evening hours, which is essential during the Las Vegas summer when daytime temperatures make midday play impractical. The Summerlin Tennis Club operates from this location, providing a full-time tennis professional, a pro shop for equipment and apparel, and organized programming including clinics, lessons, leagues, and tournaments for players of all ability levels. ## Arborville Tricycle Track One of the park's most distinctive features is "Arborville," a custom-built tricycle track designed specifically for toddlers and young children. The miniature roadway gives kids a safe, contained space to ride tricycles and balance bikes in a setting that mimics a small town with scaled-down streets. This is a feature that parents frequently cite as a favorite, and it is not replicated in other Summerlin parks. ## Basketball Court The park includes a full-sized lighted basketball court with six hoops, accommodating both half-court and full-court play. The lighting allows evening games, and the court sees regular use from teenagers and adults in The Arbors. ## Picnic and Play Areas Beyond the sports facilities, the park includes a picnic pavilion and open play areas suitable for casual gatherings and unstructured play. The combination of structured amenities and open space makes the park versatile enough for a morning tennis match, an afternoon birthday party, and an evening basketball game all in the same day. ## Connection to Adjacent Parks The Arbors Tennis and Play Park connects to the Arbors Paseo Park, which in turn links to Tree Top Park. This connected green corridor allows residents to walk or jog between three parks without leaving a landscaped path, creating a mini trail system within The Arbors village. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose highlights the Arbors Tennis and Play Park as one of the features that gives The Arbors its distinctive community feel. The combination of the tennis club, Arborville tricycle track, and basketball court creates a park that draws residents of all ages, and the connected path system makes it accessible on foot from much of the village.
