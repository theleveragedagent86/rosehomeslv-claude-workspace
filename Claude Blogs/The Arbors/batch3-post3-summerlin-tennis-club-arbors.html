# Summerlin Tennis Club at The Arbors The Summerlin Tennis Club operates out of the Arbors Tennis and Play Park at 610 Sageberry Drive, serving as the primary organized tennis program for Summerlin residents. The club offers professional instruction, competitive leagues, and social programming on five lighted outdoor courts, including a stadium court. ## Facilities The Summerlin Tennis Club has access to five well-maintained outdoor courts with lighting for evening play. The stadium court accommodates organized matches and community tournaments, while the remaining courts are used for lessons, clinics, and recreational play. A pro shop on-site provides equipment, apparel, and stringing services. The lighted courts are a significant feature in Las Vegas, where summer daytime temperatures make outdoor tennis impractical between roughly 10 AM and 6 PM for much of the year. Evening court availability extends the playing season and makes tennis a year-round activity for club members. ## Programming The club is led by a full-time tennis professional, Gordon Hammond, who oversees a range of programs for all ages and skill levels. Programming includes private and semi-private lessons, group clinics for beginners through advanced players, youth development programs, adult leagues, and community tournaments. The variety of programming means new players can learn the sport from scratch while experienced players can find competitive match play and structured league competition. The club also runs social events that combine tennis with community gathering, building relationships among players beyond the court. ## Access for Summerlin Residents The Summerlin Tennis Club is available to Summerlin residents through their community association membership. Court reservations and program enrollment are managed through the club, and pricing for lessons and clinics varies by format and group size. The club can be reached at 702-233-5831 for scheduling and program information. ## Location The club's location within The Arbors village puts it within walking or short driving distance of multiple Summerlin neighborhoods. For Arbors residents specifically, the club is accessible on foot via the connected park system that links the Arbors Tennis and Play Park to Tree Top Park and the Arbors Paseo Park. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose notes that the Summerlin Tennis Club is a genuine community asset that many buyers are not aware of until after they move in. The quality of the facilities and instruction, combined with the lighted courts for year-round play, creates a tennis experience that rivals private club offerings at a fraction of the cost.
