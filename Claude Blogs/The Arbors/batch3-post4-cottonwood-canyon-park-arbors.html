# Cottonwood Canyon Park in The Arbors Summerlin Cottonwood Canyon is a linear park running along the southern edge of The Arbors village in Summerlin, offering miles of jogging trails and green spaces that connect neighborhoods within the community. Unlike the more structured parks in The Arbors, Cottonwood Canyon is designed primarily for walking, running, and enjoying nature in a quiet, landscaped setting. ## Trail System The defining feature of Cottonwood Canyon is its trail network. Paved paths wind through the park's length, providing a continuous route for walkers, joggers, and cyclists. The trails are flat, well-maintained, and wide enough for two-way traffic, making them suitable for strollers, dog walkers, and runners alike. The linear design means residents can cover meaningful distances without retracing their steps, which is a practical advantage for runners who prefer point-to-point routes over loops. The trails connect to the broader Summerlin trail system, extending the range for residents who want longer outings. ## Green Spaces Cottonwood Canyon features grass areas and landscaped sections that break up the trail with spaces suitable for picnics, stretching, or simply sitting in a quiet outdoor setting. The mature trees and desert-adapted landscaping provide shade along portions of the trail, which is a meaningful benefit during the warmer months when exposed paths become uncomfortable. The park's position along a natural arroyo gives it a slightly more natural feel than the manicured parks elsewhere in The Arbors. The vegetation is denser, and the setting feels more like a greenway than a traditional rectangular park. ## Location and Access Cottonwood Canyon runs along the southern boundary of The Arbors, adjacent to the Cottonwood Terrace townhome community and several single-family neighborhoods. Residents of these neighborhoods can access the trails directly from their streets without driving to a trailhead. The park's southern position also makes it convenient for residents heading to Downtown Summerlin or the retail and dining options along the Charleston Boulevard corridor, as the trail system provides an alternative to driving for short trips. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose considers Cottonwood Canyon one of The Arbors' most underappreciated features. Many buyers focus on the structured parks and tennis courts, but the linear trail system through Cottonwood Canyon is what residents end up using daily. He recommends walking or jogging the trail during a community visit to experience the quiet, shaded atmosphere that makes The Arbors feel different from newer, less landscaped Summerlin developments.
