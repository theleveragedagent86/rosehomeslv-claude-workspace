# Veterans Memorial Leisure Center Near The Arbors The Veterans Memorial Leisure Services Center is a 45,000-square-foot public recreation facility located at 101 North Pavilion Center Drive within The Arbors village in Summerlin. Operated by the City of Las Vegas, the center provides fitness, aquatics, sports, and educational programming for residents of all ages at affordable rates. ## Fitness and Gym Facilities The center houses a fitness center with cardio machines, weight equipment, and dedicated workout spaces. An indoor gymnasium supports basketball, volleyball, and other court sports with open gym hours and organized leagues. Dance studios host a rotation of group fitness classes throughout the week. The variety of fitness options makes the center a practical alternative to a private gym membership, with the added benefit of being within walking distance for many Arbors residents. ## Indoor Pool In addition to the Olympic-sized outdoor pool at the adjacent Arbors Sports Park, the Veterans Memorial Leisure Center features an indoor pool for year-round aquatics. The indoor pool supports lap swimming, water aerobics, and swim instruction regardless of weather or season. This is a meaningful advantage during the cooler winter months when outdoor pools are closed. ## Classes and Programs The center offers an extensive schedule of classes for all ages. Programming includes yoga, Pilates, tai chi, Zumba, and other group fitness formats for adults. Youth programs include gymnastics, martial arts, dance, and seasonal camps. Senior-specific programming includes gentle exercise classes and social activities. Classes are priced affordably, and the center is known for offering discounts to veterans. The breadth of programming means residents can try multiple activities without committing to expensive specialty studios or private lessons. ## Community Space Beyond fitness and recreation, the Veterans Memorial Leisure Center functions as a community gathering point. The facility includes classroom spaces used for educational programs, community meetings, and special events. The center hosts activities throughout the year that bring Arbors and broader Summerlin residents together. ## Location The center sits at the intersection of Pavilion Center Drive and the Arbors Sports Park complex, making it easily accessible from all parts of The Arbors village. Parking is available on-site, and the connected park system provides walking access from nearby neighborhoods. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose considers the Veterans Memorial Leisure Center a genuine differentiator for The Arbors compared to other Summerlin villages. The combination of a public recreation center, Olympic pool, and sports park in a single location is not replicated elsewhere in the master plan at this scale. He notes that the affordable pricing and broad programming make it practical for families to use regularly rather than as an occasional amenity.
