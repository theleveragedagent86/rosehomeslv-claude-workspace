# Arbors Paseo Park Summerlin Arbors Paseo Park is a 7.6-acre linear park that serves as the green connector between Tree Top Park and the Arbors Tennis and Play Park in The Arbors village. The park creates a continuous landscaped corridor through the heart of the community, linking neighborhoods, parks, and amenities with walkways and open grass areas. ## Park Design Unlike traditional rectangular parks with defined sports facilities, Arbors Paseo Park is designed as a linear green space. Paved walkways wind through the park, flanked by grass areas and mature landscaping. The elongated design follows the natural flow of the village layout, making it function as both a park and a pedestrian pathway. The walkways are wide enough for two-way foot traffic and accommodate walkers, joggers, parents with strollers, and residents on bikes. Shaded seating areas are positioned along the path for resting or watching children play on the adjacent grass areas. ## Connecting The Arbors Park System Arbors Paseo Park's primary role is connecting Tree Top Park at one end to the Arbors Tennis and Play Park at the other. This connection creates a continuous green corridor that allows residents to walk or jog between parks without using sidewalks along major streets. The connected park system is approximately a half-mile end to end, providing a comfortable walking loop when combined with the paths within each connected park. This linked design was intentional in The Arbors' original planning and is one of the features that gives the village a more cohesive, walkable feel than many other Summerlin communities. ## Green Space and Recreation While Arbors Paseo Park does not have sports fields or playgrounds of its own, the open grass areas serve as informal recreation spaces. Residents use the grass for picnics, yoga, playing with children, and relaxing with pets on leash. The mature trees along the corridor provide shade that makes the park comfortable during the transitional months when temperatures are moderate. ## Location The park runs through the central portion of The Arbors village, placing it within walking distance of numerous residential neighborhoods. Homes adjacent to the Paseo Park corridor benefit from green space views and direct walking access to the broader park system. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose notes that homes adjacent to or near the Paseo Park corridor tend to hold their value well because of the green space buffer and walking access to the connected parks.
