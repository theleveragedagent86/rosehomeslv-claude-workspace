# Walking and Trails Near The Arbors Summerlin The Arbors is one of the most walkable villages in the Summerlin master plan, with an internal connected park system, a linear trail corridor through Cottonwood Canyon, and direct access to the broader Summerlin trail network that spans over 200 miles. For residents who value outdoor activity as part of their daily routine, The Arbors delivers a trail experience that newer communities have not yet matched. ## Internal Park Trail System The Arbors' signature feature is its connected park system. The Arbors Paseo Park creates a 7.6-acre green corridor linking Tree Top Park to the Arbors Tennis and Play Park. Paved walkways wind through landscaped grounds with mature shade trees, providing a comfortable walking route through the heart of the village. This internal system allows residents to walk between parks, the Summerlin Tennis Club, and the Veterans Memorial Leisure Center without using sidewalks along major streets. The paths are flat, well-maintained, and wide enough for strollers, dog walkers, and joggers to share comfortably. ## Cottonwood Canyon Trails The Cottonwood Canyon linear park runs along the southern edge of The Arbors, offering miles of paved trails through a more natural, arroyo-style setting. The trails are shaded by mature vegetation and connect to the broader Summerlin trail system, extending the walking range well beyond The Arbors' borders. Cottonwood Canyon is particularly popular with runners who prefer a point-to-point route over a loop, and with dog walkers who appreciate the quieter, more natural atmosphere compared to the structured parks. ## Summerlin Trail System Access The Arbors connects to the Summerlin trail system, which includes over 200 miles of street-side trails, village trails, bike lanes, and regional trails. From The Arbors, residents can access landscaped paths that lead to Downtown Summerlin, neighboring villages, and eventually to the Red Rock Canyon area via the planned regional trail connections. The street-side trails along the major corridors near The Arbors are lighted and landscaped, making them usable for early morning and evening walks year-round. ## Why Mature Landscaping Matters The Arbors' two-plus decades of growth have produced a tree canopy and landscape maturity that significantly improves the walking experience compared to newer Summerlin communities. Shade reduces pavement temperatures, improves comfort, and extends the usable hours during the warmer months. This is not a detail that shows up in community marketing, but it is one that residents notice daily. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose considers The Arbors' trail infrastructure one of its strongest but least marketed features. The connected park system, Cottonwood Canyon trails, and Summerlin trail access combine to create a community where walking and jogging are genuinely integrated into daily life rather than requiring a drive to a trailhead.
