# Best Dog Walking Spots Near The Arbors Summerlin Dog owners in The Arbors benefit from a community designed with walking paths at its core. The connected park system, linear trail corridors, and proximity to dedicated off-leash dog parks give residents multiple options for daily walks and play without driving far from home. ## Cottonwood Canyon Cottonwood Canyon is the top dog walking destination within The Arbors. The linear park runs along the southern edge of the village with miles of paved, shaded trails through a natural arroyo setting. The mature vegetation provides shade that keeps pavement temperatures lower during the warmer months, which is an important consideration for dogs' paws. Dogs on leash are welcome on the trails. The length of the Cottonwood Canyon trail system allows owners to cover meaningful distances without retracing their route, making it ideal for dogs that need a longer walk or a change of scenery within a single outing. ## Arbors Paseo Park Corridor The Paseo Park connector between Tree Top Park and the Arbors Tennis and Play Park offers a shorter, fully landscaped walking route through the center of the village. The path is flat, well-maintained, and shaded by mature trees. It is particularly useful for morning and evening walks when the light is soft and the paths are at their most comfortable. ## Barkin' Basin Dog Park For off-leash play, Barkin' Basin Dog Park in Summerlin is the closest dedicated dog park to The Arbors. The park features separate areas for large and small dogs, agility equipment, grassy fields, and shaded benches. The park closes on Mondays and Thursdays from 11:30 AM to 4:00 PM for maintenance. ## Kellogg Zaher Dog Park Kellogg Zaher Dog Park in northwest Las Vegas offers another off-leash option with separate areas based on dog weight (under and over 26 pounds), water stations, shade structures, and parking. The park is a short drive from The Arbors. ## Tree Top Park and Surrounding Areas Tree Top Park's open grass areas and walking paths accommodate leashed dogs during non-sport hours. The park's location on Sageberry Drive puts it within walking distance of many Arbors neighborhoods, making it a convenient spot for quick morning outings. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose notes that The Arbors' mature landscaping gives it a meaningful advantage for dog walking compared to newer communities where trees have not yet grown in. The shade along Cottonwood Canyon and the Paseo Park corridor makes year-round walking practical in a city where exposed pavement can reach dangerous temperatures during summer.
