Regency by Toll Brothers is a 55+ age qualified neighborhood within The Cliffs village in Summerlin, offering 425 single story homes on 100 dedicated acres with its own private recreation center, pools, and pickleball courts. ## What Is Regency by Toll Brothers? Regency occupies a significant portion of The Cliffs, with floor plans ranging from 1,665 to 2,223 square feet and prices from the high $400,000s to the mid $600,000s. Every home is single story, designed with open layouts that emphasize indoor outdoor living through covered patios and courtyard entries. The neighborhood is built exclusively for residents aged 55 and older, creating a community tailored to active adults who want low maintenance living without sacrificing quality. Approximately 100 homes remain available for purchase, making Regency one of the largest sources of new construction inventory in The Cliffs. Toll Brothers' reputation for customization means buyers can still work with the design studio to select finishes, flooring, and fixtures before their home is completed. ## What Amenities Does Regency Offer? Regency features its own private recreation and fitness center separate from the broader Cliffs amenities. The facility includes a resort style pool, a lap pool, a spa, and dedicated pickleball courts, all reserved exclusively for Regency residents and their guests. Ryan Rose frequently mentions that this self contained amenity package is uncommon for 55+ communities in the Las Vegas market, where most age restricted neighborhoods share facilities with the larger master plan. The neighborhood also benefits from its location within The Cliffs' guard gated perimeter, adding 24/7 security. Residents have access to Oak Leaf Park, the Summerlin trail system, and nearby shopping and dining along Far Hills Avenue and Town Center Drive. ## How Does Regency Compare to Other 55+ Communities? Las Vegas has no shortage of 55+ options, from Sun City Summerlin to Inspirada's age qualified sections. Regency differentiates itself through its newer construction (post 2015), desert contemporary architecture, and inclusion within a guard gated village rather than a standalone age restricted subdivision. The price point in the high $400,000s to mid $600,000s also positions Regency between entry level retirement communities and the luxury tier.
