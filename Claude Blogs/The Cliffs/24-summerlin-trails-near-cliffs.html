The Summerlin trail system is a network of over 200 miles of interconnected paths that wind through and around The Cliffs, Summerlin's guard gated village at the southern edge of the master plan. Residents of The Cliffs enjoy direct access to trails that connect neighborhoods, parks, shopping, and natural desert landscapes. ## How Many Types of Trails Does Summerlin Offer? Summerlin organizes its trail network into five categories: street side paths, village trails, bike trails, regional connectors, and natural pathways. Street side paths run alongside major roads and provide safe pedestrian and cycling routes to schools, shopping centers, and community parks. Village trails loop through individual communities like The Cliffs, connecting cul de sacs to common areas and parks such as Oak Leaf Park without requiring residents to use main roads. Bike trails offer dedicated lanes for road cyclists and commuters, while regional connectors link Summerlin's villages to the broader Clark County trail system, which spans over 2,000 miles upon completion. Natural pathways follow desert washes and ridgelines, providing unpaved routes for hikers and trail runners who want a more rugged experience close to home. ## What Do the Trails Near The Cliffs Look Like? The trails that pass through and around The Cliffs feature shaded rest stops with benches and water access, valley overlooks facing the Las Vegas skyline, and connections to Red Rock Canyon's trailheads to the west. Morning walkers and evening joggers frequently use the village paths that loop past Oak Leaf Park and along the ridgeline that gives The Cliffs its name.
