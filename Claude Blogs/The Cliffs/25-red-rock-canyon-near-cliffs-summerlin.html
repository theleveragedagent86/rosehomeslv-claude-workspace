Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is a 195,819 acre protected landscape located just 10 to 15 minutes west of The Cliffs in Summerlin. This proximity to one of Nevada's most visited natural attractions is a defining feature of life in The Cliffs and a consistent factor in why buyers choose this guard gated village. ## What Can You Do at Red Rock Canyon? Red Rock Canyon offers a 13 mile scenic loop drive that winds past towering sandstone formations, ancient limestone cliffs, and Joshua tree forests. The loop is open to cars, cyclists, and motorcyclists, making it one of the most accessible scenic drives in the American Southwest. More than 30 marked trailheads branch off from the loop, ranging from easy 1 mile walks to challenging 6 mile ridge hikes with 2,000 plus feet of elevation gain. Rock climbing draws visitors from around the world to Red Rock's sandstone walls, with routes graded for beginners through advanced climbers. Wildlife viewing adds another dimension, with sightings of desert bighorn sheep, wild burros, red tailed hawks, and desert tortoises throughout the conservation area. Guided ranger programs, visitor center exhibits, and seasonal stargazing events round out the experience. ## How Far Is Red Rock Canyon from The Cliffs? From the main entrance of The Cliffs on Hualapai Way, the Red Rock Canyon visitor center is roughly a 10 to 15 minute drive west along Charleston Boulevard. That short commute means Cliffs residents can start a sunrise hike and return home before most of the valley has finished breakfast.
