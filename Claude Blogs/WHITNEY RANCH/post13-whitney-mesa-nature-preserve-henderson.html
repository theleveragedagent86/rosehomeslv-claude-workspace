What Makes Whitney Mesa Nature Preserve Special?

Whitney Mesa Nature Preserve, located at 1990 Patrick Lane in Henderson, is one of the most unique outdoor spaces in the Las Vegas valley. This protected area features striking geologic formations, an active stream, and diverse flora and fauna that feel worlds away from the surrounding desert neighborhoods. For Whitney Ranch residents, it is practically in the backyard.

What Can You Do at Whitney Mesa Nature Preserve?

The preserve offers hiking trails that wind through its distinctive landscape, giving visitors a close look at geological features shaped over millions of years. Birding is popular here thanks to the active stream, which attracts species rarely seen in other parts of Henderson. Horseback riding is also permitted on designated trails. Interpretive centers and kiosks throughout the preserve provide educational context about the area's natural history, making it a worthwhile stop for families and students alike.

Is Whitney Mesa Nature Preserve Accessible?

Yes. The preserve includes an ADA accessible trail, ensuring that visitors of all mobility levels can enjoy the scenery and wildlife. Picnic tables and restrooms are available on site, so you can plan a longer visit without needing to leave. Whether you are looking for a quick morning walk or an afternoon of exploration, the preserve accommodates a range of visit lengths and activity levels.
