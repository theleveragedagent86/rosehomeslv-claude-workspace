Whitney Mesa ADA Accessible Trail: Inclusive Hiking in Henderson

Not every nature trail is designed for every visitor, but the Whitney Mesa ADA Accessible Trail breaks that pattern. Located within the Whitney Mesa Nature Preserve at 1990 Patrick Lane in Henderson, this one mile interpretive trail makes the desert landscape accessible to people of all mobility levels.

What Makes the Trail ADA Accessible?

The ADA trail features a smooth, graded surface suitable for wheelchairs, strollers, and mobility devices. Interpretive kiosks are placed along the route, offering information about the preserve's geology, plant life, and wildlife. The path is relatively flat, avoiding the uneven terrain found on other sections of the preserve's trail network.

What Will You Experience?

Despite its shorter length, the ADA trail delivers the same scenic value that defines Whitney Mesa. You will pass through areas with striking geologic formations, native desert vegetation, and views of the active stream corridor that runs through the preserve. Restrooms and picnic tables near the trailhead make it easy to extend your visit beyond the walk itself.

Who Is This Trail For?

The ADA trail serves a wide range of visitors: seniors who prefer a gentler walking surface, families with young children in strollers, wheelchair users, and anyone recovering from injury. It also works well as a quick nature break for residents who want fresh air without committing to the full 2.5 mile loop trail. Dogs on leash are welcome here as well.
