How a Brazilian Churrascaria Works

If you have never been to a churrascaria, the concept is simple and exciting. You receive a card at your table: green means "keep the meat coming," red means "I need a break." Gauchos, the traditional Brazilian meat carvers, circulate the dining room with skewers of freshly grilled meats and slice portions directly onto your plate. The variety is impressive, ranging from top sirloin and lamb to chicken, sausage, and pork.

More Than Just Meat

While the tableside meat service is the star, Rodizio Grill also features a gourmet salad bar loaded with fresh options. Think roasted vegetables, imported cheeses, cured meats, and Brazilian staples like hearts of palm and marinated mushrooms. There is enough variety at the salad bar alone to make a full meal, which means everyone at the table will find something they love.
