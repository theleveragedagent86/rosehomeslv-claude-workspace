# Medical Assistants and the $20,000 Worker Advantage Grant in Nevada Medical assistants play a vital role in clinics and outpatient facilities across Nevada, and the state's Worker Advantage Program recognizes that contribution. The program provides $20,000 in down payment assistance to help medical assistants purchase a home. ## Eligibility for Medical Assistants Medical assistant is one of 47 essential worker professions that qualify for the Worker Advantage Program. At least one borrower on the loan must hold a full time medical assistant position. You also need a minimum 640 credit score, six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below 150% of the area median income, which is $142,350 in Clark County. You do not need to be a first time homebuyer. Medical assistants who have owned a home before can still apply. The maximum purchase price is $832,750, and the property must be your primary residence. ## How the Grant Works The $20,000 arrives as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments required. Repayment happens only when you sell the home, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. You can put the money toward your down payment, closing costs, or a rate buydown that reduces your monthly mortgage payment. For medical assistants working at entry level pay scales, this structure is especially valuable. Having no extra monthly payment on the assistance means your housing budget stays manageable while you build equity. ## Where Medical Assistants Work in Las Vegas Medical assistants staff clinics, urgent care centers, and outpatient facilities throughout the Las Vegas valley. Major health systems like UMC, Valley Health System, and Dignity Health operate dozens of outpatient locations beyond their hospital campuses. Private practices in every part of the valley also employ medical assistants for patient intake, vitals, and clinical support.
