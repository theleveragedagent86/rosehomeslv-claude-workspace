# Physical Therapists Buying Homes in Nevada with Down Payment Help Physical therapists help patients recover mobility and strength across rehab centers and hospitals throughout Nevada. The state's Worker Advantage Program rewards that essential work with $20,000 in down payment assistance toward a home purchase. ## Physical Therapist Eligibility Physical therapist is one of 47 essential worker professions listed in the Worker Advantage Program. At least one borrower on the mortgage must work full time in a qualifying physical therapy role. The program requires a minimum 640 credit score, six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below 150% of the area median income, which reaches $142,350 in Clark County. You do not need to be a first time homebuyer. Physical therapists who have owned homes before can still qualify. The maximum purchase price is $832,750, and the home must be your primary residence. ## How the $20,000 Works The assistance comes as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. Repayment is deferred until you sell, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. You can direct the $20,000 toward your down payment, closing costs, or a rate buydown that lowers your monthly mortgage payment. Physical therapists often carry student loan debt from their doctoral programs. The zero payment structure of this grant means you can manage both your student loans and your mortgage without an extra monthly obligation from the assistance. ## Physical Therapy Careers in Las Vegas The Las Vegas valley offers physical therapists a range of employment settings. Hospital outpatient departments at UMC, Sunrise, Valley Health System, and Dignity Health's St. Rose Dominican campuses all maintain physical therapy teams. Standalone rehab centers and private practices operate throughout the metro area as well. With 23 hospitals in the valley and a growing population that fuels demand for rehabilitation services, physical therapists enjoy strong job stability. That employment consistency strengthens mortgage applications and pairs well with the Worker Advantage grant. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose works with healthcare professionals who want to put their stable careers to work toward homeownership.
