# Special Education Teachers and the $20,000 Down Payment Grant Special education teachers in Nevada can receive $20,000 toward buying a home through the Worker Advantage Program. With CCSD facing persistent demand for special education professionals, the state has recognized these educators as essential workers who deserve meaningful help with homeownership. ## Special Education in CCSD The Clark County School District operates 4 dedicated special education schools along with integrated programs across its 373 campuses. Special education teachers in CCSD work with students who have individualized education programs and collaborate with families on developmental goals. The district offers alternative licensure routes to attract more professionals into this high demand field. ## How the Grant Works The Worker Advantage Program provides $20,000 as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments required. You only repay the balance when you sell, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. The funds can be applied to your down payment, closing costs, or a rate buydown that lowers your monthly mortgage payment. ## Who Qualifies At least one borrower on the loan must work full time as a special education teacher in Nevada. You need a minimum 640 credit score, at least six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below $142,350 in Clark County. The maximum home purchase price is $832,750, and the property must be your primary residence. You do not need to be a first time buyer. ## Why This Matters for Special Ed Teachers Special education professionals often earn competitive salaries but face the same housing affordability challenges as other educators. The $20,000 grant can cover a full 3.5% FHA down payment on a home priced up to $571,000, making homeownership realistic even without years of saving.
