# School Counselors and Administrators Buying Homes with Worker Advantage School counselors and administrators in Nevada qualify for $20,000 in down payment assistance through the Worker Advantage Program. Principals, assistant principals, and counselors working across CCSD's 373 schools can use this grant to buy a home with zero interest and no monthly payments on the assistance. ## Eligible Roles in School Leadership The Worker Advantage Program covers multiple school leadership positions. School counselors who support student academic and social development qualify, along with principals and assistant principals who manage school operations. Instructional coordinators who oversee curriculum and teacher training are also included. All of these roles fall within the program's list of 47 essential worker professions. ## Grant Details The $20,000 is structured as a second mortgage at 0% interest with no monthly payments required. You repay the balance only when you sell the home, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. The funds can be applied to your down payment, closing costs, or a mortgage rate buydown that lowers your monthly payment for the life of the loan. ## Qualification Requirements At least one borrower must work full time in an eligible school counselor or administrator role in Nevada. You need a minimum 640 credit score and at least six months of Nevada residency. Household income must be at or below $142,350 in Clark County. The maximum purchase price reaches $832,750, and the property must be your primary residence. You do not need to be a first time homebuyer. ## Buying Power for School Leaders School administrators and counselors often earn salaries that place them comfortably within the program's income limits while also qualifying for competitive mortgage rates. The $20,000 grant adds meaningful flexibility. On a $495,000 home in Henderson, the grant covers a full 3.5% FHA down payment with funds remaining for closing costs. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose works with school leaders across CCSD who want to live near the campuses they serve.
