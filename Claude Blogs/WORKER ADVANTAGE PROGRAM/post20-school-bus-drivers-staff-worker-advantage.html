# School Bus Drivers and Support Staff Qualifying for Worker Advantage School bus drivers and support staff in Nevada can receive $20,000 in down payment assistance through the Worker Advantage Program. Bus drivers, custodians, school secretaries, and librarians all qualify as essential workers under this state housing grant, making homeownership accessible to the people who keep schools running every day. ## Which Support Roles Qualify The Worker Advantage Program recognizes several school support positions among its 47 eligible professions. School bus drivers who transport students across the valley qualify, along with custodians who maintain school facilities. School secretaries and office staff are also eligible, as are school librarians and media specialists. Each of these roles is essential to CCSD's operation of 373 schools. ## How the $20,000 Grant Works The grant is structured as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. You repay the balance only when you sell, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. You can apply the $20,000 toward your down payment, closing costs, or a rate buydown that reduces your monthly mortgage payment. ## Eligibility Requirements At least one borrower on the loan must work full time in a qualifying support staff role in Nevada. You need a minimum 640 credit score and at least six months of Nevada residency. Household income must fall at or below $142,350 in Clark County. The maximum purchase price is $832,750, and the home must be your primary residence. First time buyer status is not required. ## Affordability for Support Staff School support positions often come with modest salaries, which makes the $20,000 grant transformative. In North Las Vegas, where the median home price sits near $410,000, the grant can cover most of a conventional 5% down payment. The program's income cap of $142,350 also allows dual income households to qualify even if only one person holds the eligible position. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose has helped school support staff across the Las Vegas valley realize they qualify for programs they never knew existed.
