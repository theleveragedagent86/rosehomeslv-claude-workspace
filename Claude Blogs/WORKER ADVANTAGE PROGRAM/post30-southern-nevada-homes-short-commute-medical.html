# Southern Nevada Homes with Short Commutes to Medical Facilities Healthcare workers across Southern Nevada share a common priority: getting home quickly after long shifts. Whether you work days, nights, or rotating schedules, a short commute improves your safety, your sleep, and your overall quality of life. This guide maps out where hospitals sit across the valley and which neighborhoods keep you close to work while staying within budget using the [Nevada Worker Advantage Program](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained). ## Hospital Locations Across the Valley Southern Nevada has more than a dozen major hospitals spread across five distinct areas. Here is how they break down. **Central Las Vegas** hosts UMC, Valley Hospital, Sunrise Hospital, and Desert Springs Hospital. **Henderson** is served by Henderson Hospital, St. Rose Siena, St. Rose de Lima, and the new West Henderson Hospital. **North Las Vegas** has the VA Southern Nevada Healthcare System and Dignity Health. **Summerlin** features Summerlin Hospital and MountainView Hospital. **Southwest Las Vegas** includes Spring Valley Hospital, Southern Hills Hospital, and Dignity Health on Blue Diamond. This distribution means that no matter where you buy, you are likely within 15 to 20 minutes of at least two major hospitals. ## Matching Neighborhoods to Your Hospital The key to a short commute is choosing a neighborhood that aligns with your specific workplace. Here are some general guidelines. For [central Las Vegas hospitals](/las-vegas-homes-near-hospitals-medical), neighborhoods along Charleston, Sahara, and the Maryland Parkway corridor offer median prices near $482,000. For [Henderson hospitals](/henderson-homes-near-st-rose-hospital), Green Valley, Inspirada, and the Water Street area provide options around the $495,000 median. [North Las Vegas near the VA](/north-las-vegas-homes-near-va-medical) offers the best value at a $410,000 median, with communities like Aliante putting you minutes from work. [Summerlin](/summerlin-homes-near-summerlin-hospital) commands the highest prices at $686,000 median, though condos and older villages bring the entry point down significantly. The southwest corridor near Spring Valley and Southern Hills hospitals offers a middle ground at approximately $455,000 median, with newer construction and freeway access along the 215 Beltway. ## Worker Advantage Program: $20,000 for Healthcare Workers The [Worker Advantage Program](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained) provides $20,000 in down payment assistance to [qualifying essential workers](/jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant), including 13 healthcare positions. The grant is structured as a 0% interest, no payment second mortgage over 30 years. This benefit works across the entire Las Vegas valley. Whether you buy in [affordable North Las Vegas](/affordable-las-vegas-homes-400k-essential) or invest in Henderson or Summerlin, the $20,000 applies the same way. On an FHA loan, it can cover your entire down payment in most price ranges. ## Planning Your Home Search by Shift Schedule If you work rotating shifts, consider neighborhoods near freeway on ramps. The 215 Beltway, I 15, and US 95 connect most hospital clusters within 15 to 25 minutes during off peak hours. Night shift workers benefit especially because their commutes avoid rush hour traffic entirely. If you work at multiple facilities or are open to changing employers, buying in a central location along the 215 or near the I 15 and US 95 interchange gives you flexibility to reach hospitals across the valley. ## Ryan Rose: One Local Insight I have built my practice around helping [essential workers buy homes in Las Vegas](/essential-workers-homes-las-vegas-guide), and commute planning is always part of the conversation.
