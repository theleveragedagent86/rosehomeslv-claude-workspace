# North Las Vegas Homes Near Schools for CCSD Employees North Las Vegas offers the most affordable home prices in the Las Vegas valley, making it an ideal location for CCSD employees who want to maximize their $20,000 Worker Advantage grant. The city has added dozens of new schools in recent years to keep pace with rapid residential growth, and education workers can find homes within minutes of their campus at price points that stretch the grant further than anywhere else in Clark County. ## CCSD Schools in North Las Vegas North Las Vegas falls within CCSD's Northeast and Northwest geographic regions. The city is home to a growing number of elementary, middle, and high school campuses, including schools in the Aliante, Elkhorn, and Tule Springs master planned communities. Canyon Springs High School, Cheyenne High School, and Legacy High School serve the area's high school students. Newer schools continue to open as developments in the northern reaches of the city bring thousands of new families each year. ## North Las Vegas Home Prices The median home price in North Las Vegas is approximately $410,000, which is the lowest among the major communities in the valley. Single family homes in Aliante typically range from the high $300,000s to the low $500,000s. Newer construction in areas near Tule Springs and North 5th Street can start in the mid $300,000s. Townhomes and condos are available below $300,000 in some sections. At these prices, the $20,000 Worker Advantage grant can cover nearly all of the required down payment on a conventional loan. ## How the Worker Advantage Program Applies Education workers in ten qualifying positions receive $20,000 as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. Teachers, teacher aides, school counselors, bus drivers, and custodial staff all qualify. The grant covers your down payment, closing costs, or a mortgage rate buydown. Requirements include a 640 minimum credit score, six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below $142,350 in Clark County. You do not need to be a first time buyer. ## Stretching the Grant in North Las Vegas A $20,000 grant on a $410,000 home covers just under 5% of the purchase price, which satisfies the conventional loan minimum down payment. In communities where homes start in the mid $300,000s, the grant can exceed the 5% threshold and leave room to apply leftover funds toward closing costs or a rate buydown. No other area in the valley allows the Worker Advantage grant to go this far. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose works with CCSD employees in North Las Vegas who are often surprised by how much purchasing power the $20,000 grant provides at local price points.
