# Essential Workers Buying Homes in Las Vegas with Worker Advantage Las Vegas has long been a city built by essential workers. Nurses, teachers, firefighters, and construction professionals keep this valley running every single day. Now Nevada's Worker Advantage Program gives those same professionals a powerful tool to plant roots here with a **$20,000 grant** toward purchasing a home. ## What Is the Worker Advantage Program? The [Worker Advantage Program](/post1-nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained.html) provides $20,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance structured as a second mortgage at **0% interest with no monthly payments**. The balance is due only when you sell, refinance, or pay off the primary loan, giving you up to 30 years of zero cost on that assistance. This is not limited to first time buyers. If you already own a home somewhere else or purchased one years ago, you can still qualify. That flexibility sets Worker Advantage apart from many traditional assistance programs. ## Who Qualifies in Las Vegas? Eligibility covers [47 essential professions](/post10-jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant.html) across healthcare, education, public safety, and construction. You must meet a few financial benchmarks: - **Credit score:** 640 or higher - **Income limit:** $142,350 for Clark County households - **Nevada residency:** At least 6 months - **Max purchase price:** $832,750 Whether you work at UMC, teach in the Clark County School District, respond to calls with Las Vegas Fire and Rescue, or build homes across the valley, this program likely covers your profession. ## How Far $20,000 Goes in Las Vegas The Las Vegas median home price sits around $482,000 in 2026, but over **2,600 homes currently list under $400,000**. Pair the $20,000 grant with an [FHA loan at 3.5% down](/post97-worker-advantage-vs-fha-loans-essential.html), and here is what that looks like on a $380,000 home: - **FHA down payment (3.5%):** $13,300 - **Worker Advantage grant:** $20,000 - **Remaining for closing costs:** roughly $6,700 That means many Las Vegas essential workers can get into a home with **little to no out of pocket cost** beyond what the grant covers. ## Where Essential Workers Are Buying Popular areas for essential workers include neighborhoods near [major hospitals](/post26-las-vegas-homes-near-hospitals-medical.html), [top rated CCSD schools](/post31-las-vegas-homes-near-top-ccsd-schools.html), and [fire and police stations](/post36-las-vegas-homes-near-fire-police-stations.html). Communities in the southwest, the 89148 and 89113 zip codes, continue to offer strong value with easy freeway access for shift workers. **One local insight from Ryan Rose:** I help essential workers in Las Vegas navigate this grant every month. The biggest advantage I see is combining Worker Advantage with homes priced under $400,000 in emerging neighborhoods like Skye Canyon and Tule Springs, where new builds and resale inventory both offer solid options for families on essential worker salaries. ## Steps to Get Started 1.
