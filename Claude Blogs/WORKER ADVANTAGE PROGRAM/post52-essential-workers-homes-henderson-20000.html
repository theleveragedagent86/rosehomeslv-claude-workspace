# Essential Workers Buying Homes in Henderson with $20,000 Assistance Henderson consistently ranks among the safest and most livable cities in the entire country. For essential workers who want to call it home, Nevada's Worker Advantage Program offers a **$20,000 grant** that makes buying in Henderson far more achievable than many realize. ## Why Henderson Appeals to Essential Workers Henderson delivers a combination of top rated schools, well maintained parks, short commutes to major medical centers like St. Rose Dominican, and a family friendly atmosphere that keeps essential workers coming back to this market. The city also hosts growing healthcare and education employment hubs, meaning many essential workers can both live and work within Henderson's borders. ## How the $20,000 Grant Works The [Worker Advantage Program](/post1-nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained.html) provides $20,000 as a **0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments** for up to 30 years. You repay it only when you sell, refinance, or pay off your first mortgage. That structure means zero impact on your monthly budget. Key requirements include: - **640+ credit score** - **Household income under $142,350** (Clark County limit) - **6 months of Nevada residency** - Employment in one of [47 qualifying professions](/post10-jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant.html) Importantly, this program is [not limited to first time buyers](/post3-who-qualifies-worker-advantage-nevada.html). Repeat buyers who meet the criteria can access the full $20,000. ## Henderson Market Snapshot for Essential Workers Henderson's median home price currently sits near $495,000, slightly above Las Vegas overall. However, the real opportunity is in **new construction priced under $400,000**, where Henderson currently offers **232 new build options** with a median price around $369,000. Communities delivering strong value for essential workers include: - **Cadence** in east Henderson, with multiple builders offering homes in the mid $300,000s - **Beazer Homes at Jasper Point**, with floor plans designed for growing families - **Summerfield**, featuring newer inventory close to parks and retail For a deeper look at affordable options, see our guide to [Henderson homes under $400,000 for essential workers](/post42-affordable-henderson-homes-essential-2026.html). ## Pairing the Grant with FHA Financing Combine the $20,000 grant with an [FHA loan](/post97-worker-advantage-vs-fha-loans-essential.html) and the math gets exciting. On a $370,000 new construction home in Cadence: - **FHA down payment (3.5%):** $12,950 - **Worker Advantage grant covers:** the full down payment plus roughly $7,000 toward closing costs - **Your out of pocket cost:** potentially $0 With current mortgage rates between 5.5% and 6.5%, monthly payments on a $370,000 home remain manageable for households earning within the program's income limits. **One local insight from Ryan Rose:** Henderson's new construction under $400,000 is the sweet spot I point essential worker clients toward right now. Builders in Cadence especially are offering rate buydowns and closing cost incentives on top of the $20,000 grant, which means some buyers are walking into brand new homes with money back at closing. ## How to Apply 1.
