# First Time Homebuyer Tips for Essential Workers in Las Vegas Buying your first home is a big step. Buying your first home as an essential worker in Las Vegas comes with a unique advantage most buyers do not have: access to **$20,000 in free down payment assistance** through Nevada's Worker Advantage Program. This guide walks you through the process from start to finish so you can move confidently from renter to homeowner. ## Tip 1: Confirm You Qualify for Worker Advantage Before Anything Else Before you start browsing listings, verify that your profession falls within the [47 eligible occupations](/post10-jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant.html). Healthcare workers, teachers, first responders, and construction professionals all qualify. Then check the financial requirements: - **Credit score:** 640 or higher - **Household income:** under $142,350 in Clark County - **Nevada residency:** minimum 6 months One detail that surprises many: Worker Advantage is [not restricted to first time buyers](/post3-who-qualifies-worker-advantage-nevada.html). But if you are a first time buyer, you may be able to stack additional programs for even more savings. ## Tip 2: Get Pre Approved with a Certified Lender The Worker Advantage grant is only available through [Home Is Possible certified lenders](/post9-worker-advantage-home-is-possible-lenders.html). Working with a non certified lender means you cannot access the $20,000. Visit homeispossiblenv.org to find approved lenders in the Las Vegas area. During pre approval, your lender will review your income, debts, credit history, and employment verification. This is also when you will learn your exact budget and monthly payment range. ## Tip 3: Understand What $20,000 Actually Covers The [$20,000 grant](/post2-apply-nevada-20000-down-payment-assistance.html) is structured as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments for up to 30 years. It can cover your **down payment, closing costs, or both**. On a $380,000 home with FHA financing: - **Down payment (3.5%):** $13,300 - **Closing costs (estimated):** $7,500 - **Total:** $20,800 - **Grant covers:** $20,000 - **Your cost:** $800 That $800 gap is often negotiable through seller concessions. Learn more about [how the grant pairs with FHA loans](/post97-worker-advantage-vs-fha-loans-essential.html). ## Tip 4: Complete Homebuyer Education Early Nevada requires homebuyer education before closing on a Worker Advantage purchase. Do not wait until the last minute. Several online and in person courses are approved, and most take only a few hours. Completing this early removes a potential delay from your closing timeline. ## Tip 5: Target the Right Price Range Las Vegas currently has over **2,600 homes listed under $400,000** and **76 new construction options** in that range. Essential workers getting the most value are shopping between $340,000 and $400,000, where the grant covers nearly all upfront costs. Focus areas include: - **Southwest Las Vegas** near major hospitals and [top CCSD schools](/post31-las-vegas-homes-near-top-ccsd-schools.html) - **North Las Vegas** for [maximum value under $400,000](/post41-affordable-las-vegas-homes-400k-essential.html) - Neighborhoods with short commutes to [fire and police stations](/post36-las-vegas-homes-near-fire-police-stations.html) ## Tip 6: Negotiate in a Buyer Favorable Market With homes averaging **72 days on market**, Las Vegas has shifted toward buyers. That means you can: - Ask sellers to cover closing costs or buy down your interest rate - Request repairs after inspection without fear of losing the deal - Take time to compare multiple properties before committing Essential workers with pre approval and Worker Advantage backing present strong, clean offers that sellers take seriously. ## Tip 7: Do Not Skip the Inspection Even on new construction, a home inspection is worth every dollar. Foundation issues, HVAC problems, and plumbing defects are easier and cheaper to address before you close than after. Budget $400 to $600 for a thorough inspection. **One local insight from Ryan Rose:** The biggest mistake I see first time essential worker buyers make is not starting the process early enough. Between lender certification, homebuyer education, and grant paperwork, the timeline runs about 45 to 60 days from first call to closing. Start the conversation now, even if you are not ready to buy for a few months. Having your pre approval and education complete means you can move fast when the right home appears. ## Your First Home Starts Here Ready to take the first step? **[Contact our team](https://rosehomeslv.com/contact)** for a no pressure conversation about your timeline, budget, and the neighborhoods that fit your commute.
