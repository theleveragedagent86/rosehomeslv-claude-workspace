# Essential Workers Buying Homes in North Las Vegas with Worker Advantage North Las Vegas is the most affordable city in the Las Vegas Valley for essential workers, and the $20,000 Worker Advantage grant makes it even more accessible. With a median home price of $410,000, over 1,400 active listings, and new construction from four national builders, this city gives nurses, teachers, firefighters, and other qualifying professionals a realistic shot at homeownership without draining their savings. ## Why North Las Vegas Works for Essential Workers The numbers tell the story. North Las Vegas carries a $394,950 median list price with 1,412 properties on the market. Homes average 75 days on market, giving buyers time to compare options and negotiate. For essential workers earning within the $142,350 income cap, these price points mean the [Worker Advantage grant](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained) covers the entire FHA down payment on most homes here. On a $395,000 purchase with FHA financing at 3.5% down, the required down payment is $13,825. The $20,000 grant covers that in full and leaves $6,175 for closing costs. That math simply does not work as cleanly in Henderson or Summerlin.

## Where Essential Workers Are Buying Four communities stand out for Worker Advantage buyers in North Las Vegas: **Tule Springs** offers newer construction with parks, trails, and open space. Entry level floor plans start in the upper $300s, and the neighborhood continues expanding with additional phases from multiple builders. **Valley Vista** provides single family homes with direct access to the 215 Beltway, keeping commutes to hospitals, fire stations, and schools across the valley manageable. **Craig Ranch** is an established community with a regional park, and resale homes frequently land between $350,000 and $400,000. **New Windsor Park** adds 93 new homes targeting the first time buyer segment at accessible price points. ## Builders Serving Essential Worker Budgets National builders have planted roots in North Las Vegas because the land costs support competitive pricing. D.R. Horton, Lennar, Century Communities, and KB Home all operate active communities here. These builders understand first time buyer financing and regularly work with the [Home Is Possible lender network](/worker-advantage-home-is-possible-lenders) that administers Worker Advantage. ## Key Employers and Short Commutes Essential workers in North Las Vegas benefit from proximity to major employers. The VA Medical Center at 6900 N Pecos Road serves veterans and employs hundreds of healthcare professionals. Dignity Health at 1550 W Craig Road adds more medical positions. NLV Fire Department and NLV Police Department are headquartered locally, and CCSD operates dozens of schools throughout the city. Living near your workplace reduces commute stress and childcare costs, two factors that affect long term financial stability as much as the mortgage itself. ## How to Get Started The [Worker Advantage application process](/apply-nevada-20000-down-payment-assistance) requires a 640 credit score, household income under $142,350, and employment in one of the 47 qualifying professions. Start by connecting with an approved lender who can verify your eligibility and issue a pre-approval letter. ## Ryan's Local Insight I have watched North Las Vegas transform over the past five years, and what strikes me most is how the infrastructure has caught up with the housing growth. Along the 215 northern beltway, you now have medical offices, grocery stores, restaurants, and parks that did not exist a few years ago. For essential workers who want a new home with a real yard and a short drive to work, this is where the Worker Advantage grant stretches the furthest. The $20,000 covers your down payment, and the builders here are building homes designed for your budget.
