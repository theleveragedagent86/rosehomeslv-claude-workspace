# Essential Workers Buying Homes in Centennial Hills with Worker Advantage Centennial Hills sits in the northwest corner of Las Vegas and offers essential workers a suburban feel with strong schools, a major hospital, and home prices that work with the $20,000 Worker Advantage grant. With a median price of $450,000 and a range stretching from $308,000 to $574,000, this neighborhood gives qualifying buyers options at nearly every price point. ## Why Centennial Hills Attracts Essential Workers Three factors make Centennial Hills a natural fit for essential workers. First, Centennial Hills Hospital at 6900 N Durango Drive employs hundreds of nurses, technicians, and support staff who can walk or drive minutes to work. Second, the LVMPD Northwest Area Command provides law enforcement coverage and employs officers who live in the area. Third, CCSD operates well regarded schools throughout the community, attracting teachers, counselors, and support staff. When your workplace is five minutes from your front door, you save time, gas, and stress. That proximity is a financial advantage that compounds over years of homeownership.

## Home Prices and What the Grant Covers At the $450,000 median, an FHA buyer needs 3.5% down, which equals $15,750. The [Worker Advantage grant](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained) of $20,000 covers that in full and leaves $4,250 for closing costs. For essential workers who find homes at the lower end of the range near $350,000, the math is even stronger. A $350,000 purchase requires $12,250 down, leaving $7,750 from the grant for closing costs and rate buydowns. The [application process](/apply-nevada-20000-down-payment-assistance) requires a 640 credit score and household income under $142,350. With 47 qualifying professions ranging from nurses and firefighters to construction workers and teachers, most essential workers in Centennial Hills will meet the criteria. ## New Construction on the Horizon Skye Summit is breaking ground in 2026 near Centennial Hills, adding new inventory to the northwest corridor. New construction gives Worker Advantage buyers the opportunity to purchase a home with modern energy efficiency, builder warranties, and customizable features. Builders in the area have historically priced entry level floor plans to capture first time buyers, and the $20,000 grant makes those homes attainable with minimal cash at closing. You get the suburban environment, the hospital proximity, and the school quality without paying peak valley prices.
