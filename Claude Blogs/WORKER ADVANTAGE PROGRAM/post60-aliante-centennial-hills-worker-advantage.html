Both offer suburban living, proximity to hospitals, and median prices that align with the grant's purchasing power. ## Aliante: Master Planned and Move In Ready Aliante was built as a self contained community with amenities baked into the design. Walking trails, community parks, pools, and the Aliante Casino and Hotel create a neighborhood where residents rarely need to leave for daily errands and entertainment. For essential workers, the appeal is consistency. Homes in Aliante cluster around the $460,000 median, so you know what to expect when shopping. The 36 day average sale time means the market moves quickly, but it also signals strong property values and buyer confidence. A nurse or firefighter buying at $460,000 with FHA financing needs $16,100 down. The [Worker Advantage grant](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained) covers that and leaves $3,900 for closing costs. Learn more about [buying in Aliante with the $20,000 grant](/essential-workers-homes-aliante-20000). ## Centennial Hills: Range and Hospital Proximity Centennial Hills offers something Aliante does not: a wide price range. Homes here start near $308,000 and extend to $574,000, giving essential workers flexibility to buy at their comfort level. A teacher eyeing the lower end can find a starter home where the grant covers down payment and most closing costs. A dual income household can target the mid range and still benefit from the full $20,000. The defining feature for healthcare workers is Centennial Hills Hospital at 6900 N Durango Drive. Nurses, respiratory therapists, medical assistants, and lab technicians working at this facility can live within minutes of their shifts. LVMPD Northwest Area Command also operates nearby, making this area practical for law enforcement professionals. Read the full [Centennial Hills guide for essential workers](/essential-workers-homes-centennial-hills). ## Which Neighborhood Fits Your Situation **Choose Aliante if** you want a master planned community with resort style amenities, you prefer a consistent price range with fewer surprises, and your commute runs east toward the VA Medical Center or north along US-95. **Choose Centennial Hills if** you work at Centennial Hills Hospital or LVMPD Northwest, you want a wider selection of price points, or you are looking for new construction as Skye Summit breaks ground in 2026. Both neighborhoods work well with the Worker Advantage grant. The [application process](/apply-nevada-20000-down-payment-assistance) is the same regardless of where you buy: 640 credit score, income under $142,350, and employment in one of the [47 qualifying professions](/jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant). ## Ryan's Local Insight I get asked to compare Aliante and Centennial Hills more than almost any other neighborhoods in the valley. My honest take is that Aliante feels more polished and cohesive because of the master plan, while Centennial Hills offers more variety and the hospital proximity that healthcare workers specifically value. When I work with nurses from Centennial Hills Hospital, I almost always start the search in Centennial Hills because that five minute commute changes their entire work life balance. For everyone else, I suggest touring both and letting the homes speak for themselves.
