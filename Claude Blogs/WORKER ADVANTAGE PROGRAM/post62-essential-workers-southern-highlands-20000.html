# Essential Workers Buying Homes in Southern Highlands with $20,000 Help Southern Highlands is known as one of the most prestigious communities in the Las Vegas valley, but essential workers earning qualifying incomes can still purchase here. Nevada's Worker Advantage Program provides a **$20,000 grant** that makes the upfront costs of buying in this luxury community far more manageable. ## Southern Highlands at a Glance This affluent master planned community in far southwest Las Vegas features approximately 160 active listings with a median list price of **$705,500**. The neighborhood attracts buyers seeking resort style amenities, championship golf, and a polished suburban environment. While the median price runs higher than many Las Vegas communities, Southern Highlands homes still fall well under the Worker Advantage **$832,750 maximum purchase price**. That means qualified essential workers can use the full $20,000 grant on properties throughout the neighborhood. ## How the $20,000 Grant Works Here The [Worker Advantage Program](/post1-nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained.html) delivers $20,000 as a second mortgage at **0% interest with no monthly payments**. The balance comes due only when you sell, refinance, or pay off your primary loan. On a Southern Highlands home at $700,000 with a conventional loan: - **Conventional down payment (5%):** $35,000 - **Worker Advantage grant:** $20,000 - **Your portion of down payment:** approximately $15,000 That $20,000 reduces your cash needed at closing by more than half compared to going it alone. For essential workers who have been saving for a home in a premium community, this grant can be the difference between waiting another year and moving in now. ## Who Qualifies for Worker Advantage? The program covers [47 essential professions](/post10-jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant.html) including nurses, teachers, firefighters, police officers, EMTs, electricians, and construction trades. Requirements include: - **Household income:** $142,350 or less - **Credit score:** 640 minimum - **Nevada residency:** at least 6 months - **No first time buyer requirement** Southern Highlands sits near St. Rose Dominican Hospital on San Martin (8280 West Warm Springs Road) and Southern Hills Hospital, making the community especially convenient for healthcare professionals. The community also offers easy access for first responders working at [nearby fire and police stations](/post36-las-vegas-homes-near-fire-police-stations.html). ## Finding Affordable Options in Southern Highlands While the median list price exceeds $700,000, there are entry points. Townhomes, condos, and smaller single family homes occasionally list in the **mid $400,000s to low $500,000s**. Keeping an eye on new listings and working with an agent who knows the community helps essential workers identify those windows of opportunity. If Southern Highlands pricing stretches your budget, the neighboring [Mountains Edge community](/post61-essential-workers-homes-mountains-edge.html) offers a more accessible median around $485,000 with many of the same southwest Las Vegas advantages. **One local insight from Ryan Rose:** I tell my essential worker clients that Southern Highlands is not off limits just because it carries a luxury reputation. The Worker Advantage grant covers a meaningful share of the upfront cost, and dual income essential worker households earning under $142,350 combined can often qualify for homes in the $600,000 to $750,000 range here. The key is knowing the inventory and acting quickly when attainable listings appear. ## Getting Started Review the [complete eligibility requirements](/post3-who-qualifies-worker-advantage-nevada.html), then connect with a [participating lender](/post9-worker-advantage-home-is-possible-lenders.html) to get preapproved. Having your Worker Advantage approval in place before you shop lets you move confidently when the right Southern Highlands listing hits the market. --- *Sources: Nevada Housing Division Worker Advantage Program guidelines; Southern Highlands MLS data; Clark County Assessor records, March 2026.*
