# Mountains Edge Home Prices for Essential Workers in 2026 Mountains Edge remains one of the most sought after master planned communities in southwest Las Vegas, and 2026 pricing puts many homes within reach for essential workers using Nevada's Worker Advantage Program. Here is what you need to know about current prices and how $20,000 in grant assistance changes the math. ## Current Mountains Edge Pricing As of early 2026, Mountains Edge home prices break down like this: - **Median sold price:** approximately $485,000 - **Median list price:** around $499,900 - **Entry level homes:** starting near $375,000 - **Upper range:** luxury properties reaching $2.2 million The bulk of available inventory falls between **$400,000 and $600,000**, which is the sweet spot for essential worker households earning under the $142,350 income limit set by the [Worker Advantage Program](/post1-nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained.html). ## What $20,000 Does at Each Price Point The Worker Advantage grant provides $20,000 at **0% interest with no monthly payments**, applied toward down payment and closing costs. Here is how it plays out at common Mountains Edge price points: **At $400,000 (FHA, 3.5% down):** - Down payment: $14,000 - Grant covers: entire down payment plus roughly $6,000 toward closing costs - Estimated out of pocket: potentially **$0 to $2,000** **At $485,000 (FHA, 3.5% down):** - Down payment: $16,975 - Grant covers: full down payment plus about $3,000 toward closing - Estimated out of pocket: approximately **$3,000 to $5,000** **At $550,000 (conventional, 5% down):** - Down payment: $27,500 - Grant covers: $20,000 of down payment - Estimated out of pocket: approximately **$7,500 plus closing costs** These numbers show that essential workers targeting the lower to mid range of Mountains Edge inventory can get in with remarkably little cash at closing. ## Price Trends Essential Workers Should Watch Mountains Edge has appreciated steadily as one of the valley's most established master planned communities. The gap between median list price ($499,900) and median sold price ($485,000) tells you sellers are getting close to asking price, but there is still room for negotiation. Homes priced competitively in the mid $400,000s tend to move quickly. For essential workers comparing Mountains Edge to [other affordable options across Las Vegas](/post41-affordable-las-vegas-homes-400k-essential.html), the community offers a strong middle ground: newer construction than many central Las Vegas neighborhoods but more attainable pricing than premium communities to the south. ## Who Qualifies for the Grant? The program covers [47 essential professions](/post10-jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant.html) and requires: - **Credit score:** 640 or higher - **Household income:** $142,350 or less - **Purchase price cap:** $832,750 - **Nevada residency:** minimum 6 months You do not need to be a first time buyer. Workers at nearby Dignity Health Blue Diamond, Southern Hills Hospital, LVMPD Enterprise, and CCSD schools throughout the southwest are all strong candidates. **One local insight from Ryan Rose:** When I run searches for essential worker clients targeting Mountains Edge, I typically find 15 to 25 active listings under $500,000 at any given time. That is a healthy amount of inventory to work with, and it means you have options rather than feeling pressured to jump on the first listing you see. Patience in this community usually pays off with a better floor plan or lot position. ## Next Steps Get preapproved with a [participating lender](/post9-worker-advantage-home-is-possible-lenders.html) so you know exactly what price range works for your household.
