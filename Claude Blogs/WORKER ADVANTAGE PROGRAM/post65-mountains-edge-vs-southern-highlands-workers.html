# Mountains Edge vs Southern Highlands for Essential Worker Homebuyers Both Mountains Edge and Southern Highlands sit in southwest Las Vegas, share access to the same hospitals and emergency facilities, and fall within Nevada's Worker Advantage Program limits. But these two communities serve different budgets and lifestyles. Here is a factual comparison to help essential workers decide which neighborhood fits best. ## Price Comparison | Factor | Mountains Edge | Southern Highlands | |--------|---------------|-------------------| | Median price | $485,000 | $705,500 (list) | | Entry level | ~$375,000 | ~$450,000 | | Upper range | $2.2 million | $1.5 million+ | | Inventory under $500K | Strong | Very limited | Mountains Edge offers a significantly lower entry point and more inventory in the $400,000 to $500,000 range where the [Worker Advantage grant](/post1-nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained.html) has the most impact. Southern Highlands provides a premium lifestyle but requires a larger personal financial commitment. ## How $20,000 Plays in Each Community The Worker Advantage Program delivers **$20,000 at 0% interest with no payments**. The grant's impact varies based on purchase price: **Mountains Edge at $485,000 (FHA, 3.5% down):** - Down payment: $16,975 - Grant covers: entire down payment plus ~$3,000 toward closing - Estimated out of pocket: **$3,000 to $5,000** **Southern Highlands at $700,000 (conventional, 5% down):** - Down payment: $35,000 - Grant covers: $20,000 of down payment - Estimated out of pocket: **$15,000 plus closing costs** The $20,000 carries you much further in Mountains Edge. For workers prioritizing the lowest possible out of pocket cost, the math strongly favors the more affordable community. For detailed breakdowns, see the [Mountains Edge pricing guide](/post63-mountains-edge-prices-essential-workers-2026.html) and the [Southern Highlands pricing guide](/post64-southern-highlands-prices-essential-2026.html). ## Commute and Workplace Access Both communities serve essential workers at the same key facilities: - **Dignity Health Blue Diamond:** 4855 Blue Diamond Road - **Southern Hills Hospital:** 9300 West Sunset Road - **St. Rose Dominican (San Martin):** 8280 West Warm Springs Road - **LVMPD Enterprise Area Command:** 6975 West Windmill Lane - **CCSD southwest valley schools** Drive times to these workplaces differ by only a few minutes between the two communities. Neither holds a meaningful commute advantage over the other for most essential worker shifts. ## Community Character **Mountains Edge** is an active, family oriented master planned community with parks, trails, and neighborhood amenities. It draws a broad range of buyers and offers a suburban feel with accessible pricing. CCSD schools serve the area with options rated among the better performers in the southwest. **Southern Highlands** carries a luxury identity with championship golf, resort style amenities, and a higher income demographic (median household income of $126,134). The community feels more exclusive, and homes tend toward larger floor plans and higher end finishes. ## Who Should Choose Which? This is a personal financial and lifestyle decision. Here are the factual considerations: - **Budget under $500,000:** Mountains Edge offers far more options at this price point - **Dual income households near the $142,350 limit:** Southern Highlands becomes realistic in the $600,000 to $750,000 range - **Lowest out of pocket priority:** Mountains Edge pairs best with the $20,000 grant - **Premium amenities priority:** Southern Highlands delivers a higher end environment Both communities qualify under the [Worker Advantage Program](/post3-who-qualifies-worker-advantage-nevada.html) with the same [47 eligible professions](/post10-jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant.html), the same income limit, and the same $832,750 purchase price cap. **One local insight from Ryan Rose:** I work with essential workers in both communities regularly, and the deciding factor almost always comes down to budget comfort. If using the $20,000 grant means you can buy in Mountains Edge with virtually nothing out of pocket, that is a powerful position to be in. If your household income supports a higher price point and you value the Southern Highlands lifestyle, the grant still saves you $20,000 you would have spent anyway. Neither choice is wrong. The right answer depends on your numbers. ## Ready to Explore Both?
