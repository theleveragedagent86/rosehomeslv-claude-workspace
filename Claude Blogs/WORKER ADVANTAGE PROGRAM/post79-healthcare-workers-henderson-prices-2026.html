# Healthcare Workers and Henderson Home Prices in 2026 Henderson's real estate market continues to attract healthcare workers who want safe neighborhoods, strong schools, and a short commute to some of the valley's busiest hospitals. With a median home price of $495,000 in 2026, Henderson is not the cheapest option, but the [Nevada Worker Advantage Program](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained) and its [$20,000 grant](/apply-nevada-20000-down-payment-assistance) help bridge the affordability gap for [eligible healthcare workers](/jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant). ## Henderson's 2026 Market at a Glance The median home price in Henderson is approximately $495,000, reflecting steady appreciation driven by limited land, high demand, and the city's reputation as one of the safest places to live in Nevada. Prices range widely by neighborhood. Older areas near the Water Street corridor start in the mid $300,000s, while newer communities like Inspirada, Cadence, and west Henderson can reach $600,000 and above. A key number for healthcare workers: there are currently 232 new construction homes in Henderson priced under $400,000. These are primarily townhomes, condos, and smaller single family homes in developing areas, and they represent a realistic entry point for buyers using the Worker Advantage grant. ## Which Healthcare Workers Qualify The Worker Advantage Program covers [13 healthcare positions](/jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant): RN, LPN, NP, Physician, Medical Assistant, Surgical Tech, Radiologic Tech, Lab Tech, Pharmacist, Home Health Aide, Physical Therapist, Occupational Therapist, and Medical Receptionist. If you work at Henderson Hospital, St. Rose Siena, St. Rose de Lima, or West Henderson Hospital, you likely qualify. The grant provides $20,000 as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments over 30 years. The income limit is $142,350, and the maximum purchase price is $832,750. A 640 credit score is required. ## Price Breakdown by Hospital Area **Near Henderson Hospital (Green Valley):** The Green Valley corridor offers established homes from the mid $400,000s to $600,000s. Condos and townhomes start lower, often in the $300,000s. **Near St. Rose Siena (Southern Henderson):** Inspirada and Cadence feature newer construction with prices typically ranging from $450,000 to $650,000. Some attached homes come in under $400,000. **Near St. Rose de Lima (Historic Henderson):** The Water Street area and surrounding neighborhoods offer the most affordable options in the city, with homes starting in the mid $300,000s. **Near West Henderson Hospital:** This is Henderson's fastest developing area. New construction ranges from $400,000 to $700,000 depending on the builder and lot size. ## Affordability Math for Healthcare Workers Consider a Medical Assistant earning $42,000 annually looking at a $375,000 townhome near St. Rose de Lima. With the $20,000 Worker Advantage grant covering the FHA down payment of $13,125 plus $6,875 toward closing costs, the upfront barrier essentially disappears. Monthly payments on the remaining mortgage at current rates would fall in the range of $2,400 to $2,600, which is competitive with Henderson rental prices. For an RN earning $85,000, a $495,000 home at Henderson's median becomes accessible with the grant covering most of the $17,325 FHA down payment.
