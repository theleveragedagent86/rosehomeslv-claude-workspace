# Healthcare Workers and North Las Vegas Home Prices in 2026 North Las Vegas is the most affordable city in the Las Vegas metro, and for healthcare workers, that affordability creates genuine opportunities to build equity while staying close to work. With a median home price of $410,000 and the [Nevada Worker Advantage Program](/nevada-worker-advantage-program-explained) offering [$20,000 in down payment assistance](/apply-nevada-20000-down-payment-assistance), homeownership is within reach for [healthcare workers at every income level](/jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant). ## North Las Vegas Prices in 2026 The median home price in North Las Vegas is approximately $410,000, roughly $85,000 below Henderson and $276,000 below Summerlin. That gap makes North Las Vegas the most accessible market for healthcare workers earlier in their careers or in lower paying positions. Prices vary across the city. Older neighborhoods south of Craig Road offer homes from $300,000 to $380,000. Aliante and areas near Tule Springs range from $400,000 to $500,000. Newer developments provide modern construction starting in the low $400,000s. ## Eligible Healthcare Positions The Worker Advantage Program covers [13 healthcare roles](/jobs-qualifying-worker-advantage-grant): RN, LPN, NP, Physician, Medical Assistant, Surgical Tech, Radiologic Tech, Lab Tech, Pharmacist, Home Health Aide, Physical Therapist, Occupational Therapist, and Medical Receptionist. Workers employed at the [VA Southern Nevada Healthcare System](/north-las-vegas-homes-near-va-medical) or Dignity Health North Las Vegas Hospital are well positioned to buy nearby. The grant is structured as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments over 30 years. The income cap is $142,350, the minimum credit score is 640, and the maximum purchase price is $832,750. ## What the $20,000 Grant Means at North Las Vegas Prices North Las Vegas is where the Worker Advantage grant stretches the furthest. On a $375,000 home with an FHA loan, the 3.5% down payment is $13,125. The $20,000 grant covers that in full and leaves $6,875 for closing costs. Many healthcare workers can purchase a home here with minimal out of pocket expense. Even at the median price of $410,000, the FHA down payment of $14,350 is well within the grant amount. That leaves $5,650 toward closing costs, making the total cash needed at closing potentially zero beyond inspection and appraisal fees. Compare this to [Henderson's median of $495,000](/healthcare-workers-henderson-prices-2026), where the grant covers most but not all of the down payment. North Las Vegas gives healthcare workers the most purchasing power per dollar of assistance. ## Hospital Proximity and Neighborhoods **Near VA Medical Center (Pecos Rd):** Neighborhoods east of I-15 along Pecos and Craig roads offer homes in the $350,000 to $430,000 range. Commute times to the VA campus are often under 10 minutes. **Near Dignity Health NLV (Craig Rd):** The areas surrounding Craig Road between Losee and MLK provide affordable single family homes. The Eldorado and Sunrise Manor adjacent neighborhoods offer some of the lowest prices in the metro. **Aliante:** This master planned community approximately 15 minutes from both hospitals features a resort, golf course, and nature park. Prices range from $400,000 to $550,000. ## Ryan Rose: One Local Insight North Las Vegas has quietly become one of the best values in the valley.
