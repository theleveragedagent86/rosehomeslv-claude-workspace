# Teachers Buying Homes in Henderson with Worker Advantage Henderson offers CCSD teachers a strong combination of top rated schools, safe neighborhoods, and a $20,000 down payment grant through Nevada's Worker Advantage Program. With a median home price of $495,000, Henderson sits within reach for educators who pair their salary with this assistance. ## Why Henderson Appeals to Teachers Henderson is home to some of the highest rated schools in the Clark County School District. Veterans Tribute Career and Technical Academy ranks 11th in Nevada, and Coronado High School holds the 13th spot. Teachers who work at these campuses or nearby elementary and middle schools can live minutes from their workplace. Short commutes mean more time at home and less spent on gas, a real benefit on a teacher's budget. ## How the $20,000 Grant Works The Worker Advantage Program provides $20,000 as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. You repay the balance only when you sell, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. The funds apply toward your down payment, closing costs, or a rate buydown to reduce your monthly mortgage payment. ## Eligibility for Teachers You need a minimum 640 credit score, at least six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below $142,350 in Clark County. The maximum purchase price is $832,750, and the home must be your primary residence. You do not need to be a first time buyer. ## Worker Advantage Covers More Than Classroom Teachers Nevada's Home Is Possible for Teachers program offers $7,500, but only to K through 12 classroom teachers. Worker Advantage nearly triples that amount and extends eligibility to 10 education positions. Teacher aides, school counselors, bus drivers, custodians, librarians, and secretaries all qualify. If you work in a CCSD school building, there is a strong chance this program covers your role. ## Henderson Homes Under $400,000 While Henderson's median price is $495,000, the market currently features over 230 new construction homes priced below $400,000. These properties give teachers a realistic entry point, and the $20,000 grant covers a significant share of the upfront costs on a home in that range. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose has helped multiple CCSD educators purchase in Henderson's Green Valley and Inspirada communities, where school proximity and neighborhood quality align perfectly for teaching professionals.
