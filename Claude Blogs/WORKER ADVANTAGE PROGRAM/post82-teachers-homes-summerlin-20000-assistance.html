# Teachers Buying Homes in Summerlin with $20,000 Assistance Summerlin is one of the most sought after communities in the Las Vegas Valley, and CCSD teachers who work at its highly rated schools can use the $20,000 Worker Advantage grant to purchase a home here. With a median price of $686,000, the grant plays a meaningful role in covering upfront costs for educators entering this market. ## Summerlin Schools Drive Teacher Demand Summerlin is home to Northwest Career and Technical Academy, which ranks 9th in the state of Nevada. The area also features strong elementary and middle school options throughout CCSD's Northwest region. Teachers assigned to these campuses benefit from living close to work in a community known for parks, trails, and family friendly amenities. ## How the $20,000 Works for Summerlin Buyers Nevada's Worker Advantage Program provides $20,000 as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. You repay only when you sell, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. In Summerlin, teachers commonly apply the grant toward closing costs or a mortgage rate buydown, which can reduce monthly payments by $150 to $200 depending on the loan amount. ## Eligibility Requirements You need a minimum 640 credit score, at least six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below $142,350 in Clark County. The maximum purchase price is $832,750, which covers nearly every listing in Summerlin. The home must be your primary residence, and you do not need to be a first time buyer. ## Why Worker Advantage Beats Home Is Possible for Teachers The Home Is Possible for Teachers program offers $7,500 and restricts eligibility to K through 12 classroom teachers. Worker Advantage nearly triples that grant and opens the door to 10 education positions including teacher aides, counselors, librarians, bus drivers, and custodians. If you work in any role at a Summerlin school, Worker Advantage likely covers you. ## Summerlin Price Reality for Teachers Summerlin's median of $686,000 sits higher than many other valley communities. Inventory under $400,000 remains limited, though condos, townhomes, and older resale properties in the Summerlin South area occasionally fall in the mid $400K range. Teachers who want Summerlin's quality of life without the highest price points should explore properties near the edges of the master plan or consider adjacent areas like Southwest Las Vegas. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose advises Summerlin buyers to watch for newer listings in the Stonebridge and The Paseos villages, where resale homes occasionally price below $550K and offer quick access to multiple CCSD campuses. The $20,000 grant paired with a rate buydown strategy can make these homes surprisingly manageable on a teacher's income.
