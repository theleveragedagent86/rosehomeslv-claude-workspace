# Police Officers Buying Homes in Henderson with Worker Advantage Henderson consistently ranks among the safest cities in America, and the officers who keep it that way deserve a real path to homeownership here. The Nevada Worker Advantage Program provides $20,000 in down payment assistance to police officers buying a home in Henderson or anywhere in Clark County. ## How Henderson Officers Qualify Police officers, sheriffs, deputy sheriffs, correctional officers, and dispatchers all appear on the Worker Advantage list of 47 eligible professions. At least one borrower on the loan must hold a full time position in a qualifying public safety role. Requirements include a minimum 640 credit score, six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below $142,350. The program is not limited to first time buyers, so officers who already own property can still apply. The maximum purchase price is $832,750 and the home must serve as your primary residence. ## What the $20,000 Covers in Henderson The $20,000 arrives as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. You repay only when you sell, refinance, or reach the end of the 30 year term. The funds apply to down payment, closing costs, or a rate buydown. Henderson's median home price sits around **$495,000**. On an FHA loan at 3.5% down: - **FHA down payment (3.5%):** $17,325 - **Worker Advantage grant:** $20,000 - **Remaining for closing costs:** approximately $2,675 The grant handles the full down payment and puts a dent in closing costs, keeping out of pocket expenses low for officers purchasing at the median. ## Law Enforcement in Henderson The Henderson Police Department operates out of multiple facilities across the city, with patrol divisions covering areas from Green Valley to Anthem and Inspirada. Officers assigned to Henderson PD benefit from working within the same city where they can purchase a home, reducing commute time to roll call. Henderson also houses Nevada Highway Patrol troopers and Clark County detention staff at the Henderson Detention Center. Henderson's proximity to the 215 Beltway, I-11, and US 95 means officers who work for neighboring agencies like LVMPD or NLV PD can still live in Henderson and reach their assignments efficiently. ## Local Insight **Ryan Rose:** I work with Henderson PD officers who want to stay close to their beats. Henderson offers some of the best community feel in the valley, and the $20,000 grant brings monthly payments into reach for officers who assumed this city was out of budget. The median is higher than North Las Vegas, but the grant bridges that gap.
