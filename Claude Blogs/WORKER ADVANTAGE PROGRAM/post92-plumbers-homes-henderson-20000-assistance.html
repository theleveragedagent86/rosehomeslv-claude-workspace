# Plumbers Buying Homes in Henderson with $20,000 Assistance Henderson is one of the fastest growing cities in Nevada, and plumbers are helping build it. From the master planned communities of Cadence and Inspirada to the redevelopment along Boulder Highway, licensed plumbers and pipefitters keep new homes and commercial projects flowing. Nevada's Worker Advantage Program offers these tradespeople $20,000 in down payment assistance to buy a home in the city they are actively constructing. ## Henderson's Construction Pipeline for Plumbers Henderson's 2026 building activity spans residential and commercial sectors. Cadence continues to add new phases, Inspirada is expanding its footprint, and West Henderson has emerged as a major growth corridor with new retail, medical, and residential developments. The Boulder Highway corridor is seeing redevelopment that requires extensive plumbing infrastructure, from new sewer lines to commercial rough in work. Every new home requires a licensed plumber for supply lines, drain waste vent systems, and fixture installation. Every commercial building needs fire suppression, grease traps, and mechanical piping. Henderson's construction calendar ensures steady work for plumbers throughout 2026 and beyond. ## How the $20,000 Worker Advantage Grant Applies Plumbers and pipefitters are among the 12 construction trades eligible for the Worker Advantage Program. The $20,000 grant functions as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. Repayment is deferred until you sell, refinance, or reach the end of your 30 year loan term. Qualification requirements include a minimum 640 credit score, six months of Nevada residency, and a household income at or below $142,350. Most journeyman plumbers in Southern Nevada earn between $50,000 and $80,000, fitting comfortably within the income cap. ## Henderson Home Prices in 2026 Henderson's median home price is $495,000, making it one of the higher priced submarkets in the Las Vegas valley.
