# Construction Workers Buying Homes in Aliante with Worker Advantage Aliante is one of North Las Vegas's most established master planned communities, offering construction workers a neighborhood with parks, trails, a golf course, and a casino resort within walking distance. With a median home price of $460,000 and an average of 36 days on market, Aliante moves at a moderate pace that gives buyers time to plan. The $20,000 Worker Advantage grant helps construction trade workers close the gap between renting and owning in this well positioned community. ## What Makes Aliante Attractive to Construction Workers Aliante sits in the northern section of the Las Vegas valley with easy access to I 215 and US 95. Construction workers commuting to Strip projects, Downtown renovation sites, or the growing northwest corridor can reach most jobsites within 25 to 35 minutes. The community's location also puts workers close to North Las Vegas warehouse and distribution construction along the I 15 corridor. Beyond commute logistics, Aliante offers the kind of finished neighborhood that construction workers appreciate. The homes are solidly built, the community infrastructure is mature, and the amenities are already in place. You are not waiting years for a park or a trail system to materialize. ## Aliante Home Prices and Market Conditions At a $460,000 median, Aliante prices sit above the broader North Las Vegas median of $410,000 but below the Las Vegas metro average of $482,000. The 36 day average time on market indicates a balanced environment where buyers have room to negotiate without facing the extreme competition seen in some newer communities. Home styles in Aliante range from single story ranch plans popular with trade workers who want minimal maintenance to two story family homes with four or five bedrooms. Resale inventory provides the bulk of available options, though some newer infill construction appears periodically. ## Applying the $20,000 Grant in Aliante All 12 construction trades listed by the Worker Advantage Program qualify for the $20,000 grant: carpenters, electricians, plumbers, pipefitters, concrete finishers, ironworkers, heavy equipment operators, drywall installers, roofers, masons, painters, glaziers, and general laborers. On a $460,000 Aliante home, the numbers break down clearly. An FHA loan at 3.5% down requires $16,100. The $20,000 grant covers that and leaves $3,900 for closing costs. A conventional loan at 5% down requires $23,000, and the grant covers most of it. The grant is structured as a 0% interest second mortgage with no monthly payments. You repay only when you sell, refinance, or complete your 30 year term. Requirements include a 640 minimum credit score, six months of Nevada residency, and household income at or below $142,350. ## Living in a Master Planned Community Aliante's master plan includes the Aliante Nature Discovery Park, walking and biking trails, community pools, and proximity to Aliante Casino and Hotel. Schools in the area serve families with children at multiple grade levels. Grocery stores, medical offices, and dining options line the Aliante Parkway and Elkhorn Road corridors. For construction workers who spend their days on active jobsites, coming home to a quiet, well maintained neighborhood provides a welcome contrast. ## Local Insight Ryan Rose regularly helps construction workers evaluate Aliante against other North Las Vegas communities.
