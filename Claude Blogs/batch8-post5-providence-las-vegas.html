Providence Las Vegas: New England Charm in the Desert

Providence brings traditional neighborhood design to northwest Las Vegas with tree lined streets, front porch living, and a community atmosphere reminiscent of classic American small towns. This 1,200 acre master planned community consistently ranks among the top selling communities nationwide, combining thoughtful design with excellent northwest valley location.

Traditional Design Philosophy

Walking through Providence feels distinctly different from typical Las Vegas subdivisions. More than 4,000 trees line the streets, providing shade and creating an established neighborhood feel unusual for desert communities. Homes feature front porches designed for sitting and chatting with neighbors, encouraging the casual interactions that build genuine community connections. The development spans approximately 30 distinct neighborhoods with roughly 7,500 homes built by nationally recognized builders including Toll Brothers, Lennar, KB Home, Beazer, Pardee, Pulte, and Woodside.

Parks and Recreation

Three signature parks anchor Providence's recreational offerings. Knickerbocker Park covers 15 acres with separate play areas for toddlers and older children, sports fields, basketball and tennis courts, exercise stations along nature trails, and both large and small dog parks. Huckleberry Park provides basketball, tennis, and pickleball courts alongside multi use athletic fields. The Promenade Park meanders through the community from east to west, featuring walking paths with benches and beautifully landscaped resting spots. Together, these parks host community events, holiday celebrations, outdoor yoga, and organized activities that strengthen neighborhood bonds.

Location and Convenience

Providence occupies a prime northwest Las Vegas position with direct access to the 215 Beltway and US 95 freeway. The Las Vegas Strip sits approximately 20 minutes away, while the heart of Summerlin reaches residents in about 10 minutes. This location also places Providence conveniently between Creech and Nellis Air Force Bases, attracting military families and personnel. Mount Charleston and Floyd Lamb State Park provide nearby outdoor recreation, while shopping and dining options continue expanding along the northwest corridor.
