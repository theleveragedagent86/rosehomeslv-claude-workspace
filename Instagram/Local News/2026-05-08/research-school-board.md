### Story 1: Canvas Cyberattack Disrupts CCSD and Nevada Schools During Finals Week
- **Category:** School Board and Education
- **County/Area:** Clark County
- **Summary:** A global cyberattack by the hacking group ShinyHunters hit Canvas, the learning management system used by CCSD, UNLV, and nearly 9,000 schools worldwide. Approximately 3.65 terabytes of data were stolen, including student names, email addresses, student ID numbers, and billions of private messages. The hackers set a May 12, 2026, deadline to leak the data unless a settlement is reached. Superintendent Jhone Ebert confirmed no Social Security numbers, financial data, or medical records were compromised and said the district has no intention of paying a ransom.

- **Date:** May 7-8, 2026
- **Why It Matters:** Hundreds of thousands of local students lost access to assignments and exams during finals week, and parents need to watch for phishing attacks using stolen student data.

### Story 2: CCSD Finalizes Staggered Bell Schedule for 2026-27 School Year
- **Category:** School Board and Education
- **County/Area:** Clark County

### Story 12: Nevada Education Funding at Risk if Economy Dips in 2027
- **Category:** School Board and Education
- **County/Area:** Clark County / Statewide
- **Summary:** Nevada's K-12 funding formula is revenue-based, meaning if state tax revenues fall, per-pupil funding decreases proportionally. The base per-pupil amount is nearly flat: $9,416 for 2025 and $9,486 for 2026. Weakening tourism and economic uncertainty have already caused $161 million less in projected State Education Fund revenue.
