## Story 1: Mortgage Applications Rise as Homebuyers Adapt to Higher, Volatile Rates

Buyers Are Back Despite Higher Mortgage Rates, and That Says a Lot

Mortgage rates are nearly 40 basis points higher than they were at the end of February. And buyers are still showing up.

The MBA mortgage demand index rose 1.7% last week. Purchase applications jumped 4% week-over-week. Year-over-year, purchase apps are up 7% and refinance applications are up 28%.

That's not what most people expected heading into a spring market shaken by inflation fears tied to the war in Iran. Borrowing costs have been unpredictable for nearly three months now. The daily average rate hit near its highest level since March just this past Tuesday.

Joel Kan, the MBA's deputy chief economist, put it simply. Potential homebuyers "shrugged off the current economic and mortgage rate uncertainties and returned to the market."

But here's the tension. People are buying, yet the market is moving slower than it should be.

NAR reported existing home sales rose just 0.2% in April, reaching a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 4.02 million. For a spring market, that's sluggish. Lawrence Yun, NAR's chief economist, pointed out that far more people could qualify at the 6% rates we saw in February compared to what we've had in March and April.

This is the reality for Las Vegas buyers right now. Rates are higher, but inventory is solid. Brad Case, Homes.com's chief residential economist, said buyers have the power to "choose from solid inventory and be able to negotiate more evenly." He also said buyers aren't in a hurry, and shouldn't be.

That's the real story here. The market has shifted. It's not a frenzy. It's not a crash. It's a market where patient, prepared buyers actually have leverage.

In Clark County, that means if you've been waiting for the "right time," the right time might not be a rate. It might be the negotiating position you're in today.

Are you watching the rates, or are you watching the opportunities they're creating?
