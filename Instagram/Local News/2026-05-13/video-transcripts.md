### Story 1: Mortgage Applications Rise as Homebuyers Adapt to Higher, Volatile Rates

Buyers are jumping back into the market even with rates sitting way above where they were in February.

The MBA just reported mortgage purchase applications climbed 4% last week. And compared to last year, they're up 7%. That's with rates nearly 40 basis points higher than late February. The war in Iran has been stoking inflation fears for almost three months now, and it's been pushing borrowing costs all over the place.

Here's the thing most people miss. NAR says home sales only ticked up 0.2% in April. That's way slower than a normal spring market. Their chief economist said at 6% rates back in February, way more buyers could qualify. Now? Fewer can. But the ones who can are still moving.
