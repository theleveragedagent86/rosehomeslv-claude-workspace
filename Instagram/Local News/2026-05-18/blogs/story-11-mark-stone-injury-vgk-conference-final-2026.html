Mark Stone Out for VGK Conference Final in 2026 with No Return Timeline

Vegas Golden Knights captain Mark Stone is out of the 2026 Conference Final against the Colorado Avalanche with a lower-body injury and no timetable for return, leaving Las Vegas without its most important player at the worst possible moment. The injury, combined with the season-ending hip problems that sidelined defenseman Alex Pietrangelo and the upper-body issue keeping Jeremy Lauzon out, means the Golden Knights are heading into the Western Conference Final shorthanded in ways that could reshape the entire series.

What Happened

It started in Game 3 of the first-round series against the Anaheim Ducks on May 8. Stone took a hit, left the game early, and did not return. At the time, the Golden Knights were winning the series and most fans hoped it was something minor. That hope faded fast.

Stone missed Game 4. He missed Game 6. As the series wrapped up and VGK punched their ticket to the Western Conference Final against the Colorado Avalanche, Stone was still not skating in practice. Head coach John Tortorella gave reporters nothing to work with. No timeline. No update. No hint at whether Stone would be back in Game 1, Game 3, or at all.

On May 12, the Golden Knights recalled forward Braeden Bowman from the Henderson Silver Knights, the team's AHL affiliate, to add depth to the roster. That move alone told the story. When a team pulls up a player from the minors during the conference final, they are not expecting their captain back anytime soon.

Tortorella's silence is not unusual for NHL coaches, who protect injury information to keep opponents guessing. But the combination of no practice time and no comment after more than a week is not a good sign. Lower-body injuries in hockey can mean anything from a bruised knee to something structural that requires weeks of recovery. VGK fans are left reading between the lines.

Lauzon, a reliable defensive presence, is also sidelined with an upper-body injury. His status is equally unclear. And Pietrangelo, the veteran defenseman who was the backbone of this blue line for years, will not play another game this season after hip injuries ended his year entirely. The Golden Knights are not just missing Stone. They are missing depth across the roster at a time when every player matters.

Bowman, 22 years old, has limited NHL experience. He was brought in to fill a roster spot, not to replace what Stone brings to this team. That gap in production and leadership is real and significant.

Why It Matters

Mark Stone is not just a good hockey player. He is the best player on this team and has been for years. He is a two-way forward, meaning he is dangerous on offense but also one of the best defensive forwards in the entire league. He reads plays before they happen, wins battles along the boards, and has a knack for scoring goals at the right moment. He was named captain of this franchise in 2021 and has worn that "C" with a level of commitment that goes beyond statistics.

When you lose your captain in the conference final, the team does not just lose his goals and assists. They lose his voice in the locker room, his presence on the ice that changes how opponents plan, and the identity that rallies the group when things get hard. Other players will need to step up in ways they have not been asked to step up before. That kind of pressure separates contenders from teams that fall short.

The Colorado Avalanche are a dangerous opponent. They have stars of their own and they know how to win in the playoffs. If VGK shows up without Stone, Colorado's coaching staff will notice. They will find a way to exploit the depth chart. They will put pressure on players who are not used to carrying that weight. That is just playoff hockey at its most basic level.

For Las Vegas as a city, the stakes are high. The Golden Knights are one of the most popular sports teams in Nevada history. Every playoff run brings the city together in ways that few other events can match. T-Mobile Arena fills up, the restaurants near the Strip see more business, and people who don't normally watch hockey start paying close attention. A deep playoff run matters to this community. That is why an injury to the team's best player is genuinely big news in Las Vegas, not just for hockey fans but for the city as a whole.

The Golden Knights have shown they can win without certain players before. This franchise has dealt with injuries in previous playoff runs. But losing Stone and Pietrangelo at the same time, with Lauzon also out, is a lot to ask the depth of the roster to cover.

Background

Mark Stone came to Vegas in February 2019 via a trade from the Ottawa Senators. He signed a huge long-term extension almost immediately, which told you everything about how much the franchise valued him. Since then, he has been the face of this team alongside Pietrangelo and goaltender Adin Hill.

Stone has dealt with injuries before. Back problems cost him significant time in the 2022-23 season, the year VGK won the Stanley Cup. He came back for the final push and played a major role in that championship run. That history gives Vegas fans some reason to believe he could return again if the series goes deep enough.

Alex Pietrangelo's situation is different and more final. The veteran defenseman, who was the cornerstone of this blue line for years, dealt with hip injuries that made it impossible for him to play this postseason at all. He is 35 years old and his future with the team is uncertain. His absence this spring is a preview of what life might look like for VGK without him going forward.

Jeremy Lauzon has been a steady, physical presence on the back end. He is not a star player, but dependable defenders matter in the playoffs. His injury chips away at the defensive depth the team needs against a fast Colorado offense.

Braeden Bowman is a young player trying to prove he belongs at this level. He was a late-round pick who worked his way up through the Silver Knights system. Getting a call-up to the NHL during the conference final is a life-changing opportunity, but it also comes with enormous pressure for someone with limited playoff experience.

What Happens Next

The Western Conference Final against the Colorado Avalanche is underway. Every game VGK plays without Stone is a game where the team has to dig deeper. Tortorella will lean on veterans like William Karlsson, Jonathan Marchessault, and others to pick up the offensive slack. Ivan Barbashev will need to be a difference-maker. The blue line will need to be sharp to protect Hill in net.

The best-case scenario is that Stone's injury responds well to treatment and he is able to return during the series. If the Golden Knights can push this to a Game 5, 6, or 7, there is a chance Stone could get healthy enough to play a role. Coaches and trainers often work quietly to get injured players ready for the biggest games.

The worst case is that Stone is done for the postseason entirely. If that happens, VGK will need a true team effort where multiple players make up for his production on any given night. That kind of collective response is possible, but it requires everyone to be at their best.

Vegas fans should watch the practice reports closely. When Stone starts skating again, that is a signal the training staff believes recovery is possible. If he continues to miss practice with no update from Tortorella, the chances of him returning in this series grow smaller with each passing day.

The T-Mobile Arena crowd will be electric for every home game regardless. This city knows how to show up for its team. Whether Stone is in the lineup or watching from the press box, the building will be full and loud.
