Redhead Burger Brings Its Miami Beach Smashburgers to The Venetian Las Vegas

A popular Miami Beach burger spot just made its West Coast debut right in the middle of the Las Vegas Strip. Redhead Burger opened inside Grand Canal Shoppes at The Venetian on May 7, 2026, giving Las Vegas a new late-night option that runs until 2 a.m. every single day.

If you have not heard of Redhead Burger yet, you are not alone. The brand is still relatively young. It launched in 2019 in South Beach, Florida, and built a loyal following there before setting its sights on Las Vegas. Now it has a 50-seat restaurant steps away from one of the most trafficked casino floors in the world, and people are already talking about it.

What Happened

Redhead Burger officially opened its doors at Grand Canal Shoppes inside The Venetian on May 7, 2026. This is the brand's first location outside of Miami Beach, and its first presence on the West Coast. The restaurant seats 50 guests inside a space designed around a red-and-white color scheme that mirrors the look and feel of the original South Beach spot.

The menu centers on the smashburger format, which means thin beef patties cooked on a flat-top griddle at very high heat. That technique creates crispy, caramelized edges while keeping the inside juicy. The process goes fast, which is part of why this style of burger has taken off across the country over the last several years.

The star of the menu is the Redhead Burger itself. It stacks three patties with triple cheese, making it a big, bold option for anyone who wants to go all in. Beyond the burgers, the restaurant serves loaded sides that have become fan favorites at the Miami Beach location. Think elevated takes on fries and other comfort food staples.

The beef blend used at Redhead Burger is proprietary, meaning they developed it specifically for their burgers and do not share the exact mix. That kind of attention to the core product is one reason the brand earned a strong following in South Beach before ever thinking about expansion. When a restaurant is serious enough about its beef to develop its own blend, it tends to show in the final product.

Hours are set at open to 2 a.m. daily, which is a smart move for a Strip location. Las Vegas does not slow down at midnight, and having a quality burger option available late fills a real gap. Most sit-down restaurants close their kitchens well before that, leaving late-night diners to settle for fast food or casino cafe menus. Redhead Burger is positioned to capture a crowd that currently has few good options after 11 p.m.

Why It Matters

A new burger restaurant on the Strip might not sound like major news, but the details here are worth paying attention to. Redhead Burger did not just open any location. It chose Las Vegas as its very first expansion outside of Miami. That tells you something about how the brand sees this market and what kind of foot traffic they expect to see walk through the door.

Grand Canal Shoppes is not a typical mall. It sits inside The Venetian, one of the highest-volume resorts on the Strip. The shopping complex attracts both hotel guests and locals who come specifically for the dining and retail. Landing a spot there is competitive. You are up against established names with strong followings, and the landlord knows it can fill those spaces. Redhead Burger earned that spot on the strength of what it built in South Beach.

For locals, this is another sign that the Strip continues to evolve beyond gambling and big-name celebrity chef restaurants. There is a growing appetite for places that feel more casual and neighborhood-friendly without giving up quality. Smashburgers fit that need well. They are approachable, affordable compared to steakhouse options, and fast enough to fit into a busy night without slowing everything down.

The late-night hours also matter for locals in a specific way. People who work in the service industry, entertainment, or hospitality often get off shift well after midnight. Having a spot like Redhead Burger available until 2 a.m. gives those workers a real dining option, not just drive-through food. That is a community service in a city where a huge portion of the workforce operates on overnight and swing schedules.

From a real estate and neighborhood perspective, new restaurant openings on the Strip also signal broader economic momentum. When brands that have strong followings elsewhere pick Las Vegas as their first major expansion market, it reflects confidence in the city's growth. That confidence does not stay contained to the Strip. It flows outward and reinforces why people want to live here, not just visit.

Background

Redhead Burger was founded in 2019 in Miami Beach, Florida. The timing was notable because it launched just before the pandemic reshaped the restaurant industry. That the brand not only survived but built a following strong enough to support expansion says something about the quality of the product and the loyalty of its customers.

South Beach is a tough market. It draws a global crowd with high expectations and plenty of competing options. Earning a repeat-customer base there means you are doing something right. Redhead Burger developed that following by focusing on a simple formula: quality beef, a proprietary blend, smashed thin on a hot griddle, and loaded with cheese. They did not try to do everything. They picked one thing and did it very well.

The smashburger trend itself has been building for years. What started as a niche style favored by burger enthusiasts has become mainstream. Regional chains like Smashburger helped introduce the format nationally, but independently owned spots with their own takes on the style have driven some of the most interesting growth. Redhead Burger falls into that second category. It is not a franchise copying a formula. It developed its own identity around the smash technique.

Las Vegas has seen a wave of restaurant openings over the last couple of years as the city continues to attract both visitors and new residents. The Strip in particular has added a wide range of concepts across different price points and categories. Redhead Burger fits into the casual-but-quality segment that has shown strong demand from both tourists looking for something relaxed and locals who want a solid meal without a two-hour commitment.

What Happens Next

The immediate question for Redhead Burger is whether the Las Vegas market responds the way South Beach did. Opening week buzz is expected at any Strip location, but the real test comes after the novelty wears off. If the quality holds and the late-night crowd becomes a reliable part of the business, this could anchor a broader West Coast expansion for the brand.

Grand Canal Shoppes has a history of attracting concepts that then grow into regional or national presences. Being visible at The Venetian puts the brand in front of millions of visitors from across the country and the world each year. That kind of exposure is hard to buy, and it tends to accelerate brand recognition faster than traditional marketing.

For Las Vegas diners, the next few months will reveal whether Redhead Burger becomes a go-to spot or fades into the crowded Strip dining landscape. The late-night positioning gives them an edge because there are genuinely few quality options at that hour. If the kitchen can maintain consistency when the Friday night crowd rolls in at 1 a.m., they will build the kind of loyalty that turns into long lines and word-of-mouth.

It would not be surprising to see additional Nevada locations follow if this one performs well. Las Vegas is a natural testing ground for brands looking to expand westward, and a second location in Henderson or Summerlin would serve a very different but equally hungry customer base. For now, the focus is on proving the concept works here on the Strip.
