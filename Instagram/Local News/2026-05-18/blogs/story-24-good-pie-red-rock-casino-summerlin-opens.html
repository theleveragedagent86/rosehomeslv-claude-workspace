Good Pie Opens Its Third Location Inside Red Rock Casino in Summerlin

Las Vegas-born Good Pie just made its casino debut, opening a third location inside the Red Rock Casino Resort and Spa food court in Summerlin on May 12, 2026, complete with an exclusive new menu item you can only get at this spot.

What Happened

On May 12, 2026, Good Pie cut the ribbon on its newest location inside the food court at Red Rock Casino Resort and Spa, right in the heart of Summerlin. The opening marks a big step for the brand. It is their first location inside a casino, and it signals that Good Pie is ready to grow beyond the neighborhood spots where it built its name.

Good Pie started in Las Vegas and has built a loyal following around two pizza styles that are not always easy to find here. The first is Detroit-style pizza, which is baked in a deep rectangular pan and has a thick, airy crust with crispy edges and sauce on top of the cheese. The second is Brooklyn-style, which is thin, wide, and foldable, the kind you might grab by the slice in New York City. Both styles have their fans, and Good Pie has done well serving both under one roof.

The new Red Rock Casino location does more than just bring those two styles to a new part of town. It also introduces an item called the White Pie Remix, which is exclusive to this location. You cannot order it at the Downtown Container Park location or the original spot. The White Pie Remix is only available at Red Rock Casino, giving people in Summerlin and visitors to the resort a reason to make a trip just for that item.

The food court at Red Rock Casino already draws a steady crowd. It sits inside one of the most popular resort-casinos on the west side of the valley. Adding a locally born pizza brand to the lineup is a different move than bringing in a national chain, and it reflects a growing interest in keeping Las Vegas food dollars inside Las Vegas businesses.

The grand opening drew a crowd. People lined up to try both the familiar menu and the new exclusive item. Social media lit up with photos from customers who were excited to see a local brand step into the casino space. For a city that sees so many restaurant concepts come from out of state, a locally grown pizza brand landing a casino partnership is worth noting.

Why It Matters

Good Pie landing inside Red Rock Casino matters for a few reasons, and they go beyond just having another place to grab a slice.

First, it is a sign that local Las Vegas restaurants can compete at the casino level. Casino food courts are high-traffic, high-visibility spots. Resort properties typically fill those spaces with national brands because they have name recognition and can guarantee a consistent experience. When a locally born concept earns a spot at that table, it means the brand has reached a level of quality and reliability that casinos trust. That is not easy to do.

Second, this opening puts Good Pie right in front of the Summerlin and west valley market in a new way. Red Rock Casino is not just a local hangout. It draws visitors from across the valley and tourists who are staying on the west side of Las Vegas. A location there gives Good Pie exposure to people who might never have driven to the original spots. That kind of visibility can drive long-term loyalty.

Third, the exclusive White Pie Remix is a smart business move. By creating a menu item that only exists at this location, Good Pie gives regulars a reason to visit a new spot and gives first-time customers something unique to try. Exclusivity builds buzz, and buzz brings foot traffic. It also tells you something about the brand: they are thinking about how to make each location feel a little different rather than just copying and pasting the same menu everywhere.

For Summerlin residents specifically, this is good news. Summerlin is one of the most desirable places to live in the Las Vegas valley. Families there have access to great schools, green spaces, and well-planned neighborhoods. But the dining scene in master-planned communities sometimes leans toward familiar chains. A local pizza brand with a cult following is a welcome addition.

From a real estate perspective, neighborhood dining options matter to buyers and renters. When a community has a strong mix of local restaurants and not just chain outlets, it tends to feel more vibrant and lived-in. That perception shapes how people think about a place when they are deciding where to put down roots. Good Pie at Red Rock Casino adds to Summerlin's appeal as a full-service community where you can eat well without driving far.

Background

Good Pie was born in Las Vegas. The brand was built around the idea that people in this city deserve great pizza that is made with care, uses quality ingredients, and holds up to comparison with pies you would find in Detroit or New York. That is a high bar, and Good Pie set out to clear it.

The original location gave the brand a chance to prove the concept. It attracted regulars quickly, and word spread through the local food community. From there, Good Pie opened a second spot at Downtown Container Park, a popular outdoor shopping and dining destination near Fremont Street. Container Park is known for drawing a mix of locals and tourists, which helped Good Pie reach a broader audience while still feeling like a neighborhood find.

Detroit-style pizza specifically has had a moment nationally over the past several years. What was once a regional specialty tied to Michigan has spread to cities across the country, with dedicated pizza shops and even fast-casual chains adopting the format. Good Pie was ahead of that wave in Las Vegas. By the time the broader market caught up to Detroit-style pizza, Good Pie already had a loyal customer base here.

Brooklyn-style pizza offered a different lane. Thin-crust, by-the-slice pizza has its own passionate following, and offering both styles under one brand gave Good Pie a wider appeal. You could bring a group with different pizza preferences and everyone would leave happy. That flexibility likely contributed to how quickly the brand built a following.

Now, with three locations and a casino partnership, Good Pie is positioned as one of the most successful locally born pizza concepts in Las Vegas. The Red Rock Casino location is the most prominent spot yet, and it sets up the brand for continued growth if they choose to pursue it.

What Happens Next

The immediate question is whether the Red Rock Casino location performs the way the first two have. Casino food courts are busy, which is good for volume, but they can also be competitive environments where customers have many options right next to each other. Good Pie will need to deliver consistent quality and fast service to turn first-time visitors into repeat customers.

The White Pie Remix will be worth watching. If it catches on and becomes a fan favorite, it could become a permanent fixture of the menu at this location or even expand to other spots in some form. Exclusive menu items are a common tool for driving interest, but they only work if the item is actually good. Early social media reactions suggest people are excited, but the real test comes in the weeks and months after the opening buzz fades.

There is also the broader question of expansion. Three locations in Las Vegas is meaningful, but the brand could choose to grow further. They could open in other parts of the valley, pursue more casino partnerships, or even consider markets outside Nevada. Based on what they have built so far, those conversations would not be surprising.

For the Summerlin community, the near-term benefit is simple: a new and well-regarded local pizza option is now accessible within the Red Rock Casino complex. Whether you live in the neighborhood, work nearby, or are just visiting the resort, Good Pie is now part of the west valley dining landscape in a bigger way than before.
