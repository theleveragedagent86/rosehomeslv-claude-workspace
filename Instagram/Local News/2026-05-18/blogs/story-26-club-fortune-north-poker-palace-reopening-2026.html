Club Fortune North to Reopen at the Former Poker Palace This Summer

A North Las Vegas landmark is getting a fresh start. The Poker Palace, a casino that has been part of the community since 1974, is set to reopen under a new name and new ownership this summer. The property will now be called Club Fortune North, and locals can expect to see the doors open in late June 2026.

This is big news for North Las Vegas, a city that has been growing fast and attracting new investment in recent years. A $20 million renovation of a local institution says something about where the area is headed.

What Happened

The Poker Palace closed for renovations in late 2025, not long after Reno-based Truckee Gaming purchased the property in October of that year. The sale price was $20 million, and what followed was not a light refresh. According to Truckee Gaming CEO Ferenc Szony, the work amounted to "almost a complete redo" of the entire property.

The renovation touched nearly every part of the building. The bar was redesigned from the ground up. The restaurant got more space to seat and serve guests. New slot machines replaced the old ones throughout the floor. The interior was remodeled, and so was the exterior, giving the building a look that reflects the new direction of the brand.

The new name, Club Fortune North, connects this location to Club Fortune Casino in Henderson, which Truckee Gaming already operates. The company is building a small family of casinos in the Las Vegas Valley, and this North Las Vegas property is the newest addition to that group.

One thing regulars should know going in: Club Fortune North will not have poker tables or other table games. The original Poker Palace was known, as the name suggests, for its poker room. That part of the experience will not be coming back. However, the new casino will include a race and sportsbook operated through Boomer's Sportsbook, which gives sports fans a place to catch games and place wagers under one roof.

The reopening is expected to happen in late June 2026. No specific date has been announced as of early May, but Truckee Gaming has indicated it is on track for a summer launch. Local residents and casino regulars have been watching the construction with interest for months, and the finish line is now in sight.

Why It Matters

For people who live in North Las Vegas, this reopening is about more than just a casino coming back online. It is about what happens to a neighborhood when a long-vacant property gets brought back to life.

The Poker Palace site had been sitting through months of renovation work, and before the sale, the future of the property was uncertain. A $20 million investment from an outside company is a clear vote of confidence in North Las Vegas. When businesses spend that kind of money, they are betting that the area has real potential. That is a message worth paying attention to if you own a home nearby or are thinking about buying one.

North Las Vegas has been one of the faster-growing cities in the Las Vegas Valley. New construction is happening across the city, more employers are setting up operations there, and infrastructure improvements have been ongoing. A reinvigorated casino and entertainment destination fits into that broader story of growth.

The expanded restaurant and redesigned bar at Club Fortune North are also worth noting. Locals looking for a neighborhood spot to grab a meal or a drink will have a new option. The casino is not just trying to draw gamblers. It is trying to become a place where people in the community want to spend time, which is a smart approach for a local casino that is not competing directly with the big Strip properties.

The addition of Boomer's Sportsbook is well-timed too. Sports betting has grown quickly in Nevada since more states have opened up legal wagering, and local books have become gathering spots for fans on game days. Giving North Las Vegas residents a quality place to watch and bet on sports, close to home, adds real value to the area.

From a real estate perspective, neighborhood amenities matter. A freshly renovated casino with a restaurant, bar, and sportsbook is the kind of anchor that can lift the appeal of surrounding blocks. It is not the only factor in home values, but it is one piece of a larger picture that buyers and sellers should understand.

Background

The Poker Palace opened in 1974, which means it has been part of North Las Vegas for more than 50 years. That is a long run. For decades it operated as a neighborhood casino, the kind of place that attracted locals rather than tourists. It was known for its poker room, which gave the property its identity.

Over the years, neighborhood casinos across the Las Vegas Valley faced increasing competition from larger properties, and some struggled to keep up. The Poker Palace had its ups and downs like many similar spots. By the time Truckee Gaming came along in October 2025, the property was ready for a serious upgrade.

Truckee Gaming is based in Reno and already operates Club Fortune Casino in Henderson. Taking the Club Fortune name to North Las Vegas is part of what looks like a deliberate strategy to grow a portfolio of local Nevada casinos outside of the major Strip corridor. Ferenc Szony, the company's CEO, has been public about the scope of the renovation and the commitment the company is making to the property and to North Las Vegas.

The decision to drop poker and table games is a notable shift from the original identity of the property. Running poker and live table games requires dealers, supervisors, and a different kind of floor management. Slots and a sportsbook have a lower operational overhead and can still drive strong revenue. Boomer's Sportsbook is an established brand in the Nevada market, and the partnership makes sense for a casino that wants to attract a loyal local audience without the complexity of a full table game operation.

What Happens Next

The most immediate next step is the reopening itself. Club Fortune North is targeting late June 2026. Between now and then, the finishing touches on the renovation are being completed, staff is being hired and trained, and the sportsbook setup with Boomer's is being finalized.

Once the doors open, the real question is whether the community will embrace it. The Poker Palace had decades of local loyalty built up, but the audience for the new Club Fortune North may be somewhat different. Without poker tables, some of the old regulars may not return as often. On the other hand, a cleaner, modern space with a good restaurant and a sportsbook could bring in a wider crowd, including younger residents who are more interested in sports betting than card games.

Truckee Gaming will likely be watching the early months closely. Club Fortune in Henderson gives them a playbook for what works at a local Nevada casino, and they will probably apply lessons from that property to the North Las Vegas location as it finds its footing.

For the surrounding neighborhood and business community, the reopening could generate foot traffic and activity that benefits other nearby shops and services. A busy casino draws people to an area, and that spillover effect can be meaningful for a commercial corridor that has seen some quiet years.

Watch for an official opening announcement from Truckee Gaming or Club Fortune North closer to the end of June. Local news outlets covering the Las Vegas Valley will likely have details as the date gets closer.
