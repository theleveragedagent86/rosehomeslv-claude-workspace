Golden Knights Sweep the Avalanche and Head to the 2026 Stanley Cup Final

The Vegas Golden Knights are going back to the Stanley Cup Final. They closed out the Colorado Avalanche with a 2-1 victory in Game 4 on May 26, 2026, completing a four-game sweep of the best team in the regular season. This is a big deal for everyone in Las Vegas and Clark County. A team built from scratch in 2017 has now reached the sport's biggest stage three times in just nine years. That kind of success is rare anywhere in professional sports, and it is happening right here in the desert.

Colorado was no pushover. The Avalanche won the Presidents' Trophy, which goes to the team with the best record in the entire league during the regular season. They scored nearly four goals per game. Vegas made them look ordinary. Carter Hart was the backbone of it all, turning away shots and keeping Colorado off the board night after night. When the final buzzer sounded at T-Mobile Arena, the building shook. Fans who had waited all season for this moment finally got to see their team punch its ticket to the Final.

What Happened

Game 4 was tightly contested from the opening puck drop. The Golden Knights struck first when captain Mark Stone took advantage of a defensive breakdown. Stone got the puck in open ice and skated in alone on a breakaway, burying the shot at 4:42 of the first period. It was exactly the kind of goal Vegas needed. An early lead at home in a closeout game gives the crowd energy and puts pressure on the visiting team right away.

Colorado pushed back throughout the second period, trying to find the tying goal. Carter Hart was equal to every challenge. He read plays well, positioned himself cleanly in the crease, and made several stops that kept the score at 1-0. The Avalanche, who had been one of the most dangerous offensive teams in the NHL all year, could not find a way past him. Their average of 3.63 goals per game in the regular season felt like a distant memory.

Colorado finally broke through late, pulling within one goal and setting up a tense final stretch. Cole Smith answered for Vegas. With 5:45 remaining in the third period, Smith scored what turned out to be the insurance goal, pushing the lead back to two. That goal took the air out of the Avalanche comeback. Colorado could not recover in time. The game ended 2-1, and the series ended 4-0.

Hart finished Game 4 with 20 saves on 21 shots. That gave him six consecutive playoff wins. Across the four games of this series, he allowed just 10 total goals against a team that had been putting up four goals a night in the regular season. Vegas became only the second team in NHL history to sweep a Presidents' Trophy winner in the playoffs. That is a historically significant achievement. It shows how well-prepared and battle-tested this team is when the stakes are highest.

Background and History

The Golden Knights were not supposed to be good this fast. When the NHL awarded Las Vegas an expansion franchise in 2017, most hockey insiders expected the team to struggle for several years. That is what usually happens with expansion teams. They start from scratch with players no other team wanted, they learn the ropes, and over time they build a competitive roster. Vegas skipped all of that.

In their very first season, the Golden Knights won the Pacific Division, swept through the playoffs, and reached the Stanley Cup Final. They lost that series to the Washington Capitals in five games, but nobody in Las Vegas was complaining. The city had fallen in love with hockey. T-Mobile Arena, which opened in 2016, became one of the loudest and most passionate buildings in the league. The Golden Knights had done something that no expansion team in the four major North American sports leagues had ever done before.

The team kept building. They reached the Western Conference Finals in 2021. Then, in 2023, they won it all. The Golden Knights defeated the Florida Panthers in five games to claim the Stanley Cup, bringing it back to Las Vegas for the first time. The city celebrated in a way that matched its reputation for going big. The parade drew hundreds of thousands of people to the Strip. Players skated with the Cup at T-Mobile Arena. It was a moment this community will never forget.

Now, three years later, Vegas is back in the Final again. This team has a different look than the Cup-winning squad, but the culture remains the same. They play hard, they defend, they trust their goaltender, and they do not rattle easily. Carter Hart has been the standout of this postseason. His calm, focused play in the nets has reminded fans of what it looks like when a goaltender is locked in at the right time. The Golden Knights have shown, once again, that they know how to win when it counts.

What Happens Next

The Stanley Cup Final begins on June 2, 2026. Game 1 is in Raleigh, North Carolina, where the Vegas Golden Knights will take on the Carolina Hurricanes at PNC Arena. Game 2 is also in Raleigh. The series then shifts to Las Vegas for Game 3 on June 6 at T-Mobile Arena, followed by Game 4 on June 9, also at T-Mobile Arena. Games 5, 6, and 7, if necessary, will follow the same home-and-away format.

The Hurricanes are a formidable opponent. Carolina plays a high-energy, physical style and has strong goaltending of their own. This will be a competitive series. But Vegas has shown in this playoff run that they can shut down any offense in the league. Hart has been nearly unbeatable. The team's defensive structure is sound. Vegas enters the Final with confidence, momentum, and home-ice advantage for two games right away.

For fans in Las Vegas, the Golden Knights have already announced a $5 official watch party at T-Mobile Arena for Game 1 on June 2. All proceeds from ticket sales will go to the VGK Foundation, the team's charitable arm. This is a great way to experience the game with thousands of fellow fans in the building even when the team is playing on the road. The arena staff will set up the ice surface, screens, and sound system to give you a real game-day feel. Tickets are limited, so get them early if you plan to go.
