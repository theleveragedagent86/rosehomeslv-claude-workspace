Golden Knights Win Game 1 of the Stanley Cup Final 5-4, Take Early Series Lead

The Golden Knights celebrate a historic comeback win in Raleigh on June 2, 2026.

On June 2, 2026, the Vegas Golden Knights did something no road team had ever done before in the history of the Stanley Cup Final. Down two goals after the first period, playing in a loud and hostile arena in Raleigh, North Carolina, Vegas rallied, clawed back, and ultimately won Game 1 of the 2026 Stanley Cup Final by a score of 5-4. Tomas Hertl scored the go-ahead goal with just 3 minutes and 24 seconds remaining in the third period, and the Golden Knights held on to secure one of the most dramatic wins in franchise history.

For the fans back home in Las Vegas, this was the kind of moment that defines a city. The Golden Knights are not just a hockey team here. They are a symbol of what Las Vegas has become as a major American sports market, and this opening win in the Stanley Cup Final reminded the entire country that Vegas plays to win.

What Happened in Game 1

The game did not start the way anyone in the Vegas locker room had hoped. Carolina came out fast and physical on home ice, and by the end of the first period the Hurricanes held a 2-0 lead. The crowd at PNC Arena was energized, and the storyline looked like it might write itself in Carolina's favor. Road teams historically struggle to win Game 1 on the road in the Stanley Cup Final, and a two-goal deficit in that setting typically seals the outcome.

Vegas had other ideas.

The Golden Knights responded with the kind of resilient, structured hockey that has become the hallmark of this franchise. They worked back into the game steadily, cutting into the deficit and eventually tying the contest as the game moved into the third period. The back-and-forth nature of the final two periods kept fans on the edge of their seats and demonstrated just how closely matched these two teams are at the top of the NHL.

Then, with 3:24 left on the clock in the third period, Tomas Hertl delivered. His go-ahead goal put Vegas in front 5-4 and proved to be the difference. The Golden Knights' defense held firm for the final minutes, and Vegas skated off the ice in Carolina with a 1-0 series lead and a place in the NHL record books.

The History Behind the Milestone

Context matters when you are talking about a win like this. The fact that Vegas became the first road team in NHL history to rally from a multi-goal deficit to win Game 1 of a Stanley Cup Final is not a minor footnote. The Stanley Cup Final has been played for over a century. In all of those games, spanning generations of hockey greatness, no visiting team had ever come back from two or more goals down in Game 1 to take the win. Until now.

That kind of historical significance places this Golden Knights team in a conversation that extends well beyond the 2026 playoffs. Future generations of hockey fans in Las Vegas will grow up hearing about what this team accomplished on June 2, 2026, in Raleigh. What Happens Next in the Series

Winning Game 1 on the road is significant, but it is far from a guarantee of anything more. The Carolina Hurricanes are a talented, deep team that plays physical and disciplined hockey. They won the right to host the opening games of the Stanley Cup Final, and they will not roll over after one loss. If anything, a home loss in this manner will motivate Carolina to come out harder in Game 2.

The Golden Knights now hold a 1-0 series lead heading into Game 2, still in Raleigh. That means the pressure shifts slightly. Vegas can afford to lose one and return home at 1-1, still in a favorable position with two games at T-Mobile Arena ahead. But a 2-0 series lead would be seismic, and that goal will be on the minds of every player in the Vegas locker room.

For fans watching from Las Vegas, the anticipation of potentially seeing the Stanley Cup come home to the valley is real. T-Mobile Arena has long been one of the loudest and most electric environments in professional hockey, and the prospect of the Golden Knights closing out a championship series at home is something the entire city is holding onto.

The matchup between these two franchises is compelling because of how different their styles are in some ways and how similar they are in others. Both teams are built on structure, physicality, and goaltending. Both have the offensive capability to score in bunches when momentum swings their way. That combination of factors suggests this series could go deep, and every game from here forward has the potential to be decided by a single late goal, just as Game 1 was.
