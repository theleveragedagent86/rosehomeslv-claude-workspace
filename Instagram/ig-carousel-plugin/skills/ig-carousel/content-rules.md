# Content Rules for Carousel Output

These rules apply to ALL text produced for carousel slides and captions. No exceptions.

## Writing Style

- Professional but warm tone, 6th-grade reading level
- Short sentences. Talk like you're explaining to a friend.
- No jargon, no "premier", no "exclusive opportunity"
- Soft CTAs. Invite, don't push. "Want me to send the buyer guide?" beats "Call now!"

## Hard Rules

- **No em-dashes.** Use commas, periods, or "and" instead. This is the single most important formatting rule. Real estate prospects flag em-dashes as AI-generated.
- **Factual only.** Never fabricate event dates, ticket prices, restaurant details, housing stats, school ratings, HOA amounts, or program details. If you cannot verify it from an official source, mark it as `[NOT FOUND]` so Ryan can fill it in manually.
- **Las Vegas / Clark County focus.** Henderson, Summerlin, Spring Valley, Centennial Hills, North Las Vegas, Skye Canyon, Green Valley, Boulder City.
- **All market stats must cite sources inline.** Every number in the housing market slide needs a source name and data month (e.g., "LVR April 2026 report").
- **All events must have verified dates.** Cross-reference against official venue or ticketing sites. Do not rely on a single undated listicle.
- **Short text blocks.** Canva slides have limited space. Events get 3 lines each (name, date/time, venue). Bucket list items get 2-3 sentences. Market narrative gets 2 short paragraphs.

## Caption Rules

- Emojis are OK in the caption (sparingly, 1-2 per section)
- 5 standard hashtags: #LasVegas #VegasLife #LasVegasRealEstate #LasVegasEvents #VegasLocal
- Stats in the caption must match the housing market slide exactly
- Event names and dates in the caption must match the events slide exactly
