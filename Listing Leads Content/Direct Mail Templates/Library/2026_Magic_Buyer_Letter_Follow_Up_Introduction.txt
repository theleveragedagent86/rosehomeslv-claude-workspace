Introduction

The first Magic Buyer letter introduces the opportunity. This one makes people believe it.

Scott Steadman shared this Magic Letter follow-up letter with our community recently, and the reason it works is simple: it does what most agents never bother to do. It comes back. That second touch - a few weeks after the Magic Buyer Letter - is where trust gets built. The first letter planted a seed. This one waters it.

The copy is disarming on purpose. "Most mail doesn't make it to the kitchen counter" is the agent saying the quiet part out loud, and that honesty is exactly what makes the reader lean in instead of toss it. From there, the specificity does the heavy lifting: real client details, a narrowed list, a genuine reason you chose their home.
