Introduction
Renovation advice feels like consensus: upgrade everything before you list.


But that consensus is costing sellers real money. The truth is, certain upgrades drain budgets without moving offers. Over-the-top luxury kitchens in mid-tier neighborhoods typically recoup less than half their cost. Spa-level primary suites? Around 50% returns in most markets. Swimming pools? 25-50% at best. Then there's specialized additions and functional space conversions—garage-to-living-room projects that eliminate parking and shrink your buyer pool.


This postcard positions you as the agent who protects equity, not the one who cheerleads unnecessary spending.


It's sharp, direct, and data-backed. Perfect for homeowners who've been Googling renovation ideas or getting pressure from well-meaning friends. You're offering clarity before they make an expensive mistake, and inviting them into a pre-sale strategy conversation that positions you as a trusted financial advisor, not just a listing agent.
