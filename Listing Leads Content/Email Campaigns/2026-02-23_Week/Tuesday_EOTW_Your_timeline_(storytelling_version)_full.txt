Introduction

This is the storytelling version of this email. Use it when you have a real client story to drop in - the placeholder in the body shows you exactly where.

This morning, I threw out this theory in the ListingLeads Mastermind Group:

I have this feeling that clients are afraid to reach out because it’s “too early”

And it opened a floodgate

You probably feel this in conversations all the time - 

In the consumer’s mind, talking to an agent feels like a massive commitment.

We have to overcome that objection.

Here's what we're doing with this email:

1. Say what they're already thinking. The first three lines are the exact conversation happening in a homeowner's head right now. Say it before they do and the wall comes down.

2. Sit on the same side of the table. "Sometimes that plan is not to sell" tells them that you're not here to close them. It's no longer agent vs. homeowner. It's two people working together towards the solution.

If you’re not in their mailbox, inbox, or feeds - it’s really hard to win their business when the time comes.

The agent who shows up early gets the listing. Send this one and start planting those seeds.

Talking to a real estate agent right now might feel premature. 

You haven’t even decided if you are going to sell. 

And even if you were to list your home, it’s going to be a while before you put the home on the market. 

This is going to sound like counter-intuitive advice but this is the exact right time to talk to an agent.

In fact, [insert detail about recent listing appointment - i.e. I just met with a client who isn't going to put their home on the market until late spring.]

This allows us to make decisions without a deadline pressing down on us - to prepare the home intentionally, price it strategically, and go to market on our terms, not the market's.

The earlier I connect with my clients in the planning process, the better the plan we can develop.

And sometimes that plan is not to sell.

So if you're not in your forever home and just want to really understand what's happening in the market so you have a clear picture of your options - I'm here.
