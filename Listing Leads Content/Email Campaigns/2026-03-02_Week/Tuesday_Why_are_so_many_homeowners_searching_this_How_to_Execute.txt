This surprised me.

Searches for “Can’t sell my home” just hit a record high.

That usually means one thing:

A lot of sellers are about to learn a hard lesson.

Here’s what’s really happening:

1. One overpriced home can drag down the street.

When a neighbor lists high and cuts later, buyers assume everything nearby is negotiable. That affects you more than any Zestimate ever will.

2. Buyers aren’t gone. They’re cautious.

They’re scanning for risk. Homes that are properly prepared for the market get offers. You don’t want buyers being surprised at the last minute. 

3. Marketing creates leverage.

When more qualified buyers see a home early, sellers keep control. When exposure is weak, price becomes the only lever left.

Most homeowners don’t realize this until they’re already on the market.

Just sharing what we’re seeing right now.
