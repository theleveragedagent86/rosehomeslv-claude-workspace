Introduction

Searches for "Can't sell my home" just hit an all-time high. (S/O to BAM for sharing this on Instagram.)

Here's why that matters to you: if people are Googling it, your homeowners are thinking it. And the best way to overcome an objection is to address it before it ever comes up.

This email does exactly that. Instead of waiting for a seller to say "I'm worried my home won't sell," you're getting ahead of it - sharing what's actually causing homes to sit, and positioning yourself as the agent who sees problems before they start.

The tone is calm, not salesy. You're just "sharing what you're seeing right now." That's the kind of insight that builds trust long before someone's ready to list.

Copy

This surprised me.

Searches for “Can’t sell my home” just hit a record high.

That usually means one thing:

A lot of sellers are about to learn a hard lesson.

Here’s what’s really happening:

1. One overpriced home can drag down the street.

When a neighbor lists high and cuts later, buyers assume everything nearby is negotiable. That affects you more than any Zestimate ever will.

2. Buyers aren’t gone. They’re cautious.

They’re scanning for risk. Homes that are properly prepared for the market get offers. You don’t want buyers being surprised at the last minute. 

3. Marketing creates leverage.

When more qualified buyers see a home early, sellers keep control. When exposure is weak, price becomes the only lever left.

Most homeowners don’t realize this until they’re already on the market.

Just sharing what we’re seeing right now.




p.s. If you ever want to know how your home would perform before you list it, just reply to this email. A quick answer is better than guessing.
