Introduction

Homeowners don't know what they don't know.

Most people check Zillow once, decide they have a rough sense of their equity, and stop there. And for a long time, that felt good enough. But the gap between an algorithm's estimate and what a buyer will actually pay has been real - and in some cases, it's been significant.

This email doesn't explain why equity matters. It doesn't pitch a listing conversation. It just surfaces a question the homeowner probably hasn't thought to ask, and offers something specific before asking for anything in return.

That's the posture that opens doors.

With everything happening in the market right now, a lot of people I talk to have no idea what their home is actually worth.

Most homeowners settle for the online estimate and move on.

But the difference between what Zillow says and what your home actually worth has been bigger than most people expect - sometimes significantly.

Not everyone thinks to ask this, but I thought you might want to know.

It’s why I put together a proper equity update for my clients - actual comps, a full analysis of recent sales, and details that algorithms overlook.

It takes me some time to get it right, but there's no catch here.

Would it be helpful if I prepared one for your home?
