The hidden cost of overpricing your home.

I want to share a number with you that most agents will never mention, because talking about it feels uncomfortable.

12%

Nationally, homes that sit on the market for more than 60 days typically sell for roughly 12 percent less than comparable homes that were priced correctly out of the gate. Your listing was active for 107 days.

That doesn't mean you did anything wrong. It means the price signal the market got on day one set the tone for every showing that followed. Here is what usually happens when a home is priced even a touch above the comps:

  • Serious buyers skip past it because it's outside their saved search range
  • The buyers who do tour it use your home to make a lower priced listing look like a better deal
  • Each price reduction is read by buyers as "something must be wrong with the house"
  • By the time the price is right, the freshness and urgency are gone

The comps around you, Glennon, Epworth, McGinnis, and Maycott, sold in the $349,000 to $380,000 window. Your Zestimate sits at $392,500. There is a very narrow, very disciplined launch price that threads that needle and creates competition instead of hesitation. I'd love to show you where it is.
