Introduction
The hardest text to send is the one that asks for something. This one doesn't - and that's exactly why it works. It runs on two frameworks at once. The A.I.R. Method keeps it structured: the Alert is a listing that might be coming, the Insight is that it won't last long, and the Response never asks them to sell. It asks who they know. But layered underneath that is something from behavioral researcher Vanessa Van Edwards - one of three phrases proven to trigger instant likeability: "because you're so [positive label]." When you tell someone they're well connected, you're not flattering them. You're confirming how they already see themselves. That's a completely different thing. It removes the transaction and replaces it with respect.

Text Message 1
Copy

Hey [First Name] - it's [Agent Name].

I'm meeting with a client on [Day] who's thinking about putting their house on the market in [Neighborhood/Area].

I'm not sure where they'll land, but if they decide to list and price it right, I think it'll move fast.

I know you're not currently looking to make a move, but because you're so well connected - who do you know in your network that this might be worth sharing with?
