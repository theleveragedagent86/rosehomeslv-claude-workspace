Introduction
This text uses what behavioral profiler Chase Hughes calls the Lego principle - you place two pieces close together but don't connect them. The person reading it pushes them together on their own. And once an idea feels like theirs, they don't resist it. That's what's happening here. "A few things happening in the market" is one Lego. "More people should be paying attention" is the other. You never say what those things are. Their brain can't leave that alone - and the only way to close that loop is to reply. Send this to everyone who opened yesterday's email. They already leaned in once. This gives them a reason to lean in again.
