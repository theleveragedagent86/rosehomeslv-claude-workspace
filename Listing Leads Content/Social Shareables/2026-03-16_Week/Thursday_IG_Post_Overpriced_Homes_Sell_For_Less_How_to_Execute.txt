When I studied the sales in our market, the homes that sold closest to their asking price averaged about 12 days on market.

The homes that started 10% or more above market averaged 147 days. 

Same neighborhoods. Same buyers. Different pricing strategy.

And once a home sits, the psychology changes.

After about 30 days, buyers stop asking “How much is it?”

They start asking “What’s wrong with it?”

Which is why pricing isn’t really about the number.

It’s about the bracket.

Buyers don’t compare your home to your neighbor’s home.

They compare your home to the 10–12 other homes in the same price range they’re touring that weekend.

There’s a number where your home gets 15 showings in a weekend.

And there’s a number where it gets two.That gap is smaller than most sellers think.
