About

RESORT LIVING ON ONE LEVEL

The Bluffs II at Lake Las Vegas is the second phase of The Bluffs neighborhood by Century Communities. It sits inside the gated Lake Las Vegas resort masterplan in Henderson 89011, a 3,592 acre destination community built around a 320 acre man-made lake.

Every home at The Bluffs II is single story. You get to choose from three floor plans, 1,863 to 2,301 square feet, with 2 or 3 bedrooms and a 2-bay garage. Pricing starts at $512,990 and runs to $542,990 on the largest plan, with view-lot and quick move-in homes ranging from $575,000 to $665,740.

The lifestyle is the differentiator. As a Lake Las Vegas resident you get full master access to Reflection Bay golf, the marina, electric boat cruises, miles of trails (including the 34 mile River Mountains Loop), MonteLago Village shops and dining, and the resort's private white-sand beach. This guide walks you through the floor plans, true monthly cost, schools, fees, and the buying process so you can decide if The Bluffs II is the right fit.

Fees Explained

HOA, SID, AND LID FEES

Master HOA, Lake Las Vegas Master Association. Every home in Lake Las Vegas pays into the master HOA. Dues are $459 per quarter, which works out to $153 per month. The master fee covers lake maintenance, walking trails, landscaping, security patrol, and community events. The master association is run from 29 Grand Mediterra Blvd in Henderson.

Sub-HOA, The Bluffs. On top of the master, The Bluffs neighborhood has its own sub-HOA at about $244 per month per public Bluffs I listings. The Bluffs II sits under the same sub-association, so plan for a similar number. Combined, that puts most Bluffs II owners around $397 per month in HOA dues. Confirm the exact figure with the sales office at 702-789-6322 before you sign.

LID (Local Improvement District). Lake Las Vegas properties may carry an LID assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads and utilities. It shows up on your property tax bill. The amount varies by parcel. Del Webb at Lake Las Vegas, for reference, runs about $85 per month. Some Lake Las Vegas parcels have had LID balances paid off by builders, so always verify with the sales rep and pull an APN-specific lookup at amgnv.com or the City of Henderson Finance Department.

Transfer fee. Lake Las Vegas transfer fees range from $200 up to about 2% of purchase price depending on the neighborhood and timing. Bluffs-specific transfer fee is not yet posted on builder marketing. Verify at closing.

FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I need my own real estate agent at The Bluffs II?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Century Communities, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, inspections, and negotiations on extras.
Can I negotiate with the builder?
Builders rarely lower the base price because it affects comps for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate for closing cost credits, design center upgrades, or rate buydowns through the preferred lender. Always ask what is on the table when you visit, programs change month to month at Lake Las Vegas.
What upgrades are worth it at The Bluffs II?
Because Century follows an "Everything's Included" model, much of what other builders charge for is already standard. The upgrades that still make sense are anything structural (extended primary bath, garage cabinets, a third-bay or larger pad) and items that are hard to add later. View-lot premiums also matter at Lake Las Vegas, since the canyon and lake views are the main reason most buyers pay the Lake Las Vegas premium.
How long does it take to build a Bluffs II home?
Most to-be-built homes at The Bluffs II take about 5 to 7 months from contract to close. Quick move-in homes already under construction can be ready in 30 to 60 days, which is also where the strongest incentives often sit.
What is a SID or LID and does Lake Las Vegas have one?
A SID or LID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads and sewers. It shows up on your property tax bill and can run for 20+ years. Lake Las Vegas properties can carry an LID, but the amount varies by parcel. Always pull an APN-specific LID lookup at amgnv.com or City of Henderson Finance during your due diligence.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing and electrical, and 10 years on major structural components. Ask Century for the exact warranty document so you know what is covered.
Can I use VA or FHA at The Bluffs II?
Yes. VA and FHA loans are typically accepted on Century Communities homes. The current incentives may have different terms depending on loan type, so ask Inspire Home Loans about how each program stacks with the offer.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement will spell out how delays are handled and what your options are. Read this section carefully and ask your agent to explain anything that is unclear.
Is The Bluffs II a 55+ community?
No. The Bluffs II is all-ages. If you want a 55+ active adult community inside Lake Las Vegas with a private clubhouse and pickleball, look at Del Webb at Lake Las Vegas instead.
What schools does The Bluffs II feed?
Josh Stevens Elementary (K-5), B. Mahlon Brown Academy of International Studies (6-8, IB), and Basic Academy of International Studies (9-12, IB World School / magnet). Confirm zoning with CCSD before you sign, since assignments can change. Charter and private options like Coral Academy and Pinecrest Cadence are also nearby.
