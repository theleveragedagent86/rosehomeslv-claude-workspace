About
A GATED 47 HOME COMMUNITY IN 89121

Emerson Estates is a small, gated community of 47 single-family homes by Beazer Homes in East Las Vegas. The community sits on Regency Cove Court in the 89121 zip code, just steps from Las Vegas National Golf Course. It is in the final phase, so the lots remaining today are the lots that will close it out.

You get three Choice Plans on one street: a true single-story Zion at 2,191 sq ft, a two-story Valencia at 2,532 sq ft with a main-level guest suite, and an Everett at 2,735 sq ft with a small upper loft above a single-story footprint. Pricing starts at $549,990 and runs up to $615,990 on the Everett.

The big draw is energy efficiency. Every home includes owned rooftop solar at no upcharge, and Beazer markets up to $4,859 in annual savings from the system. Each home is also DOE Zero Energy Ready, ENERGY STAR certified, and Indoor airPLUS qualified, which is rare at this price point. Add a 10 to 15 minute drive to the Strip and the airport, and you have a lifestyle and energy play tucked inside an established part of town.

Fees
HOA, SID, AND LID EXPLAINED

HOA (Homeowners Association): Beazer has not published an HOA fee schedule for Emerson Estates in public sources. Because Emerson Estates is a small gated infill community of 47 homes, the HOA is likely modest. Most comparable gated infill communities in Las Vegas run $80 to $150 per month. The HOA at Emerson Estates funds gate maintenance, entry landscaping, and common-area upkeep. Call the sales office at 702-850-6015 to get the exact monthly amount and what it covers before you sign.

SID (Special Improvement District): A SID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads, sewers, and parks. SIDs show up as a separate line item on your property tax bill and can run 20 years or more. They are most common in greenfield master-planned communities. Emerson Estates is built on infrastructure that already existed in 89121, so it likely has no SID. Verify by parcel at the Clark County Treasurer or AMG parcel search before closing.

LID (Local Improvement District): A LID works like a SID but covers a smaller area. Ask the sales team for any disclosure of SID or LID assessments before you write an offer. Ryan can also help you pull this information during your due diligence period so there are no surprises after closing.

FAQ
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Do I need my own real estate agent at Emerson Estates?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Beazer, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, lender choice, inspections, and the punch list. Bring your agent on your first visit so the builder logs them as your representative.
Can I negotiate with Beazer?
You can negotiate, but not the way you negotiate on a resale home. Builders rarely cut base price because that hurts comps for the rest of the community. They will negotiate incentives instead, like closing cost credits, design center credits, or rate buydowns. The final phase is the best time to ask, since Beazer wants to close out the remaining lots.
What upgrades are worth it at Emerson Estates?
Spend on items that are hard or expensive to change later. Structural changes, electrical pre-wires, kitchen and bath layout upgrades, and any flooring change are worth doing at the design center. Hold off on light fixtures, paint, and window treatments since you can swap those after move-in for a fraction of design center pricing.
How long does it take to build a Beazer home?
Most new construction in Las Vegas takes 6 to 9 months from contract to closing. Beazer's Quick Move-In homes can close in 30 to 60 days, which is also where the strongest pricing often sits, especially in a final-phase community like Emerson Estates.
Does Emerson Estates have a SID or LID?
Likely no. A SID or LID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads and sewers. Emerson Estates was built on infrastructure that already existed in the 89121 zip, so a SID or LID is unlikely. Always verify by parcel at the Clark County Treasurer or AMG parcel search before you sign.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing, electrical, and HVAC, and 10 years on major structural components. Beazer follows this structure. Solar systems and major appliances carry their own manufacturer warranties. Keep all paperwork in one folder for the first year.
Can I use VA or FHA financing at Emerson Estates?
Yes. VA and FHA loans are typically accepted on Beazer homes. The Mortgage Choice program still applies, so you must compare loan estimates from at least two Choice Lenders. Quick Move-In homes are usually easier to close on VA and FHA than to-be-built homes.
What if my construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement will spell out builder remedies. They rarely include cash compensation. If you have a lock on your interest rate, talk to your lender early about an extension. Your agent can also push the builder to cover rate-lock extension fees on a meaningful delay.
Is the owned solar really worth $4,859 per year?
That is Beazer's marketed annual savings. Real savings depend on your usage, your NV Energy rate, and shading on your specific lot. Most Emerson Estates owners can expect a meaningful chunk of their monthly power bill offset by the system, since the home is all-electric and DOE Zero Energy Ready. The system is owned, not leased, so the value transfers with the home.
What schools does Emerson Estates feed?
Based on address, Harris Elementary, Orr Middle, and Chaparral High School. Confirm zoning with the CCSD Zone Lookup before you sign, since school assignments can change. Charter and magnet options are also available through open enrollment, and UNLV Preparatory Academy, Pinecrest Academy, and Las Vegas Day School are nearby private and charter alternatives.
