About
A SMALL INFILL COMMUNITY IN 89113

Ironwood is a small infill subdivision of just over 100 homes by Century Communities in Southwest Las Vegas. The community sits on Agate Avenue in the 89113 zip code, tucked between established neighborhoods, shopping, and easy freeway access.

Every home at Ironwood is two-story. You get to choose from four floor plans ranging from 1,684 to 2,114 square feet, with three to six bedrooms depending on the plan and options. Pricing starts at $495,990 and runs up to about $594,740 on the largest plan.

The location is one of the strongest things about Ironwood. You are about 2 miles from I-215, roughly 10 miles from the Las Vegas Strip, and minutes from The UnCommons, Durango Casino, and Mountain's Edge. This guide walks you through the floor plans, the true monthly cost, schools, incentives, and the buying process so you can decide if Ironwood is the right fit.

Fees
HOA, SID, AND LID EXPLAINED

HOA (Homeowners Association): Century Communities has not published an HOA fee schedule for Ironwood online. Because Ironwood is a small infill community, the HOA is likely modest, but you need confirmed numbers in writing before you sign. Call the sales office at 702-605-1504 or pull a title commitment to get the exact monthly amount and what it covers.

SID (Special Improvement District): A SID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads, sewers, and parks. SIDs show up as a separate line item on your property tax bill. The 89113 zip code is generally outside the SID-heavy zones, but you should still verify with the builder. If a SID exists, it can add $100 to $300+ per month and run for 20 years or more.

LID (Local Improvement District): A LID works like a SID but covers a smaller area. Ask the sales team for any disclosure of SID or LID assessments before you write an offer. Ryan can also help you pull this information during your due diligence period so there are no surprises after closing.

FAQ
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Do I need my own real estate agent at Ironwood?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Century Communities, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, inspections, and negotiations on extras.
Can I negotiate with the builder?
Builders rarely lower the base price because it affects comps for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate for closing cost credits, design center upgrades, or rate buy-downs through the preferred lender. The current $50K incentive is already strong, so always ask what is on the table when you visit.
What upgrades are worth it at Ironwood?
Because Century follows an "Everything's Included" model, much of what other builders charge for is already standard. The upgrades that still make sense are anything structural (extra room, expanded primary bath, garage cabinets) and items that are hard to add later. Cosmetic items like paint and lighting can be done after closing.
How long does it take to build a Century Communities home?
Most new construction in Las Vegas takes 6 to 9 months from contract to close. Quick move-in homes already under construction can be ready in 30 to 60 days, which is also where the strongest incentives often sit.
What is a SID or LID and does Ironwood have one?
A SID or LID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads and sewers. It shows up on your property tax bill and can run for 20+ years. The 89113 zip is generally outside the SID-heavy zones, but you should always verify with the builder before you sign.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing and electrical, and 10 years on major structural components. Ask Century for the exact warranty document so you know what is covered.
Can I use VA or FHA at Ironwood?
Yes. VA and FHA loans are typically accepted on Century Communities homes. The $50K incentive may have different terms depending on loan type, so ask Inspire Home Loans about how each program stacks with the offer.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement will spell out how delays are handled and what your options are. Read this section carefully and ask your agent to explain anything that is unclear.
Why is the $50K Inspire Home Loans incentive only on quick move-ins?
Builders use deeper incentives on standing inventory because those homes have already been built and they want to move them. Quick move-in homes give Century certainty on the closing date, so they pass more savings along to the buyer.
What schools does Ironwood feed?
Don and Dee Snyder Elementary, Lawrence and Heidi Canarelli Middle, and Sierra Vista High. Confirm zoning with CCSD before you sign, since school assignments can change. Charter and magnet options are also available through open enrollment.
