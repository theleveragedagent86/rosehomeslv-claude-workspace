## Best For Summary

**Ironwood by Century Communities** is best for buyers who want a brand-new two-story home in the $500K range with quick move-in availability and the largest builder incentive package in the area (up to $50K via Inspire Home Loans). Strong fit for first-time and move-up buyers prioritizing smart-home features and a low-maintenance infill location near The UnCommons and Durango Casino.

**Cambria Bay by D.R. Horton** is best for budget-conscious buyers who want the lowest entry price in the submarket. Smaller plan selection, but D.R. Horton's volume and frequent rate buydowns (3.99% common) make monthly payments aggressive.

**Triad Springs by Lennar** is best for larger families who want more square footage (2,670+) and the lifestyle perks of Mountain's Edge master-planned amenities, including Exploration Peak Park and the regional park system. Higher price point and HOA, but more home and community for the money.

**Reserves at Aven by KB Home** is best for buyers who want flexibility, including single-story options up to 3,066 sqft, large homesites, and KB Home's signature customization process. Brand-new community (Jan 2026 grand opening) means full plan selection and no resale competition yet.

**Landings at Aven by KB Home** is best for two-story buyers who want KB Home customization and ENERGY STAR efficiency at an entry-level price point in a brand-new community. Good fit for families who value picking finishes and floor plan structure over move-in speed.

## Key Takeaways

- **Ironwood's strongest advantage is incentive size and inventory.** Up to $50K in builder concessions via Inspire Home Loans plus 6 active quick move-in homes is the most aggressive package in this comparison set.
- **Ironwood is priced at the top of its size class.** At ~$254-$295/sqft on 1,684-2,114 sqft homes, it sits slightly above 89113 resale ($271/sqft) and competes directly with Cambria Bay's lower entry price ($472K).
- **No master-planned amenities is a real trade-off.** Triad Springs offers Mountain's Edge access (trails, regional park, Exploration Peak) for similar money on larger homes; buyers who want lifestyle infrastructure may prefer that route.
- **Ironwood beats KB Home's Aven communities on timing.** Reserves and Landings at Aven don't open until January 2026, so Ironwood is the only option in this set for buyers needing to close before year-end.
- **School ratings are middle-of-the-road across the entire comparison set.** Snyder Elementary (5/10) and Canarelli Middle (6/10) are average; Sierra Vista High (3/5) is below average.
