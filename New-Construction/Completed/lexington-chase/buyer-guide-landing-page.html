About

Fees Explained

HOA, SID, AND LID FEES

HOA (Homeowners Association). Richmond American has not yet published the HOA fee schedule for Lexington Chase. Because it is a small 76-lot subdivision with no internal pool or clubhouse, the HOA is likely modest. Expect somewhere in the $60 to $90 per month range, similar to other small Enterprise subdivisions. Call the sales office at 725-400-0550 or pull a title commitment to get the exact monthly amount and what it covers before you sign.

SID (Special Improvement District). A SID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads, sewers, and parks. SIDs show up as a separate line item on your property tax bill and can run for 20 years or more. The Enterprise corridor likely applies a SID at Lexington Chase, with annual amounts estimated at $500 to $2,000. The Clark County Treasurer parcel lookup will give you the exact number once an APN is assigned.

LID (Local Improvement District). A LID works like a SID but covers a smaller area. Ask the sales team for any disclosure of SID or LID assessments before you write an offer. Ryan can also help you pull this information during your due diligence period so there are no surprises after closing.

FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I need my own real estate agent at Lexington Chase?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Richmond American, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, design center, inspections, and negotiations on extras.
Can I negotiate with Richmond American?
Builders rarely lower the base price because it affects comps for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate for closing cost credits, design center upgrades, or rate buydowns through HomeAmerican Mortgage. The current closing-cost incentive is the headline offer right now, so always ask what is on the table when you visit.
What upgrades are worth it at Lexington Chase?
Focus on structural upgrades that are hard to add later: extra bedrooms, expanded primary baths, kitchen island extensions, garage cabinets, and electrical or plumbing rough-ins. Cosmetic items like paint, lighting, and basic fixtures can be done after closing for less money.
How long does it take to build a Richmond American home?
Most new construction in Las Vegas takes 6 to 9 months from contract to close. Because Lexington Chase just opened in January 2026, most homes will be to-be-built. If a quick move-in becomes available it can usually close in 30 to 60 days.
What is a SID or LID and does Lexington Chase have one?
A SID or LID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads and sewers. It shows up on your property tax bill and can run for 20+ years. The Enterprise corridor likely applies a SID at Lexington Chase, estimated at $500 to $2,000 per year. Verify the exact amount via the Clark County Treasurer parcel lookup once an APN is assigned.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing and electrical, and 10 years on major structural components. Ask Richmond American for the exact warranty document so you know what is covered.
Can I use VA or FHA at Lexington Chase?
Yes. VA and FHA loans are typically accepted on Richmond American homes. The closing-cost incentive may have different terms depending on loan type, so ask HomeAmerican Mortgage how each program stacks with the offer.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement spells out how delays are handled and what your options are. Read this section carefully and ask Ryan to explain anything that is unclear.
What schools does Lexington Chase feed?
Sierra Vista High School is the confirmed feeder. Elementary is likely Vassiliadis (8/10 GreatSchools rating) and middle is likely Faiss or Fertitta. Confirm zoning with CCSD before you sign, since school assignments can change. Charter and magnet options are also available through open enrollment.
How does Lexington Chase compare to Mountain's Edge?
Mountain's Edge is a 14,500-home master-planned community with full amenities (parks, trails, retail) and layered HOA fees. Lexington Chase is a small 76-lot subdivision with no on-site amenities and a lower HOA load. If you want a quiet street and lower fees, Lexington Chase wins. If you want full master-planned lifestyle, Mountain's Edge has more.
