About

A GATED TOWNHOME COMMUNITY IN 89106

Rosa Point is a small gated townhome community by Beazer Homes in northwest Las Vegas. The community sits on Hydrangea Place near North Rancho Drive and West Coran Lane, tucked into the established 89106 zip code with quick access to US-95 and I-15.

You get to choose from two two-story floor plans. The Albany at 1,485 square feet and the Cambridge at 1,851 square feet, each with three bedrooms, 2.5 baths, and a rear-entry two-car garage. Pricing starts at $358,990 on the Albany and runs to $393,990+ on the Cambridge.

What makes Rosa Point different is the standard solar package. Every home includes Beazer's Energy Series READY system with rooftop solar, ENERGY STAR certification, DOE Zero Energy Ready certification, Indoor airPLUS, and one EV pre-wire. This guide walks you through pricing, the true monthly cost, schools, the $20K incentive, and the buying process so you can decide if Rosa Point is the right fit.

Fees Explained

HOA, SID, AND LID FEES

HOA (Homeowners Association). Beazer has not published the Rosa Point HOA fee schedule online. Based on similar gated townhome communities in the valley, the monthly HOA likely lands somewhere between $150 and $250 per month and typically covers common-area landscaping, gate maintenance, and exterior building upkeep. Call the sales office at 702-329-6077 or text Ryan to get the exact monthly amount and the CC&Rs in writing before you sign.

SID (Special Improvement District). A SID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads, sewers, and parks. SIDs show up as a separate line item on your property tax bill. The 89106 zip code is a mature, fully developed area, so a Rosa Point SID is not expected. Still, always confirm in writing with the builder before you write an offer.

LID (Local Improvement District). A LID works like a SID but covers a smaller area. Ask the sales team for any disclosure of SID or LID assessments and request the recorded plat map. Ryan can also help you pull this information during your due diligence period so there are no surprises after closing.

FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I need my own real estate agent at Rosa Point?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Beazer Homes, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, inspections, and negotiations on extras and incentives.
Can I negotiate with Beazer?
Builders rarely lower the base price because it affects comps for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate for closing cost credits, design upgrades, or rate buy-downs. The current $20K YOUR CHOICE incentive is already strong, so always ask what is on the table when you visit and whether it can be combined with quick move-in pricing.
What upgrades are worth it at Rosa Point?
Solar, ENERGY STAR construction, and Indoor airPLUS are already standard, so you are not paying extra for those. The upgrades that still make sense are anything structural like extra storage or built-ins, plus items that are hard to add later such as garage cabinets and second EV charging circuits. Cosmetic items like paint and lighting can usually be done after closing for less.
How long does it take to build a Beazer townhome?
Most new construction at Rosa Point takes 6 to 9 months from contract to close. Quick move-in homes already under construction can be ready in 30 to 60 days, which is also where the strongest incentives often sit.
What is YOUR CHOICE Savings?
YOUR CHOICE is Beazer's flexible incentive program. Instead of locking the savings into one bucket, you decide how to apply it. The most common options are rate buy-down, closing cost credits, design center upgrades, or a price reduction. Ask Ryan to model each scenario before you sign so you can see which one saves you the most over time.
What does the Beazer 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing and electrical, and 10 years on major structural components. Ask Beazer for the exact warranty document so you know what is covered and what is excluded.
Can I use VA or FHA at Rosa Point?
Yes. VA and FHA loans are typically accepted on Beazer Homes, and the YOUR CHOICE incentive can usually be paired with both. The exact terms can vary by loan type, so ask the lender how the incentive stacks with VA or FHA before you commit.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement will spell out how delays are handled and what your options are. Read this section carefully and ask your agent to explain anything that is unclear before you sign.
Is the HOA at Rosa Point too high?
The Rosa Point HOA has not been published, but a $150 to $250 monthly estimate is in line with similar gated townhome communities in the Las Vegas valley. The HOA typically covers gate maintenance, common-area landscaping, and exterior building upkeep, which is meaningful when you live in a townhome. Ask for the exact figure and the CC&Rs in writing before you sign.
What is Hylo Park and why does it matter?
Hylo Park is a major mixed-use redevelopment right next to Rosa Point. The first phases include a 40,000 square foot Cardenas grocery store opening in 2026, restaurants, and the Hylo Park Arena ice rink. There is also a planned youth sports facility being marketed as an "Olympic village" that will draw tournaments. As Hylo Park comes online, walkability and property values in 89106 should both move in your favor.
