About

A SMALL POCKET INSIDE A BIG MASTER PLAN

Sierra at Skyeview is a 31 home cluster collection by Century Communities. It sits inside Skyeview, one of the newer neighborhood pockets within Skye Canyon. Skye Canyon is a 1,700 acre master planned community in Northwest Las Vegas.

The cluster layout is the key thing to understand here. Homes share a private drive instead of sitting on a typical street. That cuts down on yard work and front of home maintenance. It also keeps the neighborhood quiet, since only a small group of homeowners share each drive.

Even though Sierra itself is small, every home gets full access to Skye Canyon amenities. That includes the Skye Center clubhouse, the Skye Fitness gym with a junior Olympic pool, four community parks, miles of trails, pickleball, and dog parks. You get the feel of a tight knit boutique neighborhood plus the perks of a big master plan.

Sierra is built for first time move up buyers, small families, and California relocators who want new construction under $480,000 in a fast growing part of Las Vegas. The 22 to 25 mile drive to the Strip and the cooler 3,000 foot elevation are common reasons buyers choose this side of town.

Fees Explained

HOA, SID, AND LID FEES

HOA (Homeowners Association). Skye Canyon has two layers of fees. The master HOA is $83 per month, paid by every Skye Canyon home. That covers the Skye Center clubhouse, Skye Fitness gym, four community parks, miles of trails, pickleball courts, and community events. $83 a month is one of the lowest master HOA fees among Las Vegas master plans with this much amenity load. On top of that, Sierra has its own sub-HOA. The exact dollar amount is not yet published, but based on similar cluster home products in Las Vegas, expect $80 to $150 per month additional. Verify the exact number at the sales office before signing.

SID (Special Improvement District). Skye Canyon is well known for SIDs, which funded the original infrastructure for the master plan, things like roads, sewer, and water lines. Sierra falls inside the Skye Canyon SID range of $2,400 to $4,800 per year. The SID is billed twice a year on your property tax statement and lasts 10 to 20 years. You can prepay it with a 3% prepayment penalty, which is standard in Nevada.

LID (Local Improvement District). A LID works like a SID but covers a smaller area. Skye Canyon's primary assessment is a SID. Always ask the sales team for any disclosure of SID or LID assessments before you write an offer. One important note: SIDs transfer with the home and CAN trigger foreclosure if unpaid, since they are billed with property taxes. Make sure your lender accounts for the SID when calculating your monthly escrow. You can also call AMG at 702-796-0082 with the parcel number to confirm the exact amount.

A few weeks before closing, walk the home with the builder and mark every cosmetic issue with blue tape. The builder fixes the punch list before close.
08

Pay for an independent final inspection at $310 to $730. Do not skip this. Inspectors regularly catch HVAC, roof, and code issues that the builder warranty will then cover for free.

Sign the closing documents, get the keys, and start your 1-year fit-and-finish warranty. The 10-year structural warranty kicks in at the same time. Welcome home.
FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I need my own real estate agent at Sierra?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Century Communities, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, design center decisions, inspections, and negotiations. The builder must know your agent on your first visit, so call before you stop by.
Can I negotiate with Century Communities?
Base price rarely moves on new construction because it affects comps for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate on incentives, design center credits, lot premiums, and closing costs. Quick move-in inventory homes are the most negotiable, especially as build dates approach.
What upgrades are worth it at Sierra?
The general rule: spend on items that are hard or expensive to change later. That means flooring, cabinets, kitchen layout, and electrical pre-wires. Because Century follows an "Everything's Included" model, much of what other builders charge for is already standard. Skip cosmetic upgrades like backsplash, paint, and light fixtures. Those are easy DIY swaps after move in.
How long does it take to build a Sierra home?
Plan on 4 to 7 months from contract to closing for a to-be-built home. Quick move-in inventory homes can close in 30 to 60 days. Weather, supply chain, and trade availability can shift these timelines.
What is a SID and how much will it cost me?
SID stands for Special Improvement District. It is a tax assessment that funded the original infrastructure (roads, sewer, water) for the master plan. Skye Canyon SIDs run $2,400 to $4,800 per year, billed twice a year on your property tax statement and lasting 10 to 20 years. The exact Sierra amount can be confirmed at the sales office or by calling AMG at 702-796-0082 with the parcel number.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Century offers a standard new home warranty: 1 year on fit and finish (paint, drywall, trim), 2 years on systems (plumbing, HVAC, electrical), and 10 years on major structural components. Keep your blue tape walkthrough list for any 1-year warranty claims.
Can I use VA or FHA at Sierra?
Yes. Century currently offers FHA and VA buydown rates as low as 3.75% on select homes through Inspire Home Loans. Both loan types are eligible, and you can layer the closing cost credit on top.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen. The contract usually allows the builder a window of extra time before they owe you anything. If your rate lock expires, you may need to relock at a new rate. Build a buffer into your move-out plans and review the delay clauses in the contract before signing.
How does Sierra differ from Terra at Skyeview?
Sierra is the smaller, more affordable Century product at Skyeview. 31 homes, two story plans 1,602 to 1,965 sq ft, base prices $433K to $479K, with a shared private drive cluster layout. Terra is the larger product: 196 homes, plans 1,792 to 2,119 sq ft, base $489K to $565K, on traditional street fronted lots. Same builder, same master amenities, same school zoning.
Is Sierra a good value compared to resale?
Sierra base pricing runs $265 to $271 per square foot. Skye Canyon resale comps in the same size range are trading $245 to $280 per square foot. New construction usually carries a small premium for the warranty, the energy efficiency, and the move-in-ready condition. For Sierra, the gap is small enough that new construction often wins on total cost of ownership.
