## RESEARCH REPORT: Sierra at Skyeview

### Community Overview

- **Official community name:** Sierra at Skyeview (also marketed as "Skyeview – Sierra")
- **Builder:** Century Communities (single builder)
- **Location:** 8925 Vanhoy Creek St., Las Vegas, NV 89166
- **Master plan:** Skye Canyon (1,700-acre master-planned community) — Skyeview neighborhood
- **City/county:** City of Las Vegas, Clark County, Nevada (NW Las Vegas)
- **Cross streets:** Off US-95 at Skye Canyon Park Drive; Vanhoy Creek St. is inside the Skyeview district. Adjacent collections (Terra at 8592 Vanhoy Creek, Mesa at 8544 Vanhoy Creek, Arroyo at 8912 Vanhoy Creek) are clustered in the same Skyeview pocket.
- **Total planned homes in Sierra:** 31 homes (per homes.com / Zillow community data)
- **Phase:** Active-selling, mid-buildout (6 quick move-in inventory homes shown with June completion dates, additional lots still releasing)
- **Year opened / first sales:** Skyeview at Skye Canyon openings began rolling out 2024–2025; Sierra is currently selling in 2026
- **Estimated buildout date:** NOT FOUND (small 31-home count suggests 2026–2027 buildout)
- **Community type:** Cluster-home / shared-private-drive collection within a master-planned community. NOT 55+, NOT gated separately from the Skyeview district.
- **What makes Sierra distinct from Terra at Skyeview:**
  - Sierra: 31 homes total, smaller two-story plans 1,602–1,965 sqft, base prices $433,990–$478,990, shared private-drive cluster layout (lower-maintenance lifestyle, smaller lots).
  - Terra: 196 homes total, larger two-story plans 1,792–2,119 sqft, base prices $489,990–$519,990, traditional street-fronted lots.
  - Sierra is the most affordable Century single-family entry point at Skyeview (only Arroyo townhomes are cheaper).

### SID/LID & Special Assessments

- **SID present:** YES — Skye Canyon is well-known for SIDs funding original infrastructure buildout
- **Annual range across Skye Canyon:** $2,400–$4,800/year ($200–$400/month equivalent). Some sources cite $600–$1,800/year for older phases. Sierra-specific SID amount: NOT FOUND.
- **Duration:** Typical Las Vegas SIDs run 10–20 years, billed semi-annually
- **Prepayment:** Available with 3% prepayment penalty (standard NV)
- **AB 540 relief:** $50M state allocation in process to help reduce SID/LID assessments
- **How buyers can verify exact amount:** Call Assessment Management Group (AMG) at 702-796-0082 with the parcel number, or check the title commitment during escrow.
- **Important note:** SIDs transfer automatically with the home and CAN result in foreclosure if unpaid. Billed on the property tax statement.

- **Terra at Skyeview:** Best for buyers who want a slightly larger Century home on a traditional street-fronted lot in the same Skyeview district. Roughly $50K more than Sierra in exchange for more square footage and standard lot privacy.

- **Mesa at Skyeview:** Best for downsizers, empty-nesters, and accessibility-minded buyers who specifically want a single-story floor plan inside Skye Canyon. Higher price per square foot reflects the single-story premium.

- **Arroyo at Skyeview:** Best for first-time buyers and budget-conscious investors who want the lowest entry price in Skyeview. Townhome product trades private yard space for an attached, lock-and-leave lifestyle starting in the $370Ks.

- **Pinewood at Skye Canyon:** Best for move-up buyers and growing families who want a gated address, larger Pulte floor plans, and master-down living options. Pricing runs $100K or more above Sierra.

- **Topaz at Skye Canyon:** Best for buyers who want LGI's everything-included pricing model at a similar entry point to Arroyo. Strong fit for first-time buyers who value upgrades baked into the base price and a brand-new April 2026 opening.

### Key Takeaways

- Sierra is the lowest-priced single-family new construction inside Skye Canyon. At a $433,990 base, it undercuts Terra by roughly $56K, Mesa by $51K, and Pinewood by $46K+ while delivering the same master-plan amenities and school zoning.
- Boutique 31-home scale is a real differentiator. Buyers who feel lost in 196-home Terra or larger Pulte tracts get a tighter, quieter neighborhood feel without giving up Skye Center, Skye Fitness, or trail access.
- Cluster-home shared private-drive layout means lower exterior maintenance. This is the strongest pitch versus Terra and Pinewood for buyers who want new construction without the yard work, but it is the biggest tradeoff for buyers who want a fully private lot.
- Townhome competitors (Arroyo, Topaz) win on pure price. Sierra cannot beat $373K-$380K entry points, so the pitch against townhomes is detached single-family product, two-story flexibility up to 5 bedrooms, and resale upside on a true SFR.
- Mesa is the closest direct threat for older buyers. Sierra is two-story only, so any buyer who needs single-story will lean Mesa. Sierra wins on price and square footage but loses on accessibility.
