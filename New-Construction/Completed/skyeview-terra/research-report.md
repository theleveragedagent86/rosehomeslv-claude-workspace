### Best For Summary

**SkyeView Terra** is best for families who want a new two-story home with solid square footage (up to 2,119 sq ft) at a competitive price in an established master plan. The 3.375% flash rate and $3,000 closing credit make the financing attractive. With 5 quick move-in homes available, buyers who need to move fast have options. The sweet spot is the Residence 2119 at $519,990, which offers 5 bedrooms and 3 baths, making it one of the best values per bedroom in the Skyeview collection.

**SkyeView Mesa** is best for buyers who prioritize single-story living and want to stay in Skye Canyon. The one-story floor plans (1,635 to 1,816 sq ft) appeal to empty nesters, retirees, or anyone who wants to avoid stairs. However, the higher price per square foot (~$290) and smaller bedroom counts (2-3 bedrooms) make it less ideal for larger families.

**SkyeView Sierra** is best for buyers who want a two-story layout similar to Terra but at a lower entry price. Starting at $443,990, Sierra offers an accessible path into Skye Canyon. The Residence 1965 plan provides nearly 2,000 sq ft of living space. Buyers looking for the most affordable two-story option in Skyeview should start here.

**Arete at Kyle Canyon** is best for buyers who want a wider selection of floor plans (5 options) and the flexibility KB Home offers through its personalization studio. With homes up to 2,469 sq ft and a lower HOA (~$75/month), this community appeals to families who want more space for less monthly overhead. The newer Kyle Canyon Gateway master plan is still developing its amenities, so buyers should be comfortable with a community that is still growing.

**SkyeView Arroyo** is best for first-time buyers or budget-conscious buyers who want to be in Skye Canyon without paying single-family prices. Townhomes starting in the mid $300s offer the lowest entry point in the Skyeview collection. The trade-off is shared walls and smaller floor plans (1,531-1,729 sq ft), but buyers still get full access to Skye Canyon amenities.

### Key Takeaways

- **SkyeView Terra offers the best balance** of size, bedroom count, and price among the Skyeview collections. At ~$254/sq ft, it is more affordable per square foot than Mesa (~$290/sq ft) while offering more bedrooms and larger plans.
- **The 3.375% flash rate incentive** is a significant advantage shared across all Skyeview collections, making Century Communities competitive against KB Home and other builders that may not match this rate.
- **Arete at Kyle Canyon is the strongest outside competitor**, offering more floor plan variety, larger max square footage (2,469 vs 2,119), and a lower monthly HOA. However, it lacks Skye Canyon's established amenities and on-site retail.
- **Skye Canyon's mature amenity package** (clubhouse, pools, Smith's Marketplace, extensive trail system) gives all Skyeview collections a lifestyle edge over newer, still-developing master plans like Kyle Canyon Gateway.
- **Terra's 5 quick move-in homes** are a differentiator for buyers on a timeline, as not all competing communities have move-in-ready inventory available right now.
