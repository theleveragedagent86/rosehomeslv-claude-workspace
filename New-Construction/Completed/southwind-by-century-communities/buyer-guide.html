About
A QUIET CORNER OF SOUTHWEST LAS VEGAS

Southwind is a brand-new community by Century Communities. It sits at 9809 Lost Horse Avenue in southwest Las Vegas, just south of the Mountain's Edge master plan. The community opened in June 2024 and is now in Phase 3 of its build out.

Southwind keeps things simple. There are three two-story floor plans, all with two-car garages, ranging from 2,054 to 2,605 square feet. Base prices start at $510,990 and go up to $543,990. The HOA is just $69 per month, which is one of the lowest in the entire southwest valley.

This community is a strong fit for first-time buyers, growing families, and anyone moving up from a townhome or condo. You get a new home, a top-rated elementary school nearby, and easy access to I-15 and the Strip. If you want a no-fuss new build at a fair price, Southwind belongs on your short list.

Fees
HOA, SID, AND LID EXPLAINED

HOA (Homeowners Association): The Southwind HOA is $69 per month. That is unusually low for southwest Las Vegas. Most newer communities in this area charge between $100 and $200 per month. The dues cover basic common area landscaping and community upkeep. There is no on-site pool or clubhouse, which is why the dues stay low.

SID (Special Improvement District): A SID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads, sewers, and parks. SIDs show up as a separate line item on your property tax bill. Based on the most recent closing record we pulled inside Southwind, no SID was itemized. That said, this is not a confirmed builder disclosure. Always ask the sales office for a written SID statement before you sign.

LID (Local Improvement District): A LID works the same way as a SID but covers a smaller area. We have not found a LID at Southwind. Adjacent Mountain's Edge does have a SID that funded the regional parks. Confirm Southwind sits outside that boundary with the sales office or by pulling the parcel record from the Clark County Treasurer.

Bottom line: at $69 per month with no confirmed SID, Southwind has one of the lowest carrying costs you will find in a new-construction community in this part of town.

The builder gives regular updates and milestone walkthroughs. Stay engaged, take photos, and ask questions. Most homes take 6 to 10 months to build.
07

A few weeks before closing, walk the home with a builder rep and put blue tape on anything that needs to be fixed. Be picky here.
08

Hire your inspector again for a final pre-closing inspection. Plan to spend $310 to $730. Better to find problems now than after closing.
09
CLOSE AND MOVE IN
Sign your final paperwork, get your keys, and move in. Your builder warranty starts the day you close, so save every document.
FAQ
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Do I need my own real estate agent?
Yes, you should bring one. The builder pays the agent commission, so it costs you nothing. Your agent reviews the contract, helps you negotiate upgrades, and protects you during inspections. Just make sure your agent is with you on your very first visit to the model home.
Can I negotiate with the builder?
Builders rarely cut the base price because it lowers values for the whole community. You can usually negotiate upgrades, design center credits, or extra closing cost help. The current incentive package at Southwind is already strong, so use it as a starting point.
Are all three floor plans two-story?
Yes. Every plan at Southwind is a two-story design with a 2-car garage. There are no single-story or three-story options at this community.
What upgrades are worth it?
The best upgrades are the ones that are hard or expensive to add later. Think electrical and structural items like extra outlets, ceiling fans, pre-wiring for surround sound, taller cabinets, and rough plumbing for a future sink. Cosmetic upgrades like flooring and paint can often be done after move-in for less.
How long does it take to build?
Most homes at Southwind take 6 to 10 months from contract to close. Quick Move-In homes that are already under construction can close in as little as 30 to 60 days.
Can I use VA or FHA financing?
Yes. Century Communities currently offers a 3.875% Year 1 rate on FHA and VA loans, with 4.875% in Years 2 through 30 through Inspire Home Loans. Confirm current terms with the sales office.
What is the warranty?
Century Communities provides a limited new home warranty. Workmanship and materials are typically covered for 1 year, mechanical systems for 2 years, and major structural elements for up to 10 years. Always ask for the full warranty in writing.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen. Weather, supply chain, and labor can all push a closing back. Your contract spells out what the builder must do if there is a delay. Read this section carefully and ask your agent to flag any one-sided language before you sign.
Is there a SID at Southwind?
Based on the most recent closing record we pulled, no SID was itemized on the property tax bill. This is not a confirmed builder disclosure. Always request a written SID and LID statement from the sales office before you sign.
What does the $69 HOA cover?
The HOA covers common area landscaping and basic community upkeep. There is no on-site pool or clubhouse. The exact list of covered items will be in the HOA documents you receive at contract. Ask for the CC&Rs and budget early so you know exactly what you are paying for.
