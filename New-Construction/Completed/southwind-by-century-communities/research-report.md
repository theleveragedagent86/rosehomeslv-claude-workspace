### Best For Summary

**Southwind by Century Communities** is best for entry-level to first-move-up family buyers who want a true two-story floor plan in the $510K-$544K range with a low HOA ($69/mo), strong builder incentives (3.875% 30-yr fixed, closing credits, 2-1 buydown), and access to Tyrone Thompson Elementary (Niche A−). The simplified three-floor-plan lineup makes the buying decision easier, and the mid-2024 opening means buyers can still shape Phase 3.

**Keystone by Beazer Homes** is best for energy-conscious buyers willing to pay slightly more (~$519K-$579K) in exchange for DOE Zero Energy Ready certification and an included owned solar system that delivers up to $5,678/year in projected savings.

**Stonehaven and Arborbrook by Richmond American** is best for move-up buyers who have outgrown a 2,000-2,600 sqft footprint and want larger homes (up to 3,270 sqft), guest suites, 3-car garages, or a three-story Cityscape plan. Pricing starts around $580K and scales up to $730K.

**Triad Springs - Paradise Park by Lennar** is best for buyers loyal to Lennar's "Everything's Included" model who want upgraded finishes baked into the base price without negotiating options. At ~$580K starting, it sits above Southwind's base pricing.

**Spectrum Bay by D.R. Horton** is best for first-time buyers willing to accept a smaller home (1,715-1,865 sqft) for a lower price point and D.R. Horton's smart-home tech package. Inventory is largely sold out.

### Key Takeaways

- **Southwind has the strongest entry pricing in the immediate submarket.** It slots in below Keystone, Stonehaven & Arborbrook, and Triad Springs while still offering 2,054-2,605 sqft two-story homes.
- **The $69/mo HOA is a real differentiator.** Most competing master-planned-style communities in southwest LV charge $100-$200+/mo. Over a 30-year hold this is a meaningful lifetime savings vs. comps.
- **Schools are a Southwind strength**: Tyrone Thompson Elementary (Niche A−, top 10% in NV) gives a stronger feeder than is typical for this price band.
- **Southwind's incentive package is the most aggressive currently advertised** — 3.875% (4.827% APR) 30-yr fixed via Inspire Home Loans, $3K closing credit, 2-1 buydowns, and FHA/VA Year-1 stack better than Keystone's solar story or Lennar's "Everything's Included" upgrade model.
- **Trade-offs vs. competition**: No on-site pool/clubhouse, only three floor plans, and Sierra Vista High School ratings are softer. Buyers wanting larger homes (>2,605 sqft), 3-car garages, or guest suites should look at Stonehaven & Arborbrook instead.
