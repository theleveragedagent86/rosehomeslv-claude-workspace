Let's run real numbers. Take Residence 2308 at five hundred twenty-five thousand nine hundred ninety. Twenty percent down would be about a hundred and five thousand. That leaves a loan of around four hundred twenty thousand. At the Century 3.875 percent thirty-year fixed rate, your principal and interest payment is about nineteen hundred seventy-five dollars a month. Add the HOA at sixty-nine dollars. Add property tax at about ninety-three dollars a month, based on the eleven hundred eleven dollar annual figure from a recent comp closing. Add homeowners insurance, figure roughly a hundred twenty-five dollars a month for this size home in Vegas.

Your total monthly is around twenty-two hundred sixty dollars. If you put less down, the payment goes up and you add mortgage insurance. If you take a 2-1 buydown, your year-one and year-two payments will be lower. Always ask the sales office for a written cost worksheet.

The HOA is sixty-nine dollars a month, confirmed from the closing record on 9969 Ruby Dome Avenue. That is a strong number for southwest Las Vegas. Southwind appears to sit just outside the Mountain's Edge boundary, and the comp closing record only showed eleven hundred eleven dollars in annual property tax with no separate SID line itemized. That said, ask the sales office for a written SID and LID disclosure on the exact lot you are buying, and verify it on the Clark County Treasurer's parcel lookup before you go under contract.

The elementary school is Tyrone Thompson Elementary at 10250 South El Capitan Way. Niche grade A minus, top ten percent of elementary schools in Nevada. About a mile and a half from the community.

The middle school is Barry and June Gunderson Middle, about three miles away. A quick note here: some Sierra Vista feeder lists show Faiss or Canarelli middle instead, so verify your exact zone with the Clark County School District zone lookup before you commit.

The high school is Sierra Vista High, about three and a half miles away. SchoolDigger gives it a three out of five. Niche gives it a B minus. GreatSchools rates it four out of ten. Solid, not spectacular. There are also charter options nearby like Pinecrest Academy and Coral Academy of Science.

SECTION 7: Amenities and Lifestyle (45 seconds)

There is no on-site pool or clubhouse at Southwind. That is the trade-off for the low HOA. What you get instead is access to two regional parks and a big trail network right next door. Exploration Peak Park has a playground, hiking trails, and Native American cultural elements. Mountain's Edge Regional Park is also close. The Mountain's Edge bike and hike trail network is one of the best amenities in the whole southwest valley.

For shopping you have Mountain's Edge Marketplace and Blue Diamond Crossing within a five-mile drive. Smith's, Albertsons, Vons, and Trader Joe's are all on the Blue Diamond and Rainbow corridor. Southern Hills Hospital is about ten minutes away via Durango and the 215. Red Rock Canyon is a quick drive west if you like to hike.

Here is the current incentive stack from Century, and it is one of the better ones in the valley right now.

3.875 percent thirty-year fixed rate, 4.827 APR, through Inspire Home Loans. Three thousand dollars in closing cost credits. A 2-1 buydown, which means your year-one rate is even lower, then year two is in the middle, then it settles into the permanent rate in year three. Let me put Southwind next to four neighbors so you can decide what fits.

Keystone by Beazer is in the same zip code, 89178. Price band is about five hundred nineteen to five hundred seventy-nine thousand. Beazer's selling point is owned solar with DOE Zero Energy Ready certification. Beazer projects up to five thousand six hundred seventy-eight dollars a year in energy savings. If your priority is utility bills, Keystone is worth a look.

Stonehaven and Arborbrook by Richmond American is on the same street as Southwind, on West Meranto Avenue. Pricing runs five hundred eighty thousand to seven hundred thirty thousand. Larger homes up to 3,270 square feet. Three-car garages. Even a three-story Cityscape plan. This is your move-up choice if you have outgrown the Southwind footprint.

Triad Springs Paradise Park by Lennar is also off Blue Diamond. Pricing starts around five hundred eighty thousand. Lennar uses an Everything's Included model where upgrades are baked into the base price.

Spectrum Bay by D.R. Horton is southwest Las Vegas, two-story homes from 1,715 to 1,865 square feet. Smaller and more affordable, but it is largely sold out.

Bottom line, Southwind has the most aggressive entry pricing and the lowest HOA in this little submarket. That is the case for buying here.

Three mistakes I see buyers make at communities like this one.

Mistake three. Skipping the SID and LID disclosure. You do not want to find out at closing that there is a special assessment you did not budget for. Ask in writing.
