About
SINGLE-STORY LIVING INSIDE LAKE LAS VEGAS

The Bluffs I by Century Communities is a 90-home single-story new construction community tucked inside the guard-gated Lake Las Vegas master plan in Henderson. The community opened in March 2023 and continues to release homesites in phases, with quick move-in homes ready in 30 to 60 days.

Every home at The Bluffs I is single-level. You choose from three floor plans ranging from 1,663 to 1,831 square feet, with 2 to 3 bedrooms and a 2-car garage standard. Base pricing starts at $465,990 and runs to $485,990, with quick move-in homes priced from the low $500Ks to about $570,000 once view lots and upgrades are factored in.

The location is the headline. You live behind a 24/7 guarded gate inside Lake Las Vegas, with access to a 320-acre lake, two Jack Nicklaus golf courses, a private Sports Club, a white-sand beach, paddle boarding, kayaking, and the dining and shops at MonteLago Village. The Strip is about 22 miles away, roughly 25 to 30 minutes via I-215 and I-515. This guide walks through the floor plans, true monthly cost, fees, schools, current incentive, and the buying process so you can decide if The Bluffs I fits your life.

Fees
HOA AND LID EXPLAINED

HOA (Homeowners Association): The Bluffs I has two HOA layers. The Bluffs I sub-association runs about $244 per month and covers your sub-community. The Lake Las Vegas master association runs about $153 per month and gives you access to the broader Lake Las Vegas amenities, gates, and shared upkeep. Total runs about $397 per month. Confirm exact figures and what each layer covers in writing before you sign.

LID (Local Improvement District): Lake Las Vegas sits inside an LID, which is a tax assessment that helped pay for the public infrastructure when the master plan was built. The benchmark assessment runs about $1,020 per year, or about $85 per month. The LID is billed through AMG of Nevada and shows up separately from your property tax bill. There is a 3% prepayment penalty if you choose to pay it off early, so most owners just let it ride year over year.

Why this matters: The all-in monthly cost at The Bluffs I includes that HOA plus LID stack. When you compare to a community without those line items, like Tuscany Village a few miles away, the gap is real. The trade-off is the gated lake lifestyle and amenities, which most buyers in this price band consider worth it.

FAQ
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Do I need my own real estate agent at The Bluffs I?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Century Communities, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, inspections, and negotiations on extras.
Can I negotiate with the builder?
Builders rarely lower the base price because it sets the comp for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate closing cost credits, design center upgrades, or rate buy-downs through the preferred lender. The current 3.875% FHA/VA rate is already strong, so always ask what is on the table when you visit, especially on quick move-in homes.
What upgrades are worth it at The Bluffs I?
Because Century follows an "Everything's Included" model, much of what other builders charge for is already standard. The upgrades that still make sense are anything structural (extended great room, primary suite expansion, garage cabinets, a casita-style flex room if offered) and items that are hard to add later. Cosmetic items like paint and lighting can be done after closing.
How long does it take to build a Bluffs I home?
A dirt-start home at The Bluffs I takes about 6 months from contract to close. Quick move-in homes already under construction can be ready in 30 to 60 days, which is also where the deepest incentives often sit.
What is the LID and does The Bluffs I have one?
Yes. Lake Las Vegas sits inside an LID (Local Improvement District), which is a tax assessment that paid for the public infrastructure when the master plan was built. The benchmark runs about $1,020 per year. It is billed through AMG of Nevada and shows up separately from your property tax bill. There is a 3% prepayment penalty if you pay it off early, so most owners just let it ride.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing and electrical, and 10 years on major structural components. Ask Century for the exact warranty document so you know what is covered.
Can I use VA or FHA at The Bluffs I?
Yes, and that is exactly the buyer profile the current "Find Your YES" promotion targets. The 3.875% rate is on a 30-year Fixed FHA or VA loan through Inspire Home Loans. Confirm eligibility, lock terms, and any concession trade-offs directly with Inspire.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement spells out how delays are handled and what your options are. Read this section carefully and ask your agent to explain anything that is unclear.
What is the difference between the three floor plans?
Residence 1663 is a 2-bedroom, 2-bath plan and the entry point. Residence 1742 is a 2-bedroom plus den, where the den can be converted to a 3rd bedroom. Residence 1831 is a true 3-bedroom, 2-bath plan and the largest in the community. All three are single-story with a 2-car garage.
Are there view lots at The Bluffs I?
Yes. Premium lots back to canyon and lake exposures and run a $3,000 to $75,000 lot premium on top of base price. If a view lot matters to you, ask for the current premium map and a list of available premium homesites before you write an offer.
