Birch is the price floor at $249 per square foot. The monthly HOA is not yet published. For a 76-lot subdivision with no shared amenities, the HOA usually lands between $60 and $90 a month. There is no master HOA layered on top. Lexington Chase is in a part of Enterprise where Special Improvement District (SID) assessments are common. The annual amount has not been listed yet. A typical Enterprise SID lands between $500 and $2,000 a year. SID is a county-managed bond that pays for the infrastructure built before the homes went in. Once you stack the mortgage, Nevada property tax at about 0.5% effective rate, insurance, the HOA estimate, and the SID estimate, a Birch home with 10% down at 6% lands roughly between $3,800 and $4,100 a month all in.
