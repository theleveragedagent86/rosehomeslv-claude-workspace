# The Maple Floor Plan at Lexington Chase by Richmond American The Maple is the flexible mid-tier plan at Lexington Chase by Richmond American Homes in 89148. It is a two-story home with 3 to 4 bedrooms, 2.5 to 3.5 baths, 2,500 square feet, and a 2-car garage, with base pricing from $596,950, which works out to about $239 per square foot. For an overview of the full community, see [our Lexington Chase community guide](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/lexington-chase). ## What Makes the Maple Stand Out? The Maple is the plan you pick when you do not know exactly what you need yet. It is the only plan in the lineup with a flex configuration, which means you can choose between 3 or 4 bedrooms and 2.5 or 3.5 baths depending on which structural option you take. That flexibility lets the home grow with you, whether you start as a couple and add a kid later, or you need a downstairs guest suite for visiting family. At 2,500 square feet, Maple is the median plan in price and size. The $239 per square foot price is the middle of the range at Lexington Chase. The 4-bedroom, 3.5-bath configuration is the strongest pick if you want a main-floor guest bedroom, a separate office, or a bigger bonus space upstairs. Plan on $10,000 to $30,000 in upgrades on top of base when you visit the design center. If you want the smaller entry plan, look at the [Birch plan](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/lexington-chase-birch). If you want more square footage, step up to the [Elm plan](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/lexington-chase-elm). The Maple is best for buyers who want options. Couples who plan to grow into the home, families who want a flexible upstairs, or buyers who want a guest suite on a budget. If you are buying new for the first time, our [first time buyer guide for Las Vegas new construction](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/first-time-buyer-las-vegas-new-construction) walks through the contract and design center. ## The Lexington Chase Community at a Glance Lexington Chase sits at 8212 Kinleigh Poulson Street, off Durango Drive and Mistral Avenue in 89148. The 215 Beltway is less than a mile away, and the Strip is about 10 minutes east. Total community size is 76 single-family homes, all two-story, with no on-site amenities and no gates. Sales opened at the end of January 2026. Richmond American is the only builder. The community feeds Sierra Vista High School, which is the confirmed high school assignment for this part of southwest Enterprise. Likely elementary is Billy and Rosemary Vassiliadis Elementary at an 8 out of 10 GreatSchools rating, with Faiss or Fertitta Middle as the likely middle. UnCommons, Durango Casino and Resort, and Mountain's Edge Marketplace are all close, and Red Rock Canyon is 15 to 20 minutes west on Blue Diamond Road. ## Pricing, HOA, and SID Costs Across the three plans, base prices run from $576,950 on Birch up to $636,950 on Elm. Maple is the median at $596,950, or about $239 per square foot. The monthly HOA is not yet published. For a 76-lot subdivision with no shared amenities, the HOA usually lands between $60 and $90 a month. There is no master HOA layered on top. Lexington Chase is in a part of Enterprise where Special Improvement District (SID) assessments are common. The annual amount has not been listed yet. A typical Enterprise SID lands between $500 and $2,000 a year. SID is a county-managed bond that pays for the streets, sewer, and infrastructure built before the homes went in. Once you stack the mortgage, Nevada property tax at about 0.5% effective rate, insurance, the HOA estimate, and the SID estimate, a Maple home with 10% down at 6% lands roughly between $3,950 and $4,200 a month all in. Verify with a lender on your specific scenario. ## How Lexington Chase Compares to Nearby New Construction The closest direct competitor is Gardenia by Beazer Homes, less than 2 miles north in the same 89148 zip.
