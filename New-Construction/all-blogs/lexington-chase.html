# Lexington Chase Las Vegas: Richmond American's New 76-Home Community in 89148 Lexington Chase is a brand-new Richmond American Homes community of 76 single-family homes in 89148, just off Durango Drive in southwest Las Vegas. Sales opened at the end of January 2026, base prices run $576,950 to $636,950, and every home is two stories with a 2-car garage. The 215 Beltway sits less than a mile away, and the Strip is about 10 minutes east. ## What Is Lexington Chase and Where Is It? Lexington Chase sits at 8212 Kinleigh Poulson Street, Las Vegas, NV 89148. The sales center is reached by taking Durango Drive to Mistral Avenue. The community is in the Enterprise township of unincorporated Clark County, in the southwest Las Vegas Valley, with the 215 Beltway less than a mile north and Blue Diamond Road close by. This is a stand-alone subdivision, not part of a larger master plan. Total community size is 76 homes, all single-family and two-story, on one phase that just opened. There are no on-site amenities, no gates, and no age restriction. Most buyers will find the size feels intimate compared to the 14,500-home Mountain's Edge master plan a few miles south. ## Builders and Floor Plans at Lexington Chase Richmond American Homes is the sole builder at Lexington Chase. Richmond American is a top Las Vegas builder owned by M.D.C. Holdings and is known for its design center experience and its Seasons Collection, which is the attainable-price line used at this community. The builder also offers in-house financing through HomeAmerican Mortgage, which can help with closing costs on current incentives. For more on the builders working in the valley right now, see [our 2026 Las Vegas builder rankings](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/top-home-builders-las-vegas-2026). Lexington Chase offers three floor plans, all two-story with 2-car garages. The [Birch floor plan](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/lexington-chase-birch) is the entry plan at 2,320 square feet, 4 bedrooms, 3 baths, from $576,950, which works out to about $249 per square foot. It is the most affordable way into the community and a good fit for first-time buyers stepping up from a townhome. The [Maple floor plan](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/lexington-chase-maple) is the middle plan at 2,500 square feet, 3 to 4 bedrooms, 2.5 to 3.5 baths, from $596,950, which is about $239 per square foot. Maple is the flexible option in the lineup, with a layout that can adjust to your needs. The [Elm floor plan](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/lexington-chase-elm) is the largest at 2,780 square feet, 4 bedrooms, 3 baths, from $636,950, which is about $229 per square foot and the best price per foot value in the community. Elm is the family pick when you need the most space. ## Pricing, HOA, and SID Costs at Lexington Chase Base prices range from $576,950 for Birch up to $636,950 for Elm. Median base price is $596,950 with the Maple plan. Price per square foot drops as you move up in plan size, from $249 on Birch to $229 on Elm. Lot premiums and design center upgrade pricing have not been published yet. Expect to add $10,000 to $30,000 in upgrades during your design center visit if you go new. The monthly HOA fee has not been published. For a 76-lot subdivision with no shared amenities, the HOA usually lands between $60 and $90 a month and covers landscaping along the Mistral and Durango frontage. Lexington Chase sits in an area where Special Improvement District (SID) assessments are common. The annual amount is not yet listed, and a typical Enterprise-corridor SID runs $500 to $2,000 a year, billed twice a year. SID is a county-managed bond that pays for the streets, sewer, and infrastructure built before the homes went in. If you want a clear walkthrough, read [how SID and LID assessments work in Las Vegas](www.rosehomeslv.com/blog/sid-lid-taxes-explained-las-vegas-new-construction). Once you add the mortgage, Nevada property tax at about 0.5% effective rate, insurance, the HOA estimate, and the SID estimate, a Lexington Chase home with 10% down at 6% lands roughly between $3,900 and $4,300 a month all in. ## How Lexington Chase Compares to Nearby New Construction The closest direct competitor is Gardenia by Beazer Homes, less than 2 miles north in the same 89148 zip code. Gardenia is mostly townhomes from $417K to $519K, with smaller homes from 1,226 to 1,700 square feet. The price per square foot at Gardenia is roughly $305 to $340. Lexington Chase is more home for less per foot, and detached, but the entry price is higher. Two to three miles southeast in 89178, Pulte and Lennar both build in the Mountain's Edge master plan with two-story homes in a similar price band. Lennar currently offers the strongest published incentive in the area, a 2.75% Year 1 rate buydown plus about $9,000 in credits. Lexington Chase trades off the master-plan amenities, like Exploration Peak Park, the Mountain's Edge Marketplace, and miles of trails, for a smaller and more intimate community plus a single HOA layer instead of master plus sub-HOA. It also sits closer to the 215 Beltway than the Mountain's Edge communities. If you are weighing both, ask Richmond American to pressure-test Lennar's published incentive before you sign. ## Schools and Lifestyle Around Lexington Chase Lexington Chase is in the Sierra Vista High School feeder, which is the confirmed high school assignment for southwest Enterprise. The likely elementary is Billy and Rosemary Vassiliadis Elementary, an 8 out of 10 GreatSchools rating, with Wilbur and Theresa Faiss Middle School or Victoria Fertitta Middle School as the likely middle. Always confirm school assignment with the official Clark County School District Zone Lookup before you write an offer because boundaries can shift. Bishop Gorman, Doral Academy, and Merryhill School are private and charter options nearby. Lifestyle is one of Lexington Chase's quiet wins. UnCommons mixed-use, with restaurants, shops, and offices, is right at 215 and Durango. Durango Casino and Resort opened recently and is also nearby. Mountain's Edge Marketplace is a quick drive south, and Smith's, Albertsons, and Trader Joe's all sit within 3 to 5 miles. The Strip is about 10 minutes east via the 215 to I-15, and Red Rock Canyon is 15 to 20 minutes west on Blue Diamond Road.
