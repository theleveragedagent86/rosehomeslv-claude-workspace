This is where most YouTube videos fall apart. They quote the base price and stop. Let me actually do the math on a Captiva at 382,990.

Put 5 percent down, which is 19,150. You finance 363,840. At a 6.5 percent rate on a 30 year fixed, your principal and interest is roughly 2,300 a month. If you use Century's advertised rate buydown into the high 3s for the first year, that number is meaningfully lower in year one and then steps up.

Property taxes in Clark County run about three quarters of a percent of assessed value. On a 383 thousand dollar home that is roughly 240 a month.

Homeowner insurance on a townhome is usually 65 to 100 a month. Call it 80.

HOA total is 245 a month. That is 162 for the Arroyo sub HOA plus 83 for the Skye Canyon master HOA.

PMI on a 5 percent down conventional is going to add roughly 130 to 200 a month depending on your credit.

Add it all up at full market rate and you are roughly 3,100 a month. With the rate buydown in year one you can be a few hundred lower. Always run your own numbers with a lender. Do not trust a YouTube estimate as gospel.

That covers Skye Fitness, the pool, the trails, and community events.

Now the SID. This is the special improvement district tax. It pays for streets, sewer, and infrastructure that the developer financed up front. On Arroyo, the SID is only about 138 dollars per year, billed at 68.83 every six months.

That is wildly low for Skye Canyon. The single family homes in this master plan are paying 2,400 to 4,800 dollars per year in SID. So a buyer at Arroyo is saving 200 to 400 dollars per month versus a Sierra or Terra buyer down the street. That alone closes a big chunk of the price gap.
