About
A NEW TOWNHOME COMMUNITY IN UNION VILLAGE

The Townes at Union Village is a new community of more than 80 two-story townhomes by Century Communities. The community sits at 1001 Gearus Place in Henderson, NV 89011, inside the 170-acre Union Village master plan. Grand opening was September 28, 2024, so this is one of the newest townhome options in Henderson.

Every home is a two-story townhome with three bedrooms, 2.5 baths, and a two-car garage. You get to choose from three floor plans ranging from 1,405 to 1,479 square feet. Base pricing starts at $349,990 and runs up to $366,990, with quick move-in homes priced from $379,990 to $420,240.

What makes Union Village stand out is the master plan itself. It is built around a health and wellness theme, with Henderson Hospital as the on-site anchor. You also get walkable access to Cowabunga Bay Water Park, a short drive to Galleria at Sunset mall, and quick US-95 access for getting around the valley. This guide walks you through the floor plans, true monthly cost, schools, current incentives, and the buying process so you can decide if The Townes at Union Village is right for you.

Fees
HOA, SID, AND LID EXPLAINED

HOA (Homeowners Association): The Townes at Union Village will likely have two HOA tiers because of how Union Village is structured. There is usually a master association fee for the broader Union Village community, plus a townhome sub-association fee that covers shared building elements like the exterior, roof, and common landscaping. Total HOA for Henderson townhomes typically runs $150 to $300 a month combined. Century Communities has not published the final fee schedule yet, so contact Ryan Rose for current details and ask the sales office for written confirmation before you sign.

SID (Special Improvement District): A SID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads, sewers, and parks. It shows up as a separate line item on your property tax bill. Henderson commonly uses LIDs to fund new construction infrastructure, so there may be an assessment here. If a LID or SID exists, it can add $100 to $300+ per month and run for 20 years or more.

LID (Local Improvement District): A LID works like a SID but covers a smaller area. Ask the sales team for any disclosure of SID or LID assessments before you write an offer. Ryan can also help you pull this information during your due diligence period so there are no surprises after closing.

FAQ
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Do I need my own real estate agent at The Townes at Union Village?
Yes, and it costs you nothing. The builder pays the agent's commission. The on-site sales rep works for Century Communities, not for you. Having your own agent gives you someone whose only job is to look out for your interests during contract review, inspections, and negotiations on extras.
Can I negotiate with the builder?
Builders rarely lower the base price because it affects comps for the rest of the community. You can usually negotiate for closing cost credits, design center upgrades, or rate buy-downs through the preferred lender. The current $50K incentive plus the 3.875% APR is already a strong package, so always ask what is on the table when you visit.
What upgrades are worth it at The Townes?
Because Century follows an "Everything's Included" model, much of what other builders charge for is already standard. The upgrades that still make sense are anything structural and items that are hard to add later. Cosmetic items like paint and lighting can be done after closing.
How long does it take to build a Century Communities townhome?
Most new construction in Las Vegas takes 6 to 9 months from contract to close. Quick move-in homes already under construction at The Townes can be ready in 30 to 60 days, which is also where the strongest incentives sit.
What is a SID or LID and does The Townes have one?
A SID or LID is a tax assessment that pays for public infrastructure like roads and sewers. It shows up on your property tax bill and can run for 20+ years. Henderson commonly uses LIDs to fund new construction infrastructure, so there may be one here. Contact Ryan Rose for current details, and verify with the builder before you sign.
What does the Nevada 1-2-10 warranty cover?
Most new homes in Nevada come with a 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems like plumbing and electrical, and 10 years on major structural components. Ask Century for the exact warranty document so you know what is covered.
Can I use VA or FHA at The Townes?
Yes. VA and FHA loans are typically accepted on Century Communities homes. The $50K incentive and below-market rate may have different terms depending on loan type, so ask Inspire Home Loans about how each program stacks with the offer.
What if construction is delayed?
Delays happen, especially with weather or supply chain issues. Your purchase agreement will spell out how delays are handled and what your options are. Read this section carefully and ask your agent to explain anything that is unclear.
Why is the $50K incentive only on quick move-ins?
Builders use deeper incentives on standing inventory because those homes are already built and they want to move them. Quick move-in homes give Century certainty on the closing date, so they pass more savings to the buyer through the rate buy-down and closing cost credits.
What schools does The Townes at Union Village feed?
Harriet Treem Elementary (7/10), Francis H. Cortney Junior High, and Basic Academy of International Studies, a magnet IB high school with a 93% graduation rate. Confirm zoning with CCSD before you sign, since school assignments can change. Charter and magnet options are also available through open enrollment.
