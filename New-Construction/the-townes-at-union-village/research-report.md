### Best For Summary

**The Townes at Union Village** is best for buyers who want walkable access to healthcare (Henderson Hospital is on-site), an easy US-95 commute, and a brand-new Century Communities townhome with strong incentives. The health-themed master plan is unique in the valley, and the 7/10 elementary school is a clear win for families with young kids.

**Symmetry Trails Phase II at Cadence** is best for first-time buyers looking for the lowest entry price point in a top-ranked master plan. D.R. Horton's smaller footprints keep the monthly payment down, and no SID/LID plus a low ~$75 Cadence HOA make this one of the most affordable new townhomes in Henderson.

**Alderidge Townhomes at Cadence** is best for buyers who like the Century Communities product but want Cadence's amenity package, including the 50-acre Central Park, pool, and trails. Same floor plans as Union Village, different lifestyle.

**Juniper Trails** is best for buyers who prioritize privacy and amenities. The gated entry, community pool, BBQ areas, and five floor plan options give more variety than most townhome competitors, with a price ceiling that reaches into single-family territory.

**Inspirada Townhomes** is best for buyers who want maximum builder choice (KB, Beazer, Pardee, Toll) and a mature master plan with established schools, pools, and parks. The trade-off is a longer commute to the Strip and SID/LID assessments that can add $500-$2,000+ per year.

### Key Takeaways
- The Townes at Union Village is priced competitively for new Henderson townhomes, but Cadence comps (Symmetry Trails, Alderidge) undercut entry pricing by $10-$15K and offer a much larger amenity package.
- Union Village's biggest differentiator is location: walkable to Henderson Hospital and Cowabunga Bay, with the fastest US-95 access in the comp set, putting the Strip ~15 minutes away.
- Century Communities is the sole builder at Union Village and also builds the same three plans (1405/1478/1479) at Alderidge in Cadence, so buyers can directly compare community amenities without changing product.
- Union Village's elementary school (Harriet Treem, 7/10) is the strongest in the comp set, though middle and high school ratings (3-4/10) trail the Cadence and Inspirada feeders only marginally.
- The Townes' below-market financing (3.875% APR) and up to $50K in incentives are the most aggressive in this comp set and meaningfully offset Cadence's lower base prices for buyers using builder financing.
