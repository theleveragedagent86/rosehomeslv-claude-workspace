# Content Rules — Digital Cannonball

These rules apply to ALL narrative text generated for the digital cannonball page.

---

## Voice and Tone

- **Professional but warm.** Talk like a trusted advisor explaining something to a friend. Never corporate, never salesy.
- **6th-grade reading level.** Short sentences. Simple words. No jargon.
- **Confident, not aggressive.** You know what you're doing. You don't need to shout about it.
- **Empathetic.** The homeowner just went through a failed listing. Acknowledge the frustration without dwelling on it.

## Hard Rules

1. **No em-dashes.** Use commas, periods, semicolons, or "and" instead. Em-dashes flag content as AI-generated.
2. **No fabricated data.** Never invent prices, square footage, school ratings, HOA amounts, DOM counts, or comp details. If data is missing, mark it as `[NOT FOUND]` so it can be filled manually.
3. **No filler adjectives.** Ban these words: "stunning," "exquisite," "premier," "exclusive," "nestled," "boasts," "prestigious," "unparalleled," "luxurious" (unless quoting a listing description).
4. **No "opportunity" language.** Never say "exclusive opportunity," "incredible value," or "don't miss out."
5. **Soft CTAs only.** Invite, don't push. "Want to talk through the numbers?" beats "Call me today!" Good examples:
   - "I'd be happy to walk you through this in person."
   - "If any of this resonates, let's set up 20 minutes."
   - "Want me to run fresh comps for your home?"
6. **Active voice.** "I analyzed 27 comparable homes" not "27 comparable homes were analyzed."
7. **Short paragraphs.** 3-4 sentences max per paragraph. One idea per paragraph.
8. **Address the homeowner directly.** Use "your home," "your listing," "your market." Never "the subject property."

## Las Vegas Context

- This tool is focused on **Las Vegas / Clark County, NV** (Henderson, Summerlin, Spring Valley, Centennial Hills, North Las Vegas, Skye Canyon, Green Valley).
- Reference local landmarks, neighborhoods, and market dynamics when relevant.
- If the property is outside Las Vegas, still follow these rules but skip Las Vegas-specific references.

## Agent Positioning

- **Ryan Rose** is a data-driven, ethical real estate agent. Not a salesperson.
- He uses technology and proactive marketing strategies that most agents don't.
- His differentiator is preparation: he researches before reaching out, not after.
- Never claim to be the "best" or "#1." Let the data and the plan speak for themselves.

## Numbers and Formatting

- Spell out numbers one through nine. Use digits for 10 and above.
- Use `$X.XXM` for millions (e.g., "$1.25M"), `$XXX,XXX` for thousands (e.g., "$425,000").
- Use commas in numbers: "4,575 square feet" not "4575 square feet."
- Dates: "March 15, 2026" or "Mar 15, 2026" in narrative. "2026-03-15" only in data tables.
