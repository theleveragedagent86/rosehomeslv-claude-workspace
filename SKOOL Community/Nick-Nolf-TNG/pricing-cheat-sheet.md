# Pricing Cheat Sheet (Internal — Never Show Clients)

---

## The ROI Formula (Nate Herk)

```
Hours saved/week  x  Hourly rate  x  4 weeks  =  Monthly savings
Monthly savings  x  12  =  Annual savings
Charge 20-30% of Year 1 savings  =  Your project fee
```

### Worked Example:
- Client team spends **15 hrs/week** on automatable tasks
- Labor cost: **$25/hr**
- Monthly cost: 15 x $25 x 4 = **$1,500/mo**
- Annual cost: **$18,000/yr**

### How to price each tier:

| Tier | % of Problem Solved | Savings Captured | x 20-30% | Price |
|------|---------------------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| Starter | ~30% | ~$5,400/yr | x 30% | **~$1,600** |
| Growth | ~60% | ~$10,800/yr | x 25% | **~$2,700** |
| Complete | ~100% | ~$18,000/yr | x 22% | **~$3,960** |

**Key:** The bigger the package, the lower your percentage — this incentivizes going bigger and gives better ROI optics for the client.

---

## Single Automation Tiering

If the client only wants ONE thing automated, tier by **depth**, not by number of automations:

| Tier | Depth | Example (Email Automation) |
|------|-------|---------------------------|
| Starter | Basic — drafts responses, team reviews everything | Handles top 3-5 email types |
| Growth | Intermediate — categorizes, routes, follows up automatically | Handles all email types + urgency routing |
| Complete | Full — near-autonomous, minimal human touch needed | Auto-sends low-risk replies, custom templates, reporting |

## Pricing Rules

1. **Floor: $1,500** — Never go below this for any package
2. **If >50% say yes immediately, price is too low** (Nate Herk)
3. **Never discount price — reduce scope instead** (Hormozi)
4. **Present Option 3 first** → then Option 1 → then Option 2 as "what I recommend" (Hormozi anchor strategy)
5. **50% deposit, 50% on delivery** — non-negotiable
6. **Retainer is the long game** — don't push it in the first meeting, mention it exists

---

## Retainer Pricing Guidelines

| Level | Range | When to use |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Basic | $300-500/mo | Small client, simple automations, just needs monitoring |
| Standard | $500-1,000/mo | Mid-size client, wants ongoing optimization |
| Premium | $1,000-2,000/mo | Larger client, multiple automations, wants strategic input |

**When to bring up the retainer:**
- NOT during the initial pitch (keeps sticker shock low)
- Mention it exists when presenting the proposal ("ongoing support is available")
- Sell it properly after the build is delivered and they see results
- Retainer sells itself once they rely on the automation

---

## Presentation Order (Hormozi 3-Tier Strategy)

1. Do the ROI math LIVE on screen — let the annual number sink in
2. Present **Option 3 (Complete)** first — anchors high
3. Present **Option 1 (Starter)** — feels too small now
4. Present **Option 2 (Growth)** — "this is what I'd recommend" — feels just right
5. Most people pick the middle

## Objection Responses

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "Too expensive" | "I hear you. Let's look at the ROI — which option gets you the fastest payback?" |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | "I can adjust the scope. We could start with [smaller tier] and add more later." |
| "Need to think about it" | "Totally fair. What specifically would you want to think through?" (surfaces real objection) |
| "No case studies?" | "This is my first build in [their industry], which means you get my full attention and best pricing. I want YOUR company to be my flagship case study." |
| "What if it breaks?" | "50/50 payment protects you — you don't pay the rest until it works. Plus [30/60/90] days support." |
| "We tried automation before" | "What went wrong? [Listen] — Mine is custom-built for your workflow, not a one-size-fits-all template." |

---

## First Client Pricing Psychology

- It's OK to price at the lower end of the range for client #1
- The case study and testimonial are worth the slight discount
- But do NOT go below the $1,500 floor — it sets a bad precedent
- Frame it: "I'm offering my best pricing because I want to build a long-term partnership"
- Never say "discount" — say "best pricing" or "early partner rate"
