### SHORT 2 — THE QUICK WIN

**Script:**
Stop posting the same listing caption on Instagram and Facebook. They're two completely different platforms and your audience is scrolling for two completely different reasons. On Instagram, your first line IS the ad. It has to stop the scroll. Something like "This backyard was built for Saturday mornings" — that's a hook. On Facebook, nobody cares about your granite countertops in the first sentence. Lead with lifestyle. Lead with neighborhood. "Five minutes from Summerlin trails, ten minutes from Downtown, and the kind of street where neighbors actually wave." Same listing. Completely different post. Try it on your next one — write the Instagram caption hook-first, write the Facebook post neighborhood-first. You'll see the difference in engagement immediately.

**Caption:**
Your Instagram post and your Facebook post should NOT be the same caption.

Instagram: hook in the first line. That first sentence is the only thing people see before "more." It has to stop the scroll.

Facebook: lead with lifestyle and neighborhood. Nobody's searching Facebook for square footage — they want to picture themselves living there.

Same listing. Two different posts. Two different engagement levels. Try it on your next one and watch what happens.

Save this for your next listing day.

### SHORT 4 — THE HOT TAKE

**Script:**
Posting the same listing photo with the same caption on every single platform is lazy. And your audience can absolutely tell. Your Instagram followers, your Facebook friends, the agents in your networking groups — they're all different people looking for different things. When you blast the same "Just listed! 3 bed 2 bath, gorgeous upgrades, call me!" across every platform, you know what that says? It says you didn't care enough to spend five extra minutes tailoring the message. And here's the one nobody talks about — the agent-to-agent post is the most underrated marketing move in real estate. A professional, clean post in an agent Facebook group has a completely different tone than a consumer-facing caption. Different audience, different goal. Start treating each platform like a different conversation, because that's exactly what it is.

**Caption:**
Hot take: copying and pasting the same listing caption across every platform isn't marketing. It's just noise.
