[SCROLL TO FIRST OUTPUT]

**Number one — Instagram caption.**

[HIGHLIGHT THE FIRST LINE OF THE INSTAGRAM POST]

Look at the first line. That's your hook. On Instagram, only the first line or two shows before people have to tap "more." So this line has to stop the scroll. It can't be "Just listed! Beautiful 4-bedroom home." Nobody's stopping for that. See how Claude wrote this — it leads with something specific, something that creates curiosity. That's your Voice Profile at work combined with good prompting.

And look at the end — there's a clear call to action. Not "DM me for details" in some generic way. It's specific. It tells people exactly what to do next.

Hashtags — count them. Five or fewer. Relevant to the area and the property type. Not hashtag-blessed, hashtag-realestate, hashtag-dreamhome. Actual useful hashtags.

[SCROLL TO SECOND OUTPUT]

**Number two — the Facebook post, neighborhood angle.**

Now here's where most agents mess up on Facebook. They post the same thing as Instagram — just the house specs. Four bed, three bath, pool, two-car garage. Nobody on Facebook cares about that in their feed. They're scrolling past pictures of their cousin's kid and memes. You have to give them a reason to stop.

This post leads with the lifestyle. The neighborhood. What it actually feels like to live there. Where you're getting coffee in the morning, where your kids are going to school, what the street looks like at six PM when everyone's walking their dogs. That's what gets engagement on Facebook. The house details are in there, but they're supporting the story, not leading it.

[SCROLL TO THIRD OUTPUT]

**Number three — the Just Listed announcement.**

This one's clean. Professional. This is the one you'd post on your business page or send to your sphere. It's got energy without being over the top. No "OMG you HAVE to see this place!" — just confident, clear, and it positions you as the professional you are.

Notice the tone shift from the Instagram post. Same listing, completely different feel. That's the whole point of doing five different posts instead of copying and pasting the same thing everywhere.

[SCROLL TO FOURTH OUTPUT]

**Number four — the Reel or Story script.**

[HIGHLIGHT THE FIVE LINES]

Now pay attention here — this isn't a caption. This is five lines of text-on-screen for a walkthrough video. You know those Reels where the agent's walking through the house and text pops up on screen? That's what this is.

Five lines. Each one is punchy. Each one reveals something new about the property. You film yourself walking through the house — takes two minutes on your phone — and you just lay these text lines over the video. Done.

And here's a little hack — if you can, make line one the hook that shows before someone even taps into the Reel. That first line needs to earn the swipe. Claude already knows that because of how we wrote the prompt.

[SCROLL TO FIFTH OUTPUT]

**Number five — agent-to-agent post.**

This one's for your agent Facebook groups. And the tone here is completely different because your audience is different. You're not selling the lifestyle to a buyer. You're talking to another agent who might have a buyer. So it's professional, it's specific about the property details that matter to agents — price point, condition, what kind of buyer this is perfect for, and whether you're offering co-op compensation or whatever the relevant details are.

This is the post most agents skip entirely, and it's honestly one of the most effective ones. A good agent-to-agent post in an active Facebook group can get your listing in front of dozens of buyer's agents in one shot.
