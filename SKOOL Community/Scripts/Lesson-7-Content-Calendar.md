[BRIEF PAUSE]

So here's the thing. The number one reason agents are inconsistent on social media — and I mean the number one reason — is not that they're lazy. It's not that they don't know what to post. It's that they're trying to come up with something new every single day. And that is a losing strategy. Because on Monday you're motivated. Tuesday you're busy with showings. Wednesday you forget. Thursday you feel guilty, so you throw up a random listing photo with no caption. And by Friday you've basically ghosted your audience for the week.

Sound familiar? Yeah, it was me too.

The fix is stupid simple. It's called batching. You sit down once a week — I do mine Sunday nights, some people like Monday mornings, doesn't matter — you spend fifteen minutes, you plan all seven days, and then during the week you just post. The content is already written. The ideas are already mapped out. You're not creating anymore. You're just publishing.

Now, if you went through Lesson 5, you already know how to take one listing and multiply it into five different posts. That was powerful. Today we're zooming out. Instead of five posts from one listing, we're building a full, varied week of content that makes you look like the agent who has it all together. Because you will.

[TRANSITION — SCREEN SHARE: SHOW CONTENT FRAMEWORK AS A SIMPLE GRAPHIC OR TYPED LIST]

Alright, before we touch Claude, before we touch any AI, we need to talk about what you're actually going to post. Because here's a mistake I see all the time — agents who do post consistently just post the same type of thing over and over. Seven listing posts in a row. Or seven market update graphics that all look the same. And their engagement flatlines.

Here's why. Your listing posts? Those sell houses. They absolutely do. But your personal posts and your educational posts? Those are what get you followed. Those are what make people feel like they know you. And people hire agents they feel like they know.

So here's the weekly content framework I use. And I want you to screenshot this or write it down, because this is your new posting rhythm.

[SCREEN: DISPLAY EACH DAY AS YOU SAY IT]

Monday — Market stat or insight. Something educational. "Las Vegas median home price just hit X." "Inventory is up twelve percent." One stat, your take on it, done.

Tuesday — Listing spotlight. Feature one of your active listings. You already know how to write these from Lesson 5.

Wednesday — Behind-the-scenes or day-in-the-life. This is the one where you pull out your phone at a showing or at an inspection and shoot a quick fifteen-second clip. The AI writes the caption, you just provide the moment.

Thursday — Buyer or seller tip. "Three things to do before your appraisal." "Why you should never skip a home inspection." These are the posts that get saved and shared more than anything else you'll ever put out.

Friday — Community spotlight. A local restaurant, a neighborhood park, an event happening this weekend. This positions you as the neighborhood expert, not just a salesperson.

Saturday — Open house promo or showing recap. If you've got an open house, promote it. If you just showed houses, talk about what you saw.

Sunday — Personal post. Why you got into real estate. A client story that moved you. Even what you did this weekend with your family. People want to see the human behind the yard sign.

[FACE BACK ON CAMERA]

Now — this framework is not a straitjacket. If you don't have an open house on Saturday, swap it. If something wild happens in the market on a Thursday, post that instead of a tip. The point is you have a default plan so you never start from zero. You always know what type of content goes where.

Alright. Now let's build this thing.
