## Setup A: Set Up Claude Cowork (Recommended)

Claude Cowork (also called Claude Code) is the desktop tool that makes Claude genuinely useful for real estate work — not the stripped-down browser version most agents start with. It remembers your files, works inside your folders, and handles multi-step workflows that browser Claude chokes on. If you have Claude Pro or Max, this is the setup you want.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- Claude Cowork installed and authenticated on your machine
- Your first real estate project folder opened and ready
- A working understanding of why Cowork beats the browser for listing work, marketing, and client content
- A quick test prompt run so you know the tool is live

**Prerequisite:** Claude Pro or Max subscription. No Pro/Max yet? Skip to Setup B and use the browser Project workflow instead — you can still complete every lesson in this community.

**Action step:** Once you're authenticated and your first project is open, head straight to Lesson 3. If you hit an install snag, drop a screenshot in the Q&A and I'll jump in.

## Setup B: Set Up a Claude Project (Browser Users)

No Pro or Max subscription? No problem. A Claude Project at claude.ai gives you a persistent workspace that remembers your instructions, your files, and your context across every conversation — which is exactly what we need for the workflows in this community. This setup takes about five minutes.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- A dedicated Claude Project set up for all your real estate work
- Baseline instructions loaded so Claude knows who you are every time you open a chat
- A workspace ready to absorb your Voice Profile in the next lesson
- A repeatable home base for listings, social content, emails, and marketing

**Paste this into your Project Instructions:**

> I am a real estate agent in [market]. I use this project for all of my real estate AI workflows including listing descriptions, social media content, email templates, and marketing materials. When I share my Voice Profile in a future conversation, remember it and apply it to all content you write for me.

Swap `[market]` for your city or region before you save.

**Action step:** Save your project instructions, then head to Lesson 3 next. Post a screenshot of your set-up Project in Wins & Results so we know you're rolling.

## Lesson 3: Your First Listing Description in 90 Seconds

This is the lesson that pays for the membership. We take a real property, feed Claude the right inputs, and generate a publication-ready MLS description in about ninety seconds — written in your voice, not in the generic real estate slop every other agent is pasting into the MLS. We ban the cliché words ("boasts," "nestled," "stunning") and use a style reference so the output sounds like you wrote it.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- A full MLS-ready listing description for a real property
- A reusable prompt template you can run on every new listing
- A style-reference workflow that makes the copy sound like you, not a robot
- Hours of your week back, starting today

**Prompt template — copy, paste, fill in the brackets:**

> You are a real estate listing copywriter specializing in MLS descriptions. Write a single, publication-ready MLS listing description for the property below.
>
> **Style reference:** Match the tone, sentence rhythm, and vocabulary of this listing I've written previously:
> """
> [paste your previous listing description here]
> """
>
> **Property details:**
> - Address: [address]
> - Beds/Baths: [X] bed / [X] bath
> - Square footage: [X]
> - Year built: [X]
> - Key features: [list 5-8 features]
> - Neighborhood/community: [name]
> - What sets this home apart: [1-2 sentences in your own words]
>
> **Requirements:**
> - Stay under 250 words
> - Lead with or prominently feature the top 3 most compelling attributes
> - Do not use the words "boasts," "nestled," or "stunning"
> - Do not exaggerate or imply features that aren't explicitly listed
> - Write in a confident, factual tone
> - Ready to paste directly into MLS — no headers, no commentary, no em-dashes

**Action step:** Run this on a real listing today — active, pending, or one you wrote last month. Post the result (plus the original, if you want a side-by-side) in Wins & Results. This is the fastest feedback loop in the community.

## Lesson 4: Train AI to Sound Like You (Not Like a Robot)

Most agents hate AI-generated content because it sounds like AI-generated content. This lesson fixes that problem once and for all. You'll build a Voice Profile — a short paragraph that teaches Claude exactly how you write, so every future prompt produces content that sounds like you, not a press release.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- A ~150 word Voice Profile tailored to your natural writing style
- A repeatable method for refreshing it as your voice evolves
- A simple test to check if Claude is "getting" your voice
- The foundation piece every other lesson in this community builds on

**Prompt template:**

> Analyze the following 3-5 writing samples from a real estate professional. Extract their:
> 1. Tone (formal, casual, warm, direct, etc.)
> 2. Vocabulary patterns (words they use often, words they avoid)
> 3. Sentence structure preferences (short and punchy vs. longer and descriptive)
> 4. Personality traits that come through in their writing
> 5. Any recurring phrases or signatures
>
> Summarize this as a "Voice Profile" I can paste into future prompts so AI-generated content matches this person's natural style. Keep the profile under 150 words.
>
> Writing samples:
> [paste samples here]

**Action step:** Post your Voice Profile in Q&A. The community will flag anything that doesn't sound like the real you — tune it before you move on to Lesson 5.

## Lesson 5: One Listing = 5 Social Posts in 10 Minutes

One listing. Five platforms. Ten minutes. This is the first lesson where your Voice Profile earns its keep — you'll feed it into a single prompt that spits out an Instagram caption, a Facebook neighborhood post, a Just Listed announcement, a short-form video script, and an agent-to-agent post. Then you do a quick 2-minute edit pass, and you're done for the week.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- Five ready-to-post pieces of content from one listing
- A repeatable workflow that takes ~10 minutes instead of 2 hours
- A 2-minute edit checklist so nothing goes out sounding off
- A clear understanding of which post type fits which platform

**Prompt template:**

> Using this listing description and my voice profile below, create 5 social media posts for different platforms and angles. Each post should feel like I wrote it — not like AI generated it.
>
> Voice Profile: [paste from Lesson 4]
> Listing Description: [paste from Lesson 3]
>
> Create:
> 1. Instagram caption (under 150 words, hook in first line, end with CTA)
> 2. Facebook post (focus on neighborhood/lifestyle, not just the house)
> 3. "Just Listed" announcement (professional, exciting but not cheesy)
> 4. Short-form video script — 5 text-on-screen lines for a reel/story
> 5. Agent-to-agent post for a Facebook agent group
>
> No emojis unless I use them in my voice profile. Max 5 relevant hashtags on the Instagram post only.

**Action step:** Run this on one of your current (or past) listings and drop all 5 posts in Wins & Results. Bonus points if you share the time-savings number.

You write the same five emails every week: new listing announcement, open house invite, price reduction, showing feedback request, buyer-lender intro. At 10-15 minutes each, that's roughly 95 minutes of your life — every week. This lesson turns those five emails into reusable templates you customize in 30 seconds flat, all in your voice.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- 5 on-brand email templates ready to drop into your CRM or Gmail
- [BRACKET] variables for fast, mistake-proof customization
- A ~95 min/week time save (that's over 80 hours a year)
- A system for adding new templates as new situations come up

**Prompt template:**

> I'm a real estate agent. Create email templates for the following 5 situations I encounter every week. Use my voice profile so they sound like me, not a form letter. Each email should be concise (under 150 words), professional, and include a clear call to action.
>
> Voice Profile: [paste]
>
> Create templates for:
> 1. [email type 1]
> 2. [email type 2]
> 3. [email type 3]
> 4. [email type 4]
> 5. [email type 5]
>
> For each template, use [BRACKETS] for any variable I'll swap out (property address, client name, date, etc.) so I can reuse them.

**Action step:** Post your best template in Wins & Results so the rest of the community can steal the structure (and give you feedback on tone).

## Lesson 7: Plan a Full Week of Content in 15 Minutes

Content consistency is the #1 thing agents say they struggle with — and the #1 thing that builds a referral-based business. In this lesson, we'll batch an entire week of social content in 15 minutes using Claude + your Voice Profile, so you can stop posting randomly and start showing up like a pro.

**What you'll walk away with:**
- A 7-day content calendar covering market stats, listings, BTS, tips, community, open houses, and personal posts
- 7 ready-to-paste captions written in your actual voice (not generic "agent-speak")
- Image/video ideas for each day so you know exactly what to shoot
- A repeatable batching workflow you can run every Sunday in under 15 minutes

**Prompt template:**

> I'm a real estate agent in [market]. Create a 7-day social media content calendar for me. Mix these content types across the week:
>
> - Monday: Market stat or insight (educational)
> - Tuesday: Listing spotlight or new listing
> - Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes / day-in-the-life
> - Thursday: Tip for buyers or sellers
> - Friday: Community spotlight (local business, restaurant, event)
> - Saturday: Open house promo or showing recap
> - Sunday: Personal post (why I do this, client story, weekend life)
>
> Voice Profile: [paste]
>
> For each day, write the full post copy (ready to paste), suggest an image/video idea, and include a hook as the first line. Keep each post under 150 words.
>
> Current listings I want to feature this week: [list any active listings]
> Recent wins/closings I can mention: [any recent sales]
> Local events happening this week: [anything relevant]

**Action step:** Post your content calendar in Wins & Results. Bonus points if you screenshot your scheduled queue in Meta Business Suite — I want to see that week lined up.
