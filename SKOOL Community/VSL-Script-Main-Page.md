# Main Page VSL Script — The Leveraged Agent

## Overview

Short video sales letter for the Skool about page. Blends Saraev's direct, no-hype delivery with Herk's polished production and Hormozi's value stack + guarantee framework.

**Target length:** 3-4 minutes (added time for value stack and guarantee — these are what convert)
**Format:** Talking head, casual-professional. Shoot on your phone or webcam. Clean background, good lighting, good mic (Hormozi: audio > everything else). NOT a studio — you're a working agent, not a course guru.
**Tone:** Direct, revenue-focused, transparent. Give away the "what" freely, make the "how" irresistible.

## Production Notes

**The Blend: Saraev authenticity + Herk polish + Hormozi script quality**

- **Script is king (Hormozi).** This VSL lives or dies on the words, not the production. Rehearse the value stack and guarantee until they flow naturally. Use bullet points next to the camera — don't read a teleprompter, but don't wing the numbers either.
- **Audio is non-negotiable (Hormozi).** Bad audio = instant click away. Invest in a decent mic ($50-100 lavalier or USB condenser). This is the one production element you cannot skip.
- **Lighting > camera (Hormozi).** A well-lit iPhone video beats a poorly lit professional camera. Ring light or window light. Clean, simple.
- **Authentic delivery (Saraev).** This should feel like you're talking to a friend at a coffee shop who asked "what do you do?" A few natural pauses and "um"s are fine — they make it real. Don't over-polish.
- **Light editing (Herk).** Cut dead air and obvious stumbles. Add text overlays during the value stack section (show the dollar amounts on screen as you say them — this doubles the impact). No fancy transitions or B-roll needed.
- **Show proof (Herk + Hormozi).** During the "What's Inside" section, cut to 5-10 seconds of screen share: the Skool classroom, a real listing description being generated, a social post going live. Even brief visual proof is more persuasive than 30 seconds of talking.
- **Shoot 2-3 takes and pick the best.** Don't spend a week on this. Hormozi: "Good enough is good enough. Most people use production quality as a procrastination tool."

## Hormozi Pricing Psychology Notes

These aren't in the VSL but inform how you think about pricing and positioning:

- **Never discount — add value instead.** If someone pushes back on $86/month, don't lower the price. Add a bonus: "I can't lower the price, but I'll include a 1-on-1 onboarding call to get you set up."
- **Anchor with the annual savings number.** If an agent saves 10 hrs/week and values their time at $50/hr, that's $2,000/month in saved time. $86 is 4% of the value they're getting.
- **Cost of inaction frame.** "Every month without these systems, you're spending 40+ hours on work AI can handle. That's not free — that's the most expensive option."
- **Founding member pricing.** Consider: "The first 50 members lock in $86/month for life. Price goes up after that." Creates urgency, rewards early adopters, and gives you a reason to raise prices later (Saraev does this — price increases every 40 members).
- **Hormozi's Grand Slam Offer test:** If you describe this offer and the response isn't "How is that even possible at that price?" — the offer isn't good enough yet. At $86/month with the value stack above, you should pass this test.
