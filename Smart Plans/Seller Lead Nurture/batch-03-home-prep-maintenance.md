### EMAIL 23 — Day 309 | Category: Home Prep & Maintenance
**Delay:** 309 days
**Subject:** Which upgrades actually pay off here

Hi #lead_first_name#,

Not all renovations are created equal. Some give you a great return when you sell. Others barely move the needle.

In Las Vegas, here's what tends to deliver the best ROI:

- **Garage door replacement:** Often returns 90% or more of the cost. Buyers notice a clean, modern garage door right away.
- **Minor kitchen remodel:** Think new hardware, countertops, and a fresh backsplash. Not a full gut job.
- **Entry door replacement:** A steel or fiberglass front door is affordable and makes a strong first impression.
- **Bathroom refresh:** Updated vanity, mirror, and fixtures can transform a dated bathroom for under $3,000.
- **Exterior paint:** Especially important in Las Vegas where sun damage is real.

The upgrades that tend to have the lowest return? Swimming pool additions, high-end kitchen overhauls, and sunroom conversions. They're nice to enjoy while you live there, but buyers won't pay dollar-for-dollar for them.

**Subject:** Kitchen or bathroom: where to spend

Hi #lead_first_name#,

If you only have the budget to update one room before selling, which should it be? Kitchen or bathroom?

In most cases, a minor kitchen update wins. Buyers spend a lot of time imagining themselves in the kitchen. Clean countertops, modern hardware, and updated lighting go a long way.

But here's the exception. If your bathroom is truly outdated, with old tile, a worn vanity, or a stained tub, that can turn buyers off fast. A dated kitchen is forgettable. A dated bathroom feels unclean.

My rule of thumb: update the one that looks the worst. If they're about equal, go with the kitchen.

Hi #lead_first_name#,

In Las Vegas, your HVAC system works harder than almost anywhere else in the country. Buyers know this. A well-maintained system gives them confidence. A neglected one raises red flags.

### EMAIL 63 — Day 869 | Category: Home Prep & Maintenance
**Delay:** 869 days
**Subject:** What to fix before listing (and what to skip)

Hi #lead_first_name#,

When you're getting ready to sell, it's tempting to fix everything. But not every repair is worth your time or money.

**Fix these:**
- Leaky faucets and running toilets
- Broken light switches or outlets
- Cracked windows or damaged screens
- Peeling paint inside or outside
- Anything that looks like a safety issue

**Skip these:**
- Full room remodels (unless the room is truly unusable)
- Replacing carpet that's in decent shape
- Upgrading appliances that still work fine

**Always disclose these:**
- Past water damage, even if repaired
- Known foundation issues
- Roof repairs or replacements
- HOA violations or pending assessments

Fixing small things and being upfront about big things builds trust with buyers. That trust leads to better offers.
