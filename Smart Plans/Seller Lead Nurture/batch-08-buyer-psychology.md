**Subject:** What buyers notice in the first 7 seconds

Hey #lead_first_name#,

When a buyer walks into a home, they form an opinion fast. Really fast. Most research says it happens in about seven seconds.

So what are they noticing in those first moments?

**Smell.** This is the biggest one. If a home smells like pets, cooking, or mustiness, buyers mentally check out before they even look around.

**Light.** Dark rooms feel small and uninviting. Bright rooms feel clean and open. Something as simple as opening blinds and turning on every light can change how a home feels.

**Clutter.** Buyers can't picture themselves living in your home if your stuff is everywhere. Clear counters, tidy rooms, and open spaces let them imagine their own life there.

**The entryway.** This is literally the first thing they see. A clean front door, a clear walkway, and a welcoming entry set the tone for the entire tour.

You don't get a second chance at that first impression. The good news is that most of these fixes cost nothing. They just take a little effort.

### EMAIL 28 — Day 379 | Category: Buyer Psychology
**Delay:** 379 days
**Subject:** Deal-breakers that make buyers walk away

Hey #lead_first_name#,

Buyers can be forgiving about a lot of things. Paint color, outdated light fixtures, even old appliances. But some issues send them straight out the door.

**Water damage or stains.** Even old, fixed water stains on ceilings or walls make buyers nervous. They assume the worst.

**Foundation cracks.** Small cosmetic cracks are common. But visible structural cracks trigger fear of expensive repairs.

**Outdated electrical or plumbing.** If a home looks like it hasn't been updated in decades, buyers worry about safety and hidden costs.

**Strong odors.** Smoke, pet smells, or heavy air freshener (which makes buyers think you're covering something up) can end a showing fast.

**Neglected yards.** A messy exterior signals that the inside might have problems too. Buyers judge a home before they even get to the front door.

The best part? Most of these are fixable before you ever list. A little prep work can keep buyers from walking away and help you get stronger offers.
