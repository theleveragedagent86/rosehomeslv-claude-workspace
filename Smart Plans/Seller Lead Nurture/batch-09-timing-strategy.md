One of the most common questions I get is, "When's the best time to sell in Las Vegas?"

Here's the honest answer. It depends on your situation more than the calendar.

Yes, Las Vegas typically sees more buyer activity in the spring and early summer. Families want to move between school years. That extra demand can mean more showings and stronger offers.

**Subject:** Are you financially ready to sell?

Hi #lead_first_name#,

Selling a home is a big financial move. Before you list, it helps to know where you stand with a few key numbers.

**Your equity.** What do you owe versus what your home is worth? This tells you roughly what you'll walk away with after the sale.

**Selling costs.** Most sellers spend about 8 to 10 percent of the sale price on agent commissions, closing costs, repairs, and moving expenses. It's smart to plan for those.

**Your next move.** If you're buying another home, do you know what you can afford? If you're renting, do you have first month's rent and a deposit covered?

**Emergency savings.** Unexpected costs pop up during every transaction. Having a financial cushion keeps stress low.

**Tax implications.** If you've lived in your home for at least two of the last five years, you may qualify for a capital gains exclusion. That can save you a lot of money.

You don't need a perfect financial picture to sell. But knowing these numbers ahead of time helps you make confident decisions.

### EMAIL 39 — Day 533 | Category: Timing & Strategy
**Delay:** 533 days
**Subject:** Should you sell first or buy first?

Hi #lead_first_name#,

This is one of the trickiest decisions sellers face. Do you sell your current home first, or lock in the new one before listing?

**Selling first** means you know exactly how much money you have. No scrambling, no bridge loans. The downside? You might need temporary housing while you find your next place.

**Buying first** lets you move on your own timeline. But it can mean carrying two mortgages, which adds pressure to accept a lower offer on your current home.

There are strategies for both. Sale-leaseback agreements, extended closings, and contingency offers can all help bridge the gap.

The right path depends on your finances, your local market, and your comfort level with a little uncertainty.

Hey #lead_first_name#,

A lot of sellers want to know, "How fast can I sell?" Here's a realistic timeline for Las Vegas.

**Prep time:** 2 to 4 weeks to get your home show-ready. This includes cleaning, small repairs, and staging.

**On market:** The average Las Vegas home sells in about 30 to 60 days, depending on price, location, and condition.

So from start to finish, most sellers should plan on about 3 to 4 months total. Some homes sell faster. Some take longer.

The biggest factor in speed? Pricing it right from the start. Overpriced homes sit. Properly priced homes move.

**Subject:** Why buyer affordability matters to you

Hey #lead_first_name#,

You might be wondering why interest rates matter to you as a seller. After all, you're the one selling, not buying.

Here's the connection. When rates go up, buyers can afford less. That shrinks the pool of people who can buy your home at full price. When rates drop, buyers can afford more, and competition heats up.

This doesn't mean you should wait for the "perfect" rate environment. It means pricing strategy matters even more. A good agent adjusts the approach based on current buyer affordability, not just comparable sales.

**Subject:** Why "testing the market" backfires

Hi #lead_first_name#,

Some sellers list their home at a high price "just to see what happens." It sounds harmless. But it usually costs them money.

Homes get the most attention in the first two weeks on the market. If you're overpriced during that window, serious buyers skip right past you. By the time you drop the price, the buzz is gone. Buyers wonder what's wrong with the home.

The result? You often end up selling for less than you would have if you'd priced it right from the start.

A smarter approach is to get a realistic market analysis before you list. That way you attract the right buyers from day one.
